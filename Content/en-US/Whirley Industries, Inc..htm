Whirley Industries (doing business as Whirley-DrinkWorks!) is the company behind those collectible cups and mugs filling kitchen cabinets. The firm designs and manufactures promotional drinkware for theme parks, sports venues, convenience stores, and other businesses. The company's Toppers line casts colorful animals and licensed characters atop the lids of plastic cups. In addition to its cups and straws, Whirley Industries creates specialty lunchboxes and popcorn buckets for children, and insulated coffee containers and barware for adults. The company also produces self-serve slush stands that can be installed at festivals, water parks, and zoos.

Whirley Industries has 17 offices in the US and 8 offices in Canada, South America, Europe, and Asia.

It has sales operation in Asia, North and Central America, Europe, the Middle East, and South America, including in Australia, Canada, Japan, Mexico, Puerto Rico, and the UK.

Operations

Since 2004 the company has reground and reused more than 5 million pounds of plastic and created a product line made of 85% recycled plastic. Whirley Industries holds patents on its drinkware ideas, which include concepts for beverage containers equipped with squeeze-activated valves and RF chips that communicate with fountain machines.

The company markets its products in convenience stores, amusement parks, zoos and restaurants around the world through custom points of sale and merchandising and displays.

Whirley Industries was founded in 1960 by Bob Sokolski and Hal Conarro, and merged with fellow mug and cup manufacturer DrinkWorks in 2004.
