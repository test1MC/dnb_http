CEDAR Audio spruces up sound recordings. The Academy Award-winning company provides audio restoration and enhancement products used in movies, TV, broadcasting, forensic investigations, libraries and archives, and similar settings. The company's products are used to remaster old recordings, to suppress unwanted broadcast noise, and to analyze and clean recordings used in investigations. CEDAR Audio also offers forensic audio services such as tape authentication, enhancement, and editing, and commercial audio restoration. The technology was developed at the University of Cambridge for the British Library National Sound Archive, and the company still keeps close ties with the school.

Cedar Audio has sales offices throughout the world and also sells through dealers and distributors of audio equipment. Customers include movie studios, law enforcement agencies. Specific customers have been Deutsches Rundfunkarchiv, West Midlands Police, Abbey Road Studios, and "Lost" on ABC .
