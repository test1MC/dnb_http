Furniture may not be as American as apple pie, but American Leather is looking to become a household name. The company makes and sells custom-made leather and upholstered furniture through 650-plus stores. It offers more than 130 different collections of furnishing pieces, such as sofas, sleepers, beds, recliners, chairs, and ottomans. Offering nearly 300 colors and textures, American Leather sells its products through independent retailers nationwide and has showrooms in Dallas, San Francisco, and High Point, North Carolina. The company, founded in 1990, also makes exclusive styles for Crate & Barrel , Design Within Reach , and Room & Board . CEO Bob Duncan has a controlling interest in the company.

Based in Texas, American Leather serves the nation's furniture retailers with its 130-plus different collections. Its products are sold through more than 650 stores across the US.

American Leather sells its products through US dealers and via high-end furniture shops the likes of Room & Board , Crate and Barrel , Macy's , Design Within Reach , and Relax the Back .

American Leather regularly rolls out new products to keep both its retail partners and customers coming back. In 2013 the company unveiled new sofas, chairs, and an ottoman/bench design at High Point Market. The newer collections were designed to offer consumers a classic and comfortable feel.

Unlike other furniture makers, American Leather markets original designs by some of the industry's top international designers, including Rob Jones, Vladimir Kagan, Rick Lee, John Mascheroni, Michael Wolk, Goodman-Charlton, and Barbara and Robert Tiffany. These experts make their mark on small pieces, such as ottomans and individual chairs, to more substantial sectional collections.

The furniture maker has positioned itself in the fabric upholstery market through its acquisition of John Charles Designs, a furniture manufacturer based in California. The 2008 deal included John Charles' fabric collection, furniture designs, and marketing materials. American Leather's purchase has enabled the company to expand its non-leather offerings.

The company was founded in 1990 as LeatherTech by Duncan and Sanjay Chandra, who serves as a board member.
