David Yurman, founder and owner of the namesake company, knows that while diamonds are a girl's best friend, she also can be pretty chummy with gold and silver pieces of well-crafted jewelry. The company makes classic bracelets, chains, earrings, necklaces, rings, jewel-encrusted watches, eyewear, and fragrances; it caters to a high-end clientele and celebrities the likes of Charlize Theron and Brad Pitt. Some 35 David Yurman jewelry boutiques stretch from New York City to Beverly Hills to Las Vegas to Houston. The jewelry retailer also sells its pieces on a website hosted by Neiman Marcus and through several catalogs. Its bridal collection features Yurman's signature cable accents.

New York-based Yurman Design sells jewelry through boutiques across North America and internationally in China and France.

The company has been cultivating its brand for growth in recent years. Through an agreement with Groupe Clarins , the company developed its first fragrance. Although slowed by the recession, David Yurman also has been extending its reach into new markets abroad, such as with its focus to expand throughout the Far East.

Besides operating a catalog business and its own boutiques -- including its newer 2,000-sq.-ft. Madison Avenue flagship store -- Yurman Designs sells its jewelry and watches through high-end retailers the likes of Neiman Marcus and Saks Fifth Avenue .

It boost its visibility and turn what the industry is calling f-commerce into sales, the company enlists the help of models and artists, such as Pete Wentz, Selita Ebanks, and Andie Macdowell, as brand ambassadors. The move aims to sharpening its focus in using Facebook to elevate its luxury brand.

David Yurman, a sculptor by trade, founded his jewelry design company in 1980. He launched his signature cable bracelet design in 1982 and the Cable Watch Collection in 1994.
