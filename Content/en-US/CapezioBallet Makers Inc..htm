Ballet Makers is hoping that it reaps the plentiful rewards of a Dance Dance Revolution . Doing business as Capezio, the company, which has been operating for more than a century, makes dance apparel and footwear, including ballet "pointe" shoes, ice skating costumes, and ballroom dancing apparel. Its products are sold online and by catalog. Capezio also boasts about a dozen corporate store locations worldwide. Through its Ballet Makers-Europe unit, a joint venture with partner Norwich Dancewear, Capezio makes and markets its products in Europe. The company is run by the family of founder Salvatore Capezio, a cobbler who made a name for himself with performers at the neighborhood's Metropolitan Opera House.

Capezio's dance centers provide dance instruction for students of all ages and levels; its international student programs provide instruction around the world. Summer intensive training programs are also offered, as are workshops and master classes with choreographers, master teachers, and directors.

The dance company offers grants for dance programs on the national, state, and local levels to fund dance efforts.
