Chromalox has locked on to making switches and other electric heat and control products as a way to attract customers. The company makes switches and electrical components for heaters, grills, hot plates, defrosters, water heaters, and other products. It also makes thermostats, sensors, commercial and industrial cables, and vaporizer systems as well as heating components, circulation systems, heat transfer systems, boilers, industrial and comfort air heating, heat trace cables, and precision electronic controls. Its equipment has been used aboard the Space Shuttle, and on earth in the biopharmaceutical, food services, petrochemical, power generation, and wastewater treatment industries.

Chromalox has manufacturing, engineering, warehousing and sales locations in France, Mexico, and the US.

The company provides heating products for diverse process industries by custom-designing equipment to specifically fit customers' needs in the chemicals, food processing, petrochemicals, pharmaceutical, power generation, and oil and gas markets. It also has a wide range of custom-designed heating elements, temperature sensors, and controls for OEM applications. OEM customers can request a tailor-made custom design for their individual application, or choose from more than 2 million design drawings on file.

Chromalox has almost 1 million items in stock, and a worldwide sales force of more than 2,000 authorized distributors primarily serving the building and construction, plastics, and rental equipment industries.

Chromalox continues to expand internationally through the opening of direct sales offices in Germany (2012), Thailand (2011), and India (2010), which enable the company to respond to growing power demand (and power generation industries) in these regions.

A part of Emerson Electric for 33 years, Chromalox was sold in 2001 to an investor group led by JPMorgan Partners (now CCMP Capital Advisors ) for $165 million. Chromalox was founded in 1917 by inventor Edwin L. Wiegand. The company's first product was a flat-iron heating element.
