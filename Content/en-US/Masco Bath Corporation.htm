All washed up? That's a good thing in Masco Bath's world. The company makes both the Aqua Glass and American Shower and Bath brands of acrylic, gelcoat fiberglass, and tile showers, bathtubs, shower-tub combinations, whirlpools, and steam showers. It also manufactures special-care tubs and showers for aging Baby Boomers. Masco Bath's products, some designed especially for remodelers, are sold through a nationwide network of distributors, as well as through Lowe's home improvement stores. The company has operations in Michigan, Oregon, New Jersey, and Tennessee. Parent company Masco merged its Aqua Glass (acquired in 1984) and American Shower and Bath (acquired in 1994) subsidiaries to create Masco Bath in 2008.

Based in New Jersey, Masco Bath has facilities in Adamsville, Tennessee, and in Lapeer, Michigan.

The company has operations in Michigan, Oregon, New Jersey, and Tennessee.

Masco Bath sells its products through distributors nationwide and through major retail home centers, such as Lowe's, under the Aqua Glass, ASB, Delta, and Peerless brands. Some of its products are designed specifically for the remodeling industry.

Its primary markets include professional builders, contractors, distributors, plumbers, and home improvement retailers.

To offset losses as a result of the construction industry's downturn, Masco closed down about 100 manufacturing facilities and installation offices in 2008 and 2009, including some Masco Bath locations. It also sold the division's trucking company Tombigbee Transport Corporation to streamline operations.
