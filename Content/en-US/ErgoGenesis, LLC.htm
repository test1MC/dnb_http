ErgoGenesis Workplace Solutions helps to keep office workers productive and comfortable by making products designed to thwart such common sedentary hazards as carpal tunnel syndrome and sore backs. It makes ergonomically-friendly office furniture and accessories include stools, chairs and attachments, keyboard trays, and monitor holders under the BodyBilt and ErgoFusion names. Founded in 1988, ErgoGenesis operates a showroom and manufacturing facility in Navasota, Texas, outside Houston. It sells its products worldwide through sales representatives. President and CEO Mark McMillan started the business in 2002 after buying BodyBilt. Freestone Partners and Patriot Capital acquired ErgoGenesis in 2013.

Based in Texas, ErgoGenesis sells its products globally. The company's product lines are made at its manufacturing facility in Navasota, Texas.

A leader in the ergonomic seating industry, ErgoGenesis sells its products through an extensive network of dealers. It works with many Fortune 500 companies and sets up an initial consultation with each product user to ensure their satisfaction. The company also caters to several areas of the US government, such as the Pentagon, Department of the Navy , and the Internal Revenue Service.

In addition to this, the company also serves government entities, educational institutions, healthcare facilities, emergency call centers and individuals.

To heat up competition with its main rivals Steelcase and Herman Miller , ErgoGenesis regularly rolls out new chairs, such as its 2600 Series ergonomic chair with mesh back. The move represented the company's foray into the mesh back niche and allows ErgoGenesis to sell its chair among Herman Miller's Aeron and Steelcase's Think brands, as well as and other high-end chairs with mesh backs.

In 2015 Energis introduced the BodyBilt Guide, the world's first sit-stand document holder designed to move with height-adjustable monitor.

ErgoGenesis has been awarded green certification for its BodyBilt chairs by Materials Analytical Services.
