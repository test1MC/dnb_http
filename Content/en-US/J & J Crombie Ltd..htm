J & J Crombie makes clothes that will live on to tell the tales of their wearers. J & J Crombie manufactures woolen clothing and sells it in about 15 stores in Europe, Japan, Russia, and the US. The stores also offer other types of apparel, as well as accessories, such as trousers, shoes, handbags, and even umbrellas. Founded in 1805 as a woolen mill in Aberdeen, J & J Crombie boasts a storied heritage that includes making the coats worn by British officers in WWI and producing uniforms for the Confederate army in the American Civil War. J & J Crombie established its first shop in 1990. Chairman Alan Lewis owns the company.

While England-based Crombie sells its clothing around the world via its website, its head office is in West Yorkshire, England and its stores are in Manchester and London. The company also has other retailer stockists in Austria, Belgium, Germany, Ireland, Japan, Netherlands, Russia, Sweden, Switzerland, the UK, and the US. The company's top online sales come from Britain, but its clothes are also popular in the US, Australia, Germany, Poland, Sweden, and France.
