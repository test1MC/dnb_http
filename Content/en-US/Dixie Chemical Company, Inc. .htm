Dixie Chemical's products will make you feel better, eventually. The company produces specialty chemicals and intermediates that end up in food products and pharmaceuticals. It makes more than 60 products at its manufacturing facility in Texas, using processes such as synthesis, distillation, and hydrogenation. Dixie Chemical's products include epoxy agents for weapons and vehicles, trimethyl benzene for coatings and dyes, and ingredients for lubricants, industrial products, and paper products. It also provides contract manufacturing, laboratory support, and project engineering services. Private equity group Glencoe Capital owns a majority interest in Dixie Chemical through holding company GPH Operating Co.

The company's core expertise is processing and producing synthetic and renewable chemical materials through acrylation, epoxidation, and hydrogenation, and reactions with aziridine, butadiene, epichlorodydrin, and maleic anhydride. Its major plant features High Efficiency Hydrogenation, Multi-Purpose Batch Manufacturing, Multi-Step Synthesis Capability, and Thin Film Evaporation.

For security and environmental control purposes, Dixie Chemical operates bulk storage tanks that have vapor recovery systems that are connected to emission control devices.

Dixie Chemical serves more than 300 customers in 25 countries. Its products are used in building materials, plastics, coatings, recreation, active sports, and transportation applications. The company's major markets include alkaline paper sizing, fuel and lube additives, and thermoset materials.

Dixie Chemical works with a number of partners in order to develop its products. Its partners includes Arkema , Ashland , Crey Bioresins, Cytec , Drexel University , Maine Composites Alliance, the University of Delaware , and the US Army .

In 2013 the company signed a $15 million firm-fixed-price contract to produce, store, and distribute various types of high density, synthetic hydrocarbon propellants.

In 2012 the company signed a multi-year technology license agreement with Crey Bioresins, for the rights to make and sell polymer resins derived from renewable resources into a number of markets and applications.

The company was founded in 1946.
