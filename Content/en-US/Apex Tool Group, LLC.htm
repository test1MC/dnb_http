Apex Tool Group is near the top of the heap among global producers of hand, power, and electronic tools. Geared at industrial, commercial, and do-it-yourself (DIY) markets, the group manufactures and markets more than 30 leading tool brands, including Crescent wrenches, Jobox storage boxes, Lufkin measuring tools, and Weller soldering equipment. Directly and through subsidiaries, Apex serves a range of markets, including automotive, aerospace, electronics, energy, industrial, and consumer retail. Apex Tool Group was formed in 2010 as a joint venture between Danaher and Cooper Industries (now Eaton ). It was acquired by investment firm Bain Capital in 2013.

In 2013 Danaher and Eaton sold Apex to global private investment firm Bain Capital for about $1.6 billion. Apex Tool Group was an attractive buy for Bain Capital as it combined two premier tool businesses into a single company that holds strong market positions in developed markets, as well as in key developing markets, such as China and Brazil.

With operations in more than 30 countries, Apex has 25 plants spread throughout Australia, Asia, Europe, and North and South America.

In addition to manufacturing its own products under various brand names, it manufactures several private-label products for large retailers and industrial distributors. Its three main product types are divided into three groups: hand tools, power tools, and professional electronic tools. The company offers more than 40,000 tools; it makes more than 1 million sockets a day.

The hand tools line provides mechanic, trade, and specialty tools, chain, truck boxes, jobsite storage and drill chucks to a broad range of industrial, construction, vehicle repair and DIY customers.

The power tools line supplies electric, pneumatic and air tools, as well as high performance bits, torque measurement, metal cutting, and drilling equipment to industrial, vehicle assembly, aerospace, and utilities/energy companies.

The professional electronic tools line provides precision soldering, cutting, motion control, and air ventilation solutions to electronics assembly industries.

Apex sells its own branded products through a network of industrial distributors, contractor supply houses, automotive tool and equipment distributors, and independent regional and specialized distribution partners. Its private label products are sold through consumer retailers, home centers, hardware stores, and industrial distributors.

The company sells its hand tools through stores and online retailers including ACE , Amazon , and Home Depot .

The Power Tool Division mainly serves the global automotive, aerospace, energy, and general industry markets through 17 manufacturing and sales/service facilities, in the Americas, Europe, and the Asia/Pacific region.
