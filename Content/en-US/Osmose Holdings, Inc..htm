Osmose is a utility services provider with a background in wood preservative technologies. Through its Osmose Utilities Services unit it provides pole maintenance, inspection services, and rehabilitation services and products for electric and gas utilities. In Hawaii, utility construction unit Ikaika's services include overhead and underground utility construction and repair services, civil site work, and general masonry. Osmose also provides corrosion mitigation and engineered repairs for steel structures, structural load analysis, and a variety of turnkey infrastructure upgrading services. To raise cash, in 2014 parent Oaktree Capital sold two units of Osmose to Koppers Holdings for $460 million.

Osmose Utilities Services is an innovator and supplier of remedial wood preservative technologies to the utility industry.

The company offers its products across the US, Canada, and in Australia.

In 2016 the company sold its subsidiary Osmose Communications Services to New Jersey-based SQUAN, a leading infrastructure service provider.

In 2014 Oaktree Capital announced that it was looking to sell Osmose for about $900 million.

As part of this strategy, that year Osmose sold two of its businesses -- wood preservation and railroad services -- to Koppers Holdings for $460 million.

In 2013 Osmose Utilities Services introduced Pole Wrap CB, a preservative bandage designed to protect wood utility poles from decay in the critical area where a pole is in contact with the ground.

In 2012 this unit launched O-Calc Pro structural analysis software for utility poles.

Oaktree Capital Management, the private equity arm of Oaktree Capital, acquired the company in 2012.

Osmose was founded as the Osmose Wood Preserving Co. of America, Inc. in 1934 to promote a wood preservation process and formula invented by Dr. Carl Schmittutz in Germany in the early 1930s.
