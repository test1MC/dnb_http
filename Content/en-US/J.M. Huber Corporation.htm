As great as paint and tires may be, J.M. Huber claims to make them even better. The company makes specialty additives and minerals used to improve the brightness and gloss of paint, the strength and durability of rubber, and the flame-retardant properties of wire and cable. The diverse company also makes oriented strand board (a plywood substitute), and manages timberlands. Huber also makes hydrocolloids (thickeners for gums), among other products, through subsidiary CP Kelco , and innovative specialty products (AdvanTech flooring and ZIP System) roof and wall sheathing through Huber Engineered Woods. Huber is owned by the heirs of the company's founder.

Huber's operating subsidiaries include CP Kelco , Huber Engineered Materials, Huber Engineered Woods LLC, and Huber Resources Corp.

CP Kelco makes specialty hydrocolloids for food, beverage, personal care, household products, paper, oilfield drilling and countless industrial applications.

Huber Engineered Materials develops engineered specialty ingredients that enhance the performance of a wide range of products used in industrial, paper, and consumer-based applications.

Huber Engineered Woods makes innovative specialty product (AdvanTech flooring and ZIP System roof and wall sheathing) for residential and commercial builders.

Huber Resources manages the company's timberlands and those of third-party landowners.

The company has operations in more than 20 countries in the Americas, Europe, Asia/Pacific and Africa.

Huber (through Huber Resources) also manages more than 600,000 acres of timberlands in Maine and the southeastern US.

The company provides products for a range of industries, including Agrochemicals, Beverages, Cosmetics, Flame Retardants and Smoke Suppressants, Food, Household Products, Industrial Applications, Land Management, Mining, Oilfield Drilling, Paints and Coatings, Paper, Personal Care, Pharmaceuticals and Nutraceuticals, Plastics, and Residential and Commercial Construction.

Huber is looking to strategically invest in a few core specialty products businesses and expand geographically. In this regard, in 2016 Huber expanded its CP Kelco facility in Dubai to better serve markets in the Middle East, Africa, and Turkey. That year, Huber Engineered Woods announced that it will reopen its Spring City (Tennessee) oriented strand board plant in 2018 to meet increased demand.

Once a global leader in silica production, to raise cash, in 2016 the company agreed to sell the silica business of Huber Engineered Materials to Evonik for $630 million.

In 2014 it sold its Demica business (consulting, advisory, and technology services) to a consortium led by the JRJ Group, a financial services company.

In response to the growing global demand of pectin, in 2014 CP Kelco expanded its Limeira, Brazil based manufacturing plant. That year it also expanded its CMC (carboxymethyl cellulose, aka cellulose gum) manufacturing capacity at its Äänekoski plant, in Finland by 25%.

In 2014 the company also opened sales and technical support locations in Singapore and Dubai, to serve customers in Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, Vietnam and the rest of southeast Asia, and Australasia, as well as India.

To expand the company's product portfolio and geographic footprint, in 2016 (through its Huber Engineered Materials division) Huber acquired Germany-based Martinswerk (specialty chemicals) from Albemarle .

In 2013 CP Kelco appointed Azelis as its exclusive distributor in China, to represent its entire product portfolio for Food, Beverage, Consumer and Industrial applications.

To support the growing demand for broad range of high quality dental, food, and industrial grade silicas and silicates, in 2013 Huber Engineered Materials increased capacity at its precipitated silica plant in Etowah, Tennessee, adding 14,000 metric tons annually.

In 2012 Huber Engineered Materials completed the expansion of its silica plant in Jhagadia, India, doubling the facility's capacity to 33,000 metric tons per year. This expansion, coupled with the earlier building of dental silica plant in Qingdao, China, allowed the company to better serve the fast-growing Asia-Pacific market.

Huber is owned by the heirs of the company's founder. The company was founded in 1883 by Joseph Maria Huber.
