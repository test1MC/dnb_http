Hancor has a hankering for plastic drainage and storm management projects. The company's products are used in a variety of markets, from sanitary sewer to commercial, residential, construction, and agricultural. The company's products include pipes, fittings, catch basins, detention and retention tanks, drains, inserts, meter pits, and wastewater equipment. Hancor caters to contains environmentally-conscious customers, manufacturing its many of its drainage and water conservation products with high density polyethylene (HDPE), touted as a green alternative to concrete and steel pipe. Since 2005 Hancor has operated as a part of Advanced Drainage Systems , which is the world's largest HDPE corrugated-pipe maker.

The company operates as part of its parent's Domestic segment, which contributed about 86% of sales.

The company is headquartered in Ohio and operates 46 manufacturing plants and 22 distribution centers around the globe.

Financial Performance

Although Advanced Drainage Systems doesn't break out sales for Hancor, it has reported a 1% drop in revenue for its domestic segment, which includes Hancor. The decline was due to a weather-related dip in pipe sales.

Hancor continues to expand its production processes and product lines in promising areas such as highway construction and storm water retention/detention and sanitary leaching chambers. Its line of geo-synthetics (including nonwoven and woven geo-textiles) is well established for soil separation, stabilization, drainage, and erosion sediment control.
