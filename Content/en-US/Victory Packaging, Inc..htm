Family-owned Victory Packaging manufactures and distributes corrugated boxes, bubble wrap, shrink film, tape, equipment, and other packaging materials. The company operates through a network of centers in the US, Canada, and Mexico. Engineers at Victory Packaging provide customized package services for a diverse group of industries and processes, including freight and moving, agricultural, perishable, and just-in-time delivery and inventory management. Customers have included Solo Cup Co. and Graybar Electric , as well as AMERCO and Home Fragrance Holdings. In mid-2015, Victory Packaging was acquired by KapStone Paper and Packaging for $615 million.

Victory Packaging was acquired by KapStone Paper and Packaging for $615 million in 2015.

Victory Packaging operates through more than 65 locations in the US, Canada and Mexico. It operates in California under the Golden State Container brand.

With some 3 million sq. ft. of warehouse space, the company's network of distribution centers gives it the geographical presence to manage complex packaging needs for national companies, as well as to oversee local packaging and distribution for regional clients. It also has about 400 delivery vehicles.

The company also distributes boxes, paper and cushion packaging, and shipping supplies for its customers.

The company serves the military, moving and storage, manufacturing, distribution, food and beverages, and retail industries.

Acknowledging that location is fundamental to materials handling, the company has increased the number of warehouses and distribution facilities both domestically and north and south of the US border. Victory Packaging has more than a handful of locations in Mexico and three in Canada.

To upgrade its technology infrastructure, in 2015 Victory Packaging selected IBS Dynaman warehouse management software to drive efficiencies in the organization's warehouse and distribution centers. That year began to consolidate two Dallas locations into a single new, more efficient facility near CentrePort in Far East Fort Worth. The $12.5 million facility is scheduled to open in 2015.

To strengthen its presence in the foodservice market, in 2014 the company signed a strategic alliance with Chicago-based HAVI Global Solutions, to improve its standing in the corrugated packaging sector.

In 2013 Victory Packaging expanded its operations and relocated its Southaven branch operation to the Southeast Memphis industrial submarket.

Grow its presence in the Dallas, St. Louis, Kansas City and El Paso markets, in 2014 the company acquired Dallas-based Mover's Equipment Services Incorporated. That year it also acquired Network Packaging to expand in the Colorado market and other markets served by that company.

Founded by Victor Samuels in 1974, the company is led by his son, Benjamin.
