Although its name might imply a Japanese soft drink, Nippon Soda (NISSO) actually manufactures basic chemicals, including caustic soda, and chlorine used for industrial purposes. It also produces specialty and performance chemicals such as additives for poultry and pig feed; agrochemicals such as herbicides, fungicides, and insecticides; and pharmaceuticals. NISSO's non-chemical businesses include warehousing, transportation services, construction, and agricultural materials and equipment. The company sells its products primarily in Japan but also in across Asia (where it is expanding), North America, and Europe. It has offices in Brazil, China, France, Germany, Japan, South Korea, Thailand, and the US.
