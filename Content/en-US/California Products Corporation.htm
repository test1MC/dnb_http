ICP covers a lot of surfaces. Through its California Paints division, the company makes paints and coatings for home and office uses, ceiling whites and enamels for hard wall interiors, and finishes and primers. Its Plexipave Sport Surfacing Systems, Rebound Ace Sports, and DecoSystems units make tennis (DecoTurf), track, and other sport surfaces, and subsidiary Fiberlock Technologies manufactures environmental containment products. Its California Paints 2010 and Fres-Coat Velvet, are the top US house paints for the past decade according to a leading consumer magazine. The company is owned by Audax Private Equity.

In 2015 Audax acquired California Products from Delos Capital. Audax's acquisition of the company follows its March 2015 investment in Nicoat, a formulator of specialty packaging and printing coatings. California Products and Nicoat represent the formation of a new platform in the specialty coatings and adhesives markets that Audax plans to grow organically and through acquisitions. Delos will continue as a shareholder in the combined platform.

The company has operations in US and Australia.

California Products is a manufacturer of architectural, sports surfacing, and environmental containment coatings. Its divisions include California Paints, DecoTurf, Fiberlock, Plexipave, Rebound Ace, and Storm System.

Among the products put out by California Products is a certain tint called Fence Green (sometimes Monster Green) that is made in roughly 25 gallon batches every few years to paint Fenway Park's famed Green Monster, the 37-by-231- foot left field wall in the Boston Red Sox 's home field. The company has been manufacturing the paint for the Sox since the 1970s, though the color is proprietary to the ball club and not sold commercially.

The company's Recreational Products Division (Plexipave and DecoTurf) is the world's largest producer of acrylic athletic surfacing materials, primarily for tennis courts, running tracks and basketball courts. California Products' tennis surfacing products are used at major tennis events such as the Australian Open and the US Open.

Plexipave produces more than 50,000 gallons of coatings daily and can accommodate 10 truckloads departing simultaneously.

Rebound Ace Sports is a pioneer of innovative, multi-layered cushion sports surfaces which reduce leg, ligament, and lower back strains and injuries.

Storm System's products provide a "like-new" appearance to exterior surfaces.

California Products sells its products its products throughout North America and Asia and in Australia and New Zealand via dealers and distributors.

The company is seeking out new market opportunities to advance its brands and products, both organically and through acquisitions.

In 2015 California Products unit Fiberlock Technologies acquired Dry-Treat, an Australian based world technology leader in the protection and maintenance of porous building materials. Dry-Treat increases its technological and global footprint and enables Fiberlock Technologies to access additional peripheral markets.

In 2014, the company acquired the Muralo Paint Company, founded in 1894 and headquartered in Bayonne, New Jersey manufactures paints and coatings that are sold through paint retailers in over 20 states. The acquisition includes certain Muralo and Graham brands and product lines including Spackle, Adhesium and other associated lines. The acquisition of the Muralo Paint brands offers California Paints many opportunities for expanding its brands and product lines while reinforcing its long-term commitment to its employees and customers.

Expanding its products and geographic footprint, in 2013 California Products acquired Brisbane-based Rebound Ace Sports, adding a third brand to its California Sports Surfaces segment.

In 2013 California Products was acquired by New York City-based Delos Capital, LLC, a private equity firm.

California Products added to its operations with the 2009 acquisition of Progress Paints. That company manufactures coatings under the brand names Gray Seal, Fixall, and Duralux Marine.

DecoTurf products were used to provide tennis court surfaces in the 2004 Athens Olympics and the 2008 Beijing Olympics.

Employees are majority shareholders in the company, whose name dates back to its founding as a subsidiary of a West Coast stucco manufacturer.

The company was founded in 1926. Finding that the harsh New England winters were not at all conducive to the well-being of stucco, the firm reformulated as a maker of latex paint and was renamed California Products Corporation in the 1950s.
