When the pipes are about to blow, Detector Electronics sounds the alarm. The company, also called Det-Tronics, makes electrical detection products used to monitor industrial facilities for fire and gas leaks. Using UV, MIR, and fiber optics technology, sensors are designed to detect hazardous heat variations, radiation, and combustible and toxic gases. Its products are used to protect applications including offshore oil platforms, gas pipelines, subway systems, and aerospace and defense projects. Det-Tronics is part of UTC Climate Controls & Security , a division of United Technologies .

Headquartered in Minneapolis, the company has nearly 20 sales offices in North America, South America, Europe, the Middle East, Scandinavia, and Asia.

The company's Det-Tronics University offers a complete flame and gas detection system curriculum for customers, system integrators, sales reps, and other end-users of Det-Tronics' products.

The company serves the oil and gas, refining, automotive, aerospace, munitions, and chemical industries worldwide. It sells its products directly to end customers and through some 75 representatives, distributors, and dealers.

The UTC Climate Controls & Security segment contributed about 30% of its parent company's revenue in 2012. This segment's revenue decreased by 9% in 2012 as the company was hurt by no organic sales growth. The segment's performance was also affected by divestitures completed during the year.
