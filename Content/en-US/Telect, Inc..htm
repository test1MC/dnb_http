Telect has the hook-ups that enable hang-ups. The company develops and manufactures network infrastructure equipment, connecting consumers and businesses through unique power, fiber, rack, and copper equipment and services and other network connectivity and power distribution hardware for telecommunications service providers. Local telephone, long-distance, and wireless carriers use Telect's mainframe termination units, patch bays, fuse bays, and circuit panels. Telect also sells copper wire and fiber-optic cables. The company works directly, through resellers, and through agreements with other telecom equipment makers, primarily in the US.

The company has distribution operations in Argentina, Australia, Canada, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, France, Greece, India, Ireland, Mexico, Netherlands, New Zealand, Panama, Peru, Puerto Rico/Caribbean, Russia, Spain, UAE, UK, US, Venezuela, and Vietnam. It manufactures its products in Plano, Texas; Spokane, Washington; and Guadalajara, Mexico.

Telect's US distribution partners include Anixter , Graybar Electric , KGP Logistics , Power & Telephone Supply , TVC Communications , and Walker & Associates. Its distribution partners in Canada include Anixter Canada, Priority Electronics, and TVC Communications.

The company is upgrading its facilities to increase quality and attract more customers. In 2013 Telect's manufacturing facility near Guadalajara, Mexico was awarded ISO 14001:2004 certification, an internationally recognized badge of excellence that recognizes the sites commitment to environmental stewardship. In 2011 Telect opened an advanced development, customer services, and corporate headquarters facility in Liberty Lake, Washington.
