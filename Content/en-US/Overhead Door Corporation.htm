This company's business "hinges" on what you need opened. Overhead Door manufactures and sells wood, steel, and polyurethane garage doors, electric door operators, security grilles, and rolling doors for residential, commercial, and industrial applications. It operates through several divisions, including Access Systems Division (ASD), its largest, sells garage doors and openers under the Overhead Door and Wayne-Dalton brands, and The Genie Company, which sells remote-control garage doors. Horton Automatics makes doors for commercial and industrial use; TODCO sells doors for trucks. Overhead Door, which also has Canadian business unit, is a subsidiary of Japan-based construction products maker Sanwa Shutter .

Overhead Door sells its products mainly in North America through a network of more than 450 authorized distributors (for its Ribbon brand of products). Its primary division, ASD, operates through a network of around 20 manufacturing facilities, almost 80 regional sales centers, and more than 5,000 distributors and dealers that cater to national builders, retailers, architects, and general contractors and homeowners.

Overhead Door's five operating divisions include ASD, The Genie Company, Horton Automatics, TODCO, and Creative Door Services. ASD features the Overhead Door and Wayne Dalton brands; The Genie Company, makes remote-controlled garage door opening systems; Horton Automatics is a manufacturer of automatic entrance systems; TODCO is the largest producer of truck doors for the transportation industry; and Creative Door Services is Western Canada's leading provider of door products and services.

The company sells its products through a network of more than 450 Overhead Door Red Ribbon Distributors who provide sales, installation, and service to customers.

In 2014 Overhead Door introduced its Model 674 Security Grille, a new perforated grille constructed from lightweight aluminum panels (providing security while maximizing visibility, air flow and light infiltration) as an attractive security solution for interior spaces in retail facilities, airports, stadiums, multi-purpose buildings, and malls.

New products in 2013 included the Stormtite AP Model 627 an improved thermal efficiency and security door system; and (to meet demand from homeowners who want attractive garage doors that complement their homes' architecture) a V5 stamp that mimics the look and feel of wainscoting, a raised panel Ranch stamp on Overhead Door's Thermacore line, and decorative window inserts on its Traditional Steel Collection.

The company has a history of growing through acquisitions. In 2012 Overhead Door acquired Door Control, a supplier of automatic doors based in the US. In late 2011, it expanded its North American footprint through the $45 million purchase of Creative Door Services, a Western Canada-based provider of door products and related services to the residential, commercial and industrial sectors. It also picked up Automatic Door Enterprises that year.

Overhead Door was founded by C. G. Johnson, who invented the upward-lifting garage door in 1921 and the electric garage door opener in 1926.
