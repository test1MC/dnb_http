Showa Denko Carbon does big business on a small scale. The company manufactures primarily large-diameter graphite electrodes, rods essential to electric arc furnaces, which melt and refine scrap steel. It also produces synthetic granular and powdered graphite materials, used in automotive friction products and conductive coatings, as well as fine carbon products, used in lithium batteries and fuel cells. Showa Denko Carbon markets its lineup to steel and automotive industries worldwide. Dwindling orders from steelmakers forced the company to furlough more than 30% of its workforce in mid-2009. The company is the US subsidiary of Tokyo-based chemical and aluminum products manufacturer Showa Denko K.K.
