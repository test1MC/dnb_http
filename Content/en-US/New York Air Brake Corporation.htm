Everyone knows it's hard to stop a train, but that doesn't discourage New York Air Brake. The company (NYAB) makes brakes and related train-control equipment; it also provides repair services and replacement parts and sells its freight and locomotive air brake control systems to the rail transportation industry. Its Premtec division builds end-of-car and specialty hose assemblies as well as associated air hose products. In addition, its Train Dynamic Systems unit has expertise in train dynamics and training simulators. NYAB is a unit of Germany's Knorr-Bremse , a major manufacturer of braking systems for both trucks and trains.

The company operates four manufacturing plants in Watertown, New York; West Chicago, Illinois; China Grove, North Carolina; and Ontario, Canada. It also operates engineering centers in Watertown, New York; and Irving, Texas; and a service center in Kansas City, Missouri.

NYAB has delivered more than 2,500 systems to eight customers worldwide. Some of its customers include Etihad Rail, an operator of the United Arab Emirates (UAE) national rail network, Union Pacific Railroad , and Norfolk Southern Railway Corp .

NYAB operates as the North American unit of Knorr-Bremse. The Americas region contributed about 23% of its parent's total revenue in 2013, and the Rail Vehicle Systems division accounted for 52% of its total sales.

NYAB grows by developing new products in emerging markets. It is currently designing integrated train control systems that help make Automated Train Control (ATC) a viable reality.

As a part of a realignment strategy, in 2013 the company shut down operations of Knorr Brake Limited, which manufactured locomotive braking systems, among other things. The initiative was made to improve total cost structure and align its manufacturing facilities with a more optimal focus.

Once part of General Signal, New York Air Brake was acquired by Knorr-Bremse in 1991.
