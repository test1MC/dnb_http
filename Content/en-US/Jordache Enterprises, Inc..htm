It was the 1980s: the late Robin Williams wore rainbow suspenders, a youg Tom Hanks donned a dress, and if you were hot, you had The Jordache Look. Fast-forward to today, Jordache Enterprises offers jeans, apparel, and accessories primarily for 20-somethings, mostly through decidedly non-designer retailers such as Wal-Mart . It also licenses the Jordache name for eyewear and luggage. Jordache distributes apparel under owned brands Jordache, KIKIT, Dittos, Gasoline, LoveLola, GAS, and XOXO. The company's premium Jordache Vintage line is sold at specialty stores.

Based in New York, Jordache makes and distributes its denim, apparel, and accessories nationwide and in global markets.

As part of its business, Jordache makes denim apparel for the Gap , Tommy Hilfiger , Levi's , and Abercrombie & Fitch , among others, as a private labeler. Its other holdings include a majority interest in a private jet charter service, Israeli airline Arkia Airlines, award-winning HALUTZA olive oil company, and residential and commercial real estate in both the US and Israel.

Each year Jordache produces some 35 million apparel pieces -- including jeans, jackets, and private-label clothing -- through its privately owned plants in Jordan, Madagascar, Kenya, Mexico, Mauritius, Morocco, and other locations. Primary facilities include American Jordanian Industrial Company (AJICO), Kenya Trading (EPZ) Ltd., Mklen International, and Blue Star.

It also operates a private label division that makes garments for other well-known brands. The unit has a subsidiary in Hong Kong that handles product development, technical support, raw materials purchasing, and finance support.

Primary customers among its private label unit include Zara , Pull & Bear, Levi's , Bershka, and Next. Across several price points, Jordache has served a variety of companies the likes of Abercrombie & Fitch , American Eagle , Express , Gap , J.C. Penney , Sears , Old Navy , and Tommy Hilfiger .

In recent years, the company has enlisted the help of high-profile supermodels to give a boost to its brand and get a foothold in department stores like Bloomingdale's and Macy's . Such spokespeople include supermodel Elizabeth Hurley and Heidi Klum.

Owning its own apparel manufacturing plants has enabled Jordache to extend the reach of its business to private labeling for others. The move, too, allows Jordache to diversify its revenue streams.

The company was founded in 1979 by brothers and Israeli immigrants Joe, Ralph, and Avi Nakash. (A New York City store owned by the brothers was burned and looted in 1977; insurance money funded the launch of Jordache).
