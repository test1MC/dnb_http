Concurrent Technologies designs, manufactures, and sells computer components, such as communication adaptors, processor boards, and switch fabric boards. Its products are based around Intel microprocessors and work with a variety of CPU boards. Concurrent Technologies also offers software, firmware, and custom design services for its customers, which are drawn from the aerospace, medical, military, and telecommunications sectors, among others. The company generates 44% of it sales in the UK with about 30% of sales from North America. Concurrent Technologies Inc. is the company's US-based subsidiary (not to be confused with Concurrent Technologies Corporation ).

Concurrent Technologies makes its products in its own manufacturing facility.

The biggest market for Concurrent Technologies is its home country, the UK, which accounts for 44% of sales. North America accounts for 30% with Europe and the rest of the world, accounting for 12% and 14%, respectively.

The company has sales offices in UK, US, and China. It also has a design center in Bangalore, India.

Concurrent Technologies sells through distributors and manufacturers' representatives. Its key market sectors are defense, telecommunications, aerospace, transportation, scientific, and industrial. One customer accounts for about 30% of revenue.

Strong sales increases in the UK and US boosted Concurrent Technologies' revenue by a third in 2015 from 2014. UK revenue increased 47% (£2.4 million) and US revenue rose 60% (about £2 million). The company had particular gains products for the telecommunications and scientific markets. The revenue gain sent net income 67% higher in 2015. Cash from operating activities rose 3% in 2015 from the year before.

Concurrent Technologies continues to invest in research and development of new products for its Intel-based offerings. It increased R&D spending to £3 million in 2015 from £2.2 million in 2014. The company released seven embedded computers in 2015, all based on Intel's XEON processors. Concurrent Technologies AMC architecture has found a welcoming market in telecommunications applications.

The company in 2015 sent out its first graphics processing units (GPUs), which are used for video transcoding, wireless testing, image analysis and recognition, and other applications.
