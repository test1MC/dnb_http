Keystone Consolidated Industries can wire your world. The company, mainly through its Keystone Steel & Wire division (86% of the company's 2011 revenues), makes fabricated wire products, including fencing, barbed wire, welded wire, and woven wire mesh for the agricultural, construction, and do-it-yourself markets. Many of its products are sold under the Red Brand label. A vertically integrated company with its own steel mininmill, Keystone (which is a subsidiary of Contran ), also makes industrial wire -- used to make such items as barbecue grills, coat hangers, and nails -- and carbon steel rod.

Keystone operates two other divisions, Engineered Wire Products (EWP), and Keystone-Calumet. Ohio-based EWP manufactures wire mesh in both roll and sheet form that is used as reinforcement in concrete construction products. Illinois-based Calumet makes merchant and special bar quality steel products and special sections in carbon and alloy grades for use in a wide variety of applications.

The company's manufacturing operations include an electric arc furnace mini-mill, a rod mill, wire mill, industrial wire mill, and three steel product fabrication facilities. Its uses ferrous scrap as the primary raw material in its operations, and its steel mill is close to several sources of automobile, industrial, and railroad ferrous scrap.

Keystone reported that its revenues increased by 25% in 2011 due to increase revenues from it Keystone Steel & Wire, EWP, and Calumet segments. The company gets 99% of its revenues from the US.

Net income jumped by 151% in 2011 due to higher revenues and a decrease in interest expense. The decrease in the overall weighted average interest rate in 2011 was primarily due to a drop in the rate at which the company's primary credit facility bore interest.

In 2010 the company completed a four-year, $9 million project to rebuild the rod mill reheat furnace at its major subsidiary, Keystone Steel & Wire. It also spent $5 million for infrastructure projects at Keystone Steel & Wire and $4 million for equipment upgrades at Keystone Steel & Wire and Keystone-Calumet.

The company was originally founded in 1899 under the name Keystone Steel & Wire Company.
