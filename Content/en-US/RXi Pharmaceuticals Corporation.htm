RXi Pharmaceuticals doesn't let bad genes stand between you and your health. The company specializes in developing drugs based on RNA interference (RNAi), which cuts off a gene's ability to produce a disease. The company believes its approach can lead to a whole new class of drugs for previously untreatable conditions. In 2011 RXi acquired late-stage drug developer Apthera and its leading oncology candiate NeuVax. Following that move, RXi changed its name to Galena Biopharma . However, with two separate drug candidates, the two businesses never fully integrated and split apart in 2012. RXi Pharmaceuticals kept its RNAi-drug development business while Galena Biopharma took over NeuVax.
