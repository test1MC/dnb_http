HallStar hopes to have many star products in its hall of fame. The company operates through subsidiaries, CPH Solutions and RTD HallStar, which make polymer and personal care product additives used to improve the quality of rubber, plastics, adhesives, coatings, cosmetics, skin care items, and other industrial products. Hallstar has two US manufacturing plants and participates in a joint venture with Scandiflex to market personal care products in Brazil. The company's brands include Paraplex, Plasthall, Hallcote, Quikote, Maglite, and Marinco. HallStar is owned by members of its executive staff. In 2015 it acquired Fortinbrás, a specialty chemical manufacturer based in São Paulo.

HallStar has its corporate headquarters in Illinois, and has two US manufacturing facilities. It distributes products to customers in North America, South America, Africa, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

The company has expertise in Photostability Science, Functional Naturals, Natural Olive Chemistry, Liquid Crystal Emulsifiers, Renewable Plasticizers/Esters, Permanent/Reactive Plasticizers, Phthalate Replacements, Custom Ester Design, and Regulatory Understanding and Capability.

HallStar sells its products through distributors, including IMCD Australia, Quetzal Quimica, Dinaco, Ingredients Plus PTY LTD Australia, Specialty Business Corporation, Lavollee S A, and West Coast Polychem Pvt. Ltd.

The company grows through product innovations and acquisitions. It is looking to continue to expand its global presence, increase exports to Asia Pacific and Europe, Middle East and Africa and establish facilities on nearly every continent. The company has an extensive portfolio of patented technology and remains an independent, agile company with an ownership-oriented culture and loyal customer relationships.

In 2015 the company acquired Fortinbrás, a specialty chemical manufacturer based in Jaguariúna, São Paulo, Brazil. The acquisition of increases Hallstar's presence in the fast-growing emerging market of Brazil and in greater Latin America.

Expanding its natural functional ingredients portfolio, in 2014 HallStar acquired the Florasolvs natural esters product line of specialty ingredients from FloraTech, a provider of botanically-derived ingredients. The Florasolvs series brand bolsters the company's growing line of products for the naturals segment in the personal care ingredient industry, and strengthens its position to serve the growing market for personal care products featuring renewable natural ingredients.

The company expanded in 2012 by acquiring the TP brand of specialty plasticizers from Dow Chemical for an undisclosed amount. The buy gives HallStar a broader range of ester technology, which is used in rubber modification.

In 2012 HallStar launched Polycrylene S1, a new singlet-quenching photostabilizer that helps formulators meet FDA standards for broad-spectrum claims and enhances the water resistance of sun care and daily wear products. Polycrylene S1 allows formulators to combine active ingredients once considered too photo-unstable to be effective. Polycrylene S1 replaces the triplet-quenching portion of Polycrylene with a singlet-state quencher. The photostabilizer improves sunscreens and daily wear products, providing better UV protection.

In 2011 HallStar acquired BioChemica International, a supplier of exotic butters and oils for use in personal care products. The acquisition is a move into the natural and organic personal care products market. The industry recorded $3.8 billion in sales in 2009, with a 14% annual growth rate from 2003 to 2009. BioChemica will retain its brand and operate as a HallStar business unit.

That same year, HallStar acquired B&T S.r.l., a developer of natural ingredients based on olive chemistry for use in personal care products. B&T has $3.8 billion in annual sales, and a 14% growth rate. The acquisition bolsters HallStar's position in the market for personal care products using renewable natural ingredients.

HallStar was known as CPH Holding or the C. P. Hall Company until 2007.
