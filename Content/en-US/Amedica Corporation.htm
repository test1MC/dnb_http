You can spin a disc, or burn a disc, but when you blow a disc (in your back) the fun stops, and Amedica steps in. It develops, manufactures, and commercializes joint and spine implants made of silicon nitride ceramic, a more durable, resistant, and patient-compatible alternative to traditional implant materials. Amedica's lead product candidates are its Valeo spinal implants which are intended to restore and maintain vertebrae alignment in the neck and lower back. Valeo spinal spacer implants have received FDA and EU approval for use as vertebra replacements. Amedica's other candidates include ceramic hip and knee implants with material that mimic the porous structure of natural bone. It went public in 2014.

In February 2014 Amedica's IPO raised a modest $20 million (below its earlier projection of $35 million) and the company will use the proceeds for sales and marketing and further fund R&D for its other product candidates.

This is Amedica's second attempt at an IPO. Amedica prepared to launch its initial public offering in 2007, but citing a weak IPO market, withdrew the registration and worked with the funding it already had.
