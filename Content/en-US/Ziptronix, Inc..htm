Need to put some zip in your electronics? Try Ziptronix. The company creates and licenses semiconductor device architectures, including three-dimensional (3D) chip designs. Ziptronix's ZiBond and Direct Bond Interconnect technologies allows silicon to be bonded in a 3D fashion to maximize the density of signal paths. It also offers specialized semiconductor substrates for microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) and radio-frequency (RF) devices. In 2000, the company was spun out from North Carolina's Research Triangle Institute , an investor in the company. Other investors include IDEA Fund Partners and Intersouth Partners. Sumitomo distributes Ziptronix's products in Japan.
