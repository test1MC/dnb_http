IPC Systems makes and services "turret" communications systems, also called dealerboards, that combine PBX, data switching, computer telephony, voice recording, and multimedia capabilities. Its products are used by financial institutions for voice and data transmission and routing in their trading environments. These products are designed to integrate with products from technology vendors such as Avaya and Cisco . With more than 115,000 dealerboards worldwide, IPC's financial extranet includes some 4,000 locations in more than 700 cities. Its offerings also include enhanced voice services, business continuity, and support. IPC Systems is owned by Silver Lake Partners .

The company markets its products directly and through resellers and systems integrators worldwide. Its customers have included Goldman Sachs , JPMorgan Chase , and the New York Stock Exchange .

IPC is focused on boosting its operations in international markets. In 2012 it opened new offices in South Korea (Seoul) and Switzerland (Geneva). It also extended its network to emerging Asian markets such as China, India, Malaysia, and Thailand.

Traditionally the company generated a small portion of its revenues marketing to the public safety market through its command systems division. That unit, which does business as Positron Public Safety Systems, provided radio dispatch consoles, mapping and vehicle location applications, and 911 call handling systems. In 2008 IPC sold those operations to West Corporation for $167 million.

IPC filed to go public in 2008, but it withdrew its registration later that year.
