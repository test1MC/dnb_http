Nashua Corporation has a storied history of selling coated papers and labels. Founded in 1849 as a playing cards supplier to miners during the California gold rush, Nashua's Coated Products segment coats, converts, prints, and sells thermal coated papers and films for use in package identification systems and gaming and airline ticketing systems. It also makes paper rolls, fanfolded paper, and printer ribbons for ATMs, printers, and check statement processors. Its Label Products segment makes pressure-sensitive labels for merchants, radio frequency identification (RFID) labels, and retail shelf tags. Nashua is a subsidiary of Cenveo , a leading maker of envelopes, labels, and specialty packaging.
