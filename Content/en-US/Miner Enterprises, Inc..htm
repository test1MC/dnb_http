Miner Enterprises helps to keep the bump and grind to a minimum for some major enterprises. The rail equipment company's draft gears provide cushioning behind railcar couplers -- the connectors that link railcars and make a train a train. Miner Enterprises also makes side bearings, which help control the ride and curving characteristics of railcars and brake beams. Other products include discharge gates for covered comestibles, chemicals, and coal hatch covers ballast hopper railcars and branded TecsPak elastomer springs, as well as replacement parts.

Miner sells its products in more than 55 countries.

Miner operates four business units: Miner Enterprises, Powerbrace, Autoquip, and Miner Elastomer Products. Powerbrace is the leading manufacturer of heavy-duty door securement for cargo that is shipped by trailer or container. The company's operations include systems-driven manufacturing process that includes precision automated cutting and boring machines.

Miner Elastomer Products supplies the global industrial marketplace with quality manufactured TecsPak thermoplastic elastomer energy management systems, while Autoquip makes material handling equipment. Miner's research and development department offers complete test facilities, including a physical test laboratory and railcar impact test track.

Customers include freight car builders and private-car and leasing companies. With a US market not as strong as it could be, Miner is finding it profitable to export component sales. The company is supplying Vale , a Brazilian mining corporation, with its AggreGate ballast systems for use on Brazilian railcars.

Miner grows by improving its performance through product development and by finding ways to reduce overall costs of its railcar ownership. In 2013 the company entered the emerging portable device market when it launched ts new mobile Field Guide, developed for smartphones, tablets, laptops and desktop computers.

The company got its start originally as W.H. Miner in 1894. The company manufactured spring draft gears, automatic couplers, and braking systems. It changed its name to Miner Enterprises in 1968.
