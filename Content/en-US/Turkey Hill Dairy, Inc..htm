Hold the gravy boat, Turkey Hill Dairy boasts a buffet full of dairy products rather than the ubiquitous Thanksgiving fare. The company makes ice cream, frozen yogurt, sherbet, and frozen ice-cream novelties, as well as beverages such as refrigerated iced teas and fruit drinks. It markets about 100 varieties of ice cream and 30 different iced teas and beverages. Most of Turkey Hill's products are distributed to retailers nationwide. Its fluid milk is distributed among its Lancaster County, Pennsylvania, home base. Turkey Hill, which makes Phillies Graham Slam ice cream under license, produces light ice cream under the Dynamic Duo label. The company is owned by grocery retailing giant Kroger .

Based in Pennsylvania, Turkey Hill Dairy sells its iced teas in 49 US states, as well as across a dozen countries on three continents. The company sells its revenue-generating ice creams throughout 30 US states along the East Coast from Maine to Florida and as far west as Oregon, Washington, and Alaska.

Turkey Hill Dairy, which churns out some 26 million gallons of ice cream each year, produces the nation's fourth largest-selling premium ice cream brand. It also makes one of the top refrigerated iced tea brands, producing 52 million gallons of iced tea annually. The company generates half of its sales from ice cream, with iced tea accounting for 40% of sales and the 10% balance coming from milk products.

While 95% of its ice cream is produced at its home base of Lancaster County, the dairy operation boasts two additional production plants outside Pennsylvania located in California and Utah. Some 85% of Turkey Hill Dairy's iced teas are made in Lancaster County, as well, supplemented by a handful of production facilities located in California, Kansas, Oregon, Tennessee, and Virginia.

Turkey Hill Dairy distributes its products nationwide, distributing its milk primarily across Lancaster County. The company also makes and markets products, such as its Phillies Graham Slam variety of ice cream, under license.

The company also markets its products through other Kroger subsidiaries, including Turkey Hill Minit Markets.

The dairy company generates approximately $300 million in sales each year. A quarter of its dairy sales come from companies, such as Turkey Hill Minit Markets, owned by parent Kroger . During the past few years, Turkey Hill Dairy has seen its sales expand, thanks to an annual growth rate of between 5% and 10%.

The company, which began as a milk producer and expanded into ice cream in the 1950s, regularly launches new flavors to maintain a variety of about 100 ice creams. To this end, Turkey Hill Dairy adds limited-edition ice cream flavors like Whoopie Pie and Salty Caramel to its typical lineup of Blizburgh Crunch and Dutch Chocolate.

To keep up with demand, Turkey Hill Dairy is expanding its warehouse in Manor Township by 90,000 sq. ft. The $30-million expansion is scheduled to be completed during 2014.

Catering to consumers who are looking for healthier options, Turkey Hill Dairy has rolled out new products in recent years. The manufacturer expanded its existing frozen yogurt line by introducing an Omega-3-fortified, pomegranate, blueberry, chocolate-chunk flavored yogurt.
