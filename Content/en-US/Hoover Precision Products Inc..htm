Hoover Precision Products is having a ball. The company manufactures a variety of ball bearings and other ball products designed for a range applications. Its products, most of which are made from metals, plastics, and ceramics, are used in automobile parts, airplane navigation components, wind turbines, pumps and engines, and other products made by industrial, automotive, and aerospace manufacturers. Hoover also makes molded plastics products (petri dishes and test tubes) for medical customers. The company operates in the Asia/Pacific region, Europe, Mexico, and the US; its customers have included Wesco Aircraft , NTN Driveshaft, and Honda Motor Company . Hoover Precision is a subsidiary of Tsubaki Nakashima .

Hoover Precision has manufacturing plants in the US (Georgia, Michigan), and in China, Poland, and Japan; sales offices are in Germany, Switzerland, and the US.

Hoover Precision Products was founded as Hoover Steel Ball Company by Leander J. Hoover in 1913. It became a subsidiary of Tsubaki Nakashima in 1990.
