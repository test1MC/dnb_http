EPIC Technologies provides electronic manufacturing services to OEMs in the automotive, energy, industrial, and medical industries. It primarily makes complex printed circuit board assemblies, and its end-to-end custom services include product engineering, fulfillment, prototype assembly, test development, logistics, repair, and final assembly and integration. Volkswagen de Mexico is among its customers. EPIC maintains manufacturing, distribution, and quality testing facilities across the US and in Mexico. In 2013 Natel Engineering acquired the company.

Based in Ohio, EPIC Technologies is a global company that serves Asia, Europe, and North America. Its manufacturing facilities are located in Norwalk, Ohio; Mason, Ohio; Juarez, Mexico; Timisoara, Romania; Englewood, Colorado; and Johnson City, Tennessee. It also has a distribution center in El Paso, Texas.

EPIC Technologies serves several sectors, such as the medical, communications, industrial equipment, automotive, and energy markets.

As part of its business, EPIC Technologies offers its customers turnkey services, including DFx, board layout support, test development, prototype testing, printed circuit board assembly, and box build. Its custom solutions include product engineering, supply base management, specialized low volume support, PCB assembly, final assembly and integration, test development, fulfillment, and repair, logistics, and end-of-life support.

EPIC Technologies works to maintain its manufacturing facilities. In 2013 the company enhanced the lean manufacturing capabilities at its plant in Juarez, Mexico, by adding a new highly automated SMT line.
