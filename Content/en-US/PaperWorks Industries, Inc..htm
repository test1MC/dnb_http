PaperWorks Industries is knee deep in paper, and that's the way it likes it. With mills in Pennsylvania and Indiana, it is a North American producer of coated recycled paperboard (CRB), manufacturing about 400,000 tons of it annually. Its MasterWorks product line includes everything from freezer-enhanced CRB to uncoated eco-friendly paperboard. End-use markets for CRB include beverages, dry and frozen foods, household goods, and oral care products. In addition to its CRB offerings, PaperWorks produces specialized folding cartons. PaperWorks Industries is controlled by investment firm Sun Capital Partners .

PaperWorks has almost 20 facilities (including seven manufacturing plants) throughout the US, with a major presence in the east and northeast.

The company operates through the two segments of Paperboard Group and Packaging Group. The Paperboard Group is comprised of its original two mills and the five converting facilities inherited from the merger with Manchester Industries, while the Packaging Group operates through 10 facilities from the former SPG and Rosmar Packaging operations.

The Paperboard Group manufactures 400,000 tons of 100% recycled paperboard a year for a variety of end uses, and further specializes in customer-specific cutting and distribution of all paper types.

The company serves a range of markets, including serves baby care, beverages, electronics, fabric care, and food and frozen food markets.

PaperWorks had been steadily growing through acquisitions In 2015 the company completed the acquisition of Canada-based CanAmPac. The acquisition included coated recycled board producer Strathcona Paper, folding carton manufacturer Boehmer Box, and LYFT Visual graphic services. The acquisition expanded PaperWorks' supply source for North American paperboard and folding carton customers in multiple markets, including the food, beverage, household chemicals, and personal care sectors.

PaperWorks was founded in 2008.
