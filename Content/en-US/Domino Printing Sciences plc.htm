Domino Printing Sciences (DPS) designs, manufactures, and distributes commercial inkjet and laser printers designed for industrial coding, marking, and printing. The company's equipment and specialized inks are used for such diverse applications as applying dates to beverage cans, labeling cables, and marking food items. Established in 1978, DPS and its subsidiaries (25 subsidiary offices) serve global producers of foods, pharmaceuticals, building products, automobiles, and components. It has partnerships with 200-plus distributors, doing business in more than 120 countries. DPS's customers have included Imperial Brands and Coca-Cola . The business gets more than half of its sales outside Europe. Japan's Brother Industries acquired DPS in 2015 for $1.6 billion.

UK-based Domino has has manufacturing facilities in China, Germany, India, Sweden, the UK, and the US.

Domino Group has customers engaged in the beverage, food, cosmetics, pharmaceutical, and tobacco industries. Its products reach 120 countries sold through its 25 international subsidiary offices and 200 distributors.
