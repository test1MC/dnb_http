Airspan Networks covers a lot of ground, mostly through the air. The company markets broadband wireless base stations and equipment based on advanced radio technology that provides high-speed services in data, voice, and multimedia. Airspan's systems and subscriber terminals provide fixed and mobile broadband wireless access to phone companies, ISPs, utility companies, public-safety agencies, municipalities, transportation providers, educational and other campuses, and other network operators. Airspan sells directly and through OEMs, resellers, and systems integrators to about 500 customers in more than 100 countries.

The company is headquartered in Florida and operates in the UAE, Finland, Israel, Poland, Russia, Australia, Indonesia, Japan, Philippines and Sri lanka.

The company also offers products that extend network access to rural and other underserved areas. Among those products are Air4Gs, a 4G wireless micro base station that weighs less than 25 pounds and requires less than 90 watts of power, and MicroMAX, a micro cell base station for roof-top deployments. Several other Airspan products use hybrid platforms, such as Air4G, its most powerful LTE-Advanced and WiMAX base station. The company also offers end user Wi-Fi access point and other devices, core network products such as gateways and Internet telephony-enabling devices, backhaul products such as its AirSynergy small base station, broadband wireless access antennas, and ruggedized Wi-Fi products.
