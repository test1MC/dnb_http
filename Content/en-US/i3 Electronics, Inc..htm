The plant's sign now says "i3" instead of "IBM," and a lot fewer people work there compared to decades ago, but otherwise it's business as usual -- making printed circuit boards and semiconductor packages for a variety of electronic products. i3 Electronics (formerly Endicott Interconnect Technologies) is a former IBM manufacturing plant in upstate New York. The company has since opened plants in Pennsylvania and China. In addition to packaging chips and building boards, i3 Electronics offers design, prototyping, system integration and testing, and engineering consulting.

i3 Electronics has manufacturing facilities in Endicott, NY; Saxonburg, PA; and in Shenzhen, China. It has sales offices in France, Germany, Hong Kong, the Netherlands, Scotland, the UK, and the US.

Customers come from a wide range of industries, including aerospace and defense, computing, medical, radiation detection, and advanced test equipment. Its clients include IBM (of course), Cisco , Cadence , GE Medical, Harris , St. Jude Medical , Boeing , Northrop Grumman , Raytheon , NASA , and the US Defense Department .
