For all those double-clutchers on the interstates and highways of America, Valeo is there to keep traffic moving. Valeo Inc. is the North American business segment of automotive parts concern Valeo , which manufactures and distributes an extensive line of clutches and transmission components, security systems, electrical devices, wiper systems, engine-cooling assemblies, and climate-controllers. Joint ventures affiliated with Valeo Inc. include automotive lighting producer Valeo Sylvania, and switch and detection systems maker Valeo Raytheon Systems . The North American business accounts for about 15% of its French parent's sales.

The Valeo Inc. unit increased sales in fiscal 2012 as a result of higher new vehicle registrations in the North America market. The number of vehicle registrations came in at about 15.4 million, up 18% from 2011 results.

The company struggled financially following the downturn of the automotive industry in 2009.

The company expands its operations through joint ventures and acquisitions. In 2012 and 2013 Valeo formed partnerships with entities including V. Johnson Enterprises (climate controls) and OSRAM (automotive lighting).

Valeo formed a joint venture (Detroit Thermal Systems) with V. Johnson Enterprises in 2012 with the purpose of purchasing the climate control business of Ford 's Automotive Components Holdings subsidiary. Valeo owns a 51% stake in the new business through the deal, which strengthened the company's position in the North American thermal systems market.
