Matheson Tri-Gas tries and succeeds in supplying a range of industrial gases primarily to semiconductor manufacturers and other electronics makers. Additionally, the company supplies bulk gases to the food and medical industries. Matheson Tri-Gas' operations include the provision of gas-handling equipment like generators, filters, purifiers, regulators, and valves. The company serves customers throughout the US and in Asia, Canada, and Europe. Matheson Tri-Gas is a subsidiary of Japan's largest industrial gas company, Taiyo Nippon Sanso Corporation .

Headquartered in New Jersey, Matheson Tri-Gas has about 180 retail locations in 30 states; it has operations in more than 15 countries.

The company's products can be found in every major integrated circuit plant and R&D facilities in North America, Europe and Asia.

Matheson Tri-Gas' major regional divisions include Matheson K-Air, Matheson LINWELD, Matheson Valley, and Western International Gas and Cylinders, Inc.

The company has a complete line of specialty gas equipment. These products are designed and produced at the Montgomeryville, Pennsylvania location.

The company grows organically and through acquisitions. Matheson Tri-Gas has a couple of joint ventures with global gas giant Linde . One is called Linde Nippon Sanso and based in Germany; it supplies bulk and specialty gases to customers throughout Europe in the electronics industry. The other, Matheson Tri-Gas Global Helium, is made up of the former helium businesses of Linde and BOC , a UK-based industrial gas producer.

In 2016 Matheson Tri-Gas rolled out a line of industrial wire brushes and flap discs as part of the ongoing expansion of their exclusive Matheson Select line of welding accessories, safety supplies, welding tools, and filler metals.

In 2015 the company announced plans to build a second state-of-the-art large capacity Air Separation Unit, in the Los Angeles, California area.

In 2015 Matheson Tri-Gas has completed the construction of a new large capacity air separation unit in Mesa, Arizona to meet the growing demand for industrial gases in the region.

In 2014 it completed new liquid nitrogen and oxygen facilities in North Dakota and Florida, following through on its plan to invest in infrastructure for growth.

In 2016 the company agreed to buy 18 air separation plants in 16 US locations, two nitrous oxide plants, 4 CO2 plants, and related assets from Air Liquide .

In 2015 Matheson Tri-Gas acquired Sims Welding Supply in Southern California. Sims Welding Supply is a well-established supplier of industrial, specialty, and bulk gases, plus welding hard goods, equipment, and supplies. Headquartered in Long Beach, Sims operates out of seven locations in Southern California. The addition of Sims' bulk, industrial, and specialty gas customers provides an opportunity to add efficiencies of scale to its operations in the region.

In the same year, it continued its strategic expansion with the acquisition of the Hawaiian assets and operations of Air Liquide America L.P. The acquisition includes five locations on four islands including an air separation unit (ASU).

In 2014 Matheson Tri-Gas acquired Illinois-based Continental Carbonic Products, a dry ice and liquid carbon dioxide manufacturer. The purchase includes CCP's more than 30 branches and eight production facilities throughout the midwestern, eastern, and southern US. It also gave Matheson Tri-Gas' parent Taiyo Nippon Sanso a way to follow through on its plan to strengthen it North American business.

The company was formed by the 1999 merger of Matheson Gas Products and Tri-Gas, Inc. In 2010, the company rebranded its products and services as MATHESON, though it did not change its legal name.

It significantly added to its US business when it bought Western International Gas and Cylinders in 2010. Western is a Texas-based manufacturer and wholesale supplier of acetylene, propylene, high pressure gases, cylinders, valves, and related services. The acquisition is designed to help Matheson Tri-Gas pursue its US growth strategy.

Expanding its geographic coverage, in 2013 the company bought Evergreen Supply, Inc., based in Aberdeen, South Dakota; A & F Welding Supply, Inc. based in Odessa, Texas; and Whitmer Welding Supplies, Inc. based in Fremont, Nebraska. In 2012 it acquired Airweld, Inc., based in Phoenix, Arizona with operations across Arizona and New Mexico.

Growing its product and services portfolios, in 2012 Matheson Tri-Gas acquired control of RASRIC Corporation, which develops products that purify and deliver ultra-pure liquids and gases with a primary focus on water vapor. Using membrane technology, RASRIC offers significant synergy not only to existing purification products, but also to high purity material business, as more liquid chemicals and precursors are being used in advanced technology nodes of semiconductor manufacturing.
