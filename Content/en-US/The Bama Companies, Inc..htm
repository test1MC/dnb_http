Bama Companies bakes up business for restaurant operators. The company is a top maker of bakery products -- such as handheld and whole pies, biscuits, cookies, crumb crusts, and frozen dough -- for the food service industry. It also produces frozen cakes, donuts, and dessert items. With more than a handful of manufacturing facilities, including one each in Beijing and Guangzhou, Bama Companies distributes its products to more than 20 countries. Bama boasts a pie-making line that runs 1,400 pies per minute. The company caters to such fast food giants as McDonald's , Pizza Hut , and Taco Bell . The family-owned firm, run by CEO Paula Marshall, was formed by Cornelia Alabama "Bama" Marshall in 1927.

Oklahoma-based Bama Companies has pie baking and other facilities in Tulsa. It also operates in Beijing and Guangzhou, China, through several joint ventures there. Export markets include Hong Kong, Japan, Malaysia, the Philippines, and Singapore.

Bama Companies boasts seven manufacturing facilities worldwide. Four of them are located in its home state of Oklahoma.

The Bama Companies operates in China through several joint ventures: the Beijing Bama Food Processing Company in Beijing makes handheld pies; Beijing Baijiayi Food Co., a maker of frozen doughs, cakes, cookies, and other specialty products; and hamburger bun-maker Guangzhou Bama Food Co.
