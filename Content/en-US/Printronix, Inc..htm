Printronix produces a plethora of practical printing products. A leading maker of high-speed industrial printers, Printronix develops line matrix printers for heavy-duty jobs such as invoicing, continuous form printing, and bar code label printing. Its laser printers are used for business and industrial applications. Other products include radio-frequency identification (RFID) printers and printer management systems that let users monitor and configure networked printers from standard Web browsers. The company offers two of the most-trusted brand names in industrial, back office, and supply chain printing, Printronix and TallyGenicom.

Printronix provides on-demand form printing, certified software connectivity, bar code quality and compliance verification, and wireless print management services. The combined brand portfolios include high quality line matrix, thermal, and RFID printers, and service management products and services.

In addition to the US, Printronix has offices throughout Europe and Asia. It serves markets in China, France, Germany, India, Korea, Mexico, the Netherlands, Russia, Singapore, South Africa, the UAE, the UK, the US, and Vietnam.

Printonix serves clients in the manufacturing, distribution, retail, banking, healthcare, government, and other markets.

The company sells its products through industrial distribution partners like Ingram Micro , PEAK Resources , Compu-Call, and Valcomp. It also sells products online.

Printronix counts BMW , Hyundai , IBM , and Volkswagen among its customers. Its business partners include distributor ScanSource and enterprise resource planning (ERP) software specialist SAP .

In recent years Printronix has expanded geographically, strengthening its presence in China and India. The company has targeted the Asia/Pacific region for further growth. It is also strengthening its partner relationships in key European markets. In 2015, Printronix made a move toward the African market with a distribution agreement with Talk Technology. Talk will distribute TallyGenicom products throughout Africa as part of the Printronix strategy to invest in its African partner base.

In November 2015, Printronix sold its Thermal/AIDC business to TSC Auto ID Technology Co. The sale allows Printronix to focus on opportunities impact printing business.

In 2013 Printronix was acquired by an investment group led by Corona Investment Partners, a New York-based private investment firm.

Printronix was started in 1974 by Robert Kleist and five others. An electrical engineer who helped design the first practical VCR, Kleist had previously helped form Dartex, a tape transport manufacturer (acquired by Tally), and Peripheral Business Equipment, a maker of data entry systems (merged with Peripheral Equipment). Printronix introduced its first product, a high-speed printer designed to be rugged enough for factory floors, in 1975, and in the mid-1970s the company introduced high-speed dot matrix printers for data processing departments. It unveiled an intelligent graphics printing feature in 1982.

After establishing itself as one of the world's top 10 printer makers, the company was shaken in the mid-1980s by a slump in the computer market, a costly acquisition (printer manufacturer Anadex, 1984), and its failure to anticipate the market for PC printers. Printronix eventually introduced products for the PC market but couldn't compete against established manufacturers such as Seiko Epson and Oki Data .

At the onset of the 1990s, Printronix refocused on its original market. In 1991 it began making laser printers, and in 1992 it added thermal bar code printers to its product line. In 1994 Printronix won contracts from IBM and Unisys that helped return it to the black for the first time since 1990.
