Petrochem Carless wants to keep drivers from having to do without their vehicles by offering products such as antifreeze, brake fluids, de-icers, and racing fuels. The company is one of the top European refiners and distributors of specialty hydrocarbons and advanced automotive fluids, as well as a leading supplier of drilling fluids. It also supplies chemical solvents such as alcohols, amines, and esters, as well as printing ink distillates and white oils (used in cosmetics and polymers applications). Petrochem Carless' Harwich refinery specializes in finely cut distillation and blends a wide range of products for specialty markets. The company produces about 650 million liters of chemical products per year.

Petrochem Carless sells its products in Europe, the Americas, and in Australia and New Zealand. It has sales offices in Belgium, the UK, and the US

The company is a major producer of antifreeze and other automotive fluids. It makes dozens of high-performing oil products from drilling muds to racing fuels.

Petrochem Carless supplies its product via ships, trucks, and trains to a wide range of industries including Agrochemicals, Antifreeze brake fluids, Chemical manufacturing, Cleaning and detergents, Cosmetics and toiletries, Fuel and lube additives, Heating, Inks packaging and coating, Lubricants, Oilfield chemicals, Paints and lacquers, Pharmaceutical, Plastics, and Wood preservatives.

The company was formed in 2000 when Petrochem bought Carless (which traces its history back to 1859, and coined the word "petrol" for its petroleum distillate) from Spanish giant Repsol and merged the two companies' operations. Petrochem Carless underwent a management buyout (backed by Lloyds Banking Group ) in 2005, and the European Commission approved a move by a Lloyds unit (TSB Development Capital) to buy it outright in 2010.
