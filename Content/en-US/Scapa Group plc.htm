Scapa Group goes with the flow. The company is organized around three strategic markets -- healthcare, industrial, and electronics. It makes technical adhesive tapes, films, and chemical compounds for a wide variety of products. Scapa's commercial customers use its technical tapes for assembly and repair, protection, insulation, and identification. Specific uses range from medical bandages to shin guard protection for hockey players to electronic component assembly. Its Acutek International subsidiary is a medical products manufacturer specializing in adhesive components. Scapa operates in 11 locations in Asia, Europe, and North America, with sales in more than 100 countries.

The company has a presence in Africa, Asia, Europe, India, North America, and South America.

In fiscal 2015 (March year end) the US accounted for 39% of its total revenues; France; 17%, UK 13%, Canada 11%, and others 20% of the total revenue in 2015.

In its Industrial segment, Scapa accommodates the diverse requirements of its market segments, from construction to aerospace. The Industrial business operates across a wide range of market segments and geographies. Its market focus is on Automotive, Cable, Construction and Specialty Products where it has critical mass and leveragable positions. The segment accounted for 63% of Scapa's total fiscal 2015 revenues.

Scapa Healthcare is a leading global developer and manufacturer of innovative life-enhancing healthcare technologies. It offers skin-friendly Turnkey products and services. This segment accounted for 31% of Scapa's fiscal 2015 revenues.

Scapa Electronic engages with electronics manufacturers in the development stages of a product. Its products are application-specific and incorporate functionalities beyond tape and bonding. This segment contributed 6% of revenues.

The company's products are sold to trade and retail markets through different channels. In North America, it sells primarily through its distribution network to the trade market and in Europe it markets products under their Barnier brand which is predominantly sold through builders' merchants and distributors. In general the company are seeing increasing usage of its products as its performance improvements and advantages are displacing the more traditional fixation methods of using nails and mechanical fixings.

Scapa has recorded an increasing trend in net revenues over the last five years.

In fiscal 2015 net revenues increased by 4%.

Healthcare revenues grew by 7.9%, reflecting the successful launch of a new product in 2013 for one of Scapa's major global wound care customers.

Industrial revenues increased by 6.6%, driven by market share gain and improved share of its customers' spend.

Electronics finished the year strongly despite difficult market conditions in Asia, following a new product launch across the region.

In fiscal 2015, Scapa's net income was £9.5 million (a 242% improvement from a loss in fiscal 2014) due to an increase in net sales, exceptional deferred tax write off, and the impact of a change in tax rate on deferred tax.

Cash from operating activities increased by 6% that year.

The Electronics segment will be reported within Industrial from next year

Scapa is looking to continue to move up the value chain by expanding its offerings and capabilities in Healthcare. It is also focusing on developing strategic partnerships in Automotive and Cable markets where it has critical mass and a position on which to leverage, accelerating the shift from product to solution selling.

In the Industrial segment (Construction and Specialty Products) it is looking to leverage its brands and grow market share through expansion of both point of sales and product range.

It plans to continue to add capabilities, product offerings and services as well as geographies and channels through acquisitions:

In 2016 Scapa acquired adhesive maker EuroMed for more than $35 million. In 2015 it bought UK-based First Water Limited (a healthcare business specializing in innovation, and the design and manufacturing of a wide range of hydrogels) for £11.3m. The addition of First Water's strong patent portfolio and technological know-how to Scapa's expertise in materials, coating, and conversion capabilities and significantly enhances its value proposition.
