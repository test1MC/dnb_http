Luckily for its clients, Butterworths follows the law. The company, a UK-based subsidiary of LexisNexis Group , publishes legal, news, and business information on a subscription basis to customers worldwide in the legal, tax and accounting, academic, and public sector industries. Its products are delivered in various formats, including online, print, and CD. With roots extending back to 1818 (the year Henry Butterworth established his law publishing company), Butterworths launched in 1996 when parent Reed Elsevier purchased rival legal publisher Tolley Publishing. The company also scooped up IRS Eclipse, a publisher and conference organizer, in 2000.
