Who in the world would provide audio-gadget junkies with even more grist for the listening mill? Kenwood would. Kenwood USA, a subsidiary of Japan's JVC KENWOOD , develops, makes, and distributes car and home entertainment systems and communications products to customers in the US. Its radios and mobile audio systems are sold by electronics retailers, such as Best Buy , Fry's , Pep Boys , and Sears . Its car electronics and home audio products also may be purchased through specialized catalogs and online. Sister company Kenwood Communications, based in Georgia, specializes in making and marketing mobile and portable two-way radios and custom systems. They're used by amateur radio users, and emergency responders.

California-based Kenwood USA operates throughout the US with six service centers in California, New Jersey, New York, Virginia, and Puerto Rico.

While its Japanese parent doesn't break out financial results for Kenwood USA, the Americas region accounted for 29% of JVC KENWOOD $3.25 billion in fiscal 2012 (ended March) sales, an increase of 7% versus the prior year.

Kenwood USA's Japanese parent company merged with rival JVC in 2008 to form JVC KENWOOD Holdings , a joint holding company. The formation of JVC KENWOOD is intended to create a strong competitor in consumer car audio to rival Pioneer Corp . and others.
