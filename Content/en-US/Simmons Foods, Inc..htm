Products from Simmons Foods sizzle on summer grills and stovetops throughout the year. The company produces a variety of poultry products, including private-label fresh, partially cooked, fully cooked, and frozen, and markets them to chefs and consumers in the US and overseas. Simmons distributes its value-added poultry to big box and club stores and foodservice providers, including restaurants. It offers customization services and also makes ingredients for use by livestock feed and pet food makers. Its sister company, Simmons Pet Foods (SPF), makes dog and cat food under the Twin Pet, Strongheart, and Kitty labels, among others. Simmons is the second largest employer in Arkansas (behind Tyson Foods ).

Based in Siloam Springs, Arkansas, the company maintains production facilities in Siloam Springs, Decatur, Van Buren, and Fort Smith, Arkansas; Fairland, Oklahoma; Emporia, Kansas; Southwest City and Pineville, Missouri; Pennsauken, New Jersey; and Toronto, Canada.

Simmons sells its products in all 50 US states and more than 40 other countries around the world.

Simmons Prepared Foods is a farm to fork poultry producer based in Siloam Springs, Arkansas primarily serving customer brands as a supplier of premium chicken products for the foodservice industry. The company sources its poultry through agreements with some 300 independent chicken farmers in Arkansas, Missouri, and Oklahoma. Simmons supplies independent poultry growers and 20 plus company managed farms which raise approximately 200 million birds per year.

Simmons Pet Food is a leading North American private-label and contract manufacturer of wet and dry pet food and treats. Simmons Feed Ingredients produces specialized ingredients for the pet food, aquaculture and animal feed industries.

The company sells through supermarket retailers, mass merchandisers, foodservice industry, pet specialty retailers, and other retail and wholesale outlets.

In 2015 the company began construction of new 89,125-square-foot pet food ingredient facility (to produce fresh and frozen meat ingredients for pet food manufacturers) in Siloam Springs, Arkansas at an estimated cost of $26 million.

Besides chickens, Simmons has been extending its reach into pet food in recent years as the result of a timely investment in beleaguered Menu Foods. Simmons' pet food unit SPF purchased Menu Foods in 2010 for some $240 million in cash and assumed debt. Acquiring Menu Foods, a private-label and contract manufacturer of wet pet food in North America, complements SPF's wet and dry pet food business and makes it one of the leading makers of pet food under private label in the US. (Contributing to the downturn of the company, Menu Foods made headlines in 2007 in North America. The Canadian firm was forced to recall 60 million cans of pet food nationwide. The food, imported from China and suspected of containing melamine, killed about a dozen pets due to kidney failure.)
