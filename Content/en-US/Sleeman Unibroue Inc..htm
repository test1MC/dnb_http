Sleeman Unibroue has always shown an undiluted passion for craft brewing. The company, which was founded in 1991 by André Dion and Serge Racine, brews a variety of craft beers in the Belgian tradition. Its brands include 1837, Blanche de Chambly, Trois Pistoles, Maudite, Fin du Monde, St-Hubert, and Éphémère. Unibroue's brews are available around the world. An old school brewer, the company believes that adding preservatives to, or pasteurizing beer, makes the beer "sterile and without a soul." The brewery took its current name in 2005, after Sleeman Breweries acquired it in 2004.
