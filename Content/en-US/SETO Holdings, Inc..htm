SETO Holdings sees to it that the company stays focused on operating in niche manufacturing markets. In 2009 the company agreed to sell all of its subsidiaries to its president and move into retailing hearing aids by acquiring Advanced Hearing Centers from Sanjay Srivastava. Previously accounting for most of SETO Holdings' revenue, its East Coast Sales Company distributed industrial ceramics (insulators, rods, tubes) for airline, security, and aerospace customers; Semicon Tools and DTI Technology made diamond tools and supplies for silicon wafer manufacturers; lastly, SETO Technology tackled contract manufacturing of CPR training gear, safety helmets, law-enforcement lighting, and mobile power supplies.
