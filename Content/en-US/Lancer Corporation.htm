"Make it a Coke" is music to Lancer Corporation's ears. The company, a manufacturer of fountain drink-dispensing equipment for the foodservice and beverage industries, makes systems for dispensing soft drinks and juice, post-mix dispensing valves for mixing syrup and carbonated water, ice dispensers, beer dispensing systems, and various syrup pumps, carbonators, and valves. Lancer is a subsidiary of Japanese commercial kitchen equipment maker Hoshizaki Electric Co. Coca-Cola traditionally is one of Lancer's biggest customers; the two companies are linked throughout Lancer's history.

The company operates manufacturing plants and sales offices in the US, Mexico, Australia, New Zealand, the Far East, and Belgium. It has a presence in more than 60 countries.

Lancer stays in the forefront of its industry by continually looking to improve beverage dispensing equipment. Its historical ties with Coca-Cola were strengthened further in late 2010 when the two companies patented a beverage converter valve capable of using multiple ports without fluid crossover.

Lance's products are sold trough a network of sales representatives and authorized distributors.

Borrowing $4,200, the Schroeder brothers began making parts for beverage-dispensing machines in 1967. Lancer's first product was a quick-disconnect fitting designed for Coca-Cola. Coca-Cola designated Lancer a primary supplier and the two companies also worked out a development agreement in which Lancer would manufacture items specifically for Coca-Cola.

Lancer revolutionized the beverage dispenser industry in 1971 by creating the first mechanically cooled soft drink dispensers. The product caught on and boosted Lancer's sales by the mid-1970s.

Lancer went public in 1985. Throughout the 1990s the company expanded its foreign operations and product offerings. In 1996 Lancer formed Lancer FBD Partnership, a joint venture with Jimmy Frank, to make frozen beverage dispensing equipment. The trend continued in 1997 with its acquisition of beverage-dispenser makers Applied Beverage Systems (New Zealand) and Commercial Vila Formosa (Brazil).

In 1998 Burger King signed an agreement to spend at least $10 million on Lancer's frozen beverage dispensers. Sales to Brazil and Asia were nearly halved in 1999, contributing to a $4.6 million loss that fiscal year (the company's first loss in a decade). To strengthen its distribution system, Lancer formed Advanced Beverage Solutions in 2000 to serve customers in the US Midwest. Lancer closed its Brazilian subsidiary in 2002 due to weak sales.

In 2006 Lancer was acquired by Hoshizaki America, the US arm of Hoshizaki Electric.
