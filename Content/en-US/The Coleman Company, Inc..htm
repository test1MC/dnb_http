The Coleman Company makes what it takes to be a happy camper. As a leading manufacturer of outdoor recreation gear, it produces coolers, backpacks, tents, sleeping bags, air mattresses, lanterns, grills, life vests, and floats, among other items, which are sold under the Wake Vest, Helium, Puddle Jumper, Mad Dog Gear, Sevylor, and Stearns brands. Coleman operates sales offices and distribution facilities worldwide and sells its products mainly through mass merchandisers, home centers, and other retail stores, as well as through its website and a network of about a dozen Coleman Factory Outlet stores. Founded in 1900, Coleman is owned by Jarden Corporation .

Coleman, which became a subsidiary of Jarden Corp. in 2005, is a key part of its parent company's Outdoor Solutions business, the largest of Jarden's four business segments. In addition to manufacturing outdoor gear under the Coleman and AeroBed brands, Coleman operates outlet stores in Alabama, Colorado, Georgia, Kansas, Michigan, Missouri, New York, Pennsylvania, and Tennessee.

Coleman drives demand with a variety of new and refined products. In 2014, additions included advancements in battery lanterns with new features that increase light output and runtime. In addition, a high-performance version of the RoadTripGrill, and new comfortable, portable seating options in the outdoor furniture category were launched. In 2014 Coleman expanded its line of "instant" tents, canopies, and shelters, which can be set up in less than a minute.
