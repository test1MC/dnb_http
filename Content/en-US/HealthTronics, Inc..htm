HealthTronics trains electronic equipment to zap kidney stones and blast cancer tumors. The company contracts with medical facilities to provide urology treatment services, including equipment, scheduling, training, and clinical technicians. It makes, sells, and services lasers and lithotripters, which use shock waves to break up kidney and gall-bladder stones. HealthTronics also offers urological pathology testing and cryosurgical (freezing of tissue) treatments for enlarged prostate and prostate cancer. In addition, HealthTronics offers related consumable supplies and maintenance services. Drug and device maker Endo International sold HealthTronics to investment firm Altaris Capital in early 2014.

After several months of restructuring and selling off parts of HealthTronics, in early 2014 Endo sold the rest to Altaris Capital Partners for some $130 million.

HealthTronics' largest business segment is the provision of lithotripters and lithotripsy treatment services to medical facilities. The company has installed some 500 lithotripsy systems nationwide and manages 100-plus of the devices in the US.

In 2013 Endo agreed to sell the HealthTronics Laboratory Solutions unit, which provides anatomical pathology services, to Metamark Genetics for an undisclosed price. Endo had announced that it would explore strategic alternatives for the HealthTronics operations earlier in the year.
