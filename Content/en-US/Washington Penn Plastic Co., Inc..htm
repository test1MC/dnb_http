Washington Penn Plastic (WPP) -- based in Washington, Pennsylvania -- is a compounder of custom polyolefin and thermoplastic resins. Its products can be made to the customer's requirements, with options including custom colors, calcium-, talc-, or mica-filled compounds, fiberglass and mineral reinforced, or plastics made from recycled materials. The compounds are used by customers to make a number of products including packaging, lawn and garden, pool and spa, building materials, appliances, and automotive parts, among others. Subsidiary Southern Polymer provides distribution and reselling services. WPP is part of the Audia Group.

The company operates four manufacturing plant in the US, one in Mexico, and one in Slovakia. They have a combined annual production capacity of more than 750 million pounds.

WPP operates through two companies: Uniform Color Company (high quality custom masterbatches for the thermoplastics industry) and Southern Polymer (polyethylene, polypropylene, PET, and polystyrene for a wide variety of markets).

The company serves market such as automotive, building and construction, appliance, packaging and disposable products, lawn and garden, pool and spa, electronics, furniture, and medical.

In 2014 the company signed an agreement with ExxonMobil Chemical Company (EMC) to buy and license assets of EMC's North American specialty compounded polypropylene products business, following EMC's decision to cease production of those products in North America.
