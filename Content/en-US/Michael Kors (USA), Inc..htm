Michael Kors (USA) dresses the stars, both real and imagined. The company designs, distributes, and sells high-fashion apparel, footwear, and accessories for men and women. It has expanded its products portfolio in recent years by inking several licensing agreements, such as watches with Fossil , eyewear with Marchon , and fragrances with Est√©e Lauder . The firm's collections include three brands -- Michael Kors, MICHAEL Michael Kors, and KORS Michael Kors. The company sells its products through its own retail network, as well as through high-end specialty department stores as a wholesaler. Michael Kors maintains a presence online, assisted by Neiman Marcus . The company is owned by Michael Kors Holdings.

Michael Kors (USA) sells its upscale apparel nationwide.

Michael Kors branded goods are sold through about 290 North American retail stores and concessions. All three collections are sold in more than 2,200 department stores. The clothing company wholesales its products to high-end retailers, such as Bergdorf Goodman , Neiman Marcus , Bloomingdale's , Nordstrom , and others across the US. Michael Kors also boasts an online presence through michaelkors.com, set up by Neiman Marcus Direct.

While parent Michael Kors Holdings does not break out separate advertising expenses for its US unit, the company in fiscal 2014 (ended March) spent $65.7 million on advertising in North America and internationally, up significantly from $41.9 million in fiscal 2013.

The fast-growing company splits its revenues about 50-50 between department stores and retail operations in North America.

In fiscal 2014 (ended March), the parent company's revenue rose an impressive 52% versus the prior year (after posting a 68% gain in the previous annual comparison). North America, which includes Michael Kors (USA), posted a 43% gain in sales. Retail, wholesale, and licensing revenue rose by 40%, 46%, and 35%, respectively. The increase resulted from higher comparable and non-comparable retail store sales and wholesale sales, as well as increases in licensing revenue.

Michael Kors looks to increase its brand awareness and customer loyalty in North America. To this end, it is working to open new retail stores in highly visible locations, such as Madison Avenue in New York and Rodeo Drive in California, maintain its strong advertising position in global fashion publications, and grow its online advertising exposure and presence on the Internet. In fiscal 2014 (ended March) the parent company opened 101 new stores

To boost its wholesale revenue, Michael Kors is converting existing wholesale door space into shop-in-shops and expanding the size of existing shop-in-shops.

The company aims to extend the reach of its retail footprint, as well, to some 400 locations across North America.
