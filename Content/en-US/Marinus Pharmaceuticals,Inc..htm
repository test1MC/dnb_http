Marinus Pharmaceuticals is ready to put an end to epileptic seizures. The biopharmaceutical company is developing a drug candidate, ganaxolone, to be used as an add-on therapy for the treatment of partial onset seizures in people with epilepsy. (Epilepsy affects some 50 million people globally, and over 5 million people are under treatment in the US, Europe, and Japan.) The company is also developing ganaxolone to treat conditions associated with Fragile X Syndrome, which causes intellectual disability, behavioral, and learning challenges. Founded in 2003 by former CEO Henry Penner, Marinus went public in 2014, raising $45 million in its IPO. It plans to use the proceeds to further develop and commercialize ganaxolone.
