Senomyx nose a good thing when it smells it. The company has identified human receptor genes related to the detection of smells and tastes, and, using this genetic research, the company is developing sweet, salty, and savory flavor enhancers and bitter taste modulators. Potential products include agents that can block bitter tastes in coffee and make low-sodium snacks taste salty. Senomyx collaborates with the likes of PepsiCo , Nestl√© , and Firmenich . The company also works with Japan's largest flavors company, Ajinomoto , which has opened up the Asian market for Senomyx.

Senomyx markets it products in North America, Latin America, Asia, Europe, Africa, Australia and the Middle East.

The company operates in one business segment, which is the development and commercialization of flavor ingredients.

PepsiCo , Firmenich , and the company's other collaborators are responsible for the sales, marketing, and distribution of all packaged food and beverage products incorporating Senomyx's flavor ingredients.

Their commercial revenues under the direct sales program are generated from actual sales of the company's flavor ingredients to flavor companies and other customers. Under its direct sales program, Senomyx sells its Complimyx brand flavor ingredients, Sweetmyx, Savorymyx, and Bittermyx, to flavor companies for use in a wide variety of foods and beverages.

In 2014 Senomyx's net revenues decreased by 6% due to $2.8 million decrease in development revenues, reflecting a drop in license fee revenues from its Sweet Taste Program collaboration with PepsiCo due to a change in the upfront license fee arrangement. Partially offsetting the decrease in license fee revenues was an increase in research funding revenues related to the Salt Taste Program collaboration with PepsiCo that began in 2014 and resulted in $1.5 million of development revenues for the year.

Commercial revenues increased $1.2 million as a result of increases in royalties and commercial milestone payments related to the Sweet Taste Program and increases in direct sales.

The company have incurred significant losses since its inception in 1998 and, as of December 31, 2014 their accumulated deficit was $255 million. Senomyx's net loss increased by 2% fin 2014 due to lower net sales, an increase in selling, general, and administrative costs, and increased commercial revenue expenses.

Cash used by operating activities was $13.6 million which is increased of 40% over 2013's cash outflow.

The company is pursuing the discovery, development and/or commercialization of flavor ingredients through five programs focused on sweet, savory, bitter, cooling and salt taste areas. The goal of itsSweet Taste Program is to grow its portfolio of new flavors.

Senomyx seeks to maintain and expanding its technology position, develop industry-recognized flavor ingredients; collaborate with leading packaged food, beverage, and ingredient companies; and pursue market opportunities through direct sales.

In 2011 Senomyx entered into a collaborative agreement with Cadbury Adams USA , to develop and commercialize novel flavor modulators for potential use in the gum and medicated confectionery product categories. Senomyx has similar agreements with companies such as Firmenich , PepsiCo , Campbell's Soup , and others.

Under its collaboration agreements, Senomyx's partners are responsible for manufacturing, marketing, selling, and distributing their foods, beverages, or ingredient supplies that include Senomyx ingredients. This allows Senomyx to benefit from its collaborators' brand recognition, global market presence, sales and distribution channels, and other industry-specific expertise. Senomyx receives both upfront payments from food and beverage companies for researching and developing its products, and royalty payments for their continued use in the marketplace.
