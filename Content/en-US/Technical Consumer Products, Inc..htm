How many people does it take to screw in one of Technical Consumer Products' light bulbs? One, and they only have to change the bulb every few years. Technical Consumer Products (TCP) sells energy efficient compact fluorescent light (CFLs) bulbs, light emitting diodes (LEDs), high-intensity discharge (HID) lamps, and halogen bulbs in the US under the brands TCP, DuraBright, EcoSave, Fresh2, and n:vision. It also offers linear fluorescent bulbs and fixtures, exit and emergency lighting, CFL fixtures, and ballasts to the commercial market under the brands EcoVations and SpringLight. TCP is the US sales arm of Chinese manufacturer TCP International Holdings .

Based in Ohio, Technical Consumer Products (TCP) serves consumers throughout North America.

TCP operates as the US sales unit for its Chinese parent TCP International Holdings. To support its business, the company maintains distribution centers in California and Ohio and uses manufacturing plants in China.

The lighting expert sells its products through wholesalers and distributors, catering to several industries such as retail, restaurant, hospitality, education, and healthcare.

TCP bulbs are sold to the consumer market at major retailers including Amazon.com , Home Depot , Office Depot , and Wal-Mart . (In addition, TCP supplies the bulbs sold under Home Depot's private label). The company also sells bulbs through utility and government incentive programs that offer discounts on energy-efficient bulbs. TCP partners with utility companies to develop incentive programs that encourage customers to buy CFLs and be reimbursed by the utility.

TCP sells its products to the commercial market through a network of more than 10,000 distributors, including Consolidated Electrical Distributors , Grainger , HD Supply , Regency Lighting , and Rexel ; major commercial customers include Macy's and MGM Resorts . Distributors can also receive cash incentives to undergo sales training though the TCP Institute's webinars.

TCP has been adding to its portfolio of products in recent years. In 2013 it rolled out a smart home LED lighting system under the Connected by TCP name. The product uses LED technology and operates on a home's Wi-Fi network, controlled by an iOS or Android-enabled device. It also launched the Elite Series of its LED lighting system, which consume 20% less power than previous generations of its LED lamps. It features more than 500 lamp options.
