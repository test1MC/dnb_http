J. Lohr Winery pours it on. It produces red, white, sparkling, and non-alcoholic wines. The family-owned winery's vintages are divided into tiers with the more expensive wines falling under the labels J. Lohr Cuvée Series, J. Lohr Vineyards Series, and J. Lohr Estates. The company's less expensive wines bear the Cypress Vineyards and Painter Bridge labels. The company also makes a line of non-alcoholic wines called ARIEL. J. Lohr Winery grows mainly red varietals of grapes on almost 3,700 acres that it owns and farms in three distinct wine growing regions in California. It sells its wines nationwide and in more than 25 other countries.

J. Lohr produces wine in three regions in California: Paso Robles in San Luis Obispo County; Arroyo Seco and Santa Lucia Highlands in Monterey County; and St. Helena in the Napa Valley.

The company's wines are available throughout the US and in more than 25 other countries worldwide.

The wine maker leverages social media, as well as wine clubs and societies, to give its brands traction and sell its products.

J. Lohr grows its grapes organically, uses solar energy, and packages its wines with recycled paper products. The company's 1,300 acres of cool-climate estate vineyards in Monterey County grow Chardonnay, Riesling, Valdiguié and Pinot Noir. Its 2,300 acres in Paso Robles are dedicated to Cabernet, Merlot, Syrah, Petite Sirah and other red varietals, while its 35 acres in Napa Valley, focus on producing Cabernet and Sauvignon Blanc.

The operation was founded by Jerry Lohr, who planted his first California vineyard in 1972.
