Tuthill moves a mountain of industrial equipment. Specializing in rotating equipment, the family-owned company manufacturer of industrial goods makes a broad variety of goods such as pumps, meters, vacuum systems, and blowers for handling both fluids and gases. Key products are marketed under its Fill-Rite, Sotera, Kinney Vacuum, MD Pneumatics, and GlobalGear brands. In addition, Tuthill operates a plastics division that supplies custom injection-molding services. The company serves the agriculture, chemicals, construction, energy, and food and beverage industries and several others.

Tuthill operates some 30 locations around the world. Its wide product portfolio addresses customer demands in a myriad of diverse industry applications in more than 150 markets in Asia/Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, and Latin and North America.

The company's operating companies include Tuthill Transfer Systems, Tuthill Pump Group, Tuthill Vacuum & Blower Systems, and Tuthill Plastics Group.

The company serves the agriculture, chemicals, construction, energy, food and beverage, pharmaceuticals and medical, transportation, and utilities markets.

Tuthill implements what it calls the Tuthill Business System in organizing its manufacturing and operations. The system is modeled after the famous Toyota Production System, which Toyota Motor uses for continuous improvement of its business processes and manufacturing techniques. Tuthill also follows a "Conscious Company" approach to management, which encourages all of its employees to become "radical leaders" in improving their workplaces.

The company has in recent years been scaling back its operations and paying down debt by selling some of its holdings. In 2012 it sold its Drive Systems division to private equity firm HCI Equity Partners. The division was reorganized into a stand-alone company and renamed TDS Drive.

The company was founded in 1892 by James Tuthill.
