Cooper Farms' poultry products fly far from the coop. The company raises turkeys (180 million pounds of live bird per year) on its Ohio farm and supplies pre-cooked (oven-roasted, smoked, and cured breasts and deli meats) and ready-to-cook turkey products to retail and foodservice customers in the US and Mexico. Cooper Farms also provides private-label packaging services for other food manufacturers. Additional items produced by the company include eggs (360 million a year), weaned pigs (125 million live pounds per year), poults (baby turkeys; 15 million per year), and animal feed (400,000 tons per year). The family-owned and operated firm makes feed for diary and beef cattle, as well as for swine.

Cooper Farms' operations are located in northwest and central Ohio in St. Henry, Van Wert, Oakwood, and Fort Recovery, Ohio. It serves customers in the US and Mexico.

Vertically-integrated Cooper Farms operates two divisions: the Live Animal business oversees the care of its turkeys and pigs as they mature, and manufactures feed; the Food Processing division produces and packs cooked meats. It operates its business across four locations in agriculture-rich Northwest and West Central Ohio.

Cooper Farms relies on nearly 180 contract turkey growers, more than 80 contract hog growers, and 20-plus contract chicken growers.

The company is expanding its hog farming operation. In 2013 it opened Fox Tail Sow Farm, a 25-acre farm in Defiance County, Ohio, that's home to 2,500 mother pigs.

Cooper Farms anticipates relying on more contract growers as it expands its business.

Cooper Farms caters to retail and foodservice customers in the US and Mexico.
