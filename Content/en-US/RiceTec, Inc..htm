While the company is known for its aromatic rice products, RiceTec has built a business with seeds. The company has been researching, developing, and marketing hybrid rice seed for use in North and South America since 1988. Farmers have become familiar with its CLEARFIELD brand of hybrid rice seed. RiceTec also markets grain-based side dishes and specialty rice through grocery stores nationwide under the RiceSelect name. RiceSelect consumer products include Texmati, Jasmati, and Kasmati aromatic rices, as well as couscous, orzo, risotto, and sushi rice. The company is owned by the royal family of Liechtenstein, a small European country located between Austria and Switzerland.

Beyond its operations the US, RiceTec enjoys a global reach.

As part of its business, Houston-based RiceTec operates a 500-acre research farm, laboratory, seed plant, rice mill, and packing plant in Alvin, Texas. It also runs a research station and seed sales office in Arkansas and off-season testing facilities in Hawaii and Puerto Rico. RiceTec's test facilities extend from the US to Central and South America. The company's consumer sales organization is housed at the Houston headquarters office.

The company works with John Deere Financial and Rabo AgriFinance and its service partners to provide customers with finance options and improved terms of sale.

RiceTec's sales and marketing group aims to educate customers and service partners about hybrid seed. It works with service partners throughout the MidSouth and Gulf Coast to increase the brand awareness for its seed in the US market. Beyond product education and awareness, RiceTec taps the agriculture industry for additional avenues in getting its seed options in front of customers and farmers.

The company relies on grocery stores nationwide to get its products in the hands of consumers. Its brand names include RiceSelect, Texmati, Jasmati, and Kasmati, among others.

RiceTec in 2013 launched a new $150-million research initiative that involves developing new hybrids, new varieties, and new herbicide-tolerant rice. To support this effort, the company has added more scientists and technical service representatives to its field staff. To this end, RiceTec is rolling out two new hybrids in 2014.
