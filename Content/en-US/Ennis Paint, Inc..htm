Ennis Paint has earned its stripes in the traffic paint industry and has become the largest pavement marking company in the world. The company operates manufacturing facilities throughout the US and sells its paint and thermoplastic coating products through a nationwide network of distributors. It also sells raised road markers and components, and it serves international customers. The company was founded in 1996. In 2012 Brazos Private Equity Partners combined its portfolio companies Ennis Paint and Flint Trading, although both remained as separate entities. In 2016 Olympus Partners acquired a majority interest in Ennis-Flint.

In 2016 Olympus Partners, a Stamford, Connecticut-based private equity firm, acquired control of Ennis-Flint.

The company has 20 manufacturing facilities in the US, Canada, Australia, Malaysia, and Europe as well as worldwide sales locations.

The company serves various markets such as government bodies, contractors, commercial engineers and architects, airports, and the military.

In 2014 Ennis-Flint acquired the Australian colored surfacing specialists, TCP. TCP has operating facilities in Melbourne and Brisbane.

That year it also acquired the North American glass bead manufacturing business from Weissker Manufacturing, LLC, a Weissker Group company, widely recognized globally for its expertise in research, manufacturing, and performance. The transactions along with major capital investments further improves the company's ability to improve shared roadway user networks with the most comprehensive line of pavement marking materials and electronic control products for the traffic safety, access, and rail industries.
