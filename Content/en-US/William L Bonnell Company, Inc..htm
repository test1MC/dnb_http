William L Bonnell molds its own future by shaping up aluminum products. William L. Bonnell's extrusion operations include a handful of plants that make aluminum extrusion products and components used in industries including building materials, automotive parts, and agricultural machinery. Bonnell sells its products unfinished, coated, or painted to distributors and fabricators across North America. The company provides extensive service to the commercial and residential construction industries, offering items including open-architecture shapes, soft-alloy extrusions, and large aluminum structures for walls, walkways, frames, doors, and enclosures. Founded in 1955, Bonnell is a unit of Tredegar Corporation .

The company has manufacturing facilities in Newnan, Georgia (also the location of its headquarters); Elkhart, Indiana; Niles, Michigan; and Carthage, Tennessee.

The company's products for the construction industry include components for walls, windows, and doors; sunshades, canopies, and walkway covers; and commercial storefronts and modular frames. Other Bonnell offerings include components for medical facility equipment, electric power units, cars and trucks, and industrial machinery. The company also provides product design, development, engineering, and finishing services.

Customers of Bonnell include entities in the consumer durables; electrical; machinery and equipment; residential and nonresidential construction; and transportation industries.

Bonnell reported revenue of some $325 million in 2012.

In response to a sluggish construction market, Tredegar closed down the Bonnell plant in Kentland, Indiana, in 2012. At the same time, the company began working to expand in industries outside of the construction market. In 2013 the company conducted a $17 million expansion project at its Newnan manufacturing facility, to increase its capacity for projects in the automotive industry.

The company scaled back its operations in Canada with the sale of its Bon L Canada (aluminum, fiberglass, and wooden ladders) and Apolo Tool and Die Manufacturing units to Signature Aluminum in 2008. Tredegar used the money to fund the expansion of its Carthage, Tennessee manufacturing facility.

Mergers and Acquisitions

In 2012 Bonnell acquired AACOA, a North America-based aluminum extrusion manufacturer, for some $51 million. The purchase expanded Bonnell's fabrication capabilities, adding plants in Elkhart, Indiana, and Niles, Michigan. It also assisted Bonnell's goal of expanding in non-construction industries.
