With a combination of several mass-market and nearly 20 regional brands of artificial sweeteners -- and sales in more than 100 countries worldwide -- the Merisant Company finds life sweet indeed. It is the manufacturer of aspartame-based products, such as Equal, Canderel, and Sweet Simplicity. It also makes Pure Via, a sweetener derived from the stevia plant. Merisant's customers include retail grocery stores, pharmacies, food wholesalers, foodservice distributors and operators, and other food manufacturers. And although the little blue packets of Equal are ubiquitous throughout the US, more than half of Merisant's sales come from other countries.

Based in Chicago, Merisant makes and markets its sweetener brands worldwide in more than 100 countries. It boasts manufacturing facilities in Manteno, Illinois, and in Teplice, Czech Republic. More than half of its sales are made outside the US.

The food company operates its business globally with offices in Bilbao, Spain; Buckinghamshire, UK; Martinez, Argentina; Mexico City, Mexico; Neuchâtel, Switzerland; North Ryde, Australia; Paris, France; and Teplice, Czech Republic.

Merisant makes Pure Via brand tabletop sweetener. The product's active sweetening ingredient is Reb A (made by and supplied to Merisant by PureCircle), which is derived from the stevia plant. (Extracts of the stevia plant are said to be 200 times sweeter than sugar.) Merisant partners with PepsiCo , which owns the trademark for and uses the sweetener in its beverages, to form the joint venture Whole Earth Sweetener, which distributes Pure Via to food retailers for tabletop use.

The company is a multi-channel marketer, serving retail grocery stores, food wholesalers, foodservice distributors and operators, pharmacies, and other food manufacturers. Merisant is a global market leader, having earned a 30% market share for table top sweeteners.

While privately-owned Merisant doesn't report its financial results, the company rang up an estimated $232 million in sales in fiscal 2012 (ends December), yielding more than $40 million in operating income.

Following a 2013 refinancing of a $95-million credit facility, Merisant is focused on growth, innovation, and infrastructure development. It's also streamlining its global cash flow and optimizing its European distributor operations.
