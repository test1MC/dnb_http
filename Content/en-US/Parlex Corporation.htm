Electronic signals get a cool send-off with the flexible interconnects that Parlex makes for the automotive, computer, electronics, and telecommunications markets. Flexible interconnects, which transfer signals between electronic components, are smaller, lighter, and dissipate heat better than their rigid counterparts, making them more efficient for use in high-speed electronics. Parlex's interconnect products include flexible circuits, laminated cables, flexible interconnect assemblies, and flexible interconnect hybrid circuits. Customers include Hewlett-Packard , Raytheon , Siemens , Tyco , and Whirlpool , among others. Johnson Electric has owned Parlex since 2005, when it acquired the firm for about $75 million.

The company its based in Methuen, Massachusetts. It has manufacturing facilities in China, Singapore, Mexico, the UK, and the US.

Joint venture Parlex Shanghai makes flexible interconnects in China. Parlex has also formed a Hong Kong-based joint venture with Infineon ; that business is intended to manufacture advanced electronics substrates.

Parlex serves the automotive, computer, consumer products, life sciences, electronics, and telecommunications markets, among others.

The company is developing strategic relationships with key customers in target industries and developing innovative processes and products. Parlex engineers collaborate directly with customers to improve the company's products.
