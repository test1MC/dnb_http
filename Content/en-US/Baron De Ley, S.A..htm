Swedish meatballs, Swiss cheese, English roast beef, French coq au vin, and German bratwurst -- the people at Barón De Ley will tell you they all go well with a good glass of wine. Its wine brands include Barón De Ley, Club Privado, Coto De Imaz, Coto Real, El Coto, El Mesón, Finca Monasterio, Jabato, Máximo, and Museum Real brands. The company operates the Barón De Ley, El Coto de Rioja, El Mesón, Finca Monastery, Finca Museum, and Máximo wineries. The vintner has 320 hectares of land under cultivation. The Spanish company's overseas sales account for about 70% of its sales. Growth in Asia has been especially strong, but the company also distributes its products in Europe and North and South America.

Wine sales from the Baron De Ley, El Coto de Rioja, Maximo, Vinedos and Finca Museum wineries contribute 97% of revenue. It has four vineyards, Cenicero, Mendavia, Ausejo and Carboneras. The remaining 3% of revenue comes from its Dehesa pig-rearing operation, which produces cured hams from acorn-fed Retinto and Lampino breeds.

Based in Navarra, Spain, Barón De Ley makes more than half its money from its home market, but exports internationally to the EU and beyond.

In 2015, sales increased 7% to €93.8 million after bottled wine sales pushed up revenue. Net income fell 15% to €23.5 million due to higher operating expenses and a decreased in interest on securities available for sale. Cash flows from operating activities fell 13% to €26.9 million due to lower net income and changes in working capital.
