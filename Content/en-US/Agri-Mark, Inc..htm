Cheese lovers who make a habit of Cabot ought to know about Agri-Mark. The northeastern US dairy cooperative makes Cabot-brand Vermont cheddar cheese, butter, and cultured dairy products, as well as McCadam-branded and European cheeses. The co-op boasts more than 1,275 member-owners who operate farms throughout New England and New York, producing 300 million gallons of milk a year. Agri-Mark also sells milk to bottlers and manufacturers in the Eastern US and dairy ingredients to foodservice and industrial clients. It owns processing plants in Vermont, Massachusetts, and New York. The co-op was formed in 1916 as the New England Milk Producers Association and became Agri-Mark in 1980.

From its base in Lawrence, Massachusetts, Agri-Mark boasts US operations in Massachusetts, Vermont, and New York.

Agri-Mark's central laboratory, which performs more than 335,000 component and quality tests on members' milk annually, is located in West Springfield, Massachusetts. It maintains a plant there, as well, that produces five principle products -- heavy sweet cream, condensed skim milk, skim milk, nonfat dry milk powder, and butter -- from milk sourced from farms in New England and New York.

In Vermont, Agri-Mark makes its Cabot-brand cheese in Middlebury, producing 15,000-plus pounds of cheddar cheese each day. The facility, which has a 54,000-sq.-ft. cheddar aging warehouse onsite, also is responsible for making other dairy items such as cottage cheese, sour cream, yogurt, and specialty cheeses.

At its plant in Chateaugay, New York, Agri-Mark manufactures some 20 different McCadam-brand cheeses, from muenster to Adirondack Reserve Cheddar.

Despite a downturn in the economy in recent years, Agri-Mark and its leading Cabot and McCadam brands have remained resilient. Its member-owners have also benefited from a healthy wholesale business.

Agri-Mark merged with Cabot Creamery in 1992 and purchased the McCadam Cheese business in 2003. The co-op grew further in 2006, when it consolidated with Allied Federated Cooperatives, an alliance of some 26 dairy cooperatives with 800 member/farmers.
