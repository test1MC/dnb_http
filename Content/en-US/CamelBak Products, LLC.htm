If camels have taught us anything, it's the need for a backpack to carry water. CamelBak, however, is the one credited with inventing the hands-free hydration system. First sold to outdoorsmen in the 1990s, the company's products target two markets: sports-recreation (which include mountain bikers, skiers, snowboarders, runners, hikers) and government-military (soldiers, construction workers, airport tarmac workers). CamelBak is a major supplier of hydration products to the US and foreign-armed forces. In addition to hydration packs, it also peddles BPA-free water bottles, gloves, and electrolyte-rich drink mixes. CamelBak is owned by investment firm Compass Diversified Holdings .

The US is California-based CamelBak's largest market; key international markets include Australia, Canada, France, Germany, Japan, Norway, South Korea, and the UK. Overall, its products are sold in more than 50 countries.

CamelBak supplies its products to some 2,600 specialty and sporting goods retailers (with nearly 10,000 storefronts) such as REI , Dick's Sporting Goods , Whole Foods , and The Sports Authority . It also sells its products in more than 400 military retail exchanges. It markets internationally through a network of some 65 foreign distributors.

In 2014 the company introduced Relay, a water filtration pitcher to filter water at the speed of your faucet. In 2012 the company launched an e-commerce site to sell water bottles and accessories directly to consumers. It plans to expand those offerings to include hydration packs.
