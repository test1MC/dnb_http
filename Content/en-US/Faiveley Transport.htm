Faiveley Transport, a leading maker of railroad equipment, manufactures products used in several major market segments, including high-speed trains, locomotives, passenger cars, metro rail systems, light rail vehicles and trams, and freight cars. The company has three primary product groups: access and information; brakes and safety; and energy and comfort. In addition, Faively provides engineering services. The company's network includes more than 40 sites in about two dozen countries, mainly in Europe but also in North America and Asia. In 2015 the company agreed to be acquired by Westinghouse Air Brake Technologies (doing business as Wabtec) for $1.9 billion.
