PharmaMar (formerly Zeltia) is fishing for a cure for cancer. The company develops tumor-fighting compounds from a library of thousands of marine organisms. Its Yondelis drug (codeveloped with Johnson & Johnson Pharmaceutical Research & Development) has received approval in about 80 countries to treat soft-tissue sarcoma and is being studied for use with other cancers. Other cancer treatment drugs lie in its pipeline. Biotech subsidiaries include Genomica (molecular diagnostics and DNA testing) and Sylentis (therapeutic approaches based on gene silencing). PharmaMar also produces household cleansers, insecticides, and paints. In late 2015 Zeltia was acquired by its subsidiary and took the PharmaMar name.
