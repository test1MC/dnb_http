T&S Brass and Bronze Works manufactures faucets, fittings, valves, and specialty products for customers in the foodservice, industrial, commercial plumbing, and laboratory markets throughout the world. T&S offers lead-free fixtures, as well as environmentally-friendly low-flow products that use less water. The company operates from offices in the US, Europe, and Australia. Its products also are sold by representatives in Asia and South America. T&S Brass and Bronze Works was founded in 1947 as a developer of foodservice plumbing equipment.

The company has its head office in Travelers Rest, South Carolina and second distribution facility in Simi Valley, California. It also has offices and distribution centers in Europe and Asia.

T&S makes and sells faucets, fittings and specialty products and accessories for foodservice, industrial, commercial plumbing and laboratory markets. An industry standard, its products are found in the majority of US hospitals, schools, restaurants, prisons, and public facilities.

The company serves US hospitals, schools, restaurants, prisons, and public facilities. It sells its products via food service, laboratory, and plumbing representatives and distributors, and online.

In 2014 T&S introduced a new product to its already extensive line of hose reels and washdown solutions. The Compact Light-Duty Hose Reel, Model B-7161-C, features a stainless steel cabinet with 6-foot (182.88 cm), durable, reinforced hose, single-stream spray valve and easy-access shut-off valve. That year the company teamed up with Everpure, the world's largest water treatment equipment manufacturer, which agreed to package popular T&S products with a point-of-use EverPure water filtration system.

Boosting its profile, in 2014 T&S launched a new online media room and resource center (T&S Infohub) to provide users with easy access to the company's history and achievements, position papers, latest product information, and news releases.

Underscoring T&S's commitment to helping foodservice professionals find more sustainable and cost-effective solutions, in 2013 the company acquired Chicago-based EnviroPure Systems, Inc., a top developer of alternative environmentally-friendly technologies for organic food waste disposal.
