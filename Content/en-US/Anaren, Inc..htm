Anaren wants to make a big splash with small waves. The company makes microelectronics, microwave components, and assemblies used in space and defense (about two-thirds of sales) and wireless communications applications. Its main products are surface mount signal distribution components (Xinger); resistive products, such as resistors and attenuators; and the Anaren Integrated Radio (AIR) medium-range integrated radio modules, developed cooperatively with Texas Instruments . Lockheed Martin and Raytheon had combined to account for more than one-quarter of Anaren's sales; other customers include Nokia Siemens and Northrop Grumman . Anaren was acquired by private investment company Veritas Capital in early 2014.

The $383 million acquisition by Veritas Capital made Anaren a private company. In buying Anaren, Veritas said it would continue to develop Anaren's technology for the space, communications, and wireless markets.

Anaren's operations are sorted according to the markets it serves. The company's Space & Defense Group includes Anaren Microwave Inc., the MSK product line, and Unicircuit Inc. Its Wireless Group includes Anaren Microwave Inc., Anaren Ceramics Inc., and Anaren Communications.

The company is headquartered in East Syracuse, New York. It has manufacturing facilities in East Syracuse, Littleton, Colorado, Salem, New Hampshire, and and Suzhou, China.
