You can bet the music is turned all the way up at Line 6, a leading maker of electric guitar amplifiers and other professional audio equipment. The company focuses on integrating technology with music products, such as its digital modeling amps that are designed to reproduce the distinctive sounds of a wide range of traditional tube amps and much more. Beyond an array of amps, Line 6 makes and sells Variax guitars, stomp boxes, pedals, and other accessories, as well as professional audio equipment. Its amps are sold in more than 60 countries through major music retailers, such as Guitar Center and Sam Ash , as well as on the company's website. Line 6 was acquired by Japan's Yamaha Corp . in 2014.

Under the terms of the deal, which closed in January 2014, Line 6 will operate as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Yamaha. The Line 6 brand was retained as well as its management team. The purchase expanded Yamaha's portfolio of modeling guitar processing products and professional audio equipment.

The California-based musical instruments and audio equipment manufacturer, operates in the US, as well as in the UK, China, and Japan.

Line 6 had $69 million in sales in 2012.

Line 6 is concentrating on live sound products and expanding its business into new markets through innovation. The company is now led by an executive who has expertise in product development, sales, and marketing at Digidesign, M-Audio, and Avid.

To this end, the maker of music-creation products has been focused on launching new products. Line 6 in 2013 added the new StageSource L2m and L2t loudspeakers to expand its StageSource family with products that provide improved flexibility and portability. Previously, it rolled out its XD line of digital wireless microphone systems. The collection boasts license-free fourth-generation digital wireless technology.
