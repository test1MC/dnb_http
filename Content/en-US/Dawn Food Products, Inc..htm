Not waiting for the sun to come up, Dawn Food Products always has its ovens fired up. The company provides a comprehensive portfolio of partially- and fully-baked grain-based products, such as cakes, muffins, cookies, doughnuts, and artisan breads. For other companies in the baking sector it provides all the fixings for bakery production, including batters, fillings, fruit-bit concentrates, frozen dough, icings, and spreads. Its customers include food manufacturers, food service companies, institutional bakeries, and restaurants. It also manufactures retail bakery goods, including Weight Watchers and Disney products. The company is owned and operated by the founding Jones family.

From its headquarters in Jackson, Mississippi, Dawn Food Products operates 20 manufacturing and a number of distribution facilities worldwide. It boasts more than 30 locations in the US, about 10 locations in Canada, and a handful each in Mexico and international locations in Africa, the Middle East, Asia, Europe, Oceania, and South America. Altogether, the company's reach extends to more than 70 countries.

Dawn Food Products serves more than 40,000 customers, including food manufacturers, food service companies, institutional bakeries, and restaurants. It boasts a portfolio of more than 27,000 products.

Dawn Food Products operates through several business units. Its US Bakery Products business is a top wet, dry, and frozen products manufacturer in the nation's bakery industry. The company's US Bakery Distribution is a leading distributor to the banking industry. Dawn International trades in 70-plus countries, supplying dry blends, flour, fruit fillings, icings and glazes, and frozen products the likes of cakes and muffins. Dawn Food Products' Global Support Unit helps its strategic business units in their business activities.

The company produces more than 4,000 custom, proprietary, and branded products.

Dawn Food Products has more than doubled its net sales during the past decade and now logs more than $1.2 billion a year in revenues.

Dawn Food Products has focused on growing its business. In 2013 the company launched certified gluten-free product line that included two cake bases, two creme cake bases, and a cookie base.

In 2013 Dawn Food Products rolled out DawnFoodInsights.com to provide its in-story bakery customers with a resource to help them make their bakeries a destination for their own clients.

In recent years, the company has also been streamlining its US operations to boost efficiency and cut costs while expanding its footprint in Europe to increase sales.

Dawn Food Products shuttered its operation in Modesto, California, in mid-2012.

It has also inked licensing deals, such as its 2012 agreement with Discovery Communications to create a line of ready-to-sell celebration and dessert cakes inspired by the TLC series Cake Boss .

The company has found success in delivering American-style bakery products overseas. In 2011 Dawn Food Products acquired Royal Cosun 's Unifine Food & Bake Ingredients, which produces pastry ingredients for European bakeries and industrial customers under the Caullet, Dethmers, Fruibel, and Sucrea brand names. The deal hextended the company's global reach, as Unifine has half a dozen production facilities and about 10 sales offices throughout Europe. The addition of Unifine's pastry ingredients also expanded its own offerings for professional customers.

Dawn Food Products was founded by Jones family in 1920.
