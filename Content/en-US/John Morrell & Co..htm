John Morrell is one of the top names in meat. A leading producer of processed-meat and fresh-pork products, John Morrell & Co. makes bacon, fresh pork, hams, and sausage, as well as cold cuts and lunch meats, hot dogs and deli meats. The company's brands include E-Z-Cut, Farmers Hickory, and its flagship John Morrell banner. Most of its products are sold through supermarkets and other retail grocers. John Morrell also sells pork products to convenience stores, foodservice suppliers, restaurants, and other customers in the hospitality industry. Tracing its roots to the UK, where it was founded in 1827, John Morrell is a subsidiary of top US pork producer Smithfield Foods .

John Morrell & Co. is part of Smithfield Foods' Pork Segment alongside Smithfield Packaging and Farmland Foods. The segment's three wholly-owned US fresh pork and packaged meats subsidiaries produce products in the US to market nationwide and in foreign markets. John Morrell claims to be the oldest continuously operating meat producer in the US.

Fresh pork generated 55% of the segment's 2014 sales with packaged meats contributing the remaining 41%.

The Pork Segment, which accounts for about 91% of its parent's revenue, counts some 40 processing plants among its operations. Hogs are processed at nine plants, which combined boast a slaughter capacity of more than 116,000 hogs per day. In fiscal 2014, the segment processed about 28 million hogs. Export sales comprise some 23% of the segment's volumes and about 27% of its revenues.

From its headquarters in Ohio, John Morrell & Co. supplies meat products to the US, China, Japan, Mexico, Russia, and Canada.

John Morrell & Co. serves several sectors, such as retail, deli, foodservice, convenience stores, co-manufacturing outlets, club stores, military outlets, and direct store delivery.

While parent company Smithfield Foods does not break out separate financials for John Morrell & Co., it did report that Packaged Meats, which John Morrell & Co. is part of, contributed about 40% of revenue and that sales increased by 10% for the segment in 2014. That reflected relatively unchanged results from the previous year.

As part of its strategy, John Morrell & Co. retains a mix of both national and regional brands in its products portfolio to drive growth in several key categories.

In recent years, the pork processor has streamlined its operations. It has combined its fresh pork sales with that of another Smithfield subsidiary, Farmland Foods , to reduce costs. In 2015 it opened a $43.5 million distribution center in Ohio.
