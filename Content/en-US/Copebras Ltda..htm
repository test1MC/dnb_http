Phosphates are Copebrás' bread and butter. The company (73%-owned by UK-based minerals giant Anglo American ) produces phosphate-based products, primarily for the agricultural industry. Its products, which include phosphatic fertilizers and phosphoric acid, are used in a multitude of agricultural and cleaning products, and as raw materials for the animal feed industry. Copebrás runs several plants throughout Brazil. Founded as Companhia Petroquímica Brasileira in 1955, the company's original business was producing carbon black, an important raw material used in the manufacture of rubber, plastics, and paints.
