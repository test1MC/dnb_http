If it's a product of the downstream chemical manufacturing process, then China National Chemical Corporation probably makes it. The state-owned company, among China's largest industrial firms, is known as ChemChina, and is divided itself into six units. The largest is Advanced Materials & Specialty Chemicals, followed by Chlor-alkali & Basic Chemical Products, and Agrochemicals. ChemChina also refines petroleum, manufactures rubber and silicone products, and manufactures specialty chemicals. The company operates in 140 countries worldwide. ChemChina is owned by the Government of China.

The company operates through 118 subsidiaries. The largest include China National Bluestar and China National Haohua Chemical (Group) Corporation and other companies affiliated with the former Ministry of Chemical Industry. It operates 24 research and design institutes.

The main business of ChemChina is made up of six segments: New chemical materials; Basic chemical materials; Oil processing; Agrochemicals; Rubber products; and Chemical equipment.

To enhance its technology and increase its market position, ChemChina has developed and implemented an international expansion strategy. It seeks proactive partnerships in both the Chinese and international markets. ChemChina has set up a science and technology division and commission to provide decision-making and technical support for major projects. It founded the ChemChina Science and Technology Awards for organizations and individuals contributing to chemical science R & D, applications, and other activities that popularize advanced science and technology. The award encourages patent inventions.

In 2011 ChemChina acquired a majority stake in Israel-based Makhteshim Agan Industries for $2.4 billion to become the world's sixth largest pesticide producer and dealer.

That year it also acquired Norway-based Elkem , a major metallurgy solar technology company

It was formed in 2004 from the break up of the former Ministry of Chemical Industry and is among the country's largest industrial firms. In 2007 Blackstone paid $600 million for a 20% stake in China National Bluestar.
