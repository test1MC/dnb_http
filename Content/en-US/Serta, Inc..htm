In the competitive mattress-making industry, Serta sleeps with one eye open. The company is one of the nation's top mattress producers, but has seen rival Tempur Sealy leapfrog it in industry rankings. Serta asserts that it's also one of the world's top manufacturers of mattresses. To its benefit, Serta supplies hotels and motels, such as Hilton and Waldorf, with its products. Its Perfect Sleeper line is the nation's best-selling premium mattress; its top-of-the-line collection sells under the Perfect Day name. Founded in 1931, Serta boasts nearly 30 manufacturing facilities in North America. Serta is controlled by the private equity firm Advent International .

From its headquarters in Hoffman Estates, Illinois, Serta boasts half a dozen licensees. National Bedding Company is its largest licensee with 23 manufacturing plants in the US and another four in Canada.

The company sells its products via distributors in more than 150 countries.

Serta and its licensees together are the #1 mattress manufacturers in the US. Serta's product portfolio includes the Perfect Sleeper, which is the Only Official Mattress of the National Sleep Foundation, and the iComfort Sleep System, both named Consumers Digest Best Buys. Serta's brands also include the iSeries Hybrid Sleep System and the exclusive Trump Home and Bellagio at Home mattress collections.

National Bedding Company, LLC is owned and managed by Serta Simmons Bedding, LLC, which also owns Simmons Bedding Company. Under Serta Simmons Bedding's leadership the two companies work together where joint activities will achieve efficiencies, enhance sales or provide better products and services to their respective customers.

Serta partners with hotel groups such as Hilton Worldwide, Marriott International, Intercontinental Hotels Group, Choice Hotels International, Best Western International, Omni Hotels and Resorts, as well as Bellagio Las Vegas.

With the two mattress makers under one ownership umbrella, Serta and Simmons anticipated heating up competition with Tempur Sealy in the US and abroad through licensing deals. While super high-end mattresses garnered much attention in recent years, the housing bust and concurrent downturn in the US economy has bedding manufacturers focused on getting consumers back into stores. To that end, Serta has rolled out promotional and mid-priced lines, such as its Five Star Mattress, that retails from $799 to $1,599. Serta has also been working to update and expand its upscale lines with new features.

To improve customer retention, in 2014 Serta rolled out its latest iComfort gel memory foam and iSeries hybrid mattress collections, new online shopping tools, and launched its VIP Rewards consumer loyalty program

That year The Trump Organization and Serta redesigned the Trump Home iSeries mattress collection, offering a luxurious yet affordable mattresses for shoppers interested in the benefits of gel memory foam, but who prefer the support of a traditional innerspring.

In 2014 Serta launched a new corporate social responsibility program, 'Serta Comforts Our Heroes' through a partnership with Fisher House Foundation. Since 1990, Fisher House Foundation has provided more than 5,200,000 days of lodging to 200,000 families that have loved ones receiving treatments at military and veteran's hospitals. Serta's new program will aim to bring comfort to the growing number of occupants staying at Fisher House locations all over the US

Growing its presence in Canada, in 2015 Serta International Holdco, LLC acquired Western Sleep Products Ltd, a Serta licensee in British Columbia. Western Sleep has been servicing British Columbia and Alberta since 1972.

In 2012 private-equity firm Advent International took control of Serta Simmons Holdings, one of the nation's biggest mattress makers, in a deal valued at about $3 billion. Ares Management and Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan maintained a significant stake in the venture post sale. As part of the deal, Serta and Simmons operate as separate entities.
