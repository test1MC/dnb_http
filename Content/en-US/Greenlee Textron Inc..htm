Greenlee Textron likes to lend a hand (or a power tool) to help others install cable. The company, a subsidiary of Textron , has been selling tools for about 150 years. For much of that time, it focused on woodworking tools; these days its selection includes a wide variety of hand and power tools designed for electrical, maintenance, telecommunications, and plumbing work. Its products include power tools, specialized drill bits, electrical testing and metering equipment, hydraulic pumps, and storage. Greenlee also offers cable pullers, benders, crimpers, and other tools used in wire and cable installation. It markets its products under the Greenlee, Klauke, Paladin Tools, Progressive, and Tempo brands.

The company operates 13 plants in four countries (including the US and China). Almost 50% of its revenues comes from outside the US.

Greenlee is a part of Tools and Test Equipment segment of Textron . The Tools and Test Equipment product line includes products sold by businesses that design and make powered equipment, electrical test and measurement instruments, mechanical and hydraulic tools, cable connectors, fiber optic assemblies, underground and aerial transmission and distribution products, and power utility products. Brands include Greenlee, Greenlee Communications, Greenlee Utility, HD Electric, Klauke, Sherman & Reilly, Rothenberger and Endura.

Greenlee sells its products through home improvement retailers, original equipment manufacturers, and a global distribution network. It also distributes hand and power tools to plumbing and mechanical customers in North America and China through joint ventures. Products are distributed through a global network of sales representatives and distributors and are also sold directly to home improvement retailers and OEMs.

Textron's Tools and Test Equipment segment reported an 11% revenue growth in 2013, primarily due to acquisitions.

In 2015 Greenlee launcheed a new website dedicated to the Greenlee Communications product line. www.greenleecommunications.com allows the company to post content that educates and informs customers about new product capabilities in the telecommunications and broadband markets.

In 2013, Greenlee acquired Sherman & Reilly, a maker of underground and aerial transmission products based in Tennessee in a deal that enhanced its position in the utilities segment. That year it also purchased HD Electric Company, a designer and maker of power utility products.

In 2013, Greenlee extended its hand tool offerings to industrial manufacturing, automotive, and other industries after acquiring a 51% stake in China-based Shanghai Endura Tools Co.

Greenlee became a part of global manufacturer Textron in 1986.

The company was founded in 1866 as Greenlee Brothers & Co.
