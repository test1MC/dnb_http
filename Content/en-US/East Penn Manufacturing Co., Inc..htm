East Penn Manufacturing has charged forward to become one of the US's leading makers of lead-acid batteries for the stationary, industrial, automotive, motive power, commercial, marine, and specialty markets. Under the Deka brand, the company manufactures batteries that provide juice to everything from farm equipment and road signs to vans and snowmobiles. East Penn also makes battery cables, starter cable, terminals, and other accessories. OEM customers have included such noteworthy names as Daimler Trucks North America , Deere & Company , and Harley-Davidson . The company exports its products worldwide.

East Penn has operations in US, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, the Bahamas, and in Central and South America. It has more than 90 warehouses and distribution centers in North America.

The company offers its products globally via East Penn Canada (Canada), East Penn International (China and the entire Asia-Pacific market), Ecoult (Australia), and MK Battery (all seven continents).

East Penn has technical joint ventures and partnerships with Banner GmbH in Austria, Gohner de Mexico, Acumaladores Duncan in Venezuela, Acumuladores Moura S.A. in Brazil, and Exide Industries Limited in India. These joint ventures have expanded the company's reach into some of the fastest-growing global markets.

East Penn sells its products through a network of warehouses in the US and Canada. It has a network of sales representatives throughout North America (including Mexico) for the selling of its Deka brand poser batteries.

East Penn's growth strategy involves expanding its line of batteries and adding space in order to enhance its manufacturing and warehousing capacities to meet the rising demand for its battery products. In 2013 the company invested more than $50 million to construct a 458,000 sq. ft. battery manufacturing plant in Berks County, Pennsylvania. The move allows East Penn to produce batteries that will accommodate the company's growth in strategic areas and markets.

The expansion of wireless telecommunications and data storage companies and their need for battery backup systems has been a boon for the battery industry, and batteries for uninterruptible power supply systems are expected to be a growth area. (The company's wide range of customers helped during a time of sluggish demand within the automotive industry; as a result, the loss of business for East Penn was minimal compared with companies that were heavily dependent on US automotive manufacturers).

In 2013 East Penn launched Deka Fahrenheit, a revolutionary heat-tolerant valve-regulated lead acid monobloc battery.

East Penn was founded in 1946 under the Deka name by DeLight Breidegam Jr. and his father DeLight Sr.
