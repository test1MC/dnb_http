Aesculapius, the god of medicine, won't be unleashing his wrath on this company anytime soon. Aesculap, Inc. is the US-based unit of Aesculap AG, a leading global supplier of surgical instruments, which itself is a subsidiary of German medical device manufacturer B. Braun Melsungen . The company's products include handheld surgical instruments, implants, and sutures used in neurosurgery, as well as in cardiac, orthopedic, laparoscopic, reconstructive, thoracic, and gynecological surgical procedures. Aesculap, Inc. also makes electrosurgical devices and power systems, and it offers consulting, training, maintenance, and supply chain management services.

The global Aesculap organization has operations in about 40 countries in the Americas, Europe, Asia, and the Asia/Pacific region.

In addition to selling its products through the global parent organization's sales and marketing network, Aesculap, Inc. promotes its offerings through distribution partnerships. For instance, the company partners with distribution firm SRI/Surgical Express to provide surgical equipment delivery and retrieval services to hospitals.

The company conducts innovative research and development efforts to expand its product offerings and improve surgical procedures for its customer base. In 2012 Aesculap, Inc. introduced the Caiman 5 device for greater efficiency in sealing and cutting during laparoscopic procedures; it also launched the PL-AGE anterior cervical fusion system for spine surgeries in 2013. Other new offerings serve the cervical, neurosurgery, and microsurgery device markets.

Aesculap also advances its product and service lineup through partnerships. It formed a new product and development partnership with health care IT firm Microsystems in 2013 to sell improved products for disinfection and sterilization.
