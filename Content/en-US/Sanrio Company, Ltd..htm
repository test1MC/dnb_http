A friendly cat called Hello Kitty and a bad-boy penguin tagged Badtz-Maru anchor the lineup of the mega-merchandised world of Sanrio. Its primary business is making and marketing what the firm calls social communication gifts. The company also operates restaurants and a pair of theme parks in Japan, produces movies, and publishes books and magazines -- all based on its multitude of characters. Sanrio licenses or sells thousands of items -- including Hello Kitty stationery, school and desk accessories, clothing, cosmetics, and room décor -- that are sold worldwide. More than 4,000 stores sell the products in the Americas alone, including some 200 Sanrio boutiques. CEO Shintaro Tsuji founded Sanrio in 1960.
