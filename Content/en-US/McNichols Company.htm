McNichols Company manufactures metal products that are full of holes ... by design. Its products include perforated and expanded metals, wire mesh, bar and plank gratings, fiberglass grating, floorings, handrail components, ladder rungs, and mattings. Its perforated metal (aka "hole") products are sold under the brand names Eco-Mesh, Grate-Lock, Perf-O-Grip, Safplate, and Vinylmesh, among many others. The company also provides custom fabrication services. McNichols operates is business through some 20 service centers nationwide. It was founded in 1952 by the late Robert McNichols, grandfather of current president Scott McNichols. The McNichols family runs the firm based on Christian principles.

Tampa, Florida-based McNichols Company operates its business in the US, Mexico, Canada, the Caribbean, and Central and South America.

The company's specialties range from shearing, sawing, notching, banding, and welding to a variety of finishes, including powder coating and galvanizing. McNichols is one of the nation's largest distributors of perforated and expanded metals, wire mesh, bar, plant, and safety gratings, stair treads, matting, and more. The company's architectural products are used on building facades and interiors, including the Tampa Museum of Art, Kaufmann Stadium in Kansas City, and Baltimore's Mercy Medical Center.

McNichols Company reported sales of about $167 million in fiscal 2013 (ended September), and increase of $7 million over the prior year, due to the impact of a single big contract from Burgerfi, a fast-growing hamburger chain, which sourced its metal ceiling panels from the company.
