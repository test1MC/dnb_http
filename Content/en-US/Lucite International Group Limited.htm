Mitsubishi Rayon Lucite (formerly Lucite International) has its roots firmly planted in plastics. The top producer of acrylic monomers (ahead of Dow , Degussa , and Asahi Kasei ) with quarter-share of the world market, Mitsubishi Rayon Lucite has a history that dates back to the discovery of methyl methacrylate (MMA) by an ICI chemist in 1934 and DuPont 's opening of the first US plant three years later. MMA is the main building block of acrylic materials. The company's brand names include Lucite, Perspex, and Lucite Tufcoat. Charterhouse Capital , which owned 78% of Lucite International, first put it up for sale in 2006, meeting with no success. It was bought by a unit of Mitsubishi Chemical Holdings in 2008.

The company has manufacturing, sales, and distribution capabilities across the Europe/Middle East/Africa region and has a presence in the Americas, and the Asia-Pacific region. It has 22 plants at 14 manufacturing sites worldwide and serves customers in more than 100 countries.

Mitsubishi Rayon Lucite divides its business into the production of upstream products (MMAs and specialty methacrylates, or SpMAs) and downstream products (acrylic sheet, specialty polymers, and resins and composites). Its leading brands, Lucite and Perspex, are produced in its downstream operations. Mitsubishi Rayon Lucite is the only MMA producer active in markets around the world.

The company's acrylic products are known for their workmanlike uses in signage, baths and showers, adhesives, electronics, and other commercial and industrial markets. Mitsubishi Rayon Lucite is hoping to branch out into higher profile product areas.

In 2013 the company changed its name from Lucite International Group Limited to Mitsubishi Rayon Lucite Group Limited.
