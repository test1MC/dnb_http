Bajaj Auto makes scooters for commuters. The company makes two- and three-wheel motorcycles used for consumer transportation and light delivery. Models, which include the Avenger, Boxer, Discover, Platina, and Pulsar, are produced at three plants in India. Besides India, where it sells about 2 million motorcycles, key markets for Bajaj Auto are Africa, Latin America, the Middle East, and South and Southeast Asia. Founded in 1926, the company is the flagship firm of Bajaj Group, which also makes home appliances, lighting, and steel, as well as provides finance, insurance, and travel services. Bajaj Auto exports some 1.5 million units to about 60 countries; exports accounted for 42% of fiscal 2014 net sales.
