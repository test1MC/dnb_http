Let it rain! But if it doesn't ... Rain Bird makes watering systems for agriculture, universities, parks, farms, golf courses, residential and commercial landscapes, and sports fields in some 130 countries. The company offers more than 4,000 products, including computer-operated control systems, timers, valves, sprinkler heads, and rotors. Rain Bird keeps the water on at Las Vegas' Bellagio Hotel, Rome's Olympic stadium, and Beijing's Tiananmen Square. Its first patented product in 1935 -- the horizontal-action impact-drive sprinkler head -- launched a new era in efficient irrigation of food crops. Over its history, the company has been awarded more that 130 patents.

Rain Bird has offices spanning more than 20 countries, including manufacturing and assembly facilities in the US (California, Arizona, and Alabama) and additional offices in France, Sweden, and Mexico. The company's products are sold across Africa, Asia, Europe, the Indian Subcontinent, Latin America, the Middle East, North America, and Oceania.

Among its slew of patented products, the company has launched a Rain Curtain irrigation technology for efficient water distribution. Its pressure regulating stem (PRS) rotor sprinkler prevents water loss caused by uneven water pressure. Highlighting convenience, the company offers user-friendly, water-saving automatic sprinkler controllers that work off a battery to irrigate areas that lack a main power supply. Its wireless remote control systems operate irrigation areas from a personal computer. Rain Bird also offers an online calculator that provides customized watering schedules for homeowners.

Rain Bird products are sold domestically and internationally across the five markets of agriculture, consumer, residential contractor, commercial, and golf. Its irrigation products are used to water golf courses, botanical gardens, farms, sports arenas, amusement parks, private homes, and vineyards throughout the US and other countries.

The company's products are sold at major home improvement centers, hardware stores, and by online retailers.

In order to grow its business, the company continues to introduce more convenient, eco-friendly products that respond to every aspect of irrigation and installation. To this end, Rain Bird maintains an indoor irrigation testing center to test product reliability and durability under a range of conditions.

It also makes strategic acquisitions to improve its technology. In 2015 the company partnered with software integration of crop production data provider OnFarm, to integrate its ClimateMinder irrigation management system with the OnFarm crop production decision support software. The integration will allow large agro-growers with multiple sources of information to make the right decisions faster and more accurately.

With a focus on product introduction and innovation, the company launched a range products and products upgrades in 2014 and 2015, including Rain Bird Sub-Surface Emitter Tubing, (drip irrigation); The Rain Bird In-Ground Automatic Sprinkler System; and the new GBS25 Golf Black Solenoid for Rain Bird valve-in-head golf rotors and case assemblies.

In 2014 the company assigned two new golf distributors (Atlantic Irrigation; and Northeast Golf and Turf), covering New York, Connecticut, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, Vermont, New Hampshire, and Maine.

In late 2012 it bought ClimateMinder, a Glendale, California-based company that provided wireless monitoring and control technology used control the costs associated with water and nutrient system applications. The ClimateMinder system automates the collection of data from a variety of sensors and controls the flow and volume of irrigation equipment. From any Web-based device, including cell phones, users can perform a wide variety of tasks including viewing sensor information, defining irrigation schedules, and setting e-mail or text alerts based on real-time sensor readings.

Rain Bird was founded 1933 by Clement and Mary LaFetra.
