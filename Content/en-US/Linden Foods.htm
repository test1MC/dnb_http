Linden Foods does its business between the moo and the meal and the baa and the banquet. The meat processor offers an array of fresh beef, lamb, and veal, with capacity to slaughter and process about 1,800 cattle and 4,000 lambs a week. It serves the UK retail food and foodservice markets, including Marks & Spencer , Musgrave , Booker , RussellHume, and Tesco stores, as well as fast food giant McDonalds . Linden Foods is part of the Linden Food Group, including Slaney Foods, Kettyle Irish Foods, and Irish Country Meats, which is majority owned by the Fane Valley Co-operative Society .

Linden Foods supplies beef and lamb products across the UK, Ireland, and Continental Europe.

The company supplies its retail packs to many of the major High Street supermarkets such as Booker and RussellHume.

Linden Foods targets a broad customer base that spans retail chain stores (as well as their convenience food manufacturers), catering and foodservice operators, and the general meat packing industry.

Its plans to grow include investing in meat plant and equipment. Manufacturing improvements enable the meat processor to cut costs and increase its lineup from whole carcasses and retail packs to individual portions of steak cuts and other specialty meats, such as extra-matured beef and gourmet burgers and veal. Among its introductions, Linden foods launched Banquet Royale Rose Veal, an alternative to traditional white veal for restaurants demanding quality coupled with ethical rearing and finishing of calves.

Linden Foods is the result of the 1999 merger of two of Northern Ireland's larger red-meat processors and exporters, Granville Meats and Milltown Livestock & Meat.
