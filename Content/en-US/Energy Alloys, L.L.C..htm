Grabbing a fistful of steel is all in a day's work for employees of Energy Alloys. The company provides oilfield metals and equipment to global oil and gas manufacturers and service companies. Energy Alloys stocks oilfield-specific inventories of carbon, alloy, stainless steel, and nickel bars, as well as tubes and engineered steel products. It also offers made-to-order machining capability for finished and semi-finished components and expert materials consulting when clients need help selecting materials, material grades, or sizes. The company has international sourcing expertise and is a global supplier.

Energy Alloys has operations in Canada, Mexico, Singapore, South Korea, the UAE, the UK (Arbroath, Rotherham, Bellshill, and Sheffield), and the US (Houston, Tulsa, Lafayette, and Houma).

Energy Alloys' Advanced Tubulars meet demanding Oilfield Tubular requirements, supplying corrosion-resistant tubulars for high-pressure, high-temperature wells and high-performance drilling riser main tube and auxiliary lines.

The company keeps about 8,000 distinct sizes and grades of materials in stock.

Energy Alloys is an oilfield metals provider, partner, and problem-solver for global oil and gas manufacturers and service companies. Customers include big names in the industry such as Baker Hughes , Halliburton , National Oilwell Varco , and Weatherford International .

To meet the needs of its customers globally Energy Alloys has expanded its operations to Canada, England, Scotland, Singapore, and Mexico. While acquisitions have helped expand the company's geographic footprint, Energy Alloys has also grown its operations organically, adding offices in Canada and expanding its services in the Middle East and Asia.

In 2013 Energy Alloys Pte Ltd in Singapore contracted Boustead Projects Pte Ltd to design, build, and lease an integrated manufacturing and distribution facility in that country.

In 2012 Energy Alloys relocated its corporate headquarters from Houston to a 37,000 sq. ft. space at 3 Waterway Square, a new 232,000 sq. ft. office building under construction in The Woodlands Town Center.

In 2011 the company expanded its Singapore operations and consolidated its Houston warehouses into one service center. In 2010 it opened an service and distribution center in Nuevo Leon in Mexico, and a service and distribution center in South Korea.

Energy Alloys has experienced rapid growth, buying a number of companies in the 2000s, including Sunbelt Steel, Southwest Energy Metals, and Ryerson's distribution assets in the US, and units of IMS UK and Van Leeuwen Wheeler in the UK.

Energy Alloys was founded in 1995 by Dave Warren, Ralph Mullens, and Robert Pond.
