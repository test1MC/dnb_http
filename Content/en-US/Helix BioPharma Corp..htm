Helix BioPharma discovers, develops, and seeks licensing for pharmaceuticals to prevent and treat cancer. The company's drug candidates include treatments for female conditions such as human papillomavirus (HPV), a precursor to cervical cancer; common forms of lung cancer, and potential therapies for breast and prostate cancers. To help fund its drug discovery operations, Helix BioPharma distributes pharmaceuticals and other products for conditions such as gastrointestinal, orthopedic, and immune system disorders in Canada through subsidiary Rivex Pharma. However, facing insufficient funding, in 2012 the company halted development efforts and began exploring strategic alternatives.
