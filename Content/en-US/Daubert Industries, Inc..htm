For Daubert Industries, metal health is the top concern. Its three subsidiaries protect metals by different methods. Daubert Cromwell makes VCI (volatile corrosion inhibitors) protective coatings for almost every metal -- ferrous, nonferrous, and multi-metal. Its transparent coatings protect metal that is stored or shipped until it is unwrapped and the coatings break down. ECP (Entire Car Protection) offers paints, carpet dyes, detailing products, and under-the-hood cleaners for automobiles. Daubert Chemical focuses on developing corrosion prevention, industrial anti-skid, and sound-deadening coatings. Daubert Industries was formed in 1935 by George Daubert, whose decendants still control the company.
