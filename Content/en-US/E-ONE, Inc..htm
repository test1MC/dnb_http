Where there's smoke there's fire, and where there's fire, there's E-ONE. E-ONE (also known as Emergency One) is a leading maker of fire and rescue vehicles. The company manufactures pumpers, tankers, aerial ladders and platforms, industrial trucks, and aircraft rescue firefighting equipment. E-ONE operates facilities in Florida and New York, and it sells through a network of some 40 dealers in 80 countries. Its dealers also provide service and maintenance for E-One vehicles. E-One is part of Allied Specialty Vehicles, which is owned by private equity firm American Industrial Partners.

The company operates facilities in Ocala, Florida, and Hamburg, New York.

Customers include Chevron , City of Byron, City of Edmunston, Clay FireTerritory, Cordova Fire & Rescue, NORTH AMHERST VOL FIRE, Township of Sparta, and Township of West Windsor. The company offers its products through dealers such as A2Z Fire Apparatus and West Shore Fire worldwide.

E-ONE was founded in 1974 and soon it introduced the first modular, all-aluminum fire truck chassis. Federal Signal purchased the company five years later. Federal Signal owned E-One until 2008, when it sold the company to American Industrial Partners.
