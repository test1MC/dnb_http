Go ahead and give SCHOTT Solar a shot - they may be able to serve your solar power needs. The company develops, manufactures, and markets components for photovoltaic (PV) applications and solar energy plants, which are made in the US of A. Through two business divisions -- Photovoltaics (PV) and Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) -- it produces crystalline silicon wafers, cells, and modules, as well as receivers, which are key components in large-scale power plants that use solar energy to generate electricity. SCHOTT Solar is the US arm of SCHOTT Solar AG in Germany. Both are subsidiaries of SCHOTT AG .

The company was formerly a joint venture of multi-services utility company RWE Group and glass manufacturer SCHOTT AG . The SCHOTT Group bought out RWE's stake in 2005.

In 2005 SCHOTT Solar was one of a handful of companies awarded contracts by the Chinese government for bringing electricity to more than 25 villages in the Gansu province. The project is part of the government's plan of bringing solar "minigrids" to remote areas of the country.

In 2006 the company introduced a lead- and arsenic-free P-SF67 glass to North American manufacturers of compact lens systems, for use in items such as cell phones and cameras.

In 2007 SCHOTT Solar and Wacker Chemie established two joint ventures to make and sell solar-grade silicon wafers. The JVs were to manufacture multicrystalline silicon ingots and wafers, the basic materials for solar cell manufacturing. The JV partners invested €370 million for two production facilities in Germany.
