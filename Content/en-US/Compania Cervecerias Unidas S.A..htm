When Chileans say "mas cerveza, por favor," they're usually asking for Cristal, Escudo, or another beer made by Compañía Cervecerías Unidas (CCU). The company, also known by its English name, United Breweries, manufactures Córdoba beer in Argentina. In addition CCU makes wine, mineral water, rum, pisco (a grape spirit favored by Chileans), and soft drinks; it is Chile's bottler of PepsiCo drinks. The company also distributes Heineken , Corona , Negra Modelo , and Guinness beer brands, as well as RedBull and Absolut Vodka . CCU also operates in Uruguay and Paraguay.

In 2003, Compañía Cervecerías Unidas formed Viña Tabalí, a joint venture in equal parts with Sociedad Agrícola y Ganadera Río Negro for the production of premium wines. The winery is located in the Limarí Valley, Chile's northernmost winemaking region, which is noted for the production of outstanding wines.

The following year, the company formed a 50-50 joint venture with Quiñenco, owner of Calaf and Francisca Calaf, makers of traditional Chilean candy. Further expanding its product offerings, in 2006, it entered into a 50-50 joint venture agreement with Watt's, a Chilean fruit-product company, to form Promarca, which sells fruit juices and fruit and dairy-based beverages under the Watt's, Ice Frut de Watt's, Yogu Yogu, and Shake a Shake brands in Chile.
