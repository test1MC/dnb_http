Founded by a man of the cloth, Robert B. Pamplin, Pamplin makes denim cloth and a lot more. The family-owned conglomerate has operations ranging from entertainment to retail stores to manufacturing interests (asphalt, concrete, and textiles). The company's Mount Vernon Mills is one of the top denim producers in the US. Pamplin's entertainment concerns include radio broadcasting, and newspapers serving the northwestern US. Other units are as diverse as retail stores and Columbia Empire Farms, which grows berries and grapes used in jams and wine; it also raises cattle.

Pamplin employs people in Alabama, Georgia, Mississippi, North Carolina, Oregon, South Carolina, Texas, and Washington and sells its products worldwide. The Portland Tribune serves in Beaverton, Lake Oswego, Prineville, Clackamas, Madras, Sandy, Canby, Milwaukie, Sellwood, Columbia Co., Molalla, Sherwood, Estacada, Newberg, Tigard, Forest Grove, Oregon City, Tualatin, Gresham area, Portland, West Linn, King City, Portland SE, Wilsonville, Hillsboro, Portland SW, and Woodburn.

Pamplin's Mount Vernon Mills is a denim producer. The company runs several broadcast, print, and web communications companies serving the Oregon area. Pamplin Communications Corporation includes the Oregon Publication Corporation's The Portland Tribune newspaper, and 25 community newspapers as well as the Pamplin Broadcasting Corporation's radio stations -- KPAM and KKOV.

It also owns four construction companies that serve the Pacific Northwest: B. Gentle Concrete Construction, Ross Island Sand & Gravel,K.F. Jacobsen & Co., and Pacific Northwest Aggregates.

It also owns four restaurants in the Portland area (Bugatti's: three casual dining and its original fine dining ristorante in the West Linn area) and farms hazelnut orchards, marionberry fields, honeycombs, and vineyards (and raises alfalfa and cattle) through Columbia Empire Farms.

In 2013 Pamplin grew its regional publishing business, buying seven community newspapers from Salem-based Eagle Newspapers: Canby Herald, Central Oregonian, Madras Pioneer, Molalla Pioneer, Newberg Graphic, Wilsonville Spokesman, and Woodburn Independent , boosting its total number of regional newspapers to 25. It introduced the Hillsboro Tribune in 2012.

The company was started in 1957 by ordained minister and company chairman and CEO Robert Pamplin Jr.
