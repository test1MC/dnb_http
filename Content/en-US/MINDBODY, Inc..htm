The goal of many fitness programs is to help the mind and body work together. The goal of Mindbody Inc. is to help fitness and wellness companies integrate front and back office functions into a smooth operation. The company offers a Software-as-a-Service platform for small businesses gathered under the wellness umbrella (yoga, massage, fitness centers, pilates, spin-cycling studios, spas, salons, and more) to schedule appointments and manage billing and payments. Mindbody counts more than 42,000 local business subscribers. It went public with a stock offering in June 2015.

Mindbody raised $100 million in an initial public stock offering in June 2015. The company, founded in 2001, plans to use the money to increase its financial flexibility, general corporate activities, working capital and acquisitions. The stock was priced at $14 a share. It rose to more than $16 a share during the first day of trading before closing at $11.56 per share. The company has received rounds of venture capital over the years, including $50 million in 2014. Investors included W Capital Partners, Montreux Equity Partners, Bessemer Venture Partners, Institutional Venture Partners, and Catalyst Investors.

Mindbody has developed mobile applications consumers and corporations. The company's Connect platform lets customers of wellness and fitness providers book and pay for fitness programs and find other providers using the Mindbody platform. For corporate customers, MindBody's Connect Workplace app helps employees find fitness and wellness providers and pay for them over the platform with Mindbody branded debit cards.

Mindbody is a subscription-based service. Pricing had been based on the number of service providers employed by a subscribing company. But in 2015, Mindbody began basing subscription pricing for new subscribers on software functionality. Most of Mindbody's customers prepay their monthly subscriptions. The company also gets revenue based on the value of transactions processed by subscribers on Mindbody's payment platform, net of the costs charged to Mindbody by its processing vendors. More than $4 billion in transactions were processed over the platform in 2014.

Mindbody is based in San Luis Obispo, California and has sales offices in New York, the UK, and Australia. It has subscribers in 124 countries.

Mindbody has a direct sales force that qualifies and manages prospective and current subscribers. Once a sale is made, sales people work to retain customers and expand their use of the company's services. The company targets small and medium-sized businesses in the wellness services industry. The company's definition includes barre, personal training, martial arts, dance exercise, spas, salons, music instruction studios, dance studios, children's activity centers, and integrative health centers. Clients include New York Health & Racquet Club, YogaWorks, Pure Barre, and Pure Hong Kong.

As a relatively young company, Mindbody doesn't seem concerned with integrating revenue and profit quite yet. Its revenue has been on the rise, hitting $70 million in 2014, a 44% increase from 2013. The company gained about 9,500 subscribers in 2014 (finishing the year with more than 40,000). Monthly revenue per customer rose to $115 from $146.

The company posted a net loss of $24.6 million for 2014, compared to a net loss of $16.2 million in 2013. Mindbody's costs for sales and marketing, research and development, and general corporate expenses increased in 2014.

In 2015, Mindbody acquired Fitness Mobile Apps, a mobile app that was created on the Mindbody platform. The acquisitions helps Mindbody expand its consumer mobile capabilities for customers keep pace with clients who rely on mobile devices.
