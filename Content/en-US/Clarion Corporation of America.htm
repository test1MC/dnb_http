Clarion Corporation of America wants your car stereo to be loud and Clarion. The company markets and distributes electronic entertainment, communications, security, and navigation products for automobiles and boats to retailers, distributors, and OEMs including BMW , Ford , Honda , and Subaru . The company's products include amplifiers, car stereos, mobile CB radios, navigation and computing systems, and satellite radios. It also offers specialty mobile electronics for the recreational vehicle, motorcycle, marine, and personal spa industries. Established in 1967, Clarion Corporation of America is the US sales and marketing subsidiary of Japan-based electronics manufacturer Clarion Co ., part of the Hitachi Group .

As traditional car stereo systems evolve to become sophisticated infotainment and navigation centers, Clarion Corporation of America (CCA) has kept up by offering cloud connectivity and access to smartphone apps and services via the vehicle head unit. Indeed, in 2014 CCA partnered with automaker Subaru of America to introduce the Subaru STARLINK infotainment system. STARLINK, co-developed by CCA and Subaru, supports both Apple iPhone and Android smartphones. Clarion's cloud-based Smart Access platform is available in all 2015 model year Subaru Legacy and Outback models. Clarion in 2012 launched its cloud-based information network for vehicles and quickly began releasing devices designed to allow customers to integrate mobile devices and Clarion systems.

As the company moves to accommodate apps and mobile devices, it literally moved closer to that world when it opened an office in Silicon Valley in mid-2013. Shortly thereafter, CCA said it would begin using fellow valley dweller Google 's mapping technology and voice search capabilities.

Clarion Corporation of America has offices in California, Michigan, Kentucky, Ohio, and Tennessee.
