Big Ass Fan Company, the name says it all. Delta T Corporation, doing business as Big Ass Fans, makes six-to-24 foot diameter ceiling fans. The size of the fans allow them to move massive amounts of air over large spaces while the foils (or blades) moves at a slow to moderate speed. Applications for the company's oversized products include environmental control (circulating hot or cold air) in immense spaces such as warehouses, manufacturing facilities, retail spaces, institutional buildings, or athletic stadiums. The company was founded in 1999.

The company has its US office in Lexington, Kentucky, and international offices in Australia, Canada, Hong Kong, Malaysia, and Singapore. Internationally its products are sold in more than 125 countries.

Delta T serves the agriculture, aviation, commercial/public spaces, education, fitness, government, industrial, and motor vehicle sectors; homes; and architects and engineers. Its customers include such big names as Costco, Ikea, Planet Fitness, Starbucks, Target, Home Depot, Lowe's, Whole Foods, Coca-Cola, PepsiCo, Toyota, Tesla, Delta, American Airlines, and United Airlines.

In order to grow, Delta T often launches new products and operations to corner niche markets. As such, the company in 2016 launched a new division, Haiku Home, to cater to residential home customers. In 2015 the company expanded its reach into Canada by opening an office outside Toronto.
