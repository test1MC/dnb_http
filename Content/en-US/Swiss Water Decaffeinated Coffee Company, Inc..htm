Guess what the Swiss Water Decaffeinated Coffee Company (SWDCC) makes? (Hint: it isn't Swiss water.) The Canadian company makes coffee of the decaffeinated variety. Using a chemical-free process pioneered in Switzerland in the 1930s, it uses water to remove caffeine from coffee. SWDCC, which was spun off by Kraft 's General Foods unit in 2000, distributes its products to licensed vendors in the US, Canada, Europe, Australia, and Asia. The company is owned by the Swiss Water Decaffeinated Coffee Income Fund. (The company makes the coffee; the Income Fund's shares trade on the Canadian stock exchange.)
