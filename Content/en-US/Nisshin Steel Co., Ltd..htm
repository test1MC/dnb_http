Nisshin Steel (formerly Nisshin Steel Holdings) is a heavyweight in Japan's steel-making industry. Nisshin Steel manufactures coated, cold- and hot-rolled, stainless, and specialty steel. The company's customers are in the automotive, construction, electronics, and machinery industries. Nisshin Steel has locations and venture interests in China, Malaysia, Singapore, and Thailand, as well as in the US through a number of operational subsidiaries, including Nisshin Automotive Tubing and Wheeling-Nisshin . The company's domestic customers, however, account for the majority of sales.

Nisshin Steel is headquartered in Tokyo and has offices across Japan. It also has presence in Singapore, Thailand, the US, and China. Japan accounted for 81% of the company's total revenues in fiscal 2014 (March year end); East Asia, 8%; North America, 7%; and other regions, 4%.

Steel operations are central to the Nisshin Steel's business development. From raw materials procurement to steel products deliveries, its companies specialize in every stage of steel product creation, including manufacturing, processing and sales. Steel products accounted for 96% of the company's revenues in fiscal 2014.

Nisshin Steel produces 150 types of steel. It offers a variety of coated steel sheets with special post-treatments containing no environmentally-unfriendly hexavalent and other chromium compounds.

The company's subsidiaries include Nippon Metal Industry Co., Ltd, Nisshin A&C Co., Ltd., Nisshin Kokan Co., Ltd., and Shinwa Kigyo Co., Ltd.

Nisshin Steel sells its products though its offices and subsidiaries, and via third parties.
