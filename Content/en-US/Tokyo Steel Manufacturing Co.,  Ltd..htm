Tokyo Steel Manufacturing has built a business out of scraps. Founded in 1934 as a minor player in pre-war Japan's steel scraps recycling industry, Tokyo Steel is today a major manufacturer of steel products. It makes basic long steel products used largely in public works projects, as well asÂ steel plates for the automobile industry, H-beams, and coils. Tokyo Steel operates five mills spread throughout the country. Tokyo Steel agreed to sell its share of the TAMCO steel mini-mill joint venture to Gerdau Ameristeel in 2010 for about $40 million. Its other partners, Ameron and Mitsui & Co. , also sold their shares in the project to Gerdau.
