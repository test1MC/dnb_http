While the cows that supply Garelick Farms with milk have their hooves planted firmly on the ground, the dairy products maker does produce Over the Moon milk. Along with its lunar-leaping white milk, the company makes TruMoo chocolate-, coffee-, and strawberry-flavored milk. It also offers butter, cream, half and half, buttermilk, egg nog, bottled iced tea and juices under the Garelick label. A subsidiary of the nation's largest milk bottler, Texas-based Dean Foods , Garelick's customers include retail food stores and foodservice operators in upstate New York and New England. Garelick operates production facilities in Massachusetts and New York.

Headquartered in Massachusetts, Garelick sources its milk from more than 1,000 local farmers with non-GMO dairy operations in Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New York, Rhode Island, and Vermont. It has production facilities in Franklin and Lynn, Massachusetts, and in Rensselaer, New York.

Citing competitive pressure, the company in 2012 closed its production facility in Bangor, Maine, which primarily produced fluid milk.

The company's Over the Moon brand milk is a low-fat product with added calcium and protein, which the company claims makes it richer and creamier than other low-fat and fat-free milks.

Garelick Farms is part of its parent company's Fresh Dairy Direct business.
