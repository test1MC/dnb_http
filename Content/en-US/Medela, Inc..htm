Medical suction technology specialist Medela, Inc., is the US arm of Swiss firm Medela Holding AG . Its Medela Breastfeeding US division distributes hospital-grade and home-use breast pumps, bottles and accessories, and nursing and maternity intimate apparel. The same division also distributes phototherapy lamps and neonatal beds for newborns with jaundice. The Medela Healthcare US division distributes Medela's medical vacuum systems, negative pressure wound care devices, and other suction equipment for hospital use.

The Medela-branded breast pumps are an industry standard and are used by about 80% of US hospitals.

Hospital products are supplied to medical facilities directly and through distributors, while Medela, Inc.'s home-use products are sold through medical supply retailers and specialty shops (including online stores).

In 2014, Medela acquired the enteral feeding assets of California-based Acacia, expanding its presence in the neonatal intensive care unit (NICU) market.

The parent entity, Medela Holding, was formed in Switzerland in 1961 and is owned by the founding Larsson family. The global firm entered the US market in the 1980s.
