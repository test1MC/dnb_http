Business is piling up for Nucor-Yamato Steel. The joint venture between Nucor Corporation (51%) and Yamato Kogyo produces structural steel shapes, such as carbon steel wide-flange beams, H-piling, sheet piling, and standard I-beams. Manufactured in Arkansas at the largest structural steel mill in the Western Hemisphere. Annual production capacity at the mill is more than 2.5 million tons of finished steel products. Nucor-Yamato Steel supplies steel products to steel service centers and companies in the fabrication, construction, and manufacturing industries.

The company's products are made at its plant in Blytheville, Arkansas, and are sold throughout the US and exported worldwide.

Nucor-Yamato Steel's steel mill in Blythesville, Arkansas produce steel products used in a range of construction projects. It has two rolling facilities (Mill 1 and Mill 2). Mill 1 rolls channel, angle, small wide flange and H-Pile, sheet piling, and special sections. Mill 2 produces large wide flange and H-pile sections. Together, the mills combined have an annual finished product capacity of 2.4 million tons.

The plant uses twin 120 ton electric arc furnaces, two ladle furnaces, and two continuous casters and requires a hot metal supply of about 2.8 million tons per year.

Nucor-Yamato Steel's sheet and H-piling products are used in bridge and highway construction, heavy civil construction, marine construction, and underground parking and environmental containment projects.

In 2012 the joint venture approved a $115 million plan to expand production of hot rolled sheet piling. The project is scheduled for completion in 2014.

Completion of the expansion will add several new sheet piling sections and will increase the single sheet widths by 22% to produce a stronger sheet. The new sections will also provide piling distributor customers a broader range of American-made piling products. The project also is in line with Nucor-Yamato Steel's strategy for building its piling and foundation products, which are serviced by its Skyline Steel and Harris Rebar businesses.

In 2012, German plant maker SMS Meer revamped Nucor-Yamato Steel's No. 1 Steel rolling mill to enables the rolling of wider sheet piling products. The revamp included the installation of three-stand finishing mill and two more stands.

Nucor-Yamato Steel was founded in 1987.
