Evonik Corporation is the North American arm of German chemical giant, Evonik Industries . The specialty chemicals company operates through six divisions: Advanced Intermediates, Inorganic Materials, Consumer Specialties, Health and Nutrition, Coatings and Additives, and Performance Polymers. Products range from amino acids and building protection coatings to another of its specialties, specialty acrylic products including its best known brand, Plexiglas. In addition to the automotive industry, Evonik serves makers of coatings, pharmaceuticals, and plastics.

North America is one of several home bases for Evonik's chemicals business, which produce hydrogen peroxide, silicas, interfacial chemistry, superabsorbents, feed additives, iso-phorone, and high-performance polymers.

The company's 43 North American operations include units in Canada and Mexico, as well as acrylics maker CYRO Industries . Evonik's products are used by makers of everything from toothpaste to concrete to dietary supplements. The diverse array of specialty chemicals produced by the company is also used in oil and gas exploration and the manufacture of paints and coatings.

In addition, to its six divisions, its Multi-Business Unit Site based in Mobile, Alabama, has developed a comprehensive single-stream recycling program, increasing recycled materials by approximately 200 tons.

With an annual name plate capacity of 245,000 metric tons, Evonik's Advanced Intermediates business unit is North America's largest producer of the eco-friendly bleaching and oxidizing agent hydrogen peroxide, which is used primarily in paper and pulp bleaching. The heart of the Performance Polymers Business Unit includes both methylmethacrylate chemistry and integrated production facilities for polyamides. The core capabilities of the Inorganic Materials Business Unit are particle design, surface chemistry, and process technology for the production of chlorosilanes, organosilanes and silicas. The Coatings and Additives unit focuses on the adhesives and sealants, and lubricants industries, as well as the fast-growing pharmaceutical market at sites in Deer Park, Texas; Mobile, Alabama; and Morrisburg, Ontario.

The Consumer Specialties Business Unit is a supplier of high-quality raw materials, additives and active ingredients for a variety of applications in the areas of skin and hair care, detergents, and special cleaners. The Health and Nutrition Business Unit makes and markets amino acids for animal and human nutrition as well as active pharmaceutical ingredients, excipients and drug delivery technologies for the pharmaceuticals industry.

Headquartered in Parsippany, New Jersey, Evonik has two large plants in Mobile, Alabama, and Lafayette, Indiana, more than 28 additional production sites, and distribution centers in across North America (including Mexico). Its global R&D network comprises about 35 locations.

Evonik serves customers in a range of industries, including Agriculture, Automotive, Transportation and Machinery, Metals and Mining, Oil and Gas, Pharma and Healthcare, Printing, Paints and Coatings, Pulp, Paper and Packaging, Plastics and General Industry, Environment and Utilities, Care Food, Feed and Beverages, and Construction.

The parent company derives about 20% of its revenue from North America.

Evonik has established a strong position in the North American coatings market with resource-efficient and environmentally-friendly products.

To take advantage of growing demand, in 2016 Evonik announced plans to build a plant for precipitated silica in South Carolina to supply the tire industry. To help it meet challenging CAFE standards, the US automotive industry and Evonik are looking to high-quality precipitated silica in order to produce fuel-efficient tires with good wet road holding properties.

In 2015 the company increased capacity at its sodium methylate plant in Mobile, Alabama, to 72,000 metric tons.

Growing its portfolio of US assets, in 2016 Evonik acquired the silica business of JM Huber for $630 million. The purchase of Huber's consumer goods silica applications (such as dental products) complements Evonik's focus on industrial applications.

In 2014 Evonik acquired Evonik Goldschmidt Corporation, a leading provider of specialty surfactants, following which Evonik Goldschmidt was fully integrated into Evonik.

In 2014, the company's Inorganic Materials business unit announced new agreements with The Cary Company and Dorsett & Jackson, to broaden distribution of its silica products in the US Midwest and Western regions, respectively. The Cary Company deal also covers all of Iowa, Illinois, Missouri, Kansas and Nebraska. The agreement includes ACEMATT Matting Agents, SIPERNAT and SIDENT Precipitated Silica, AEROSIL Fumed Silica, AEROXIDE Mixed Metal Oxides, and AERODISP Dispersions.
