Less is more at the Carl Buddig & Company, which makes and markets some of the thinnest lunchmeats available. One of the largest-selling brands in lunchmeats, its Original Buddig brand is a svelte 10 to 15 calories per slice and comes in choices such as beef, chicken, turkey, ham, pastrami, and corned beef. It also offers the Old Wisconsin brand of smoked beef jerky sticks and snack bites, along with wieners, bratwurst, Polish sausage, summer sausage, and ring bologna. Carl Buddig's products are available in the US, Puerto Rico, and Canada, and are sold at retail food outlets, including Kroger , Albertson's , Safeway , Food Lion , Giant , Roundy's , and others. It's owned by the founding Buddig family.

The company, based in Illinois, sells its meat products throughout the US, Canada, and Puerto Rico.

Carl Buddig markets its lunchmeats through US and Canadian foodservice operators and retailers (including Puerto Rico), such as Albertson's , Kroger , Safeway , Food Lion , Giant , and Roundy's .

Besides its primary Carl Buddig business, the company operates subsidiary Old Wisconsin Sausage.

Carl Buddig is the fifth-largest-selling brand of sliced lunchmeat. It makes vacuum-packed, pre-weighed portioned packages of lunchmeats in several varieties, such as turkey, chicken, ham, and beef.

The company entered the convenience category in recent years to extend the reach of its portfolio. For people on the go, the company makes individual Fix Quix packs, featuring Buddig meats that are cubed and ready to be added to salads or pasta. And for another kind of convenience, the company offers its Buddig Original products in resealable containers and its deli products in zippered plastic packages.

The company was founded by Carl Buddig, who opened a small butcher shop in 1886.
