Safe days and pipelines turn into paydays and bottom lines for Hill & Smith Infrastructure Products Group (IPG). IPG is a family of more than 30 businesses that provide a range of engineered products primarily for the transportation and utility markets. For roads and buildings IPG supplies barriers, fencing, bridge parapets, and other safety fixtures, as well as other related products, such as signs, modular car parks, and lighting columns. IPG also provides plastic drainage pipes and pipe supports for power, liquefied natural gas (LNG), water, and petrochemical plants. UK-based IPG represents about 71% of the revenues of its parent, Hill & Smith Holdings .

The parent group has subsidiaries in the UK, China, France, Sweden, Thailand, and the US.

The company is part of the Infrastructure segment of Hill & Smith Holdings.

IPG provides road infrastructure products and rail infrastructure.

In 2014 the Infrastructure segment contributed 71% of the parent company's total revenues in 2014.

IPG has been expanding internationally, especially in Scandinavia, a region where highway construction has been robust, to compensate for lower demand in the UK. The Brifen unit gained more business when the parent company bought out the business of licensed representatives in Australia and Sweden. IPG's utilities businesses have been focusing on the development of new power generation facilities in emerging markets and the upgrading of old facilities in developed markets. These businesses have recorded strong orders for power plants in India; LNG terminals in Australia, Singapore, and Papua New Guinea; and a nuclear reprocessing plant in the US.

In 2011 IPG acquired Sweden-based ATA Bygg-och Markproduckter, which supplies road safety barriers and signage, and a US-based company, The Paterson Group, which provides pipe supports and hangers for the power generation, commercial, and industrial sectors.
