Boasting one of the nation's top five refrigerated salad dressings, Naturally Fresh sells most of its products in iconic Mason jars. The company's a maker and distributor of Naturally Fresh-branded salad dressings, dips, sauces, marinades, and other condiments. It also makes salad toppings and the Jackaroo brand of meat sauce for restaurants. Naturally Fresh produces, packages, and delivers its products to grocery stores, quick-serve operations, casual- and fine-dining restaurants, and foodservice operators. The company operates some 20 distribution centers throughout the US. Founded in 1966 as Eastern Foods, Naturally Fresh was acquired in 2012 by TreeHouse Foods for $25 million in cash.

Naturally Fresh, purchased for $26 million, has enabled TreeHouse Foods to expand its manufacturing and packaging capacities, extend its distribution footprint, and strengthen its presence in the growing fresh foods category.

Based in Atlanta, Naturally Fresh provides its refrigerated dressings, sauces, marinades, dips, and specialty items to customers across the US.

The food maker's processing facility is located in Atlanta. It operates seven distribution centers nationwide.

Naturally Fresh serves several sectors, including casual- and fine-dining restaurants, foodservice operators, grocery stores, and quick-serve operations. Customers include HEB , Safeway , Whole Foods , Meijer , and Piggly Wiggly , among others.

The Naturally Fresh brand name is affixed to dressings, sauces, and dips. It's used in both the North American retail grocery and Food Away From Home segments of the industry.

Now under the TreeHouse Foods umbrella, Naturally Fresh's dressings and condiments are part of the TreeHouse products portfolio.

Before the TreeHouse deal, the condiments manufacturer had been working to diversify its revenue through a partnership with restaurant chains, such as its deal with Hooters of America . Hooters enlisted the help of Naturally Fresh in 2010 to make a line of Hooters-branded salad dressings. Consisting of Bleu Cheese and Ranch varieties, the dressings are sold in supermarket produce sections. Naturally Fresh is looking to develop additional restaurant-branded products for the retail market.
