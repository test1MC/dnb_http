Perhaps no force could breathe more ch'i into the Old Country than Hitachi Europe. The UK-based Hitachi subsidiary manufactures, markets, and distributes an array of consumer and business goods, through seven business segments: power and industrial systems; industrial components and equipment; smart cities; nuclear power; information systems; digital media and consumer products; and procurement and sourcing. For consumers Hitachi Europe sells home appliances, electronics, and travel and finance services. Business offerings are IT hardware, software, electronic devices/semiconductors, and system services, both medical and power.

The company has offices in 13 countries across Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. Its research and development laboratories are located in Cambridge, Munich, Paris, and Sophia-Antipolis (France).

Hitachi Europe operates through seven main business areas; power and semiconductor systems, digital media and consumer products, industrial components and equipment, procurement and sourcing, smart cities, nuclear power, and information systems.

The company's customers include Ministry of Health Turkey, Land & Water Group Plant Hire, Hilton Istanbul House of Waterford Crystal, and Bank BPH SA.

Financial Performance

Parent company Hitachi got 8% of its fiscal 2015 revenues from Europe.

Hitachi Europe is focused on expanding its significant presence of four strategic sectors (Power Systems, Rail Systems, Construction Machinery & Enterprise Storage System) through organic growth.

In 2014 the company launched a newly designed AT200 commuter train. It also opened a new R&D base in the UK, the European Nuclear Research Centre (ENRC). The facility will coordinate Hitachi's nuclear research activities in Europe and provide additional resources to facilitate the development of safe and efficient nuclear power technologies in Europe.

Boosting its IT operations, in 2015 the company acquired the Cosmic Blue Team S.p.A., an IT service company based in Italy. The unit will operate as Hitachi Systems CBT S.p.A.

In 2014 Hitachi bought IMGROUP, a specialist UK-based provider of information management and business intelligence products and services. The acquisition will increase Hitachi Consulting's capability to deliver measurable, sustainable business results for clients in the areas of decision making, asset optimization, risk management and customer experience.

The company also acquired Valcom S.r.l. (an Italy-based engineering firm) in 2014. The acquired company became Hitachi Engineering Europe S.r.l.

The company boosted its rail business in late 2012 with the acquisition of The Railway Engineering Company from James Fisher and Sons.

Hitachi Europe was established in 1982.
