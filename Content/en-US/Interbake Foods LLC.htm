Interbake Foods is for consumers who want a balanced diet; it makes a cookie for each hand. Founded in 1899, the company is one of the nation's biggest manufacturers of cookies and crackers. It supplies private-brand baked goods for retailers, as well as the contract-manufacture of custom goods for the supplemental, new-product, and seasonal baking needs of regional and national food manufacturers. Through its ABC Bakers unit, the company has been a Girl Scout cookie supplier since 1939. Interbake Foods' businesses include Interbake Foods in the US and Colonial Baking in Canada. The company has operated as the North American cookie and cracker division of Canadian baking behemoth George Weston since 1946.

Virginia-based Interbake Foods has baking facilities in North Sioux City, South Dakota; Front Royal, Virginia; and Canada.

Interbake Foods operates its business through three distinct segments: Retail Private Brands, Contract Manufacturing, and ABC Bakers, which makes Girl Scout cookies.

The Retail Private Brands unit is a top supplier of private brand cookies.

The Contract Manufacturing business makes cookies and crackers for national and regional food producers. They're available in bulk and retail case packs.

ABC Bakers has been making Girl Scout cookies since 1939.

While parent George Weston doesn't breakout sales for Interbake Foods, it reported that 2013 biscuit sales -- which include the sale of wafers, ice cream cones, cookies, and crackers -- accounted for about 19% of Weston Foods' sales, up from about 18% in 2012. Thanks to higher volumes among cookies and ice cream cones and pricing and changes in the sales mix, biscuit sales rose 5% during the reporting period. The company's new product launches, including Mrs. Fields-branded pre-packaged cookies, began to prop up sales beginning in Q4 2012.

In 2012 Interbake Foods and Mrs. Fields Famous Brands formed a long-term licensing partnership under which Interbake Foods became responsible for the manufacture and sale of Mrs. Fields branded pre-packaged cookies sold at retailers throughout North America. In 2014 the pair rolled out Nibblers miniature cookies in grocery stores. Previously, Nibblers were only available for purchase through catalogs, online, and through Mrs. Fields kiosks.

After purchasing a Kitchener, Ontario, bakery, Interbake Foods began offering sandwich cremes and wirecut cookies.
