Please, do judge Disc Graphics by its cover. The company makes and prints paperboard-based packaging for home entertainment, cosmetic, pharmaceutical, and related consumer goods markets. Its capabilities tap commercial printing to folding cartons, mini-flute corrugate, and foil stamping. Although Disc Graphics' name goes back to the 1970s in producing labels for vinyl records, the company today specializes in packaging for food and beverage to sporting goods, publishing for audio books, and vitamins. It touts sustainable practices including an eco-design packaging program and non-cellulose based (tree-free) paper.

Headquartered in Hauppauge, New York, Disc has facilities located in California, New Jersey, and New York. The company also has manufacturing alliances in Asia and Europe.

The company targets the beauty and personal care, entertainment media, health care, consumer products, and private label sectors.
