Optos has opted to provide improved imaging tools to eye doctors. The company is a manufacturer of digital diagnostic imaging equipment used by ophthalmologists and other eye care professionals in the detection and management of eye disease. Its Optomap laser ophthalmoscopes are used by ophthalmologists to perform precision retinal examinations. Additional tools are used to detect ailments such as retinal detachments, age-related macular degeneration, and glaucoma and are marketed to physicians primarily in North America but increasingly in Europe. Camera and lenses giant Nikon acquired Optos as it pushes for growth in both the medical sector and in the European market. The 2015 deal was valued at some $400 million.

Nikon bought Optos as a way to enter the medical sector while utilizing its existing optical technologies. Optos was previously listed on the London Stock Exchange .

The company's core devices are unique in that they produce ultra-widefield, high-resolution digital images of 200 o of the retina. Optos also makes optical coherence tomography devices and optical ultrasound scanners for use in diagnosing eye diseases.

Headquartered in the UK, Optos has operations in the US, Canada, Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Spain, Norway, Sweden, and Australia.

Optos was founded in 1992 by engineer Douglas Anderson after his son's retinal disorder was overlooked by eye doctors.
