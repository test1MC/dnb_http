In the foothills of the Cascade Range, Cascade Microtech makes test systems for microelectronics. Semiconductor makers such as Broadcom , Fujitsu Semiconductor , IBM , Intel , Samsung , and Toshiba use the company's probe cards, probe stations, and analytical probes to ensure the quality of their integrated circuits (ICs). Many of Cascade's customers use its tools to test their wireless, broadband, or other communications ICs at the wafer level, before the wafers are cut into individual chips. The company has a development alliance with test equipment giant Agilent Technologies . in February 2016 Cascade agreed to be acquired by FormFactor Inc., a key competitor, for $352 million. The deal is expected to close in mid-2016.

If the proposed acquisition by FormFactor goes through the combined company would be known as FormFactor with that company's CEO and chairman maintaining their leadership posts. The companies, in announcing the deal, said the combination would increase resources, achieve savings, and be able to grow more quickly than as separate companies.

Cascade gets more than 70% of sales from outside the US, primarily from customers in Asia.

The company's revenue has been growing year-over-year. It reported revenue of $144 million in 2015, up from $136 million in 2014. Sales in its systems unit fell 6% to about $78 million while the probes segment's sales jumped 24% higher to $66.1 million. Net income increased to $12.4 million in 2015 from $9.9 million in 2014. Cascade has had six straight years of revenue growth and has been profitable for four straight years.
