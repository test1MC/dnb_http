It's possible that this company might be holding your community's newspaper. Community Newspaper Holdings (CNHI) owns more than 150 daily, weekly, and semiweekly publications serving smaller markets in about 25 states. The company's Eagle Tribune Publishing Company subsidiary publishes the Eagle-Tribune in Massachusetts. In addition to publishing, the company runs companion websites for many of its papers and it owns four TV stations. Ralph Martin, formerly with the newspaper operations of Thomson Reuters (then known as Thomson Corporation), founded CNHI in 1997. The company is backed by state pension manager Retirement Systems of Alabama.

Most of the company's newspapers operate in the Midwest, South, and eastern regions of the US. It has a large presence in Indiana, Oklahoma, and Texas.

CNHI owns 80 daily newspapers, 48 nondailies, and numerous specialty publications. The Eagle-Tribune , with a circulation of about 45,000, is CNHI's largest newspaper. Eagle Tribune Publishing was sold to the national chain in 2005 after 107 years of local family ownership.

Built through a series of acquisitions, CNHI operates most of its papers in market clusters which allows the company cut expenses by combining certain business operations and centralizing print production. The economic recession and downturn in advertising, however, has forced CNHI to slow its expansion and find new ways to cut costs.
