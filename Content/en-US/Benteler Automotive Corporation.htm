Benteler Automotive is the US automotive parts manufacturing outpost of Germany's Benteler Group . Benteler Automotive -- the largest revenue generator of the Benteler Group's primary business divisions -- manufactures such auto parts as chassis components (crossmembers and suspension modules), structural components (bumper and door beams, pillar reinforcements, roof and side rails), exhaust manifolds, and exhaust and engine systems. The company also offers engineering services. Founded in 1935, Benteler Automotive got its start manufacturing exhaust pipes for the Ford Eifel.

The company is headquartered in Auburn Hills, Michigan. Benteler Automotive's operations include nearly 20 engineering and sales offices and almost 75 plants in some 30 countries.

The company operates under the Automotive division of its parent company. This division contributed about 79% of its parent's total revenue in 2012. The segment also increased its revenues by 6% from 2011 to 2012 due to growth in Asia, especially in Japan as the country fully recovered from the impact of the earthquake and tsunami from the spring of fiscal 2011.

Automotive production also continued to gain ground in China and India as production volume grew 6% in China, to 18.4 million vehicles, and 5% in India, to 3.8 million in 2012. This region accounted for some 50% of world production, and China alone represented 23%.

Benteler Automotive has invested in 28 new production locations over the past 10 years, in Asia, the Americas (in two plants), Africa, and Europe. At the same time, the company has expanded its lightweight construction and hybridization business.
