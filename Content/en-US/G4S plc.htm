G4S is a market leader for providing global security and safety services, challenging Securitas for the #1 spot. Via subsidiaries and joint ventures, it provides security guards and bodyguards, runs prisons, and offers cash management and transportation to customers in some 125 countries. G4S also develops and installs security and electronic surveillance systems and offers safety consulting, training, and advisement services. Its US unit, G4S Secure Solutions (USA) , specializes in armed guards and related security services for government and industrial clients. G4S was established in 2004.

Much of the company's revenue (around 85%) is fueled by its Secure Solutions segment, which provides a full slate of services, such as personal protection, venue security, prisons, mine detection, alarm systems, fire detection, CCTV, video analytics, key holding, response services, integrated facilities services, and employment assistance services. Cash Solutions, which carries out secure cash handling, accounts for the remaining 15%.

The UK & Ireland, North America and Asia-Middle East are G4S's biggest markets, at over 20% of revenue each; the rest of Europe brings in nearly 20%. Africa and Latin America bring in the remainder.

Europe accounts for half of its total sales, while North America contributes nearly 20%.

G4S's performance is rooted in its broad range of customers, cutting across all industries from financial institutions to retail stores. Its largest markets include major corporations and industrial companies (32% of revenue), government entities (24%), and financial institutions (16%).

G4S restated its 2014 financials to correct accounting of alarm sales and related leases in Denmark, and errors in accounting for acquisitions and disposals and related tax balances in North America; the adjustment saw 2014 reported revenue of £6,848 million restated as £6,889 million, which does not include a £350 million gain from a divestment in the year.

In 2015, revenue fell 5% on prior year (including the gain on the divestment) to £6.9 billion, with falls in both Secure Solutions and Cash Solutions.

Net income fell £137 million to £8 million due to loss from continued operations. G4S incurred a £66 million charge for goodwill impairment, relating to businesses expected to be sold or closed, and an Estonian operation. Cash from operating activities increased 7% on prior year to £283 million due to changes in working capital.

A portfolio review program, begun in 2013 and which has so far resulted in the sale of 23 businesses and netted proceeds of £281 million, is still ongoing. G4S expects to sell or exit a further 40 businesses.

In 2016, G4S sold G4S Honduras to Security Services Honduras, a subsidiary of GSI of Mexico, and G4S Finland to Sector Alarm of Norway for €17.5 million.
