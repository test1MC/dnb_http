Canada's RS Technologies (formerly Resin Systems) manufactures advanced composite materials designed to replace wood, steel, and concrete. The company's Version polyurethane resins are the basis of its composite materials. RS Technologies manufactures power transmission poles made of composite materials. The poles, designed to replace conventional wooden and steel poles, are sold to power and utility companies in Canada and the US. Home Depot subsidiary HD Supply Utilities is the company's sole distributor. It also makes composite roller tubes used in material handling systems, and in 2011 acquired the silicon wafer reclamation business of Japan-based Rasa Industries .
