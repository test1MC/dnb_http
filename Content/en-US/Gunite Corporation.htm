Gunite didn't invent the wheel, but it is a leading maker of wheel-end assemblies and components for the heavy-duty truck manufacturing market. Products include brake drums, disc wheel hubs and rotors, spoke wheels, automatic slack adjusters and related accessories. It also offers wheel and rim painting as well as coating and finishing services. Customers have included such noteworthy names as Ford Motor and Daimler Trucks North America . Gunite is owned by wheel maker Accuride and makes up the company's Accuride Wheel End Solutions segment.

Gunite is stationed in Rockford, Indiana, and it has plants in Canada, Mexico, and the US.

Gunite offers four basic wheel-end assemblies: disc wheel hub and brake drum; spoke wheel and brake drum; spoke wheel and brake rotor; and disc wheel hub and brake rotor. It also manufactures wheel-end components such as brake drums, disc wheel hubs, spoke wheels, rotors, and automatic slack adjusters. It caters to the heavy- and medium-duty truck markets, off-highway and bus markets, and their related aftermarkets.

The company markets its products through dealers, wholesale distributors, and aftermarket buying groups throughout the Americas, Europe, and Asia. It also sell directly to end users, particularly to large truck fleet operators.

PACCAR , Navistar , Daimler Trucks North America , and Volvo collectively accounted for 52% of its total sales in 2012.

Gunite, founded in 1854 by Duncan and Alexander Forbes, originally made sled runners, farm equipment components, and cooking utensils. It started making cast-iron brake drums in 1924 and added cast-iron wheels for heavy-duty trucks in 1932.
