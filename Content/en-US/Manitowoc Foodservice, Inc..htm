Manitowoc Foodservice never finds the kitchen too hot to handle. The company is one of the world's largest makers of foodservice kitchen and restaurant equipment. Its cook, chill, and prep/hold offerings are sold via such brands and subsidiaries as Garland, Frymaster , Cleveland Range , Delfield , Kysor/Warren , Lincoln , Convotherm, and Merrychef . This lineup is primarily designed for rapid cooking, or to keep hot foods hot and cold foods cold in restaurants, convenience stores, and other quick-service outlets. Manitowoc Foodservice was spun off from The Manitowoc Company in early 2016.

In addition to its US sites, Manitowoc Foodservice maintains major manufacturing capabilities in Canada, China, Germany, Thailand, and the UK. The company supplements those facilities with distribution centers and local inventory throughout the world.

Manitowoc Foodservice retains the Manitowoc Ice brand, enabling it to offer a one-stop shop of hot and cold kitchen equipment. Its portfolio includes more than 1,000 products, 500 of which qualify for the ENERGY STAR rating -- satisfying demand for efficient, green alternatives. In addition, Manitowoc Foodservice has enlarged its manufacturing and distribution footprint to cater to developing markets in the Asia/Pacific region. Domestically, the business is targeting the US government's School Stimulus Program and related initiatives that support investment in K-12 foodservice kitchens.

Manitowoc Foodservice servers the ice, beverage, refrigeration, food-preparation, and cooking needs of restaurants, convenience stores, hotels, healthcare, and institutional applications.

To build market share the company regularly launches new products. In 2013 Manitowoc Foodservice launched a new brand of cube ice machines marketed and sold under the Koolaire brand name. That year it also launched a resource library application in iTunes store.
