Owen Mumford gets to the point. The family-owned company designs and manufactures medical devices and disposables that include a variety of injection-based drug delivery systems, blood sampling equipment, neuropathy testers, and sexual health care devices. Its Autopen® line of insulin injectors use a side-mounted slide instead of a plunger, which is part of a design that is meant to minimize pressure and bruising. The company also makes Unilet® lancets in a number of sizes for use in blood sampling and testing. Owen Mumford also offers custom design services and forms partnerships to create new products.

Owen Mumford sells its products more than 50 countries, maintaining offices in the UK, the US, France, Germany, China, Mexico, and Malaysia.

The company acknowledges that growing stresses on the health care system will make self-management vital for such conditions as diabetes. To provide advancements in this area, Owen Mumford works from concept to commercialization with its own team as well as with health care professionals and pharmaceutical and diagnostic partners to develop custom-made and private-label medical devices.

In addition to its own sales force, the company sells across five continents through specialist distributors.

Owen Mumford's products and services are used as part of self-management plans and in hospitals, doctors' surgeries, pharmacies, care homes and clinics around the world. Its customers include Alliance HealthCare , Johnson & Johnson , Janssen , Chemo, NHS, Novartis , MSD Animal Health, Lincoln Medical, Bayer , US World Meds, and Bioprojet.

In 2015 the company opened a new regional hub in Nusajaya, in the south of Malaysia.

In 2014 Owen Mumford launched Autoject Micro, a disposable auto-injector that features a patented drive mechanism technology that enables auto-injection in a more discreet package.

Ivan Owen and John Mumford started the family-owned company in 1952, originally making products for the auto industry.
