3M Purification looks at liquids and gases through rose-colored filters. The company, a unit of 3M 's Industrial and Transportation segment, makes a full line of filtration products for the health care, fluid-processing, and potable-water markets. Its filters remove contaminants as small as molecules and particles as large as sand from liquids and gases. They are used to purify drugs, paints and resins, oil and gas, and home drinking water. 3M Purification caters to the life sciences, residential air and water filtration, industrial filtration, food beverage manufacturing, food service, and custom engineered products sectors.

The company sells its products through online retailers and a network of retail stores to customers in the US and as well as internationally.

3M's Industrial and Transportation segment accounted for roughly 35% of its parent's total sales in 2013.

The market for filtration products is estimated at nearly $3 billion worldwide, and filtration markets are projected to grow at a rate of 7% to 8% annually. Growth rates are expected to be the highest in emerging economies such as India, China, and the Middle East, which are regions the company is targeting.
