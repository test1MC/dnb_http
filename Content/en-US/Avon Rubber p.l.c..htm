Victims of biological attacks and cow udders alike have reason to be grateful to Avon Rubber. The company custom-engineers polymers for a variety of applications, but primarily respirators and gas masks for defense applications and liners and tubes for milking dairy cows. Avon Rubber supplies much of the world's milking products. The company also makes skirting for hovercraft and fendering products for marine transportation. Its Avon Engineered Fabrications unit (formerly Bell Avon) makes products such as temporary fuel or water storage tanks. The US Department of Defense and the British military are among Avon Rubber's largest customers.

Design and engineering company Avon Rubber focuses on two core markets, Protection & Defence and Dairy. Avon Protection (more than 70% of Avon Rubber's revenues) is the global market leader in advanced chemical, biological, radiological and nuclear respiratory (CBRN) protection systems technology. Manufacturing liners and tubing for the dairy industry, Avon Dairy Solutions is a leading dairy equipment business and has a global brand, Milkrite/InterPlus.

Avon Rubber operates in the US (Wisconsin, California, Maryland, Michigan, Mississippi, and Florida), the UK (Wiltshire, Dorset, and Essex), and in Italy and Malaysia. The US accounts for about 80% of Avon Rubber's total revenue.

Avon Protection serves the military, homeland security, emergency services, fire and industrial markets, while Avon Dairy Solution serves dairy customers.

In fiscal 2016 (September year end) Avon Rubber's revenue rose by 6% from £134.3 million to £142.9 million. Thanks to a new protective hood product, the effects of acquisitions, and improved operational performance, the company reported a 2% sales increase in the Protection & Defence segment over fiscal 2015. Dairy sales were up by 18% due to the effects of an acquisition and the growth of the segment's proprietary Milkrite/InterPuls brand. Both segments benefited from favorable currency translations.

Avon Rubber's adjusted net income increased in fiscal 2016 from £16.9 million to £22.5 million. Operating profit in Protection & Defence increased by about 4% thanks to new products, the effects of an acquisition, and better pricing on a long term contract. The Dairy segment rose by 12%, reflecting the acquisition of InterPuls, which offset lower volumes caused by the impact of lower milk prices.

Cash flows from operating activities rose from £17.1 million in fiscal 2015 to £30.3 million in fiscal 2016 due to higher net income, improved amortization and depreciation and a more favorable payables and provisions position.

Avon Rubber is looking to leverage its long pedigree in the mask business (which dates back to the 1920s) to develop new products and acquire new customers. In 2016 the company received a $9 million order from the New York City Police Department for its CBRN/CO (carbon monoxide) Escape hood.

Protection & Defence has a long-term M50 mask contract with the Department of Defense (DOD). In 2016 it had an annual order of 189,000 mask systems from the DOD.

The company is also look to grow its lining and tubing business across the global dairy industry. To help expand in China, in 2016 Avon Dairy Solutions moved to a larger facility in the Shanghai Jiading District.

Growing its reach, in 2014 Avon Rubber formed a strategic partnership with CBRN consultancy and training services company SecureBio, which has a list of global blue chip clients.

In 2015 the company acquired the Argus thermal imaging camera business from e2v technologies for 3.3 million

That year it also bought air respiratory system electronic control system designer Hudstar Systems for $5.6 million.

On the Dairy side of the business, in 2015 the company boosted its portfolio by acquiring of InterPuls S.p.A (Italy), the global mechatronic leader in milking components (pulsators, milkmeters, and automated milking clusters).

Avon Rubber was founded in 1885 when E. G. Browne and J. C. Margetson acquired a the Avon Mill cloth mill, known as Avon Mill, on the banks of the River Avon at Limpley Stoke in Wiltshire. The owners of the mill had been timber merchants, but had later diversified into the production of rubber goods.

By 1890 the business had transferred to premises in Melksham in Wiltshire and Avon established itself as a leader of rubber-based products.
