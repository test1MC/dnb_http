Country Pure Foods has built a portfolio of liquid assets. The company, which boasts more than 350 products, makes single-serve, aseptic, frozen, and chilled fruit drinks, juices, nectars, and concentrates for retail food purveyors and food service operators. It also offers spring water and iced coffee and provides food makers with fruit ingredients. Brand names include Natural Country, Juice4U, Glacier Valley, Golden Gift, and Ardmore Farms. Country Pure, which also packages juice for other beverage companies, operates plants in four states. Country Pure was formed in 1995 and was jointly acquired by Sapporo Holdings (51% stake) and Toyota Tsusho (49%) in 2015.

Country Pure Foods was jointly acquired by Sapporo Holdings (51% stake) and Toyota Tsusho (49%) for $100 million in 2015.

The Akron-based company operates nationwide through plants located in Ohio, Florida, Connecticut, and Texas.

Country Pure Foods is one of the largest independent processors of juice products in the US.

The company was formed by combining Natural Country Farms of Ellington, Connecticut, and Ohio Pure Foods of Akron, Ohio. It has since expanded with the addition of Ardmore Farms of DeLand, Florida, and Cal-Tex Citrus Juice of Houston, Texas.

Country Pure Foods distributes its products to the retail, food service, club stores, and co-pack markets. Customers include supermarkets, convenience stores, and foodservice companies. It also provides contract packing for top juice companies nationwide.

To enhance its manufacturing and production capabilities, Country Pure purchased Cal-Tex Citrus Juice in 2012. The acquisition gives the company a fourth production facility located in Houston. As part of the acquisition, Cal-Tex products are sold under the Ardmore Farms brand name.
