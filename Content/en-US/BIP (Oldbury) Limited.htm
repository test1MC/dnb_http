BIP (Oldbury) has been dipping into specialty chemicals since its creation in the late 1800s. The company -- called British Industrial Plastics once upon a time -- is the oldest polymer material manufacturer in the UK. A pioneer in developing thermoset resins and plastic molding materials, it was the first company to patent urea-formaldehyde resins. BIP now makes molding materials and textile and paper chemicals. It manufactures industrial chemicals such as formaldehyde resins, adhesives, and water-based polyurethanes. It also produces a biodiesel product, BIPSE, from discarded vegetable oils. BIP is a subsidiary of Synthite, the UK's leading formaldehyde producer.

The company makes industrial chemicals, thermoset molding materials, and plastic blast media. BIP's trademarks include Beetle and Betafin. Beetle is the European market leader in producing performance resins for paper making and coating, and a world leader in developing plastic blast media for the surface finishing industry.

The company operates through Beetle-Elliott in Australia, Bipel Maschinen in Germany, Materiales Moldeables in Mexico, British Industrial Plastics in South Africa, and BIPEL International in the US.

The company serves a range of industries, including textile processing, paper manufacture and coating, specialist surface treatments, inks, leather finishing, polyurethane dispersions, adhesives, and thermoset moldings.

BIP grows through product innovation and geographic expansion.

In 2011 the company launched two new aqueous polyurethane dispersions for use in protecting cords and ropes. In 2010 it appointed CPS Chemicals as an exclusive distributor in South Africa for the products of the industrial chemicals division of its BIP (Chemicals) unit.

The company traces its roots to the British Cyanide Company, which was founded in 1894. Other company names over its history include The Beetle Products Company and Colfast Buttons. Welsh company Synthite acquired BIP in 2004.
