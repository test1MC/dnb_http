PureSafe Water Systems (formerly Water Chef) makes and markets water purification systems under its name brand. The company's PureSafe Water Station, a six-stage system that produces up to 40,000 gallons of water per day, is marketed in the US for government programs, as well as in China. It also makes a line of mobile water decontamination and purification systems, as well as desalination units. PureSafe Water looks to provide its systems to customers in Bangladesh, Peru, Egypt, India, and Honduras. Former chairman, president, and CEO David Conway resigned in 2007, and Leslie J. Kessler took the top spot. The company adopted the PureSafe name in late 2008.
