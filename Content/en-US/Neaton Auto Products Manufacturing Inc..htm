You can't drive a car without the parts that Neaton supplies. Neaton Auto Products Manufacturing makes steering wheels, airbag modules and covers, interior and exterior trim, instrument panels, ventilators, and other components for the auto industry. The company uses manufacturing processes that include injection molding, blow molding, reaction injection molding (water-blown polyurethane formula), vacuum forming, welding, die-casting, and automated paint and assembly processes; it also offers testing and design services. Neaton is a subsidiary of Japan-based Nihon Plast , a global maker of airbags, instrument panels, and other engine components.

Neaton operates two manufacturing facilities in US in Georgia and Ohio and three in Mexico. Its headquarters is based in Eaton, Ohio.

Neaton serves some big-name automakers, such as Nissan, Honda, and Toyota.

Its Eaton, Ohio, facility manufactures steering wheels, airbags, and internal/external trim for major car makers Honda , Nissan , and Toyota . A second facility in the US manufactures steering wheels, airbags, and internal/external trim for Nissan and Honda and is based in Rome, Georgia. Its injection molding machines, which range in size from 17 to 2,000 tons, offer CAD/CAE capabilities such as product design and drawing and file maintenance services.

Nihon Plast Mexicana S.A. is its primary plant in Mexico, supplying leather wrapped steering wheels, as well as steering wheels and internal/external trim for Nissan and Honda for the Mexico market. Other facilities include Nihon Magnesio S.A. de C.V. and Neaton Auto Mexicana, S.A. de C.V.

To keep pace with the automotive industry, Neaton works to regularly expand and improve its product lines. This involves incorporating new technologies and processes. Neaton has also expanded its business with a 397,000-sq.-ft. facility.

Neaton was founded in 1984.
