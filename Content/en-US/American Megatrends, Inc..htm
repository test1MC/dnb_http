American Megatrends Inc. (AMI) knows PCs inside and out. The company provides core computer technologies, including basic input/output systems (AMIBIOS) for conveying information between a computer's hardware and operating systems; USB drivers and firmware; storage systems; and diagnostic software. Founded in 1985, the engineering-dominated company (more than 60% of its employees are engineers) sells its products to original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), such as Intel and Renesas , and to resellers and systems integrators. Co-founder and CEO Subramonian Shankar owns most of the company.

AMI's line of products to transfer information to and from and throughout a PC or server includes StorTrends IP Storage Area Network and Network Attached Storage, Aptio and AMIBIOS system software and firmware, and MegaRAC remote management software and firmware. The company makes hardware and software for the Linux and Android operating systems. The company's partners included DMTF, Intel , Nvm Express, PCI SIG, and Trusted Computing Group.

Beyond the PC, AMI offers applications and services for mobile, cloud, and the internet of things platforms. Its cloud services include data centers and assistance in helping customer set up private cloud environments/

Headquartered in Norcross, Georgia, AMI has locations in the US and in China, Germany, India, Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan. Its global research and development center in Chennai, India develops the company's new storage products. AMI is targeting resellers in the region who are more focused on products used as part of a larger IT services offering, rather than out-of-the-box distributors. Outside of the IT industry, for which India is well-known, AMI sells to such growing markets as banking and finance, government, and media.
