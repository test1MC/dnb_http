Cooper Lighting designs lighting and fixtures for commercial, residential, and utility applications. The company's products are primarily used in commercial buildings, homes, landscapes, shopping centers, high-security areas, and industrial plants. Cooper Lighting's brand names include AtLite, Fail-Safe, Halo, IRiS, Lumark, Streetworks, and Sure-Lites, among others. The Cooper Lighting SOURCE facilities offer continuing education lighting classes, which cover lighting fundamentals and lighting basics to landscape lighting for the lighting professional. Cooper Lighting's origins date back to 1958, when the manufacturer was founded as Halo Lighting Company. Since 2012 Cooper Lighting has been owned by Eaton .

Georgia is home to Cooper Lighting's new 60,000-sq.-ft. LED Innovation Center. The facility promotes development and design through its team of mechanical, electrical, thermal, and optical engineers, along with industrial designers and manufacturing experts.

In addition to residential and commercial lighting, the company also provides emergency lighting and worklights, as well as its branded Fail-Safe illumination for visual therapy. The company's fixtures have provided lighting in places like Microsoft 's headquarters and Bill Gates' house, Green Bay's Lambeau Field, Austin's LBJ Library and Museum, and Chicago's United Center.

Cooper Lighting has used strategic partnerships to grow its business. It also rolls out new products on a regular basis.
