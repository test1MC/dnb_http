Land O'Frost makes meat that's sandwich-worthy. As the nation's largest family-owned brand of packaged deli meat, Land O'Frost produces lunch and deli meats, such as beef, chicken, turkey, Canadian bacon, and ham. It markets its meats under the DeliShaved, Land O'Frost, Bistro Favorites, Taste Escapes, and Premium names. Land O'Frost customers include food retailers, food processors, and foodservice companies across the US. It also provides meat-derived ingredients to other food makers and bulk products to foodservice providers. Operating plants in Illinois, Arkansas, Nebraska, and Kentucky, Land O'Frost is still owned and managed by descendants of founded Antoon Van Eekeren.

Illinois-based Land O'Frost sells its products through US supermarkets and mass merchandisers, as well as in Mexico, Puerto Rico, and other US territories. The company operates four facilities, one each in Lansing, Illinois; Madisonville, Kentucky; Searcy, Arkansas; and West Point, Nebraska.

Besides its primary Land O'Frost brand, the company owns and operates Nebraska-based Wimmer's Meat, which makes sausage and hot dogs under several brand names, including Wimmer's, Bassett's, Fairbury, and Ambassador. They're sold through grocery stores across Colorado, Illinois, Iowa, Kansas, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, and South Dakota.

In addition to supermarkets and merchandisers, the company sell products through retail outlets throughout the US.

Land O'Frost also operates a website -- LandOMoms.com -- to connect with moms and help them bond with its brand. The website is part of the company's social media campaign, which also includes maintaining a presence on Facebook , Twitter , and YouTube . LandOMoms.com works to appeal to consumers by offering a place where they can exchange recipes, look for parenting advice, download coupons, read women's magazine articles, and communicate with bloggers.

In 2015, Land O'Frost announced that its Wimmer's Meats manufacturing plant in West Point, Nebraska, will close .

To serve the growing number of consumers who snack rather than eat sit-down meals, Land O'Frost in 2013 rolled out its Minis product line. Minis are two inches in diameter and are marketed to be paired with crackers, served on a party tray, or eaten straight out of the package. Additionally, the new line is gluten-free and MSG-free, has zero trans fats, and are 100 calories or fewer per serving.

Land O'Frost is also building a new headquarters as part of a planned move from Illinois to Indiana. The meat producer is investing some $6.4 million to construct a new 25,000-sq.-ft. facility that's due for completion sometime in 2014.

Land O'Frost is owned by descendants of the founding Van Eekeren family. The company was founded by Antoon Van Eekeren in the 1950s.
