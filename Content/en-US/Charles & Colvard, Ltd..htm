Charles & Colvard hopes that it isn't just some shooting star. The company makes gemstones made from moissanite, a diamond substitute created in laboratories. Composed of silicon and carbon, moissanite (aka silicon carbide or SiC) is typically found in meteorites. Charles & Colvard makes its gemstones from SiC crystals purchased primarily from Cree, Inc. , and Swedish company Norstel. Charles & Colvard markets its gemstones through two distributors ( Stuller and Rio Grande) and jewelry manufacturers such as K&G Creations, Reeves Park, and Samuel Aaron International.
