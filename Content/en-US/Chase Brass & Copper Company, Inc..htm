If life's just a merry-go-round, Chase Brass & Copper Company (CBCC) grabs the brass ring. The company manufactures Blue Dot-brand round and hexagonal brass rods for machining and forging processes. Its brass rods are used in end-products from plumbing fixtures to industrial machinery components, tire valve stems, and thermostats. CBCC also partners with five companies to lead as an exclusive North American producer of Green Dot, an Eco Brass license for a silicon-based lead-free alloy, desirable in eco-friendly plumbing fixtures and food processing. Founded in 1837, the company is a nameplate of Global Brass and Copper, formerly Olin Corporation , which was acquired by KPS Capital Partners in 2007.
