Matco Tools is there for the ride. A unit of professional instrumentation group Danaher , the business makes and distributes more than 13,000 professional automotive products, including hand tools, diagnostic equipment, and toolboxes, across the US, Puerto Rico, and Canada. Its products include wrenches for working on the front axle shafts of Asian-made motorcycles and scanners that interface between PCs and vehicles. Matco also makes aviation and air conditioning equipment. It markets its products to mechanics and other professionals via its website, industrial representatives, and a network of franchised distributors (recognized by their white trucks with the Matco logo). Matco Tools was founded in 1946.

Ohio-based Matco Tools has distributors in all 50 US states, Canada, and Puerto Rico. International sales are made through Matco's export distribution arm located in Australia, New Zealand, Europe, Asia, and South America.

Matco sells its products through independent franchised mobile distributors, who sell primarily to professional mechanics. The company promotes its brand through sponsorships of motorsports teams, including the National Hot Rod Association's Top Fuel driver Antron Brown.

Matco is looking to grow in the automotive aftermarket tools sector by adding some 500 distributors who will wholly own and operate mobile distribution centers in select US markets, including Portland, Oregon, in 2014. This new franchise strategy is part of the toolmaker's initiative to double the annual growth rate of new distributors. In 2013 the company added more than 200 new distributors.
