For Netgem it doesn't matter what screen you watch, the company wants the TV experience to be the same. Its "TelcoTV" platform delivers a range of TV content from central computers (the cloud) to customer's big screen TVs, tablets, PCs, and phones. TV viewers get the flexibility watching what device is most convenient at the time as well as on-screen access to information about programs and casts. Content providers get their services to viewers wherever they are well as a wealth of data. Netgem, which is based in France, serves some seven million households in 20 countries in Europe, Latin American, and the Asia/Pacific region.

Content partners include the BBC , Netflix , AGAMA, Corazon, DogTV, and YouTube . Customers include Telstra , Totalplay, Viaplay, and H1 .

Netgem reported revenue of about €79 million in 2015, a 9% increase from 2014. Net income skyrocketed to €1.8 million in 2015 from about $100,000 in 2014 on increases in one-time contracts and recurring revenue. The company also had favorable impact from currency exchange and lower prices for streaming content.
