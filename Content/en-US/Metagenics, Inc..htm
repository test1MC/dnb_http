Metagenics manufactures and markets nutritional supplements focused on improving the immune system and addressing cardiovascular, gastrointestinal, orthopedic, and other health concerns. The company offers about 40 products including premium vitamin and mineral blends, herbal formulas, sports nutrition supplements, and medical foods -- all held to higher production and absorption standards than most mass-marketed supplements. Metagenics markets its products to more than 100,000 health care practitioners and pharmacies who, in turn, prescribe and sell the products to about 1 million patients. Amway parent company Alticor holds a majority stake in Metagenics.

In addition to its California headquarters, Metagenics has research and development headquarters in Washington; internationally, the company has operating subsidiaries in Australia, Belgium, Canada, the Netherlands, and New Zealand. It markets its products in additional countries in the Americas, Europe, and the Asia-Pacific region. In 2012 the company moved its California headquarters to a new building to accommodate its growing employee base.

Metagenics sells its products through a network of licensed health care professionals and retail outlets. Its partnering distributors receive training and support on the company's offerings.

Strategy

Metagenics expands its product offerings via acquisitions and organic development. Its development efforts are focused on discovering new therapies and nutritional formulas to meet unmet medical needs in fields including endocrine, metabolism, cardiovascular, gastrointestinal, and neurological health. The company has introduced several new products in recent years, including its 2012 release of the NutraGems CoQ10 300 chewable gels for cardiovascular health.

In 2010 it acquired Catalina Lifesciences, maker of the Bariatric Advantage line of professional nutrition products for patients who have undergone weight-loss surgery.
