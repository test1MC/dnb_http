Nortek subsidiary Nortek Global HVAC (formerly Nordyne) makes and markets residential and light commercial heating and cooling products. The company's portfolio includes air conditioners, heat pumps, gas and oil furnaces, gas and electric heating and cooling units, water heaters, air filters, fireplaces, programmable thermostats, and related parts and accessories. They are marketed under such recognized brand names as Broan, Frigidaire, Kelvinator, Maytag, Philco, Tappan, and Westinghouse. Its Intertherm and Miller brands are designed specifically for manufactured homes. Nortek Global HVAC, which sells its products throughout the US and abroad, boasts manufacturing and distribution facilities in Missouri, Tennessee, and Mexico.

In its home state of Missouri, Nortek Global HVAC operates a 200-employee manufacturing plant in O'Fallon (St. Louis). The company has other plants in Dyersburg, Tennessee and Saltillo, Mexico. The company's international headquarters operates out of Miami while its business south of the border, Miller de Mexico, is headquartered in Monterrey, Mexico.

Missouri-based Nortek Global HVAC sells its products worldwide and operates in Missouri, Tennessee, and Mexico. Outside North America, it peddles its products mostly in Latin America and the Middle East.

Nortek Global HVAC sells residential HVAC items intended for site-built homes through independently-owned distributors who sell the items to HVAC contractors. It sells its manufactured housing products directly to builders and through distributors to manufactured housing retailers and owners.

Reflecting its parent company Nortek's wishes to bring a more unified strategy for accelerated global growth, energy-efficient product innovation, and expanded product offerings, Nordyne and its Nordyne International subsidiary changed their names to Nortek Global HVAC LLC and Nortek Global HVAC Latin America, Inc in early 2015, respectively.

Nortek Global HVAC has been winding down many of its US manufacturing plants in recent years to cut costs. In 2014, for example, the company shut down and relocated both of its Missouri factories, which had employed some 700-plus workers in Polar Bluff and Boonville, to consolidate the operations into its newly-opened plant in Saltillo, Mexico.

During the downturn in the construction industry, parent company Nortek filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in 2009, but emerged soon after. The filing included Nortek's subsidiaries. As part of the reorganization Nortek was able to eliminate some $1.3 billion of debt.
