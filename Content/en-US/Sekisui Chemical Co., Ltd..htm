Sekisui Chemical (named for a concept of power taken from Sun-Tsu's Art of War) is a central corporation of the Sekisui keiretsu -- companies affiliated through cross-ownership. Its main segments include housing, urban infrastructure and environmental products, and high performance plastics. Prefabricated housing (detached steel-framed and wooden-framed construction), accounts for about half of its sales. Other products include plastic pipes and fittings (used in water and sewage systems), medical multifrequency body fat meters, pipe valves, foams for automotive and construction applications, and rain gutters.

The company operates in North America, Central and South America, Europe, Asia and Oceania, Japan, and China. Japan is its largest market, accounting for 73% of sales in 2015. Other major markets include Asia (13%), the US (7%), and Europe (6%).

Sekisui Chemical has divided its business operations into three segments: Housing (44% of net sales), High Performance Plastics (HPP; 33%), and Urban Infrastructure & Environmental Products (UIEP; 19%). An "Other" segment accounts for the remainder.

Sekisui Chemical's revenues were consistent from 2014 to 2015. HPP segment sales climbed 5% in 2015, while its UIEP segment experienced a 5% decline in sales. The company experienced a 38% surge in sales from China and a 9% increase from the US during 2015.

The company is focused on expanding its business in its three strategic business fields of information technology materials (IT), automotive materials (AT), and medical products (MD).
