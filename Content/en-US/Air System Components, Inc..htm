An air conditioner is nothing but a sum of its parts, with Air System Components (ASC) supplying the parts. It makes components such as grilles, registers, diffusers, and terminals for air conditioning, heating and ventilation systems, and other air distribution products. ASC's products are used in industrial facilities, institutions, and commercial and residential buildings. Its brands, which operate as independent companies, include Titus, Krueger, Tuttle & Bailey, Superior Rex, and PennBarry. Its Trion Indoor Air Quality arm focuses on air filtration supplies. Products are available through wholesalers and distributors. ASC, which has about 10 plants, is a subsidiary of Air Distribution Technologies.

To expand its buildings business, in 2014 auto parts supplier Johnson Controls agreed to acquire Air Distribution Technologies from the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board for $1.6 billion. This investment expands Johnson Controls' position in the buildings space with additional products that are complementary to its existing HVAC offerings.

ASC has multiple brands that operate as independent companies. The Titus, Krueger, and Tuttle & Bailey brands have been major market forces selling grilles, registers, diffusers, and terminal units. PennBarry focuses on making commercial and industrial fans and blowers as well as kitchen exhaust equipment. The Superior Rex brand produces fan coils and related products, while Reliable offers a unique line of grilles, registers, diffusers, and louvers.

ASC's products are available worldwide through a network of independent distributors, wholesalers, and manufacturer representatives for each brand. It products can be found in all types of industrial facilities, commercial buildings, schools, hospitals, universities and premier residences throughout the world and beyond (they are even on the International Space Station).

A recent acquisition, Trion Indoor Air Quality, is home to the Trion, Envirco, and Herrmidifier brands (as well as Koch Filter) that focus on air filtration products. These brands complement ASC's existing lines of air distribution products, with a special focus on clean indoor air quality.

In 2012 the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board acquired Air Distribution Technologies from British engineering firm Tomkins for $1.1 billion.

In 2010 Tomkins was acquired by Canada's Onex and the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board for $5 billion. The deal helped steady Tomkins in the midst of an uneasy market and provided shareholders a nice payday.

Also in 2010 Tomkins added to ASC's lineup of brands with the acquisition of Koch Filter Corporation, a maker of non-residential air filters in the US.
