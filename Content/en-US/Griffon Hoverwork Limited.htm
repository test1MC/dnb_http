Griffon Hoverwork Ltd. (GHL) offers a line of hovercraft that are designed to go where other vehicles fear to tread. GHL's hovercraft can carry payloads of up to about 22 tons, as many as 180 passengers, and can hit speeds of up to 45 knots. A unit of the Bland Group, The company's commercial models are used as passenger ferries and in civil engineering support, search and rescue, and beach patrol. Military uses include amphibious assault, minesweeping, and commando missions. In a Griffon hovercraft there aren't many places you can't go -- they feel right at home on water, mud, snow, ice, land, swamp, rocks, and rapids.

The company's hovercrafts are used in more than 40 countries across five continents.Operations

GHL's hovercrafts have commercial, rescue, military, and paramilitary applications, and are used as passenger ferries, for hydrographic and seismic surveys, as oil spill response craft, to mine counter measures, as logistics/troop and vehicle carriers, and for fast attack and amphibious assault and range patrol.

Other applications for GHL's hover technology include hovering covers for cricket pitches, hovering crop sprayers, and hovering reed cutters. The firm also sells used hovercraft.

The company sells its products through distributors.

In 2014 the company teamed up with Cougar Powerboats, to market the Griffon-Cougar, a British brand offering very fast monohull and catamaran boats to the military and para-military market worldwide.

In 2009 the Bland Group purchased Griffon Hovercraft and merged the firm with Hoverwork Ltd. to form Griffon Hoverwork.

Bland Group also bought Hovertravel, Hoverwork's sister company, a commercial hovercraft operator which provides passenger ferry services to the Isle of Wight.
