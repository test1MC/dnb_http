Singularly, General Electric is the world's #1 maker of jet airplane engines, while Honda is primarily known for automobiles -- together, the two giants have revved up to build engines for small jets. Their joint venture, GE Honda Aero Engines (a 50/50 joint venture between GE Aviation and Honda Aero ) was formed in 2004 to produce engines in the 1,000 to 3,500 pound thrust class for business jets that seat up to eight passengers. Its first product is the HF120, a 2,000-pound compact turbofan engine for very light jets; testing began in 2009. GE Honda Aero Engines operates facilities in Ohio and North Carolina and offers services, including maintenance, repair, and overhaul, as well as spare engine leasing.
