Motion granted! If the judge is Galil, that is. The company manufactures an array of motion controllers. Whether the job is point-to-point positioning, linear and circular interpolation, or electronic gearing, the company touts the right tool. Its lineup includes single- and multi-axis motion controllers, bus-based or stand-alone, and card- and box-level controllers. A global sales network pushes Galin motion controllers to a diversity of markets, from TomoTherapy 's radiation device, to WET Design's fountains, Thermoforming Systems' yogurt cup molder, and the University of California Observatories' spectrograph. Started in 1983, the company is led by its founders, chairman Jacob Tal and president Wayne Baron.

Galil's motion controllers use a 32-bit microcomputer and run in software capable of handling simple and complex applications. Modes of motion include point-to-point positioning, linear and circular interpolation, electronic gearing, electronic cam, and position following.

The company designs and makes controllers at its headquarters in Rocklin, California.

Galil's product lend motion to devices used in medical, semiconductor, machine tool, food processing, test and measurement, and textiles applications. Customers include Brooks Automation , Veeco Instruments , East Carolina University , WET Design, and JIT/CMM. The company's motion controllers are used in devices ranging from 3D medical instruments to earthquake simulators.
