The Press Association is no newbie to the news business. The company was founded in 1868 and, since that time, has been providing news agency services to media industry clients throughout the UK and Ireland. News agency services include text, photos, data, video, and betting data, which is collected and distributed via a reporting network that stretches from the UK across Europe. Stories and images from The Press Association are published in newspapers and websites, as well as broadcast on television and radio. The company is owned by PA Group Limited, a private company with some 25 shareholders, including media firms such as Daily Mail and General Trust , News UK , Trinity Mirror , The Telegraph , and UBM .

The Press Association's Media and Broadcast activities include Newswire, a national news agency; PA Images, the photography arm; Video; a data service that includes football statistics and golf scorecards; and TV listings; among other things. Its content is split into six interest categories: News, Sport, Entertainment, Real Life, Motoring, and Lifestyle.

The company's Racing & Betting activities include supplying live data, form guides, statistics, editorial content, images, graphics, and infographics for horse racing and greyhound racing. Its offers a number of hosted solutions: NagMe, a horse racing alert product that delivers reminders about upcoming races; iCard, which delivers racing odds; Racing Text Commentary, a live text commentary service; and Vector Silks, which provides graphics of jockeys' jerseys. It covers every horse race in the UK, Ireland, South Africa, and Dubai, as well as selected meetings in the US, Australia, and India. Its greyhound coverage is limited to Great Britain.

The company is headquartered in London, and has offices in Bristol, Edinburgh, Glasgow, Nottingham, and East Yorkshire.
