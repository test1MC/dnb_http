Static Control Components (SCC) isn't stuck on static cling. The 3M company, which made its name by selling anti-static products, has made an even greater impression by selling parts for rebuilt toner cartridges. Its Imaging Division, which sells parts to recycle used printer cartridges, is the largest manufacturer of aftermarket imaging systems and components supporting genuine cartridge remanufacturers within the global laser and ink jet industry. SCC makes in-house more than 10,000 imaging products and offers more than 14,000 imaging products to the aftermarket industry (including chips, toner, drums, blades, PCRs, developer rollers, and more).

SCC maintains some 20 manufacturing locations (more than 1.5 million sq. ft) worldwide. It sells its products in the US, Asia, Europe, and other regions.

Research, development, engineering, and primary manufacturing (as well as global distribution) are located at SCC's global headquarters in Sanford, North Carolina.

SCC sells its products worldwide through its website, company offices, and authorized distributors.

To maintain its high quality and to support its customers, the company makes major investments in research and development, customer education programs, multilingual customer support, and building its inventories.

Expanding its geographic coverage, in 2014 SCC opened an office and distribution facility in Istanbul, Turkey and a distribution center in Butzbach, Germany. It also selected Makkon as its authorized distributor in the Ukraine.

That year SCC also expanded the availability of its e-commerce website to its European customer base. The site has been designed specifically to make ordering easier and more convenient.

Rival Lexmark International sued SCC, claiming its components and software for Lexmark toner cartridges violated both general copyright law and the federal Digital Millennium Copyright Act of 1998. SCC ultimately prevailed in the federal appellate courts.

Former President and CEO Ed Swartz founded SCC in 1986.
