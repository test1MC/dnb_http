Allegion's allegiance involves keeping you safe. The company makes an assortment of residential and commercial electronic and mechanical locks, door closer and exit devices, steel doors and frames, and access control and workforce productivity systems. Featured products include biometric systems that verify identity through the size and shape of a person's hands; and vehicle recognition technology, which detects a vehicle's make, model, and license plates. In addition, Allegion provides consulting and customized engineering services for customers regarding their security needs. Its 23 product brands are sold in more than 120 countries around the world. Allegion was spun-off from Ingersoll-Rand in late 2013.

Allegion was spun-off from Ingersoll-Rand in late 2013 as a publicly traded company.

Stationed in Ireland, Allegion has regional corporate centers in Carmel, Indiana; Brussels, Belgium; and Shanghai, China.

Allegion caters to the commercial real estate, education, government, health care, hospitality, manufacturing, residential, and transportation sectors. Its flagship brands include CISA, Interflex, LCN, Schlage, and Von Duprin.

Allegion saw its revenues rise 2% to $2.1 billion in 2013 despite the organizational hardships involved with being a spin-off.

Allegion believes the future of the security industry lies in addressing the needs of an increasingly connected world. It plans to invest in the electronic side of its business in order to grow. It will capitalize on trends such as increased global urbanization, the shift toward electronic and intelligent security products, and the anticipated global rebound of commercial and residential construction markets.

In early 2014 Allegion acquired Schlage Lock de Colombia S.A., one of the largest mechanical lock manufacturers in Colombia. The acquisition will enable Allegion to leverage its branded residential and commercial product lines to grow its presence in the Spanish-speaking South American security market.
