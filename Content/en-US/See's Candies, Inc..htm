One suspects that investment guru and billionaire Warren Buffett has a sweet tooth. Owned by Buffett's firm Berkshire Hathaway , See's Candies makes more than 100 varieties of premium chocolate truffles, caramels, toffee, and other confections, including boxed assortments, lollipops, peppermints, and licorice treats. The company sells its sweets through 200-plus franchised, black-and-white-decorated shops, primarily located in California and other western states. It also markets them worldwide. Its products also are sold for fundraisers, through its website, and by mail order. A highly seasonal operation, some 45% of its revenue is generated in November and December.

California-based See's Candies is a global marketer of chocolates and other sweets. Its manufacturing operations are based in the US.

The company's retail and quantity discount shops are located in more than a dozen US states, including Arizona, California, Colorado, Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Minnesota, Missouri, New Mexico, Nevada, Oregon, Utah, and Washington. Overseas, See's has stores in Macau, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and Japan.

See's Candies makes its treats at two large kitchens in Los Angeles and San Francisco. (The chocolate assembly line at the company's LA kitchen is said to be the inspiration for the famous I Love Lucy episode.)

Besides its network of international retail shops, See's Candies sells its products online. The company is working to win over a younger target audience to supplement its loyal clientele.

The company is extending the reach of its network of stores into existing and new markets. It has opened See's Candies stores in Ohio, Minnesota, and Pennsylvania. New stores are also slated for Missouri, Michigan, Kentucky, and Tennessee, with an eye on establishing new stores well outside its existing footprint in the likes of Florida and Washington, DC, by 2015.

See's Candies' recipe for success has been not to mess with it. The more than 90-year-old business still features a picture of Mary See -- whose recipes are still used -- on every candy box and she's featured on the company's website.

Founded in 1921 by Charles A. See, the company was bought by Berkshire Hathaway in 1972 for $25 million.
