The Japanese have developed a yen for potato chips, thanks to Calbee Foods Co., Ltd. The company sells a line of potato chips under the Calbee brand and other snack foods such as SAYA snow pea crisps, Vegetaberu vegetable snacks, Yakimorokoshi corn puffs, breakfast cereal, and the very popular Kappa Ebissen shrimp-flavored potato sticks and crackers. It also produces limited-edition regional-flavored chips and snacks in curry, kimchee, and even Caesar salad. Besides Japan, Calbee has operations in China, Hong Kong, Thailand, and the US (as Calbee America ). The company was founded in 1949.
