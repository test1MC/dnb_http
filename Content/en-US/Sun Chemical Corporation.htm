Sun Chemical shines brightly as the largest inks maker on the planet; it also makes all manner of coatings. In addition to inks and coatings for paper, packaging, metal, and other products, Sun Chemical manufactures printing plates for corrugated packaging. Its inks and coatings products are sold under the Sun Chemical, Kohl & Madden, US Ink, and Coates brands. A part of Japan's DIC , the US-based company operates more than 300 manufacturing, sales, and service locations in Europe , Asia, and the Americas. The company's products include magnetic tapes for plastic cards and degasification modules for water treatment facilities.

Sun Chemical provides materials to various markets such as cosmetics, plastics, photovoltaic, coatings, publications, packaging, screen graphics, and other segments. The company also offers consulting services, smart color applications, and Sun Care services.

Sun Chemical Electronic Materials Group develops inks for the photovoltaic market. The group has designed materials for crystalline, thin film, and organic photovoltaic technologies. With an eye towards future, the company is developing materials in the US, in Europe, and in Asia to improve efficiency of crystalline silicon solar cells/modules, organic PV, and thin film modules.

The company also offers a range of security products, from relatively low entry level features up to highly sophisticated proprietary technologies such as Verigard and Hidden Indicia and Smart Holograms.

Geographic Reach

The company's operations are spread across various regions, including North America, Europe, Latin America, and Asia.

Sun Chemical's products serves a range of end markets and include commercial and industrial electronics, magnetic tapes for plastic cards, and degasification modules for water treatment facilities.

The company, together with its parent, generate annual sales of more than $7.5 billion.

In 2015 the company acquired Canada-based Colmar Inks. Colmar's customer lists, contracts, and finished inventory will be included in the acquisition. The acquisition allows the company to expand its liquid ink customer base in Canada and further strengthen its market position as the leading supplier of lithographic, flexographic and gravure inks and coatings in the region.

That year Sun Chemical also acquired UK-based Kingfisher Colours, a privately-owned decorative cosmetics color manufacturer. Kingfisher's European-based production sites enhances the company's global cosmetics colors manufacturing capabilities and will allow for further penetration into developing markets.

Sun Chemical has been a unit of DIC since 1987 when the latter bought the graphic arts division of what is now Sequa .

In mid-2012 the company entered the metallic pigments market with its purchase of Austria's Bend-Lutz Werke. The acquisition, which Sun Chemical used to form its new Metallics Business Unit under the Performance Pigments Division, adds metallic pigment plants in Austria, Poland, Russia, and the US to the company's existing aluminum pigment site in China.

That year Sun Chemical and the Druckfarben Hellas Group merged their publications divisions in South Eastern Europe to strengthen their position in a growing region. The joint venture entities offers customers publications inks and coatings, as well as sheetfed, UV, screen graphics, and consumables in Greece, Bulgaria, Cyprus, Romania, Serbia, Bosnia/Herzegovina, Montenegro, Croatia, and Moldova.
