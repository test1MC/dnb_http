Riviana Foods puts woks to work in the US and abroad. With its Carolina, Minute Rice, Gourmet House, River, Water Maid, Mahatma, and Success brands, the company has become one of the largest sellers of rice, and, the seller of many of the country's most well-known rice brands. The company's offerings include wild, long-grain, short-grain, aromatic, parboiled, and boil-in-a-bag rices. In addition to manufacturing rice products for consumers, the company also supplies foodservice and industrial customers throughout the world. Its industrial ingredients include white and brown rice flour, broken rice, and rice hulls. The company is a subsidiary of Spanish company Ebro Puleva .

Houston-based Riviana sells its rice and rice products primarily in the US, but also worldwide.

Riviana has production facilities in Tennessee, Arkansas, and Minnesota. In addition to its branded products, the company is also a large maker of private-label rice.

Sister company New World Pasta Company concentrates on making and marketing pasta, with its Healthy Harvest, Ronzoni, San Giorgio, Creamette, American Beauty, Skinner, Prince, No Yolks, Catelli, and Lancia brand names. It sells this pasta portfolio in the US and Canada.

While Ebro Puleva does not disclose separate financial data for Riviana, the parent company reported that in 2013 the North American segment contributed about 38% of its total revenue.

Riviana serves several sectors, such as supermarkets, club stores, US foodservice companies for the top restaurant chains, and companies that leverage Riviana for ingredient and foodservice applications. In the Caribbean, Riviana's Mahatma and Minute Rice brands hold leading positions.

The company sells more branded and private label rice products to retail grocers than any other company.

Its association with Ebro has given Riviana access to a global network of raw product sources, top technologies, product innovations, and research and development capability. The company continues to innovate, offering fully cooked, quick-to-fix rice mixes in single- and family-sized portions. It also regularly rolls out new products, such as its 2013 debut of Minute Maid-label multi-grain medley, ready-to-serve black beans and rice, and ready-to-serve fried rice mix.

Riviana's roots reach back to 1911, when Frank A. Godchaux consolidated 25 rice mills in southwestern Louisiana to form the Louisiana State Rice Milling Company. His family controlled the company until 1965, when River Brand Rice Mills purchased it and changed the name to Riviana Foods. In its early days, Riviana was tied to the commodities market, but it changed its focus to domestic retail in the late 1960s. Godchaux's descendants stuck with the company even when it was sold to a subsidiary of Colgate-Palmolive in 1976. Joe Hafner was named CEO in 1984, and Godchaux's grandsons led a management buyout of Riviana two years later.

Riviana and grain cooperative Riceland Foods formed Rivland Partnership in 1989 to produce rice flour. In 1995 Riviana went public. Riviana teamed up with Tiger Oats to market rice products in South Africa in 1997 and began distributing Riceland's consumer products in the US and the Bahamas the next year.

In 1999 Riviana took over the marketing and distribution of S&W brand rice from longtime customer Tri Valley Growers. That year it also acquired its longtime wild rice supplier, Gourmet House. Through its Belgian subsidiary, Riviana purchased Euryza Reis GmbH, one of Germany's largest rice milling operations, in 2000.

In 2003 Riviana acquired the rice specialties business of ACH Food Companies , increasing its presence in the instant-rice category. Acquired brands included China Doll and Fiesta. That year it also purchased Kraft Foods ' European rice businesses.

The company was acquired by Ebro Puleva in 2004 in a deal valued at $380 million and has since ceased production in south Louisiana, where the company was founded.

In 2006 Ebro purchased the Minute Rice brand and operations for the US and Canada from Kraft and added them to Riviana's roster.
