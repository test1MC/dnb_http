Corden Pharma Colorado is a contract research and development firm and manufacturer of advanced pharmaceutical intermediates, active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs), and drug formulations. It specializes in such areas as anaesthesiology, antibiotic therapy, cardiovascular therapy, oncology, and the central nervous system. The company offers full-service manufacturing packages from developing APIs and assisting with approval procedures to formulating and producing products to complete pharmaceutical logistics. Corden Pharma Colorado is part of the Corden Pharma pharmaceutical platform of International Chemical Investors Group (ICIG).

ICIG's Corden Pharma business also operates other plants under the Corden Pharma moniker that were former manufacturing units of pharmaceutical companies AstraZeneca , Bristol-Myers Squibb , Cambrex , and Genzyme .

Corden Pharma Colorado is focused on synthetic peptides. It serves as a research center for the discovery of new production processes for medicinal compounds. Its manufacturing complex includes production plants, small-scale production labs, research labs, pilot plants, warehousing space, and a waste water treatment facility, as well as administration and engineering buildings.

The company began as Arapahoe Chemicals in 1946, producing organic chemicals. It was acquired in 1965 by pharmaceutical company Syntex, which was in turn purchased by Roche in 1994, and most recently by ICIG in 2011. These operate in Germany, Ireland, Italy, and Switzerland.
