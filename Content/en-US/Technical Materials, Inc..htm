Technically speaking, it's not the US Mint , but Technical Materials, Inc. (TMI) knows how to turn metal into money. The company provides a variety of metals processing services, including cladding, electroplating, electron beam welding, and solder coating, to produce specialty strip metals. The company works with materials such as aluminum, copper, nickel, and steel. It maintains a 125,000 sq. ft. facility in Rhode Island and serves customers in the aircraft, automotive, computer, alternative energy, medical, jewelry, and telecommunications industries. Founded in 1968, TMI is a subsidiary of Brush Engineered Materials . TMI has offices worldwide.
