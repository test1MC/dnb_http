SRG Global is one of the largest manufacturers of chrome plated plastic parts and coatings for vehicles and household appliances. A subsidiary of Guardian Industries , SRG Global is a Tier 1 supplier of automotive front, rear, and body side components. It also produces grilles and exterior trim for commercial trucks. Products for other industries include solar panel frames for renewable energy, roof vents for buildings, and rings, knobs, and trim for consumer appliances. The company has manufacturing operations in North America, Europe, and Asia/Pacific, as well as an advanced development center in Taylor, Michigan, which conducts product research and development on next-generation plastics coating technology.

Guardian Automotive sells to global OEMs including GM , Chrysler , Toyota , and VW , as well as non-automotive customers. Focusing on organic growth and key acquisitions has resulted in a diverse customer base and geographic presence.

The company's 2008 deal for Siegel-Robert Automotive added a production lineup of injection-molded plastic and chrome-plated decorative parts principally sold to automotive manufacturers. (The Siegel-Robert buy included a manufacturing plant and sales office in China, as well as another sales outlet in Japan.) A year earlier, Guardian Automotive pocketed Viracon/Curvlite, a manufacturer of glass-clad laminates for exterior glazing of buildings, from Apogee Enterprises .

Although chrom-plating on automobiles has had its ups and downs, many trucks have kept their chromium accents and consequently kept Guardian busy. However a hit to its workforce and bottom line has been unavoidable with a bad dip in the auto industry. Plating appliance parts, such as Hamilton Beach blender bases and microwave oven doors has helped to reduce its financial exposure.

Guardian Automotive supplies the global automotive industry through its facilities in Asia, Europe, Latin America, the Middle East, and North America.
