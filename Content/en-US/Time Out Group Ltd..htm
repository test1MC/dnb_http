Time Out Group publishes destination guides to the world's top cities, ranking the best restaurants, hotels, and sights. It publishes more than 75 city magazines in some 35 countries. Titles include Time Out Sydney and Time Out Tokyo . Its Time Out New York (or TONY ) is produced through a separate, partially-owned business. The company also publishes a variety of travel magazines and guide books, and operates websites that offer theater ticket sales, film listings, and online reviews of shopping, movie, and dining options. The firm traces its roots to 1968, when co-owner Tony Elliott published the first issue of Time Out London.

All of its media properties together reach a worldwide audience of more than 36 million people. In addition to magazines, books, and websites, Time Out's network includes mobile applications and live events. In addition, its Time Out Deals provides daily deals similar to services provided by Living Social or Groupon .

Most of the company's magazines are not wholly-owned, but published by third parties under license.

Time Out has been focusing its efforts on digital publishing as of late. In 2012 its Time Out New York launched a new e-commerce website, and iPad and iPhone apps. The website allows users to purchase tickets to shows and to book tables at restaurants, while the mobile apps provide information and recommendations about arts and entertainment to users on the go. The apps feature personalization technology that Time Out gained through its 2011 acquisition of LikeCube.

The company plans to grow its portfolio of traditional print magazines as well.

In 2014 the company acquired crowd-sourced event guide Hugecity. The acquisition expanded Time Out's digital offering to a much wider global audience.
