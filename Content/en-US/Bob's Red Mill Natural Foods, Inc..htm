Bob's Red Mill Natural Foods always keeps its nose to the grindstone. The company stone grinds whole grains into bread mixes, cereals, natural and organic flour, and pancake and waffle mixes, as well as gluten-free options. Its 400-plus products are sold at grocery stores throughout the US and Canada. The company also sells bulk mixes to large-scale food manufacturing customers. Bob's Red Mill operates a visitor's center, store, and bakery/cafe in Milwaukie, Oregon, that altogether span 15,000 sq. ft. Octogenarian founder Bob Moore and his wife, Charlee, established Bob's Red Mill in 1978. Fulfilling Moore's intent to share his success with his employees, it became employee-owned in 2010.

From its headquarters in Milwaukie, Oregon, Bob's Red Mill supplies US and Canadian grocery stores with its products.

As part of its business, Bob's Red Mill operates a visitor's center, store, and bakery and cafe beside its headquarters in the Oregon city of Milwaukie, roughly 15 minutes from Portland. The company specializes in making cereals, granola, mixes, steel-cut oats, entrees, and baking aids, among other items. Its lineup includes some 80 gluten-free products made in a gluten-free facility. Some products are packaged in transparent bags in 5-, 25-, and 50-pound paper bags.

In 2013 Bob's Red Mill consolidated its warehouse and distribution operations by leasing a 175,000-sq.-ft. warehouse in Clackamas, about two miles from Milwaukie. The move gives the company 70,000 sq. ft. of additional space for new production and packaging functions.

Bob's Red Mill sells its products in more than 11,000 grocery stores throughout the US and Canada.

Bob's Red Mill has expanded its portfolio of products in recent years to cater to those who suffer from Celiac Disease. To this end, the company has rolled out several new gluten-free products and today sells about 80 items for consumers who follow gluten-free diets.

Based on customer feedback and industry shifts, Bob's Red Mill is focused on five new trends in baking. Product introductions in recent years have addressed many of these trends. Almond Meal is used in low-carbohydrate diets. Xanthan Gum is used in gluten-free baking. Its popular Whole Wheat Pearl Couscous spawned newer versions such as its Whole Wheat Pearl Couscous with Pesto and Vegetables. Coconut Flour is a health alternative to wheat and other grain flours. And, lastly, chia seeds are an ancient South American super food that packs dietary fiber, protein, nutrients, and Omega-3.
