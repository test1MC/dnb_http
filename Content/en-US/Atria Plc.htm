One of the leading meat processors in Finland, Atria Group supplies meat and poultry products and value-added convenience foods. The group, through flagship subsidiary Atria Oy and several other subsidiaries, provides packaged beef and pork products, ready-to-cook steaks, and chicken and turkey products in Finland and the Baltic region. It also offers pre-made meals, fresh salads, and restaurant and catering products. The Finnish food company, founded as a co-op in 1903, manufactures under brand names such as Atria, Forssan, Arboga, Maks & Moorits, and Sibylla. Its primary subsidiaries include Atria Oy, AB Sardus, Liha-Pouttu Oy, and OOO Pit-Product.

Finland is Atria's largest market, accounting for more than 60% of annual sales. Scandinavia accounts for a little more than 25%. Russia represents nearly 10% of sales, while the Baltic states bring in the remainder.

Atria reported net sales of €1.4 billion ($1.9 billion) in 2013.

The Finnish food company is aiming to increase the efficiency of beef and pork procurement and processing at its plant in Jyvaskyla to improve profitability and productivity. Overlapping functions in meat procurement and administration, resulting from the deal concluded between Atria and Saarioinen Oy, will be eliminated. Going forward, the Jyvaskyla plant will focus on bovine and pig slaughtering and cutting. Atria estimates the restructuring and efficiency improvements will generate annual cost savings of about €5 million ($6.8 million).
