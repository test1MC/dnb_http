Crane Composites is a real heavyweight when it comes to the production of lightweight fiberglass. A subsidiary of industrial products and machinery giant Crane Co. , the company manufactures fiberglass-reinforced plastics to replace traditional metals and woods for applications including containers, building products like wall and ceiling tiles, and for sidewalls and roofs of truck trailers and recreational vehicles. Crane Composites sells directly to truck trailer and recreational vehicle (RV) manufacturers, but uses distributors for its construction markets.

Crane Composites operates manufacturing plants in Channahon (Illinois), Florence (Kentucky), Goshen (Indiana), and Grand Junction (Tennessee), and Jonesboro (Arkansas). It also has a non-active plant in Alton, in the UK. It has 12 distribution warehouses in Asia, Europe, Mexico, and the US.

Crane Composites resides within its Engineered Materials division of Crane Co.

Its Florence, Kentucky facility is engaged in manufacturing Building Products Panels; Channahon, Illinois -- Building Products and Transportation Panels; Jonesboro, Arkansas and Goshen, Indiana -- Recreational Vehicle and Transportation Panels.

Crane Composites' Film and Fabrication Services provides custom embossing of thermoformed plastic film. Working with everything from bi-axially oriented polyester to nylon and PVC, it offers a number of popular patterns.

The company serves customers in the recreational vehicle, transportation, film, embossing, fabrication, and building products markets.

There are separate financials for the company. However, the parent company reported that Engineered Materials segment sales (which accounted for 9% of Crane Co.'s revenues in 2013) increased by 7% that year, reflecting higher sales to RV makers, partially offset by lower sales to its transportation-related, non-US, and building product customers.

The demand for Crane Composites' products is closely tied to the fluctuating conditions within the transportation industry. In 2012 the company shut down production at its UK-based Alton manufacturing plant due to slump in demand in Europe stemming from weaker economic conditions.

On the innovation front, in 2013 the company launched a fiberglass reinforced panel line for cleanroom applications requiring "cleanability", low particle emission, biological resistance, and chemical resistance.

The company became a subsidiary of Crane Co. and part of its engineered materials segment in 1985.

Crane Composites was established in 1954. Crane Co. was founded in 1855.
