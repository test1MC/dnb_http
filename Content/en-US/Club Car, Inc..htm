Golfers are thankful for Club Car's products on that long par 5 on the back nine in August. A subsidiary of industrial powerhouse Ingersoll-Rand , Club Car designs and manufactures golf cars, as well as utility cars used in industrial, factory, academic, rough terrain, and grounds maintenance applications. The company has about 80 base models and sells through dealer, distributor, and factory locations worldwide. Club Car's other offerings include mobile refreshment carts used on golf courses, street legal low-speed passenger vehicles (LSV), and GPS systems.

Club Car makes a wide range of gasoline- and electric-powered small vehicles. Best known for its market-leading golf cars and utility vehicles for use on golf courses, the company also manufactures golf cars, utility, and transportation vehicles for commercial, industrial, and recreational uses.

Club Car's worldwide distribution network includes more than 600 distributor, dealer, and factory branch locations. Though the majority of its business is done through either industry or government sales, the company sells a healthy portion of its products to private owners. Its products are sold under the brand names Cafe Express, Precedent, TransPorter, and DS Player.

To improve the performance of its vehicles, in 2012 Club Car has launched a 19-mph upgrade known as the Xcelerator for its gasoline-powered golf cars. These kits feature a drive clutch, driven clutch, CVT belt, accelerator cable, and associated hardware.

Club Car has been making golf carts since 1958.
