Next time you watch a Hollywood blockbuster on the silver screen, give GDC Technology credit for the high-tech experience. The company is a leading manufacturer of digital cinema servers, which enable movie theaters to screen digital video formats. Its digital cinema servers are sold alone or in a package with projectors, lamps, screens, and software from third-party companies. Its systems are sold to movie theaters, and more than half of its customers come from Asia. (The US has its second-largest customer base.) Since its founding in 1999, the company has sold more than 25,000 digital cinema servers. GDC Technology, which is owned by investment firm Carlyle Group , filed an IPO seeking up to $75 million.

GDC Technology plans to use the proceeds for working capital and general corporate purposes.

GDC Technology uses contract manufacturers in Asia to produce the components and parts for its digital cinema servers. It does have two factories of its own, in Hong Kong and China, which assemble and ship the final products.

The company has sales offices in nine countries -- China, Hong Kong, India, Japan, Mexico, Malaysia, Singapore, Spain, and the US. In 2012 60% of sales came from Asia and another 35% came from the US.

GDC Technology sells directly to movie theater chains in China, India, Latin America, and Southeast Asia. In Europe, Japan, South Korea, and the US, it relies on reseller partners. US-based reseller partners include American Cinema Equipment, New England Theatre Service, and Tri State Theatre Supply Co.
