Is a Japanese car technically Japanese if it's manufactured in Canada? You bet! Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada (TMMC) is Toyota Motor's Canadian manufacturing hub. TMMC rolls more than 300,000 Toyota vehicles -- including the Corolla, Matrix, Lexus RX350, and RAV4 models -- off its assembly line each year. The company's plants, which are located in Southern Ontario, include stamping, welding, painting, and assembly operations. Subassembly shops make plastics and powertrain components that are then sent to the assembly units; plastic parts are also produced for the replacement market. Toyota's first new Canadian plant in more than 20 years began production in Woodstock, Ontario, in late 2008.
