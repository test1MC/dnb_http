Wherever you see an electronic glow, it might be a Lite-On -- component. Lite-On Technology manufactures computer software, hardware, peripherals and components; monitors, multi-function and regular printers, cameras and Internet systems and image-processing equipment; information storage equipment, electronic components and office equipment; electronic car products; and optical lens modules and optoelectronic components. Customers have included such noteworthy names as Nokia and Siemens . Lite-On Technology was established in 1975 as Lite-On Electronics to make LEDs for clock displays.

The company has almost 50 factories worldwide, with its global headquarters located in Taiwan. Asia is the company's largest market, representing 73% of net sales. Other major markets include the US (17%) and Europe (10%).

Lite-On divides its operations across four primary segments: information technologies (45% of net sales), optoelectronics (26%), storage (17%), and mobile mechanics and others (12%).

Among Lite-On's many LED products, photo-couplers occupy a 14% market share globally. Lite-On also claims to be the world's largest notebook adapter manufacturer, holding a 60% global market share. Other key products include projector power supplies, phone camera modules, keyboards, and enclosures.

Lite-On's revenue increased 8% from 2013 to 2014. However, its net income decreased 26% during that same time period due to higher operating costs and selling and marketing expenses in addition to a spike in research and development expenses and general and administrative expenses.

The revenue growth for 2014 was fueled by a 30% spike from optoelectronic products due to a surge in market demand for lighting, consumer, and portable devices. It was also driven by higher global demand for energy-saving devices and successful deliveries to Taiwanese and US customers. Lite-On's revenue from server management systems also rose to a record-high in 2014 due to an increase in demand for cloud application server power management systems and portable devices.

Lite-On expands its core offerings through the use of acquisitions. In 2014 it purchased 83% of LarView Technologies Corp. in order to upgrade its capability in the automated processing of camera modules.
