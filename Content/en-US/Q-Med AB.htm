Q-Med seeks a smooth future and hopes a pretty face will help it go far. The company develops and manufactures the high-tech goo Restylane dermatologists inject to fill in lines and creases in facial skin. Its Durolane product, also based on its non-animal sourced hyaluronic acids, is used as an injection to treat osteoarthritis of the knee. Its Deflux and Solesta products are injected to treat certain types of urinary and fecal incontinence, respectively. Its products are distributed in over 70 countries, but only Restylane has received FDA approval for use in the US. Dermatology firm Galderma acquired the company in 2011.
