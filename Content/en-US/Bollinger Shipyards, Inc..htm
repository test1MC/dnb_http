Bollinger Shipyards is a leading provider of shipbuilding and ship repair services for both the military and commercial marine industry on the US Gulf Coast. The family owned and operated company specializes in the construction, repair, and conversion of US Navy and Coast Guard patrol boats, oil tankers, and supply and utility vessels, such as barges and tugboats. It operates from 10 shipyards, primarily in Louisiana, but also in Texas, and has access to the Mississippi River. Its 29 dry docks have the capability to accommodate ships ranging in size from 400 tons to 10,500 tons. It also has a more than 629,000-sq.-ft. facility for constructing vessels indoors.

The company has 10 shipyards strategically located between New Orleans and Houston with direct access to the Gulf of Mexico, Mississippi Rive,r and the Intracoastal Waterway. It is the largest vessel repair company in the Gulf of Mexico region with a total of 29 dry-docks in Louisiana and Texas.

Bollinger Shipyards provides a wide range of shipbuilding, dry-dock, repair and conversion services for both shallow and deepwater vessels and rigs.

The company's new construction programs include both commercial customers, and US military and government programs.

With nearly 600 vessels built to date, the company has delivered a wide variety of commercial vessels including: Tugboats, OPA '90 Tank Barges, Offshore and Inshore Barges, Articulated Tug Barge Units, Crewboats, Power Barges, Pushboats, Other Special Purpose Vessels, Offshore Supply Vessels, Multi-Purpose Support, Vessels, Lift Boats, Dredges, Drilling Rigs, Casino Vessels, Oil Skimmers, and Fishing Vessels.

Bollinger Shipyards was founded in 1946 by Donald Bollinger.

The company's marine construction and repair business, as well as its offshore supply vessel activities were seriously curtailed due the US federal government's moratorium on deepwater drilling in the Gulf of Mexico, which went into affect in the spring of 2010 following the Deepwater Horizon disaster. Bollinger Shipyards was forced to downsize its company and lay off workers.

In 2013 the company delivered the fifth and six Fast Response Cutters (154 foot patrol crafts) to the United States Coast Guard . That year it also delivered the fourth of four Ocean Class tugs for Crowley Maritime .
