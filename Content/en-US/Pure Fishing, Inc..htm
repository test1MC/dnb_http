We're not telling a fish story when we say Pure Fishing is one of the world's largest makers of fishing tackle. It manufactures about 30,000 different types of bait, fishing lures, rods, reels, and lines under brand names including Abu Garcia, All Star, Berkley, Mitchell, Fenwick, SpiderWire, Trilene, and Gulp!, among others. The recreational products company has operations in about 20 countries, from which it provides direct sales, merchandising, and marketing support to more than 15,000 retailers. Pure Fishing is owned by Jarden Corporation and is part of The Coleman Company .

Headquartered in South Carolina, Pure Fishing enjoys a global reach. Besides the US, the company sells its products in Germany, the UK, the Netherlands, Italy, France, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Japan, Australia, New Zealand, Taiwan, Thailand, Malaysia, Europe, and Korea.

To support its business, Pure Fishing maintains warehouse and distribution facilities in Canada, Europe, Latin America, the Pacific Rim, and the US. It makes its products at facilities in China, Europe, Latin America, and North America, as well as through third-party sourcing, primarily in Asia.

Pure Fishing's sales force is divided by geographic region: Canada, Europe, Latin America, the Pacific Rim, and the US. Looking to serve the spectrum of beginner outdoorsmen to extreme outdoor enthusiasts, the company leverages the use of promotions and PR campaigns.

The company also supports the Association of Collegiate Anglers (ACA), which through a 2012 partnership receives more than $100,000 in direct support.

Pure Fishing operates as part of Jarden's Outdoor Solutions segment alongside Coleman. The segment focuses on the global consumer active lifestyle market and contributes about 40% of Jarden's revenue, up from just about 25% several years ago.

While Jarden does not break out separate financials for Pure Fishing, sales in the Outdoor Solutions segment increased 1% in fiscal 2013 as compared to 2012, driven by a 3% boost in net sales on a currency-neutral basis, as well as increases across its camping and outdoor, fishing, technical apparel, and winter sports businesses. Indeed, parent Jarden logged sales rises during the reporting period in certain international markets, specifically Asia, offset in part by unfavorable foreign currency translation.

Jarden, which bought Pure Fishing in 2007 for more than $300 million in cash, had been looking for an acquisition that would extend its reach into more niches of the recreational industry and bring with it a portfolio of recognized brand names. Pure Fishing has benefited from brand growth and a deeper pool of capital resources for expansion.

Pure Fishing boasts licensing agreements with the likes of the NFL , MLB , NBA , numerous collegiate athletics departments, Marvel Entertainment , and Mattel to market and distribute products across several categories.

Pure Fishing makes acquisitions in the US and abroad. The company picked up the Rob Gaden brand name in 2014, when it acquired Cutting Edge Lures, an Australian provider of tropical and freshwater fishing lures. In 2013 it bought Hardy & Greys Limited to strengthen its business in fly fishing. In 2012 the company acquired Beyond Fishing and Sporting Goods (Pty) Ltd., a South African fishing products provider, to get a foothold in South Africa.
