Archos believes entertainment should be personal and portable. The France-based company makes phones and tablets, and connected objects such as quadcopter 'drones' and smart home devices. It makes a range of around 20 smartphone devices and around 10 tablets. It also operates PicoWAN, a low power connected network that enables remote lighting, motion detection, and other such applications. Most of its products operate Google's Android operating system. The majority of its revenues are generated in Europe, but the company markets its products directly and through resellers worldwide. Retail partners in North America include Office Depot , Target , and Wal-Mart .

Archos is headquartered in France, and has offices in Germany, the UK, China, and the US.

Archos sells via distributors, such as Tech Data, Baltine, and Westcoast; retailers such as Argos, ASDA, and Currys; and Online-Shops like Amazon, Clove Technology, Staples, Tesco.

In fiscal 2015, Archos' revenue climbed 20% to €158.7 million amid higher revenue in all segments, but particularly Europe. Europe recorded revenue growth of 14% to €114.5 million. The company recorded a net loss of €1.6 million, although this figure was €10.3 million higher than 2014's figure. Cash from operations increased 44% to €7.9 million.

Archos is strengthen its position as a major European low-power device network. Subsidiary PicoWAN is aiming to be the first LP-WAN (Low Power - Wide Area Network) RF network for connected objects, which will be collaborative, global, crossborder. It was launched in Europe in June 2016.
