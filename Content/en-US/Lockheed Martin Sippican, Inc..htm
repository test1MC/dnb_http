Lockheed Martin Sippican specializes in naval cat and mouse, focusing on military intelligence and defense systems for submarines and naval surface ships. Areas of operation include Sea-Air Systems (expendable probes, oceanographic instrumentation, communications, submarine antennas, and Stinger Missile fuzes), Countermeasures (chaff, infrared, and acoustic decoys), and Underwater Vehicles (self-propelled acoustic and anti-submarine training targets). A subsidiary of Lockheed Martin , the company also has a contract manufacturing subsidiary, Polaris Contract Manufacturing, which makes electronic products.

Sippican works collaboratively with neighboring scientific and educational institutions, including the Massachusetts Ocean Technology Network, the Naval Submarine League, and the Navy League of the US, to name a few.

The Sea-Air Systems division also develops and produces meteorological instrumentation to gather weather information for customers such as the National Weather Service and the US Department of Defense . Additionally, the division manufactures the M934E6 electromechanical fuze for the Stinger Missile, which is a proven missile system in combat situations. The fuze performs the timing and initiation functions of the flight motor ignition, as well as the arming and detonating of the warhead.

Other main divisions include Countermeasures (chaff, infrared, and acoustic decoys) and Underwater Vehicles (self-propelled acoustic and anti-submarine training targets).

When Sippican was acquired in 2004 by Lockheed Martin it became part of Lockheed's Maritime Systems and Sensors division located in Manassas, Virginia. As such, Sippican gained access to Lockheed's vast array of engineering, technical, and financial assets.
