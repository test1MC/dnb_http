Efficient energy use is the cause of Kemco Systems, which designs, manufactures and supplies customized water heating and heat recovery systems and water recycling and treatment units used by commercial, industrial, and government clients. The company has sold more than 5,000 systems worldwide to the automotive, food, laundry, ready-mix concrete, and textile industries. It also sells system components, which include boiler stack economizers, ceramic microfiltration systems, rotary filters, steam generators, and wastewater filtration separators. Kemco Systems is owned by president Lee Kemberling, who founded the company in 1969.

Kemco sells custom-engineered products designed for water treatment, filtration, and processing purposes. In addition to offering process water, water heating and heat recovery systems, it also makes discharge treatment and wastewater recycling systems. The company also sells system component parts, and even previously owned equipment.

Florida-based Kemco sells its systems worldwide.

The company has sold more than 5,000 systems worldwide to the automotive, food, laundry, ready-mix concrete, and textile industries.

To grow its business, Kemco Systems has continued to innovate, touting its customized, energy-efficient, and cost-saving water solutions designed for commercial and industrial clients. In mid-2014, for example, Kemco designed a Ceramic Microfiltration (CMF) system for specialty metals fabricators and metal finishing plants that continuously treats water from their metals parts washer. The CMF system removes suspended solids, metals, oils, and other contaminants and sends them away to be treated and disposed, leaving the filtered water clean enough to be reused.

In 2013, it designed a CMF system with a reverse osmosis component for a bacon processing facility looking to treat and reuse its fat, oil, grease, and sanitation chemical-filled wastewater; a system that earned it an award for one of the top Wastewater Treatment System projects of the year by Water & Wastes Digest.
