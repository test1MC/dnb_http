British-based motorcycle maker Triumph Motorcycle designs and manufactures a range of bikes. Brands include Cruiser (America, Rocket, Speedmaster), Classic (Bonneville, Scrambler, Thruxton), Supersport (Daytona), Roadster (Speed Triple), Adventure (Tiger) and Touring (Sprint). Its Thunderbird edition can be outfitted as a cruiser, street rod, or touring bike. Triumph offers a clothing/accessories line designed to enhance both bike and rider style. Triumph sells its bikes in roughly 30 countries. Around 80% of its net sales come from outside the UK.

Triumph has subsidiary operations in the UK, the US, France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Japan, Sweden, Benelux, and most recently, Brazil. Its manufacturing facilities reside in Hinckley, Leicestershire, and Thailand. In addition, it has an assembly facility in Brazil.

Triumph's product line includes more than 20 models across several motorcycle families. The company manufactures about 50,000 motorcycles per year -- 80% of those bikes are sold outside the UK. In 2014 it launched its newest Triumph Tiger 800 line of motorcycles.

Triumph's journey traces back to 1887, when two Germans, Siegfried Bettmann and Maurtiz Schulte began selling the "safety" bicycle. The Triumph motorcycle was rolled out in 1902. The original company went bankrupt in the early 1980s, but was resurrected in 1991 by Bloor Holdings, which is owned by Sir John Bloor. Bloor was knighted for bringing the motorcycle maker back to life.
