Forget thermometers, Mercury Medical manufactures the medical equipment used to administer and monitor medical gasses for respiratory, anesthesia, and critical care. It also offers technical consulting services, equipment repair, and equipment reconditioning services. Customers include hospitals, biomedical clinics, and home care clients. Along with marketing its own products, its sales team also represents products from other life-support equipment manufacturers. It distributes products directly to institutional customers in the southeastern US; elsewhere it relies on a network of distributors. Mercury Medical ships its medical supplies for home health directly to customers through its ShurShip division.

Mercury Medical represents life support manufacturers exclusively in Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Louisiana, Maryland, Mississippi, North Carolina, Oklahoma, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, and Virginia, and Washington, DC. It also serves customers in Europe, Latin America, the Middle East, Asia, Oceania, and Africa.

The company is a medical device manufacturer and wholesale supplier of airway management products, including cardiopulmonary resuscitators and laryngoscope instruments used to perform intubation procedures. It also makes and supplies specialty accessories used in anesthesia, respiratory therapy, neonatal intensive care, emergency medicine and biomedical technical repair departments. Mercury Medical also manufactures Respiratory Devices for anesthesia, emergency and critical care products.

The company's core operations include Biomedical Clinical Services, EMS and Alternate Site Services (Home Care), Hospital Sales, International, Medical Gas Certification, and Medical Manufactured Products.

Its Home Care unit provides respiratory equipment and services, including CPAP/BiPAP, home ventilation, Oxygen, HME, enteral equipment and supplies including specialized high-tech pediatrics.

Mercury Medical products sells via US based exporters and in-country business partners through exclusive stocking wholesale supplier agreements, OEMs, and strategic partnerships.

The company pursues partnerships to expand its reach into key US and international markets by offering potential partners expertise in manufacturing, OEM partnerships and engineering. It also focuses on product portfolio expansion.

In 2013 Mercury Medical signed a two-year deal with ETView Medical to market ETView's VivaSight platform (which combines an airway ventilation tube with integrated continuous high resolution airway imaging for patient airway control and lung isolation capability) in Florida, Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, Virginia, Maryland, Tennessee, Arkansas, Alabama, Mississippi, Louisiana, Texas, and Oklahoma, and Washington, DC..

In 2012 it formed an alliance with Parker Medical to sell and distribute Parker's airway management products. These products include Parker's Flex-Tip Endotracheal Tubes, which are designed to prevent damage to the patient's airway anatomy, even during intubation of difficult airways. It also sells the Parker Flex-It Intubating Stylet which remotely curves the endotracheal tube from outside the mouth during intubations.
