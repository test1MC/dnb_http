Hammond Power Solutions (HPS) has its hands deep in the power business across North America. The company designs and manufactures transformers and related electrical products used in industrial applications. The HPS product line includes control transformers, dry-type power transformers, rectifier transformers, electrical line reactors, and voltage regulators. The company also touts its expertise and dominant market position in custom magnetic equipment. Hammond Power Solutions operates manufacturing plants in Canada, Italy, Mexico, and the US and sells products worldwide through distributors.

HPS is headquartered in Guelph, Ontario, Canada and has seven facilities located in Guelph, Ontario; Walkerton, Ontario; Baraboo, Wisconsin; Monterrey, Mexico; Compton, California; and Granby, Quebec. In addition, it has operations and manufacturing plants in the US, Mexico, Italy, and India with 11 distribution centers.

The US and Mexico collectively contributes 58% of its net sales. Other major markets include Canada (30%), Italy (7%), and India (5%).

The company sells its products via a direct sales force and independent sales agencies. It also sells via sales representatives and distribution centers in Canada, US, India, and Italy.

HPS' revenues increased 2% from 2013 to 2014. (Note: growth rates may differ after conversion to the US dollar.) Its net income, however, declined 58% from 2013 to 2014 mainly due to foreign exchange losses.

The revenue growth for 2014 was fueled by an uptick in sales from India; an expansion of its North American Electrical Distributor (NAED) footprint; and increases in alternative energy, commercial projects, and capital equipment market segments in Canada. In addition, bookings jumped due to stronger bookings in both the distributor and direct channels in the North American and international markets.

For growth, the company continues to expand its distribution network and OEM supply agreements. Hammond Power Solutions also looks to sell its custom engineered transformers to both new and existing markets, including the alternative energy, mining equipment, oil and gas, and motor control drives industries. Its transformers have also been used in restaurants, including Pizza Hut , Dominos , Tim Hortons , and Wendys .
