Thomas the Tank Engine might be a good read, but Thomas Built Buses makes K-12 children really happy, especially if they live a long way from school. The company manufactures approximately a third of the school buses operating in the US, ranging from mini to full liner in size. It also makes non-school vehicles for commercial and special activity transit markets. Its core portfolio consists of activity, childcare, commercial, green, school, and specialty buses. Thomas Built Buses operates as a subsidiary of Daimler Trucks North America from plants in High Point, North Carolina.

Thomas Built Buses operates through its several manufacturing plants located in High Point, North Carolina.

The portfolio of Thomas Built Buses is sold through a network of more than 50 independent dealers dotting North America.

Thomas Built Buses was founded in 1916 as Perley A. Thomas Car Works, to manufacture and renovate streetcars (and later, trolley buses). It moved into the production of school buses in 1936 and was acquired by Freightliner in 1998.
