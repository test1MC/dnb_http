Fluke's products are really hip (pocket-sized, that is). Fluke, a subsidiary of Danaher (maker of Craftsman tools), makes handheld electronic test tools used by electricians, HVAC technicians, and engineers to install, maintain, and service electrical and electronic equipment. Its multimeters, oscilloscopes, and other devices measure current, voltage resistance, frequency, pressure, temperature, and air quality. It also makes calibrators and calibration software, waveform generators, and power harmonics meters. Its Fluke Biomedical unit makes patient simulators, diagnostic imaging, and radiation safety products, among others. Fluke has production facilities in the US, the UK, Asia, and the Netherlands.

Companies owned by Fluke include Comark (thermometers, pressure, and humidity meters), Datapaq (temperature measurement for hostile thermal environments), DH Instruments (pressure and gas flow instruments), Fluke Networks (network testing of copper, fiber, and wireless networks), Hart Scientific (thermometers and temperature calibration), Ircon (industrial temperature measurement), and Raytek (thermometers and thermal imagers).

Like Tektronix , which was Fluke's traditional instrument rival in the US prior to its acquisition in 2007, Fluke is part of parent company Danaher's Electronic Test and Measurement business; the two subsidiaries continue to operate as stand-alone businesses.

In addition to sales and services subsidiaries located in North and South America, Europe, Australia, and Asia, Fluke sells products through distributors and manufacturer representatives in more than 100 countries worldwide.

Fluke continues to grow by expanding applications for its tools and by acquiring companies and complementary product lines. In 2012 Fluke acquired TTL Technologies, a distributor that has represented Fluke in India since 2001. Fluke also purchased test tool manufacturer Martel Electronics Corp. in 2012 and bolstered its calibration product group.

In 1948 John Fluke, an MIT roommate of Hewlett-Packard co-founder David Packard, started the John Fluke Engineering Company in his Springdale, Connecticut, basement; he produced a power meter the next year. The company, renamed John Fluke Manufacturing, moved to the Seattle area in 1953.
