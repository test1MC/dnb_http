Thomas & Betts (T&B) bets on its good connections. The company provides electrical connectors, HVAC equipment, and transmission towers to the commercial construction, industrial, and utility markets through thousands of distributor locations and wholesalers in North America. Its segments include electrical (electrical connectors, enclosures, raceways, installation tools); and steel structures (poles and transmission towers for power companies). Brands include Color-Keyed, Elastimold, Kindorf, Red Dot, and Steel City. T&B is a subsidiary of power and automation technology powerhouse ABB Ltd .

T&B's headquarters is stationed in Memphis, Tennessee, and it operates in the Asia Pacific, Canada, Australasia, Europe/Middle East/Africa, and the US.

The company has a portfolio of more than 200,000 products which its markets under more than 45 premium brand. It operates as a part of ABB 's Low Voltage Products segment,

TB distributes its products through 6,000 distributors and wholesalers in North America, and through ABB's well-established distribution channels in Europe and Asia.

The Low Voltage Products segment accounted for about 19% of the ABB's total revenues in 2014.

In mid-2014 ABB divested T&B's HVAC business for $260 million to Nortek . A few months later ABB completed the sale of T&B's Meyer Steel Structures business to Trinity Industries . The transaction was valued at $600 million. Both divestitures were in line with ABB's strategy to continuously optimize its portfolio and to focus its efforts on driving growth in its core automation and power businesses.

Improving its visibility and customer access, in 2013 T&B launched an iPad application which provides full access to the company's product, application and industry information, as well as distributor services, multimedia presentations, and electrical industry news and information.

In 1898 in New York City, Princeton engineering graduates Hobart D. Betts and Robert McKean Thomas founded a distribution company for the infant electrical industry; they quickly moved into designing conduits and fittings. In 1905 the company incorporated as Thomas and Betts Company, and in 1912 it began manufacturing with the purchase of Standard Electric Fittings. In 1917 the two merged to form The Thomas & Betts Company, consolidating operations in Elizabeth, New Jersey.

In mid-2012, a new chapter in T&B's history occurred when it was acquired by ABB Ltd for about $3.9 billion. ABB made the transaction in order to double its low-voltage products inventory and gain significant ground in the US market. At the time of the transaction, Zurich-based ABB had a presence in about 100 countries and was primarily known for selling circuit breakers, switchgear, transformers, sensors, breakers, switches, generators and power electronics systems.
