EDAC Technologies designs, manufactures, and services complex rotating aircraft engine parts and other precision industrial components and assemblies (such as rotating seals, disks, shafts, and turbine cases) for leading engine OEMs. It serves the aerospace and industrial markets through three major product lines: EDAC AERO, EDAC Machinery, and Apex Machine Tool. EDAC also provides grinding, welding, painting, and assembly services, and its facilities include computerized, numerically controlled (CNC) machining centers. Aerospace clients, such as manufacturer United Technologies Corporation (UTC), account for most of the company's sales.

In early 2013 EDAC agreed to be acquired by GB Aero Engine LLC, an affiliate of private equity firm Greenbriar Equity Group, for $104 million.

EDAC operates throughout Asia, Europe, and North America. International sales represented about 30% of revenue in 2011, which is up from about 20% in 2010.

UTC, which contracts with the company to make jet engine components for the Joint Strike Fighter, accounts for more than a third of EDAC's sales; MTU Aero Engines accounts for 12%. UTC subsidiary, Pratt & Whitney , is also a client, along with Boeing . Some 80% of the company's business is generated by its aerospace customers.

EDAC reported $87 million in revenue in 2011, which is up nearly 20% from 2010 (itself up more than a third from the prior year). The company saw strong growth across all product lines, including 15% growth in its largest product line (EDAC Aero) as sales to major aircraft engine makers increased.

Profit hit nearly $4 million that year, up some 320% from 2010 when net income was at its lowest level since a loss in 2002.

Over the years, EDAC has made a number of acquisitions, allowing it to actively grow its product lines to strengthen its business in the aerospace industry. In mid-2012, it strengthened its core offerings when it obtained EBTEC Corporation, a provider of advanced precision manufacturing and fabrication processes catering to the aerospace, power generation, industrial, semiconductor, and medical sectors. EDAC bought EBTEC for $11 million.

In 2010 EDAC acquired New Hampshire-based Accura Technics, a grinding machinery specialist, for $300,000. In 2009 the company purchased the assets of Service Network Incorporated (SNI) for $775,000; SNI manufactures and rebuilds precision grinders. Accura Technics, EBTEC, and SNI give the company new capabilities and technologies that expand its market share in the aerospace industry.
