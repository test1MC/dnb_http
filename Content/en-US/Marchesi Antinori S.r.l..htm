For a company more than 600 years old, Antinori certainly has a lot of spirit(s). It is one of Italy's largest and most prestigious wineries, producing more than 10 million bottles of wine each year. The company owns 4,200 acres throughout the country's Tuscany and Umbria regions, as well as investments in vineyards in Napa Valley, California, and Szekszard, Hungary, among other locations. Antinori, known for rebelling against traditional wine-making methods, has created unusual wines (such as its Tignanello) that defy classification. Antinori also runs a handful of restaurants in Italy, Austria, and Switzerland. The family-owned and -run company traces its roots to 1385.
