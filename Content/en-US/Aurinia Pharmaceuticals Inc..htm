Aurinia Pharmaceuticals (formerly Isotechnika Pharma) may have isolated a new drug to combat organ rejection. Lead drug candidate voclosporin could replace cyclosporine as the immunosuppressant of choice for organ transplant recipients and patients with autoimmune conditions. Studies show it is more effective and has fewer side effects than cyclosporine. The company is testing the drug for kidney transplant recipients and psoriasis. Minority shareholder Paladin Labs has signed on to develop the drug in certain markets. The Diagnostics division sells "breathalyzer" tests to diagnose H. pylori infections, which cause chronic gastritis and ulcers. In mid-2013 Isotechnika merged with Aurinia Pharmaceuticals.
