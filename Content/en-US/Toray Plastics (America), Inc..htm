Toray Plastics (America), or TPA, makes polyolefin foam that is used for automotive and flooring applications. The company also produces polypropylene (PP) and polyester films for the packaging and industrial markets. It produces 188 million pounds of film annually. The only US producer of both PP and polyester films, TPA is a subsidiary of Tokyo-based Toray Industries (a manufacturing conglomerate of synthetic and carbon fibers, plastics, chemicals, and high performance films). TPA was founded in 1985.

TPA operates from two locations. Its Rhode Island headquarters manufactures polyester and polypropylene films, used for magnetic tape, food packaging, capacitor, and industrial applications.

A Virginia plant manufactures foams for the automotive and flooring markets. The foam is used for automotive interiors, such as door and instrument panels, armrests, seat backs, consoles, and air ducts.

TPA operates through three divisions: Torayfan Polypropylene Film, Lumirror Polyester Film, and Toraypef Olefin Foams.

Automotive customers have included such noteworthy names as Nissan , Honda , BMW , Toyota , and General Motors .

TPA is part of its parent company's Plastics and Chemicals segment, which generated about 25% of total sales in 2014. This segment also saw its sales rise by 19% in 2014 as a result of a rise in automotive applications in North America and Japan.

The company has invested more than $700 million in its operations, and it has increased its production capacity by over 1,000% in the past 20 years. It focuses on attracting additional clients by expanding its product offering through technology expansion.

In 2014 TPA introduced the Torayfan CBS2 film, an environment-conscious, clear, high-oxygen- and moisture-barrier BOPP film designed for use as the inside sealant web for the packaging of bakery items, as well as pet food and treats and non-food items.
