Custom Building Products (CBP) likes to hold things together. The producer of tile-setting grout and adhesives makes such products as epoxies, cement, household and industrial glues, sealants, acrylic resins, clay mortars, putties, and latex, as well as gypsum products and concrete repair products. The company also offers installation tools, cleaning and maintenance equipment, and a library of how-to-do-it and other resource materials. CBP manufactures and distributes its wares from about a dozen locations in North America. Its customers include architects, contractors, dealers, distributors, and do-it-yourselfers. The QUIKRETE Companies owns CBP.

In 2013 former parent Kelso , a private equity firm, sold CBP to The QUIKRETE Companies for $765 million.

The company manufactures its products in about a dozen locations across North America. It has manufacturing plants in California, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Nevada, New Jersey, and Texas, and in Canada.

The company's operations and products include Surface Preparation (cement backerboards, flooring prep, membranes and underlayments, mud bed and patching compounds); Setting Materials (additives, mastics/adhesives and a wide range of mortars); Grout Materials (caulk, cement grout, epoxy grout, grout enhancers); Care and Maintenance (cleaners, enhancers, grout colorant, sealers, stone restoration); Shower Installation Systems; Tools and Accessories; and Thin Veneer Installation Systems.

CBP offers surface preparation products such as underlayments (for leveling, sound reduction, waterproofing, and crack prevention) and backerboards.

Its tool offerings range from prep, set, and finish tools to wet saw replacement parts and blades. CBP also offers a line of Platinum Tools, designed in consultation with contractors to perform specific jobs that regular tools are not designed to do.

CBP's product brands include CEG and Polyblend grouts; a comprehensive line of lightweight mortars; TileLab and Aqua Mix maintenance products; WonderBoard Lite backerboards; RedGard waterproofing membrane; SimpleMat tile setting mat; Fusion Pro; Prism; and SuperiorBiltÂ® tools.

The company has distribution centers in California (Bakersfield, Bell, and Stockton), Florida (Miami), Georgia (Lithia Springs), Indiana (Frankfort), Nevada (Las Vegas), New Jersey (Logan Township), Texas (Grand Prairie), and in Canada. It sells its products to distributors to contractors, architects, designers, homeowners, and do-it-yourselfers in the US and around the world.

CBP seeks to maintain its edge as an industry leader in the tile and stone industry by providing not only a growing portfolio of premier products but by also providing strong customer support to back up the installation and use of its various products.

In 2013 it introduced a new line of medium-bed mortars designed for large format tile and stone installation and an industrial grade commercial epoxy grout, a water-cleanable epoxy grout that is specially formulated for the harshest chemical environments. That year it also extended its RedGard waterproofing product line with the launching of the RedGard Waterproofing Fabric Membrane system, a waterproof membrane for walls and floors.

The company has been the exclusive distributor of WonderBoard backerboard since 1983, and it gained exclusive manufacturing rights to it in 1989.

CBP was founded in 1964 by Thomas Peck and Mike Bilek. Kelso bought California-based Custom Building Products for $600 million in 2005.
