General Bearing has been on a roll for more than half a century. The SKF subsidiary sources, assembles, and distributes roller bearings primarily under the Hyatt brand name. Products include ball bearings, tapered roller bearings, precision roller bearings, spherical roller bearings, and other related components. The lineup is sold primarily to OEMs of trains, trucks, trailers, office equipment, and appliances, as well as to industrial aftermarket distributors. Based in the US, General Bearing operates an array of plants and an engineering and technology center through a handful of facilities in China.

In 2012 General Bearing was acquired by rival bearings manufacturer SKF for about $125 million.

Based in West Nyack, New York, the company has factories in China.

General Bearing primarily targets OEM and end-user customers within the truck, trailer, automotive, and industrial transportation markets. The company's products are used in automobiles, railroad cars, locomotives, trucks, office equipment, machinery, and appliances. It markets its products through distributors and sales representatives.

General Bearing operates as a part of the automotive segment of its parent. This segment contributed 27% to SKF's net revenues in 2013.

Being acquired by SKF significantly extended General Bearing's product reach worldwide as SKF supplies bearings, seals, mechatronics, lubrication systems, and other products across more than 130 countries. Its new parent company also has 15,000 distributor locations around the globe.

The company was founded in 1958 by Seymour Gussack.
