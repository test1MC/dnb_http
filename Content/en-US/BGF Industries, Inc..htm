BGF wants to be your new best friend when it comes to high performance fabrics. The company manufactures woven and non-woven fabrics from glass, carbon, aramid, and silica yarns. The fabrics strengthen, insulate, and stabilize hundreds of products within the sectors of aerospace, transportation, construction, electronics, power generation, and oil refining. Products include hot gas filtration systems, aircraft and auto parts, bullet-proof vests, movie theater screens, and surfboards. Aerialite is its flagship fabric brand. BGF is a US subsidiary of France-based Porcher Industries.

BGF is stationed in Greensboro, North Carolina, and it operates six facilities in three states, including a sales and research center in North Carolina, four manufacturing facilities in Virginia, and one in South Carolina. International locations resides in Brazil, Hong Kong, and France.

BGF sells its more than 2,000 product lines worldwide through sales offices and distribution channels. It targets the advanced composites, construction, electrical, filtration, insulation, protective, and specialty industries.

Founded in 1885 as Hess Goldsmith & Company, a maker of fine silk fabrics, the company teamed up with Owens Corning in 1928 to develop fiberglass textiles. Following its merger with Horace Linton & Brothers in 1951, the company was acquired by Burlington Industries in 1956. Current owner Porcher Industries bought the unit from Burlington Industries in 1988.
