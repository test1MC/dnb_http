Faurecia Exhaust Systems, once known as AP Automotive Systems, ends each day exhausted but rewarded. Reportedly, one in five vehicles in the world operates with an exhaust system made by this company. Faurecia Exhaust Systems produces complete automotive exhaust systems, including manifolds, tailpipes, and other exhaust system components for such major carmakers as Volkswagen , Peugeot , and Ford . Its manufacturing facilities are located in the US, as well as Mexico, the Netherlands, Sweden, the Czech Republic, South Africa, and China. The company operates as part of the emissions control technologies business group of French automotive equipment maker Faurecia , which is owned by Peugeot .

Faurecia Exhaust Systems operates as part of the Emission Control Technologies segment of Faurecia . Sister company Faurecia Interior Systems makes and markets automotive interiors, including door panels, seats, instrument panels, and consoles.

Customers include General Motors , Volvo Car , and Volkswagen and other global automakers.

The Emission Control Technologies segment accounted for 35% of the parent company's revenues in 2012. Faurecia also got 27% of its revenues from North America that year. North American revenues were up 19% in 2012 as the result of growing light vehicle production, Faurecia's commercial momentum ( Mercedes ML and GL, Volkswagen Passat, Chrysler 200 and Jeep Grand Cherokee), and the Saline (Michigan) factory acquisition. (The Saline plant was purchased from Ford and integrated into Faurecia Interior Systems).

The company is taking advanced of the increased public to control pollutant emissions, especially in the developing world which has seen a rapid growth in automobile use.

In 2013 Faurecia Emissions Control Technologies established a new joint venture with Suzhou PowerGreen Emission System Solution Co. Ltd, to jointly manufacture emissions control systems in Shanghai for Guangxi Yuchai Machinery, one China's largest commercial engine makers.

That year Faurecia Emissions Control Technologies opened a plant in Bangalore to provide products for Toyota , which produced almost 215,000 vehicles in India in 2012.

Faurecia Exhaust Systems capitalized on the accelerating recovery after the automotive industry's near-collapse in 2009. In late 2010 Ford announced plans to retool its assembly plant in Louisville, Kentucky, and produce a new fuel-efficient vehicle. Faurecia Exhaust Systems has responded with a $5 million investment to ramp up a neighboring manufacturing plant. The move nearly doubles its plant's workforce as well as gains $2.5 million in state tax incentives.
