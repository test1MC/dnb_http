Hear that siren behind you? Pierce Manufacturing makes the fire trucks and equipment that help put out fires. The company is a subsidiary of Oshkosh Corporation 's Fire & Emergency business and the top North American maker of custom fire equipment. Its lineup includes aerials, commercial pumpers, elliptical tankers, and rescue trucks. The company also builds homeland security apparatus designed to help police and emergency crews better respond to terrorist events. Many vehicle bodies are assembled on major commercial chassis brands to meet customer needs, such as the US Army 's. Its innovations include the industry's first side roll protection and frontal airbag systems for fire apparatus.

Pierce has manufacturing facilities in Wisconsin and Florida and operates more than 50 service centers throughout the US.

Pierce's fire apparatus are sold through over 30 independent sales and service organizations (supported by approximately 75 product and marketing support professionals) with approximately 300 sales representatives in the US and Canada. Pierce also sells directly to the Department of Defense and other government agencies; and to numerous international municipal and industrial fire departments through international dealers. Sales to the US government accounted for 36% of its parent's net sales, of which the majority was derived from multi-year contracts and programs in the defense vehicle market during 2013.

Oshkosh Corporation's Fire & Emergency segment contributed about 10% of its parent's total revenues for 2013. About 80% of its total revenue comes from the US.

Pierce is targeting municipal and volunteer fire departments for growth. It continues to generate orders for its new Dash CF fire vehicles from these customers throughout the states of Arizona, Delaware, Colorado, Indiana, Illinois, Kansas, Pennsylvania, Maine, Missouri, New York, Tennessee, Texas, and Wisconsin.
