James Avery has crafted himself quite a successful enterprise. James Avery Craftsman is a top jewelry designer, manufacturer, and retailer in the US, mostly throughout the southern states. Its jewelry collection, numbering some 1,100 designs and 14,000 items made in sterling silver, 14-, and 18-karat yellow and white gold, and gemstones, is sold through about 200 independent retailers and more than 70 of its own stores mostly in Texas, but also in 10 other states. James Avery also sells its products through a catalog and via the Internet. The company got its start in 1954 when James Avery set up shop in a two-car garage with about $250 in capital. The company is still family owned and is run by Avery's sons.

James Avery has five manufacturing plants, one each in Hondo, Fredericksburg, and Kerrville, Texas, and two in Comfort, Texas. Texas is the jewelry chain's largest market, home to 55 of its 70-plus stores. James Avery also has stores in several other US states, including Alabama, Arkansas, Colorado, Georgia, Kansas, Louisiana, Missouri, North Carolina, Oklahoma, and Tennessee.

The company is focused on designing, crafting, and producing jewelry in sterling silver, gold, and gemstones. Its portfolio of products includes bracelets, charms, earrings, crosses, necklaces, pendants, pins, and rings. It also offers engravable, religious, and purity jewelry. James Avery carries other items, including bookmarks, chains, cameo cards, cuff links, jewelry keepers and cleaners, key chains, money clips, ornaments, and wall plaques, among others.

James Avery typically targets men and women, but its portfolio boasts items for children, as well.

At a time when many jewelry manufacturers and retailers are still recovering from the downturn in the economy, James Avery is busy breaking ground. In 2014 the chain opened two new stores in Texas -- one in Frisco outside Dallas and another in Austin -- that feature an updated store design. In 2013 it opened a shop in Georgia, and a retail store and visitors' center at its headquarters in Kerrville, as well as its first store in Laredo.

Namesake founder James Avery stepped down in 2007; today his sons Chris (president and CEO) and Paul (EVP) run the business.
