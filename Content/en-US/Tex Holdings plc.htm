You won't find Tex Holdings home on the range, but the company is quite comfortable at construction sites. The company, operating through several subsidiaries, produces industrial boring and construction equipment (asphalt and concrete mixers, chip spreaders, compactors, drills, emulsion sprayers, and hammers), plastic products (custom injection moulding), and retail display boards and panels. Its Tex ATC unit designs, manufactures, and installs prefabricated air traffic control rooms. The company's Allied Kiosks & Enclosures makes wall-, post-, or floor-mounted enclosures. The UK accounts for about 85% of sales.

The company operates three divisions: Engineering, Plastics, and Boards & Panels.

The Plastics division conducts precision injection molding, assembly and finishing services, and is the largest division, accounting for over 60% of total sales.

The Engineering division (around 30% of total sales) designs and manufactures a range of piling and dynamic compaction equipment.

The Boards & Panels division (9%) manufactures and sells boards and panels.

The company is based in the UK and its home market accounts for around 85% of total sales; the rest is made up by the eurozone (6%), the US (4%), and other countries.

Selected clients of Tex Holdings include Keltbray, Colets Piling, Watson & Hillhouse, Van Elle Ltd, Dynamic Ground Solutions, PTC France, Casagrande, and Jinnings.
