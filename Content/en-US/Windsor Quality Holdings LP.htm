Windsor Quality Holdings makes frozen foods for lovers of ethnic flavors. Also known as Windsor Foods, it offers stuffed pastas, egg rolls, pot stickers, barbecue, chili, and burritos, among other flavorful fare. Brands include Bernardi (Italian), Golden Tiger (Pan-Asian), José Olé (Mexican), and The Original Chili Bowl (Southwest). Windsor serves the nation's foodservice operators, chain restaurants, and industrial customers, along with retail grocery, convenience, warehouse, and club stores such as Costco and Sam's Club . It provides private-label, custom blending and packaging, and marketing support services for its customers. Windsor operates nearly 10 production plants in seven states.

Based in Houston, Texas, Windsor develops, produces, and markets some of the nation's favorite ethnic foods from operations in seven US states.

As part of its business, Windsor operates nine FDA -approved manufacturing facilities in Texas, California, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, Missouri, Illinois, and Mississippi. It boasts 27 production lines that pump out a variety of products under name brands such as Bernardi, Golden Tiger, The Original Chili Bowl, José Olé, Posada, Whitey's Chili, Cripple Creek, and Fred's.

During the past few years, Windsor had been boosting its presence in the grocery retail market, fortifying its sales force, rolling out new ethnic items, and streamlining its manufacturing costs. As part of the agreement, Windsor picked up a pair of plants in California and Texas.

Windsor has earned the distinction as the brand leader across the largest categories of ethnic frozen foods, such as Asian, Mexican, and Italian. The company caters to US foodservice operators, chain restaurants, and industrial customers, as well as retail grocery, convenience, warehouse, and club stores such as Costco and Sam's Club.

To give teeth to its Golden Tiger-branded Asian business, Windsor in 2011 bought Discovery Foods, based in California. The maker of frozen Asian snacks, appetizers, and entrees sells products under the Ling Ling, Tai Pei, and VIP brand names. While Windsor holds top positions in the Italian and Mexican frozen food segments, purchasing Discovery Foods positions the firm's Asian category for similar accolades.

The company was founded in 1996 after it acquired certain frozen-food operations from Keebler (now Kellogg U.S. Snacks ). Windsor continued to grow in 2004 with the purchase of Specialty Brands (maker of Mexican and Italian frozen entrees).
