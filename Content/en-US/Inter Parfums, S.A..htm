Inter Parfums, S.A. is the French sister company to US-based fragrance and personal care firm Inter Parfums, Inc . Inter Parfums, S.A. is focused on the company's prestige fragrance lines, licensed by the European brands Jimmy Choo , Lanvin, Montblanc , Nickel, Paul Smith, S.T. Dupont , and Van Cleef & Arpels. Its eight Burberry fragrances are top sellers, accounting for more than half of sales. The company generates the majority of its sales in Europe. Inter Parfums, S.A. also distributes Nickel skin care products for men and owns three Nickel Spas for men in each of London, New York, and Paris. Inter Parfums, Inc. controls the company.

Inter Parfums, S.A. also majority-owns three distribution subsidiaries in Europe, Inter Parfums Limited (in the UK), Inter Parfums Gmbh (Germany) and Inter Parfums srl (Italy), and it's the sole owner of Spain's distribution subsidiary, Inter España Parfums et Cosmetiques, SL. Other wholly owned subsidiaries include Inter Parfums (Suisse) S.A., a company formed to hold and manage certain brand names, Interparfums Singapore Pte., Ltd., an Asian sales and marketing office, and Interparfums Luxury Brands, Inc., which distributes prestige brands in the US.

The company sells directly to customers through department stores, specialty stores and duty free shops.

Burberry bought back its fragrance license from Inter Parfums in 2013 and sales dropped as a result. Burberry had been Inter Parfums' most important partner. However, since then the company has shaken off the hit and returned to growth, post a sales increase of 10% in fiscal 2015 €326 million. The increase in sales relates to the acquisition of fashion and fragrance brand Rochas from Procter & Gamble in 2015. Net income rose 26% to €29.1 million as a result.

The loss of the Burberry license in 2013 was a blow to the company, and it has been working to replace it by striking new agreements. In 2015, it acquired the Rochas fashion brand from Procter & Gamble for $108 million, and in the same year it reached an 11-year agreement with Coach Inc, a New York design house, to produce, create and distribute new perfumes for men and women. It also extended its exclusive worldwide license to make perfumes for Montblanc through to 2020.

Marketing perfumes for established luxury brands has proved a lucrative concept for founders Philippe Benacin and Jean Madar, who started the company in 1982 and took it public in 1995.
