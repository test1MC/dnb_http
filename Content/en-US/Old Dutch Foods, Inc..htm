You don't need to be an old Dutchman (or Dutchwoman) to enjoy this company's products. You just have to enjoy crunching and munching. Old Dutch Foods makes salty snack foods, such as potato chips, popcorn, cheese curls, pretzels, tortilla chips, meat sticks, dips, and salsas under the Old Dutch and Dutch Gourmet labels. The company's products are available in Iowa, Michigan's Upper Peninsula, Minnesota, North and South Dakota, Wisconsin, and Canada. It enjoys a presence in Canada through its 2006 purchase of Humpty Dumpty Snack Foods . Humpty Dumpty-branded snacks, which are sold only in Canada, include corn chips, party mix, cheese sticks, potato sticks, and onion rings.

Based in Minnesota, Old Dutch Foods operates manufacturing facilities in Minnesota, as well as in Canada in Airdrie, Calgary, Winnipeg, Dartmouth, Mississauga, and Hartland.

Old Dutch Foods markets its snack foods through retailers across North America. Its brands include Old Dutch Potato Chips, Humpty Dumpty, Dutch Crunch, Dutch Gourmet, Ripples, and Restaurant Style Tortilla Chips.

Old Dutch Foods produces and sells snacks, offering traditional potato chips, ripple potato chips, Dutch crunch kettle chips, Dutch gourmet products, party mix products, restaurant style premium tortilla chips, restaurant style salsas, dips, gourmet popcorn, puffcorn, pretzels, baked crunchy curls, and box chips.

With a handful of production plants in Canada, Old Dutch Foods shuttered its Lachine plant in late 2013 rather than renovate the former Humpty Dumpty facility, which was built in 1964.

Founded in 1934 in St. Paul, Minnesota, the company used to be known as Old Dutch Products. It changed names to Old Dutch Foods during WWII to identify itself as a food company since it was frequently mistaken for the manufacturer of the popular-at-the-time powdered-cleaning product Old Dutch Cleanser.
