Telkonet runs its own Internet of things, but its things are thermostats and other energy controls for hotels, campuses, and other properties. The company's SmartEnergy and EcoSmart line of energy efficiency-related systems are used to manage and monitor HVAC consumption. It also provides high-speed Internet access without the high-cost network upgrades through its EthoStream product line, which enables computer network and Internet access over electrical lines rather than communications cables. In addition to equipment sales, it recognizes recurring support revenue from the 2,300 hotels that use its EthoStream broadband Internet systems. All of its product are marketed primarily to the hospitality industry. Customers include InterContinental , Marriott , and Wyndham .

Telkonet offers the EcoSmart line of products that can automate heating and cooling and manage energy costs. The products run on the EcoCentral platform that manages the half a million intelligent devices connected to its network. Everything runs on the EcoStream Internet service the company provides.

The company receives about three-quarters of its revenue from products and the rest through recurring charges for its Internet service. It supports about 237,000 rooms with high-speed Internet access and about eight million monthly users.

The company is based in Waukesha, Wisconsin and does most of its business in the US.

Telkonet's primary markets are Hospitality, Education, Military, Health Care, and Public Housing. Hospitality customers include Hilton Worldwide , Hyatt Hotels & Resorts , and White Lodging. On its educational customer list are the University of California Davis , Northern Oklahoma College, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology , Fordham University , US Military Academy , Columbia University , and the University of Wisconsin .

Telkonet reported a 7% revenue increase in 2014, reaching $14.8 million compared to $13.9 in 2013. More than half of the increase came from sales through channel partners and value-added resellers. The company's net income slipped into the black in 2014 with a $40,000 profit, compared to a $4 million loss in 2013.

Telkonet has emphasized sales through partners and hopes to get a lift from a partnership with Stanley Energy, a subsidiary of Stanley Elevator. Stanley has relationships with thousands of building owners and universities in New England.

In 2014, Telkonet released the EcoCentral Virtual Engineer for iOS and Android operating systems, which give building managers the capability of controlling HVAC systems from their phones.
