Vera Bradley loves women with lots of baggage. The company designs, makes, and sells quilted handbags and travel bags, as well as accessories, such as cosmetic bags, curling iron covers, and wallets. Its goods are available through 2,700 gift and specialty stores and about 100 Vera Bradley full-price stores in 25-plus US states. It also operates about 30 factory outlet shops and an online store. In addition to its core products, the company licenses its name for use on Vera Bradley-branded rugs, eyewear, stationery, and home decor items. Founded in 1982 as Vera Bradley Designs by Patricia Miller and Barbara Bradley Baekgaard to provide stylish luggage for women, the company went public in 2010.

The company operates its retail business nationwide, with stores in more than 25 states, and globally through e-commerce sites.

Revenue from bags accounted for 45% of total net sales in fiscal 2015.

The Vera Bradley name has a strong brand identity and courts an upscale market with its range of product offerings, stylish store d√©cor, and knowledgeable staff and personalized services. Its visual displays work to create the feeling of hominess and warmth, both of which appeal to the shopper with discretionary money.

The company hopes to increase e-commerce sales even more through the use of social networking sites such as Facebook and Twitter .

Vera Bradley reported $508.9 million in revenue for fiscal 2015. That was a decrease of about $27 million compared to the previous fiscal period. In fiscal 2015, the company's net income also decreased by $20.3 million compared to fiscal 2014. The primary reason was due to decreased sales and increased expenses partially offset by decreased income tax expense.

As it works to grow its stores network, Vera Bradley is concentrating on expansion in underpenetrated markets. It's focused mainly in the Midwest, Southwest, and West. The company is also looking abroad for additional sales, launching an international marketing campaign to increase its brand awareness in Japan.
