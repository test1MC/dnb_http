If you want to kiss and make up, Shiseido can supply the accoutrements. Shiseido Americas is a subsidiary of Shiseido , Japan's largest cosmetics maker. The Americas arm manufactures and markets makeup and skin care products, toiletries, and professional beauty salon products in North and South America for sale worldwide. Its products, which carry the Shiseido and NARS brand names, are sold through department stores, travel stores, and specialty outlets. The company also operates the professional salon business of Helene Curtis. Another Shiseido Americas unit, Zotos International , markets products to professionals. The Americas business has grown to account for about one-third of its parent company's sales.

For Japanese parent Shiseido, Shiseido Americas operates in both North and South America and sells its products globally. The Americas business accounted for 32% of its parent company's sales in 2015. The US, alone, represented 47% of the Americas' 2015 revenues.

Shiseido Americas expanded its 265,000-sq.-ft. factory in East Windsor in 2013 by 54,000 sq. ft. The facility handles the Americas' division's international exports. The move boosted local production capacity by a third.

As part of its business, the company operates a pair of research and development centers in the US.

Shiseido's Americas arm boasts several big-name brands in its portfolio, such as Bare Escentuals, Beaute Prestige, NARS, Shiseido, and Zotos.

The North American retail business has boosted its brand awareness through television commercials and infomercials. It's also expanding its sales force and enhancing its training for store employees. The beauty company's North American direct marketing business has been working to roll out limited-edition products and renew program content to reinvigorate shopping via TV.

Shiseido 's revenues in the Americas rose 523% in 2015 compared to 2014. In the Americas, the makeup artist brand NARS and the fragrance business performed well. Other drivers of sales growth included the global brand, which scored a hit with the launch of ULTIMUNE beauty serum in 2014, and bareMinerals, which benefited from a favorable response to BARESKIN, its first liquid foundation.

Shiseido Americas has focused on expanding into major cities in the US, as well as in Europe and Asia, coupled with a significant investment to fuel momentum and volatile currency exchange rates.

The company is looking to grow its market share in North America across the department store channel supported by makeup brand NARS, which enjoys strong customer support, and bare Minerals, which boasts a leading share in the US mineral foundation market.

Shiseido Americas planned to reengineer the value of company's brands and will concentrate investments where they will generate potent global growth, with emphasis on ULTIMUNE (launched in 2014).

In 2015 the company expanded its US production plant in New Jersey to add research and development facilities, office space, and warehouses.

In 2016 the company acquired Gurwitch Products, LLC from Alticor, Inc. The purchase of the upscale cosmetics maker, known for its Laura Mercier and ReVive brands, complements Shiseido Americas' existing portfolio of upscale makeup and skincare products and adds new customers and an expanded market reach.

Shiseido's effort to boost sales of cosmetics in department stores was aided by its acquisition of Bare Escentuals . The 2010 $1.7 billion deal for the mineral-based makeup maker gave Shiseido a needed leg up in the US cosmetics market.
