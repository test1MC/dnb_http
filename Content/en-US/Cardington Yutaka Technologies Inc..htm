Cardington Yutaka Technologies (CYT) and its parent company, Honda Motor Co ., possess a certain synergy; subsidiary CYT supplies its parent with automotive parts. The primary parts manufactured by the company include exhaust systems, torque converters, and catalytic converters. The subsidiary also manufactures an assortment of other parts for manufacturers of both automobiles and all-terrain vehicles. CYT operates a manufacturing plant in Ohio and works in tandem with sister US-based Honda parts manufacturer, Alabama Cullman Yutaka Technologies, LLC. CYT is also an automotive parts supplier for Honda of America Manufacturing .

CYT is based in Cardington, Ohio; its sister company Alabama Cullman Yutaka Technologies is located in Cullman, Alabama.

The company operates as a part of Honda 's Automobile Business segment. Alabama Cullman Yutaka Technologies also produces torque converters, exhaust systems, and catalytic converters.

2013, Honda's consolidated unit sales in North America increased by 31% to 1,731 thousand units, mainly due to the positive impact of the launch of the all-new Accord, as well as strong sales of the Civic, CR-V and other models.

The Automobile Business contributed about 78% of the parent company's revenue in 2013. Honda got derived about 41% of its revenues from the US that year.

CYT is banking on Honda's recovery from natural disasters and their aftermath in Japan and Thailand, and the upswing in car sales across the industry in 2012 (and the promise of further increases in the future), to lift its production in Ohio.

Like many facilities that supply parts to Honda auto-making plants, CYT provides its products on a just-in-time basis to Honda.

The company has been hiring steadily as Honda's rebounded from the earthquake disasters and the flooding impact in 2010 and 2011

CYT was founded in 1995.
