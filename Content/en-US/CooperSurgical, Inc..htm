CooperSurgical, a wholly-owned subsidiary of The Cooper Companies , develops, makes, and markets medical devices, diagnostic products, and surgical instruments for gynecologists and other women's health care providers. Its 600-plus-item product line includes bone density scanners, cervical cancer screening systems, examination instruments, fetal monitors, insemination tools, and surgical sterilization devices. Some products are developed internally, while others are acquired. They are primarily marketed to hospitals, doctors' offices, and health care clinics in the US. Sister company CooperVision is a global contact lens manufacturer.

The company has manufacturing and distribution facilities in four US states (California, Texas, Virginia and Connecticut) and in Germany, Netherlands and Denmark; it also has sales offices in Europe, Canada, and China.

CooperSurgical's products are sold to obstetricians and gynecologists (represent 65% of revenue) and fertility clinics (represents 35% of revenue).

The company's network of direct, indirect, and distributor sales teams sells the company's products to physician's offices, hospitals, and clinics across the US. Products made by the company's Origio division are sold globally by in-house field representatives and distributors.

CooperSurgical net revenues in 2014 increased by 2% thanks to a growth in the demand of fertility products (contributing 35%) partially offset by slower growth in sales of medical equipment. Sales from medical office and surgical procedures by ob/gyns (contributing 65%) decreased, also due to a decline in medical equipment sales.

Operating as a segment of The Cooper Companies , CooperSurgical accounted for 19% of its parent's net revenues in 2014.

Although CooperVision accounts for the bulk of its parent company's revenues, CooperSurgical maintains a steady strategy of acquiring smaller businesses and complementary product lines that either improve its existing market position or serve new clinical areas.

Currently, it is working to expand its offerings to larger hospitals and outpatient surgical procedures. CooperSurgical also spends and focuses on R&D to fuel growth.

In 2014 CooperSurgical began directly selling its Plume-Away 4.0 and Ultra Plume-Away 6.0 (part of the company's surgical device portfolio) to clinical users.

In 2013 the company released the new MityOne Vacuum-Assisted Delivery System for vaginal and C-section deliveries.

It has made more than 25 acquisitions since its inception, including the $147.4 million 2012 purchase of Origio, a Danish company that designs and manufactures in-vitro fertilization devices.

CooperSurgical was founded in 1990.
