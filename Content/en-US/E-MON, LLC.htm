E-MON makes electronic metering devices and related accessories and software. Its customers include commercial, residential, retail, industrial, sports, and government facilities. The company also sells products made by third parties, including gas meters, cold water meters, hot water meters, and other meters that can be read with its reporting software. E-MON was a division of Hunt Power (which is part of Hunt Consolidated ) until early 2007, when the company was sold to its management team, financially backed by Branford Castle, a New York-based private equity investor. E-MON was established in 1981 and introduced its first electronic submeter in 1983.
