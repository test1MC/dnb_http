Whether in the classroom or at home, Learning Resources wants to help make learning fun for kids. The company makes hands-on educational toys and materials for children ages 2 and older. For parents who want their children to learn while they play, Learning Resources offers educational toys, games and puzzles designed to enhance motor skills and teach letters, word formation, counting skills and color and shape recognition. For teachers, it offers activity kits, role-play sets, workbooks, and classroom supplies (including demonstration materials and decorations). Learning Resources markets its 1,100-plus products through its e-commerce site, direct mail catalogs and to toy and educational retailers in more than 80 countries.

The Illinois-based company has offices in Illinois and in Norfolk, England. Its products are sold in more than 80 countries.

Learning Resources offers more than 1,100 proprietary products for families and the elementary and middle school markets. Its portfolio of brands includes Pretend & Play, Reading Rods, Smart Snacks, Rainbow Fractions, Gears! Gears! Gears!, Simple Technology, and Primary Science. The lines cover a range of subject areas, including early childhood learning, reading and language, math, science, and geography, among others. The educational toy company also makes products for special needs kids, including those on the autism spectrum.

In addition to its online sales and distribution to retailers, Learning Resources offers custom development and manufacturing of educational products for governments and other manufacturers.

New product development is key to keeping sales going at Learning Resources. To that end, the company plans to launch more than 100 new products in 2014, including math, literacy, and fine motor skills themed toys.
