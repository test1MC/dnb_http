Oxford BioMedica has no reservations about launching attacks on disease. The biotechnology company uses gene therapy and immunotherapy to develop treatments for cancer, as well as neurodegenerative and ocular diseases. Its four ocular gene therapies are partnered with Sanofi ; the company works with Pfizer on another targeted antibody therapy. The company has three lead products: TroVax, ProSavin, and RetinoStat (being developed with Sanofi). TroVax is in late-stage clinical trials for the treatment of prostate cancer. ProSavin is being tested to treat Parkinson's disease using the company's LentiVector platform. RetinoStat is in clinical trials to treat wet age-related macular degeneration.
