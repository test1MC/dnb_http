Vocera Communications makes high-tech walkie talkies for hospital employees who have their hands full. Its digital voice communication system works over wireless computer networks and includes a communications badge worn around the neck and server software. The company also offers an optional standard-size phone handset. Its systems support text messaging and can be configured to make and receive telephone calls using smartphones and other mobile devices. Vocera markets its products primarily to health care facilities; it counts more than 1,150 hospitals as customers and about 270 other customers in hospitality, retail, and libraries.

Vocera has been a public company since in 2012, when it raised $92 million in its offering.

Vocera's gets most (54%) of its revenue from the sale of its products with devices accounting for 39% of revenue and software for 15%. The rest comes from maintenance and support (37%) and professional services and training (10%).

Its products are manufactured by SMTC Corporation , a contract electronics manufacturer in Canada.

The company has sales and support offices in Canada, the UK, and the US. It makes most of its money from customers in the US; just 9% of sales come from customers outside the US.

Vocera uses a direct sales model to sell its products in Australia, New Zealand, the UK, and the US. It uses a US government-authorized reseller for sales to Veterans Administration and Department of Defense health care facilities. In other international markets, the company uses resellers and distributors.

In 2015, net income rose 9% to $104 million from $95 million in 2014 on gains in product and service revenue.

Vocera's loss narrowed to $17 million in 2015, down from $28 million in 2014. The net income increase combined with a decrease in R&D spending led to the lower loss. The company's negative cash flow was $135,000 in 2015 compared to a negative $4.7 million in 2014.

Vocera is looking for growth on all fronts. It works to extend its product portfolio to a widening array of healthcare providers and to other businesses with the need for quick communication. Another area is to develop new products based on the underlying technology of current Vocera products.

Vocera acquired mVisum Inc., a provider of alarm management technology, for $3.5 million in 2014. The deal added to Vocera's product line. Another acquisition was for Prana Technologies for $3.45 million. The transactions brought Prana's cloud-based applications to Vocera.
