InterfaceFLOR is flooring its customers with carpet tile. A subsidiary of carpet making giant Interface , it's the largest modular carpet maker in the world, offering "free-lay" carpet to businesses, government and education entities, retailers, and hospitals. Its sister company, FLOR, offers a residential do-it-yourself carpet line sold throughout the US at Lowe's , Design Within Reach , and some 160-plus specialty retailers. The company's flooring systems consist of carpet tiles cut in squares or rectangles and laid to resemble traditional carpet.

InterfaceFLOR is headquartered in LaGrange, Georgia, and showcases its carpet products through showrooms located in Atlanta, Chicago, Dallas, New York, San Francisco, and Toronto.

Based on the fact that almost 5 billion lbs. of old carpet end up in landfills every year, and that virgin nylon, a preferred carpet material, has a major negative impact on the environment, the company is taking the path to "Mission Zero" to achieve a zero environmental footprint by 2020. Modular carpeting amounts to less waste ending up in landfills.

The company no longer uses wool in its carpets based on the environmental impact associated with grazing animals. It instead uses nylon 6.6 which is longer lasting and can be recycled at the end of its first life. InterfaceFLOR has also developed a partial nylon replacement using polylactic acid which is derived from cornstarch.

InterfaceFLOR markets its modular carpet lines to be used in education, government, health care, travel lodging, public spaces, transportation, and retail settings.
