Nothing can foil Novelis, because it has the art of rolling aluminum in the can -- the aluminum can, that is. It is a global leader in aluminum rolled products and can recycling. A spinoff of what is now Rio Tinto Alcan , it manufactures aluminum rolled semi-finished products used by the construction and industrial, foil products, transportation, and beverage and food can industries. The rolled aluminum is made with alloy mixtures in a range of hardnesses, thicknesses, and widths, with various coatings and finishes designed specifically for its end-use segments. The company also recycles more than 35 billion beverage cans annually. India's Hindalco Industries , part of the Aditya Birla Group , owns Novelis.

The company's four operating segments are Europe, North America, Asia and South America, each of which manufactures aluminum sheet and light gauge products, and recycles aluminum.

Novelis Europe (33% of total sales in fiscal 2015, March year end)operates ten aluminum rolled product facilities, and manufactures a broad range of sheet and foil products. Its markets include beverage and food can, automotive, architectural and industrial products, foil and technical products and lithographic sheet.

Novelis North America (30%) operates eight aluminum rolled products facilities, and manufactures a broad range of aluminum sheet and light gauge products. Its markets include beverage and food cans, containers and packaging, automotive and other transportation applications, architectural and other industrial applications.

Novelis Asia (20%) operates five facilities, including three facilities with recycling operations; and manufactures a broad range of sheet and light gauge products. Its markets include beverage and food cans, electronics, architectural, automotive, foil, industrial and other products.

Novelis South America (17%) operates two aluminum rolled product facilities and manufactures aluminum rolled products, including can sheet, industrial sheet and light gauge. The main markets are beverage and food can, specialty, industrial, foil and other packaging and transportation end-use applications.

In addition to aluminum rolled products plants, South American businesses have historically included primary aluminum smelting and power generation facilities. In fiscal 2015, Novelis ceased operations at its remaining smelting facilities in South America and sold the majority of the power generation operations which supported those facilities.

Novelis has operations in nine countries - the US, Canada, Italy, Germany, the UK, Switzerland, Malaysia, South Korea, and Brazil - organized into North America, Europe, Asia, and South America segments.

The company's North American and European units together represent about three-quarters of annual sales.

Novelis' serves a range of markets, including Packaging, Automotive & Transportation, Architecture & Building, Lithography & Printing, Consumer Goods, Industrial Applications, and PAE Casthouse Equipment.

The company's single largest end-use market is beverage can sheet. It supplies various end-use markets all over the world through a direct sales force operating from individual plants or sales offices, as well as from regional sales offices in 13 countries. The direct sales channel typically serves very large, sophisticated fabricators and original equipment manufacturers. Supply contracts for large global customers generally range from one to five years in length. It also uses third party agents or traders in some regions mostly in Asia to complement its own sales force.

Novelis also sells its products through aluminum distributors, particularly in North America and Europe. Distributors sell mostly commodity or less specialized products into many end-use markets in small quantities, including the construction and industrial markets.

The company's three largest customers are Rexam , Ball Corporation , and Anheuser-Busch , which accounted for 18%, 10% and 7% respectively of net sales in fiscal 2015. Approximately 55% of its sales in fiscal 2015 were to its ten largest customers.

Other major customers from various industries include Can-Pack S.A., BMW , Fiat Chrysler , Ford Motor , Pactiv , LG International , and Samsung Electronics .

In fiscal 2015, Novelis' net sales increased by 14% due to higher sales from Asia and South America (reflecting an increase in average aluminum prices and higher shipments of can and automotive products).

Net income grew by 42% compared to 2014 due to higher sales.

In fiscal 2015, net cash provided by the operating activities decreased by $98 million as the result of a change in inventories and accounts receivable.

The company's strategy is to be the top aluminum rolled products company in the world by focusing on capturing growth in the beverage can market, as well as the automotive and electronics markets. Novelis seeks to increase its production capacity through making capital investments in its high-growth and emerging markets, particularly in Asia and South America; finding and eliminating bottlenecks in its production and operations; and increasing its sustainability by utilizing an increased amount of recycled material in its products. The company set a goal of using 80%-recycled metal in its rolling operations by 2020. As part of its strategy for growth, the company may pursue acquisitions, divestitures, or strategic alliances.

Novelis is focused on managing its costs by pursuing a standardization of its core operations globally. It has implemented numerous restructuring initiatives, including the shutdown or sale of facilities, staff rationalization and other activities, all of which have led to significant cost savings that will benefit Novelis for years to come.

During fiscal year 2015, the company sold its consumer foil operations in North America. Additionally, it took steps to exit certain non-core operations in Brazil, including ceasing operations at the smelter in Ouro Preto, Brazil and selling certain hydroelectric facilities.

That year Novelis opened an aluminum automotive heat treatment line in Nachterstedt, Germany, to serve European and global automotive customers. Located adjacent to Novelis' existing rolling mill and the world's largest aluminum recycling center, the $85 million investment will increase production capacity by 120,000 metric tons of automotive sheet annually.

In 2014 Novelis opened two new aggregation centers, serving areas in and around London and the South East. The opening of the centers is in line with Novelis' strategy to augment its existing network of used aluminum can suppliers and reach its ambitious target of achieving 80% recycled content by 2020. The addition of these two new delivery points will make it easier for the company's suppliers to deliver high-quality aluminum locally.

That year Novelis sold its North American consumer foil products business to Reynolds Consumer Products LLC for $33.75 million. That year Novelis sold three foil and packaging plants in Europe to focus on North American operations for the auto market. It picked up $34 million for the transaction and began investing in its auto production lines.

In 2014 the company added new lines in Brazil, Italy, and Germany to keep up with aluminum demand in those markets.
