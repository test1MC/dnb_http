Wyandot asks the consumer, why not trust your impulses and snatch a bag of its chips on your way out of the supermarket? The company makes potato chips and other salty snacks under private-labels, as well for the retail food and foodservice sectors. It is one of the leading US snack-food contract manufacturers, with an annual production capacity of 100 million pounds. Its products are available worldwide. Founded in 1936 as a raw popcorn supplier to ball parks and movie houses by Hoover and Ava Brown with just $750, Wyandot was family-owned and -managed until 1994, when Nick Chilton, the first non-family member to run the company, was named president and CEO.
