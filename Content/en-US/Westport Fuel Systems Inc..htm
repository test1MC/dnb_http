Westport Fuel Systems (formerly Westport Innovations) makes fuel systems and components that let engines run on gaseous forms of fuel rather than liquid petroleum. Working through partnerships and joint ventures (JVs), it has sold more than 38,000 truck and bus engines to manufacturers in about 20 countries. Major subsidiary Westport Power, through a JV with Cummins , makes liquefied natural gas (LNG) and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) engines for transit and commercial vehicles. Juniper Engines, the other key subsidiary, makes natural gas and LPG engines for automotive and industrial OEMs and offers aftermarket engine conversions. Westport was formed in 1995; in 2016 it acquired Fuel Systems Solutions and changed names to the current label.

As demand grows for cleaner burning engines and lower fuel costs, the company has found more buyers for its wares. It contracts with major vehicle manufacturers, including Ford , BMW , Isuzu , Peterbilt , and Caterpillar , as well as Freightliner and Mack Trucks , to produce alternative fuel engines for their branded vehicles. Continuing to add new OEM partners is part of the company's growth strategy as are adding new fuel options and extending the market penetration of each of its divisions and JVs.

The majority of Westport's revenues come from North America, with markets developing in Asia, Europe, India, and South America. Pursuant to its overseas expansion strategy, the company has been focusing on foreign partnerships and acquisitions. Its Westport HD (Heavy Duty) segment and Chinese partner Beijing Tianhai Industry Co. each own a 50% stake in BTIC Westport, which develops LNG fuel transport tanks. JV Weichai Westport Inc. develops and sells alternative fuel engines for auto, industrial, and marine applications in China.
