Welders and army tank drivers alike owe their continued ability to see to Epolin. The company develops near-infrared dyes used to make eyewear for protection against lasers (used in range finders in tanks) and face shields used by welders. Epolin also makes intermediates for the specialty chemical industry. Additionally, Epolin sells its dyes as security inks for credit cards, drug and food labels, and official documents. Products are sold in 20 countries; the US accounts for about 80% of sales. Epolin was acquired by New Jersey-based investment managing firm Polymathes Holdings in 2012.
