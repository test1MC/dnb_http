Vranken-Pommery Monopole has corked the notion that fine champagne does not belong on a supermarket shelf. Founded in 1976, the company has built its name selling its libations at grocery retailers -- a market its older competitors have historically shunned. It makes the Charles Lafitte, Cuvée Diamant, Demoiselle, Heidsieck & Co Monopole, Pommery, and Vranken brands of champagne. It also owns the ROZÈS, San Pedro Das Aguias and Terras do Grifo port brands. Vranken-Pommery operates subsidiaries in Belgium, Germany, Portugal, Switzerland, the UK, and the US. The company was founded by founder Paul-François Vranken. CHC, a holding company owns 70% of the company.

The company operates two businesses: Champagne & Port, which brings in some 87% of sales, and Provence des Sables (13%).

Vranken-Pommery has production facilities in seven major European nations, as well as Australia, Japan and the United States. France is the largest market, accounting for 57% of sales; the rest of Europe brings in 33%.

The company sells products through commission contracts as well as exports through its subsidiaries. The company has its own sales force, representing more than 200 vendors.
