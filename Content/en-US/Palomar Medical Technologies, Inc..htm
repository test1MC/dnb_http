Palomar Medical Technologies' laser light show is more function than flash. The Cynosure company makes laser and pulsed-light systems, along with related disposable products, for use in aesthetic procedures. It products include the Icon Aesthetic System which enables aesthetic practitioners to provide a comprehensive suite of the most popular treatments, from hair removal to wrinkle reduction to scar and stretch mark treatment; SlimLipo, which offers a minimally invasive body sculpting solution, and The Vectus Laser, which provides permanent way to remove unwanted hair.

Palomar was purchased by Cynosure in 2013 for some $294 million. The merger widens Cynosure's product offerings and strengthens its global distribution network; it also creates cross-sales opportunities for both companies. Cynosure plans to eventually relocate its headquarters to the Palomar facility.

Palomar's Professional medical and cosmetic products and services business segment accounts for 97% of its sales. The tiny Consumer Products segment's sales are limited to the US. The firm's Palomar Medical Products subsidiary develops, manufactures, and sells its laser and lamp-based systems. Palomar also has five international subsidiaries that oversee the sale and marketing of its products abroad.

The company's products include the Icon Aesthetic System, SlimLipo, and The Vectus Laser.

Palomar's main customers are plastic surgeons and dermatologists, but it is expanding its offerings to appeal to family practitioners, gynecologists, and doctors at "medi-spas."

Palomar focuses on expanding its portfolio of professional products and making this same laser technology available to consumers, through the PaloVia brand.

To protect its sales and profits from future economic malaise, Palomar is diversifying its product offerings to make it less reliant on high-priced equipment sales. To extend its market internationally, Palomar over the years the company has opened offices in the Netherlands, Australia, Japan, Germany, and Spain. Palomar intends to continue to work with its current distributors, as well as seek new distributors to improve international sales and marketing efforts.

Palomar also grows its product base by enhancing best-sellers with additional functionalities and capabilities; most of its products have been FDA -approved for additional uses over the years, allowing for Palomar to capture a wider customer base and earn additional sales with the same products.

Founded in 1991, the company sold the first FDA-approved high-powered hair removal system to dermatologists, in 1997.
