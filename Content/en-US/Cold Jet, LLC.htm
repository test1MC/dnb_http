Dry ice blasting sounds like a pseudo-sport any adolescent male could enthusiastically get behind, but for Cold Jet, it's serious business. The firm makes dry ice blasting equipment used in industrial cleaning applications. Dry ice blasting can remove ink, glue, oil, paint, mold, and rubber from industrial equipment. The company, which operates in Europe, Asia, the Middle East, Canada, and Mexico, uses recycled carbon dioxide to eliminate the need for solvents or chemicals in the cleaning process. In addition to its cleaning products, Cold Jet makes dry ice production equipment sold worldwide. The company was established in 1986, in conjunction with the creation of its "Cryoblast" dry ice blast machine.

Cold Jet has more than 100 contractors and distributors. Industries it sells to include aerospace, automotive, electrical, general maintenance, packaging, rubber, and wood. Some of its customers are A&T Industrial Services, Aqua Glass Corporation, Granite State Environmental, Hill & Griffith, RR Donnelley & Sons , and ServPro .

Cold Jet pushes out new products regularly including the Aero Series, which came out in 2015. The Aero, according to the company, guarantees the best pellet integrity and maximum cleaning aggression. The series has three levels of functionality.

In 2014, Cold Jet acquired IceTech, which made similar projects.
