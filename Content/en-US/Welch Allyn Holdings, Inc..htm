Chances are, if you're in a medical office, Welch Allyn is nearby. The manufacturer of physical examination instruments and accessories (including electronic medical record-connected vital signs and cardiac monitoring equipment) distributes such items as blood pressure scopes, otoscopes, stethoscopes, thermometers, exam lamps, patient monitoring systems, and cardiac defibrillators. Welch Allyn also makes diagnostic veterinary devices, and it provides technology and supply chain consulting and product support and training services. Fellow hospital equipment maker Hill-Rom Holdings acquired Welch Allyn for $2.05 billion in 2015.

Hill-Rom acquired the company from the founding Allyn family, who'd held Welch Allyn for 100 years. The deal was struck to help Hill-Rom expand into the point-of-care diagnostics market.

The affiliated Allyn Foundation conducts philanthropic work in central New York. Welch Allyn also operates The Lodge at Welch Allyn, a conference center also in central New York near the company's corporate headquarters. Along with business conferences, The Lodge hosts weddings, educational seminars, and special events.

From its headquarters in New York State (Skaneateles Falls), the company operates manufacturing plants and sales and distribution centers in 26 countries around the globe. It has operations in the Americas, Africa, Asia, Europe, the Middle East, and the Pacific Rim.

Welch Allyn markets its products to hospitals, emergency clinics, medical schools, physician practices, nursing homes, veterinarians, and other health care facilities both directly and through independent representatives. In addition to its own catalog of products, the company also sells to other distributors including Henry Schein .

Welch Allyn has extended its product lines, distribution methods, and geographic reach through acquisitions over the years. The company has also expanded its product offerings through internal development programs and through licensing agreements, distribution partnerships, and alliances with other medical and technology manufacturers. While it will continue to develop new products in its traditional core lines, it is focusing on building up its selection of high-tech monitoring systems and diagnostic cardiology products.

In 2015 the company partnered with Nonin Medical (which is engaged in designing and manufacturing noninvasive medical monitoring solutions) to provide its clinically-proven PureSAT SpO2 technology.

In 2014 it teamed up with LKC Technologies, a visual electrophysiology and inventor of the RETeval DR diabetic retinopathy assessment device. Under the terms of their exclusive distribution agreement, Welch Allyn will distribute the RETeval DR to healthcare providers outside of the US who are focused on diabetic patients who face increased risk of sight-threatening disease such as diabetic retinopathy.

Also in 2014 the company partnered with Iatric Systems, a comprehensive healthcare IT integration company to offer hospitals an automated solution for sending confirmed vital signs and other data to electronic health records (EHRs).

That year Welch Allyn also began offering cardiopulmonary devices in Canada through a partnership with QHR Technologies . It also partnered with ABELMed to integrate Welch Allyn vital signs devices with one of Canada's leading electronic medical record and practice management software systems, the ABELMed EHR-EMR/PM.

In 2013 Welch Allyn introduced a new connectivity-ready electrocardiograph device (Welch Allyn CP 150) in Canada which offers flexible report formatting, intuitive design and functionality in addition to administering patient tests and managing information via a touch screen, color display.

That year the company increased efficiencies in the European organization -- at the same time positioning for growth in Europe and the Middle East -- by consolidating its regional operations (including logistics, product configuration and repair, customer service, and technical support functions) to a new operating center in Amsterdam.

In 2015 Welch Allyn acquired Tennessee-based Hubble Telemedical, a privately held health care company that enables remote diabetic retinopathy screening and analysis in primary care and other convenient settings.

To better serve its U.S. customers by offering them an mHealth solution optimized for cost-effective collection and delivery of remote health information, in 2014 it bought Nebraska-based Prairie Ventures telehealth company HealthInterlink

It also acquired PediaVision (a developer of vision technology) so that it could to offer its customers a diagnostic device for conducting eye examinations in a variety of settings.

In 2012 the company introduced its Green Series 777 Integrated Wall System. That year it launched the Connex virtual channel software for patient monitors in 2012. The company also partnered with DETECTO to enhance connected patient data solutions for hospitals and physician practices.

Citing pending changes to tax policies for medical device makers, in 2012 Welch Allyn announced a three year restructuring, including a reduction of its work force by 10% and consolidations of some manufacturing facilities.

A partnership with scale maker Pelstar, struck in 2011, resulted in co-branded Health-o-meter professional scales being offered in the Welch Allyn catalog of products.

The company was founded in 1915 when Dr. Francis A. Welch and William Noah Allyn built the world's first handheld direct-illuminating ophthalmoscope.
