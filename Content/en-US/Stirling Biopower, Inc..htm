Stirling Biopower designs, manufactures, and sells electrical generators. Unlike competing products, the generators feature an external combustion engine that produces electricity from most forms of heat or renewable fuel -- waste gases from landfills or sewage treatment plants. Fuels can include liquid from waste streams, and used motor or vegetable oils. The technology targets markets such as waste water treatment plants, and municipal and industrial landfills, agricultural waste, flare gases, coal mine emissions, and biomass. Formerly STM Power, the business was established in 1979 and revived in 1990. The company shuttered in early 2007 and launched under new ownership in mid-2007 as Stirling Biopower.
