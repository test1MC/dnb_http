Argon ST has the five C's of defense down: command, control, communications, computers, and combat. It also develops systems for intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance. Together these areas are known as C5ISR. Electronic systems tailored for these purposes track, intercept, and interpret data needed to make mission decisions on land, at sea, or in the air. Argon ST's portfolio includes sensors that detect hostile signals and radar; ISR, force protection, geolocation, navigation, and threat warning systems; integrated communications networks; and engineering, integration, and training services. The Boeing subsidiary primarily serves the US defense and homeland security markets from about a dozen US offices.

Argon ST's products and systems are used on a broad range of military platforms, including ships, submarines, unmanned underwater vehicles, aircraft, unmanned aerial vehicles, land vehicles, fixed site installations, and relocatable land sites. It develops many of its systems by incorporating proprietary software and design processes with commercially available hardware and software in configurations that can be deployed or adapted to meet a customer's complex specifications on varied platforms. Argon ST's business is conducted primarily through contracts with the US government. Its primary customers include the US Department of Defense and various agencies and commands within the US Navy . Argon ST also acts as a subcontractor, providing products and services to major domestic prime contractors. To a lesser extent, the company also serves US government-approved foreign military customers.

In 2013 and 2012 the US Navy extended two contracts with Argon ST, worth a combined $100 million, for signals intelligence systems. In 2011 the company and partner Atair Aerospace received a $45 million contract from the Marine Corps for the procurement, testing, delivery, training, and logistical support of the Joint Precision Air Drop System Ultra Lightweight (JPADS-ULW), an aircraft deployed steerable canopy system that is capable of dropping large weights of disaster relief and medical supply cargo to military units on the battlefield.

Founded in 2004 through the merger of privately-held Argon Engineering Associates and publicly-held Sensytech, Argon ST was purchased in 2010 for $782 million by Boeing as part of an expansion of its C5ISR capabilities. Argon was made a part of Boeing's Electronic and Mission Systems division within the Boeing Defense, Space & Security operating unit.
