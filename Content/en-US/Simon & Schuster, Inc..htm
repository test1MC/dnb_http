If you're a fan of Stephen King, Mary Higgins Clark, or Larry McMurtry, then you're a fan of Simon & Schuster. The publishing arm of CBS prints the work of these and other popular authors of consumer fiction and nonfiction books under various imprints. The company leverages its connection with CBS through products related to its various TV properties (such as The Bro Code based on CBS's How I Met Your Mother ). It also publishes audio books and e-books compatible with devices such as Amazon's Kindle and Apple's iPad, and operates Simon & Schuster Children's Publishing .

Internationally, Simon & Schuster has publishing and distribution capabilities in Canada, India, the UK, and Australia. It also has a sales presence in major markets across the globe, and distribute its titles in more than 100 countries and international territories.

All total, Simon & Schuster publishes some 2,000 titles annually. The company's books are published through Simon & Schuster Adult Publishing, which reaches a variety of consumers through various niche imprints. Its Howard Books targets religious readers, its Threshold Editions targets conservative readers, and Strebor Books targets African-American readers. Its Simon & Schuster Children's Publishing division publishes titles from high-profile brands and characters such as Dora the Explorer and SpongeBob SquarePants.

The company's revenue increased by 2% in fiscal 2013 compared to the previous year. The small spike reflected growth in digital book sales. Digital book sales increased 22% year-over-year and represented 27% of Publishing's total revenues for fiscal 2013 compared to 23% in fiscal 2012.

Through its Simon & Schuster Digital, the publisher is looking to the Internet to reach readers in a variety of ways. Seeking a more direct line to consumers, Simon & Schuster sells books directly to readers via its website. In addition, a mobile edition of its site lets users shop, search, and view book excerpts and video on iPhone, Blackberry, and other mobile devices.

The company continues to foster relationships with popular authors in order maintain a stable of notable works. In 2012 Simon & Schuster imprints placed 317 titles on the New York Times bestseller list, including 35 #1 bestsellers.

During 2014 the company formed a sales and distribution agreement with Regan Arts. The deal means Simon & Schuster will handle global sales and distribution for all of Regan Arts' titles in print and electronic editions. Also during Simon & Schuster entered into a sales and distribution agreement with Victory Belt Publishing. Simon & Schuster will handle global sales and distribution for all Victory Belt Publishing titles. Victory Belt Publishing is a leading publisher of books on health and fitness.

Simon & Schuster was founded in 1924 by Richard L. Simon and M. Lincoln Schuster.

Richard Simon and Max Schuster founded the company in 1924, selling more than 100,000 copies of a crossword puzzle book. The firm's first success outside puzzle books was Will Durant's The Story of Philosophy in 1926. Over the next few decades Simon & Schuster published a range of best-selling books (mainly under its Pocket Books division launched in 1939), from authors such as Pearl Buck and Ernest Hemingway. Ownership passed to a company owned by tycoon Marshall Field in 1944 and then back to the publisher's principals in 1957. The company was bought by Gulf + Western Industries (later Paramount Communications) in 1975. Acquisitions in the 1980s included textbook publisher Prentice Hall.

Viacom acquired Paramount in 1994, and Jonathan Newcomb replaced Dick Snyder, the hard-charging chairman and CEO widely credited with making the publisher a powerhouse. Four years later Viacom sold Simon & Schuster's reference and educational divisions to Pearson for $4.6 billion and shifted its focus to consumer books. In 2000 the company became the first major publisher to offer a book online. The company launched an e-bookstore selling electronic books for download at its SimonSaysShop.com site in 2001.

Newcomb left the company in 2002 to work at an investment firm, and president and COO Jack Romanos took over as CEO. That year parent Viacom integrated Simon & Schuster into its Viacom Entertainment Group unit. The company also acquired Distican, its Canadian distributor of books and audio products, in 2002.

When Viacom underwent a reorganization in late 2005, the company became part of the CBS Corporation unit. The following year Simon & Schuster expanded through purchasing and/or launching new imprints, including Howard Books, a religious publishing company; Threshold Editions, for conservative readers; a Hispanic/Latino line within Atria; African-American publishing line Strebor Books; and Simon Scribbles, a children's coloring and activity imprint.

In 2007 Romanos announced plans to retire; Carolyn Reidy, former president of the Simon & Schuster Adult Publishing unit, was named his replacement. The company launched a new, redesigned SimonandSchuster.com in 2009.
