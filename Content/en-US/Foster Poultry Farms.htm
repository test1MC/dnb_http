Besides being one of the nation's top poultry processors, Foster Poultry Farms never uses hormones (nor does it inject salt water into its birds to plump them up). Vertically integrated, the company takes chickens and turkeys from the incubator to grocers' meat cases, delis, and freezers. Doing business as Foster Farms, it sells some 750 poultry products mostly under the Foster Farms brand. Aside from its primary retail-centric operations, the company supplies prepared foods to the foodservice industry, including restaurant and other hospitality operators. The family-owned and -operated company was established in 1939 by Max and Verda Foster. The family also owns and operates sister company Foster Dairy Farms .

From its headquarters in Livingston, California, Foster Poultry Farms operates its business nationwide in Alabama, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Louisiana, Oregon, and Washington.

Foster Poultry Farms serves a variety of customers, such as retailers, warehouse clubs, foodservice clients, and home cooks.

As part of its business, the poultry company operates thefosterimposters.com website based on some 20 commercials it has produced since 1993. The website works to educate consumers in the health benefits of eating chicken. Foster Poultry Farms has also earned the distinction of providing turkeys pardoned by President Barack Obama during his annual ceremony commemorating Thanksgiving at the White House. The company also operates a free 24-hour turkey helpline during Thanksgiving.

To support agricultural education and help to advance poultry science, Foster Poultry Farms partially funded the engineering, design, and construction of the Foster Farms Poultry Education and Research Facility at Fresno State's Jordan College of Agricultural Science and Technology. The facility opened in spring 2013.
