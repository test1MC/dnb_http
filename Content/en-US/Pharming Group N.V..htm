Pharming has put pharma into farming. The biotech firm created Herman, the first genetically engineered bull, whose offspring produce human proteins in their milk. Pharming has extended the technology to rabbits as well. The proteins are then purified and used to develop potential treatments for blood disorders, transplants, and injuries, as well as for nutritional ingredients. The company's first commercial drug, Ruconest, is sold in the EU by partner Biovitrum as a treatment for hereditary angioedema, which causes tissues to swell; it is also under development as Rhucin with Santarus for the US market. To focus resources on Rhucin development, the company restructured and cut its staff in 2012.
