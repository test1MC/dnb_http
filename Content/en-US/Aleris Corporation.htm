Aleris is a global leader in the manufacture of aluminum rolled products. The company's rolled and extruded products unit makes aluminum products for manufacturers operating in most major industries (including aerospace, automotive, building and construction, distribution, molding and tooling, and transportation). To raise cash, in 2015 the company sold its North American and European Recycling and Specification Alloys businesses to an affiliate of Signature Group Holdings for $525 million. Facing a tough commodity market, in 2016 Aleris agreed to be acquired by China's Zhongwang International in a $2.3 billion deal.

Aleris has 13 production facilities in North America, Europe (one in Belgium and one in Germany), and China. North America contributed 52% of the company's 2015 revenues; Europe, 45%; and Asia-Pacific, 3%. Aleris is intent on capitalizing on growing demand from China.

The North America segment consists of nine manufacturing facilities that produce rolled aluminum and coated products. It produces rolled products for a wide variety of applications, including building and construction, distribution, transportation, automotive and other uses in the consumer durables general industrial segments.

The Europe segment produces rolled products for a wide variety of technically sophisticated applications, including aerospace plate and sheet, brazing sheet (clad aluminum material used for, among other applications, vehicle radiators and HVAC systems), automotive sheet, and heat treated plate for engineering uses, as well as for other uses in the transportation, construction and packaging industries.

Asia Pacific uses a state-of-the-art aluminum rolling mill in China to produce value-added plate products for the aerospace, engineering, distribution, building and construction, and other transportation industry segments worldwide.

Aleris' rolled and extruded products are sold to end-users, as well as to distributors, for use in the aerospace, automotive, transportation, building and construction, electrical, mechanical engineering, metal distribution and packaging industries throughout North America and Europe.

Sales of rolled products are made through each segment's own sales force, which are strategically located to provide international coverage, and through a broad network of sales offices and agents in North America, major European countries, as well as Asia and Australia. The majority of its customer sales agreements in these segments are for a term of one year or less.

Major customers in North America include American Construction Metals, Ryerson , Utility Trailer, Cuprum Metals Laminados, and D&W Fine Pack. European customers include Bombardier , Renault , and HallaVisteon; Asia-Pacific customers include Avic , AMS , and Hengtai.

The company saw a continuous decline in revenues for four years prior to 2015. Revenues recovered by 1% to $2.9 billion due to a 5% decline in Europe and a 2% decline in North America, partially offset by a3% growth in Asia-Pacific.

Aleris' revenue decline in Europe was due to a stronger US dollar, while the drop in North America was due to lower aluminum prices.

The company's net income saw a decline in over last five years except in 2014. Net income decreased by 44% to $49 million in 2015 due to the decline in benefit from income taxes and higher restructuring charges.

Aleris' operating cash flow has followed the revenue trend of the last five years. In 2015 it reported operating cash flow of $120 million compared to no operating cash flow in 2014 due to a change in inventories.

The company plans to continue to grow their core business and enhance its product mix, supply higher value alloys targeting aerospace, automotive and other transportation industries. It also intends to expand the company's global operations where we see the opportunity to enhance its manufacturing capabilities, grow with existing customers, gain new customers or penetrate higher-growth industries and regions.

The company's research and development organization includes three locations in Europe, one in Asia, and one in North America, focused on new product and alloy offerings and process performance technology.

To raise cash to pay down debt, in 2015 Aleris sold its North American and European Recycling and Specification Alloys businesses (including 18 production facilities in North America and six in Europe) to an affiliate of Signature Group Holdings. That year it also sold its extrusions business to Sankyo Tateyama (a Japanese building products and extrusions manufacturer) for $33.5 million.

Growing its portfolio of assets, in 2014 the company acquired Nichols Aluminum, a leading North American producer of aluminum sheet for the transportation, building and construction, machinery and equipment, consumer durables, and electrical industries.

Aleris was formed in 2004 through the merger of Commonwealth Industries, Inc. and IMCO Recycling, Inc. It was acquired by Texas Pacific Group (now TPG Capital ) in 2006 and taken private. Oaktree took up a major stake in Aleris in 2010. The name Aleris combines alliance, aluminum, and era.

To meet fast-growing demand for technically advanced aluminum plate products for global aerospace and commercial plate customers, in 2013 the company introduced a new $350 million hot rolling mill in Zhenjiang, China.
