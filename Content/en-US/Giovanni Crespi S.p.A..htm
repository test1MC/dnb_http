If you click your heels together three times while slowly repeating, "Giovanni Crespi... Giovanni Crespi," you might not be going home, but you could be testing the company's products. Founded in 1936, the company began by producing synthetic shoe uppers and still makes polyurethane foam resins for use in shoes, auto interiors, and household goods. It also makes synthetic materials (used in the fashion industry and as leather substitutes) and non-woven fabrics for footwear, auto interiors, home furnishings, and medical equipment. Giovanni Crespi has production units in Italy, Poland, China, and Brazil.
