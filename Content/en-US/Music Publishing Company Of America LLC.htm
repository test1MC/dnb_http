Whenever an aspiring guitarist records a tune by the late Mississippi bluesman Robert Johnson, MPCA makes a buck. The Music Publishing Company (or MPCA) owns the rights to songs and music that can be recorded by other musicians or used for sampling.  Among the music MPCA owns is the catalog of songwriter Chris LeDoux (whose songs have been recorded by Garth Brooks) and works from 70's funk group BT Express (which have been sampled by Jennifer Lopez and Sean "Puffy" Combs). MPCA also licenses its songs for film, commercials, TV shows, and Web sites. The company was founded in 2006 by music industry veterans John Hecker and John Titta.
