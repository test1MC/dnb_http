FARO Technologies is arming companies with a means to inspect the world around them. With the touch of its mechanical arm, FARO's measuring systems can facilitate reverse engineering of an undocumented part or a competitor's product. The portable FaroArm, FARO Laser ScanArm, and FARO Gage are jointed devices that simulate the human arm's movement. Along with the FARO Laser Scanner LS, and Laser Tracker, inspections and measurements are integrated with 3-D software. Aerospace, automotive, consumer goods, and heavy equipment companies such as Boeing , Honda , GM , GE , and Johnson Controls use FARO Arm units in their factories. Customers located outside the Americas account for about 60% of sales in 2014.

FARO's computer-aided measurement hardware and software offer portability and a link to its computerized measurement processing system, giving customers improved speed and precision. In addition to manufacturing applications, its products are used in accident and crime scene reconstruction, as well as to create digital scans of historic sites.

About 83% of revenue comes from product sales with the rest generated by its service component.

FARO has some 15,000 customers worldwide and manufactures from facilities located in Germany, Singapore, Switzerland, and the US. The company also has sales offices located in Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Italy, Japan, Malaysia, Mexico, the Netherlands, Poland, Singapore, South Korea, Spain, Thailand, Turkey, the UK, and Vietnam.

The Americas contributed 41% of FARO's revenue in 2014), followed by Europe/Africa with 35%, and Asia-Pacific with 24%. The US is the company's biggest market accounting for 35% of its revenue. Germany is next with 15%.

FARO conducts its sales and marketing efforts on a decentralized basis in three main regions around the world: Americas, Europe/Africa and Asia/Pacific. The regional headquarters for the Americas resides in Lake Mary, Florida; the Europe/Africa regional headquarters is located in Stuttgart, Germany; and the headquarters for Asia/Pacific s located in Singapore.

The company also sells its products -- primarily its FARO Focus Laser Scanner -- through distributors, although this channel represents a small portion of sales.

Overall FARO has about 30,000 installations with 15,000 customers around the world.

FARO continued a streak of robust growth with a 17% revenue increase in 2015 to $341.8 million. The company reported increases in all its product lines and geographic markets in 2015.

FARO's net income jumped 56% to $33.65 million in 2015 from $21.5 million in 2014. The rise resulted from higher revenue and gains from foreign exchange transactions.

Cash flow from operations plummeted to $5.6 million in 2014 from $34.2 million in 2014 because of accounts receivable and inventories.

FARO's strategy primarily revolves around the launching of new innovative measurement products to attract additional customers.

The company focuses on strategic acquisitions to expand software offerings. The company in 2015 closed three deals to expand software offerings within the Architecture, Engineering and Construction and Law Enforcement vertical markets.

In 2015, the company acquired kubit GmbH, a developer of software for surveying and as-built documentation. The deal includes the assets of its US distributor kubit USA Inc. The acquisition is part of the company's strategy to develop integrated 3D documentation products for the Architecture, Engineering and Construction market.

Also in 2015, FARO bought ARAS 360 Technologies Inc., a developer of accident and crime reconstruction, simulation and animation software. The acquisition of ARAS 360 will help FARO create a complementary suite of integrated 3D documentation product offerings for law enforcement customers.
