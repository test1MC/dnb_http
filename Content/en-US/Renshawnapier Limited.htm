N. Brown Foods (aka Renshawnapier) has found a sweet spot in the food biz. The UK-based company is a supplier of sugar (Whitworths and nbf), marzipan and other baking ingredients (Renshaw), as well as icings (Regalice), and jams (R&W Scott). The company also makes caramel, frosting, marshmallows, cooking chocolate, sauces, and syrups, as well as dairy ingredients (milk powders, stabilizers, emulsifiers, boxed fats, and dextrose, glucose, and fructose). It serves customers in the food manufacturing, retailing, wholesaling, and foodservice sectors throughout the UK. It also exports its products to mainland Europe and the US. N. Brown Foods is owned by The Real Good Food Company .

N. Brown Foods is based in Wakefield, West Yorkshire, and maintains a location in Immingham, East Yorkshire. It operates in 30-plus countries worldwide.

The company makes cake decoration products and premium food ingredients for the baking sector.

N. Brown Foods sells through wholesalers and retailers directly as well as through an e-commerce site.

The company operates as one of the segment of parent company The Real Good Food Company , and contributed 20% of total revenues in 2015.

In 2015 that company's net revenue increased by 9%, mainly due to increase in sales from territories of Europe (via Real Good Food Europe), the US, and Australia.

In 2004 Napier Brown acquired James Budgett Sugars and baking-ingredients company Renshaw Scott.

The company has suffered from the rising costs of fuel and raw materials. In a cost cutting measure, in 2009 the company combined its sugar (Napier Brown) and bakery ingredients operations (Renshaw) under the name Renshawnapier.
