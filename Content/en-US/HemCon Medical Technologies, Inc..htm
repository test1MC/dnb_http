HemCon Medical Technologies, operating as Tricol Biomedical, is a boon to US soldiers wounded on the battlefield. The company's proprietary HemCon bandages and ChitoFlex dressings stop bleeding in trauma patients; in fact, the dressing becomes adhesive upon contact with blood and can prevent hemorrhage. HemCon bandages are made from chitosan, a polysaccharide derived from dried shrimp shells and known for its clotting abilities. First responders, oral surgeons, and hospitals also use the company's products. HemCon was founded in 2001 with partial funding from the US Army ; its bandages are now part of every Army medic's standard kit. HemCon was acquired by microfiber specialist Tricol International in 2016.
