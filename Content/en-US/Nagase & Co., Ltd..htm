Venerable Nagase & Co. is a major Japanese trader, developer, and manufacturer of chemical products. Although chemicals, plastics, and related production machinery account for much of its sales, the more-than-182-year-old company also produces and distributes electronics, medical supplies, and health care and beauty products. Nagase maintains a worldwide network of sales and distribution branches in Asia, Europe, and North America. The company operates through subsidiaries and alliances with companies such as Eastman Chemical Company and Union Carbide .

In Japan the company has 42 companies; Northeast Asia, 29; Southeast Asia, 18 companies; North, Central, and South America, 9 companies; and Europe, 2.

Japan contributed 52% of Nagase's fiscal 2014 (March year end) revenues; China, 21%; and other countries, 27%.

Nagase operates in five segments: Advanced Materials & Processing; Functional Materials; Electronics; Automotive & Energy; and Life & Healthcare (functioning within the corresponding industries).

Advanced Materials & Processing (32% of Nagase's fiscal 2014 revenues) is engaged in the sales of dyestuffs, dyes, functional dyes, information printing products, thermoplastic resins, thermosetting resins, synthetic rubber, inorganic materials, plastics products, resins molding tools/ dies, and external inspection equipment, and more to the industries of dye/additive, information printing, textile processing, raw resin material, resin molding, and functional film and sheet.

Functional Materials (24%) is engaged in the sales of materials for paints/inks, urethane materials, plastic materials, plastic adhesives, industrial oil solutions, surfactants, fluorochemicals, encapsulment materials, silicone materials, and more to the industries of the paints/inks, resins, urethane foam, organic synthesis, surfactants, semiconductor.

The Electronics segment (19%) sells pre-processing materials and devices for LCD/semiconductors, materials for LCD panels, materials and devices for semiconductor assembly, low-temperatures/vacuum equipment, high-function epoxy resins, precision electronics abrasives, and more to the industries of the display, touch panel, LCD, semiconductor, HDD, electronic components, heavy electrical, and other.

Automotive & Energy (14%) is engaged in sales of plastics products, thermoplastic resins, thermosetting resins, resins molding tools/dies, battery materials, solar cell/secondary battery-related materials, and more to the industries of the automotive, automotive component, and energy.

Life & Healthcare (11%) sells pharmaceutical/agricultural chemicals and materials, research products, in-vitro diagnostics, enzymes, food additives, additives for cosmetics, and feeds/fertilizers and radiation measurement services to the industries of the pharmaceutical, food, cosmetics, and other. In addition, this segment sells cosmetics, health foods, and beauty foods directly to consumers.

The company's global sales network sells its products in Japan and in overseas. Industries served includes Performance Chemicals (Paint/ink, resin, synthetic fiber, urethane foam and film industries), Speciality Chemicals (Oil solution, surfactant, organic synthesis, electronics materials, and semiconductor industries); Colors & Imaging (Dye/pigment, manufacturing/thermal papers, printing and printing materials, fiber processing and auto body repair industry industries); Polymer Global (Office automation equipment, electronics and consumer electronics industries); Polymer Products (Cosmetics and toiletries, electric and electronics, and sheets and films industries); Electronic Chemicals (Display device industry -- LCD/organic EL, semiconductor, electronic components, heavy electric machinery, automotive, and solar battery industries); Electronic Materials (Smartphone and tablet, display, touch panel, and LED industries); Automotive (Automotive and automotive parts industries); Energy (Battery, photovoltaic, electric equipment, and automotive and construction industries); Life & Healthcare Products (Food, pharmaceutical and medical, diagnostic drug, cosmetics, household, agricultural, fisheries and livestock industries); and Beauty Care Products (General consumers).

In fiscal 2014 Nagase's net revenues (in local currency) increased by 9%.

Sales from Advanced Materials & Processing increased mainly due to higher gross profit from revenue gains and better profitability among manufacturing subsidiaries that operate in the colors and imaging business.

Sales from Functional Materials segment increased due to higher gross profit in Japan and Northeast Asia offset by decreased gross profit of manufacturing subsidiaries.

Sales from electronic increased, but its operating profit decrease due to weak performance in thin-glass processing revenues in Northeast Asia that offset gross profit gains owing to higher touch panel materials sales.

Automotive & Energy saw overall gains in net sales, with solid gains in automotive-related business revenues in Northeast Asia, the United States, Europe, and Japan, as well as growth in battery-related materials revenues.

Sales from the Life & Healthcare segment also grew.

In fiscal 2014, Nagase's net income declined by 18% due to an increase in selling, general, and administrative expenses driven by higher R&D and manufacturing costs. In addition, a decrease in gain on sale of property, plant, and equipment also contributed to the drop in net income.

Cash from operating activities decreased by 32% due to changes in notes and accounts receivable, inventories, exchange loss, and changes in gain on sales of property, plant and equipment plus increase in depreciation and amortization.

Facing rising oil and natural resources costs, Nagase is seeking to reduce its expenses and bring more efficiency to its operations in the mature market of Japan, while expanding in growth markets (including setting up new petrochemical plants in China, where it has 14 manufacturing companies) and in the Middle East.

The company is refocusing on quality and cost management in Advanced Materials & Processing, and on new business products that make the most of the unique technological strengths of their customers. It is also focused on expanding and creating new plastics businesses, restructuring its overseas sales structure for trehalose (natural alpha-linked disaccharide) and building a more robust research and development function at the Nagase R&D Center, specializing in biotechnology. It also plans to create a new parent division and support businesses for the company in specialty chemicals.

The company will also continue to grow existing businesses through stronger relationships with their customers, select and focus development projects to bring current projects to the market faster and more clearly define their business portfolio.

Nagase plans to work with major EMS manufacturers, focus on potential customers in leading Taiwanese, Chinese, and Korean companies, and leverage its close relationships with customers in Japan to develop more Japanese-affiliated customers and suppliers around the world.

Geographically, it plans to find new customers in the newly developing markets in China's interior, capture new demand in the emerging economics of Southeast Asia, and work in cooperation with Nagase Plastics to grow business in Japan. It is also looking to continue to improve its global cooperative business structure and human resources training at local sales and manufacturing entities in Japan, Europe, the US, China and other Asian regions, South Korea, and ASEAN regions.

Nagase was formed in 1832 as a wholesaler of dyestuffs.
