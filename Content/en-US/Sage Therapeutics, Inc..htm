Sage Therapeutics has a wise approach to drug development. The biopharmaceutical company's drug candidates target rare, life-threatening central nervous system disorders that are usually treated in a hospital setting with clearly defined end results in mind. It focuses on disorders that have inadequate treatments or none approved by the FDA . The development-stage company's pipeline includes three candidates designed to treat various stages of status epilecticus, a state of persistent seizure with a 20% mortality rate. Sage Therapeutics began operations in 2011 as Sterogen Biopharma but quickly changed its name. Its 2014 IPO raised $83.7 million to fund continued development of its drug candidates.
