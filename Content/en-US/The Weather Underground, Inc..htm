Want to know the weather for your upcoming ski vacation or how volcanoes effect climate change? Check out The Weather Underground's wunderground.com. The site draws climate data from more than 24,000 personal weather stations worldwide, offering zip code searches, radar and satellite images, and historical weather data. Its website has more than 10 million visitors per month. The Weather Underground also provides blogs and mobile apps, and supplies services to media companies such as Google and Hearst. Formed in 1995, the company traces its roots to meteorologist Jeff Masters' PhD work at the University of Michigan and takes its name from a Bob Dylan song. The Weather Channel acquired the company in 2012.
