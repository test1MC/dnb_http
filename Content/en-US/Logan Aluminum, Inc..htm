The folks who like to crush beer cans against their foreheads owe a lot to Logan Aluminum. Logan manufactures aluminum sheet that is used primarily by the beverage (beer and soft drink) can industry. A joint venture of Novelis (55%) and Tri-Arrows Aluminum (45%), Logan Aluminum consists of one production facility that also manufactures products for makers of building products and rigid containers as well as for the automotive industry. Its technology includes ingot casting, hot and cold rolling, and finishing. Logan also operates an aluminum recycling facility.

In 2013, joint venture owner Tri-Arrows Aluminum became a subsidiary of Japanese consortium UACJ Corporation.

At its plant in Russellville, Kentucky, Logan produces two main aluminum alloy sheet products: the 5182 alloy is used for making beverage can tops and tabs; and the 3104 alloy is used to manufacture can bodies.

To save costs at its production facility, in 2013 the company announced the results of its energy efficiency efforts in partnership with the Tennessee Valley Authority . Logan refitted its light fixtures and upgraded equipment (including pumps and air dryers), cutting the mill's annual power consumption by more than 2 million kilowatt-hours.

That year it completed upgrades to the air pollution-control equipment at one of its cold mills, resulting in an 85% reduction in particulate emissions and an 82% reduction in VOC emissions.

In a move to streamline its expense management processes, in 2011 Logan selected Oracle Financials-based ExpenseAnywhere-Enterprise cloud application for automating its corporate expense management processes.

Logan is a joint venture of Tri-Arrows Aluminum and Novelis (owned by Indian aluminum producer Hindalco).

The company was formed in 1983.
