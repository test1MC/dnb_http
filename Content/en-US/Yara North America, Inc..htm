Yara's theme is about using Nature's resources to improve plant growth and reduce pollution by developing environmentally friendly applications for its fertilizers and specialty chemicals. Yara North America is the North American subsidiary of Norwegian fertilizer company Yara International . Its activities range from phosphate mining and ammonia/urea production, to commodity trade and highly specialized agriculture and industrial products. Yara North America produces both nitrogenous and phosphatic fertilizers for the agricultural and horticultural industry from Yara's network terminals across the US and Canada.

Yara International has operations in more than 50 countries and sells to 150 countries.

As part of a major industrial application initiative, in 2013 Yara North America launched a new Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) production and distribution line at its plant in Belle Plaine, Canada. DEF is a high purity urea solution used for NOx reduction in diesel vehicles. That year it also opened a storage facility in Virginia to provide fleets and retail outlets throughout the Mid-Atlantic region increased access to Air1® DEF, an operating fluid used by diesel-powered vehicles equipped with selective catalytic reduction technology to meet EPA 2010 emissions regulations. The Chesapeake terminal joins Yara North America's network of storage terminals in order to supply what is likely to become the largest DEF market in the world. By 2019, Integer Research estimates DEF consumption in North America will be 1.2 billion tons per year.

In 2012 Yara North America opened a new storage terminal in Bayonne, New Jersey to provide fleets and retail outlets throughout the East Coast with increased access to Air1® DEF.

Yara International has been operating in North America since 1946.
