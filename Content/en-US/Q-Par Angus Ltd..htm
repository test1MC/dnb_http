Antennas are par for the course at Q-Par Angus (dba Steatite Q-par Antennas). The company makes microwave antennas, including broadcast, helical, reflector, omnidirectional, sinuous, spiral, patch, and horn antennas. The company also makes related components and accessories such as coaxial adaptors, splitters, software, and tripods. Its research and consulting services range from radiation hazard surveys and microwave materials analysis to distance, depth, and moisture content measurements, as well as design and manufacturing. Customers include BAE Systems , Boeing , NASA , and the UK Ministry of Defence. The company is a division of Steatite Limited, which is part of Solid State .

Steatite Q-par's facilities include a secure 26,000 square feet site, located in Redditch dedicated to Design, Engineering, Manufacturing and Service support. The company's Leominster facility has a Microwave Laboratory and is dedicated to antenna production, electronics, and software as well as a design engineering workshop.

Steatite Q-par serves a variety of sectors. It makes products for defense and civil customers for aeronautical, naval, and land applications. The electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) market uses horn and other antennas for emission and immunity testing. Environmental uses include the of water surface movements to help combat flooding. Among the navigation, jamming, and intelligence gathering uses of its products, Steatite Q-par antennas also serve surveillance needs, broadcasting, and universities.

The company is an expert in the research, design, and manufacture of high specification commercial-off-the-Shelf and custom designed microwave antennas, subsystems, and associated antenna components typically operating from 100 MHz up to 500 GHz.

Its manufacturing services also include etching, ion deposition, protective finishing, and electroforming. Its facilities are also capable of developing software using languages such as C++, C#, Labview, and Visual Basic, as well as designing printed circuit boards.

The company serves several UK and international government organizations, and defense companies and system integrators who operate in a wide variety of areas including ELINT, COMINT, EMC, and Spectrum management.

In 2014 Steatite Q-par launched a new ultra-small multi-octave wideband 1-18 ghz left and right hand circularly polarized spiral antenna.

Expanding its reach, that year the company opened a new sales office at Las Vegas, Nevada, and appointed AA Matech as its distributor for France.

In 2013 Q-par Angus was acquired by Solid State , and was integrated into its Steatite operation,. As part of the transaction the company was renamed Steatite Q-par Antennas.

Q-par Angus was founded in 1973.
