Do you ever have the feeling that you are being watched? Might be because you are, thanks to Pelco's products. Pelco designs, develops, and manufactures video surveillance equipment, including security cameras and enclosures, discreet camera domes, video matrix systems, next generation digital video recorders, megapixel network cameras, and fiber optic transmission systems for video and data. Its 5,500-plus products are sold through a network of dealers in more than 130 countries. Customers come from a variety of industries such as retail, gaming, corrections, universities, mining, and offshore drilling. The company is a subsidiary of French electric power company Schneider Electric .

The company has offices Europe, Asia, the Middle East, Africa, Australia, and the US.

Pelco's products have been installed in landmark locations such as Windsor Castle, Tiananmen Square, Niagara Falls, and the Statue of Liberty. The company's products also have a presence in many major airports, banks, and casinos. Pelco's brands include acclaimed Spectra, Sarix, Esprit, Camclosure, Endura, and Digital Sentry.

The company sells its products through a global network of sales representatives.

Pelco has moved to develop a more global organization (using parent Schneider Electric 's infrastructure) that can capitalize on the growth in developing regions and elsewhere. It is also growing through the launch of innovative products, developed both organically and through partnerships.

In 2014 Pelco announced a new technology partnership with UK-based icomply and the integration of its V-TAS Pro platform into their product range. The Physical Security Information Management integration platform id used in a wide range of markets, from government and retail to transportation.

That year March Networks and Pelco announced the integration of the March Networks Command video management system with Sarix Series IP cameras, Spectra HD Dome PTZ and Sarix TI Thermal Imaging camera systems.

In 2013 Pelco and HuXinHuTong (Megaeyes) integrated Pelco's network cameras, including Sarix Mega-Performance Cameras, Spectra IP high-definition cameras, and multi-channel network encoders with the full range of HuXinHuTong's video management platform.

That year, the company released its new Pelco Sarix IL10 Series mini box and micro dome cameras, the first of more than 50 new Sarix fixed IP camera products to be released in 2013. It also launched its Pelco Infrared and White Light LED Illumination products for security camera installations.

The company expanded its sales operations in Asia and Latin America via a 2009 acquisition of Crockett International. Crocket had previously served as a provider of sales and service support for Pelco customers in those regions.

Pelco was acquired by Schneider Electric in 2008 in a deal worth some $1.5 billion. Schneider Electric purchased Pelco in order to strengthen Schneider's Buildings business, which includes power and HVAC control, and electronic security.

Pelco was founded in 1957.
