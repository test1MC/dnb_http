Across the Western US, black top roads that give a smooth ride and roofs that don't leak are the direct beneficiaries of Paramount Petroleum, a company for which the production of asphalt is paramount. The company, which was founded in the 1930s and owned by Alon USA Energy , manufactures, produces, and distributes asphalt products. Its core operations are a 54,000 barrel-per-day refinery (the largest and most efficient asphalt refinery in the state) located in Paramount, California and a 35,000 barrels per day refinery in Long Beach, California. It produces and transports jet fuel, diesel fuel, and gasoline to terminals and other refineries in Southern California.

Paramount Petroleum is stationed in California and operates primarily in the western US. It has offices in Phoenix, Flagstaff, and Fredonia, Arizona; Paramount, Long Beach, Elk Grove, Mojave, and Bakersfield, California; Fernley, Nevada; Seattle; Portland, Oregon; and Big Springs and Channelview, Texas.

In addition to specialized roads and roofing asphalt products, Paramount Petroleum produces diesel fuel, jet fuel, heating oil, gasoline, and gasoline feedstocks. Paramount Petroleum additionally produces and markets a wide range of modified and rubberized asphalt products and emulsions. Except for asphalt products, almost all of the company's products are shipped via pipelines to terminals and other refineries in Southern California.
