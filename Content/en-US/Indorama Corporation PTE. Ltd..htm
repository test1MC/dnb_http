Holding company Indorama Corporation owns stakes in nine companies that produce synthetic and cotton yarn, rubber gloves, plastic polymers, and petrochemicals. Its Asian subsidiaries include spandex firm Indorama Industries, rubber glove maker Medisafe Technologies, construction firm Indorama Real Estate, cotton and synthetic yarn mill ISIN Lanka, plastic and polyester company Indorama Ventures, and Indonesia's largest polyester producer, PT Indorama Synthetics. It also owns Eleme Petrochemicals, a polyolefins plant in Nigeria, and two other yarn mills in Turkey and Uzbekistan. Indorama Corporation, which traces it roots to a textile mill founded by ML Lohia in the 1950s, is owned by his descendents.
