If life deals you lemons or oranges, make the lemonade or the packaging to hold it. Smurfit Kappa Orange County (SKOC) manufactures paperboard and paper-based packaging, including recycled containerboard and a variety of graphics corrugated containers, folding cartons, and point-of-purchase displays. SKOC also offers coated options for enhanced product appearance, functionality, and specialty end-uses. It maintains its own recycling centers in the Southwestern US and multiple manufacturing facilities in the US and Northern Mexico. In late 2012 the company was acquired by Smurfit Kappa Group (SKG), a global corrugated box producer based in Ireland.

SKOC is part of SKG's Latin America operating segment, which specializes in forestry and papermaking and makes paper sacks and corrugated and folding cartons. It was acquired by SKG in late 2012 for $340 million.

SKOC has 10 packaging facilities and one recycled container board mill, primarily located on the West Coast. Other recycling centers reside in Arkansas, Oklahoma, and Texas.

SKOC engages in four lines of business: the designing, manufacturing, and supplying of paper-based packaging; paper manufacturing (for packaging, printing and writing purposes); recycling services; and forest estate management services and forestry products.

The company -- originally called Orange County Container Group (OCCG) -- was formed in 2008 through a merger between Texas-based Corrugated Services and predecessor Orange County Container. The former OCCG focused on making and distributing corrugated paper products to customers concentrated in Arizona, California, Texas, and Mexico.
