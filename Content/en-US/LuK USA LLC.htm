Unsatisfied with nothing but talk when you need more torque in your converter? As luck -- make that LuK -- would have it, there's a solution. LuK USA makes torque converters, clutches, flywheels, gearboxes, and other related transmission products for both the automobile and agriculture machinery industries. Its manufacturing facility on Wooster, Ohio measures 500,000 sq. ft. The company's customers include Ford , General Motors , Honda , Nissan , and Toyota Motor . LuK USA is a unit of German parts manufacturer LuK GmbH , which in turn is part of the Schaeffler manufacturing conglomerate.

LuK USA is engaged in the manufacture and distribution of automotive aftermarket products, primarily dampers, torque converters, clutches, and other transmission components.

The automotive division contributed about 69% of Schaeffler 's revenues in 2012. The division's revenues increased by 7% that year thanks to new customer projects and production start-ups, and favorable currency translations. In 2012 Schaeffler generated about 15% of its revenues from North America.

LuK USA is taking advantage of the boom in the US production of automobiles with automatic transmissions (as the industry rebounds from the recent recession) to develop torque converters and dampers, one of the main components of transmissions.

LuK USA was established in 1977.
