Speed nuts find acceptance at A. Raymond Tinnerman Manufacturing. Formerly known as Tinnerman Palnut Engineered Products, the company makes some 2,000 types of standard and specialty metal and plastic fasteners, including speed nuts (one-piece nut and washer units). The company's fastener lineup also includes spring steel, multi- and single-thread, and clamps and cable clips. It also offers custom-molded items, such as those used for automotive headlight adjustors. Customers include GE , Lear , Mattel , and Toyota . The company, founded in 1870 as Tinnerman Stove Company, developed the speed nut in 1936. France-based A. Raymond & Cie acquired Tinnerman in 2009.
