Aavid Thermalloy is a fan of fans and other cooling devices. The company makes heat sinks, fans, heat spreaders, liquid cooling products, cooling assemblies, and other devices used to manage temperature in computer, networking, and industrial electronic systems. It also makes interface materials including insulating films and thermal greases, as well as insulating covers. Aavid offers consulting and systems analysis services for data center operators looking to improve the efficiency of their facilities. The company's Aavid Design subsidiary provides product design, testing, and prototyping services related to thermal, mechanical, electronic, and industrial design.

Aavid has offices at New Hampshire and California in North America; Italy, Germany, and France in Europe; and in China, Taiwan, Singapore, and India in the Far East.

Aavid targets the enterprise computing, telecommunications, transportation, power, LED lighting, and consumer industries through distributors such as Future Electronics, Arrow Electronics , Avnet , Bisco Industries, Heilind Electronics , Sager Electronics, Allied Electronics, Digi-Key, Mouser Electronics, Hammond Electronics, and Richardson Electronics .

The company is looking to new markets such as alternative energy and LED lighting and to custom designed products for industry verticals including semiconductor manufacturing, data centers, and networking for continued growth.

In 2014, General Electric and Aavid Thermalloy teamed up to commercialize GE's Dual Piezoelectric Cooling Jets (DCJ) technology.

Aavid made two acquisitions in 2014 that augment its offerings. With the acquisition of Allcast, Aavid adds aluminum die casting products for LED lighting, recreational vehicle, consumer, and industrial applications to its lineup. In Nuventix, Aavid gets a range of fanless thermal management products. Offerings from Nuventix, based in Austin, Texas, include its Synjet technology for LED lighting applications.
