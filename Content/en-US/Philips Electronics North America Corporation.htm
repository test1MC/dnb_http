Whether you need an early morning shave or a late night TV fix, Philips Electronics North America has got the goods. The US arm of Dutch company Royal Philips Electronics , it oversees Philips operations in the US, Canada, and Mexico. Its divisions include Philips Healthcare (imaging, patient information systems, home healthcare products, clinical management and equipment financing), Consumer Lifestyle (consumer electronics, home appliances, personal care), and Lighting (home, industrial, and municipal light bulbs, fixtures, and systems). Philips Electronics North America's Corporate Technologies group conducts R&D and partners with outside groups to feed the development pipeline for the other units.

Parent Royal Philips Electronics operates in more than 100 countries. It has more than 22 R&D centers and 40 manufacturing sites.

The company has operations in its parent's three major segments: Philips Healthcare (41% Royal Philips Electronics' total revenues); and Lighting (36%); and Philips Consumer Lifestyle & Lighting (23%). Its Healthcare business is organized around four strategic business groups: Imaging Systems, Patient Care & Clinical Informatics, Home Healthcare Solutions, and Healthcare Transformation Services.

Philips Electronics North America continues to be an integral business for Royal Philips Electronics. North America is considered a mature market for Royal Philips Electronics, alongside Western Europe, Japan, Korea, Australia, and New Zealand.

Its Healthcare segment has been a primary focus for Philips Electronics in North America. The subsidiary's Healthcare operations represent its largest division. Strategy for the unit includes establishing a stronger imaging business, expanding both the geographic reach and product line of its home healthcare business, and continue to focus on women's health, cardiology, and oncology.

Within its Healthcare sector it has established the Healthcare Informatics Solutions & Services business group, which is focusing on a digital connected healthcare delivery platform, advanced informatics and big data analytics, and world-class integration and consulting services. At Consumer Lifestyle has new business initiative on Personal Health.

The electronics manufacturer relies on its technological expertise and its ability to chase emerging markets for growth. To this end, the firm is looking to expand its TV operations. To ensure its products are marketed well against its competitors' in the aggressive TV niche, parent Royal Philips Electronics inked a deal with Japan's Funai Electric Co . As part of agreement, Funai sources, distributes, markets, and sells TVs under the Philips and Magnavox brands in the US and Canada. Royal Philips Electronics is hoping its agreement with Funai will boost its profitability in North America so that it can shift its focus on developing its TV presence in Europe and other emerging markets. Philips is also counting on the introduction of more green products to drive growth.

The company sees lighting as a segment that should grow by 7% annually based on consumers moving to more energy efficient lighting, to more LED lighting, and the incorporation of control systems into existing and new lighting plans. In response Philips is increasing its efforts in LED outdoor lighting for streets and roadways and pushing green products (more than 50% of sales) across the sector. Philips Electronics North America is the largest lighting supplier on the continent and it continues to concentrate on developing energy-efficient products.
