Gardner Denver doesn't grow flowers in the Mile High City. The company makes blowers, petroleum pumps, and a variety of compressors, such as reciprocating, rotary screw, and sliding vane compressors, as well as positive displacement and centrifugal blowers. Compressed air products are its principal offering. Manufacturing plants and industrial facilities use the compressors to produce durable goods, process petroleum and pharmaceuticals, and treat wastewater. Gardner Denver also makes vacuum products, fluid-transfer products, and well-servicing pumps for oil and natural gas companies. It is adding new lines, such as water-jetting products. The company was taken private in mid-2013.

The company has three reportable segments. Industrial Products makes rotary screws, air and gas compressors, blowers, and vacuum pumps. The Energy Group manufactures liquid ring pumps and engineered systems for power generation, environmental, petrochemical, pulp and paper and industrial applications and for the oil and natural gas well drilling, petrochemical, and industrial sectors. In addition, the Medical Group manufactures single-piece piston reciprocating, diaphragm and linear compressors and vacuum pumps, which are used in medical, environmental, and laboratory applications.

Gardner Denver has offices in roughly 30 countries and serves its global customers with products manufactured at about 40 manufacturing facilities located in the Americas, EMEA (Europe, the Middle East, Africa), and Asia/Pacific.

The company's products and engineered services are sold through multi-channel and worldwide distribution systems and are used for applications in virtually every market sector. These include industrial manufacturing, transportation, environmental processes, health care applications, food and beverage, labs and life science, mining and construction, chemical, and energy production.

The company focuses on organic growth, innovation and expansion of aftermarket services; business acquisitions; and on improving its operating margins.

With predecessor companies dating back to 1859, the company's corporate history includes the 1927 merger of the Gardner Company with Denver Rock Drill, forming the Gardner-Denver Company. Cooper Industries acquired the company in 1979 and spun it off 15 years later. In the summer of 2013, Gardner Denver was acquired by investment firm Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co. L.P. in a deal (which included debt) valued at $3.9 billion. Gardner Denver had previously put itself up for sale in October 2012.
