Russian warplane maker IRKUT wants to be known for more than its Sukhoi fighter jets. The company designs and builds combat and amphibian aircraft that have, for over 75 years, been delivered to more than 37 countries. While military aircraft account for about two-thirds of sales, IRKUT also makes unmanned aerial vehicles, combat navigation and targeting systems, aircraft components, and civil airplanes. The company has partnered with Airbus for help in adapting operations to global industry standards. As part of the partnership, IRKUT and Airbus are collaborating to make components. United Aircraft Corporation, which is controlled by the Russian government, owns about 80% of IRKUT.
