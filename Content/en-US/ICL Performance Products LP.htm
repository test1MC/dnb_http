ICL Performance Products LP is phosphorific! The North American unit of the performance products segment of Israel Chemicals Limited (ICL), it produces phosphorus chemicals, phosphoric acid, and phosphate salts. The chemicals are used in foods, cleaners, water treatment, flat-panel displays, oral care products, paints and coatings, and pharmaceuticals. The company also manufactures flame retardants. ICL Performance Products operates plants in Brazil and the US (California, Kansas, Missouri, and New Jersey) and elsewhere. It represents about a quarter of its parent's total business.

ICL Performance Products has manufacturing plants in Argentina, Australia, Austria, Brazil, China, France, Germany, Israel, Mexico, the UK and the US. It also maintains research and development laboratories in North America, South America, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

It develops, produces, markets and sells a range of phosphate based products as part of ICL 's strategy to increasing its production of downstream products with higher added value. It also develops, produces, markets and sells alumina based products and other industrial performance products.

ICL Performance Products' two major product lines include Products Based on the Phosphate Value Chain (Food-grade & technical-grade phosphoric acid for food and industrial applications), and Phosphate salts (blends and phosphorus derivatives for industrial applications); its Other Products and Services include pharmaceutical and cosmetic ingredients, thermoplastic materials. and synthetic gypsum.

In Mishor Rotem in Israel, the company manufactures pure phosphoric acid by means of purifying fertilizer-grade phosphoric acid produced by ICL Fertilizers. It also makes thermal phosphoric acid in the US by using elemental phosphorous and the segment also purchases purified phosphoric acid from third parties.

The production capacity of its main products include Pure phosphoric acid (291,000 tons per year); Phosphate salts and food additives (474,000 tons); Other phosphate-based products (134,000 tons); and Other products (491,000 tons).

Brand names include Fibrisol, Brifisol, Joha, and Tari.

The parent company derived about 25% of its revenues from the Performance Products segment in fiscal 2013.

ICL Performance Products sells its products to industrial and commercial customers in Europe, North America, South America and Asia. The company's main markets include food, detergent, metallurgy, paint and coatings, electronics, footwear, paper, pharmaceuticals, water treatment, concrete, oil additives, and fire fighting.

Its marketing network is based primarily on an extensive internal marketing organization and, to a lesser extent, on external distributors and selling agents.

Streamlining their sales and marketing efforts, in 2012 ICL Performance Products and sister company BK Giulini combined their previously separate sales and marketing activities under the umbrella of a new North American food division, ICL Food Specialties.

ICL Performance Products' revenues rose by 7% in 2013 primarily from increased quantities sold in the P205 based businesses due to the market recovery in Europe; growth in the P2S5 business as a result of the incremental sales from the Knapsack acquisition; higher sales in North America, and the favorable results from currency exchange rates.

The company is focusing on expanding its operations in markets with high growth potential, mainly in Asia, South America and Eastern Europe; growing its portfolio; investing in R&D ($19 million in 2013); and on acquisitions and joint venture formation.

In 2012, as part of an ongoing effort to provide customers with improved, cost-effective products, ICL Performance Products developed an improved process for preparing asphalt binders containing styrene-butadiene block polymers and polyphosphoric acid.

In 2014 ICL acquired Hagesud Group, a leading German maker of premium spice blends and food additives for processing meat.

In 2013 ICL agreed to buy Vale Fertilizantes ' stake in Fosbrasil SA, a Brazil-based joint venture producer of phosphoric acid and premium. fertilizers.

Growing its phosphorus-based specialty ingredients assets, in 2013 ICL agreed to acquire the phosphorus pentasulfide business of Germany-based Thermphos International.

In 2011 ICL bought US-based HALOX (a division of Hammond Group) an industry leader in specialty phosphate-based, corrosion inhibitor products for the paint and coating industry.

In 2011 ICL acquired Cosmocel Quimica S.A. de C.V., a subsidiary of Mexico-based Cosmocel Group, a manufacturer of functional food ingredients and specialty chemicals. In the deal, ICL picked up the company's state-of-the-art manufacturing facility in Monterrey, and its extensive sales and distribution network in Mexico, Central, and South America.

ICL Performance Products was formed at the turn of this century as Astaris, a joint venture of FMC and Solutia . ICL, wanting to expand its operations in North America, bought Astaris from the two US companies in late 2005. Solutia was in bankruptcy from 2003 to 2008 and had been looking to lighten its load a bit. ICL was able to increase its international business in a significant way with the purchase of the former Astaris.
