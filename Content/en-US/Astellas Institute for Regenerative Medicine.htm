No need to choose between embryonic and adult stem cells -- Astellas Institute for Regenerative Medicine (AIRM, formerly Ocata Therapeutics) works with both to develop therapies to regenerate human tissue. The firm has developed three product platforms based on stem-cell technology: retinal pigment epithelial therapy for the treatment of degenerative retinal disease, myoblast stem cell therapy to treat chronic heart failure and other heart problems, and the hemangioblast platform for the treatment of blood and cardiovascular diseases. Though AIRM is focused on bringing the clinical-stage technologies to market, it also conducts research for other regenerative medicine treatments. Astellas Pharma acquired Ocata for $379 million in 2016 and gave it the present name.
