Morrison Bowmore Distillers takes its time. The company produces Auchentoshan, Bowmore, and Glen Garioch whiskies, all aged to perfection. The company's other brand, McClelland's, is scotch whisky, or as the company calls it "uisge beatha" -- "water of life." Founded in 1951, Morrison Bowmore's products are distributed in retail and wholesale outlets in countries throughout the world. Morrison has three production sites in Scotland and guarantees it uses only Scottish-grown grain. The company is a subsidiary of Japan-based liquor giant Suntory Holdings , which acquired Morrison in 1994.

The company offers its products in Europe, North America, Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and Australasia.

Its distilleries have been recognized as being some of the most environmentally friendly in Europe.

Morrison sells its products through distributors and duty free channels.

In 2010 Morrison replaced Glenmorangie as Drambuie's distiller and bottler.

Morrison was founded in 1951 by Stanley P. Morrison and J. Howatt.
