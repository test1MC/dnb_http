Witton Chemical keeps it wits about it in order to be competitive in a tough market. It makes fine chemicals, dyes, and pre-polymer products, as well as a line of polyacrylic dispersants (produced by its Acriflow division) used by the water treatment, paints and coatings, and paper industries. The company operates a single manufacturing plant in East Anglia with a total storage capacity of more than 450,000 litres. It produces glutaric acid, which is used as an intermediate ingredient in the production of pharmaceuticals and in the process of chemical synthesis. Witton Chemical specializes in the customer-specific development and contract manufacturing of polymers and fine chemicals.

The company offers a manufacturing service for producing polymers, fine chemicals, contract drying, and for drying chemicals.

The development and optimization of polymerization processes is a core part of Witton Chemical's business. Its reactors are equipped with condensers and solvent can be stripped from polymer solutions using vacuum if required.

The company is also skilled in manufacturing moisture curing prepolymers based on methylene diphenyl diisocyanate (MDI), isophorone diisocyanate (IPDI), oligomeric isocyanates and silanes for use in sealants and adhesive, and in the production of latexes for use in coatings and surface treatments.

The company seeks to use its expertise and ongoing process developments to reduce costs for the many companies which outsource their production to Witton Chemical.

Witton Chemical was founded in 1962 and remains an independent, private company. It moved its plant to its present site in Mildenhall, Suffolk, in 1974.
