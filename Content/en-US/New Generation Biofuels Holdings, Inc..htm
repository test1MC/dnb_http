What's new about New Generation Biofuels (NGB) is that it's all biofuel without the complex chemistry. Typical biofuels require some petroleum-based fuel as an additive and a very tricky chemical reaction to bring it all together. But NGB claims it has a more simple and efficient process than most biofuels, which it uses to produce its fuel from vegetable oil or animal fat feedstocks with less waste, by-products, and emissions. The fuel can be used to replace diesel, heating oil, kerosene, and other fuel oils. NGB, a development stage company, is building a manufacturing facility in Maryland with a 50-million-gallon-per-year capacity. The firm traces its roots to Wireless Holdings, formed in 2003.
