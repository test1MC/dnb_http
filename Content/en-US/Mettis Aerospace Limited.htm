Mettis Aerospace makes parts that other companies put into airplanes. Typical offerings include aerofoil products such as compressor vanes, stators, and rotors; ferrous and non-ferrous forged components; and complex machined components made of aluminum, titanium, and alloys. Applications include compressor discs, pinions and gears, seals, and engine casings; ribs, spars, struts, access panels, window frames, and ailerons; manifolds, pistons, and cylinders; helicopter rotor hubs and propellers; and landing gear components. Mettis Aerospace also offers kitting and logistics services. Customers have included such noteworthy names as Airbus , Boeing , and Rolls-Royce .

Mettis Aerospace is based in Redditch, Worcestershire, UK, and also operates in China.

The company's engineering and materials expertise translates to business beyond the aerospace sector, Mettis' diverse product portfolio spans aerospace and airframe, automotive, and industrial products, wheel and braking systems, and landing gear.

To its credit, Mettis Aerospace made the nozzle forgings for the Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer, mounted on the International Space Station. The equipment is used to measure cosmic rays and search for antimatter.
