Aqualisa helps its customers clean up their act. The company makes showers and shower-related products (shower heads, shower valves, and digital, thermostatic, and electric showers). Showers marketed under the Quartz and Axis brand names can be turned on remotely, letting users know when "the water's fine." Its Aquavalve bi-metallic shower valve is more resistant to limescale buildup than conventional valves. In addition to operating in the UK, Aqualisa contracts with Malaysian manufacturers and is exploring the global export business. The Premier Collection (ilux Digital, HiQu Digital, HiQuXT and Futori) was shortlisted in the Best Bathroom Product category in the prestigious House Beautiful Awards 2013.

Aqualisa is headquartered in Westerham, Kent, employing nearly 250 people at its corporate office and across the UK.

One of the UK's leading shower manufacturers, Aqualisa develops, designs, and makes showers and their accessories. Its has develeop an range electric showers that deliver enhanced performance and improved safety, and well as showers with features that encourage better water usage. Aqualisa's digital showers have an optional eco-setting capable of reducing water consumption by up to 25%.

The company, which boasts a turnover of some Â£40 million, sells its products primarily through distributors. It also maintains an online accessories store through which it sells a comprehensive range of shower accessories.

Known for its early successes with the thermostatic shower valve, the company has since pioneered digital showers while also further developing thermostatic shower technology and electric showers.

Aqualisa works to keep its portfolio of products fresh. In 2014 it launched a new range of Quartz Digital and Visage Digital showers that includes new looks plus dual outlet systems branded as Quartz or Visage Digital Divert. Digital Divert allows users to switch from handset to drencher head or handset to bath fill -- all at the touch of a button. The 2014 collection of dual control valve (DCV) showers includes four different shower/bath configurations. Two single outlet designs offer a choice between a wall-mounted drencher shower head or adjustable shower head, whilst two divert configurations allow users to switch between a drencher shower head and adjustable shower head or, alternatively, between an adjustable shower head and a bath fill.

In 2013, as part of an experiment in modern living, the ilux brand was chosen to feature in the family bathroom of Channel 4 's lifestyle program 'Home of the Future.'

That year Aqualisa launched a new, multi-platform website as a fully interactive resource for the shower manufacturer's trade and consumer customers.

The company is also exploring the global export business.

Aqualisa, founded in 1977, was acquired through a management buyout led by private equity firm CBPE Capital in 2004.
