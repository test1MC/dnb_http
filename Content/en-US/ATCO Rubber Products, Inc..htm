Even when things heat up, ATCO Rubber Products keeps its cool and remains flexible. The company is an industry leader in manufacturing flexible ducts used in commercial and residential heating, cooling, and ventilation systems. Its products include both insulated and uninsulated sleeves, sheet metal, and aluminum and special purpose items. ATCO operates about 15 manufacturing sites across the US and international sites in the Netherlands, the United Arab Emirates (UAE), and India. All of ATCO's products are developed and manufactured to meet specific international codes and standards.

Based in Fort Worth, Texas, ATCO boasts a dozen plants across the US and three international locations. ATCO has US production facilities in Arkansa, Maryland, Indiana, Georgia, Texas, North Carolina, California, Arizona, Florida, and New Jersey. It also has operations in New Delhi, India; Oldenzaal, Netherlands; Berlin; and Sharjah, UAE.

The company's product line includes flex duct systems for residential and commercial HVAC applications, as well as sheet metal, accessories, and component parts.

ATCO's product have been used in such projects as Tarrant County College, Fort Worth, Texas; Burj Khalifa Tower, Dubai, UAE; and Queen Alia International Airport, Amman, Jordan.

ATCO's factories and sales hubs work together to create a streamlined logistics network capable of shipping product quickly and efficiently to customers around the world. The company's products are sold in more than 50 countries.

With a presence in Europe, India, the Middle East, and in the US along the Gulf of Mexico and the Pacific and Atlantic coasts, ATCO is strategically located to offer customers low freight and labor costs, high production capabilities, and 5- to 7-day service turnaround.

The company is looking to find new ways to expand its strategic global footprint, shorten supply lines, improve costs, and ensure efficient delivery of ATCO products to any location around the world. In this regard, in 2012 ATCO moved its flex division from Indianapolis to Crawfordsville, Indiana and its sheet metal division from Crawfordsville to Gary.

ATCO's flexible duct products have received certification from Greenguard, a nonprofit organization that promotes indoor air quality.

ATCO was established in the 1961 to serve the aerospace industry and eventually began producing and selling insulated duct for both residential and commercial applications.
