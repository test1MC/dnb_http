As can be gleaned from its name, Mitsui Chemicals America is the American home base of the Japanese Mitsui Chemicals, Inc. The company's products range from adhesive resins and electronic materials to chemical solvents and intermediates. It serves makers of pharmaceuticals, paper and packaging, semiconductors and electronics, and vehicles. It also serves growers of crops with its insecticides for pest management and protection of a wide variety of vegetables and fruits. Mitsui Chemicals America has locations and affiliates throughout the US as well as in Mexico; it serves customers in both North and South America.

The company supplies value-added chemical products and plastic materials to markets in North and South America.

Mitsui Chemicals America covers a broad range of automotive, advanced, and other performance materials. It is committed to the development of specialty products such as Chemicals and Intermediates, Chemical Solvents, Specialty Polymers and Engineering Plastics, Functional Materials, Electronic Materials, and Adhesive Resins.

It major divisions are Functional Chemicals; Functional Polymeric Materials; Polyurethane; Basic Chemicals; Petrochemicals; and Films and Sheets.

The company focuses on the manufacture and sales of its ADMER-brand adhesive resin, as well as on the marketing of other chemical products made by its parent company. ADMER creates strong adhesion and durability and is used in several applications, including plastic bottles and containers, in film and coating for food packaging, and for coating steel pipes.

The parent company makes a range of functional chemicals for the healthcare industry, as well as agrochemicals, functional films, coatings and adhesive materials, and engineering plastics and compounds. Its petrochemical and basic chemical operations include phenols (plastic compounds) and polypropylene (PP) compounds. The group supplies PP compounds to the automotive industry for use in instrument panels, door panels, and exterior bumpers.

Another widely used Mitsui Chemicals brand is TAFMER, which is a resin developed from polymerization (elastomers) technology. The process combines the TAFMER resin with other resins to enhance the strength or flexibility of those products. It is used primarily in automotive components, but is also used in sports shoes and packaging.

The company's polyolefin products, which are derived from crude oil, are also supplied primarily to Japanese automakers.

Subsidiary Anderson Development Company develops specialty chemicals including polyurethanes and nitrogen trifluoride. Advanced Composites, Inc. manufactures polypropylene compounds. Image Polymers Company makes specialized resins for use in toner for laser printers.

Mitsui Chemicals America serves customers in a range of markets: Agrochemicals; Automotive; Building & Civil Engineering; Healthcare/Pharmaceutical/Personal Care; Packaging; and Semiconductor/Electronics/ Electrical.

In 2015 the company's revenues were 8% up on 2014. That year Mitsui Chemicals America's revenues accounted for 11% for its parent company's total sales.

Mitsui Chemicals America develops technologies and materials harmonizing with the global environment while maintaining competitive advantage in its business domains: The parent company has been making efforts to develop the business through several measures, including mergers and acquisitions. With the increased efforts to develop shale gas in the US, the company has been working toward development of a major petrochemicals project. In addition to projects in North America, the parent company is expanding its operations in South America and other parts of the Americas. Among its strategic goals is to bolster its ADMER operations in the US. It is also striving to meet product specifications for its customer in the US, which are becoming increasingly more stringent.

The company is working to expand the use of polyolefin products in other markets, such as consumer goods, that will perform well even in bad economic times.

The Mitsui Chemicals Group is targeting production expansion of its polypropylene for automotive materials, made in eight major world markets (Japan, US, Mexico, Brazil, Europe, India, Thailand, and China). In 2015, polypropylene compound production capacity reached 1 million tons per year which reflected an increase in North and Central America demand and output.

In North America and Mexico, the group plans to reinforce its supply capacity at a pace faster than market growth. Its basic approach is to continue investing steadily while monitoring conditions in each region.

Mitsui Chemicals America was founded in 1997.
