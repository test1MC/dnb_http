Tnemec (pronouced tah-KNEE-mick, it is cement spelled backwards) makes more than 100 different paints and coatings that can be used as primers on concrete, masonry, steel, and flooring materials. It also provides waterproofing, corrosion prevention (for wastewater facilities), and exterior finishing products. Tnemec serves customers in different markets including architectural, industrial, manufacturing, oilfield services, water and wastewater, and water storage tanks. Its Chemprobe line specializes in masonry products, while the company's StrataShield line focuses on floor and wall coatings.

Tnemec has manufacturing and distribution facilities in 8 US states.

The company operates about 390 projects in 40 US states (primarily in California and Illinois), as well as in Canada and China. It makes more than 120 architectural and industrial coating products at facilities in Kansas City and Baltimore. It also operates distribution facilities in Atlanta, Dallas, Indianapolis, New Orleans, Seattle, and Compton (California).

It products are used in more than 10,000 water tanks, as well as in treatment plants, manufacturing plants, industrial facilities, and specialty architectural buildings.

The company sells its products in North America, Latin America, Asia, Australia, Europe, and Africa. It serves the architectural, industrial, manufacturing, oilfield, processing, and water storage tank markets. Tnemec sells its products through its sales representatives, including McCarty Equipment, Bob Herbert & Associates, and Phoenix Specialty.

Innovation is key to the Tnemec's growth. For instance, it has introduced Aerolon, an insulative coating that features Cabot 's Enova aerogel. Aerolon is tailored for industrial customers that use pipes, tanks or steel in refineries, processing plants, and other industrial facilities.

To better serve its increasingly diverse customer base, Tnemec has a product labeling program that combines a distinctive graphic design with multilingual capabilities.

To increase brand awareness with the public, the company has an annual online "Tnemec Tank of the Year" contest.

In 2010 Tnemec purchased the Glass Armor epoxy lining technology for above-ground storage tanks from Bridgeport Chemical. The linings are marketed by Tnemec under the Tank Armor name, along with the company's other offerings of protective coatings and linings.

Tnemec was established in 1921. The family of founder Albert Bean owns and runs the company.
