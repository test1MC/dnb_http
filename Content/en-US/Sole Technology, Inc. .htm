Sole Technology aims to put soul into its sportswear. The company makes and markets action sport footwear and gear, including apparel, backpacks, hats, wallets, and other accessories, for men and women. Its brands include etnies, etnies Plus, éS, Altamont, Emerica, and ThirtyTwo. Etnies is not only eco-friendly, the brand is working to become completely carbon neutral by 2020. Targeting skateboarders and BMXers, Sole Technology distributes its products through specialty retailers in the US and internationally in 70-plus countries. The company operates a large distribution facility in California and a New York City showroom. Formed in 1996, Sole Technology is owned by founder and CEO Pierre Senizergues.

From its headquarters in Lake Forest, California, Sole Technology maintains regional offices in New York, China, the Netherlands, Switzerland, the UK, and Spain. Its European operations are based in Amsterdam. The company boasts a showroom in New York City and a distribution facility in California from which Sole Technology serves specialty retailers worldwide in more than 70 countries.

The company reports annual revenue approaching $200 million.

To serve its target audience and cater to the growing endurance requirements of action sports, the company operates the Sole Technology Institute. The biomechanics research organization is the first of its kind to focus exclusively on skateboarding. Through the institute, it tests and invents new technologies. Sole Technology aims to develop the highest performing action sports footwear in the world.

Through an exclusive partnership with Chinese retailer I.T Limited, Sole Technology has extended its reach into mainland China, Macau, and Hong Kong. Beginning in 2012, I.T Limited sells the company's etnies, Emerica, and Altamont products through its stores, which operate under the double-park banner. Sole Technology will see its footprint expand further as I.T opens new double-park retail stores in China.
