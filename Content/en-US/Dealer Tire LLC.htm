Dealer Tire tirelessly makes deals to meet the tire needs of America's car dealers. The family owned company is the only US tire dealer focused exclusively on delivering tires and accessories direct from OEMs to auto dealers nationwide. Dealer Tire is a factory-authorized tire supplier to dealerships, primarily those of leading German and Japanese automakers. It assists OEMs with their tire programs and builds replacement tire and customer satisfaction strategies at its car dealerships. Tire brands distributed by the company include such notable names as BF Goodrich, Continental , Dunlop , Goodyear , Michelin , Pirelli , and Yokohama .

The company manages retail tire programs for 31 auto makers' retail sales networks, covering thousands of installation locations. Its programs help automotive dealers and OEMs to sell tires, accessories, services, and parts. It supplies tires to more than 10,000 dealers affiliated with a range of automakers, including Audi , BMW , Ford , Infiniti, Jaguar , Mazda , Mercedes-Benz , Volkswagen , and Volvo .

Based in Cleveland, Dealer Tire operates 43 warehouses and distribution centers across 20 US states including in Atlanta; Baltimore; Boston; Charlotte, North Carolina; Chicago; Cleveland; Dallas; Denver; Elmsford, New York; Hayward, California; Houston; Irwindale, California; Kansas City, Missouri; Lyndhurst, New Jersey; Miami; Minneapolis; Phoenix; Philadelphia; Seattle; and Tampa.

Dealer Tire sells its products through dealers, and as well as online (through RightTurn.com). As a channel of advertising RightTurn.com, the company uses "tire smart" campaign using local television and radio throughout the targeted region.

Dealer Tire is growing and adding additional people to its workforce. In mid-2016, it announced it will hire at least 100 more local employees over the next three years, given that the move is being funded in part by public money. This includes $8 million in financing, mostly loans, from the city and Cuyahoga County in Ohio, undisclosed incentives from JobsOhio, and more than $11 million in federal and state historic preservation tax credits.

Dealer Tire also embraces technology as a means for growth. It has launched an online tire-buying service in northeast Ohio under the name "RightTurn.com" that directs users to nearby car dealers. The online system uses a step-by-step menu to recommend tires based on a person's vehicle make and model (and lifestyle). The company is experimenting with other technologies that could help dealers compete and is focused on how to harness data from cars. It also aims to help dealers get a leg-up as electric cars, and even autonomous cars, become more prevalent within the industry.

Dealer Tire is led by its founder's grandsons, CEO Scott Mueller and president Dean Mueller. The company traces its origins to Cleveland, Ohio, where it was founded in 1918 by Walter J. Mueller.
