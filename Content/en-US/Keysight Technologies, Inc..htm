Keysight Technologies maintains keen insight when it comes to measurement. The company provides electronic measurement instruments and systems and related software and software design tools used to make electronics equipment. It provides related services in the realm of start-up assistance, instrument productivity, and application services in addition to instrument calibration and repair. Keysight also offers consulting services throughout the product life cycle. It has a client base of 12,000 customers and targets the communications and electronics sectors. The company was spun off from testing equipment maker Agilent Technologies in late 2014.

The company operates through three segments: Communications Solutions Group (CSG; 60% of net sales), Electronic Industrial Solutions Group (EISG; 25%), and Services Solutions Group (SSG; 15%).

CSG targets customers within the worldwide commercial communications sphere, which includes internet infrastructure, aerospace, defense and government end market. EISG provides test and measurement products across an array of electronic industrial end markets, and SSG provides repair, calibration and consulting services and remarkets used Keysight equipment.

The company's research and development and manufacturing sites reside in California and Colorado. Outside the US, it has locations in China, Germany, India, Japan, Malaysia, Singapore, and Spain. Roughly 35% of Keysight's net revenues come from the US. Other major markets include China (20%) and Japan (almost 10%).

The company serves customers in more than 100 countries through a direct sales force, distributors, resellers, manufacturer's representatives, telesales, and electronic commerce.

Keysight's revenues have fluctuated over the years. After declining in 2015, revenues increased 2% to $2.9 billion in 2016. The growth for 2016 was associated with additional revenue from acquisitions coupled with data center expansion.

Profits have also oscillated over the years. After rising in 2015, profits dipped 35% to $335 million in 2016. This was largely due to the absence of income tax benefits it incurred the previous year. In addition, cash flow from operations has followed this same up and down pattern, rising to $416 million in 2016 after falling the previous year.

One of the ways Keysight achieves revenue growth is through the use of acquisitions. In early 2017, the company agreed to buy Ixia , a California-based data technology firm that makes equipment that monitors and tests networks, for $1.6 billion. The combination will create a technology firm with expertise in electronic design, device and network measurement, and application and security performance.

In mid-2015, the company picked up Anite plc, a UK-based supplier of software products for wireless research and development, for around $600 million. The acquisition enabled the company to offer a broader portfolio of products throughout the wireless research and development cycle.
