Bellying up to a PowerBar won't slake your thirst, but it might boost your energy. A leader in the world of high-carbohydrate snacks, PowerBar originally designed its energy bars for athletes. However, the company's products are available in a variety of flavors and are just as likely to be pulled out of purses and desk drawers and eaten as meal replacements. PowerBar also markets sport drinks called Endurance and Ironman, a protein powder drink mix under the ProteinPlus name, and a carbohydrate gel that comes in a variety of flavors. The company, a unit of Nestlé 's performance nutrition segment, sells its products in supermarkets and at health-food and sports retailers worldwide in more than 30 markets.

Based in California, PowerBar sells its sports nutrition products worldwide in the US, Canada, Europe, and Australia.

The company boasts an assortment of sponsorship and partnership agreements with non-profit organizations and with major sports events, including the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) and the ESPN X-Games. It also sponsors top endurance events, such as the Ironman, Tour de France, and the Boston, New York City, and Berlin marathons.

PowerBar's owner is looking to expand its business of selling nutrition products for athletes. The move means extending the reach of the PowerBar business or making a complementary acquisition.

Nestlé's Nutrition and HealthCare division posted 10% revenue gains in fiscal 2012 as compared to 2011. PowerBar helped to generate about $200 million of its segment's sales.
