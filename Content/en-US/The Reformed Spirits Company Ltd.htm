Martin Miller's Gin is distilled by obsessive gin makers at The Reformed Spirits Company for obsessive gin connoisseurs around the world. The premium gin, which has won many awards for its quality and taste, is distilled in a century-old copper still outside of London, then shipped to Iceland where it's blended with spring water. Reformed Spirits has given close attention to packaging and public relations to establish Martin Miller's as a top premium brand. The company exports to Asia, Australia, Ireland, Russia, and the US. Reformed Spirits was founded in 1998 by an entrepreneur, the late Martin Miller, along with friends David Bromige and Andreas Versteegh.

The company makes two types of gin: Super Premium (80 proof) and Westbourne (90 proof).

Since the company was first established, the company's gin has made its mark in such markets as Malta and Miami. The hotel business has also been an important mark for Martin Miller's gin. The company exports to Asian markets such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and China; Shanghai has been a major force for the company. The company continues its quest in Asia and has set its sights on India as a major market.

Reformed Spirits launched Martin Miller's original Super Premium gin in 1999, and the Westbourne variant in 2002.
