Prosensa is taking a proactive approach to treating muscular dystrophy. The company is developing biotherapeutics to treat three rare neuromuscular and neurodegenerative disorders -- Duchenne muscular dystrophy (DMD), myotonic dystrophy, and Huntington's disease. Its main drug candidate, drisapersen, is being developed with GlaxoSmithKline to treat DMD. Currently, there is no disease-modifying therapy on the market for DMD, a muscle-wasting disease caused by a lack of a protein needed to build and maintain muscles. If approved, drisapersen would be the first RNA-based therapeutic for DMD. Prosensa went public in the US in mid-2013, raising $65 million to further fund drisapersen and other drug stage candidates.
