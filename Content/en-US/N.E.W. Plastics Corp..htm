N.E.W. Plastics is into both new and used plastics. The company produces custom blow-molded products that include stock bottles, cylinders, ovals, handleware, and jars. Its VBS Total Packaging unit distributes packaging supplies and labels. N.E.W. Plastics serves customers in the food, chemical, personal care, agricultural, and pharmaceutical industries. The company's RENEW Plastics division produces recycled plastic lumber for ramps, walkways, decking, pallets, tables, and other applications. N.E.W. Plastics, which has the manufacturing capacity to convert some 25 million pounds of discarded plastic annually, was founded in 1968 by Irvin and Nancy Vincent.

N.E.W. Plastics has three distinct business units: Custom Blow Molding (injection and extrusion blow-molding); VBS Total Packaging LLC (distributor/packaging support and supplies); and RENEW Plastics (extruded plastic lumber and components).

The company converts more than 25 million pounds discarded plastic into environmentally friendly products each year.

N.E.W. Plastics in 2016 expanded its upper Midwest base through the acquisition of Fulcrum, a custom blow molder. Fulcrum is based in St. Paul, Minnesota, and manufactures products ranging in size from 10 cubic centimeters to 2.5 gallons. Fulcrum serves the agricultural chemical, oil, lubricant, household chemical, industrial chemical, personal care, and pet care segments.

The custom plastics manufacturer and blow molder was established in 1968 by Irvin and Nancy Vincent, parents of N.E.W. Plastics' president Vern Vincent.
