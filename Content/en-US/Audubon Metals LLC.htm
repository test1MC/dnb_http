What is scrap to some is a precious metal to Audubon Metals. The company recycles residue from shredded automobiles and smelts the aluminum for use by die casters throughout the Midwest. Once it receives the shredded automobile, Audubon Metals processes, dries, melts, alloys, and delivers the new aluminum alloy castings to the customer. The company produces 400 million pounds of aluminum alloy per year. By-products such as brass and copper are also recovered from the automobile scrap. Other products handled by Audubon include zinc and magnesium. The company is owned by Koch Enterprises .

The company processes automobile shredder residue and produces secondary aluminum alloy castings for die casters. It offers various metallic products, including copper, zinc heavies, brass, and magnesium for resale.

Audubon Metals first began operating in 1996.
