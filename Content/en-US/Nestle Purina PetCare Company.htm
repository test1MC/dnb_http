When kitty meows for his chow, Nestlé Purina PetCare listens. The subsidiary of Swiss food giant Nestlé S.A. is the world's largest pet food producer. Its portfolio of some 30 brands includes Alpo, Beneful, Cat Chow, Dog Chow, Fancy Feast, Friskies, Mighty Dog, Pro Plan, and Purina ONE. It also makes cat and dog litter products under the Tidy Cats, Yesterday's News, and secondnature names. Purina products are sold internationally by mass merchandisers, supermarkets, pet supply stores, and online retailers, including Wal-Mart , Target , PetSmart , Hy-Vee , and Amazon.com . Besides pet supplies, the company offers health insurance for dogs and cats through its PurinaCare Insurance Services subsidiary.

Based in St. Louis, Missouri, Purina operates its business nationwide and in Canada. It sells its products worldwide.

Purina counts two dozen manufacturing plants among its operations. Purina has litter plants, dry plants, and wet plants. US facilities that make litter are located in California, Virginia, and Missouri. The company maintains two litter plants in Canada. Dry plants are located in the US in Arizona, Oklahoma, Georgia, Denver, Iowa, Wisconsin, Ohio, New York, Pennsylvania, and a pair in Canada. Its wet plants run out of Nebraska, Missouri, Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, and Iowa. The pet food maker is also supported by more than 12 sales offices in California, Arizona, Texas, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, North Carolina, Kansas, Connecticut, Michigan, and Minnesota.

The company works to capture an increasing share of the pet food market while boosting its sources of revenue. To this end, Purina focuses on product research and development. It has followed the wave of companies that market functional foods -- in its case, foods with health-promoting or wellness-maintaining properties for pets. The company launched Purina ONE beyOnd, a natural food with real meat, fruits, and vegetables, for adult dogs and cats in 2011, and Purina ONE SmartBlend, a nutrient-rich blend to satisfy more active pets, in 2010. Other product developments include Chef Michael's (2009), a super-premium food for small dogs, and Breeze litter box (2008) for cats. Produced under the Tidy Cats name, the litter box features a rack lined with non-absorbent pellets to separate solid waste from liquids, which collect in an odor-controlling pad below.

Operationally, Purina is trimming its manufacturing expenses. The company sold its steel can-making assets to packaging giant Silgan Holdings for $25 million. The 2011 deal, which included a long-term agreement to supply Purina with cans for its products, followed a pilot program between Silgan and the pet food producer.

As part of its business, Purina offers insurance coverage for pet-related accidents and illnesses. Domestic animals have become an integral part of many families and their proper care can cost their owners more than they can spend at any given time. To this end, Purina has added insurance coverage as an extension of its brand and as another revenue vehicle. The company's PurinaCare Insurance Services is available to dog and cat owners in most US states, and is offered as a benefit by a growing list of employers and member organizations. Coverage is underwritten by Central States Indemnity Co. of Omaha, a subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway .

Purina has agreed to buy Petfinder, an online pet adoption website from Discovery Communications LLC . The deal is expected to close in July 2013. The purchase will allow Purina to broaden support for pet welfare organizations and strengthen its role as a leading provider of online pet-related information. It also positions Purina further as an advocate for pet well-being and adds yet another avenue for potential sales.

The pet products company sells its products online and in stores internationally.

Awards and Recognition

Purina has been recognized by the St. Louis Business Journal as the "Best Place to Work" for six consecutive years. It was also recognized in 2012 as the best place to work in St. Louis by the St. Louis Dispatch and the previous year Glassdoor.com named Purina the company with the best work-life balance.

Formerly named Ralston Purina, the company has roots reaching back to the 1890s. It was acquired by Nestlé in 2001.
