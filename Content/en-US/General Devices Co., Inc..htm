Left to its own devices, General Devices wants to let things slide, and enclose them, if necessary. The company makes telescoping slides, commercial- and military-grade cabinets and enclosures, and enclosure hardware and accessories for computer equipment. It also offers sheet metal fabrication services such as stamping, welding, and finishing. The company's three facilities include more than 160,000 sq. ft. of manufacturing, research and development, and office space. General Devices, which was founded by Herbert and Gladys Fall in 1953, is owned and operated by the Fall family.

The company offers its products in the US and internationally in Canada, Mexico, Europe (primarily the UK), Israel, Japan, and South Korea.

General Devices' domestic distributors have included such noteworthy names as Austin Hardware & Supply, Brevan Electronics, and Carlton-Bates Company. International distributors include Irwin Industrial, Miraesang Engineering Co., and Sliding Systems.
