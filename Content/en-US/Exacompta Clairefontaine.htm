A paper maker grows in France? Exactly, Exacompta Clairefontaine produces a diversified lineup of printing and writing papers, and paper goods. It churns out art books, calendars, copy paper, diaries, envelopes, office folders, labels, ledgers, school memo pads, and notebooks. Flagship brands include Clairefontaine and Exacompta. Products are also marketed under Exatime, Mignon, and Quo Vadis brands for diaries, and Clairalfa, DCP, and Trophée for office papers. In addition to France, Exacompta Clairefontaine operates in Africa, Europe, Japan, the Middle East, and US. Established under Jean-Baptiste Bichelberger in 1858, the company is controlled by Ets Charles Nusse, the family of the founder's great grandson.
