Kavlico manufactures sensors for pressure, position, force, level, tilt, and other specialty applications. Its products are designed for use in a wide range of applications, including passenger cars, heavy equipment, cargo loading systems, medical instruments, power generation, airplanes, satellites, and missile guidance systems. Other markets include wastewater monitoring and emission control. Kavlico is a subsidiary of Custom Sensors & Technologies and a global business unit of Schneider Electric . Kavlico was founded in 1958 by Norwegian immigrant Fred Kavli. It has its main locations in the US and Germany, and a network of sales engineers across Europe.

Kavlico has manufacturing sites in Germany, Mexico, and China; design, development, and customization centers in Germany and North America, and sales engineering offices in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Transportation applications include sensors that measure air, fluids (oil, coolant, hydraulic, and brake), and temperature for passenger cars, heavy-duty trucks and buses, motorcycles, and for vehicles used in mining, construction, agriculture, rail, marine, and recreation. Sensors are used to ensure occupant comfort, vehicle safety, and filtration of particulate matter. Kavlico also launched a line of liquefied petroleum gas and compressed natural gas pressure sensors for vehicles that use alternative fuels.

Industrial applications include irrigation control, pipeline monitoring, HVAC compressors, refrigeration pressure, power generation, and hydraulic systems for heavy equipment. Medical pressure sensors are used in respirators, ventilators, surgical equipment, and medical beds. The company also has a line of sealed stainless steel industrial pressure sensors for harsh environments, which utilizes Kavlico's oil-filled piezoresistive (a change in the electrical resistivity of a semiconductor or metal when mechanical strain is applied) sensing technology.

Kavlico manufactures all-systems sensors and cockpit control devices for the aerospace and defense market. Products include thin-film force sensors for rudder controls, ailerons, and flight data recorders, as well as landing gear, speed brakes, thrust control, steering, and auto throttles. The sensors are used in commercial aviation, helicopters, spacecraft and missiles, fighters and reconnaissance aircraft, bombers and attack aircraft, and other special-purpose aircraft. Customers have included such big names as Boeing , Sikorsky , Lockheed , and the US government.

The company offers multiple pressure-sensing technologies including ceramic capacitive, piezoresistive, silicon capacitive, and thin-Film along with an extensive network of development and support functions to design, develop, validate, and make pressure sensors for the most demanding applications.

Kavlico targets the transportation, building equipment, energy and infrastructure, food and beverage, medical, and the aerospace and defense markets.

The company's customers included Airbus , Bombardier , Embraer , ARJ 2, 1MRJ and Sukhoi.

The company's growth is driven by manufacturing custom products for OEM applications and by maintaining long-term relationships with OEM customers, while making continuous improvements to reduce cost and increase efficiency of its products. Kavlico's sensors serve three distinct markets: transportation, industrial, and aerospace and defense.

In 2014 Kavlico Position & Force Sensors introduced a new industrial Linear Variable Differential Transducers product range. In 2013 it launched the PTE5000 family of modular sensors designed for industrial applications.
