US aerospace and defense giant Lockheed Martin conducts all of its UK operations through Lockheed Martin UK Holdings, Limited (LMUK). The aforementioned operations include military fighters and transport planes, and systems for air traffic management, missiles and fire control, transportation, and command and control. UK programs and projects include work on the F-35 Lightning II (also known as the Joint Strike Fighter), the Javelin Light Forces Anti Tank Guided Weapon System, Royal Navy Merlin Helicopter, Warship Electronic Chart Display and Information System (WECDIS), and the Battlegroup Command and Control Trainer (BC2T).

The company has 20 locations in the UK.

LMUK engages in contracting and integrating systems for major defense and civilian equipment and programs. It also offers combined arms tactical trainers, multi-launch rocket systems, counter battery radars, submarines, and the longbow integrated fire control radars and missile systems for attack helicopters.

The company serves defense, government, civil, and commercial customers.

In 2015, NATS , the UK-based air traffic control company, and LMUK signed a strategic partnership that strengthens their existing supplier relationship and underlines their commitment to further enable improved efficiency, safety and passenger experience across airports and air traffic systems worldwide. The partnership pools the companies' skills, technologies, and resources in deploying Single European Sky ATM Research (SESAR) technologies over the next five years and deliver new innovative products and services. (SESAR is a collaborative project aimed at radically overhauling European airspace and air traffic management).

In March 2012 LMUK inked a deal to provide cloud IT services, including integration, security, energy optimization, and virtualization consulting, for the UK government. Known as CloudStore, the agreement was set to last for six to nine months, with the option of becoming a rolling contract. The deal followed another LMUK IT initiative -- the opening of its first cyber security intelligence center.
