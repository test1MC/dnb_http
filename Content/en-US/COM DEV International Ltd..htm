COM DEV's products are found in space and at Honeywell International (in 2016). Com Dev designs and manufactures a variety of space hardware subsystems, including microwave and optical components for use in communications, space science, remote sensing, and military satellites. COM DEV's equipment and instruments enable telecommunications services, geographic mapping, and pollution monitoring. The company's clients includes major aerospace and communications companies such as Boeing , Airbus Astrium, Lockheed Martin , Orbital Sciences , Space Systems/Loral , and Thales Alenia Space. In 2016 Com Dev was bought by Honeywell , while Com Dev's exactEarth business was spun off as an independent public company.

With the $455 million acquisition, Com Dev becomes a part of Honeywell's Defense and Space business. Com Dev brings a portfolio that complements Honeywell's space business with radio frequency and microwave engineering products and expertise.

In association with the Com Dev-Honeywell transaction, exactEarth, Com Dev's subsidiary, becomes an independent public company. exactEarth makes Automatic Identification System (AIS) vessel tracking available to the maritime industry.

Com Dev is headquartered in Cambridge, Ontario, where it also has manufacturing operations. The company has facilities in Canada, China, India, the UK, and the US. It has other manufacturing facilities in California and the UK.

While Com Dev sells products to aerospace contractors, the end users usually are national space agencies such as NASA in the US, the Canadian Space Agency, and the European Space Agency .
