PNY Technologies has a herd of ponies for almost any memory ride. The company makes high-speed flash memory cards, USB flash drives, solid-state drives, and computer memory upgrade modules. It also offers HDMI cables, as well as PC graphics cards that combine NVIDIA 's graphics chips with its own memory devices. PNY focuses on selling to the business, photography, gaming, and wireless sectors. The company sells its products directly and through retail stores like Best Buy , Staples , and Wal-Mart . It also sells through online distributors including Newegg , Amazon.com , and TigerDirect.com.

PNY sells products in more than 50 countries. It has nearly a dozen offices in China, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Taiwan, the UK, and the US.

PNY products are available in over 50 countries directly through online stores or at various retailers, wholesalers, and distributors, including Wal-Mart, Meijer , Sears , Target , Best Buy, Avnet , and Tech Data . The company caters to a wide array of industries and applications, including health care, automotive, defense, government, financial, computation, cloud storage, networking, advanced mobile, tablet, and consumer electronics.

PNY's strategy for growth involves maintaining its market share in its largest international retailers: Best Buy, Carrefour , Fnac , Media Markt, Dixons, Staples, and Wal-Mart. PNY is also focused on increasing its brand awareness and sales in key emerging markets such as Latin America, Scandinavia, Poland, Russia, and Turkey.

The company maintains a steady pace of product releases to keep with changes in memory technology. In 2016, the company announced a new group of high-performance solid-state drives (SSDs) designed to optimize the performance of both laptops and desktop systems. That followed its 2015 release of its fastest flash memory card line, PRO Elite.

The company was founded in Brooklyn, New York, as PNY Electronics in 1985. It originally bought and sold memory chips. The company changed its name to PNY Technologies in 1997 to reflect its expansion into producing new types of memory and related products.
