Elizabeth Shaw won't mind if you eat that after-dinner mint before dinner. Elizabeth Shaw Ltd. manufactures premium liquor and mint-laced chocolates. Its product line includes truffles and liqueur-filled, as well as its best known -- chocolate mint crisps -- all sold under the Elizabeth Shaw brand. The chocolate confectionery maker sources a very specific blend of cocoa from the Ivory Coast. The company's boxed confections are sold in supermarkets in the UK and exported widely to other countries. The company is owned by Icelandic confectionery company Nói Síríus.

Elizabeth Shaw is based in Bristol and sells its product in stores in the UK, Germany, Ireland, and Sweden.

Elizabeth Shaw products are sold in the following stores across the UK: Tesco , Sainsbury's , Asda , Morrisons , Waitrose , Lidl , Wilkinson, and Booths , as well as in other independent stores.

The company's products are also available from major wholesale companies, including Booker , Bestway/Batleys, Palmer & Harvey , 3663, Brakes, Hancocks Cash & Carry, Makro , and other buying groups.

To even out the seasonality of its sales, the company has reintroduced Easter-themed products and hopes to lure younger consumers with vodka-filled chocolates.

In 1937, a woman with the first name of Elizabeth put her delicious Mint Crisp chocolates on sale in her brother-in-law's shop. The venture was a great success, so she combined her name with part of her husband's old employer's (confectionery maker Page & Shaw) to create the Elizabeth Shaw brand.

In 1968, the company was sold to a Bristol factory steeped in chocolate history and innovation, that was formed by former Fry & Son's employee Edward Packer in 1881.
