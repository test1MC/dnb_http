Whether you call it cacao or chocolate, Natra will make sure you get enough of it. the company makes chocolate in powder, liquid, tablets and solid bars forms, and produces countlines (a chocolate-based bar), spreads, tablets, chocolates, and Belgium specialties. It sells its products in 60 countries across five continents. With production plants in Spain, Belgium, France, and Canada, Natra produces private-label finished confections for other European companies. Customers include food makers and confectioners. The company holds a majority stake in Reig Jofre , which extracts caffeine and other ingredients from fruits and vegetables for use in food, drugs, and cosmetics.

Natra operates two main businesses: Consumer Products and Industrial Products.

The Consumer Products division, which brings in some 70% of revenue, manufactures chocolate tablets, countlines, spreads and Belgian chocolate, for commercialization mainly in Europe. Germany, France, Belgium, Holland, Spain and the UK are the main markets.

The Industrial division (30% of revenue) makes cocoa-derived products, mainly cocoa powder and butter and chocolate coatings, for the international food industry.

It also has a controlling stake in Natraceutical, which commercializes natural nutritional supplements in Europe.

Natra has six production plants in Spain, Belgium, France, and Canada. It also has an expansive commercial network in Europe, the US, Canada and Asia. The EMEA region (Europe, Middle East and Africa) accounts for some 83% of revenue, while sales from the US account for around 12%. The Asia-Pacific region accounts for the remainder.
