AstraZeneca France does its bit to help its parent, global pharmaceutical powerhouse AstraZeneca , fight diseases from A to Z. In addition to its headquarters at Reuil Malmaison, the company operates an R&D facility for cancer treatments and packaging in Reims. The plant, which produces cancer as well as cardiovascular drugs, sends about a third of its product to Italy, the rest to North Africa and Portugal. The company's Dunkirk production plant makes Pulmicort, Symbicort, Mopral, and INexium in its chemical unit, while the pharmaceutical unit makes Bricanyl and Turbuhaler. Dunkirk ships its finished products to the US. In all the company makes and ships about 40 drugs.

AstraZeneca France has three main locations-- Rueil-Malmaison, Reims, and Dunkirk.

The company focuses on six major therapeutic areas -- cardiology, cancer, gastroenterology, infection, neuroscience and respiratory diseases, and has more than 1,000 active collaborations with external partners.

The company accounts for about 5% of parent AstraZeneca 's total revenues.

In 2014 AstraZeneca signed a strategic agreement with Spain's Almirall to help fight respiratory diseases. AstraZeneca France also signed a deal that year with France-based Gustave Roussy, a leading European cancer treatment center, to develop oncology treatment drugs.

AstraZeneca France was formed in 1975.
