As part of Tata Steel Europe , Cogent Power has a clear and logical steel production strategy. The joint venture, owned 75% by Tata Europe and 25% by SSAB Svenskt Stål , produces and supplies electro-technical materials and products. Electrical steels, having magnetic properties, are used for generating, transmitting, and distributing power. Cogent specializes in grain-oriented steels, 3% silicon-iron alloys that provide lower power loss and are used for power transformers. Its non-oriented steels are used for motors, generators, alternators, and other electromagnet applications. The venture has plants in South Wales and Sweden and a North American subsidiary, Cogent Power Inc.

Cogent Power is part of the specialty businesses of Tata Steel Europe . It is also the largest independent transformer core manufacturer in North America.

The company sells its steel products via distributors and dealers.

In fiscal 2015, Cogent Power's net revenues were £696,000, remaining flat compared to 2014.

Net loss was £29,000, a decrease of £35,000 compared to 2014, due to higher administrative expenses.

In Europe, the company is looking to innovation to secure its long term future. In 2015 it launched 35 new products and piloted an innovative new iron-making technology to improve resource efficiency. It has more than 110 new products commercially available, including SERICA (a premium hot dip galvanized surface finish product for the automotive sector) and Celsius 420 (a range of hot finished hollow structural sections for use in challenging building projects).

As part of a consolidation plan, the parent company announced plans in 2015 plans to transfer its UK long products operations into a separate, wholly-owned subsidiary of Tata Steel .
