Brigade Electronics is a leading UK manufacturer of products that make backing (or going in reverse) for commercial or construction vehicles safer. The company makes the all-too-familiar backing alarm that emits a loud, repetitive beeping sound, but also offers quieter backing alarms that are only audible behind the vehicle. The company's assortment of camera systems lets drivers see what is behind them through a closed-circuit TV screen. Brigade's cameras, proximity sensors, reversing alarms, and recorders are used on everything from heavy trucks (lorries) and delivery vans to excavators and dump trucks.

The company offers a range of products to reduce the risk of collision and maximize efficiency, including camera monitor systems, reversing alarms, ultrasonic proximity sensors, digital recorders, and thermal image cameras.

With about 20 regional sites, the company's subsidiaries are located in Germany, Italy, and the US, whereas its joint ventures are located in France, Belgium, the Netherlands, Luxembourg, and South Africa.

Brigade markets its products through a network of 150 international distributors, subsidiaries, and partners worldwide.

Brigade has a substantial growth opportunity now that many regulations require vehicles to have rear-view cameras. The company has achieved R46 compliance for numerous camera monitor systems, including the Backeye 360 Select system, all of which can be used in place of a conventional mirror for the Class V and Class VI areas.

Brigade was founded in 1976 by Christopher Hanson-Abbott.
