Sauder Woodworking takes the fear out of furniture and makes furniture for the God-fearing. The firm is a top US maker of ready-to-assemble (RTA) furniture and, through Sauder Manufacturing , is a leading manufacturer of church furniture and institutional seating. Its RTA products include such items as computer workstations, desks, entertainment centers, and wardrobes. They're sold under brand names Sauder, Beginnings, and Studio Edge. The company's products are sold through retailers in North America and abroad. Progressive Furniture , a subsidiary, makes traditional fully-assembled furniture. The company was founded in 1934 by Erie Sauder and is still family-owned and -operated.

Based in Ohio, Sauder operates across North America and in Asia.

About 90% of Sauder's furniture is made in Archbold, Ohio.

Sauder counts several subsidiaries among its operations. Progressive Furniture designs and imports wood bedroom, dining room, and accent furniture. Sauder Manufacturing Company supplies seating for the worship, educational, and health care markets. Sauder Funeral Products makes and markets a line of wood finish caskets and other funeral industry products. WoodTrac, which caters to the residential and commercial markets, produces a line of wood finish ceilings and closet systems.

The company has grown to become North America's leading producer of ready-to-assemble (RTA) furniture and the fifth largest residential furniture maker in the nation. Established in 2007, the company's Sauder Woodworking C. Dongguan Representative Office facilitates and oversees Sauder's sourcing activities. Known as Sauder Asia, the unit supports Progressive Furniture 's presence in Asia.

The furniture manufacturer supplies thousands of retail outlets located in the US and Canada. Sauder makes and sells residential and light commercial furniture under its own brands and private-label brands for others. It markets more than 30 distinct furniture collections of RTA furnishings for entertainment, home office, bedroom, kitchen, and storage under brand names Sauder, Beginnings, and Studio Edge. Its products are sold at office supply chains ( Staples , OfficeMax , Office Depot ), and mass merchants ( Wal-Mart , Target , Best Buy , Lowes ).

In 2014 Sauder launched an updated brand image that features a modern, contemporary logo, stylish packaging, redesigned digital platforms, and an updated website. For its salesforce, Sauder implemented Accellion 's mobile productivity tools, which allows its sales team to share, access, and collaborate on documents through iPads. The move offers more flexibility for those on the road or on the tradeshow floor.

To capture other markets, Sauder started manufacturing caskets and wood-grained ceilings. It also makes kitchen cabinets that are sold in North America by Swedish retailer IKEA .

To showcase its new and existing products, as well its Progressive Furniture products and expanded RTA categories, Sauder in 2013 extended the reach of its High Point, North Carolina, showroom by 5,500-sq.-ft. Now measuring nearly 30,000-sq.-ft., the showroom provides more room for Progressive Furniture to expand its accent and dining displays, while still giving ample room to host dealer traffic and meetings.

In 2012 Sauder Funeral Products moved its funeral products manufacturing division to a larger 107,000-sq.-ft. facility on its Archbold, Ohio, campus. The move was spurred by strong casket sales and expanded product distribution.

Sauder generates sales of nearly $500 million annually.
