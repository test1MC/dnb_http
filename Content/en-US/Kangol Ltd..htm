Dressing a woman (or a man) head to toe is a corporate affair. Kangol Limited licenses its signature hats, bags, and accessories (gloves, scarves, and boots) to be sold in North America, Europe, and Asia/Pacific. The company also extends the brand to some apparel (polo shirts, jackets, sweat pants, and hoodies). In addition to nearly 10 stand-alone stores in China, Japan, and South Korea, Kangol sells its designs through specialty and department stores, Selfridges , Nordstrom , and Harrods , and online. the brand enjoys increasing popularity with the hip-hop music community. The Kangol brand is owned by sporting goods retailer Sports Direct International (SDI).

Kangol manufactures hats and accessories for men and women.

The company has stores in New York, New York; Shenzhen, China; and Tokyo, Japan.

Kangol distributes its products through distributors in Armenia, Australia, Austria, Bahamas, Baltic States, and Brazil.

Among its licensing agreements, Kangol teamed up with headphone company Aerial7 to offer hats with built-in headphones. Kangol entered the apparel market by partnering with Los Angeles-based Apparel Limited to design, produce, and distribute contemporary sportswear for men and women. The lines debuted in upscale specialty and department stores like Saks Fifth Avenue , Barneys New York , and Fred Segal.

SDI owns the worldwide rights to the Kangol brand, except in Japan, where it only owns the rights in relation to headwear.

It purchased the brand from private equity firm August Equity Trust for about Â£12 million (about $20 million) in 2006.

The Kangol name is actually an acronym: "K" for knitted, "a-n-g" from angora, and "o-l" from wool. Its products are easily identified by its kangaroo logo.

Founded in 1938 to make military berets in England, Kangol is a former subsidiary of The Marmon Group.
