The Terlato Wine Group embraces the grape from vine to wine. Owned and run by the Terlato family, the company operates both vineyards and wineries and is involved in the import, export, sales, and marketing of premium ($20 and up) wines. Several of its vineyards are located in the celebrated California wine regions: Napa Valley, Sonoma, and Santa Barbara. Flagship division Terlato Wines International markets more than 70 foreign and domestic brands of wine (including Japanese sak√©) in the US through a direct sales force and network of some 135 distributors. Customers include more than 9,000 fine restaurants and wine retailers. Terlato also has global joint venture wine operations in Australia and Italy.

Based in Illinois, Terlato sells its wines in the US in California and Oregon, as well as in Argentina, Australia, Austria, Canada, Chile, England, France, Greece, Italy, Japan, New Zealand, Panama, Scotland, South Africa, and Spain.

The company's operations consist of a variety of businesses, including vineyard ownership, winery ownership and management, wine import and export, and wine marketing and sales. Its companies include Terlato Wines International, a Chicago-based full-service wine sales and marketing unit, and joint ventures Domaine Terlato & Chapoutier, Australia (with Michel Chapoutier); Glass Mountain California (with Markham Vineyards); MAZZONI, Montalcino, Italy (with Il Poggione).

The company's portfolio consists of several names, such as Alderbrook, Chimney Rock, EPISODE, GALAXY, Luke Donald Collection, Quickfire, Rutherford Hill, Terlato Family Vineyards in Napa Valley and Sonoma County, Sanford in Santa Barbara, Tangley Oaks in Napa and Santa Barbara County, and Seven Daughters Winemaker's Blend in California.

Terlato markets its products to wine shops, retailers, and restaurants globally.

The company's growth is fueled by expansion of its portfolio of international name brands. Labels are further separated from the herd of high-end competitors by efforts to target luxury consumer niches in the US. Terlato boasts a 20% market share in wine, retailing more $20 a bottle, according to Nielsen .

Terlato works to extend its geographic reach through partnerships in target markets. In 2013, for example, the firm entered long-term partnership agreements with Langley's No. 8 Gin and Tigre Blanc Vodka through which it launched a new Artisan Spirits portfolio of gin, vodka, Scotch, Cognac, tequila, and rum, among others. In 2014, Terlato began producing and marketing luxury American craft spirits through a partnership with Indiana's Heartland Distillers and began importing wines from Northern Italy's Anselmi and Rioja's Bodegas Valdemar.
