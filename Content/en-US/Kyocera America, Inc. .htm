Kyocera America (KAI) believes that great things are housed in small packages. KAI is the semiconductor and optoelectronic components and packaging services division of Kyocera International . The company produces ceramic packages that house semiconductors and other micro and optoelectronics, such as microwave and radio-frequency devices. Its products are used in a broad range of applications, including personal computers and workstations, as well as handsets, base stations, and other wireless telecommunications equipment. KAI's services include package design, assembly, and testing. It also provides nickel, gold, copper, and tin-lead plating services.

Other applications for the ceramic packaged microprocessors include automotive and consumer products; they are also used in satellites and in aerospace applications, including probes. Biotech uses of KAI's products include human body implants (pacemakers and defibrillators). KAI's assembly services offer semiconductor interconnectivity such as flip chip, wire bond, and plating, used to connect various semiconductor and related components.

Kyocera America operates a maquiladora assembly plant in Tijuana, Mexico (Kyocera Mexicana). A maquiladora is a particular manufacturing arrangement that allows US firms and other foreign companies to import raw materials into Mexico duty-free for assembly there by lower-wage Mexican workers.
