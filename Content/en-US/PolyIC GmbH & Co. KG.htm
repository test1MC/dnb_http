PolyIC longs to usher in the days of polymer ICs. The company develops technology for making integrated circuits (ICs) that are printed on flexible polymer films rather than etched into traditional rigid wafers of silicon. Flexible microchips stand to have many applications, for example in radio-frequency identification (RFID) chips used to track goods in transit. If PolyIC meets its stated goal, it will develop equipment that makes it as easy to print semiconductors as it is to print newspapers today. The company was formed late in 2003 as a joint venture between industrial titan Siemens and printing and stamping specialty company LEONHARD KURZ.
