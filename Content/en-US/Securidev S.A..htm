Sécuridev has its market locked in. It makes and sells old-fashioned metal locks, as well as new-fangled electronic-access systems. Its products, which the company wholesales and retails, are used in everything from cars to vending machines to computers. Brand names include Dény, Titan, Picard, Métalux, Fontaine, and Ronis. The company's subsidiaries outside France make metal fasteners and cylinder-lock inserts. Its UK-based Ronis unit specializes in fashioning locks for wooden and steel office furniture. Sécuridev chairman Henri Morel is also director of financial consortium SFPI (Société Financière de Participation Industrielle), which holds a 57% stake in Sécuridev.
