Fisher Controls International has control issues, all around the world. The subsidiary of Emerson Electric 's Process Management segment manufactures control valves and regulators for the chemical, food and beverage, oil and gas, life sciences, liquefied natural gas (LNG), power, pulp and paper, refining, and water and wastewater industries. Fisher's products include valve positioners, steam conditioning equipment, ball valves, regulators, oil and gas control equipment, and sliding stem valves and actuators. It maintains operations in the US, Asia, and Europe.

Process Management is Emerson's largest segment and represents about 30% of its annual sales. The US generates the majority of sales for this segment, along with Asia and Europe. Fisher Controls has operations in China, France, Hungary, Japan, Malaysia, Mexico, Singapore, and the US.

Fisher Controls expands its reach through the opening of additional facilities in key regions. In 2013 it launched a new nuclear center in France in order to enhance Emerson's nuclear expertise and better cater to its European customers in this area.

Fisher Controls' products and services are disseminated via its direct salesforce and independent sales representatives. Service and repair sites are located in Asia/Pacific, Europe, Latin America, the Middle East, and North America.

Fisher Controls dates back to the late 19th century. It was family-controlled until sold to Monsanto in 1969 and became a part of Emerson in 1992.
