Heat Biologics is ready to put cancer in the line of fire. The biopharmaceutical company is developing a cancer vaccine that uses a person's own immune system to combat cancer cells. Called ImPACT (immune pan antigen cytotoxic therapy), the drug HS-110 is first being tested to combat non-small cell lung cancer. HS-110 was to begin Phase II clinical trials in 2013. In addition, Heat Biologics is developing ImPACT therapeutic vaccines for bladder cancer, breast cancer, and ovarian cancer. The company also believes it can be used to prevent infectious diseases, such as hepatitis C, malaria, and HIV. Heat Biologics went public 2013.
