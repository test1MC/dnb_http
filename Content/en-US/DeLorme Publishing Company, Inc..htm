Feeling adrift? DeLorme Publishing can steer you in the right direction. A leader in map publishing, DeLorme makes maps, software, and global positioning system (GPS) products for consumers and professionals. Products include its XMap GIS (geographic information system) software, and the Earthmate GPS Receiver that displays DeLorme color topographic maps and aerial imagery via laptop, wireless device, or handheld receiver. DeLorme's Yarmouth, Maine, headquarters houses Eartha, the world's largest globe (41 ft. in diameter). David DeLorme founded the company in 1976 when he sat down at his kitchen table to create his own map of Maine's northern woods. In 2016 DeLorme was acquired by competitor Garmin Ltd .

Garmin was to maintain DeLorme's facility in Yarmouth, Maine as a research and development operation for DeLorme'tw0way satellite communications technologies. Of particular interest to Garmin was DeLorme's inReach series of two-way satellite communication devices. The GPS-enabled devices allow the user to send and receive satellite text messages or trigger an SOS for emergency help. Terms of the acquisition were not disclosed.

The company targets professional customers working in areas such as government, transportation, and civil engineering. Customers of DeLorme's XMap platform include the California Department of Transportation , FEMA , the Environmental Protection Agency , Maine State Police, and a variety of oil and gas and wind energy companies.

In 2009 the company expanded its product offerings in the worldwide digital map data market when it introduced the DeLorme World Base Map. The product, which provides a digital elevated, topographic view of the earth, is aimed at the GIS and OEM markets. After the product's launch, GIS software firm ESRI adopted the DeLorme World Base Map as a template for its world topographic map generation.
