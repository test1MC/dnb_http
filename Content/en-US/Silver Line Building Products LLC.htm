At Silver Line Building Products, when employees look out the window, they're not daydreaming -- it's their job! Silver Line is one of the largest manufacturers of vinyl windows in North America. It targets the new-construction and replacement markets with a range of designs, from double-hung and picture windows to custom-made octagonal and quarter-round window styles. It also makes acoustic and commercial windows and sliding-glass patio doors. Customers include building-supply stores, specialty window dealers, and lumberyards. Silver Line, which is owned by Andersen Corporation , was founded in 1947 by Arthur Silverman.

The New Jersey-based company has manufacturing plants located in Georgia, Illinois, New Jersey, Ohio, and Texas.

Altogether, Silver Line serves 39 US states, including Rhode Island and the District of Columbia.

Silver Line sells its products under the brand names American Craftsman and Silver Line Windows & Doors.

Silver Line's offerings are marketed to architects, builders, and homeowners, as well as to retailers and wholesale dealers. The company uses direct sales representatives to promote its products.

Silver Line has gained some market exposure as its products have been featured on the popular television show "Extreme Makeover: Home Edition."

Working to reclaim sales lost to the recession in the building industry in recent years, Silver Line is emphasizing that its windows meet ENERGY STAR specifications for all 50 states. The ENERGY STAR program was developed by the US Department of Energy and the US Environmental Protection Agency to help consumers identify products that save energy.

Silver Line also tests and certified its windows and patio doors to the National Fenestration Rating Council's standards for U-factor, solar heat gain coefficient, and visible transmittance. In 2013 the company launched a new line of LoE-LS glass for Southern areas, as well as windows and doors designed to withstand severe coastal weather conditions.
