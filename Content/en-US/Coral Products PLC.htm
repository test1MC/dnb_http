Coral Products corrals its business activities around media packaging, or jewel cases, for compact disc (CD) and DVD products produced by such entertainment moguls as Cinram , Paramount , Universal Pictures , and Warner Home Video . Coral Products holds a manufacturing license for Red Tag, the retail security case for DVDs, software, and games. It also produces a diverse range of plastic injection-molded household goods, such as storage containers, buckets, dish drainers, and brushes, sold under supermarket private labels, as well as containers for storage, food, recycling waste, and medical applications. The company's services include design, budgeting, manufacture, packaging, and delivery of special projects.

The UK accounted for 97% of the company's revenues in fiscal 2015.

The company's revenues increased by 1% in fiscal 2015 due to an increase in food container and trade molding sales, partially offset by a drop in media and recycling container revenues.

Coral Products reported net income of £0.2 million in fiscal 2015 (compared to a loss of £0.9 million a year earlier due to the absence of impairment loss recognized on tangible assets, intangible assets, and inventory).

The company's cash flow increased by 50% that year.

In 2015 Coral Products adopted a new five-year plan aimed at substantially increasing revenues and profitability from its specialist plastic products manufacturing and distribution activities.

As part of this strategy, in 2015 the company took the initial step along this plan when it acquired certain injection molding, extrusion blow molding and injection blow molding plant and machinery from Neiman Packaging Limited.

In 2014 Coral Products acquired Yorkshire-based Tatra Plastics Limited, for £2.5 million. Tatra makes injection molding products for the rail and telecoms.
