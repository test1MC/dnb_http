Wheeling-Nisshin, a subsidiary of Nisshin Steel , produces a variety of hot-dip coated steels, such as stainless steel. The company's output includes 400,000 tons produced at its aluminizing and galvanizing line facility and 300,000 tons produced at its continuous galvanizing line facility. Both of the facilities are located at the company's headquarters site in West Virginia. Its primary customers are in the automotive, appliance, and construction industries. Wheeling-Nisshin was founded in 1986. It had been a joint venture between Nisshin and US steel producer Wheeling Pitt (now operating as Severstal Wheeling ) until the Japanese steel company bought out its partner in early 2008.
