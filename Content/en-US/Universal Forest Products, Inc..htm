Universal Forest Products has no trouble separating the trees from the forest. The company is a leading manufacturer and distributor of engineered wood and construction materials, which it sells to do-it-yourself retail stores, residential and mobile home builders, and industrial customers. It also offers composite wood and plastic products. Universal Forest buys its wood from lumber mills and pressure-treats it to make such products as roof trusses, wall panels, flooring, pallets, and shipping crates. The company operates 94 facilities in the US, Canada, Mexico, and Australia.

Michigan-based Universal Forest has 94 facilities located throughout the US, Canada, Mexico, and Australia. The US accounted for 97% of its sales in 2015.

Beyond wood products, Universal Forest's consumer products subsidiary offers a large portfolio of outdoor living products, including wood composite decking, decorative balusters.The firm also provides framing services to the residential market and forming products for concrete construction. Universal's Industrial Wood Industries business supplies moving boxes, bulk shipping and storage bins, and other specialized wood products and bins mostly to industrial and agricultural customers in the Western US.

The company's operating segments consist of North, South, West, Alternative Materials, International, and Corporate. West accounted for 39% of Universal Forest's total net sales in 2015; North, 32%; and South, 23%.

The company's three primary markets are retail, construction, and industrial.

The customers comprising retail market (39% of Universal Forest's total net sales in 2015) are national home center retailers, retail-oriented regional lumberyards and contractor-oriented lumberyards. The retail market accounted for

The construction market (31%) is made up of customers in three submarkets, manufactured housing, residential construction and commercial construction. The customers comprising the manufactured housing market are producers of mobile, modular and prefabricated homes and recreational vehicles.

Its industrial market (30%) is defined as manufacturers and agricultural customers who use pallets, specialty crates, wooden boxes, and other containers used for packaging, shipping and material handling purposes, as well as other products used in a variety of different applications.

The Home Depot is the company's top customer, accounting for 19% of Universal Forest's 2015 revenues.

The company has seen seen healthy revenue and profit growth over the past few years. In 2015 net sales increased by 9% due to higher sales from the North and West segments.

Net sales attributable to the North segment increased due to higher sales to retail, residential construction, and manufactured housing customers.

West segment sales rose due to an increase in sales to the retail, commercial construction, and industrial markets. Acquired businesses contributed to the growth in sales to the industrial market, while sales to the retail market grew due to higher unit sales.

Universal Forest's net income of the company has followed a similar trend to revenues.

In 2015 net income increased by 33% due to higher sales partially offset by increased selling, general, and administrative expenses.

The increase in selling, general, and administrative was primarily due to an increase in compensation and related expenses resulting from annual raises and hiring additional sales and design personnel to support sales growth, and a increase in incentive compensation expenses tied to profitability and a return on investment.

In 2015 net cash provided by operating activities increased by 131%.

Universal Forest is working to extend its reach into areas where there is potential for growth by diversifying through a series of acquisitions in recent years. From 2012 through early 2015, it's been purchasing both industrial lumber and specialized packing companies that have helped the company enrich its wood services expertise, enter new markets, and expand into new geographic markets (including one purchase which expanded its presence internationally into Australia), with the goal of making the company more resistant to future dips and downturns in the homebuilding market.

Universal Forest still embraces its construction roots. Although fewer manufactured homes are being built, Universal Forest anticipates that by offering more products it can gain a larger share of the remaining market. Recent acquisitions include manufacturers of treated wood, composite products, engineered wood components, millwork, ornamental woodwork, truss work, and related construction materials.

Through all these strategies, Universal Forest plans to reach $3 billion in sales by 2017.

A key component of the company's growth strategy is to complete business combinations. Business combinations involve inherent risks, including assimilation and successfully managing growth. The company's goal is to increase ssales by developing new, value-added products, international business expansion, and gaining additional market share.

The company has focused on its growth strategy through acquisitions. It completed two acquisitions during 2015 and five during 2014.

In February 2015, Universal Forest Products purchased select assets of Idaho-based lumber supplier Rapid Wood manufacturing company, which specializes in the region's manufactured housing and recreational vehicle industries, to extend its reach into Idaho.

In January 2015, the company secured a controlling stake in the Australia-based integrated packing product manufacturer and distributer, Integra Packaging Proprietary, Ltd., which expanded the company's reach internationally.

With an eye on growing its Industrial business, Universal Forest in 2014 acquired a 50% ownership interest in Upshur Forest Products, LLC, which owns sawmill operations in Gilmer, Texas. It also purchased High Level Components, LLC, a North Carolina-based building component manufacturer.

In November 2014, it secured an 80% stake in certain assets of Packnet, enriching the company's manufacturing and design capabilities and expanding its geographic reach. Also that month, it bought select assets of Bigs Packaging and Lumber, LLC to grow the company's industrial business.

In March 2014, Universal Forest purchased certain assets of the North-Carolina based Container Systems, Inc., which wil give the company's Industrial Wood Industries business a presence in the Eastern United States (it was primarily operating in the Western US prior to the deal).

In January 2014, a Universal Forest subsidiary bought leading cellulose insulation manufacturer Massachusetts-based National Fiber.

In 2013 a subsidiary of Universal Forest acquired Minnesota-based Custom Caseworks, a high-precision business-to-business manufacturer of engineered wood products for the commercial market.

In late 2012 the company bought Washington-based Nepa Pallet and Container Co., which makes pallets, containers, and bins, to supplement its agricultural and industrial business. In another move to increase capacity and reduce costs, Universal Forest bought out Alabama-based MSR Forest Products in 2012. The deal gave Universal Forest a regional supplier of roof trusses and cut-to-size lumber used in manufactured housing.

Universal Forest was founded in 1955.
