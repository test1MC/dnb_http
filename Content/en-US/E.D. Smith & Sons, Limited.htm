Whether it's a slice of toast or a hamburger, E.D. Smith is on top of things. E.D. Smith & Sons is one of Canada's leading jam, pie-filling, and ketchup manufacturers. It also makes barbecue and pasta sauces, fruit topping, mayonnaise, salsas, and syrups. The company's products are offered under the E.D. Smith brand, as well as under private labels to grocery stores, foodservice suppliers, and industrial food manufacturers throughout North America. Family-owned for four generations, E.D. Smith was acquired by US-based TreeHouse Foods in 2007.

The company operates in the US and Canada.

E.D. Smith is a manufacturer of jam, pie-filling, and ketchup manufacturers.

The company's products are sold via retail and foodservice organizations across North America.

E.D. Smith's private-label customers include Loblaws and Sobeys in Canada, and HEB and Wegmans in the US. The company also formulates products for its foodservice customers. Birds Eye Foods packs all of E.D. Smith's Canadian retail pie fillings.

Jams represented 1.6% of the consolidated net sales of E.D. Smith's parent company in 2015.

TreeHouse Foods , a major specialty foods manufacturer (pickles, cheese sauces, liquid egg substitutes), purchased E.D. Smith in 2007 for $220 million plus the assumption of $100 million in debt. The deal expanded the Canadian's product portfolio and gives it a presence in Canada.

E.D. Smith, previously controlled through an income trust (E.D. Smith Income Fund), agreed to the buyout due to a heavy debt load, looming tax problems, and continuing poor stock performance. A new tax structure for income trusts announced by the federal government in 2006 led to a strategic review and a cut in payouts to shareholders. CEO Michael Burrows later left the company and was replaced by former ConAgra executive, F. Martin (Marty) Thrasher.

Focused on acquisitions, E.D. Smith had purchased private-label salad dressing and marinade maker North Coast Processing (now E.D. Smith USA) in 2005 and Seaforth Creamery, Canada's largest private-label salad-dressing manufacturer, the following year.
