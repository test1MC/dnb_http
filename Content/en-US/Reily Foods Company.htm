Reily Foods, which hails from New Orleans, hopes that you'll slap gobs of its mayo on your po'boy and wash it down with a gigantic tumbler of the company's tea. Besides making and marketing regionally revered Blue Plate mayonnaise and Luzianne teas, Reily Foods counts several other popular brand names among its portfolio of products. It manufactures and sells more than 100 products, including Swans Down cake flour, No Pudge! brownie mix, CDM Coffee & Chicory, Wick Fowler's 2-Alarm Chili, and Carroll Shelby Chili Kits. The food firm sells its products nationwide and across Europe. Chairman William B. Reily III is the third-generation member of the Reily family to run the company, which was founded in 1902.

Headquartered in New Orleans, Louisiana, Reily Foods sells its products in the US and Europe.

One of the company's top brands, Luzianne, was established in 1932 and has since become known as the region's finest tea.

Reily Foods sells its products through grocery stores and restaurants nationwide and select products internationally. It also makes products for other companies under private label.

While Reily Foods is not considered to be an acquisitive company, it does make strategic purchases on occasion. In 2013 it acquired Massachusetts-based New England Tea & Coffee Company, one of the country's largest independent coffee roasters. It also purchased the American Coffee Company, maker of restaurant and other foodservice coffee brand French Market Coffee, in 2008.

Reily Foods was founded as Wm. B. Reily & Co.
