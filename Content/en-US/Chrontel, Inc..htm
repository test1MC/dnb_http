Chrontel designs specialized semiconductors used in visual interfaces of electronic devices, such as notebook computers, smartphones, and high-definition television sets. The increasing popularity of HDTV equipment and DVD players is helping drive demand for Chrontel's encoders and transmitters. The company's innovations include the world's first VGA-to-TV encoder. Chrontel's video display chips are incorporated into reference designs offered by many microprocessor and graphics processor vendors. WBL Corporation Limited owns a small stake in the company.

The company has offices in Bermuda, China, Taiwan, and the US.

Chrontel's major customers include Acer , AMD , ASUSTek Computer , Compal Electronics , Dell , Flextronics , Foxconn , Freescale , Fujitsu , Hewlett-Packard , MiTAC Technology , NEC , Panasonic , Sony , Toshiba , and Wistron .

The company's strategy is to be the leading supplier of high-quality semiconductor and software solutions for a wide range of computing and consumer platforms. It seeks to do this by extending its leadership in the display interface market; moving into new markets such as CMMB, USB3.0 and SATA3.0.; and leveraging its unique combination of technologies in and its strong relationships with leading industry partners.

In 2011 Chrontel launched two new devices for computer and display manufacturers converting from legacy LVDS to the emerging DisplayPortâ„¢ standard for high-speed, high-resolution displays. The CH7510 timing controller is used in display panels to convert Display Port input signals into mini-LVDS outputs that directly drive row and column pixel data on LCDs. The CH7511 is used in notebook computers to convert DisplayPort input signals into LVDS (widely used to drive video signals across the notebook's hinge to the computer display).

Chrontel was established in 1986 by President and CEO David Soo.
