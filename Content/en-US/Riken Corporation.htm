Riken Corporation wants to be lord of the rings, more specifically the piston ring. Riken manufactures piston rings for cars and motorcycles, as well as a variety of other automotive products, including brake parts, camshafts, steering knuckles, valve lifters, and valve seats. It rounds out more business with fittings for iron and steel pipes. Riken also makes alloy materials, anechoic chambers, electrical-heating materials, incinerator and industrial furnaces, and seal rings. Founded in 1927, Riken Corporation operates three manufacturing plants in Japan and some 10 subsidiaries in about 10 other countries.
