Edgewell Personal Care Company has the edge by offering some of the most popular brands in your household. Its portfolio of personal care products includes razors, shaving cream, moist wipes, pet care products, sunscreen, and feminine care products sold under brands such as Schick, Edge, Skintimate, Wet Ones, o.b., Playtex, Carefree, Banana Boat, Hawaiian Tropic, Litter Genie, and Stayfree. Edgewell Personal Care was formed in mid-2015, when famous battery maker Energizer Holdings was spun off as a publicly traded company. Edgewell was created to focus solely on its personal care products operations.

Battery making giant Energizer Holdings was spun off from the company's operations in mid-2015. The remaining operations rebranded as Edgewell Personal Care in order to focus solely on its personal care products operations.

Edgewell's personal Care includes wet shave products sold under the Schick, Wilkinson Sword, Edge, Skintimate and Personna brands as well as private label razors and skin care products sold under the Banana Boat, Hawaiian Tropic, Wet Ones, Stayfree, Carefree, and Playtex brands. Infant care products are sold under the Playtex and Diaper Genie brands.

The company has a broad global footprint in more than 50 countries. It has 57 properties, 24 in the US and 33 in other countries. (About 13 of these properties are used as production plants.) The US accounted for 59% of its net sales in 2016. Other countries accounted for the remaining 41%.

Edgewell's products are marketed primarily through a direct sales force, but also through exclusive and non-exclusive distributors and wholesalers. It distributes its products to consumers through numerous retail locations worldwide, including mass merchandisers and warehouse clubs, food, drug and convenience stores, military stores, and e-commerce.

Wal-Mart accounted for approximately 25% of its net sales from continuing operations in fiscal 2016.

Revenues declined by 2% from 2015 to 2016 due to decreased sales from infant care and other segment (26%) and skin products (8%). The company was also hurt by currency fluctuations from Venezuela in 2016.

Edgewell posted positive net income of $178 million in 2016 compared to a net loss of $275 million in 2015. The was fueled by decreased selling, general, and administrative expenses driven by lower pre-tax spin costs in addition to fewer impairment charges.

Both internal development and acquisitions have fueled Edgewell's growth. In 2015 it exited its industrial business, which was part of its all other segment, due to a shift of management focus to other segment products.

In late 2016, Edgewell acquired Bulldog Skincare Holdings, a UK-based men's grooming and skincare products company. Bulldog offers male skincare products including moisturizers, face washes, scrubs, balms, shave gels and creams, body washes, lip balm, and lotions. The acquisition allowed Edgewell to expand its personal care portfolio in a growing global category where it can leverage its large geographic footprint.
