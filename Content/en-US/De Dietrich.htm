Tracing its origins back to more than 330 years, De Dietrich has forged its way into the 21st century through the production of a slew of equipment for fine chemical, pharmaceutical, and related industries. Organized as De Dietrich Process Systems, the company's product lineup includes reactors, agitators, tanks, receivers, pipes, and valves. Its glass-lined equipment arm supplies labs engaged in chemical syntheses, and De Dietrich also manufactures filters and drying equipment and supplies borosilicate process plant equipment.

De Dietrich has expertise in separation, mixing, heat transfer, reaction, powder handling, drying, and sterility. It supplies manufacturing chemical and pharmaceutical industrial equipment and operates production facilities across Africa, Asia, Europe, and the Americas.

The company has operations across Africa (South Africa), Asia (China, Singapore, India); Europe (France, Belgium, Germany, Hungary, the UK, Ireland, Switzerland and Spain); and the Americas (the US and Brazil).

The company serves companies in a range of industries, including pharmaceuticals, chemicals, research and development, environment, mining and precious metals, and agricultural chemicals.

De Dietrich, one of the oldest companies in France, began its long history in 1684, when Jean Dietrich bought the Jaegerthal forge and built a blast furnace.
