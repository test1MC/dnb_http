For True Textiles' workers, it's home sweet office. The commercial contract manufacturer offers a range of panel fabrics for office interiors from upholstery to wall panels, cubicle curtains, and window treatments. True Textiles also specializes in eco-friendly fabrics for health care, hospitality, and automotive markets. The company's office lineup is marketed under the Guilford of Maine, Teknit, Infinity, and Terratex labels. True Textiles has supplied a roster of leading furniture makers, including HNI , Herman Miller , Steelcase , and Haworth . The company also attracts automotive work; it has made a sustainable body-cloth fabric for Ford and Hyundai hybrids.

True Textiles is headquartered in Grand Rapids, Michigan, and has manufacturing facilities in Maine and North Carolina.

Amidst the wash of manufacturers marketing their concern for the environment, True Textiles is pushing its expertise in eco-friendly textile engineering. The company purchases energy from renewable sources, such as wind, solar, landfill gas, biomass, and hydroelectric, to power its mill production.

Operations are guided by objectives for zero waste, zero harmful emissions, and efficient transportation. Targeting consumers looking to make a difference with their purchases, the company's Terratex brand fabrics are made from recycled polyester or renewable materials (such as corn derivatives).
