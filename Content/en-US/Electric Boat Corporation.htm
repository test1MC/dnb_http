Electric Boat designs, builds, and maintains nuclear attack and ballistic-missile submarines for the US Navy . A subsidiary of defense giant General Dynamics , Electric Boat was established in 1899 and won its first US Navy contract within a year. It has delivered the lead ship of the Virginia class ( USS Virginia -SSN774) and its third and final Seawolf-class submarine ( Jimmy Carter -SSN23) to the US Navy, and is working on several Virginia-class submarines with Northrop Grumman . Electric Boat continues to provide support and maintenance for Seawolf-, Ohio-, and Los Angeles-class submarines. Electric Boat operates in Connecticut and Rhode Island.

The company has its presence in Washington, DC; Massachusetts; Rhode Island; and Hawaii. It has maintenance or repair facilities at Kings Bay, Georgia; and Bangor, Washington. The company has also supported maintenance or repair activity in the Persian Gulf, Europe, and Guam.

Quonset Point, Rhode Island is home to the company's automated hull-fabrication and outfitting facility. Submarine components are manufactured using digitally controlled machines for precision cutting, machining, and bending. The completed hull cylinders are equipped with tanks, propulsion machinery, torpedo and missile tubes, wiring and lighting, and piping, and then sent by barge to the Groton (Connecticut) shipyard or to Northrop Grumman Newport News (Virginia) for completion. The Groton facility designs, engineers, assembles, tests, and delivers the submarines.

Electric Boat built its first submersible vessel, developed by John Philip Holland, in 1899. The company's first nuclear submarine, the USS Nautilus , was christened in 1954 by first lady Mamie Eisenhower. Since then, Electric Boat has delivered over 100 submarines to the US Navy.
