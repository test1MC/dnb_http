Icicle Seafoods is out fishing even when icicles hang low. The fishery and seafood-processing company catches, processes, and distributes fresh, canned, and frozen fish. Products include cod, herring, pollock, halibut, wild salmon, crab, and trout. Customers include food retailers, as well as industrial, wholesale, and food service operators worldwide. The company operates floating seafood processing plants, shoreline plants, and other facilities in Alaska and Washington. Its Alaskan cannery has been in operation since 1899. Icicle also participates in a Chilean trout- and salmon-farm JV. The seafood supplier is owned by private investment firm Paine & Partners, part of Fox Paine & Co.

Across Alaska and Washington, Seattle-based Icicle Seafoods maintains floating seafood processing plants, shoreline plants, and other facilities. It also enjoys a global reach through a joint venture agreement.

Icicle Seafoods owns the nation's largest owned and operated salmon farming company located in the Pacific Northwest. It operates in Chile through a joint venture focused on trout and salmon farming.

One of North America's largest and most diversified seafood companies, Icicle Seafoods regularly seeks strategic opportunities to expand its business and boost its market share in processing salmon and other seafood products. To this end, the company in 2012 acquired Snopac Products, which operates a seafood processing plant in Dillingham, Alaska, and serves the Bristol Bay sockeye salmon fishery. A processing vessel, the M/V Snopac Innovator, allows the company to also tap herring and pink salmon fisheries. Snopac's collective process capacity reaches more than 1 million pounds of round weight daily. Past purchases include salmon-farming company Smoki Foods, which added salmon fillets and value-added salmon products, and included Smoki subsidiary American Gold Seafoods. Buying American Gold Seafoods gave Icicle Seafoods a pair of hatcheries near Rochester, Washington, and 120 pens off Bainbridge Island, Port Angeles, Cypress Island, and Hope Island (all within the waters of Puget Sound).

Icicle Seafoods has raised funds for growth through the 2010 sale of its Bellingham, Washington, surimi analog business to Trident Seafoods . Icicle Seafoods said the surimi operation was not strategic to its future.

Products sourced by Icicle Seafoods are sold worldwide through several sales channels, such as industrial, food service, wholesale, and retail. Its products are distributed and marketed nationally under private labels, national brands, and the company's own Honey Boy, Black Top, and Ship Ahoy names.

Icicle Seafoods was founded in 1965, when a group of fisherman and employees purchased a cannery from Pacific American Fisheries, once one of the world's top salmon canning operations.
