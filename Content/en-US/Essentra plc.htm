Now that all the smoke has cleared, the news has surely filtered through that leading cigarette-filter maker ESSENTRA (formerly Filtrona) is about more than serving the tobacco industry. In addition to its cigarette filters (used by such tobacco giants as Altria and British American Tobacco , ESSENTRA supplies a variety of other specialty plastic, fiber, foam, and packaging products, including pen wicks and self-adhesive tear tape and labels. It also makes fluid handling components. The company changed its corporate name in 2013 to reflect its widening range of products and the growing number of industries it serves.

ESSENTRA has operations in about 30 countries. It 40 primary manufacturing facilities and 64 sales and distribution centers, and five research and development centers scattered across the Americas, Asia, and Europe. It has major plants in Brazil, Canada, China, France, Hungary, Italy, Mexico, the Netherlands, Poland, Singapore, South Africa, Spain, Thailand, the UK, and the US.

ESSENTRA concentrates on manufacturing and distributing small but essential products in high volumes to a wide range of customers. Serving niche markets worldwide, the company operates through four main divisions: Component and Protection Solutions, Porous Technologies, Packaging and Securing Solutions, and Filter Products.

The Component and Protection Solutions division supplies mostly plastic protection and finishing products for markets such as electrical controls, hydraulics, oil and gas, pneumatics, and tubular metals.

In addition to producing its self-adhesive tear tape (used for opening packages of cigarettes, snack crackers, and other products), ESSENTRA's Packaging and Securing Solutions division makes custom packaging components for consumer goods, and other products. It also supplies authentication technologies.

The company's Filter Products division focuses on filters and other items for the cigarette industry -- including roll-your-own filter tips. Its Porous Technologies division's products include medical diagnostics and wound care items, inkjet printer cartridges, writing instruments, and air fresheners.

ESSENTRA posted a 23% increase in revenues in 2012 thanks to a 46% spike in Protection and Finishing Products sales due to continued range expansion, new site roll-outs, and improved marketing penetration (including contributions from recent acquisitions). Porous Technologies' revenues increased by 17% thanks to new products and geographic expansion, offset by the bankruptcy filing of a global OEM. Coated and Security Products' revenues grew by 17% thanks to higher demand from a major multinational tobacco customer, and new packaging and promotional innovations in the wrapped food sector. The strong Tobacco results also lifted label sales by 14%. Filter Products revenues grew by 12% in 2012 thanks to higher sales of special filters across all of ESSENTRA's geographic markets. all geographic regions.

The company posted a 21% jump in net income in 2012 as the result of higher net sales and increased profits from its continuing operations.

Pivotal to ESSENTRA's growth has been finding acquisitions that complement the company's holdings, particularly businesses involved in manufacturing, sourcing, and distributing products for consumer, medical, and industrial markets. ESSENTRA seeks businesses that have strong positions in niche international markets.

Mergers and Acquisitions

Growing its packaging portfolio, in 2013 ESSENTRA bought UK-based Contego Healthcare Limited (a specialist packaging supplier to the pharmaceutical and healthcare industries) for £160 million (US$257 million). That year it also acquired Sweden-based Ulinco Components AB, a distributor of plastic protection and finishing products, hardware and specialist masking solutions in the Nordic region.

In 2012 the company added to its Coated and Security Products division by acquiring UK-based Securit World, which distributes personal ID card systems, printers, software, and associated accessories. The purchase also complements ESSENTRA's Payne Security personal ID products business for the consumer packaging, identity, and valuable documents markets.

Also in 2012, ESSENTRA acquired South Korea-based Jae Yong, which makes reservoirs, tubes, and nibs for writing instruments. Jae Yong extends the company's global presence in the Asia/Pacific region and also expands its products line by including other components for markers, highlighters, and ink-based writing instruments.

The company expanded its product offerings again in 2012 by acquiring for an undisclosed amount US-based Lymtech Scientific, which manufactures and distributes porous speciality wiping materials used in cleanrooms and other environments by the medical, life science, electronic, and industrial markets. Lymtech, now under the company's Porous Technologies unit, also adds a distribution selling channel and expands ESSENTRA's presence in Europe and Asia.

In 2011 the company acquired US-based Reid Supply, which distributes industrial components such as hardware, handles, and clamps, and added it to the PFP division. Later in 2011, the company acquired US-based Richco for $110 million. Richco, which manufactures plastic and metal fasteners, cable ties, and circuit board hardware, also became a part of the PFP portfolio.

Company Background

Founded in the 1940s, ESSENTRA (as Filtrona) was spun off from Bunzl in 1984 and began trading alongside its former parent on the London Stock Exchange .
