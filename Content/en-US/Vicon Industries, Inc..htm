This company keeps an eye out for its customers -- an electronic eye. Vicon Industries designs, manufactures, and markets a broad line of products used in integrated video surveillance systems. It is a leading maker of network digital video (ViconNet and Kollector brands) and dome camera systems (Surveyor brand) used in surveillance and security. The company's products include cameras, monitors, recorders, video servers and software, and access system controls. It sells through independent dealers, distributors, and systems integrators who distribute or install its products in commercial and industrial facilities and residences. Vicon sells primarily in the US, but it also serves clients in about 40 countries.

Headquartered in New York, Vicon oversees worldwide operations from the US and its European headquarters in the UK. In addition, it operates sales and service offices in Israel, Hong Kong, Germany, and other local sales offices throughout Europe.

US customers account for about two-thirds of company sales; the majority of the company's international sales come from the Asia/Pacific region, Europe, and the Middle East.

Vicon's product line consists of video systems and video management system (VMS) applications, which configure and manage network devices, including cameras. It also sells and makes video system edge devices such as video encoders, decoders, network and digital video recorders, analog and IP fixed position or robotic cameras, HD digital cameras, matrix video switchers, and system controls.The company's Europe-based operations consist of Vicon Industries Limited and its Vicon Deutschland GmbH subsidiary, which market and distribute Vicron's products in Europe and the Middle East.

A short list of Vicon's customers includes Johnson & Johnson , UCLA , the New York City Transit Authority , Charles deGaulle Airport in Paris, Her Majesty's Prisons in the UK, and the Beijing National Stadium in China.

Vicon sells its products are sold principally to independent dealers, system integrators, and security products distributors. It also promotes and markets its products through industry trade shows, product brochures, catalogs, and direct marketing and electronic mailings to existing and prospective customers, webinars, technical seminars for system designers, customers and end users, road shows, end user magazines, and its website.

The company reported a 20% drop in revenues in 2013, primarily due to a slump in North American sales and order rates, which were negatively affected by, among other things, a technical matter relating to a prior release of its ViconNet video management software application

Thanks to a gain on the sale of its Hauppauge, New York, headquarters facility in August 2013, Vicon reported net income of $19,000 for 2013 compared with a net loss of $1.4 million in 2012. Vicon has posted a loss in cash flow from its operations for the last four years.

Vicon plans to continue investing heavily in product development, in spite of its losses. It sees development of new products, such as high definition surveillance cameras, as key to staying ahead of its competition and remaining relevant in the security products industry. Vicon is constantly seeking out other security and video companies whose products complement its video surveillance offerings. As a result, it enters into several strategic partnerships with companies who meet our criteria of product excellence, innovation, and value.

In 2013 the company partnered with Axis Communications, engaged in network video, for applications development at an event held in Mexico City. That year Vicon also introduced the V920D Series of rugged indoor and outdoor network domes, with a choice of standard definition, 720p and 1080p resolutions. It also launched the H264-16CH-ENCDR (a high-performance, 16-channel H.264 encoder that allows for the use of analog cameras side-by-side with IP models within a VMS network) and the HDExpress, a revolutionary IP video product.

In 2012 the company introduced the 1080p version of its Surveyor HD PTZ dome at ISC West; a new corner-mount camera, V-CELL-IP, for Prison Cells; ViconNet IP video management software; and other products.
