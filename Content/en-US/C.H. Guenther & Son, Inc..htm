Business is a grind for C.H. Guenther & Son; the flour mill produces the Pioneer brand of pancake, biscuit and gravy mixes, as well as the White Wings, Peter Pan, and Morrison brand name flours, tortilla mixes, and ready-to-eat tortillas, used in homes, restaurants, and commercial kitchens across the US. Guenther also makes frozen bakery products to supply McDonalds , Burger King , and other customers in the food service industry. In addition, the family-owned company operates The Guenther House, home of founder Carl Hilmar Guenther, restored as a restaurant, museum, and gift shop located in San Antonio, Texas. C.H. Guenther & Son was started in Fredericksburg, Texas in 1851.

San Antonio-based Guenther has manufacturing facilities in Kansas, Indianapolis, Missouri, New Jersey, New York, Oregon, Texas, Tennessee, and South Carolina. The company also has three facilities in Canada, and three in Europe (Belgium and the UK).

From its milling legacy, C.H. Guenther has swelled its portfolio with an array of baked goods, doughs, and convenience food products via an active acquisition schedule.

In May 2014, the company acquired Tribeca Oven, Inc., a New Jersey-based maker of artisan breads and rolls. In 2013, the company bought Golden West Baking Company (GW), Gizella Pastry Ltd., and Les Boulangeries Rene (LBR), all in Canada. GW and LBR produce buns, rolls, and English muffins for quick-serve, food service, and retail grocery channels. Gizella Pastry makes frozen desserts, such as cheesecake, layer cakes, and brownies.

Other recent purchases include: Kansas-based Williams Foods, a maker of dry seasonings, mixes, and easy-to-prepare side dishes under the Wagner's, Williams, and Williams Country Store brand names; and Portland-based Pizza Blends, which makes frozen and dry mix pizza crusts, for an undisclosed price in 2012. The company's customers come primarily from the wholesale food industry.

In 2006 Guenther acquired Texas-based Morrison Milling Company, maker of more than 300 flour- and corn-based dry mix and frozen food products. Later that same year, it sold its White Lily brand of products to J. M. Smucker .
