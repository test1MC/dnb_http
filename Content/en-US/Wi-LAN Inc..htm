Wi-LAN is amassing a trove of patents to drive up revenue. The company, previously a wireless equipment manufacturer, pivoted to focus primarily on licensing its technology to equipment manufacturers. Wi-LAN has licensed its 1,00-plus patents to nearly 300 clients. Its customers incorporate Wi-LAN's technology into wireless, wireline, and V-Chip (used to filter out inappropriate television programming) technology. Wi-LAN's customers include the likes of Cisco , Nokia , Panasonic , and Samsung .

Wi-LAN owns a panoply of patents that relate to 3D TV technologies, automotive headlight assemblies, phased loop technology, microcontrollers for aerospace applications, medical, and industrial applications, computer gaming, medical stent technologies, irrigation technologies, CMOS image sensors, streaming video technologies, Internet search, building automation, non-volatile Flash memory, semiconductor clocking technologies, smart meter monitoring, LED lighting technologies, and more.

Wi-LAN is based in Ottawa, Ontario and has a research lab in San Diego, California.

Revenue increased 5% in 2015 while profit rose 2% for the year.

While Wi-LAN continues to enter into license partnership agreements at a fast pace, the company started a restructuring in the 2015 fourth quarter in the face of what was termed a difficult licensing environment. The company said the process would affect about 30% of its work force.
