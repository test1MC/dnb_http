Going with the flow, Weir SPM (formerly SPM Flow Control) manufactures flow control equipment and other heavy-duty products for various customers in the oil and gas industry. The company's products include made-to-order well service pumps, basic control valves, piping, high-pressure control components, and safety products. Weir SPM makes all its products at its 300,000-sq.-ft. manufacturing plant and corporate office in Fort Worth, Texas, and distributes finished products around the world. The company is a unit of UK-based The Weir Group 's oil and gas division and accounted for about 1/3 of the parent company's revenues in 2013.

The company distributes its products through stocking and service centers in Africa, Asia, the Middle East, North and South America, and the UK. It has operations in Australia, Canada, Mexico, Scotland, Singapore, the UAE, and the US.

Weir SPM operates as a part of the Pressure Pumping segment of its parent company. Its maintenance and service offerings include cement head testing, flow control and restraint system rental packages, flow control recertification and repair, and pump repair services. Its line of reciprocating plungers pumps are used in various applications including cementing, acidizing, and fracturing.

The Pressure Pumping segment's revenues increased by 9% in 2013 driven by strong aftermarket growth as a result of the normalization of inventory levels and a product bundling strategy. Weir SPM aftermarket orders exceeded 2012 levels.

Responding to the slower than expected recovery in upstream markets in 2013 the company increased its range of products and leveraged its industry-leading service center network to grow aftermarket revenues. It also consolidated operations to improve operational efficiency. Pressure Pumping manufacturing was consolidated in Fort Worth, Texas, and two small satellite manufacturing units were closed.

In 2012 the company also invested in flow capacity expansion and in integration projects at UK-based Seaboard and US-based Novatech, (both acquired by The Weir Group in 2012).

Seeking to boost its trained workforce at its manufacturing plant, in 2012 Weir SPM partnered with Tarrant County College to provide job training using a $282,116 Skills Development Fund grant from the Texas Workforce Commission to custom train 334 new and existing workers.

In 2011 the company began a major capacity expansion of its manufacturing and service facility in Fort Worth, Texas. The $40 million investment is part of its parent's growth strategy to take advantage of increased drilling and hydraulic fracturing activities in oil and liquid-rich North American shale formations. The expansion includes a pump overhaul facility, several warehouses, an assembly and engineering office, a pipe shop, a swivel manufacturing plant, and a service center.

SPM Flow Control was acquired by The Weir Group in 2007 for $653 million. The acquisition increased Weir's presence in the rapidly growing upstream oil and gas market, especially in the high-demand well stimulation segment.
