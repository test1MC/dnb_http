Funai Electric is in the business of digital transition. The company manufactures LCD TVs, DVD and Blu-ray disc players, computer printers, and other audiovisual gear. Its electronics are sold under six of its own and licensed brands, including Emerson , Funai, Magnavox, and Philips , and sold by major US retailers, including Wal-Mart and Sears . The company, which has manufacturing facilities in the Asia/Pacific region, primarily serves the Asian and North American markets. It operates US-based sales subsidiary, Funai Corporation. The company was founded in 1951 as Funai Sewing Machines Co. by executive chairman Tetsuro Funaw.

Japan is the Osaka-based company's largest market, accounting for nearly 40% of its annual sales. Other Asian countries and North America each contribute about 30% of sales.

Audio visual equipment, including LCD TVs and DVD and Blu-ray disc-related products, accounts for about 80% of Funai's sales. Inkjet and laser printers and ink cartridges account for about 5% of sales. The company also makes headphones, speakers, and antennas, which represents the remainder. Funai Electric as corporate, production, sales,and development operations in Japan, numerous production facilities in other Asian nations including China (Hong Kong, Guangdong), the Philippines, and Thailand, and beyond Asia in Europe (Germany, and Poland), the US, and Mexico. The company's P&F USA sales subsidiary in located in Alpharetta, Georgia.

Wal-Mart Stores , the world's largest retailer, is Funai Electric's largest customer, accounting for about 38% of its annual revenue in fiscal 2013 (ended March), down from 43% the prior year.

The company's fiscal 2013 (ended March) sales topped $2 billion. a 22% decline versus the prior year. Indeed, Funai has posted sales declines in each of the past three years, with sales down 39% since fiscal 2010. On a regional basis, Funai's sales fell in Japan and the rest of Asia, and in Europe (a tiny market for the company), partially offset by an increase in sales in North America. Strong sales of attractively-priced LCD TVs and sales of Philips-brand audio and accessory products (beginning in September 2012), contributed to sales growth in the North American market. Adverse economic conditions in Japan, Funai's largest market, and the transition to digital terrestrial broadcasting there depressed demand for LCD TVs and Blu-ray products.

Also, the company has been unprofitable in six of the past seven years, posting a net loss of $90.9 million in fiscal 2013. Fierce competition and falling TV prices have hurt the company's bottom line.

Weakness at home, has Funai looking to emerging markets for growth. To that end, the company recently established a sales subsidiary in India and is looking to growth sales in Mexico and elsewhere in Latin America. Thailand is another important new market for the consumer electronics maker. The firm is also looking to recent acquisitions for spur growth. However, a deal struck in 2013 between Philips and Funai under which Funai had agreed to acquire the audio, video, multimedia, and accessories business of Philips Consumer Lifestyle (PCL), for $202 million fell through because of a breach on contract.

In 2013, the company bought the inkjet printing assets of Lexmark International for about $100 million in cash, adding a production facility in the Philippines.
