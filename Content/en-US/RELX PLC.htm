RELX PLC (formerly Reed Elsevier PLC) traces its roots to a paper manufacturing operation founded at the beginning of the 20th century, but today the company operates through RELX Group plc , of which it owns over 50%. RELX Group plc is a publisher of business, legal, and scientific information. The Netherlands-based RELX NV owns the other half of RELX Group plc. In addition, RELX PLC and RELX NV share ownership of Elsevier Reed Finance, the financing arm for RELX Group plc. (RELX PLC owns 39% of the financing business, while RELX NV owns the rest.)

RELX plc operates in four major market segments: Scientific, Technical & Medical; Risk & Business Analytics; Legal; and Exhibitions.

Its Scientific, Technical & Medical division (35% of revenue) serves up scientific, technical and medical journals such as Cell , The Lancet , and Gray's Anatomy. The Risk & Business Analytics arm (more than 25% of revenue) provides publications such as New Scientist , and credit risk for the banking and agriculture industries, among others. The Legal division (around 25% of revenue) operates through LexisNexis Legal & Professional and provides journals and legal information for lawyers. Exhibitions (15% of revenue) puts together upwards of 500 exhibitions a year that together bring in more than 7 million attendees.

The group serves customers in more than 180 countries, and has offices in about four. Roughly half its workforce is based in the US, which accounts for around 55% of total sales. Outside the US, sales are geographically diversified, with the second largest market, the UK, responsible for some 8%.

In 2015, sales increased 3% on prior year to £6.0 billion due to positive performances in the Scientific, Technical & Medical; Risk & Business Analytics; and Legal segments, countered to an extent by a drop in Exhibition revenue. The positive segment performances was mainly down to good growth in electronic and face-to-face revenue, offset by a decline in print. The strongest growth was in Risk & Business Analytics, up 7%.

Net income was up 6% to £1.0 billion due to higher net sales. Cash from operating activities likewise rose, by 2% to £1.9 billion.

RELX is working add value to its core information services by increasing its analytics capabilities. Examples include the development of tools that can help lawyers predict the outcome of cases before they even go to trial; a cornerstone of this is the acquisition of Lex Machina Inc. a Silicon Valley-based predictive legal analytics platform that helps develop litigation strategies.

In 2015, Relx acquired Lex Machina Inc., a Silicon Valley-based predictive legal analytics platform that helps develop litigation strategies. The platform mines, tags, categorizes and enhances millions of court dockets, and help legal practitioners track records and key decisions made by judges.

Also in 2015, the Group acquired Korbitec from the Naspers Group, enhancing LexisNexis' conveyancing capabilities in the South African legal market. Its leading products are GhostConvey and Windeed, and it has offices in South Africa, as well as one in East London.
