Vertellus Specialties manufactures chemicals for the agricultural, nutritional, pharmaceutical, personal care, and performance material markets. The Wind Point Partners portfolio company is a leading global producer of pyridine (used in herbicides and other applications), picolines (used in herbicides, fungicides, and insecticides), and Vitamin B-3. Vertellus' Specialty Materials operations include the manufacture of DEET (a primary ingredient in mosquito repellent), and pharmaceutical intermediates, as well as castor oil derivatives and citrate polymer additives for use in plastics, adhesives, coatings, and sealants. The company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in 2016.

Vertellus operates manufacturing plants in Belgium, China, India, Taiwan, the UK, and the US (6 locations).

The company's focus is on producing fine chemicals and intermediates for chemistries, and on systems and additives to enhance the physical properties of its customer's formulations. Its operating units include Agriculture, Coatings, Inks and Imaging, Industrial Specialties, Nutrition, Personal Care, Pharmaceutical & Medical, Polymers & Plastics, and Sealants and Adhesives.

Vertellus produces a wide range of industrial specialties, including alkanes that can be used as solvents or phase change materials (PCMs), substances that melt and solidify at a certain temperature and can store and release large amounts of energy. The company's personal care products also use alkanes, castor oils, DEET, and specialty pyridine derivatives, among others. Vertellus is the leading supplier of castor oil and castor oil derivatives. For personal care items, the products can perform as emollients, emulsifiers, and pigment wetters (to increase viscosity to maintain suspension in products like lip gloss).

Vertellus has split its operations and products according to end markets. Its agriculture unit offers an array of products ranging from building blocks to make herbicides or products for pest control or elimination of bacteria, fungi, or viral compounds. Another group, its coatings, inks, and imaging unit, includes castor oil-based products and citrate esters, used in a wide range of applications. The unit's products can be used for several functions, including as plasticizers, binders, and intermediates. The nutrition unit includes the company's picolinates and B-3 products and is a leading producer of niacinamide (found in proteing foods like milk, eggs, and fish) and niacin (found in beans, peas, and other legumes).

The personal care unit also includes several castor oil and castor oil derivative formulating ingredients and are used in skin and hair care products. Its products range from ammonium benzoate to zinc salicylate. The pharmaceutical and medical unit also includes the company's castor oil derivatives and castor oils, as well as its citric acid esters, picolines, and pyridine. The company's intermediates are used in more than 50 prescription drugs, veterinary medicines and nutritional supplements. The polymers and plastics unit also provides intermediates based on products such as castor oils and castor oil derivatives, citiric acid esters, and vinyl plasticizers. The sealants and adhesives unit include a wide variety of castor oils and derivatives, as well as citric acid esters, and specialty pyridine derivatives.

The company's US government clients include Department of Defense , Department of Energy , Environmental Protection Agency , and General Services Administration . Its commercial and industrial clients include companies in the automotive, chemical, forest products, manufacturing, oil and gas, pharmaceutical, technology and communications, and utility industries.

The company pursues partnerships to leverage its influence and secure additional contracts. In 2013 Vertellus teamed up with Netcordant, to use Netcordant Web Application Optimizer to improve the performance of Vertellus' federal goverment clients' web applications, to help them manage costs and improve productivity.

Boosting its distribution network, in 2013 the company formed Vertellus Specialty Chemicals (a joint venture with Weifang Green Olive Chemical) to expand Vertellus' distribution of pyridine and picolines in China.

To broaden its market capabilities to include pharmaceutical use for Niacin, in 2013, Vertellus' Belgium plant completed capital and other upgrades for the manufacture of Niacin for pharmaceutical use.

That year the company signed a deal with National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration 's Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory, to facilitate sharing of joint research and development.

Vertellus formed a joint venture in India with Vapi Products Industries in 2011 to manufacture and distribute specialty chemicals globally. The venture, named Vertellus Specialty Materials, is Vertellus' first in India, an important region for the company's strategy to expand into emerging markets. Vertellus Specialties, the majority partner, operates the manufacturing plant in India.

Vertellus was formed in 2006 when Arsenal Capital Partners joined together its portfolio companies Reilly Industries and Rutherford Chemicals . The company derives its name from the mashup of the words verte , Middle English for green, and Tellus , the name of the Roman Earth Goddess. Arsenal cashed out its investment in 2007, selling Vertellus to Wind Point Partners.
