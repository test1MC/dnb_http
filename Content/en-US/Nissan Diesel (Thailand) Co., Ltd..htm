Nissan Diesel (Thailand) keeps its home country truckin'. The company -- established as a joint venture between Siam Motors, UD Trucks (formerly Nissan Diesel; Japan), Marubeni Corporation (Japan), and Marubeni Thailand -- manufactures 6- and 10-wheel trucks, tractor trucks, cement mixers, and buses. Now 100% owned by TC Machinery (which acquired control in 2002), Nissan Diesel (Thailand) sells through a network of more than a dozen domestic dealers. Its vehicles are sold under the UD brand name. The company supports its sales efforts with after-sales service offerings and spare parts. Nissan Diesel (Thailand) was established in 1987.
