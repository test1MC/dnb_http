Churchill China is another of the UK's stately Churchills. The firm manufactures and markets china dishes and tableware. Its earthenware, glassware, and bone china items are distributed to the hospitality industry, including hotels, restaurants, and pubs in the UK and worldwide. Churchill China's products are also sold through home goods wholesalers and independent and mass retailers. Its portfolio of products includes dinnerware, mugs, and glassware. The china maker, which has been transitioning its business away from low-margin volume channels, has a license to make Jamie Oliver Ceramics for mass merchants. Founded in 1795 as Sampson Bridgwood, the company first floated on the public stock exchange in 1994.
