This manufacturing company's products are not really as light as a feather, but Featherlite aluminum trailers are a lot lighter than those made of steel by some other suppliers. The Universal Trailer Holdings' company offers a full line of standard and specialty trailer models. The company provides cargo utility and flatbed trailers, passenger trams, truck beds, kitchen and concession trailers, and aluminum cargo trailers. Featherlite's trailers are used to haul horses, racecars, livestock, advertising displays, construction equipment, ATVs, and motorcycles. It sells its consumer products through dealers.

Featherlite sells trailers throughout Canada and the US through a network of 180 dealers; it also has dealers in Australia and Europe.

Featherlite builds all-aluminum trailers for a spectrum of uses. Its consumer trailers include horse, livestock, car, recreation (ATV, motorcycle and snowmobile) and commercial/utility trailers. It also builds specialty trailers (includinge 53-foot racecar transporters, event marketing and display trailers, hospitality trailers, office trailers, classroom trailers, kitchen and vending, medical trailers, mobile command centers, and museum trailers.

In North America Featherlite's sells its consumer trailers through a dealer network. Featherlite's specialty trailers are sold directly from its specialty sales office at its corporate headquarters. the company's distributors include Allan-Dale Industries, Boespflug Trailers, and Roeder Brothers, Inc.

Customers include companies and organizations and major motorsports drivers and teams.

The company is targeting niche markets, including ice fishing. It has designed a trailer, outfitted with beds, a kitchenette, bath, and flat screen TV, which is lowered to the ice by hydraulic lifts. Featherlite's Mod Pod transforms from a trailer to a camper and is suitable for rough road travel.

In 2013 Featherlite extended its long term partnership with NASCAR ensuring that Featherlite will be the Official Trailer of NASCAR through 2018. About 90% of NASCAR Sprint Cup Series and NASCAR Nationwide Series teams rely on Featherlite transporters to move equipment to every racing location. (Featherlite has worked with NASCAR since 1993.)

In 2012 the company launched Featherlite's Model 1570, a new fuel trailer designed to make farming operations run more efficiently; it features two fuel tank sizes, 550 or 760 gallons, and has options to accommodate larger fuel tanks as well.

Fellow trailer maker Universal Trailer bought Featherlite for $29 million in cash and the assumption of $109 million in debt late in 2006.

Featherlite was founded in 1973.
