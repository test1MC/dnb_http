Making television audiences laugh is deadly serious to Comedy Partners. The subsidiary of MTV Networks operates cable network Comedy Central, the home of such hits as The Daily Show with Jon Stewart , The Colbert Report , RENO 911! , and South Park. The channel, which reaches approximately 98 million US homes, also broadcasts feature film comedies, stand-up comedy specials, and syndicated sitcoms (such as Scrubs ). In addition to its TV network, Comedy Partners releases its shows on DVD through a distribution deal with Paramount , and broadcasts multimedia content through its various websites that are anchored by flagship site ComedyCentral.com. MTV Networks is the cable entertainment arm of media giant Viacom .

Comedy Central has been riding high on the success of its back-to-back faux news shows, The Daily Show and The Colbert Report . Both programs continue to draw big audiences, an they have also attracted critical acclaim in the form of Emmy nominations and awards. While its other original programming has lagged in terms of audiences, the network continues to produce new shows in the hopes of catching lighting in a bottle again.

Meanwhile, Comedy Partners has been expanding its use of digital media to reach more of its target audience, including its website and Apple 's iTunes music and video store. The company broadcasts clips and recently-aired episodes of The Daily Show and The Colbert Report via its fan websites (TheDailyShow.com and ColbertNation.com, respectively). Its ComedyCentral.com website averages about 65 million video streams per month.

Comedy Central was formed in 1991 as CTV: The Comedy Network following the merger of The Comedy Channel (launched by Time Warner in 1989) and HA! (created by Viacom in 1990). Time Warner sold its 50% stake in Comedy Partners to Viacom in 2003.
