CQ-Roll Call examines the issues on the Hill, but leaves the yea- and nay-saying to its readers. The company publishes the nonpartisan newspaper Roll Call , which covers a variety of Congressional topics, including policy making, legislation voting, elections, and investigations. The publication is written for lawmakers, lobbyists, and others in DC politics; it also covers lighter topics such as DC area entertainment, features on lawmakers, and even the Capitol Hill softball league. Other CQ-Roll Call titles include CQ , Gallery Watch , CongressNow , and Capitol Advantage. Founded as Roll Call in 1955 by former Capitol Hill press secretary Sid Yudain, today CQ-Roll Call is owned by The Economist Group .

The company offers its Roll Call (and other publications) on a subscription basis and through its website. Roll Call is published Monday-Thursday while Congress is in session and once a week otherwise. The Economist bought the paper in 1992.

To stay ahead of its competitors in the industry, CQ-Roll Call constantly works to broaden its online content. In 2013 its expanded its online coverage with the launch of five new political blogs authored by notable journalists featuring Mort Kondracke, Stuart Rothenberg, Taegan Goddard, David Hawkings, and David Drucker. Since re-launching RollCall.com, the site has experienced a 67% increase in unique visitors, compared to the same period the previous year.

Also in 2012 company launched the CQRC Engage, its new advocacy platform. Recognizing the evolving advocacy landscape and the need to reach audiences with greater efficiencies, CQRC Engage provides tools that help organizations affect change and move advocacy forward.

Also using acquisitions to grow, CQ-Roll Call in 2011 purchased Illumen, a provider of tools for organizations seeking to engage their constituents. Illumen's technologies included complex issue monitoring tools, customized dynamic content delivery from key media sources, digital advocacy networks, and targeting technology that allow organizations to inform and engage their audiences. With the addition of Illumen, CQ Roll Call greatly enhanced the offerings of both its flagship legislative tracking services and its advocacy tools.
