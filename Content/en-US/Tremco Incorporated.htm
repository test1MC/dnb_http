Got a leak in your roof, deck, wall, floor, bathroom, or just about anywhere else? Tremco's bound to have a product that can help you out. The company, a subsidiary within RPM International 's Building Solutions group (a part of the industrial segment of the parent company), manufactures roofing materials and services, as well as industrial coatings and sealants. Its operating subsidiaries include Tremco Roofing & Building Maintenance (general contracting and services); Tremco Commercial Sealants & Waterproofing (sealant, weatherproofing, and passive fire control applications); and Tremco Barrier Solutions ( moisture, thermal, vapor and air barrier systems).

Among Tremco's other subsidiaries are Weatherproofing Technologies (roofing and general contracting services); Republic Powdered Metals (restoration services for roofs and walls); and Canam Building Envelope (air barrier products).

The company has manufacturing plants in Canada, the UK, and the US. It sells its products throughout North America.

Tremco sells its products directly to contractors, distributors, and other end-users (including industrial manufacturing plants, other commercial customers, and public institutions).

RPM International reported that its industrial segment sales (including Tremco) increased by 5% in fiscal 2014 as the result of organic growth and acquisitions, offset by the impact of unfavorable foreign exchange rates and organic decline. Contributing to the year-over-year growth in net sales in the industrial segment was improved performance by most of its European businesses. The industrial segment contributed about 63% of the parent company's total revenues that year.

Tremco is part of the growing US paint and coatings segment of the building industry that includes some 1,100 companies with combined annual sales of $20 billion. The industry is dominated by a few large companies, but many smaller companies like Tremco are able to carve out a niche, such as roofing materials and sealants, where it can operate.

The company was founded in 1928 when William Treuhaft opened a small roofing materials manufacturing plant in Cleveland, Ohio.
