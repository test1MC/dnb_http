JPS Industries' glass and plastic products can be found surfing the waves and saving lives. Its JPS Composite Materials arm makes high-strength fiberglass and synthetic fabrics for the aerospace, military, electrical, construction, and other industries. The products are used in myriad applications, including body armor, insulation, circuit boards, and even surfboards. JPS Industries also operates as JPS Elastomerics, which includes Stevens Urethane, a maker of polyurethane film, sheet, and tubing, used to make an array of goods, such as athletic shoes, medical devices, and scuba equipment. JPS Elastomerics' traces back to the 1863 founding of predecessor Easthampton Rubber Thread.

Several of JPS Composite Materials' fiberglass and substrate businesses were acquired from rival Hexcel Corporation in 2007. Reinventing itself in 2008, JPS narrowed its business operations and sold off Stevens Geomembranes and Stevens Roofing to Dow Building Solutions, a part of The Dow Chemical Company .
