Kyocera International, the holding company for the North American operations of Japan's Kyocera Corporation , makes ceramic products (the "cera" in Kyocera) ranging from mobile phones to kitchen utensils. Having expanded through acquisitions beyond its origins in specialty ceramics, Kyocera International also makes LCD panels, thin-film devices, printers, industrial cutting tools, and solar energy systems. Its Kyocera Communications unit provides sales, marketing, and customer support for Kyocera and SANYO brand wireless phones in the US. Kyocera International's Kyocera Solar subsidiary is one of the world's largest producers of photovoltaic solar cells.

Although Kyocera International serves North America for a larger global group, the division has, itself, operations outside of the region. Subsidiary Kyocera Document Solutions America (formerly Kyocera Mita America) has locations not only in Canada and Mexico, but also Brazil; Kyocera Solar has subsidiaries in Brazil and Australia; and Kyocera TYCOM has subsidiaries in China and Hong Kong, in addition to its Costa Mesa, California headquarters and Canadian location. Tijuana-based Kyocera Mexicana makes solar and ceramic packaging and displays.

Parent Kyocera Corporation only reports sales for the US, lumping the rest of North America into an "others" segment. Both geographic segments saw declining sales for fiscal 2012 (ended March). In 2013 the company introduced its new Consumer Products Division, a new business unit combining the strengths of the Application Products Division of Kyocera Tycom Corporation (KTC) and Kyocera Industrial Ceramics Corporation (KICC). The new division will try to facilitate the expansion of Kyocera's North American ceramic cutlery and kitchenware business. The combined enterprise will streamline operations.

Kyocera Solar sells directly and through distributors; its photovoltaic solar cells are used in consumer residential, government, railroad, communications, rural development, and other industrial applications.
