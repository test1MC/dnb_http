Royal Cup Coffee is enjoying a hot cup o' robust business. Founded in 1896, the coffee and tea importer roasts and distributes its products to consumers and commercial customers, including the foodservice industry. Typical customers include offices, convenience stores, resorts, and restaurants such as Chick-fil-A , Red Lobster , The Ritz-Carlton , Hilton Hotels , and Waffle House . Royal Cup Coffee, which also boasts Rainforest Alliance Certified coffees, imports coffee and tea sourced globally and distributes products throughout North America, as well as to the Caribbean Islands. It is owned and managed by the family of chairman Bill Smith, which bought the company from the founding Batterton family in 1951.

Royal Cup Coffee offers the training and equipment for selling its beverages from carts and kiosks. In addition to coffee and tea, the company's food service operation offers biscuit and pie mixes, syrups, gravy, jellies, sugar, sweeteners, creamers, and juices.

Its coffee division designs, implements, and maintains office break room programs on a local and national basis. It also operates a coffee truck available for special events.

From its headquarters in Birmingham, Alabama, Royal Cup Coffee sources its coffees and teas worldwide and serves food service, office, and specialty coffee markets in the US, Mexico, and the Caribbean.

Its core office coffee service markets include the Alabama cities of Birmingham, Huntsville, Mobile, and Montgomery, as well as Denver, Colorado.

As part of its marketing strategy, Royal Cup Coffee was one of the first North American roasters to acquire farms that are Rainforest Alliance Certified. Customers who serve these environmentally-friendly blends include Holiday Inn, the National Parks Division of Delaware North , Fairmont Hotels and Resorts, and The Breakers Hotel in Palm Beach, Florida. Royal Cup Coffee typically roasts more than 1.5 million pounds of Rainforest Alliance Certified coffees each year.

Royal Cup Coffee is in growth mode. In 2013 the company began a $30 million expansion at its Birmingham, Alabama, corporate campus. The move involved purchasing 16 acres and a 153,000-sq.-ft. industrial building adjacent to its operations.

In 2014 the company joined the food truck craze and launched its mobile cafe for special events.
