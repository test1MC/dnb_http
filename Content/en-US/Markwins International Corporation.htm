Markwins International believes beauty comes in all shades and ages. It makes and markets cosmetics and personal care products, including lipstick, nail polish, and bath collections, for tweens and adults. Its ethnic-designed cosmetic lines include Black Radiance (for African-Americans) and Tropez (for Hispanic and Asian women). Markwins also makes the value-priced Wet 'n' Wild cosmetics, and packages beauty kits under The Color Workshop and Spa Institute labels. Through partnership agreements, the company produces cosmetics under licensed brands. Markwins, which owns Physicians Formula Holdings , is buying the Bonne Bell and Lip Smacker brands from Aspire Brands.

Markwins boasts sales and distribution offices in Canada, Brazil, Italy, and Korea. Besides its operations in the US, Markwins has production facilities in China. It exports its products to customers in North America, Germany, the UK, Italy, and Spain.

Based in City of Industry, California, the personal care products company sells its products in more than 60 countries, spanning half a dozen continents. Markwins operates facilities in the US and China.

Markwins sells its products worldwide through more than 42,000 retail outlets, such as mass merchants, drug and food stores, and department stores. As part of its focus, the company serves a variety of consumers, including all ages, ethnicities, and skin tones. Its larger customers include the likes of Kmart, Wal-Mart, and Target.

Markwins has grown through successful contracts with global suppliers, as well as international licensing deals. Markwins' licensed introductions include a line of Calvin Klein -branded Beauty cosmetic collections in Asia, and the CK fragrance in Spain. It also extended a deal to distribute gift sets in Asia and Europe based on Disney's movies and television programs, Hannah Montana, High School Musical, Snow White, and Cinderella .

In early 2015 it announced it was acquiring the Bonne Bell and Lipsmacker products from Norwegian health and beauty company ASPIRE Brands AS. Bonne Bell started out offering flavored lip gloss to tweens but now sells lip, face, and eye makeup while leaving the lip gloss, in flavors like buttered popcorn and Thin Mint Girl Scout Cookie, to Lipsmacker. Besting a $65-million offer made by Swander Pace Capital in August 2012 to take Physicians Formula Holdings (PFH) private, Markwins acquired the prestige makeup and skin care company for $4.90 per share, or nearly $75 million. (For bowing out, PFH was required to pay Swander Pace some $1.3 million.) PFH is a maker of cosmetics and skin care products for the mass market and counts Wal-Mart, Target, and CVS among its customers. While Markwins and PFH share similar sales channels, they have non-competing brands and attract new customers as part of their alliance.
