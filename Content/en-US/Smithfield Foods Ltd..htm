The world's largest pork producer Smithfield Foods has the chops to operate in the UK through Smithfield Foods Ltd. The UK subsidiary is part of Smithfield Foods' international meat processing and distribution business. Smithfield Foods Ltd. supplies a variety of branded and private label packaged foods, such as cooked hams, sausages, bacon, hot dogs, and canned meats, and some fresh pork, poultry, and beef products. Its offerings include Fiorucci Italian meats, owned by Campofrio , a minority interest of Smithfield Foods. Customers are UK supermarket chains, as well as wholesale distributors, food processors, and foodservice operators (fast food restaurants and hotel chains, hospitals, and other institutions).

The company operates throughout the UK.

Smithfield Foods Ltd. works in all sales channels and market segments with a broad portfolio of branded and private-label meat products, providing everything from traditional deli meats to more modern, convenient foods.

It offers tailored solutions for the foodservice and manufacturing sectors, working in partnership with its customers.

Smithfield Foods Ltd is part of international segment of Smithfield Foods, Inc. The segment accounted for 10% of total net sales of the parent company in fiscal 2016.

Smithfield Foods Ltd. is the result of its parent's Smithfield Foods, Inc.'s acquisition of UK-based meat company Norwich Food and Polish company Ridpath PEK in 2004. Norwich supplied fresh meat, poultry, and game to retailers, wholesalers, food processors, and caterers; Ridpath distributed PEK and other brand name canned and deli meats.

Combined as Smithfield Foods Ltd., the deal enabled Smithfield Foods to target the entire UK food supply chain with a domestic presence. The UK subsidiary benefits from Smithfield Foods' well-known brand name and deep pockets, particularly a supply relationship with Polish and Romanian hog farms, and a minority interest in one of Europe's largest packaged meat companies, Campofrio. In 2006 Smithfield Foods Ltd. went on to take over Sara Lee 's European meats business, which strengthened its portfolio of deli and processed meats.
