Special Devices aims to feel, well, special among the leading US makers of initiators (pyrotechnic devices) -- used primarily in automotive airbag systems. The airbag initiators activate inflators that enable the airbag to be deployed. The company also makes micro gas generators that remove seat belt slack in the event of a collision. Other products include bolts, nuts, fire suppression systems, cable cutters, pin pullers and pin pushers, propelling cartridges, and other engineered energetic devices. Special Devices sells its products primarily to airbag manufacturers, including Autoliv and TRW Automotive . It is a subsidiary of Japan's Daicel Corporation .

SDI has operations in the US. It is also established in Japan and Thailand.

SDI offers pyrotechnic products to the government, primes, and subcontractors in the US. In addition, the company serves automotive markets. Its products are used in industrial applications such as explosive bolts and nuts, gas generators, pyrotechnic electrical disconnects, fire suppression systems, and cable cutters.

The company operates as a part of the Pyrotechnic devices segment of its parent. The segment contributed about 18% of Daicel's net revenues in 2013 as Daicel experienced a 24% rise in sales for this segment due to the acquisition of SDI.
