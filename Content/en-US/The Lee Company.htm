Miniaturization is hardly a problem for The Lee Company. The company designs and manufactures microhydraulic fluid control components, such as plugs, restrictors, nozzles, valves, pumps, and tubing. It also makes electro-fluidic systems, including control valves, MINSTAC (miniature inert system of tubing and connecters), and plugs. The Lee Company's industrial microhydraulics lineup includes items such as betaplugs, check valves, and safety screens. Its products are sold to a diverse marketplace, from aerospace OEMs to Formula 1 car racing and companies in the instrumentation, medical, oil exploration, and machine tool industries.

Headquartered in Westbrook, Connecticut, Lee operates in six states in the US, with four manufacturing sites of over 700,000 sq. ft. in Connecticut; it serves international customers through offices in France, Italy, Germany, the UK, and Sweden. It has distributors in Europe, Australia, Asia, South America, and Middle East.

Lee also has wholly owned sales and service subsidiaries in London (Gerrards Cross), Frankfurt, Paris (Voisins-Le-Bretonneux), Milan and Stockholm, and is represented in over forty countries.

The company sells and markets its products through representatives worldwide and caters to the aerospace, down-hole oil tools, machine tools, ink-jet printing, auto racing, oil exploration, and automotive and off-highway equipment industries.

Aviation engineer Leighton Lee II established The Lee Company in 1948. The company initially focused on making miniature parts to meet the needs of the nascent jet aircraft industry.
