Houstonian Stewart & Stevenson helps its customers quench their thirst for Texas Tea. The company is a leading supplier of equipment used in oilfield services. Stewart & Stevenson operates three segments: Equipment (oil well stimulation, coil tubing, engines, and material handling equipment); Aftermarket Parts and Service (parts and service for customers in oil and gas, marine, power generation, mining, and construction industries); and Rental (rental of generators, material handling equipment, and air compressors). While the company primarily sells and markets its products directly through its own sales and service centers, it also uses authorized dealers and independent overseas sales representatives.

Stewart & Stevenson operates in Canada, China, Russia, South America, and the US. It has 600 service bays in the US, Canada, and in other countries and also provides aftermarket parts through a network of more than 150 authorized dealers.

The company manufacturing segment designs, makes, and markets equipment for oilfield service providers, oil companies, drilling, and workover contractors. Its products include pumping, acidizing, coiled tubing, cementing and nitrogen units, drilling rigs and workover rigs, power generation systems and electrical support and distribution systems. Stewart & Stevenson partners with leading manufacturers, including MTU, Detroit Diesel , Electro-Motive Diesel , Deutz , Allison , and Hyster , to market diesel and natural gas engines, material handling equipment, and transmissions. It has five main manufacturing facilities across North America, comprising more than 600,000 square fee of space.

The distribution segment provides stand-alone products and aftermarket parts and service for products manufactured by Stewart & Stevenson and other OEMs. It also distributes Hyster, Manitou and Cushman material handling equipment, as well as Stewart & Stevenson's patented Rail King railcar.

Stewart & Stevenson's diverse base of more than 2,000 customers includes major oil and gas service companies, integrated and national oil companies, drilling contractors, as well as clients in the construction, marine, mining, and power generation industries. It sells directly to customers through sales and service centers, as well as through an authorized dealer network.

Demand for the company's products are driven by capital spending in the oil and gas industry. A booming oil industry in the last few years has lifted Stewart & Stevenson's prospects for growth.

In 2014 the company was appointed as a US distributor for Atlas Copco 's full line of generators, portable air compressors, boosters, and associated equipment.

In 2012 it opened a remanufacturing center to support to customers involved in industrial and oilfield operations. The 220,000 square foot facility has the capability to rebuild and remanufacture most engine, transmission, and oilfield platform configurations, as well as assemble new units, manufacture skid units and upgrade unit controls and designated components. Equipment includes fracturing pumpers, blenders, hydration units, pump trucks, tractors, and trailers.

Growing its portfolio, in 2011 the company acquired US-based diesel engine marketer and distributor EMDSI - Hunt Power from ITOCHU for an undisclosed price. Based in Harvey, Louisiana, EMDSI markets and distributes medium speed diesel engines for marine propulsion, drilling, and power generation applications and is positioned to capitalize on growth opportunities in the US Gulf Coast, Central and South America and the Pacific Rim.

In 2011 Stewart & Stevenson filed an IPO, looking to raise $250 million to pay down debt, but never completed the offer due to poor financial market conditions.

The company was founded in 1902.
