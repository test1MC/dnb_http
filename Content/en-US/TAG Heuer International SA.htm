TAG Heuer charges a lot for its time. TAG Heuer International, operating through its Swiss-based TAG Heuer SA unit, designs and creates men's and women's prestige sports watches and chronographs (timepieces with stopwatch features). Some collection names include Carrera, Formula 1, Link, and Monaco. Long aligned with the sports world (its ancestor, Heuer, began keeping official time for the Paris Olympics in 1924), TAG Heuer promotes its upscale timepieces and other products through endorsements by athletes and celebrities and via sporting event sponsorships. French luxury goods company LVMH , which makes and markets several other high-end lines, owns TAG Heuer.

TAG Heuer is headquartered in Switzerland and has an international presence in 120 countries and 150 customer service centers.

The company sells online and through a network of stores and boutiques.

The company is a large part of LVMH's Watches & Jewelry segment, which contributes 9% to the parent company's revenue.

The company's strategy is based on introducing new products. New releases in 2015 were added to the Formula 1, Aquaracer and Carrera lines. It also developed a luxury smartwatch, the first of its kind, in association with Google and Intel (TAG Heuer provided the design, Google the software, and Intel the innards), the TAG Heuer Connected Watch.
