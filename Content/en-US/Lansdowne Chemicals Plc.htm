Hands down, Lansdowne Chemicals is the largest consumer of hydrazine hydrate in the UK. The specialty chemicals distributor and producer is also a market leader in diluting the product, a key raw material used by power stations to remove dissolved oxygen from boiler water. Hydrazine hydrate is also used to produce pharmaceutical chemicals, plastic additives, herbicides and fungicides. The company's aroma unit is a leading supplier to the European aroma industry, and its nutrition unit supplies essential additives for the feed and food industries.

Based in the UK, the company also has locations in the Netherlands, the UAE, China, and the US.

Lansdowne serves the animal nutrition industry as well as the flavor and fragance industries.

The company's manufacturing facilities in the UK have been producing specialized nitrated reagents since 1993. It also has one of Europe's most modern facilities for dilution of hydrazone hydrate.

Its product Floculan is a replacement for traditional coagulating agents in the food processing and oil industries. The company also produces a polymer-based water treatment line, Lanflock, and the brand Mercaptazine for metal recovery.

The Lansdowne Nutrition unit supplies essential additives for the feed and food industries for animals and humans. Its product lists include amino acids and vitamins.

Lansdowne serves customers in a wide range of industries, including animal nutrition, aromatics, base chemicals, personal care; and water treatment.

Through Lansdowne's business divisions the company tailors products and services to better support and service its customers' requirements. This structure also allows the company to focus its marketing campaigns more effectively to certain industries.

In 2014 the company introduced an enterprise resource planning system (a business process management software that allows an organization to use a system of integrated applications to manage the business and automate many back office functions related to technology, services, and human resources).

Lansdowne was formed in 1977 to provide an Asia-based source of specialty and aroma chemicals to the UK.

The company expanded its marketing reach in 2004 with the establishment of Lansdowne Chemicals Europe in the Netherlands.

The Lansdowne Aquatec division was formed in 2008 to further develop the company's vegetable-based biodegrable coagulant/flocculant (agents that separate suspended solids) in the UK market.
