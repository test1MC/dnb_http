Sling Media has a cure for anyone who suffers from separation anxiety when they're away from television too long. The company's Slingbox allows viewers to watch their home televisions from a computer or mobile device. The Slingbox, which looks like any set-top box, is compatible with cable and satellite broadcast services, as well as digital video recorders. For mobile devices, the SlingPlayer Mobile is compatible with Apple , Blackberry , and Android devices. Sling Media products are sold by major retailers such as Amazon , Best Buy , and Wal-Mart . The company is a subsidiary of satellite products and services provider EchoStar .

Sling Media made quite a splash when it debuted the Slingbox in 2004, but it is still working to make its devices as commonplace as the TiVo in the market. Of course, parent EchoStar offers an HD DVR integrated with Sling Media technology for DISH Network customers. (EchoStar is a former subsidiary of DISH).

Based in California, the company runs a research and development center in Bangalore, India.

The Slingbox is distributed directly to customers in more than 5,000 retail stores in 20 countries. Commercially, ARRIS Group is its exclusive distributor to cable, telecom and wireless service providers in North America, Europe and Asia.

EchoStar bought Sling Media for $380 million in 2007.
