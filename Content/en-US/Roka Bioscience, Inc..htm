It's not "rocket science", it's molecular diagnostics. Roka Bioscience provides molecular technology used to detect foodborne pathogens. Its main product is its Atlas Detection Assays and Atlas automated instrument, which caters to customers in the North American food safety testing market and helps customers reduce labor costs and alleviate operating errors. The company offers molecular tests for Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli, Shiga toxin-producing E. coli, and Listeria monocytogenes. Roka Bioscience was founded in 2009 through the acquisition of certain industrial application market assets belonging to Gen-Probe . In mid-2014 the company launched an IPO.

Roka Bioscience launched an IPO in the summer of 2014 and raised $60 million. Of this total, the company plans to use $24 million for the commercialization of its Atlas Detection Assays and $16 million for research and development activities. About $14 million will be paid toward a license agreement it has with Gen-Probe .

Roka Bioscience's revenues skyrocketed from $105,000 in 2012 to $2.2 million in 2013 as it sold 205,000 Atlas Detection Assays compared to 8,000 the year before. The company posted net losses of $23 million in 2012 and $39 million in 2013.

The company's strategy is centered around the commercialization of its Atlas Detection Assays for foodborne pathogens in North America. It is also focused on driving its innovation in foodborne pathogen testing beyond its current portfolio of assays and expanding beyond foodborne pathogen tests. To achieve this, Roka Bioscience is looking to extend its reach into global markets through a market-specific, hybrid model of direct sales and strategic partnerships with established food safety companies.
