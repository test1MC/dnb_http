Medegen served the medical industry through a thin, clear tube. Its Maximus Medical unit was a leading manufacturer of specialty intravenous (IV) therapy products and devices. Its needleless IV products featured a valve technology to help prevent bloodstream infections. The company's custom molding and tooling operations provided contract manufacturing of products for medical device makers and pharmaceutical companies. It also manufactured and marketed its KippMed line of proprietary components to IV device makers and suppliers. Medegen had manufacturing operations in California and Tijuana, Mexico. It was acquired by medical products maker CareFusion in 2010 for $225 million.
