Mission NewEnergy is on a mission to grow the feedstock that supplies its biodiesel. Under contracts with farmers in India, the Australia-based company cultivates more than 194,000 acres of Jatropha Curcas, an nonedible oilseed tree that is used to make biodiesel. Through refineries in Malaysia, it also has the capacity to refine the feedstock into more than 105 million gallons (or 2.6 million barrels) of biodiesel per year. Some palm oil is also used in the production of its biodiesel. Mission NewEnergy distributes its biodiesel in Europe and is launching US operations. It is sold as a cleaner alternative to pure petroleum-based diesel, commonly used as transportation fuel.
