One inside job after another adds up as an honest day's work at Trim Masters. The company makes and supplies automotive interior door panels and seat assemblies for Toyota vehicles. It uses vacuum forming and injection molding to produce trim; advanced cutting methods to make trim-cover assemblies of leather, cloth, or vinyl; and metal fabrication and welding to build its seats. The company was formed in 1987 as a joint venture, 49%-owned by Johnson Controls (JCI), and 51%-owned by Toyota Boshoku and Toyota Tsusho . Boshoku and JCI phased out the JV in 2008, with Boshoku acquiring five of the 11 plants and JCI taking two of the production bases. Trim Masters retains three facilities in the US and one in Mexico.
