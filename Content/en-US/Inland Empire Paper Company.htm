On paper, this "empire" is inland from the Pacific Northwest coast. Founded in 1911, Inland Empire Paper Company (IEP) produces more than a hundred tons of newsprint daily from its Millwood, Washington, facility. A forestry department oversees the 115,000 acres of timberland owned by IEP in Washington and Idaho. This unit provides logs for area sawmills, purchases their chips for Inland Empire's paper mill, as well as replants acreage. Recycled paper, sourced from municipal recyclers, accounts for about 40% of the material used in IEP paper production. A fiber reclaim-unit handles recycling and transportation. Cowles Publishing Company owns IEP.

The company is based in Spokane, Washington, and has a presence located in nine geographic units that extend from St. Maries, Idaho, in the south to Northport, Washington, in the north. Its largest contiguous block spans the Mt. Spokane, Twin Lakes, and Spirit Lake area, consisting of nearly 60,000 acres.
