Avery Weigh-Tronix puts the whole weight of its being into its work. The company makes a full slate of industrial and retail weighing systems, from bench scales to conveyor scales, counting scales, floor scales, truck scales, and weigh bars. It also produces accessories, such as printers, remote displays, signal processors, and wireless transceivers. Clients come from such sectors as transportation and logistics, as well as public safety. The company operates globally through regional sales offices; much of its revenues come from outside North America. It has about 500 locations worldwide, with about half of those in the US. The company is a subsidiary of Illinois Tool Works (ITW).

The company serves customers in 100 countries. It has operations in Africa, Australia, Canada, China, France, India, Ireland, Latin America, Malaysia, the UK, and the US.

Avery Weigh-Tronix includes a number of the world's best-known weighing companies, including Avery India, Brecknell, Dillon and Railweight. It operates worldwide through a network of wholly owned companies and international dealers and distributors.

The company's clients includes ABCD Farm, Costco , East Kent Hospital Trust, Republic Services Inc. , Snug Harbor Seafoods, and Mikula Custom Combining. It sells through dealers and distributors.

In 2015, the test and measurement and electronics segment of Illinois Tool Works contributed 15% of its revenue of the parent company.

The company grows through organic innovations and acquisitions. In 2013 Avery Weigh-Tronix introduced its latest floor scale, the Stainless Steel ProDec, for environments that require strict sanitation. The new platform scale, based on the company's industry-recognized ProDec model, was designed with the food and chemical industry in mind. The scale is available with capacities ranging from 2,000 lbs to a more heavy-duty 10,000 lbs.

Avery Weigh-Tronix is a part of ITW's Test and Measurement group. Since joining ITW in 2007, Avery Weigh-Tronix has ramped up its portfolio of automated weighing systems. In 2009, for example, the company added a self-service truck scale. The device eliminates the need for an onsite scale attendant, collecting the legal for trade driver and truck weight information, accurately and efficiently. A wireless, trade-approved, scale weighting system for forklifts was also introduced.

Although several of Avery Weigh-Tronix's predecessor companies, such as Avery and Salter, date back to the 18th century, Avery Weigh-Tronix was formed in the 21st through the merger of Weigh-Tronix and Avery Berkel.
