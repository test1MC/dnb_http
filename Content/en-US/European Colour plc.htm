European Colour colored primarily inside the lines of its US subsidiary, EC Pigments USA which produced organic pigments for the printing ink, plastics, and coatings markets. The products of EC Pigments USA were promoted through E. W. Kaufmann and sold globally through a network of distributors. EC Pigments was formed in 2000 when European Colour (Pigments) Limited and Roma Color merged. European Colour divided its business into US and UK operations in 2004. The firm is now winding down its organic pigment businesses. European Colour (Pigments) is closed. EC Pigments USA ceased operations in 2013. European Colour has its roots in the mid-1800s; it once traded on the London Stock Exchange but delisted in 2006.

In 2002 the company exited the coatings business, selling Tor Coatings Ltd., to Deancove Limited.
