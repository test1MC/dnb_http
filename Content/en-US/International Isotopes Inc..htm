Despite its name, International Isotopes is confined to a single US state. The firm operates primarily through subsidiary International Isotopes Idaho, where it makes calibration and measurement equipment used with nuclear imaging cameras. Most of its nuclear imaging products, including dose measurement devices and testing equipment, are made under contract with RadQual, a privately held firm that markets the devices; International Isotopes owns a minority stake in RadQual. The company partnered with RadQual in late 2010 to acquire Technology Imaging Services. The joint venture called TI Services LLC distributes products and services for nuclear medicine, nuclear cardiology, and PET imaging.
