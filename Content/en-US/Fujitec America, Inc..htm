When you're up, you're up, and when you're down, you're down; Fujitec America offers to take you to both places. A subsidiary of Fujitec Co., Ltd. the company designs, develops, manufactures, markets, installs, maintains, and modernizes elevators, escalators, moving walks, vertical parking equipment, and new transportation systems. Its "vertical transportation system" projects have included the installation of transit escalators in the subway systems of Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Washington, DC. Fujitec America operates a 220-ft. elevator research/test tower located near its headquarters in Mason, Ohio.

The company operates more than 10 manufacturing facilities, four R&D centers, and about 50 sales and service offices throughout the world. It has offices in Atlanta, Boston, Calgary, Chicago, Cincinnati, Columbus, Dallas, Houston, Los Angeles, New York, Philadelphia, Seattle, Tampa, Orlando, and Washington, DC.

Fujitec America's products include gearless and geared elevators, machine roomless elevators, and architectural products (including elevator cabs and ceilings, car operating panels, hall indicators, in-car indicator, hall lanterns, hall button stations, and LCD screens). It makes its Millennium geared and gearless elevators for medium to high-rise buildings. Talon brand roomless elevators employ drive pulleys which are connected to the permanent magnet motor, a technology totally different from conventional hoist machines. Fujitec America designed a "smart" elevator for use in Seattle's Metropolitan Park West Tower. The technology lets passengers enter their destination floor, and the system then processes the requested floors and informs waiting passengers which elevator car to enter. It is designed to avoid the wait and rush to get in the next available elevator.

Fujitec America was established in 1977.

Suffering from reduced demand for heavy-duty elevators and escalators, the company scaled back its operations and sold its 180,000 square-foot facility in Lebanon, Ohio. It financed a smaller and more technologically advanced facility in Mason, Ohio, where it took up residence in 2008.
