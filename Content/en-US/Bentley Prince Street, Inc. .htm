Bentley Prince Street rolls out its regal broadloom carpets for the corporate, government, education, health care, hospitality/retail, institutional, and residential markets. Its primary brands include Bentley and Prince Street for its commercial carpets and Prince Street House & Home for residential carpets. The company had been a subsidiary of Interface (the world's largest manufacturer of commercial carpets) until 2012, when Interface sold Bentley Prince Street for about $35 million to an affiliate of  private equity firm Dominus Capital in 2012. Interface is focusing on its modular carpet business.
