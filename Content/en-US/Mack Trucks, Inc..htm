Mack Trucks and its "Built like a Mack" bulldog logo are synonymous with heavy duty trucks. A subsidiary of Sweden's AB Volvo , the company is one of North America's largest manufacturers of heavy duty trucks used in construction, heavy hauling, highway, and refuse work. They are sold and serviced in countries worldwide through a global network of hundreds of sales, parts, and repair centers. Mack Trucks also makes powertrains, an integrated system that consists of engine, transmission, and axles, and offers truck leasing and financing services. The company was founded in 1900 as Mack Brothers Company.

The company's trucks are available in some 45 countries, primarily across the Americas and the EMEA (Europe, Middle East, and Africa) region. In addition to the US (all North American Mack trucks are built in its Macungie, Pennsylvania plant), Mack has production sites in Australia and Venezuela.

The Mack TerraPro model is primarily used as a refuse truck. About 50% of the refuse vehicles used in the US are made by Mack Trucks, giving it a hefty market share. They collect more than 11,000 tons of garbage every day, mostly in metropolitan cities like New York, Boston, and Philadelphia.

The company's products are sold through its network of dealers, and it also provides parts sales through an online platform: Mack Select. It provides vehicle service through more than 500 authorized dealers and a network of service facilities operating more than 5,000 locations.

As the auto market recovers, Mack and sister truckmaker Volvo Trucks North America are trying to capitalize on their more extensively-tested, highly fuel-efficient, EPA -certified products that comply with new emissions standards introduced in North America in 2010. Throughout 2013 the company launched two new models powered by natural gas: Mack Pinnacle and Mack Granite.

Mack has also introduced the mDRIVE automated manual transmission, which was developed to work with Mack's MP engine to deliver better fuel economy, drivability, and safety. In fact, Mack has experienced rapid growth with its mDRIVE transmission, which in 2014 represented nearly 50% of highway vehicle sales of its parent company -- only four years after its introduction.
