DenseLight Semiconductors makes photonic microchips and modules based on indium phosphide (InP), a material that allows for higher performance than silicon. Laser diodes for testing, biomedical imaging components, and specialty lasers are among its products; DenseLight also offers foundry, packaging, and testing services. The company was founded by four former professors from the Nanyang Technological University in Singapore, and began operations in 2000. DenseLight Semiconductors has received more than $31 million in private equity funding from 3i Group and 3i's Asia Pacific Technology Fund.
