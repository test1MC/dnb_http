Silence is golden to Industrial Acoustics Company (IAC). The AEA Investors LP portfolio company makes noise and acoustic control products and soundproof rooms and equipment to muffle the noise of industrial machinery. It sells a line of products designed to suppress noise and to condition the acoustical environment inside enclosed spaces, including absorption systems, recording studios, jet-engine test cells, and sound-control doors. IAC serves the architectural, air-conditioning, industrial,medical, power-generation, production and aviation markets in more than 50 countries throughout the world. The company was acquired by Sound Seal in 2016.

In 2012 AEA Investors bought IAC from MML Capital Partners LLP.

IAC has 15 facilities in 11 countries in Europe, North America, Asia/Pacific, and Australasia.

The company provides a wide range of noise and acoustic control products, structures, and test facilities. Products include multi-engine test cells, intake/exhaust systems for warships and luxury ferries, TV and radio studios, audiology rooms, enclosures for noisy machinery, road barriers, anechoic test rooms, acoustic doors and windows, air handling units, and air conditioning and power plant silencers.

IAC serves the aerospace, aviation, commercial construction, energy, healthcare, industrial engineering, and media industries, among others.

In 2014 IAC teamed up Diagnostic Instruments to develop a portfolio of sound proof cabins, from small standard single occupancy cabins and booths to large custom-built rooms.

Strengthening its position in the North American and Canadian noise control market for the power, oil, gas, marine, transport, pneumatic conveying and industrial vacuum industries, in 2012 the company acquired the Maxim Silex Group (including Maxim Silencers and Silex Innovations), based in Houston and Toronto. Maxim Silex is a leader in making and installing a complete range of industrial silencers and emission control solutions for a wide range of applications, and is a leading top provider of vent silencers and heat recovery systems.

Moving into the North American energy and power market, that year IAC acquired Nebraska-based GT Exhaust, a provider of sound and emissions control solutions to the energy, power, and heavy transportation industries.

In 1998 the company, a subsidiary of IAC Holdings, went private after an unnamed European investor bought its remaining public shares.

Martin Herschorn founded the company in 1949.
