The first step to climbing a mountain is likely to begin with Berghaus. The company is a UK manufacturer of outdoor clothing, footwear, and backpacks, especially high performance mountain- and rock-climbing equipment. Founded in the late 1960s, Berghaus (German for "mountain center") lays claim to being the first UK retailer to import and stock the SCARPA walking boots and to revamp the rucksack (several times); the first European brand to introduce GORE-TEX fabric into its lines, followed by the Yeti Gaiter, and outdoor gear for women. The company operates three flagship stores in the UK, and sells its lines via retailers in Europe and Asia. It is owned by The Pentland Group , which bought Berghaus in 1993.
