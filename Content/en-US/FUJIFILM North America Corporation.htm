Smile, you've been captured by FUJIFILM North America, a sales and marketing subsidiary of Tokyo's FUJIFILM Holdings . Catering to the US and Canada, the business offers cameras, film, printers, and other photographic products and services (such as printing and sharing services for digital pictures). Its customer base includes consumers, professional photographers, cinematographers, and retail photofinishers and processing labs. FUJIFILM North America operates in a number of segments, including photo imaging, electronic imaging, motion picture products, and graphic systems. Its FUJIFILM Canada subsidiary peddles products north of the US border.

The company operates in the US and Canada.

FUJIFILM North America operates through a handful of divisions: Imaging (consumer and commercial photographic products and services, including photographic paper digital printing equipment, personalized photo products, film, one-time-use cameras, and motion picture archival film); Electronic Imaging (consumer digital cameras);Graphic Systems (products and services for the graphic printing industry); and Optical Devices (optical lenses for the broadcast, cinematography, closed circuit television, videography and industrial markets, and the marketing of binoculars).

The US accounted for 18% of parent FUJIFILM Holdings ' revenues in 2012.

The business has been rapidly shifting the focus of its business to digital, including in its print division, to take advantage of the increasing number of cell phones that capture digital photos. To this end, customers of AT&T and Verizon , among other carriers, can use FUJIFILM's Web-based service to print and share their digital photos.

In 2013 FUJIFILM North America became a service provider to Printronix products yo help expand Printronix' service capabilities across North America.

Further growing its network, that year FUJIFILM North America joined leading global distributor CMYK Distributors.

Before it went calling on mobile phone owners, FUJIFILM North America in 2008 developed a photo services agreement with Rite Aid . As part of the deal, the drugstore chain offers FUJIFILM products and photofinishing services at its locations. Rite Aid customers can also upload their photos online and pick them up in-store.

In 2010 FUJIFILM North America halted production of pictography products in light of increased demand for inkjet and other dry digital print technologies.

FUJIFILM North America has been shaking up its organizational structure to present a more unified business to customers. To this end, subsidiary FUJIFILM Optical Devices U.S.A. was converted into FUJIFILM North America's optical devices division at the start of 2011. Also FUJIFILM Hawaii Inc., which provides photographic products on the Hawaiian Islands, became a regional sales and marketing office of FUJIFILM North America.
