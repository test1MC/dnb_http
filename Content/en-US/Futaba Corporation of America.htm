Futaba Corporation of America likes to show off and take off. The company, a subsidiary of Japan-based Futaba Corporation , makes vacuum fluorescent display modules used in factory automation and fabricating machines, jigs, and tools used to enable industrial processes like press-die setting and plastic injection molding. Futaba also manufactures radio controllers for the hobby and industrial markets. Its radio control products are used to steer battery- powered cars and airplanes for fun, as well as to maneuver heavy-duty cranes and agricultural machinery.

Futaba Corporation of America makes organic light emitting diode (OLED) displays and touchscreen displays in addition to its VFDs. The company also offers contract manufacturing services from its facility in Huntsville, Alabama.

In the US, Futaba Corporation of America has operations in Alabama, Illinois, and Michigan, which focuses on automotive sales.

Futaba Corporation of America had $71 million in revenue in 2015 (ended March), which was about 6% less than in 2014. The US subsidiary accounts for about 12% of the parent company's annual revenue.
