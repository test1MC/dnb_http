JFC International is spreading the joy of Asian flavors to food retailers and foodservice operators in North America. A subsidiary of Kikkoman Corporation , the company distributes thousands of Asian cooking items made by Japanese food companies, including dried seaweeds and frozen vegetables; crackers and other salty snacks; oils and vinegars; teas and canned coffees; rice, flours, and beans; and soy and other sauces. In addition, JFC manufactures its own food products (such as rice, noodles, sauces, and edible oils) under the names Dynasty, Hapi, Hime, JFC, Nishiki, and Wel-Pac. It also distributes Tanzan, Daishichi, and Tomoju sakes, along with about a dozen plum wines.

US-based JFC operates about 45 offices worldwide, including the Americas, Europe, and the Asia-Pacific region. It has 20 branches and offices in the US, four branches in Canada, and one in Mexico.

JFC is part of Kikkoman's Overseas Foods-Wholesale segment, which contributed 31% of fiscal 2013 revenue.

The company boasts a 15,000-item product line, which consists of Asian food products. It directly imports and exports fine sake, plum wine, and JFC premium rice brands such as Nishiki and Tamanishiki.

JFC sells its products through branches and regional sales offices. When volumes spike, it enlists the help of public warehouses and sub-distributor facilities.

Among its more than 12,000 customers are supermarkets, convenience stores, wholesale club stores, restaurants, military bases, and other distributors and wholesalers.

Sales across Kikkoman's Overseas Foods-Wholesale segment rose some 7% in fiscal 2013 vs. 2012, thanks to its efforts in gaining a foothold in the Asian American market.

Looking to expand, in recent years JFC has acquired specialty and health food producers to complement its fast-growing line of Asian food products portfolio.

JFC was formally founded in 1958 (as a soy sauce distributor) but traces its origins to the early 1900s.
