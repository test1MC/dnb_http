Sure, "The King" may have left the building, but Shure Incorporated continues to make the microphones that were good enough for Elvis to use. Aside from its arsenal of microphones, the company manufactures, markets, and sells high-end phonograph cartridges, mixers, digital signal processors, earphones, and wireless systems. It's focused on wired microphones, wireless microphone systems, and monitoring. Shure's devices are sold through authorized dealers, including Guitar Center and Sam Ash music stores, in the US, Europe, and Asia, as well as online. Sidney Shure began selling radio kits in 1925 under the name Shure Radio Company. His heirs still own the company, which was renamed Shure Incorporated in 2000.

Global audio products manufacturer Shure sells its products nationwide, as well as in Europe and Asia. Shure is based in Niles, Illinois. It has additional manufacturing facilities and regional sales offices in the US as well as in China, Denmark, Germany, Hong Kong, Japan, Mexico, the Netherlands, Denmark, and the UK.

The company's affiliates include Synaudcon; Technical Excellence & Creativity; The Engineering And Recording Society of Chicago; Avolution; and The National Association of Broadcasters.

Artist endorsements play an integral role in the firm's marketing campaigns. Pop stars and rock icons who have sung Shure's praises include Alice Cooper, Buddy Guy, Josh Rouse, KC & the Sunshine Band, and Slash. The company's products are available through Shure Retail stores as well as online through All Pro Sound; AMS; B&H; BSW; Full Compass; Guitar Center ; Musician%u2019s Friend ; SamAsh ; and Sweetwater .

Shure has been recognized for producing high-fidelity recording gear for consumers amid surging interest in user-generated multimedia content. Shure's most commended product line in recent years has been its PG side-address condenser microphones, which plug into USB ports on computers and feature a built-in headphone jack for zero-latency monitoring and mix control. The microphones are suited for instrumental and vocal recording, and are primarily targeted to performers using a home studio setup.

Marketing expansion and product innovations are key factors in the company's business strategy.

In 2014 the company opened a new office in Miami, expanding its global presence and broadening its reach throughout the Americas. As part of its integration of business in Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg, Shure launched Shure Distribution Benelux (opened a new office in a suburb of Brussels) in 2013.

In 2014 Shure launched GLXD6, company's first wireless receiver in guitar pedal format, delivering continuous interference monitoring in a standard pedal board.

In 2013 the company began distributing Danish Interpretation Systems conferencing and discussion systems; garnered several awards at the annual National Association of Music Merchants trade show in Anaheim, California, including TEC Award (for Axient), ProSound Network Best of Show (for GLX-D), Musical Merchandise Review's Dealers' Choice Award (for the SM58Â®), and Readers' Choice Awards (for the PSM 1000, KSM9HS, and Axient). Other introductions included GLX-D Systems, a new groundbreaking wireless product; SE846 Sound Isolating Earphones; and the SRH1540 Premium Closed-Back Headphones.

In the same year, the company launched two new headset microphones, the SM31FH Fitness Headset Condenser Microphone for fitness instructors and the SM35 Performance Headset Condenser Microphone for live sound applications. It also unveiled the PGA31 Headset Condenser Microphone and the Centraverse Lavalier Condenser Microphone.

Shure in 2012 launched ULX-D Digital Wireless, which provides 24-bit audio clarity and extremely efficient RF performance with single, dual, and quad channel receivers for any size professional sound reinforcement application.

The heirs of the founding Shure family own Shure Incorporated.
