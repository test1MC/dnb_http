In the world of power, Cummins Power Generation is a leader. The company produces a slew of diesel power generating, transfer, and control technologies. Its commercial products, including diesel engines ranging from 5kW to 2.75MW, are used in standby power and emergency systems, and multi-megawatt peaking facilities. In developing countries, where reliable utility power can sometimes be sketchy, Cummins Power Generation's products are used as a primary source of energy. In addition to the Cummins name, products are sold under the Onan, Stamford, Markon, and AVK brands. The company is a major unit of diesel-engine maker Cummins .

Cummins Power Generation is stationed in Minnesota and distributes its products to 190 countries around the world. The customer base for its power generation products is highly diversified, with customer groups varying based on their power needs. India, China, the UK, Western Europe, Latin America, and the Middle East are its largest geographic markets outside of North America.

Cummins Power Generation operates as the Power Products business of Cummins .

Its products include alternators, generator-drive engines and pre-integrated power systems, combining generator sets with power control and transfer technologies. They include diesel and alternative-fueled electrical generator sets from 2.5 to 2,700 kW, alternators from 0.6 KVA to 30,000 KVA, transfer switches from 40 amps to 3,000 amps, paralleling switchgear and generator set controls.

Services range from system design, project management, operations and maintenance contracts to development of turnkey power plants. Power products range from 2 kW to 1 MW and diesel fuel-based generator sets of more than 1 MW.

It also provides natural gas fuel-based turnkey solutions for distributed generation and energy management applications in the range of 300-2000 kW products.

Cummins Power Generation serves customers with less reliable electrical power infrastructures, typically in developing countries. In addition, it provides an alternative source of generating capacity located close to its point of use, which is purchased by utilities, independent power producers and large power customers for use as prime or peaking power.

Alternator products are sold under the Stamford, AVK and Markon brands and range in output from 0.6 kVA to 30,000 kVA.

The company accounted for about 15% of its parent's sales in 2014.

Cummins Power Generation continuously explores emerging technologies and provides integrated power generation products using technologies other than reciprocating engines. It uses its own research and development capabilities as well as those of business partners to develop cost-effective and environmentally sound power solutions.

To increase its market share and secure business growth in South East Asia, in 2014 Cummins Power Generation signed partnerships with two leading local companies in Vietnam.

That year, Cummins Energy Solutions Business (a project division of Cummins Power Generation) signed up Energy Solutions (Private) Limited as its gas generating set distributor and service provider for Pakistan.
