Achillion Pharmaceuticals is looking for the Achilles heel of infectious disease. The firm is developing treatments for infectious diseases, including antiviral treatments for HIV infection and hepatitis C, as well as antibacterials for fighting drug-resistant hospital-based infections. Achillion is focused on the development of three hepatitis C treatments. It has out-licensed elvucitabine, a late-stage treatment of HIV and hepatitis B, to GCA Therapeutics and sent its eye and skin infection treatment to Ora, Inc. Achillion has other candidates aimed at combating drug-resistant staph. The company was formed in 1998 and went public in 2006.
