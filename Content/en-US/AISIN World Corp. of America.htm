The core of AISIN World Corp. of America's (AWA) business is automotive parts and systems. These include brake systems, suspensions, chassis components, engine parts, drivetrain components, body parts, and information devices. AWA's aftermarket products include clutch plates, water pumps, master cylinders, and brake pads. It also provides lifestyle- and amenity-related products including home sewing and embroidery machines, and energy system-related products that include indoor/outdoor gas heat-pump air-conditioning systems. Automotive customers include Toyota Motor Engineering & Manufacturing North America , GM , Honda , and Johnson Controls . AWA's parent company is AISIN Seiki (Japan).

AWA operates more than 30 manufacturing, R&D, and sales center subsidiaries in North America.

AWA has been active in the development of technology for electric vehicles (EV); intelligent transport systems (ITS); information products for "intelligent vehicles," such as parking assist systems and lane departure warning systems; energy and environmental system products, including cooling, vacuum technologies, and optical engineering. It has also added door frames and aluminum die cast parts to its myriad of manufactured items.

The company sells its products through distributors and sales personnel. Its aftermarket products are marketed through ADVICS North America.

The company was formed in 1992 through the merger of AISIN U.S.A., Inc. (established in 1970) and AISIN America, Inc. (established in 1988). In early 2010 AWA and Aisin Holdings of America (AHA) were integrated.
