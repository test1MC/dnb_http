Konica Minolta puts its image on the line with its lines of imaging products. Through its subsidiaries and affiliates, the company offers an array of imaging-based products and services. It develops, manufactures, and sells multifunctional peripherals (MFPs) and printers for corporate offices; production print systems for commercial printing houses and graphic arts; medical diagnostic imaging systems for the health care market; measuring instruments for industrial applications; display materials that go into LCD TVs, PC monitors, and mobile phones; and optical products like lens units. Services such as optimized print, IT, and equipment maintenance are intended to help Konica Minolta clients further streamline their work environments.

Business is Konica Minolta's main business with business technologies generating about 81% of revenue. Its industrial brings in 11% of revenue with healthcare accounting for 8%.

Konica Minolta group companies operate worldwide in Asia/Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, North America, and South America. The company's revenue is well dispersed throughout its major markets. Customers in Europe account for a third of revenue, followed by Asia, 24%, North American, 23%, and Japan, 20%.

Konica Minolta's global sales and service network targets its products and services according to the particular office needs of different countries and regions.

The company's revenue increased 7% in 2015 (ended March) from 2014, boosted by 10% growth in its business technologies unit. Color units and digital printing systems drove sales. Sales fell in the healthcare and industrial segments. Buoyed by the higher revenue, net income jumped 50%. The sale of investments also contributed to the rise in net income. Cash flow from operations moved 13% higher in 2014 from 2013.

Konica Minolta's strategy essentially is to expand the scale of its business in certain growth markets. Its priorities include maintaining a top market position by launching new series and models of color multifunctional peripherals (MFPs) and production print systems; strengthening its optimized print services sales (OPS involves enhancing corporate printing environments for clients via centralized network-based management); and accelerating business development in emerging markets, especially in Asian markets. It opened Business Innovation Centers in five major markets in 2014. Then in 2015 the company opened a new R&D center to concentrate on digital printing systems.

In emerging markets, Konica Minolta has established regional head offices in Singapore and the United Arab Emirates, an office in Thailand, and sales subsidiaries in Vietnam and Turkey to better serve high-growth Southeast Asian and Middle Eastern markets. A new production site is operating in Malaysia.

In 2015, the company's acquisitions didn't center on any one geography. It acquired a printing production business in India, a technology distributor in Brazil, an Australian marketing services firm (which serves Asian markets), service companies in the US and the UK, and business services firm in the Czech Republic. For its display business, Konica Minolta picked up Radiant Vision Systems, which provides testing systems for the flat panel display industry.
