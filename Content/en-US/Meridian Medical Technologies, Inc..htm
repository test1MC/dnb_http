Meridian Medical Technologies (MMT) wards off attacks of allergies and nerve gas alike. The company develops and manufactures auto-injectors designed to provide emergency drug delivery. As a primary supplier to the US government and law enforcement agencies, MMT provides auto-injectors filled with antidotes for chemical nerve agent and insecticide poisoning, as well as anti-convulsant and pain medications used by emergency responders. The product best-known to civilians is the EpiPen auto-injector, which is filled with epinephrine to treat anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction). The company became a subsidiary of Pfizer in 2011 when Pfizer acquired former parent King Pharmaceuticals .

The company operates a manufacturing facility in St. Louis, Missouri, where the auto-injector products are developed and produced. MMT markets the EpiPen directly to medical health professionals in Canada; the product is sold by distribution licensee Dey in the US and other international markets.

Key customers include the US Department of Defense , emergency medical personnel, NATO , and customers in more than 30 allied countries worldwide.

One of MMT's largest customers is the US Department of Defense , which purchases nerve agent antidotes and seizure and pain treatments. Other customers include governments of allied nations, state and local governments, homeland security agencies, and health care response organizations.

MMT's brands include AtroPen and DuoDote.
