Nestlé Waters' love affair with water runs deep. A division of the world's top food company Nestlé S.A. , Nestlé Waters offers 59-plus brands of bottled sparkling and still waters to quench six types of "taste profiles." The company's Nestlé Pure Life is the world's #1 water brand and is available in 33 countries. Its US brands include Ice Mountain, Calistoga, Poland Spring, Arrowhead, Ozarka, and Zephyrhills. Nestlé Waters' portfolio also features international brands (Perrier, Vittel, S. Pellegrino, and Acqua Panna) as well as a slew of local brands. Its Home and Office Delivery (HOD) division will drop five-gallon jugs of drinking water at your door.

The company operates nearly 100 production sites in 33 countries and sells its products in 130 countries.

Nestlé Waters has a portfolio of more than 59 brands including Nestlé Pure Life, Perrier, S. Pellegrino, Poland Spring, Vittel, Buxton, Erikli, and offers a wide range of packaging options.

The company distributes its brands through retail, modern trade, out-of-home, restaurants, and offices.

In 2015 the company's net revenues increased by 3%, mainly driven by volume growth.

Despite the bottled water giant's breadth of offerings and global presence, it has to be innovative to counter rising commodity costs and fluctuating currency exchange rates as well as pricing pressure from rivals and challenging economic conditions.

Part of this has to do with improving its image in order to help protect or gain market share. Nestlé Waters is working to alleviate consumers' concern about the environmental impact of plastic bottles, which, depending on their size, can take anywhere from 450 to 1,000 years to fully biodegrade. Among the company's efforts, it has undertaken a multi-year program to reduce the weight of its packaging.

In 2013 Pulmuone Waters, a joint venture between the company and Pulmuone , opened a new $53 million bottled water factory in South Korea.

That year the company acquired the mineral water spring Vale do Sol and its assets, located in the city of Silva Jardim. This project involves the total investment of R$ 117 million, which will warrant the sustainability and growth of Nestlé's water business in the Brazilian market, especially in the State of Rio de Janeiro where it already owns Água Mineral Petrópolis.
