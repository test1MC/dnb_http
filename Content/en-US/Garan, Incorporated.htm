If you've ever enlisted the help of a monkey to match your shirt and shorts, you're likely to be familiar with Garan. The company designs, manufactures, and distributes coordinated apparel for infants, toddlers, and children. Products are sold under its customers' private labels, as well as its own brand, Garanimals. Characters like Charley Chimp help young children dress themselves with coordinated hang-tags. Most of Garan's products are sold through distribution centers in the US to major national chains, such as Wal-Mart , department stores, and specialty stores. Founded in 1941, Garan is a subsidiary of Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway .

Based in New York, Garan boasts more than a dozen operating subsidiaries worldwide.

Garan operates production facilities located in the US, Central America, and Asia.

The apparel maker relies on one customer for most of its sales. Sales to big-box retailer Wal-Mart accounts for more than 90% of Garan's revenue. The company also earns revenue through licensing agreements with independent third parties.

It's focused on social media and content generation to boost its brand online. In 2012 Garan launched a Learn, Play, Share website that incorporates its Garanimals blog, which offers information, tips, and trends related to animals, fashion, and technology.

To extend its reach and provide its primary customer with more products, Garan partnered with Wal-Mart to make an exclusive line of toys for play and learning. The preschool toys, including a Pull-Apart Sorter and a 5-in-1 Learning Cube, boast the Garan animal characters used on its apparel.

Garan operates as part of Berkshire Hathaway's Other Manufacturing, Service, and Retailing Businesses segment, which in fiscal 2013 contributed 23% of the parent company's net revenue.
