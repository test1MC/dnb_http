As China's oldest steel company, Wuhan Iron and Steel, known by the less industrial moniker WISCO, anchors China's steel industry. Each year it produces a wide variety of steel products including hot-rolled coil, hot-rolled section steel, hot-rolled heavy rail, plate of moderate thickness, cold-rolled coil, galvanized plate, cold-rolled oriented and non-oriented silicon steel sheet, color coated plate, and high-speed wire rod, etc. It also makes chemical products and granulated slag and operates six iron ore mines in Hubei and Jiaozhuo. The company is also engaged in the hi-tech industry and in international trading. In 2016 Wuhan Iron and Steel agreed to be absorbed by Baoshan Iron & Steel via an equity swap.

The company has facilities in a number of Chinese cities, including Ezhou, Nanning, Wuhan, and Anning.

WISCO has the world's most advanced and complete process flow for iron-making, steel-making, and steel rolling, and has developed 700 product varieties in 63 categories in 177 series. (The company has researched and developed more than 300 new varieties in 85 series, with an annual output exceeding 2 million tons). It has developed a full set of advanced iron and steel production processes and equipment for mining, coking, steel-making, iron-making, rolling and supporting auxiliary facilities, making it an important production base of high-quality sheets and plates. In addition to iron and steel the company also has interests in hi-tech industries and in international trade.

WISCO Echeng Iron & Steel Co., Ltd develops the comprehensive annual capacity of producing 4 million tons of steel products.

WISCO Kunming Iron & Steel Co., Ltd mainly produces cast iron, steel billet, high-speed wire rod, hot-rolled ribbed bars, and hot-rolled thin and medium plates.

Guangxi Iron & Steel Group Co., Ltd. carries out the application, approval, and construction of the project.

The company serves industries such as project design and construction, mechanical manufacture and processing, transportation, logistics and warehousing, automation technology, development of overseas resources, investment, and financing.

As part of a global expansion strategy, WISCO began the preparation for the establishment an office in Russia in 2012. Responding to the rapid economic development in Russia, the new office will focus on serving the iron and steel markets of Eastern Europe, and promoting the influence of WISCO in international marketd. That year it also signed a long term cooperation agreement with POSCO Asia and POSCO Vietnam, subsidiaries of South Korea's POSCO .

In 2011 WISCO formed a joint venture with Canada's Adriana Resources to develop and gain access to that company's iron ore resources. It also formed a JV that year with Nippon Steel to make and sell tin plate.

WISCO was established in 1958.
