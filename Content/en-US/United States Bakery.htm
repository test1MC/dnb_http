Man may not live by bread alone, but we could do pretty well with products from the ovens of United States Bakery. The company, also known as Franz Family Bakery, owns and operates nine regional bakeries, as well as a chain of some 50 bakery thrift stores, located in California, Idaho, Oregon, and Washington. In addition to selling baked goods at its retail and thrift outlets, United States Bakery sells a variety of breads, pastries, bagels, donuts, and cookies to grocery stores, restaurants, food manufacturers and foodservice providers. It also offers private-label services. United States Bakery was founded in 1906 by Engelbert and Joseph Franz and is still owned and operated by the fourth generation of Franz family members.

Based in Oregon, United States Bakery operates seven bakeries through the Northwest and about 50 bakery thrift shops in its home state of Oregon, as well as in California, Idaho, and Washington.

United States Bakery sells its products to supermarkets, food makers, foodservice providers, and restaurants. As part of its business, the company also makes baked goods for other companies under private label. United States Bakery boasts that it serves 80% of restaurants and nearly all the area grocers in the Northwest, including "virtually all" of the fast-food restaurants in the region.

In addition to selling its products under the Franz name, the bakery also markets its items under the William's, Snyders', Gai's, Big Loaf, Smith Cookie Company, Bay City, Seattle International, McKenzie Farms, Svenhard's, and Aunt Katie's brand names.

Benefiting from the bankruptcy of Hostess Brands , United States Bakery in 2013 acquired several Hostess bread brands in the Northwest. They include Sweetheart, Eddy's, Standish Farms, and Grandma Emilie's. The $28.85-million deal comprised four baking plants, 14 depots, the four brands, and other assets. The plants are located in Alaska, Montana, Utah, and Washington while the depots span Idaho, Montana, North Dakota, Utah, and Washington.
