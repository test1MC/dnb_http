Crocs has taken a bite out of the footwear industry. The company's shoe collection has grown by leaps and bounds from its ubiquitous colorful slip-on shoe to mainstream fashion. Branded as Crocs, its shoes are made of proprietary closed-cell resin and designed for men, women, and children; its collection includes 300-plus four-season footwear styles. Jibbitz are their decorative add-on charms. The company sells its products in more than 90 countries, operating distribution centers worldwide and manufacturing plants in Mexico and Italy. Crocs sells through retailers, such as Dillard's , Nordstrom , and Dick's Sporting Goods , as well as through about 250 of its own stores and kiosks worldwide.

Based in Colorado, Crocs enjoys a global reach, doing business in more than 90 countries.

The company has four operating segments: Americas, Asia Pacific, Japan, and Europe. Its Other segment comprises its manufacturing operations in Mexico, Italy, and Asia.

As part of its business, Crocs relies on third parties for its distribution centers, which are located in the US, China, Japan, Hong Kong, Australia, Korea, Singapore, India, Taiwan, the United Arab Emirates, Russia, Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Puerto Rico, and Italy.

Crocs has worked in recent years to transform its brand from what Crocs calls its clog silhouette to an all-season footwear brand. It's also looking at licensing to further diversify its revenue. The company's products fall into four categories: Core-Comfort, Active, Casual, and Style. The footwear maker partners with the likes of Disney , Marvel , and Viacom to sell Crocs-licensed shoes and Jibbitz-branded shoe charms.

The company moderated the pace of its retail expansion in 2015 with a focus on outlet and kiosk locations. It halted the expansion of its retail channel and is now focusing on the long-term profitability of current locations. Crocs opened 70 company-operated stores, half of which were outlet or low investment kiosk/store-in-store locations. It also closed 104 company-operated stores, 20 of which were identified in its initial restructuring plan.

The company sells its products through three primary channels: wholesale (57% of sales), retail (33%), and Internet (10%). Wholesale customers include national and regional retail chains, department stores, sporting goods stores, independent footwear retailers, and family footwear retailers, such as Dick's Sporting Goods , Famous Footwear, Academy , Kohls , Nordstrom , Xebio , and Murasaki Sports, as well as online retailers the likes of Zappos.com and Amazon.com . Additionally, Crocs has nearly 540 branded retail locations worldwide.

Crocs boasts sales offices in the US, Canada, South America, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Australia, Korea, China, the United Arab Emirates, India, and Europe.

In 2014 the company spent $44.7 million in total advertising, marketing, and promotional activities. It taps digital, social, and traditional media outlets for its campaigns. The company's in-house digital marketing team oversees digital marketing programs and platforms that include paid-and-organic search, display, re-targeting, email, and affiliate marketing.

Although Crocs posted record-setting revenues of $1.2 billion in 2014, it suffered its first net loss in five years for the year.

The historic revenue growth in 2014 was primarily due to a 3% spike in global footwear unit sales driven by improved year-over-year performance across its wholesale and Internet channels. The increase was partially offset by a decrease of $0.35 per unit, or 2%, in average footwear selling price.

The $5 million net loss for 2014 was mainly due to the presence of restructuring charges related to strategic plans for its long-term growth. These charges included severance costs, retail store closure costs, and a write-downs of obsolete inventory related to an exited business line.

To reach into the accessories and sports businesses, Crocs looks for add-on products (such as its purchase of Jibbitz) and technologies. Buying EXO Italia, maker of ethylene vinyl acetate-based finished products, has given Crocs the tools to compete in the popular performance footwear market against rivals the likes of Teva .
