BSD Medical has developed equipment to provide hyperthermia treatment, specifically for treating cancer (including melanoma, breast cancer, brain cancer, and cervical cancer). Its systems are used in tandem with chemotherapy and radiation therapy or as a stand-alone treatment. BSD Medical was the first to develop an approvable hyperthermia system, which uses focused radio frequencies and microwaves to heat cancer cells until they die. The company's devices are designed to target superficial tumors, as well as tumors located deep within a patient's body. Its products are sold to clinics, hospitals, and other cancer-treatment institutions through its sales force and external distributors.

BSD's products have gained regulatory approval and are sold in the US and a number of countries in Europe and Asia.

The company sells its products through distribution agreements in some regions. In 2013 BSD signed a major distribution agreement with Terumo Europe, a division of Terumo Corporation , that gives Terumo exclusive rights to distribute the MicroThermX systems in about 100 countries in Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

BSD is working to develop its medical systems towards treating other medical conditions, such as arthritis, psoriasis, and hemorrhoids. It also aims to increase the geographic areas where its products are available.

The company sold its TherMatrx subsidiary, which used BSD's technology to treat patients with enlarged prostate conditions, to American Medical Systems in 2004 in order to fund its primary product application.
