Insurance companies step aside, please; Keystone Automotive offers a different kind of accident forgiveness. The company distributes new and recycled automotive parts used to repair damaged vehicles. It also refurbishes steel bumpers and plastic bumper covers, as well as alloy wheels, grilles, radiators, condensers, and supplies paint and other materials used to make damaged vehicles like new. Keystone serves auto body shops and dealerships via service centers in the US and Canada. It operates multiple wheel remanufacturing plants and bumper recycling facilities, too. Keystone is a subsidiary of automobile recycling giant LKQ Corporation .

The company operates nearly 135 distribution facilities, of which 22 serve as regional hubs, located in 39 states and Canada.

In addition to providing recycled automotive parts, Keystone markets its own line of auto parts: Platinum Plus and Value Line.

Keystone sells the majority of its products through sales personnel to collision and mechanical repair shops, independent manufacturers, and distributors. The company's products are sold to more than 25,000 repair shops throughout Canada and the US.

Keystone operates as part of the North American segment of LKQ Corp . This segment represents 75% of its parent's total revenue and increased 11% from 2012 to 2013 as a result of organic growth and acquisitions.
