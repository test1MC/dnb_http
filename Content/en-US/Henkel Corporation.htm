Henkel Corporation sticks with glue and a slew of other consumer products. The North American unit of German giant Henkel KGaA , manufactures branded household and personal care goods in three categories: laundry and home care, beauty care, and adhesive technologies. Some of consumers' favorite brands are made by Henkle, including Dial soap, Soft Scrub cleaner, Purex laundry detergent, Right Guard antiperspirant, g√∂t2b hair styling products, and Loctite and LePage adhesives and sealants. Customers are retail groceries and wholesalers and distributors. The founding Henkel family controls the business.

Henkel has 51 manufacturing facilities and accounts for nearly 20% of Henkel KGaA's worldwide sales.

Henkel's operations consist of the North American market for laundry and home care (detergents and cleaning products), beauty care (products for the body, skin, and oral care, and hair salon business), and adhesive technologies (decoration and renovation products). The structure, which is supported by four US R&D sites, is intended to flex for growth in a positive market yet provide stability in a downturn.

Henkel utilizes media advertising and point-of-sale campaigns to sell its products. It uses digital marketing and social media to address potential customers. Consumers purchase their products from retailers, primarily drug stores, supermarkets, and department stores. Field sales representatives support the salons at the local level.

Its primary direct customer group is the grocery retail trade with distribution channels in the form of supermarkets, large-scale mass merchandisers/hypermarkets, and discount stores. Adhesive technologies serves a wide range of customers with different uses for their products. Customers range from large international industrial clients to small and medium-sized industrial businesses, craftsmen, do-it-yourselfers, and private households.

In 2015 North American revenues increased by 26%, driven by positive foreign exchange effects and acquisitions in 2014. Organically, the region posted sales growth of 2%.

Acquisitions and new product launches are expected to fuel Henkel's growth. (Manufacturing expanded in North America by around 2% in 2015).

The parent company is also continuing its globalization strategy, with a particular focus on emerging markets.

In 2017 Henkel agreed to acquire Darex Packaging Technologies from GCP Applied Technologies for $1 billion.

In 2015 the company acquired Indiana-based Magna-Tech Manufacturing, which is engaged in vacuum impregnation services, with a focus on process center capabilities. Magna-Tech will operating as part of Henkel's Adhesive Technologies business. Magna-Tech's vacuum impregnation services provide a complementary service business that leverages Henkel's strong presence in the passenger vehicle market. This acquisition also enhances Henkel's global footprint in vacuum impregnation services by adding to Henkel's existing network of European and Asian process centers.

Among several additions, in January 2011 the German parent took over Schwarzkopf in a deal that deepened Henkel's foothold in the US hair salon market. Aiming to increase its product portfolio, in 2012 Henkel launched Loctite 3F7900 Ceramic Shelf for Welding, a protective coating that prevents spatter adhesion on welding equipment.
