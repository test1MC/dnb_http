An in vitro diagnostic company, Bioptix (formerly Venaxis) has switched its focus after its lead candidate APPY1 failed to secure clearance from the FDA . APP1 is a blood test which measures a biomarker associated with acute appendicitis; the company was also developing reproductive drugs for animals based on recombinant hormone analogs. The company found new life in 2016 when it acquired BiOptix Diagnostics, a Colorado-based company which is developing a platform for the detection of molecular interactions. The instrument will help determine whether a biologic or small molecule drug will be effective, as well as what dose is recommended. Later that year, the company changed its name from Venaxis to Bioptix.
