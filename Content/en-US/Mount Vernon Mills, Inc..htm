Mount Vernon Mills weaves a tapestry of textiles. The second largest privately owned textile maker in the US makes a myriad of yarns, fabrics, and chemical and finished goods for the apparel, automotive, home textile, and healthcare industries. Among its divisions, LaFrance Industries focuses on automotive seating upholstery and fabrics for commercial paint rollers. A consumer products arm supplies table linens, bedding, and towels for retail, hospital, and hotel use. The company's apparel fabrics division, its largest, produces denim and piece-dyed fabrics. Mount Vernon Mills' origins date back to 1847. It operates plants in the Southeast US.

The company has its headquarters in Mauldin, South Carolina. It operates 13 production facilities in the US, located in North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, Mississippi, and Texas.

It provides truckload and less-than-truckload hauling services to internal and external customers throughout the US from terminals in Alabama and South Carolina. Internationally, it is active throughout Latin America, the Caribbean, and Asia.

Mount Vernon Mills is focused on the apparel, industrial, institutional, automotive, transportation, residential, and commercial sectors.
