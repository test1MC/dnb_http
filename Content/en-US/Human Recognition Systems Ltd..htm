Human Recognition Systems (HRS) takes identification beyond the ID card. The company provides biometric security devices, including facial recognition systems, iris scanners, fingerprint readers, and access control systems for customers in the UK. The company also provides the management software used to provide security analysis, and attendance monitoring to corporate clients. Serving clients in the aviation, construction, energy, and government markets, HRS specializes in pairing biometric technology with back end support to meet the specific needs of its customers. BAE Systems , the Manchester, London Gatwick, Glasgow, and Edinburgh airports, and the Ministry of Defence are among HRS's customers.

As an independent biometric consultancy and systems integrator, HRS is not beholden to any one biometric system or manufacturer and can pick the appropriate mix of equipment, software, and services for the unique needs of each client. Although security is a primary application of the technology, HRS has also made use of biometrics for more general corporate functions. The company's MSite solution provides workforce management options that integrate security, health and safety regulatory enforcement, and payroll management within a single system.

The company serves a range of sectors, including construction, airports, and oil and gas.

Highlighting its commitment improving its offerings to clients, in 2014 HRSpartnered with Camwatch to offer its MSitecustomers access to the JCB Site Security Mobile CCTV Tower a unique, fully-integrated, rapidly-deployable security product.

In 2013 the company partnered with Construction Skills Certification Scheme (CSCS), integrating its MSite solution into CSCS's database.

CEO Neil Norman founded HRS in 2002 after recognizing the potential that biometric technology would play in the future of business.
