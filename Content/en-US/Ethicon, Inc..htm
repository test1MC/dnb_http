Johnson & Johnson subsidiary Ethicon has the operating room all sewn up. The company's Ethicon Products division is a leading maker of sutures and wound-closure products, including sutures, Dermabond skin glue, Mentor surgical mesh products, and wound drains, as well as breast implants and plastic surgery devices. Another division, Ethicon Biosurgery, provides a line of absorbable hemostats and other products that help control bleeding during surgery, while its Biopatch product offers infection-resistant wound dressings. Ethicon Women's Health and Urology makes surgical grafts, devices, and equipment under the Gynecare brand.

Ethicon is a part of the surgical care segment of J&J 's Medical Devices division. The company's products are marketed directly to surgeons, hospitals, and other health care providers, and distributed through wholesale and retail outlets around the globe.

Other products include the Harmonic Focus+ Shears with adaptive tissue technology, which allows for fine dissection and sealing of vessels and lymphatics. Ethicon's Endo-Surgery generator powers both ultrasonic and advance bipolar technologies in the operating room.

Ethicon's business extends its reach to more than 15 locations in Asia, Europe, the Middle East, North America, and South America. The company has educational institutes, innovation centers, manufacturing facilities, and sales centers in more than 50 countries.

Johnson & Johnson's Medical Devices division, of which Ethicon is a subsidiary, provided 37% of total revenues in 2014.

In 2014 the company introduced three new endocutter and energy products designed to help address the unique challenges of bariatric procedures, such as sleeve gastrectomy and gastric bypass.

Ethicon has been a strong growth segment for its parent, with rising sales in its biosurgical, suture, surgical mesh, and women's health and urology product lines. As a result, J&J has been investing in the expansion of Ethicon's product offerings through a number of acquisitions, which in turn have further contributed to rising revenues for the unit. It expanded in the ear, nose, and throat (ENT) market by purchasing Acclarent in 2010 for some $785 million. Acclarent, which makes minimally invasive devices for balloon sinuplasty (sinus surgery) procedures, operates as an autonomous division of Ethicon. The deal followed previous purchases of medical supplies maker Mentor Corporation (renamed Mentor Worldwide ) and Omrix Biopharmaceuticals , producer of surgical biomaterials such as biologic-based hemostats.

Sister firm Ethicon Endo-Surgery was once a unit within Ethicon but was spun off into a separate entity (still within the J&J family, of course) in 1992.
