The company makes cold- and hot-rolled stainless and specialty steel, as well as grain-oriented (GO) and non-grain oriented (NGO) electrical steel, and nickel alloys in the form of bars, strips, wire, wire rod, and plates. From its five steel mills in Europe (France and Belgium) and one in Brazil, the company has a production capacity of 2.5 million tons. Aperam also operates about 20 steel service centers, where the metal is processed and distributed to more than 40 countries for customers in the aerospace, construction, food service, medical, automotive, and oil and gas industries. Aperam formed in September 2010 when steel maker ArcelorMittal spun off its stainless steel operations.
