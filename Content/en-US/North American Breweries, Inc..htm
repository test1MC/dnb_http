A can of Genny is to upstate New York what a can of PBR is to the south - the cheap beer of choice. North American Breweries (NAB) owns The Genesee Brewery Company, which has been brewing up Genesee, Genny Light, and Genesee Cream Ale since 1878. When NAB was formed in 2009, it also swallowed Independent Brewers United, the parent of Magic Hat , Pyramid , and MacTarnahan's. In addition to its four breweries, NAB is the sole importer and distributor of Canada's Labatt beers and Costa Rica's Imperial beers to the US and holds a perpetual license to bottle Seagram's Escapes, a line of fruit-flavored malt beverages, from Pernod Ricard USA . NAB is owned by Cerveceria Costa Rica, owned by Florida Ice and Farm Co.

Rochester, New York-based NAB owns and operates four US breweries and about half a dozen retail locations in New York, Vermont, California, Oregon, and Washington.

NAB's flagship brewery, the Genesee Brewery in Rochester, New York, is one of the largest and longest continually-operating breweries in the US.

With a production capacity of some 3 million barrels a year, the Genesee brewery acts as the contract brewer for dozens of beers, malt beverages, and non-alcoholic beverages for other companies, including Boston Beer's Sam Adams. Besides Labatt's and Imperial, it also imports and distributes (within a 500-mile radius of Rochester) Steinlager from New Zealand, Toohey's New from Australia, and Thwaites from the UK.

In creating North American Breweries, KPS Capital Partners worked to assemble a complete portfolio of drinks brands, from the blue collar appeal of Genny beer to the craft beers of Magic Hat, Pyramid, and MacTarnahan's; while Seagram's Escapes is primarily marketed to women. Each beer brand is sold regionally, except for Labatt, which is sold across the US. KPS picked up Labatt's US operations when antitrust regulators forced the sale before they would approve the InBev - Anheuser-Busch merger in 2009. In Canada, Labatt exists as a subsidiary of Anheuser-Busch InBev . NAB manufactures, imports, markets and sells its brands through an independent network of wholesalers throughout the US.

Craft beers are a bright spot in the otherwise declining beer market. NAB's sale to Cerveceria Costa Rica came amid of flurry of deals for craft breweries.
