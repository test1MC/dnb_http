NEC Display Solutions of America works at drawing stares. A US-based subsidiary of Japanese tech titan NEC Corporation , the company sells desktop and large-screen LCD monitors primarily for commercial customers. Its displays and digital cinema projectors are used for such diverse applications as computer monitors, airport information displays, medical equipment, merchandising displays, classroom and boardroom presentations, and restaurant menu boards. In the US, NEC Display's off-the-shelf electronics are distributed by B&H, CDW , Newegg , and Zones . Some of its products can be rented for tradeshows and other corporate functions.

NEC Display Solutions also sells interactive whiteboards and portable "short-throw" projectors designed for use in classrooms and offices. On the other end of the display spectrum, the company has a presence in the entertainment industry through its digital cinema projectors.

The company also offers software for digital signage content management and introduced software that brings whiteboard-type interactivity to its network-capable displays. The Interactive Software allows users to control displayed content, whether on a compatible NEC LCD screen or projector, from an Apple iPad.

In addition to the US, NEC Display Solutions has offices worldwide, primarily across Asia and Europe. Its products are manufactured in Asia.

The company continues to expand via new product launches. Its VUKUNET software is used to run and manage advertising on its screens. The VUKUNET content management systems offers templates and can integrate a variety of feeds, such as weather or sports, as well as emergency notifications.

In 2013 it introduced a digital projector for use by schools, churches, retail, and corporate clients and the fourth generation of its E Series LED-backlit commercial-grade displays.

Formerly called NEC-Mitsubishi Electronics Display of America, NEC Display Solutions started as a joint venture between NEC and Mitsubishi Electric before Mitsubishi transferred its holding to NEC.
