Having trouble keeping your cool on the road? Chill out with Thermo King. The company manufactures transport temperature control and HVAC-Refrigeration systems for trailers, truck bodies, buses, shipping containers, and rail cars. Produced at about 10 plants in six countries, the lineup is marketed worldwide through a global dealer network. In addition to its line of new systems, Thermo King offers used and remanufactured refrigeration units to customers. Frederick Jones and Joseph Numero started Thermo King in 1938 to transport fresh chickens across long distances without spoiling. The company is part of the Climate Solutions (formerly Climate Control Technologies) division of Ingersoll-Rand .

The company has operations in Africa, Europe, Middle East, North America, Asia Pacific, Latin America, Russia/CIS, and the US.

The Ingersoll-Rand Climate Control Technologies division originally comprised Thermo King and refrigeration equipment maker Hussmann ; both were manufacturers of refrigeration systems. With the integration of Trane , which provides residential and commercial HVAC systems, Ingersoll-Rand created its Climate Solutions division to house the three businesses, which account for almost 70% of Ingersoll-Rand's revenues. The organizational changes were facilitated to create a more customer-focused business.

Looking to strengthen relationships with its customers -- including Wal-Mart Stores and Sysco -- Thermo King has been developing custom refrigeration units incorporating fuel-efficient engines to help companies control costs. Thermo King designed the Spectrum SB, a multiple-temperature refrigerated trailer unit with a 30-foot frozen compartment in the middle, for Wal-Mart, which subsequently equipped thousands of its trailers with the unit.

Thermo King's Frederick McKinley Jones Research and Development Center is the backbone of the company's operations. The center is named for the African-American engineer who developed the first transport refrigeration unit in 1935, and later co-founded Thermo King. The company has evolved through various owners. It was purchased by Westinghouse Electric in 1961 and then acquired by Ingersoll-Rand in 1997 for a little more than $2.5 billion in cash.
