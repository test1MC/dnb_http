Rippin's tippin' the scales when it comes to manufacturing steel components for the construction industry. The company produces structural steel fabrication components primarily for construction projects throughout the UK. It makes use of methods including beam rolling, MIG (metal inert gas) welding, and peddinghaus punching in the manufacture of its structural steel components. Rippin has contributed to building the Cameron Toll Shopping Center, the central command facility for the Strathclyde Fire Brigade, Glasgow University Sports Center, and Queen Street Railway Station.

The company's primary areas of operation are Scotland and the north of England, although it also has other locations in the rest of the UK.

Rippin is engaged in many large scale structural steel construction projects providing structural steel frames and site construction services for buildings in many major cities throughout Scotland and the UK.

The company sells its product across the European Union.

In 2015 Rippin partnered with Fife Welding & Safety Supplies Ltd.
