The brains behind Ternium have welded together a number of Latin America's largest steel entities and created one giant, roll-formed monolith of a holding company. Luxembourg-based Ternium controls steel companies throughout the Americas, such as Argentina's Siderar (of which it owns about 60%) and Mexico's Ternium Mexico . Ternium's 16 facilities (including plants in Colombia, Guatemala, and the US) have an annual production capacity of 10.8 million tons. While it sells primarily to clients in the Americas it is developing a sales presence in Asia and Europe as well. Investment group Rocca & Partners controls Ternium; it also controls steel tube producer Tenaris .
