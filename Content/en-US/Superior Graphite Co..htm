The writing on this company's wall is graphite, not graffiti. Superior Graphite's products include graphite powders for battery makers; silicon carbide for the ceramics industry; and graphites used in lubricants, coatings, and rubber compounds. The company has about 20 brands with a variety of applications, such as railway lubrication, batteries and fuel cells, technical ceramics, heated pavement systems, and toner for printers. Founded in 1917, Superior Graphite provides graphite internationally, and the company maintains its US headquarters in Chicago and its Europe headquarters in Sundsvall, Sweden.

The company has customer service centers in China, Sweden, and the US. It has three research and development centers in the US, and manufacturing plants in Sweden (1) and the US (6).

Superior Graphite's variety of capabilities include continuous electro-thermal purification technology, carbon coatings, and granular processing. The company's proprietary technologies are applied to a broad spectrum of materials including natural and synthetic graphite, carbon black, silicon carbide, and cokes.

The company's Kentucky-based High Temperature Research Center focuses on the thermal purification of carbon and graphite along with the carbo-thermal synthesis of advanced ceramic materials. Its Peter R. Carney Technology Center focuses on carbon and graphite products while the Research Center at Russellville, Arkansas supports ARK graphite electrode production and the development of other extruded and/or heat-treated carbon and graphite products.

Superior Graphite's thermally-purified products include such brands as Desulco recarburizer for ductile, gray, compacted graphite iron casting and carbon steel production; RGC premium friction modifier and polymer additive; Formula FX friction modifiers; Desulco MC non-ferrous melt cover Formula BT energy materials; and ThermoPURE diverse applications.

Other brand names include ARK Electrodes, Bottom Line (rail lube), PROTECT TILES (ceramic armor), PureBlack (graphitized carbon black), and Signature (graphites and carbons).

An expert in natural and synthetic graphite purification and the process of synthesizing graphite or other carbon-based materials, the company focuses on exploring new avenues in continuous graphitization and on developing innovative processes, technologies, and products to support the technical needs of current and emerging markets.

Company Background

Superior Graphite is owned and managed by the founding Carney family.

The company was established in 1917 by the Carney.
