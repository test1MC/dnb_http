Heavy metal -- the industrial kind -- is hip at Hitachi Metals America (HMA). The North American outfit of Hitachi Metals makes and markets a slew of groundbreaking products. Its lineup includes auto castings, ceramics, cutting tools, gas piping components, permanent magnets, radio frequency components and power inductors, sensors, and specialty steel. HMA primarily serves the automotive, consumer products, energy, industrial, information technology, semiconductor, and telecommunications sectors. The company has seven manufacturing subsidiaries, five sales offices (Illinois, Pennsylvania, Michigan, California, and New York), and one research facility in the US.

HMA's manufacturing subsidiaries include AAP St. Marys Corp., Hitachi Cable America Inc., Hitachi Cable America Inc. and Hitachi Metals Automotive Components USA, LLC.

HMA's high-quality special steels are mass-produced from carefully selected materials using state-of-the-art equipment and technologies in melting, refining, hot working, cold working and heat treatment. The company is also a global supplier offering a complete line of permanent magnets: cast alnico, ceramic, and neodymium-iron-boron magnets.

Other divisions include Amorphous & Finemet Materials (used in the production of electrical distribution transformers, high frequency magnetic components for power electronics, material for anti-theft tags, brazing alloys, and solder); Ferrous and Non-Ferrous Castings (which consists of two main companies -- AAP St. Marys Corp. and Hitachi Metals Automotive Components, USA LLC -- and four production plants; Performance Cable Systems and Materials designs and manufactures copper and fiber optic communication cables and assemblies for the telecommunications industry; and Hitachi Tool Engineering, Ltd., a producer of high-performance cutting tools, uses leading raw materials and advanced engineering techniques to produce tools for a wide variety of applications.

In addition, HMA offers a broad range of ceramics which include bio-medical products (Scintillators), and high-temperature resistant materials (Sialon); and Mass Flow Control (MFC) and Flow Products (including SAM and Aera brand analog, digital, and pressure insensitive MFCs which provide leading edge MFC functionality; and Rolls (mill rolls for metal productions).

HMA offers a comprehensive line of sensor devices (digital compasses and magnetic encoders) and Soft Ferrites for the international electronics market.

HMA was established in 1965.
