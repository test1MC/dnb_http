Pac-Van is trying to chomp its way to the top of the modular building business. The company sells and leases modular buildings, mobile offices, portable storage containers, and special event trailers through about 40 offices in North America. Pac-Van supplies factory-built structures that adhere to the same building codes required for on-site construction; the company touts such advantages as speed of delivery, portability, financial flexibility, and cost-effectiveness. Pac-Van also provides financing and customization options. Customers include retailers, schools, churches, hospitals, homebuilders, banks, restaurants, golf pro shops, auto dealerships, and government and industrial clients.

The company has a presence has 39 offices in the US and Canada.

Pac-Van also offers customized office containers called Ground Level Offices (GLOs) which, similar to its Mobile Offices, provide office space but without the need for steps and skirting.

The company targets industries such as the construction, education, health care, retail, government, industrial, commercial, and mining/energy.

Pac-Van has accounted 38% of the parent company's revenues in 2015. The company's revenues grew by 24% in 2015.

The company expands through acquisitions. In recent years, Pac-Van completed more than 20 acquisitions of companies of varying sizes, adding more than a dozen new office locations to its service network.

In 2015 the company acquired Bristlecone Ventures, LLC, doing business as Falcon Containers, for $7 million. Falcon Containers primarily leases and sells portable storage and office containers from its two locations in Austin and San Antonio, Texas. The company also acquired AB-TK Leasing, Inc. and Budget Mobile Storage, LLC which lease and sell portable storage containers.

In 2014 Pac-Van bought Black Angus Steel & Supply Co. and Bulkhead Express, LLC for $5 million. Black Angus leases and sells containers from two locations in Texas.

The company also acquired LongVANS, Inc. for $14 million. LongVANS designs, manufactures, leases and sells portable storage containers, portable security containers and modular office trailers from two locations in Wisconsin.

Founded in 1993, Pac-Van was bought in 2006 by a group of investors including private equity firm D.E. Shaw and Pac-Van's management. General Finance Corporation acquired the company in 2008.
