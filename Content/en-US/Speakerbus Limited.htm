Need to communicate by audio and video remotely? Then hop on the Speakerbus. The company makes audio- and videoconferencing systems that combine hardware and software to enable remote users to engage in multimedia communication over phone lines, the Internet, and via hosted cloud-based services. Speakerbus' Hoot 'n' Holler product is used primarily by customers in financial trading to enable secure audioconferencing. The company also makes products for corporate videoconferencing and intercom applications.

Speakerbus is headquartered in the UK with offices and support centers across Europe, the US, and Asia.

Speakerbus sells primarily through resellers, including communications and multimedia software vendors Voice Print International and Atrium Communications. The company also makes sales through systems integrators, including such business partners as AS Solutions, Eze Castle , and Tel-Networks USA. Avaya , Cisco Systems , NICE Systems , Plantronics , and Red Box Recorders are among the company's technology development partners.

Speakerbus has added products for the defense and government markets, including Push To Talk (instant communications to one, many, or groups of users) and Netted Communications (voice and video communications systems over local, wide area, and global networks).

The company opened a website for its partners in 2015 that provides them with information about Speakerbus products. The site's content is for the sales and technical staffs of resellers and it offers a quick communications link with Speakerbus staff.

In 2014, Speakerbus sold its Network Services business to FLEXeNET for an undisclosed sum. The move helps Speakerbus focus more on its core financial business and forge stronger ties with telecoms companies, integrators, and technology partners.
