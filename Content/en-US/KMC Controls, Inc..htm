KMC Controls allows its customers to remain cool under pressure. The company makes HVAC actuators, relays, sensors, switches, thermostats, and transformers, as well as building automation system components, such as digital controllers, power meters, and modem interfaces. Products are sold by distributors throughout Canada and the US. KMC Controls pioneered the development of pneumatic control devices for HVAC applications, and was a major player in the production of analog electronic controls used in building system design. Ken Kreuter, an engineer in the controls industry, formed Mairco Controls in 1955. He sold that company to Robertshaw Controls in 1969 and founded KMC Controls that same year.
