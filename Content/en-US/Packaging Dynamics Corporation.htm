Specialty papermaker Packaging Dynamics operates through four units: BagcraftPapercon converts paper, film, and foil into food packaging, such as bags and wraps, butcher paper, pan liners, and tissue. International Converter produces adhesive and film extrusion lamination materials used to produce decorative cartons, bags, cans, and other packaging for consumer, medical, and industrial goods. De Luxe makes bags and sheets of paper and foil that come printed, laminated, waxed, coated, or slit under the names De Luxe, Coflex Packaging, and General Packaging. Last but not least, Pac Paper manufactures an assortment of paper products.

Packaging Dynamics was acquired by Novolex, a Wind Point Partners portfolio company, in late 2014.

BagcraftPapercon, Packaging Dynamics' main operating division, operates out of five facilities located in Illinois, Iowa, Kansas, Georgia, and California. International Converter has two plants in Ohio and Mississippi. De Luxe operates four facilities in Montreal; Toronto; Chicago; and Saugerties, New York. In addition, Pac Paper operates a facility in Washington.

Making good on its growth strategy, Packaging Dynamics has added new businesses and trimmed operations. In 2013 it sold Thilmany Papers to a new company formed and owned by investment funds sponsored by KPS Capital Partners . By selling the operations, Packaging Dynamics is shifting focus to its packaging and converting businesses while allowing the new specialty papers company to focus on its own set of customers.

In 2014 Packaging Dynamics acquired Pac Paper, located in Vancouver, Washington. Pac Paper manufactures and sells a broad range of specialty paper packaging products primarily for foodservice, food processing, agricultural, and industrial applications. The deal grew the company's operations in the Pacific Northwest and Western Canada.
