Why start a business in the chemicals industry? The raison d'etre for Interplastic is resins, but it also has a portfolio of esters, gel coats, and additives as well. The company's Thermoset Resins Division manufactures high-performance materials for the composites and cast polymer industries. The Molding Products Division makes molding compounds for automotive, industrial, and marine applications. Its North American Composites unit provides international distribution services. Interplastic markets its products to makers of everything from marine coatings to shower stalls.

Interplastic has development laboratories in Minneapolis and St. Paul, Minnesota; Fort Wright, Kentucky; Hawthorne, California; and South Bend, Indiana. It has two manufacturing plants in Minneapolis and one each in California, Kentucky, Oklahoma, and Washington. Its CoREZYN products are manufactured in Minneapolis and Vadnais Heights, Minnesota; Pryor, Oklahoma; and Kent, Washington.

Interplastic's Thermoset Resins Division manufactures unsaturated polyester, vinyl ester, specialty resins, gel coats, and colorants under the CoREZYN, Integrity, and Silmar brand names for the composites, cast polymer, and solid surface industries.

The North American Composites unit is a national distributor of composites raw materials to the marine, cast polymer, corrosion, construction, consumer, and related composites industries. The segment partners with more than 150 raw material suppliers and manages some 250 vendor product lines.

The company's Molding Products Division makes custom sheet molding compounds serving automotive, consumer, marine, and industrial thermoset compression molders. The division can manufacture sheet molding compounds in lots of up to 70,000 pounds.

The company serves fiberglass reinforced plastics, marine, transportation, and construction industries.

Interplastic sells its products directly and through a network of North American distributors (including Durr Marketing , Fiberglass Coatings, Fiberglass Florida, Fiberglass Hawaii, North American Composites, OP Fiberglass and Marine Supplies Ltd, Resiglass (PTY) LTD, Revchem, and Min-Chem Canada) and through distributors around the world.

By working in three interrelated divisions (Thermoset Resin, Molding Products, North American Composites) the corporation can offer integrated and customized services to fiberglass reinforced plastic and cast polymer manufacturers in a wide range of industries. It also invests heavily in product research at its five development laboratories.

In 2012 Interplastic expanded its North American Composites unit through the acquisition of a 41,000 sq. ft. facility in Mishawaka, Indiana, from which it serve scustomers across Indiana, as well as in Illinois, Michigan, and Ohio. Improvements to this facility included temperature controlled rooms for the storage of initiators, vinyl ester resins, and gel coats, the installation of a fiberglass mat slitter, and the addition of truck docks and a tanker unloading station.

Growing its asset portfolio, that year the company also acquired product technologies from paint and coatings giant Valspar , including Blush Guard (high performance gel coats for the marine industry), Conductive Primer Gel Coat (technology primer to improve transfer efficiencies of electrostatically applied coatings), and Quick Tint (general purpose Maximum Achievable Control Technology-compliant custom colored gel coats).

Interplastic was founded as a manufacturer of thermoset resins, gel coats, and colorants in 1959. It established its Molding Products division in 1978.
