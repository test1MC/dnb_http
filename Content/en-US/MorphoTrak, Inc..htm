MorphoTrak would be quite delighted to have your fingertips all over its products. The company develops and manufactures law enforcement biometric identification systems, products and services -- such as fingerprint readers and eye and facial recognition scanners -- for law enforcement, government, and commercial applications. Key uses for the company's products include applicant background checks and civil identification. MorphoTrak is a US subsidiary of French high-tech industrial firm SAFRAN . The company markets its products to the US and Canada.

MorphoTrak has corporate offices in Federal Way, Washington; Alexandria, Virginia; and Anaheim, California, as well as regional facilities throughout the US.

MorphoTrak's combined US customer base includes more than 50% of the law enforcement biometric identification systems at the state and local level. At the Federal level, the company provides Morpho algorithms at the core of the current FBI Integrated Automatic Fingerprint Identification System, and Printrak fingerprint identification systems used by a number of national security and defense programs.

The company offers its products to Law Enforcement Customers and Civil/Government Customers. It provides biometric identity solutions to more than 35 federal, state, and local governments across North America. State and local law enforcement is the company's biggest customer, representing more than 50% of sales.

In 2014 the company partnered with Identity One (as integrator) to provide a seamless biometric access and time and attendance system to a large North American freight railroad network company.

In 2013 the Morpho Mobile Gateway was developed by Morpho ( SAFRAN ) and supplied by its US subsidiary, MorphoTrak. The software allows Morpho mobile biometric devices to interface not only with the FBI's Repository for Individuals of Special Concern database, but with any Automated Fingerprint Identification System regardless of the vendor.

In addition, MorphoTrak has OEM relationships with major US integrators such as Northrop Grumman and Lockheed Martin .

MorphoTrak expanded in mid-2011 when its parent company acquired L-1 Identity Solutions for more than $1 billion and changed its name to MorphoTrust . SAFRAN garnered L-1's Solutions (biometrics, secure credentialing, and enterprise access) and Enrollment Services divisions. The purchase makes SAFRAN the leader in biometric identity solutions and significantly enhances MorphoTrak's earlier acquisitions, which include the 2010 purchase of tattoo matching technology developed by Michigan State University . The technology, known as Tattoo ID, allows for the searching of a tattoo image database in order to identify suspects, criminals, and victims.

Formerly SAGEM Morpho, the company became MorphoTrak in 2009 after SAFRAN acquired Printrak, the biometric business division of Motorola.
