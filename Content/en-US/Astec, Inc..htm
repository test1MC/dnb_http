Astec, a member company of the Asphalt Group at Astec Industries , designs and manufactures continuous and batch-process portable, relocatable, and stationary hot-mix asphalt (HMA) plants (Double Barrel-brand drum mixers, Six Pack-brand portable asphalt plants, Coater II facilities, and hot-mix storage silos), and soil and environmental remediation equipment. Astec also offers services that include plant safety, maintenance and operation, and emissions control classes for plant operators, service personnel, and superintendents. The company sells its products, new and used, around the world.

The company's geographic footprint extends throughout the US, Asia, Africa, Australia, Canada, Europe, the Middle East, South America, Southeast Asia, and the West Indies.

Astec also offers used products for asphalt (controls, generators, plants, silos, etc.), mobile asphalt (pavers, material transfer vehicles, milling machines), underground and utility (chain trenchers, drilling systems, and rocksaws), and aggregate applications (crushers, screens, washing).

The Asphalt Group business unit represents almost 30% of the total revenues generated by Astec Industries. Besides Astec, other members of this group include Heatec and CEI Enterprises. However, in 2012 Astec's operations received a boost when it assumed management for all asphalt plant production for all the Astec Industries' businesses, including CEI's lineup of Nomad and Rap King branded hot mix asphalt plants.

Astec's products are sold through 50 direct sales agents, nearly 20 domestic distributors, and roughly 50 international distributors.

Astec was founded in 1972 by Al Guth, Gail Mize, Norm Smith, and Mike Uchytil.
