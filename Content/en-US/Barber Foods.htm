Barber Foods doesn't produce mullets, Mohawks, or crew cuts. It specializes in developing and producing meal-sized portions of chicken. The company offers some 500 value-added poultry products, including all white-meat chicken breasts, chicken nuggets and fingers, and stuffed chicken entrees. Its packaged, frozen, fully cooked chicken is sold in retail supermarkets and club stores, including Safeway and Wal-Mart stores, as well as at BJ's Wholesale Club , Costco , and Sam's Club outlets nationwide. Barber Foods also provides meat products to the foodservice industry. The company was founded by Gus Barber in 1955. Cincinnati-based packaged foods maker AdvancePierre acquired Barber Foods in 2011.

AdvancePierre Foods , a packaged foods giant that makes value-added proteins and sandwiches, bought Barber Foods to boost its retail business, particularly on the US's East Coast and in Canada.

Based in Maine, Barber Foods operates its business nationwide.

To support its business, Barber Foods maintains a 150,000-sq.-ft. production facility in Portland, Maine. Its new parent has been investing millions of dollars in the manufacturing plant.

Barber Foods serves several sectors, such as retail supermarkets, warehouse stores, and foodservice outlets.

Parent AdvancePierre Foods has been focused on expanding the Barber Foods lineup. In 2014, for instance, the company introduced Tortilla Crunchers.
