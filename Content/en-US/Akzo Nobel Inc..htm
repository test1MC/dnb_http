Akzo Nobel Inc. works hard to create the right chemistry. The US and Canadian subsidiary of Dutch paints and chemicals giant Akzo Nobel N.V. , the company develops and makes performance coatings, and specialty chemicals. Its chemicals business includes catalysts, functional chemicals, polymers, pulp and paper chemicals, and surfactants. The coatings sector includes car refinishes, industrial coatings, and wood finishes and adhesives. Although the parent company is the world's largest coatings maker, its American subsidiary traditionally has been driven by its chemicals business.

Akzo Nobel has locations across the US and Canada.

The company's Performance Coatings business provides industrial customers with liquid and powder coatings for a wide range of OEM products made from wood, plastic, metal and concrete and includes Industrial Coatings, Wood Finishes and Adhesives, Marine and Protective Coatings, Automotive and Aerospace Coatings and Powder Coatings.

The Specialty Chemicals segment offers Surface Chemistry, Pulp and Performance Chemicals, and Functional Chemicals for use in shampoo, nutritional supplements, paper, plastic, building materials, pharmaceuticals, and food products.

Akzo Nobel its customers include Boeing , Etihad Airways , Exxon Mobil , Hyundai , and Royal Dutch Shell .

In 2013 North America contributed 15% of the total sales of parent company Akzo Nobel N.V. (6% down on 2012). Performance Coatings contributed for 38% of its total revenues; Specialty Chemicals, 34%.

The parent company's Decorative Paints revenues declined by 3% due to adverse currency effects and divestments. During 2013, Akzo Nobel N.V. reported continued soft demand for Specialty Chemicals, especially for construction-related products, pulp bleaching, and plastics.

In 2012 Akzo Nobel also divested its Decorative Paints North America unit to PPG Industries for more than $1 billion. Nobel's decorative paints included brands such as Glidden, Ralph Lauren, Devoe, Dulux, and Freshaire Choice.

In 2012 Akzo Nobel was the primary paint supplier to US-based mega-retailer Wal-Mart . Akzo Nobel began developing and manufacturing a portfolio of interior and exterior paints for Wal-Mart's more than 3,500 US stores with the Glidden and ColorPlace brands in 2011. The agreement extended a partnership with Wal-Mart for paints in Puerto Rico and Canada, and for Liquid Nails adhesives in the US. The company also relaunched its Glidden brand in 2011 with US-based partner Home Depot. The brand has become the most widely available paint brand in the US.

In Canada the company marketed decorative paints through major retailers like RONA, which it had been supplying since the mid-1970s. It distributed through other big box stores, independent and company-owned paint stores, and small and midsize outlets. In 2011 the parent company began a rollout of a single global brand identity for its retail consumer paint range with a "Let's Colour" marketing campaign. Canada, which holds a #1 market leadership position in the company's Decorative Paints segment, was chosen as a pilot market. That year the 235 company-owned and franchise stores in Canada were branded with the Dulux name.

The company was incorporated in 1982 as Akzo America, Inc. and changed its name to Akzo Nobel Inc. in 1994.
