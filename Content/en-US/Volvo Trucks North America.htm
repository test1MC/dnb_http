From Gothenburg to Greensboro, Volvo Trucks North America knows your 10-20. The company, a subsidiary of Sweden-based AB Volvo , makes big rigs and tractors for the 18-wheeler saturated North American market. The company manufactures heavy-duty Class 8 commercial trucks and tractors sold under the Volvo brand. Its models, which comprise its VHD vocational series and VN tractor series, include nine cab models designed for a variety of long-haul transportation jobs. Volvo Trucks North America also makes powertrain products designed for heavy loads and steep grades (its D16) and lighter loads (D13 and D11). Sales and service are provided through a dealer network that spans the US and Canada.

The company's trucks are assembled at the New River Valley Plant in Dublin, Virginia; Volvo engines for North America are assembled in Hagerstown, Maryland.

Volvo Trucks North America is part of a larger truck manufacturing organization operated by parent AB Volvo (e.g. Volvo Trucks, Renault Trucks, UD Trucks, Eicher, and Mack Trucks ). In North America, Volvo Trucks North America and its sister company Mack represent the bulk of its parent's heavy-duty truck business.

The company sells its trucks through more than 350 dealers and thousands of service locations across North America.

AB Volvo got 67% of its 2014 revenues from the Truck segment, and 27% from North America.

In 2015 the company introduced the Volvo VAH 630, a premium sleeper version for long-haul operations, an addition to its lineup of Volvo Autohauler models. It also launched a rugged version of the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission for severe-duty applications.

Growing its service offerings, in 2014 Volvo Trucks North America increased its service capabilities in the Northeast US with the expansion of the Young Truck Sales facility in Canton, Ohio, growing its dealer network and the capabilities.That year it also added a new Bruckner's Truck Sales facility in Albuquerque, New Mexic. expanding its service capacity in the Southwest.

Driving some of the demand for its trucks was the company's 2010 introduction of an EPA -compliant truck equipped with selective catalytic reduction (SCR) technology. The trucks run at near-zero emissions and are up to 5% more fuel efficient. Rallying sales, customer Talon Logistics led took delivery of the first certified trucks in late 2009, followed by significant orders from Knight Transportation and Penske Truck Leasing in 2010. Going forward, the company intends to center its vehicle development efforts on improving emissions and fuel efficiency.

In 2010 increased demand for the new compliant trucks spurred the company to recall former employees to its New River plant, which boosted headcount by 23%. The New River plant, located in Dublin, Virginia, is Volvo Trucks North America's newest facility and manufactures Volvo's VN and VHD lines. (New River employees took a hit the year before when Volvo Trucks North America made a series of heavy layoffs.) Volvo Trucks North America has also taken steps in recent years to streamline its operations; in 2010 it began sharing campus headquarters in Greensboro, North Carolina with Mack.

AB Volvo built its first truck in 1928. It entered the North American truck market in 1959.
