WABCO is a global auto parts maker. The company designs and sells braking, stability, suspension, transmission control, and air compressing systems for commercial trucks, trailers, buses, SUVs, and passenger cars. It also makes shock absorbers, cruise control components, and tire pressure monitors. The company's products are sold directly to auto and component manufacturers, including Daimler , Ashok Leyland , BMW , PACCAR , and Volvo , while spare parts are sold through independent aftermarket dealers. WABCO derives the majority of its sales outside the US, primarily in Europe.

The company's manufacturing activities are located at roughly 28 sites in 13 countries. The company also has a dozen engineering centers. Its largest market is Germany, representing 22% of net sales. Other major markets include the rest of Europe (56%) and the US (16%).

WABCO also extends its international reach through the use of joint ventures. It has a German-based JC named WABCO Würth Workshop Services with Würth Group . In North America, WABCO markets anti-lock braking systems (ABS) through its 50%-owned partnership with ArvinMeritor Automotive called Meritor WABCO. It also owns a 70% stake WABCO Compressor Manufacturing Co., a JV that allows it to sell air compressors with Cummins .

About 55% of the company's revenues come from bus and truck OEMs; the second largest revenue generator is its aftermarket business. WABCO's top 10 customers account for almost half of its sales, with Daimler as its biggest customer, generating 10% of sales. Other customers include trailer manufacturers and automobile and SUV manufacturers.

After posting record-setting revenue of $2.85 billion in 2014, WABCO saw its revenues dip by 8% to $2.6 billion in 2015. Its profits also fell 6% from $292 million to $275 million during that same time period.

The declines for 2015 were fueled by a 12% decrease in Europe sales and a 9% decline in other international countries. It also experienced lower demand from the OEM (7%) and aftermarket (10%) segments.

The company's advanced technology keeps up with the industry, as well as paves new roads for the design of control systems for safety and performance. There is a growing market for a higher electronics content supporting control systems in commercial vehicles. Both European and Asian governments have enacted government mandates for more safety systems, opening opportunities for WABCO to answer the demand. Some of WABCO's innovations include the first heavy-duty truck ABS, the first commercial vehicle automated manual transmission controls system, and the first electronic stability control system (ESC), to name a few.

WABCO has achieved growth through the use of acquisitions. In 2016 WABCO acquired MICO Incorporated, a global market provider of hydraulic components, controls and brake systems for heavy-duty, off-highway vehicles in agriculture, construction, mining, and similar industries for a purchase price of $75 million. MICO is headquartered in North Mankato, Minnesota. The acquisition provided it with access to new growth opportunities in the off-highway industry.

In 2014 it acquired Transics International, a provider of fleet management products and services in Europe. The deal enabled the company to broaden its business relationships with fleets around the world. WABCO also integrated Transics' telematics technology product with its own TX-TRAILERGUARD fleet management product.

WABCO started out in 1869 as the Westinghouse Air Brake Company. It was founded by George Westinghouse who invented the first air brake in 1868.
