Good health is on a roll at SCA Hygiene Products UK. Its operations, consolidated under SCA Hygiene Products (Fluff), are the UK arm of hygiene and paper giant Svenska Cellulosa Aktiebolaget SCA . SCA Hygiene Products UK develops, makes, and sells personal care and tissue products under the Tork brand for restrooms away from home in the healthcare, foodservice, and industrial markets. Other UK brands for home use are TENA (incontinence care), Bodyform (feminine care), Plenty, Tempo, and Zewa (handkerchiefs, toilet tissue, napkins, kitchen rolls). The company also offers retailers' private-label items. The UK is its parent's second largest market (Europe accounted for 63% of 2015 sales).

The company operates in the UK and Ireland. It has three commercial offices (Dunstable, Manchester, and Dublin); six manufacturing sites (in Derbyshire, Manchester, Flint, Northumberland, and Lancashire); and two depots in the UK and Republic of Ireland.

SCA Hygiene Products UK Ltd. is a part of SCA Hygiene Products, which operates in three business areas including consumer goods, away from home and Continence Care. SCA's Consumer Goods business is made up of personal care and tissue products for use in the home, including leading household brands Bodyform, TENA, Velvet, Plenty and Cushelle. SCA's B2B or 'Away from Home' business provides Tork branded toilet tissue, paper hand towels, soaps, wipes and cloths to professional offices and work places in the commercial, manufacturing and industrial sectors across the UK, as well as a wide selection of napkin products for the hospitality markets. SCA's Continence Care business offers the global TENA brand to care homes, hospitals, and other healthcare professionals.

The company sells it products to the commercial, manufacturing, and industrial sectors.

SCA Hygiene Products accounted for about 85% of Svenska Cellulosa 's 2015 sales.

For better return on investments, in Svenska Cellulosa plans to split into two listed companies (hygiene and forest products) in 2017.

The UK subsidiary plays a role in its parent's plan to meet demand for sustainable hygienic products through productive, cost-effective production plants and other efforts. For its part, SCA Hygiene Products UK benefits from one of Svenska Cellulosa's large, high-tech paper and pulp mills and sawmills located in the UK. SCA Hygiene Products UK is also involved in a program to replant trees that goes beyond industry standards.

The company came under fire from Kimberly-Clark Europe , which challenged advertisements for the Velvet tissue brand as misleading. In 2012 the council for The Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) sided with SCA Hygiene Products UK, saying that the company substantiated its advertisement that Velvet planted three trees for each one used, adding three million extra trees, among several claims.
