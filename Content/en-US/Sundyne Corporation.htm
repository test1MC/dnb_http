From sun-up to sun-down, Sundyne's products get businesses pumped up. The company designs and manufactures centrifugal pumps, specialty water technology pumps, magnetic drive pumps, compressors, and blowers for the process fluid and gas industries. Its products are geared primarily towards petrochemical, food and dairy, refinery, pharmaceutical, and general industry applications. Sundyne also offers factory overhaul and repair, parts supply, and engineering services. Brands include Anisimag, Sundyne, Sunflo, HMD Kontro, and Marelli. Sundyne is owned by BC Partners and The Carlyle Group and is part of their Accudyne Industries operations.

The company maintains about five manufacturing locations in France, Spain, China, the UK, and the US. It serves customers in more than 117 countries.

Sundyne's pumps and compressors are used by oil and gas processing companies, the chemical and petro-chemical industries, the power generation and refining industry, and pharmaceuticals. The company markets its products from over 300 independently owned channel partner locations (through representatives and distributors) to more than 2,000 customers globally.

The company was originally founded in 1905 by David Sundstrand, who invented the 10-key adding machine. It was known as the Sundstrand Machine Tool Company until Sundstrand Corporation was formed in 1962. United Technologies Corporation bought Sundstrand Corporation in 1999 and merged it with another of its subsidiaries, Hamilton Standard, to form Hamilton Sundstrand. In late 2012 UTC sold Sundyne to BC Partners and The Carlyle Group for $3.46 billion.
