Plymouth Tube manufactures alloy, carbon, and stainless steel tubing at its network of metalworking mills. The Plymouth Extruded Shapes unit makes near-net extruded and cold drawn shapes in alloy, carbon, and stainless steel, as well as in nickel-based alloy and titanium. The family-owned and-operated company serves customers in the aerospace, nuclear, oil and gas, performance racing, alternative energy, and mining markets, among others, from facilities in about half a dozen US states. Plymouth Tube's Trent (Wisconsin) plant is one of a few 21-6-9 (high-manganese, nitrogen-strengthened stainless steel) tubing manufacturers and the only 21-6-9 approved supplier for Boeing 's 787 Dreamliner aircraft.

Plymouth Tube has a dozen US mills: Illinois (2), Indiana (3), Louisiana (1), Kentucky (1), Maryland (1), Mississippi (2), and Wisconsin (2).

It provides a wide range of specialty tubing and extruded shapes for global customers from its large network of metal-working facilities (more than 1.6 million sq. ft. of manufacturing and office space).

Plymouth Tube's products are used in a wide range of applications, including fuel and hydraulic lines and landing gear for the aerospace industry; drill rods and drilling components for the oil and gas industry; feedwater heater and boiler tubing for the nuclear industry; and wall anchors and roof bolts for the mining industry.

Plymouth Engineered Shapes unit manufactures titanium, stainless, nickel alloy, carbon and alloy semi-finished extrusions and semi-finished shaped bar.

The company serves markets such as aerospace, commercial, oil and gas, mobile equipment, performance racing, energy/power generation, petrochemical and chemical processing, and mining.

To grow capacity to meet demand, in 2011 Plymouth Tube announced plans to invest more than $12 million in its 130,000-sq.-ft. Hopkinsville Kentucky plant, to add a new product line to increase output of titanium extrusion products for commercial aircraft.

Plymouth Tube was founded in 1924.
