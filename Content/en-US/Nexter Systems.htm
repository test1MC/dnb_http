Nexter Systems is state-owned and operates through its main Nexter Systems (weapons systems, armored vehicles), Nexter Munitions (munitions, warheads), Nexter Mechanics (mechanical maintenance, repair), Nexter Robotics (air and land robots), and Nexter Electronics (electronic maintenance, repair). It makes the Leclerc main battle tank; mine-clearing and wheeled armored infantry fighting vehicles (VBCI); Caesar artillery systems; helicopter cannons; tank and medium-caliber ammunition; and infantry, tank and armored vehicle battle management systems. Nexter provides engineering support, training, technical documentation, test equipment, maintenance, and modernization services.

The company sells to armed forces in France and about 100 other countries. Its munitions division produces ammunition, warheads, pyrotechnic components, and safety mechanisms for use in missiles, rockets, torpedoes, drones, and submarine systems.

Nexter, especially its electronics division, also serves civil markets through aeronautics and energy management and distribution applications. The company sees the diversification in business as essential to long-term development. Customers include Dassault Aviation , Liebherr, and Technofan, to name a few.

The company offers Generic Virtual Training (GVT) through its subsidiary Nexter Training, which is part of Nexter Systems. The company partnered with the French Engineer School -- Ecole Supérieure et d'Application du Matériel (ESAM) at Bourges -- to design and integrate technical training materials and systems to instruct in the operation of the Leclerc battletank.

In 2014 Nexter Systems acquired Mecar (in Belgium) and Simmel Difesa (Italy) from Chemring Group Plc . The deals strengthened Nexter's ammunition division and established its position among the European leaders in this area. The acquisitions enriched its range of ammunition products in the three domains of land, air, and sea ammunition. It additionally extended its commercial footprint on key export markets.
