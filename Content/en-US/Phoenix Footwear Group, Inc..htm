Phoenix Footwear is a well-shod bird. The company manufactures comfort  footwear under the Trotters and SoftWalk names for women and H.S. Trask for men. Trotters products include sandals, boots, and dress and casual footwear. Focused on comfort, the SoftWalk brand boasts clogs, slings, and casual styles. Its Western-inspired footwear, H.S. Trask, comprises boots, oxfords, moccasins, and slippers. Phoenix Footwear's products are manufactured overseas -- primarily in Brazil and China -- and are sold through some 1,155 US retailers, including mass merchants, major department stores, mail order companies, and specialty shoe retailers. Founded in 1882 as Daniel Green Company, the shoemaker changed its name in 2001.
