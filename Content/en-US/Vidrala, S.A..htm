If drinking out of one's hands ever becomes a new fad, Vidrala might be in trouble. The company is one of Spain's largest glass bottle and container manufacturers, primarily targeting the food and beverage industry. It makes some 600 products in six colors (white flint, emerald green, amber, dark green, dead leaf, and moss green), including bottles for soft drinks, water, oil, vinegar, juices, beers, and wine. Founded in 1965, Vidrala operates six manufacturing plants and about a dozen melting furnaces -- in Belgium, France, Italy, Portugal, and Spain -- with an annual sales exceeding $652 million. It serves more than 1,600 customers in Spain and the European Union.
