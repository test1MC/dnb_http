Made in the USA, Toshiba America Electronic Components (TAEC) aims to turn the world on to its semiconductors, flash-memory storage devices, LCDs, and radio-frequency components. A subsidiary of Toshiba America , which is owned by Japan's goliath semiconductor maker Toshiba Corporation , TAEC designs and makes the key components for consumer electronics and mobile devices, digital music players, cameras, medical devices, car electronics, and more. Electron tubes and optoelectronics are also part of its lineup. The company sells to OEMs, original design manufacturers, contract manufacturers, fabless chip makers and distributors around the globe.

TAEC has more than a dozen locations across the US, as well as one location in Brazil.

Leading distributors of TEAC products include Avnet , Arrow Electronics , Ingram Micro , Synnex , and TTI .

The company grows via expansion of its product lines. Over the past several years it has added to its lines of gallium nitride high electron mobility transistors, Low Drop-Out regulators, LEDs, optocouplers, and memory cards.
