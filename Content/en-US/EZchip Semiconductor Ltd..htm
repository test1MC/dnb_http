When it comes to semiconductors, EZ does it: EZchip Semiconductor, that is, produces networking chips. Through its EZchip Technologies subsidiary the company develops high-speed processors used in enterprise and metropolitan-area networks. The company provides products that scale from a few to hundreds of Gigabits-per-second. In 2013 Cisco accounted for 27% of EZchip Semiconductor's revenue; Juniper, 19%; and ZTE, 15%. The company phased out its legacy products (including the LANmaker line of Gigabit Ethernet switches and the StackNetPro and StarNet models of network hubs) in order to focus on network processor products.
