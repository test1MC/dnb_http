Conn-Selmer loves the big band sound. The company is the US's largest maker of band and orchestral instruments, such as brass, woodwind, percussion, and string instruments, as well as their accessories, under such brands as Armstrong, C.G. Conn, Glaesel, Ludwig, Scherl & Roth, and Selmer. Conn-Selmer operates manufacturing facilities in Illinois, Indiana, North Carolina, Ohio, and Wisconsin, as well as in France. It also sources some instruments from Asia to keep costs down. The company, which is a division of piano giant Steinway Musical Instruments and represents Steinway's band segment, established the Conn-Selmer Institute to help music graduates transition into careers as teachers.

Indiana-based Conn-Selmer operates production facilities in Elkhart, Indiana; Cleveland and Eastlake, Ohio; Monroe, North Carolinba; LaGrange, Illinois.

Conn-Selmer relies on a network of independent dealers and distributors to distribute all of its band instruments. The majority of the company's instruments are purchased or rented from dealers by students enrolled in music education programs in the US.

Prior to parent Steinway Musical Instruments being taken private in 2013, the company reported band sales of about $137 million for 2012, an increase of 5% versus the prior year. (In 2012 Conn-Selmer represented 39% of Steinway's total revenue.)

In 2012 student level instruments accounted for about 60% of band and orchestral shipments and approximately one-third of band instruments sales, with intermediate and professional instruments making up the balance.
