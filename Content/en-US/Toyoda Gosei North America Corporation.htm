The rubber hits the road and then some for diversified manufacturing company Toyoda Gosei North America. One of four regional subsidiaries of Japan-based Toyoda Gosei , the company researches, develops, and manufactures rubber- and polymer-based products (airbags, drivetrain parts, brake hoses, plastic fuel tanks, and instrument panels). In addition, it makes optoelectronics (LED lighting modules) for automotive, aviation, traffic control, and telecommunications markets. Key customers include Toyota , General Motors , Honda , and Nissan . The company operates a dozen manufacturing plants, R&D facilities, and sales offices throughout North America.

Toyoda Gosei North America has locations in the US (California, Kentucky, Michigan, Missouri, and Texas); Canada (Quebec and Ontario); and Mexico.

The company operates twelve sales, research and design, and manufacturing locations.

Toyoda Gosei North Americas serves a wide range of industries including from automotive, agriculture, and telecommunications. Its automotive OEM customers include Toyota , General Motors , Daimler , Ford , Honda , Isuzu , Mitsubishi , and Nissan .

Parent company Toyoda Gosei gained 27% of its revenue from the Americas operations.

The company announced plans in 2014 to expand its operations in Troy, investing up to $7.9 million.

In 2014 Toyoda Gosei North Americas invested $67 million into a 398,000-square-foot plant, which will serve the automotive industry with plastic components, including radiator grilles and console boxes across Mexico. Production is expected to begin in 2016.

In 2015 the company's TG Fluid Systems USA Corporation subsidiary acquired a new plant to consolidate operations and raise efficiency. It plans to move and consolidate production equipment from two of its plants at Brighton and Howell into the new plant by 2017.

Toyoda Gosei began operating in 1949 as the rubber research division (Nagoya Rubber) of Toyota Motor . It launched the North American business in 1986.
