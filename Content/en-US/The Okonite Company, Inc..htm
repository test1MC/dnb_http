Thomas Edison had a bright idea, but it was The Okonite Company that prevented it from being too shocking. Employee-owned Okonite is one of the first US makers of insulated electrical wire and cable. Early customers included not only Edison but also telegraphy pioneer Samuel Morse. Okonite specializes in control, distribution, instrumentation, power, signal, communication transmission, and special purpose wire and cable products for high and low voltage needs. The company supplies its products to chemical plants, electric utilities, paper mills, railroads, refineries, and other industries.

Based in New Jersey, Okonite operates primarily in the US and boasts a global reach through its international unit. It also has an office in Singapore.

The company's six manufacturing plants are all US-based, located in Ashton, Rhode Island; Paterson, New Jersey; Orangeburg, South Carolina; Richmond, Kentucky; and Santa Maria, California. It also operates a handful of service centers, a research and engineering laboratory, and an international division. Okonite's South Carolina plant specializes in cable flame testing.

Okonite makes its products, including its branded Okonite Okoguard all EPR (ethylene propylene rubber) insulation system, using laminated polypropylene paper, thermosetting, and thermoplastic insulation and jacketing compounds. It also offers metallic and non-metallic coverings for use in different environments.

The company serves several industries to maintain a diverse revenue stream. It caters to sectors such as electric utility, oil and gas, chemical, military bases, railroad, transit, and pulp and paper. Okonite also sells cables to hospitals, colleges and universities, water treatment plants, and data centers. For the renewable energy field, the company provides its cables to wind farms, solar farms, and biofuel generating facilities.

Okonite's boasts a nationwide network of about two dozen district sales offices staffed by cable professionals. These sales offices are supported by half a dozen service center locations and an equal number of manufacturing locations. Okonite's Kansas plant houses a sales division that serves the railroad industry.

Okonite was founded in 1878 and has been employee-owned since 1976.
