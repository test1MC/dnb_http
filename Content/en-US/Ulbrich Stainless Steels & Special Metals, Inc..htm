Ulbrich Stainless Steels & Special Metals has had a hand in objects ranging from the down-to-earth (spoons) to the high-flying (space vehicles). Ulbrich makes a diversified line of stainless steel and specialty metal products, including custom-shaped wire and foil, bar, plate, and cut near-net shapes. Its fabrication plants provide brushing, coating, painting, and cut-to-length shapes. The family-owned business serves the aerospace, power generation, and automotive industries through service and distribution centers throughout North America as well as in Asia and Europe.

Ulbrich has manufacturing plants in Austria, Canada, China, Mexico, and the US. The company has nine subsidiaries in the US, Mexico, Canada, and Austria.

Through its 15 companies, Ulrich is a master manipulator of high-temperature and corrosion-resistant metals. It is a precision re-roller processing stainless steel, nickel alloy, and titanium alloy strip for high performance applications. Ulrich produces stainless steel strip, special metals strip, foil, sheet, plate, bar and PV ribbon products. It also shapes rod or wire into various cross sections, and rolls fine round wire into flat wire to dimensions that are only a fraction of a human hair. Ulbrich also operates a network of specialty service centers with six North American locations supplying various forms of stainless steel and specialty metals.

The company has sales and marketing operations in Argentina, Brazil, China, Colombia, Czech Republic France, Germany, Israel, Italy, Japan, Korea, Malaysia, Poland, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Turkey, the UK, and the US.

Ulrich's sells its products to companies in the aerospace, appliance, automotive, chemical and petrochemical, consumer goods, electronics, medical, and power generation markets, among others.

The company continued to invest in equipment, technology and staff to meet the needs of their customers in demanding markets such as medical, nuclear, aerospace, solar, electronics.

In 2012 Ulbrich relocated its North Haven, Connecticut corporate headquarters (which suffered heavy damage from Hurricane Irene) to a new and more modern facility offering improved amenities.

Boosting its Asian portfolio, in 2011 the company opened a new Precision Coil Center in Malaysia specializing in light gauge stainless steel and special metals strip. It offers more than 170 types of strips, many not readily available in Asia. Alloys used range from Austenitic, Ferritic, Martensitic to Precipitation Hardening Grade Stainless Steels.

That year the company also opened Ulbrich Precision Metals Asia in Hong Kong, a flat wire manufacturing facility providing solarcell tabbing and string interconnect for the solar industry.

However, Asia is not the only growth area for the company. Growing its North American capacity, in 2011 Ulrich opened a 65,000 sq. ft. facility in Canada to allow it to add aluminum sheets to its stainless steel products and other customer offerings.

The Ulbrich company was established in 1924 as a cutlery manufacturer by Fred Ulbrich, Sr.
