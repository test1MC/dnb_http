True to its name, Diversified Chemical Technologies manufactures a diverse range of specialty chemical products for a diverse range of customers. It serves a number of industries, but is best known for its process and maintenance chemicals for metalworking, and cleaning and sanitation chemicals for the food and beverage markets. Working through subsidiaries, it produces various adhesives (hot melt, pressure sensitive, and water-based adhesives), polymeric materials (including PVC seals, epoxies, acrylics, polyesters, and polyurethanes), custom-formulated specialty products, and recycled polymeric materials to make polyurethane foam for autos. It also offers office supplies through Detroit-based Paperworks.

The company operates primarily in Detroit, where it is based, but also has facilities in Aguascalientes, Mexico, and a European distribution center in Munich, Germany.

Its Adhesive Systems unit manufactures a range of adhesives for consumer and industrial packaging. Customers include Kraft Foods , Pepperidge Farm , Philip Morris USA , and Procter & Gamble . The company works with its customers to develop adhesives that maximize efficiencies at production line speeds of 50 to 200 units per minute. It has a capacity to produce more than 30 million pounds of hot melt and 10 million pounds of water-based and pressure-sensitive adhesives a year.

Its Diversitak unit makes polymeric materials and engineered products for various end markets, including the aerospace, automotive, construction, consumer product, electronic, furniture, ransportation, food and beverage, packaging, electrical, and consumer products industries. industries. The unit capitalizes on its alliances and joint ventures worldwide. Customers include DaimlerChrysler , Ford Motor Company , Nissan , Johnson Controls , and Lear Corporation .

The flagship unit, Diversified Chemical Technologies, produces more than 200 custom items for applications such as cleaning and processing. Its customers include Deere & Company , ISG , Timken Company , and Wendy's International . The unit provides such services as custom-designed fluid-management products, usage tracking, and materials handling and safety programs.

Its Paperworks unit distributes paper, record storage and shredders, and other office supply products.

The company's Recycled Polymeric Materials produces components and materials that are available as either manufactured components or bulk compounds. It supplies polyurethane recycled rubber that is based on components and materials for the automotive, appliance, aerospace, and heavy transportation industries. The custom formulations include 25% recycled rubber from used tires (such as its custom polymer foam) and 17% annually renewable bio resources (such as soybeans and corn). Its products are found on more automobiles worldwide. Recycled Polymeric Materials' products can be found in almost 25 million automobiles worldwide.

A fourth of the company's employees are involved in its research and development operations. The company operates six R&D laboratories and maintains affiliations with as many Michigan state universities to support some of its R&D efforts.

Diversified Chemicals' customers include companies in such industries as automotive, consumer packaging, electronics, steel, and transportation. In addition to those mentioned above, its clients include Magna Interantional , Molson Coors Brewing , Kroger Company , Lear Corporation , Miller Brewing Co , Pepperidge Farm , and Visteon .

Its major European distributor is TecServ GmbH, based in Munich, Germany.

The company is looking to take advantage of the growing societal push for clean and renewable energy by expanding 'green' chemistry applications to include battery and light military vehicle parts; agricultural bio-fuel products; and solar and wind turbine adhesives.

To raise cash and focus on its core operations, in 2011 the company sold COAT-IT (a producer of sealers, dampers, adhesives and reinforcing parts) to REVOCOAT Americas. The transaction did not include the COAT-IT Trim adhesives unit, which remained as a part of Diversified Chemicals' portfolio.

Minority-owned Diversified Chemical Technologies was founded in 1971 by high school friends George Hill and Arnold Joseff in an area in Detroit that had been almost deserted after the 1967 Detroit riots.
