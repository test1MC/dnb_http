Phibro Animal Health (Phibro) makes feeding time down on the farm a happy occasion. The company's Animal Health and Nutrition segment (more than 65% of Phibro's revenues) produces animal feed additives, including trace minerals, vitamins, and antibiotics for livestock, poultry, and aquaculture markets worldwide. Phibro's Performance Products segment manufactures specialty chemicals for agricultural and industrial uses, including ingredients for the ethanol, automotive, metal, and personal care industries. Phibro also supplies antimicrobials used in ethanol production.

The company's Animal Health and Nutrition segment is its largest and contains its Prince Agri Products subsidiary, a global supplier of animal feed and nutrition products for the livestock and pet food markets. Prince Agri Products is the largest supplier of trace minerals to the US animal nutrition market.

The Animal Health and Nutrition segment also holds Israel-based ABIC Biological Laboratories, which develops and supplies products such as poultry vaccines, anti-infectives, and disinfectants for the poultry market. It holds the leading market share in poultry vaccines in Israel and has strong brand recognition worldwide. The company is the largest global manufacturer of two major anticoccidial drugs (nicarbazin and amprolium) used to combat coccidiosis, a parasitic disease in chickens.

Divisions of the Performance Products segment include PhibroChem and Ferro Metal & Chemical, which market and distribute specialty chemicals to consumer product companies. Performance chemicals include active ingredients for dental and hair care markets. Ferro Metal & Chemical also provides products for metal plating and surface finishing.

In addition, the segment includes Phibro Ethanol Performance Group, which supplies fermentation processing additives for ethanol producers. It includes inorganic chemicals manufacturer Phibro-Tech, which supplies copper-based chemicals to US industrial markets. Phibro-Tech supplies products for water treatment and metal etching, including a line of specialty etchants used in printed circuit boards. It also recycles acid, bases, and certain metals. The Performance Products segment manufactures various custom chemicals for its customers.

Phibro has manufacturing facilities in southern Israel and Brazil, along with state-of-the-art research laboratories and a tech center. It holds more than 750 animal health product registrations (including 450 medicinal feed additives registrations and 300 vaccine registrations) in 50 countries, and sells more than 1,400 product presentations in more than 65 countries to about 3,000 customers.

The company maintains sales offices throughout the world in countries including the US (63% of sales), Canada, Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, Chile, the UK, Belgium, Turkey, Israel, South Africa, China, Malaysia, and Australia.

Phibro sells animal health and mineral nutrition products to poultry, swine, and cattle integrators and commercial animal feed manufacturers, wholesalers, and distributors.

The company has a 300-strong sales, marketing, and technical support organization. It also works with 200 distributors.

The company's fiscal 2016 (June year end) net sales grew by less than 1% to $751.5 million.

Animal Health net revenue grew by 3% due to volume increases across all segment product groups. Nutritional specialty products grew by 15%, due to US and EU volume growth of products for the dairy and poultry industries. Medicinal feed additives and other grew by 1%, as volume growth in international markets offset declines in US volumes. Vaccines declined by 2%. Excluding the prior year $8 million in vaccine licensing milestone revenue, vaccines grew by $6.8 million, or 15%, principally from volume growth.

Mineral Nutrition net sales decreased by 5% due to lower average selling prices as the result of lower raw material commodity prices. Increased volumes for trace mineral products partially offset lower average selling prices.

Net sales of  Performance Products decreased by 4% due to lower average selling prices of copper-based products and personal care ingredients and lower volumes of chemical catalyst products.

Phibro's fiscal 2016 net income grew by $22.4 million to $82.7 million. Animal Health gross profit increased $6.8 million due to volume growth, lower unit costs from improved operating efficiencies, and favorable currency movements. Mineral Nutrition gross profit grew by $0.3 million due to lower material costs, partially offset by lower selling prices. Performance Products gross profit declined by $1.3 million due to lower prices of copper-based products and personal care ingredients, partially offset by a drop in material costs.

Phibro Animal Health expands its product portfolio organically and through partnerships and acquisitions. It has focused its efforts on geographic regions where the majority of livestock production is consolidated in large commercial farms such as the, Brazil, China, Russia, Mexico, Australia, Turkey, Israel, Canada, Europe, and the US.

Chairman, President, and CEO Jack Bendheim controls about 91% of Phibro, which went public in April 2014. The company raised $176.5 million through the IPO which it used to pay down debt, pay related fees and expenses, and for general corporate purposes.

In 2011 Prince Agri Products, a Phibro Animal Health subsidiary, expanded by acquiring Animate, a major nutrition technology used for dairy cows, from US-based Granco Minerals. Prince had been working with Animate for more than 10 years before acquiring the product.

In 2009 Phibro made two acquisitions to boost its animal health and animal feeds businesses. The first was of Abic Animal Health from Teva Pharmaceuticals ; it makes vaccines, pharmaceuticals, and antibiotics. The second deal was for the trace minerals business of Baltzell Agri Products; trace minerals are added to animal feed to provide added nutritional benefits. Also that year, the company sold its wood preservative business, PhibroWood, to Osmose Holdings .

In 2000, shoftly after Phibro was formed as a subsidiary of Phillip Brothers Chemicals, it acquired a medicated feed additives line from Pfizer. Phillip Brothers changed the parent company's name to Phibro Animal Health in 2003.
