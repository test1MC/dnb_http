Toyoda Gosei has a yen to make more of its revenue in dollars, yuans, and euros. The manufacture of auto components, such as instrument panels, hoses, and airbags, composes about 95% of sales non automobile sales include light-emitting diodes (LEDs) and plastic cases for cell phones and air purifiers. Though Japan accounts for almost 45% of its sales, Toyoda Gosei is focusing its sales growth on foreign markets. To support that expansion, Toyoda Gosei has increased its operations in China, Vietnam, and the US. Major customers include Toyota Motor , which founded Toyoda Gosei in 1949 as Nagoya Rubber and continues to own nearly 43% of the company.

Toyoda Gosei operates a network of around 100 plants and offices in 18 countries and regions, including Japan, Americas, Asia, Australia, Europe, and Africa. Japan is its largest market, generating 44% of net sales. Other major markets include the US (27%), Asia and Australia (23%), and Europe and Africa (6%).

Toyoda Gosei operates across seven operating divisions. Automotive Parts develops and produces rubber and plastic products for automobiles, while Automotive Sealing Products manufactures water-based surface treatment agents with the aim of reducing volatile organic compounds.

Functional Components produces plastic fuel filler pipes and lightweight parts made of plastic, including turbo ducts and fuel filler lids. Interior and Exterior Parts produces driver-side airbags, compact passenger-side, side, curtain, and knee airbags.

In addition, LED produces various lighting devices that use LEDs, including fluorescent and security lights, while Optoelectronic Products develops and supplies LED chips for room and area lighting. General Industry Products produces LED lighting products and backlights for liquid crystals, light sources for lighting devices, air conditioning products, home construction components, and construction and industrial machinery components.

Toyoda Gosei's revenue increased by 15% from 2013 to 2014 due primarily to growth in the number of automobiles produced and active expansion of sales to European and American automobile manufacturers; it also earned increased revenue from a currency exchange effect during 2014. In addition, the company's net income climbed 22% from 2013 to 2014 due to the increased automotive parts sales and reduced expenses as a result of cost reduction initiates.

In 2014 the company's Toyoda Gosei North America subsidiary launched a new manufacturing facility in Central Mexico to meet a strong car production increase in the country. The company will start operations in April 2016 and will supply radiator grilles, console boxes, and other plastic parts to automakers in the rapidly expanding North American market.
