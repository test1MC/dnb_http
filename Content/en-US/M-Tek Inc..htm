Products of auto parts manufacturer M-Tek include both seen and unseen components of car interiors. The company makes door and trunk trim, dash insulators, headliners, roof systems, and other trim products. It also provides design, material development, testing, and tooling manufacturing. Major customers have included car making giants such as General Motors , Honda , Volkswagen , and Nissan . M-Tek is a subsidiary of Japanese auto parts maker Kasai Kogyo . It operates from facilities in Alabama, Michigan, Mississippi, Ohio, and Tennessee that are located near the assembly plants of its customers.

The company serves several North American assembly plants from its locations in Tennessee, Alabama, Mississippi, Ohio, and Michigan. It exports products to Mexico and Asia.

In 2014 M-Tek announced plans to build a new headquarters in Murfreesboro, Tennessee at a cost of $13.4 million. The facility will support operations in Mexico, Michigan, Ohio, Mississippi, and Alabama, as well as Manchester, Smyrna, and Chattanooga in Tennessee. It is expected to be completed in 2016.
