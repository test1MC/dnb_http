Roush Industries can put a roar under your hood. Through its sister companies, Roush Industries provides automotive engineering, development, and parts manufacturing services, as well as motor sports marketing services. Specialties including prototyping, engineering of performance vehicles, and manufacturing of appearance and engine performance components. The company's Roush Fenway Racing unit operates several NASCAR racing teams. Roush CleanTech converts truck engines to run on liquid propane injection fuel technology. Roush Life Sciences develops laboratory research products and medical devices.

Roush Industries has more than 50 development centers across North America. Its major facilities are in Chicago; Detroit; and Salem, New Hampshire.

The company operates side-by-side with other businesses, including Performance Assembly, a full-service assembly supplier of niche and specialty powertrain modules for the transportation industry; Roush CleanTech, which converts light- and medium-duty commercial fleet Ford vehicles to run on liquid propane injection fuel technology; Roush Fenway Racing, which offers a diverse NASCAR motorsports marketing platform; Roush Life Sciences, which develops and makes precision plastic products for medical applications; and, Roush Performance, which makes specialty vehicles.

In 2012 Roush Enterprises acquired a 60,000 square-foot building adjacent to its existing Livonia, Michigan, headquarters campus to accommodate the growth of its Roush CleanTech business.

The company was founded in 1976 as Jack Roush Performance Engineering.
