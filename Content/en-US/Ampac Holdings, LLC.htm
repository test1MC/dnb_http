Ampac is amped up about its packaging. It designs, manufactures, and prints packaging products for a slew of uses, including stand-up pouches and rollstock for food and beverages, high-end performance flexible packaging for pharmaceutical, health, and beauty products, retail bags of all styles for garments and more, and security products (cash transfer and deposit bags). More than a dozen Ampac manufacturing facilities dot North America, Europe, and Asia, producing 200-plus custom films. Customers include major retailers Gap and Target , restaurants, groceries, convenience stores, and casinos.

Ampac has manufacturing plants in Cambodia, China, Germany, Switzerland, the UK, the US (14 locations), and Vietnam.

The company's market segments include Beverages, Cash-In-Transit, Casinos, Forensics, Fresh Fruit and Produce, Frozen Foods, Processed Foods, Home & Garden, Industrial/Chemical, Restaurants, and Retail.

The company looks for strategic growth through acquisitions, organic growth initiatives, investment in new production capabilities, and in creating packaging design and innovation. In 2014 Ampac introduced the Design and Sample Lab (DASL), from which it can quickly produce custom sample concepts and technologies.

Ampac has added new machine capabilities like the Windmoeller & Hoelscher (W&H) rotogravure printing press (in 2013), and the Mamata VegaPlus 1000 bag/pouch machine and a Nordmeccanica Duplex Combi Horizontal laminator (in 2012). The addition of the machines helps the company reinforce its commitment to the growth of the flexible pouch and better serve existing and new customers' needs for value-added laminations, bags, and pouches.

In 2012, Ampac added Pour & Lok, a foldable zipper applied to film in the side gusset area of a package, and Inno-Lok, a patented method of applying a reclosable zipper transversely to packaging films.

In 2014 Ampac acquired Business Deposits Plus (BDP) to strengthen its secure packaging business. BDP provides bank deposit supplies to the cash management/loss prevention industry. Products include deposit tickets, bank deposit bags, and endorsement stamps for the retail, restaurant, and service company markets.

The company was created in 2000 from the merger of Amko Plastics and Interstate Packaging Corporation.
