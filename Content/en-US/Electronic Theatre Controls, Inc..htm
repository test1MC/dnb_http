If all the world's a stage, Electronic Theatre Controls (ETC) will help you play your part. The company makes lighting control consoles and other equipment for entertainment and architectural lighting. Its products include architectural dimming and controls, automated fixtures, conventional fixtures, entertainment dimmers, and lighting design software. The company's offerings are used in theatres, churches, opera houses, schools, theme parks, and TV studios and include the Eos, Congo, Unison, and Smart Solutions brands. At least 90% of the products the company sells worldwide are made at its headquarters in Middleton, Wisconsin. Electronic Theatre Controls was founded in 1975 by Fred Foster.

Based in Middleton, Wisconsin, ETC has sales and service offices in New York City; Hollywood, California; Orlando; London; Rome; Holzkirchen, Germany; Ede, The Netherlands; and Hong Kong. It also has over 200 authorized service centers around the world.

The company's illumination lineup has contributed to high profile projects like the renovation of Lincoln Center's Alice Tully Hall in Manhattan. Other locations range from Monona Terrace and the Overture Center in Madison, Wisconsin, to the Royal Festival Hall in London, Cirque de Soleil shows in Las Vegas, and the Grand Mosque in Abu Dhabi.

ETC sells its products and services across the world through dealers and distributors as well as through its online stores. It primarily serves the entertainment lighting, specified architectural lighting, and industrial weighing and measuring markets. It offers its products to themed parks, opera houses, TV studios, performing arts centers, casinos, malls, museums, restaurants, houses of worship, and schools in North America, Latin America, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, Europe, the Russian Federation, Asia, and Australia.

In 2013 ETC announced it is expanding its manufacturing capacity through the purchase of a 350,000 sq. ft. building in Mazomanie, Wisconsin. ETC is moving portions of its manufacturing and warehousing to the Mazomanie facility, while maintaining its headquarters in Middleton, Wisconsin. This expansion will allow the company to combine its three local manufacturing sites into two and gain sizable space for future growth.
