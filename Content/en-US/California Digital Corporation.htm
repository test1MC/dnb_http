California Digital Corp. (CDC) develops massively parallel supercomputers. The company's Linux-based and OS X clusters are used by companies involved in biotechnology, digital content creation, financial services, manufacturing, and oil and gas exploration. The "Thunder" supercomputer that CDC installed at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory is one of the fastest and most powerful computers in the world. CDC acquired the systems division of VA Linux Systems (later VA Software and now SourceForge ) in 2001. The company was established in 1992 by chairman and CEO B. J. Arun, who owns California Digital. CDC has offices in India and the US.
