Cutanea Life Sciences knows skin conditions aren't exactly cute. That's why the pharmaceutical company in-licenses and develops therapies for the treatment of dermatological disorders. Lead candidate omiganan, a chemical entity with anti-microbial and anti-inflammatory properties, is being clinically studied to treat rosacea, which causes persistent redness and sometimes lesions on the face. Other studies are testing omiganan's effectiveness against atopic dermatitis, a type of eczema. Additional candidates in its portfolio include a dermal filler to treat fine lines and wrinkles and a topical product for non-genital warts caused by human papillomavirus (HPV).
