NYX is a proponent for creating components. The company is a supplier of automotive molded interior assemblies and under-hood components to OEMs. NYX's interior sub-systems line features latches and handles for doors and glove box, overhead and center consoles, door panels, and seating components. Its under-hood products include air and vacuum management systems, wiper/washer tubes and sub-assemblies, and sunroof drain tubes. In addition to its plastics processing, steel stamping, and assembly expertise, NYX boasts a design center in India, offering cost-competitive engineering, design and development services.

NYX is headquartered in Livonia, Michigan, and has production facilities residing in Indiana, Michigan, and Tennessee. It has a design center in India.

Clients have included such notable names as Ernst & Young , Honda , Lear Corporation , Textron Automotive Company, Visteon , Ford , and General Motors .

NYX has been selling off units and offices in order to focus solely on its molding operations. In mid-2013 it sold off a facility in Fort Wayne, Indiana, which specialized in vacuum boost technologies and structural parts for the auto and heavy truck industries. Also during that time, NYX sold its Tennessee-based Bates LLC subsidiary to Park-Ohio Holdings in order to focus on new product releases and launch operations in Mexico.
