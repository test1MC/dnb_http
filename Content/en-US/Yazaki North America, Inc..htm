Yazaki North America (YNA) is a leading supplier of wiring harnesses and other vehicle power and data components for the automotive industry. A subsidiary of diversified Japanese manufacturer Yazaki Corporation , Yazaki North America also offers passive and active sensors; controls for power distribution, terminals, and connections; vehicle instrumentation; and products for hybrid and electric vehicles. YNA has about two dozen facilities throughout North America.

The company operates throughout the US, Canada, and Mexico.

YNA's customers include leading auto manufacturers and parts suppliers such as Subaru , Daimler , Honda , and Toyota .

Some 29% of Yazaki Corporation 's revenue is generated in the Americas.

In the fast-paced world of automotive technologies, YNA continues to stay competitive through the development of new and innovative products. One of the company's more recent products is its trademarked IntelliTow, a small device that monitors the electrical system of trailers and other towable vehicles. It can diagnose problems with fuses, wiring, and lights, and relay the information back to the driver in real-time. The company has also had some success as a result of the increase in hybrid and electric vehicles.

In 2013, subsidiary Circuit Controls Corporation (a maker of automotive electrical terminals) announced plans to expand its manufacturing plant in Emmet County, Michigan, in order to support new business.

YNA began operations in 1966.
