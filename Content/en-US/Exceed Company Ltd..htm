Being the Nike of China would certainly exceed this company's expectations. Footwear and apparel maker Exceed sells athletic wear to mostly 15-to-35-year-old customers in smaller urban markets. Its Xidelong branded merchandise, including basketball, skateboarding, and tennis shoes; sports tops, pants, jackets, and track suits; and accessories like bags, socks, hats, and caps, is sold at about 3,700 stores of the same name via distributors. Running shoes are Exceed's biggest seller as more Chinese take an interest in physical fitness. It makes some shoes in-house and outsources the rest of its production. The company began as Xdlong Fujian in 2001 and took its present form through a reverse merger in 2009.
