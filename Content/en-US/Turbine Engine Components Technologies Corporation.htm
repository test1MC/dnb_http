You can't make aircraft engines without parts from TECT. Turbine Engine Components Technologies (TECT) manufactures and distributes semi-finished and finished engine components through its manufacturing facilities located across the US. Its products include airfoils, forgings, turbine wheels and rings, and shafts. TECT also provides engineering, forging, inspection, metallurgy, tool and die, and welding and brazing services. Its customers include Airbus , Bell Helicopter , Cessna , GE Aircraft Engines , Rolls Royce , and Siemens Westinghouse. The company, which was founded in 2001, is controlled by the Stony Point Group investment firm.

TECT has almost 15 facilities located in California, Kansas, Georgia, Kentucky, Ohio, New York, and Washington. It has 2.4 million sq. ft. of total manufacturing space.

The company's operations are divided into two units. TECT Aerospace makes structural and mechanical components such as cross beams, door assemblies, shafts, struts, and elevator assemblies for all kinds of aircraft, while TECT Power makes rotating parts like airfoil blades, rings, seals, and shafts used in turbine engines.

TECT states it generates more than $450 million in revenues each year.

TECT leverages the pockets of its parent company Stony Point Group to help it fund acquisitions to fuel its growth strategy. It also opens new offices in what he sees as key regions that can act as a springboard for growth. In 2012 it opened a new sales office in Munich, Germany. The company hopes to sell its products further into Europe and Asia as a result of the office opening.

Also in 2012, the company launched TECT Aerospace Components, located in Guanajuato, Mexico, which makes products under the purview of its TECT Aerospace division.
