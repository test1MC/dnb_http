PolyOne Corporation (a top North American plastics compounders and resins distributor) has one focus -- providing specialized polymer products and services through a handful of business segments. The company's Performance Products and Solutions unit produces custom-made compounded plastics and custom-formulated colorants for plastics manufacturers throughout North America and Europe. Other units include Color, Additives and Inks; Specialty Engineered Materials; PolyOne Distribution, which distributes more than 3,500 thermoplastic resins and compounds from 25 major material suppliers: and Designed Structures and Solutions (plastic sheet, color, and engineered materials).

PolyOne has some 75 manufacturing plants and 8 distribution facilities in North America, Europe, Asia, Africa and South America. The US, the largest market, accounted for 66% of the company's revenues in 2015, followed by Europe (13%), and Canada and Asia (each 7%).

The company has five reportable segments: PolyOne Distribution; Color, Additives, and Inks; Performance Products and Solutions; Specialty Engineered Materials; and Designed Structures and Solutions.

PolyOne Distribution (29% of 2015 sales) distributes more than 3,500 grades of engineering and commodity grade resins, which are sold to over 6,000 custom injection molders and extruders; Color, Additives and Inks (23%) provides specialized custom color and additive concentrates in solid and liquid form for thermoplastics, dispersions for thermosets, as well as specialty inks, plastisols, and vinyl slush molding solutions; Performance Products and Solutions (20%) consists of the Geon Performance Materials and Producer Services business units; Specialty Engineered Materials (15%) provides specialty polymer formulations, services and solutions for designers, assemblers and processors of thermoplastic materials; while Designed Structures and Solutions (13%) is made up of the former Spartech Custom Sheet and Rollstock and Packaging Technologies operations.

PolyOne offers more than 35,000 polymer products to more than 10,000 customers around the world. It sells through direct sales personnel, distributors, and commissioned sales agents.

The company serves a wide range of industries, including appliance, building and construction, consumer, electrical and electronics, healthcare, industrial, packaging, textiles, transportation, and wire and cable.

After steady growth between 2010 and 2014, the company's revenue dipped by 12% in 2015 due to decline in all its segments primarily as a result of as a result of lower volume associated with the ongoing integration of the legacy Spartech businesses and also due to unfavorable foreign exchange, lower selling prices in particularly for the Performance Products and Solutions and PolyOne Distribution segments due to lower hydro-carbon based raw material costs.

In 2015 net income increased by 83% due to s decrease in selling and administrative expenses as a result of lower restructuring charges, compensation costs, foreign currency (primarily driven by the weaker Euro), and a reduction in its pension mark-to-market adjustment.

Operating cash flow increased by 9% due to higher net income and cash generated from accounts receivable.

PolyOne pursues a strategy of tailoring its offerings of polymers, formulations, and polymer processing to meet customer needs, while expanding its operations to serve the increase in demand from high-growth markets around the world.

In 2016, the company launched OnFlex LO thermoplastic elastomers (TPEs), featuring reduced levels of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) for vehicle interiors, and the availability of its IQ Design Labs service in Europe.

In 2015 the company introduced a new over molding technology based on its Versafle VDT thermoplastic elastomer, developed to reduce noise by minimizing vibrations transmitted from motors and pumps. Applications range from sports and leisure equipment, lawn and garden tools, and consumer electronics cases to kitchen appliances and power tools. It also launched ColorMatrix Fiber Colorant Solutions, ColorMatrix Select, a cloud-based liquid color creation and supply service; and InVisiOSM Color Inspiration 2016, a collection of emerging colors used when inspiring and collaborating with our customers during their product design process.

In 2014 PolyOne signed an expanded supply agreement with Dow Corning , enabling PolyOne Distribution to sell Dow Corning brand silicones to healthcare customers in China.

As part of its Platinum Vision for 2020, the company has established margin improvement targets for all businesses. In the short term, the company will maintain its focus on sales growth with expanding margins, with a goal of offsetting weaker foreign currencies and raw material volatility.

In 2016 the company acquired Kraton Performance Polymers ' compounding business for $72 million.to expand its global footprint and expertise in thermoplastic elastomer design.

In 2015 it acquired Magenta Master Fibers, a developer of specialty color concentrates for the global fiber industry, for $18.3 million from BASF , which expanded PolyOne's specialty colorant business for fiber applications, as well as grew our resources and presence in Europe and Asia.

Growing its Global Color, Additives and Inks portfolio, in 2014 the company acquired specialty assets from Accella Performance Materials, a leading North American manufacturer of liquid polymer formulations, for $47.2 million. Accella retained its polyurethanes and rubber products businesses.

In 2013 PolyOne acquired Spartech, a leading producer of plastic sheet, compounds, and packaging products, for $393 million. The Spartech acquisition expanded PolyOne's specialty portfolio with adjacent technologies in attractive end markets, allowing it to form the Designed Structures and Solutions segment.

In 2013 PolyOne launched Percept Authentication Technologies. This new suite of technologies draws from covert, overt and forensic techniques, enabling manufacturers to efficiently protect their products from counterfeiting or unauthorized distribution.

Expanding geographically, in 2013 the company opened a new facility in Pune, India to manufacture specialty solid masterbatch and liquid color and additive formulations. The facility also includes development labs and the sales and customer service center for the region. Products produced at the facility serve multiple end markets, including transportation, healthcare, wire and cable, and packaging.

To raise cash, that year the company sold its vinyl dispersion, blending, and suspension resin assets to Mexichem, S.A.B. de C.V. for $250 million.

Moving into the advanced composite technology market, in 2012 the company acquired Glasforms Inc., a top maker of glass and carbon fiber reinforced polymers and advanced composite products, with annual sales of $51.1 million.

In 2012 the company opened an Innovation Center in Korea and announced plans to open a new facility in Africa through its subsidiary ColorMatrix (which it acquired in 2011 for $486.1 million) to deepen support for PET container manufacturers for the beverage and personal care markets in the region.

PolyOne sold its 50% stake in the SunBelt Chlor Alkali plant joint venture in 2011 to its JV partner Olin Corp. for $175 million in cash and debt. The plant produces chlorine and caustic soda. PolyOne used the cash from the deal to accelerate growth in its other business lines.

In 2011 PolyOne bought ColorMatrix (a specialized developer and maker of performance enhancing additives, liquid colorants, and fluoropolymer and silicone colorants) for $486.1 million.

To expand its Global Color, Additives and Inks business into the Middle East, in 2011 PolyOne formed a Jeddah, Saudi Arabia-based joint venture with E.A. Juffali & Brothers. That year PolyOne acquired Uniplen Industria de Polimeros Ltda. (Uniplen), a Brazilian producer of specialty engineered materials and distributor of thermoplastics.

PolyOne was formed by the 2000 merger of plastics companies Geon and M.A. Hanna .
