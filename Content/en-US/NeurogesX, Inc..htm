NeurogesX is paving the way for treating neuropathic pain, or chronic pain caused by damaged nerves. The company's lead candidate is Qutenza (formerly known as NGX-4010), a skin patch that uses a synthetic version of capsaicin (an ingredient in hot peppers) to lessen nerve pain. NeruogesX is approved in the US as a treatment for pain related to postherpetic neuralgia (caused by shingles) and in Europe for peripheral neuropathic pain. It is also under development for other pain indications. However, in early 2012 the FDA rejected Qutenza for use on pain from HIV-associated peripheral neuropathy in the US market. As a result, NurogesX cut its workforce and focused its operations on other development programs.
