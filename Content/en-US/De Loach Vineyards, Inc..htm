De Loach Vineyards hopes to set its wines apart by setting its vineyard apart. The company uses biodynamic, organic and sustainable methods to cultivate its vineyards. Biodynamic farming treats the land as a self-sustaining entity that avoids input from outside resources, mainly fertilizers and pesticides. Unlike organic farming, biodynamics also relies on lunar phases to help decide when harvesting of the fruit should take place. The De Loach winery produces pinot noir, chardonnay, sauvignon blanc, merlot, syrah, and zinfandel, among others. The French firm, Boisset, owns De Loach Vineyards.
