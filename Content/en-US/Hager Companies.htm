C. Hager & Sons Hinge Manufacturing Company (doing business as Hager Companies) makes commercial door hardware, specializing in architectural and residential hinges, roton continuous geared hinges, trim and auxiliary, thresholds and weather-stripping, locks, and door closers. Custom wares are made, too. Its diverse 6,000-plus products are distributed worldwide, many with an industry lifetime warranty. Reflecting a shift in the door hardware market, Hager's lineup includes consulting services to architectural firms for door hardware specification writing, code compliance, design development, and budget preparation. The company is owned and managed by the founding Hager family.

Headquartered in St. Louis, Hager has a manufacturing plant in Montgomery, Alabama where it makes the bulk of its products. It has distribution centers in Phoenix and Tolleson, Arizona.

Hager offers datasheets for specifiers, architects, project managers, engineers, contractors, consultants, and owners in the commercial construction industry. It offers its products for customers in the Middle East, Asia, South America, Central America, and North America. Clients have included Calvert Independent Hardware Specifications and John Oatley Builders Hardware.

The company sells its products through distributors in Canada and the US. Some of these have included D. H. Pace Company, Door Service, H&G Sales, Newger Materials, and American Building Supply.

The manufacturer traces its roots to a St. Louis blacksmith shop that hired German immigrant Charles Hager as an apprentice locksmith in 1849. The original owner was lured away by the California Gold Rush, and the company fell into the hands of Hager, who ran the business making metal wheel rims and hinges for the Conestoga wagons heading west. Charles Hager eventually revolutionized the hinge industry by developing compact butt type hinges in the early 1900s. This strong hinge formed the basis for Hager Companies' diverse stock.
