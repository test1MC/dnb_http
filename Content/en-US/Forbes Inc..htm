Repeat after Forbes: Capitalism is good! The company publishes the biweekly business magazine Forbes , long promoted as the "Capitalist Tool." It also publishes a handful of other magazines, including ForbesLife , a lifestyle magazine for business leaders. Forbes Web properties include sites such as Forbes.com, Investopedia.com, and RealClearPolitics.com. In addition, the company produces business-related conferences and publishes newsletters and custom magazines. Scottish immigrant and journalist B.C. Forbes launched Forbes in 1917. Chairman and former CEO Malcolm "Steve" Forbes is B.C.'s grandson. Forbes is majority-owned by private equity firm Elevation Partners.

In addition to its flagship magazine, Forbes has international editions such as Forbes Asia , and about ten other local-language editions. While Forbes has a North American circulation of of more than 900,000, its global print editions together reach a worldwide audience of more than 6 million readers. The company also produces TV programming (such as Forbes on FOX through a partnership with FOX News ). Its Forbes Newsletter Group publishes and edits newsletters such as Forbes Growth Investor , Forbes/Lehmann Income Securities Investor , and Forbes/Wolfe Emerging Tech Report .

In an industry plagued by a decrease in advertising revenues, Forbes has taken cost-cutting measures in recent years, including layoffs and combining its print and Web staffs (both sales and editorial). In 2011 the company launched a redesign of Forbes.com with re-architectured channel pages such as business, investing, and technology.

Scottish immigrant B.C. Forbes launched Forbes ("devoted to doers and doings") in 1917. Son Malcolm began to focus on the family business after being badly wounded in WWII. Malcolm convinced B.C. to have Forbes introduce an annual feature highlighting top corporations in 1949, a feature that would evolve into the in-depth Forbes 500 issue. Malcolm's brother Bruce ran the business following the death of B.C. in 1954 and was succeeded by Malcolm in 1964.

Over the next 25 years the bon vivant Malcolm built the magazine into a leading business publication. In 1986 his company acquired the struggling history magazine American Heritage and turned it into a fast-growing publication. Malcolm persuaded Caspar Weinberger, the former defense secretary, to become publisher of Forbes in 1989. Malcolm died in 1990, and his eldest son Steve, deputy editor-in-chief of Forbes since 1982, took over the business.

Forbes continued to prosper under Steve's leadership, with its advertising revenue far outpacing that of archrival FORTUNE . In 1995 Steve Forbes threw his hat into the ring as a presidential candidate, a boon for Forbes' visibility. In 1997 Forbes sold its Forbes Newspapers to MediaNews Group's North Jersey Newspapers and bought the New York Times Company's custom publishing unit. The next year Forbes named West Coast high-tech evangelist Rich Karlgaard Forbes publisher (Weinberger is now chairman).

In 1999 Forbes named James Berrien (former head of American Express Travelers Cheque Group) as president of Forbes magazine. In 2000 Forbes sold its academic custom publishing division to Thomson Learning. The company teamed with the WPP Group in 2001 to create a custom publishing venture called the Custom Media Group. Forbes ASAP , the company's magazine focused on the digital economy, was shut down in 2002.

The company established a Forbes Russia edition in 2004, and shortly thereafter tragedy struck when the magazine's editor, Paul Klebnikov, was shot and killed while leaving the Moscow office

Caspar Weinberger died in 2006. Weinberger was chairman and a regular Forbes columnist until is death at age 88. Also that year Elevation Partners invested about $300 million in the publisher.

In 2007 Forbes put its more than 50-year-old American Heritage title up for sale. In the absence of a buyer the company decided to cease publication of the title. In 2008 the company merged its print and Web staffs in order to cut costs.

The company hired Mike Perlis as its new CEO in 2010. Steve Forbes continue to serve as chairman and editor in chief of Forbes magazine.
