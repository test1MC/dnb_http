Dig this: J C Bamford Excavators, commonly known as just JCB, is such a popular brand in the UK that its residents often substitute "JCB" for the word digger, the same way Americans use " Kleenex " for tissue. Best known for making backhoes, JCB produces a variety of heavy equipment used in construction and farming, including crawler excavators, backhoe loaders, front-end loaders, material handlers, and the speed tractor (the Fastrac, which moves at about 45 mph). JCB is Europe's largest maker of construction equipment. Subsidiaries JCB Finance and JCB Insurance Services, respectively, provide equipment financing and insurance for equipment and workers.

JCB has a presence in six continents and sells its products in 150 countries through 2,000 dealer depot locations. The company has its manufacturing facilities at 22 locations in the UK, Brazil, Germany, China, North America, and India.

The company has 750 dealers across the world. It serves various industries such as the construction, agriculture, government and defence, waste and recycling, and power generation sectors.

JBC's financial foundation is also supported by a vertically integrated cost-saving business model -- more so than most construction equipment makers. One plant, for example, makes transmissions for JCB machinery as well as for other OEMs, enabling competitive volume part pricing. JCB also supplies such related machinery parts as lubricants, tires, and batteries.

On the day his son, Anthony, was born (October 23), Joseph Cyril Bamford founded his namesake company in 1945 from a rented garage in Uttoxeter, England.
