Ordnance Survey is Great Britain's national mapping agency. The agency provides current and historical maps and digital geographical information used by government entities, utilities, insurers, emergency services, property developers, and others. Its maps are also used for leisure and educational purposes. Ordnance Survey is part of the UK government; however, it covers its own operating costs by selling its products and services or licensing its copyrighted material to others for use. The agency has been collecting data for more than 200 years; it was established in 1791.
