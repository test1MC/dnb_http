QSC Audio Products wants its customers to feel and hear the Power. The company is a leader in audio amplifying loudspeaker, line array systems, digital signal processing (DSP) and digital mixer engineering resources, and accessories, such as adapters, cables, rack ears and handles, input and output transformers, and replacement parts. It makes about a dozen amplifier models, using its PowerWave and PowerLight technology. QSC also manufactures digital signal processors, network audio and loudspeaker systems, software, and accessories. Its customers include sound-applications designers and contractors for symphony houses, movie theaters, stadiums, and NASCAR racing facilities, as well as touring professionals.

The company manufactures its products in its 81,000 sq. ft. facility located in Costa Mesa, California. QSC has additional offices in Boulder, Colorado, and Hong Kong, with sales and support staff located throughout North and South America, Europe, Asia and the Middle East. Other facilities reside in Tennessee, Belgium, and China.

QSC's products are sold in music stores (including Guitar Center and Sam Ash Music ) across the US, and to wholesale distributors.

Innovation and new product launches are key to the company's growth.

In 2014 QSC added three new loudspeaker models designed for fixed installation applications. The new AD-S6T and AD-S112sw surface-mount loudspeakers are additions to the popular AcousticDesign Series, while the new AC-C4T (part of the upcoming AcousticCoverage loudspeaker line) offers integrators a cost-effective, in-ceiling loudspeaker solution that is ideal for voice paging and light background music applications.

The company also launched a new full-line Pro and Systems Product Catalog Application, designed for use on tablet computer platforms. A companion to the product pocket guides introduced earlier in 2014, this new interactive app allows users the ability to browse the entire QSC product portfolio and get detailed product information including descriptions, key features, specifications and images.

In 2014 AV Express, an audio-visual equipment hire company based in Chennai, India, added QSC WideLine-10 system to their sound reinforcement inventory comprised of 16 three-way WL2102-w modules with six GP218-sw subwoofers.

Barry Andrews and Patrick H. Quilter founded QSC Audio in 1968.
