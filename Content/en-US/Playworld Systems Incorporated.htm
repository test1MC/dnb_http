No longer called monkey bars or jungle gyms -- they're "playscapes" and Playworld Systems makes them, along with a lot of other things that would probably make you wish you were a kid again. The company manufactures a variety of structures for climbing, sliding, skating, and exercising under the brand names PlayDesigns, Playworld, NEOS (electronic playground systems), and LifeTrail (outdoor exercise stations). Playworld customers include schools, parks and recreation departments, retirement communities, churches, and residential housing developers. In addition to its various products, Playworld offers playground owners advice about budgeting, needs, and risk management. In 2016 playground manufacturer PlayPower Inc. acquired the company for an undisclosed amount.

Headquartered in Lewisburg, Pennsylvania, the company has more than 250 employees and an office in London as well as a network of representatives throughout the world.

In mid-2015 Playworld opened its new office in London. The office will help to better service customers throughout the UK, meeting the needs of towns, councils, and schools throughout the region.

Playworld was founded in 1952 as QE Manufacturing, a machine parts maker, and began supplying playground products in 1959. The family-owned business, now one of the largest in the industry, launched its own line of play structures in 1971.
