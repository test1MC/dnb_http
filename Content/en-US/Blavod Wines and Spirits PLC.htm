Consumers of alcohol who have an extreme devotion to the color black, can look to Distil (formerly Blavod Wine and Spirits) which produces and sells Blavod Black Vodka, a midnight-hued vodka. Its other mainstays include Blackwood's gins and vodkas, as well as Jago's Vanilla Vodka Cream liqueur. Distil also distributes other companies' spirits and liqueurs, including Mickey Finn Schnapps, Cariel Vanilla Vodka, Bruichladdich Islay Malt Whisky, and Arette Tequila, as well as the wines of Château Lafite Rothschild and Royal Tokaji. The UK is the company's major market.

The company markets and sells its brands in a number of markets including the UK, the US, Germany, Spain, Australia, and Russia. The UK market accounted for 62% of its total net sales in fiscal 2016 (March year end).

A purveyor of premium spirits, Distil's branded products include RedLeg Spiced Rum, Blackwood's Vintage Gin, Blackwood's Limited Edition Vintage Gin, Blackwood's Vodka, Blavod Original Black Vodka, Diva Vodka, and Jago's Vanilla Cream Liqueur.

Distil's advertising and promotional costs grew in fiscal 2016 from £148,000 to £280,000.

Some 66% of the company's revenues came from one wholesale distributor compared to 54% in the fiscal 2015.

After seeing a downward trend between fiscal 212 and 2015, Distil's net sales increased in fiscal 2016.

In fiscal 2016, net sales increased by 68% due to increased sales in the UK following new listings with major customers and continued growth of its brands through existing stockists. RedLeg Spiced Rum increase in volumes as its brand reached new consumers.

The company incurred a net loss of £0.1 million in fiscal 2016 (compared to a net loss of £0.29 million in 2015) due to increased sales.

In fiscal 2016, net cash used for the operating activities decreased by £0.2 million.

Distil Plc acts as a holding company for the entities in the Distil Plc group. The principal activity of the group is the marketing and selling of premium spirits, both in the UK and internationally.

The company is focused on the development of its brands in key markets.

Blavod was formed in 2003 as a result of the merger of Blavod Black Vodka and Extreme Beverage. It changed its name to Distil in 2014.
