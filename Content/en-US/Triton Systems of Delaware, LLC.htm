Triton Systems of Delaware is actually located in Mississippi. The company, which does business as Triton, manufactures automated teller machines (ATMs) and related products. The company's hardware products include through-the-wall and freestanding ATMs, as well as cash dispensing mechanisms sold under the brands Triton, ARGO, Traverse, and FT5000. It also makes safes sold under the VersaSafe and VersaLink brands. Triton has an installed base of 200,000 machines in airports, casinos, convenience stores, financial institutions, hotels, restaurants, and shopping centers in some two dozen countries.

Triton also offers a ATM maintenance and repair division, known as ATMGurus.

The company's ATMs are made in the USA. Its manufacturing plant and the ATMGurus training facility are located in Long Beach, Mississippi.

In 2012 the company sold its UK-based ATM subsidiary InfoCash Holdings for about $19 million. InfoCash and its 4,700 ATMs were bought by Canada-based DirectCash Payments in order to boost DirectCash's presence in Europe.

In North America, where ATMs are ubiquitous, Triton's efforts are focused on encouraging existing and potential customers to upgrade their aging ATM's with new equipment.

Founded in 1979, Triton was acquired by publicly-traded electronics and industrial conglomerate Dover in 2000. A group of investors bought the company from Dover in 2010, taking it private.
