Italian milk drinkers know lots about Parmalat , but what about Granarolo? The company is one of the largest dairy companies in Italy. The Granarolo group is composed of Granarolo, which is responsible for producing and selling the group's consumer products, and Granlatte, a milk-producing agricultural cooperative comprised of 1,300 dairy farmers who own Granarolo. The company's most important products include shelf-stable milk, cheese, frozen desserts, yogurt, and cream, and they are sold throughout the world but primarily in Italy. Founded in 1959, the company operates five production facilities in Italy. To up its position in the dairy market, Granarolo acquired cheese maker LatBri for €100 million in 2011.
