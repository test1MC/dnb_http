Through four operating divisions, Illinois Auto Electric (IAE) sells, services, and distributes industrial, railroad, trucking, and landscaping/outdoor equipment. The company's products -- locomotive controls, engine bearings and parts, AC systems, windshield wiper systems, air compressors, and pressure washers, among them -- are from a variety of manufacturers, including Thermo King , Oregon Cutting Systems, and Briggs & Stratton . It also operates a portal site to connect motorcycle enthusiasts with manufacturers. IAE, which was founded by Joseph Sirotek in 1915, is owned by the Sirotek family.

The company serves customers nationwide, but its primary market is the Midwest (Illinois, Kansas, Nebraska, Indiana). It also has an office in Utah.

The company's IAE Power Products unit supplies industrial and railroad power equipment from a variety of manufacturers; it also remanufactures and sells locomotive components. Illinois Auto Central/Great Lakes Thermo King sells and services Thermo King refrigeration equipment, which is used primarily on truck trailers, and distributes other equipment for heavy-duty trucks.

IAE's MWE division distributes engines and accessories and a wide range of outdoor equipment from manufacturers such as Briggs & Stratton, Oregon, Dolmar, WORX, and Walbro. Lastly, the company operates a transportation brokerage firm THAHT Company, which contracts with other companies to provide a full range of distribution, logistics, and supply chain management services.

IAE targets service professionals, contractors, landscapers, municipalities, arborists, cemeteries, golf courses, do-it-yourselfers, catalog houses, manufacturers, and mass retailers.
