Focusing on the water closet keeps Bemis Manufacturing flushed with cash. One of the world's largest manufacturers of toilet seats, the company produces a lineup in dozens of styles and colors. The family company also makes molded and extruded disposable plastic products for the healthcare industry, as well as industrial caps, gauges, and reservoirs via a plastic fluid management unit, Kelch Manufacturing. Bemis also offers contract manufacturing services for auto, large appliance office furniture, and other OEMs. The company's brands include Bemis, Carrara & Matta, Church, Mayfair, Olsonite, and Wesport.

Bemis sells its products in Asia, Australasia, Europe, the Middle East and Africa, and North and South America, and operates manufacturing plants in Chile, Mexico, the UK, and the US.

The company market products under its own brands (Bemis, Carrara & Matta, Church, Mayfair, and Olsonite) and provides expertise to others, producing private-label products and component parts for a wide variety of industries.

Bemis serves consumer, commercial, medical and industrial markets worldwide. The company's medical products are sold through distributors including Owens & Minor , McKesson , Medline , Cardinal , and PSS World Medical . It also operates a sales office in Hong Kong, which serves customers in the region from China to Australia.

Its growth has been fueled by efforts to sell the lineup through retailers (such as Home Depot and Lowes ) and wholesalers worldwide, as well as directly distributing them through national and regional dealer networks. In addition, management invests in new technologies and designs. R&D helps to differentiate Bemis products, and their manufacture and installation, from those of other US and international rivals.

Product introductions have included seats with improved hinge and fastening systems, which deliver less cleaning maintenance and better hygiene. A personal cleansing spa is another idea brought to market. Bemis has even developed a self-closing toilet seat.

In 2013, the company consolidated all of European manufacturing capabilities into its Burnley facility in the UK.

That year it also opened a market and business development office in Amsterdam. The office sells Bemis' products across Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.

Bemis was founded in 1901 by Albert Bemis. The firm initially made its products (toilet seats and bowling balls) out of wood. In the 1950s the company began experimenting with a mixture of wood flour and resins. It switched in the 1960s to making all-plastic products based on injection molded techniques. In the 1990s Bemis pioneered the use of articulating-arm robots, installing six-axis robots on every one of the 500-plus presses in one building.

The privately owned and operated company is led by descendants of its founder.
