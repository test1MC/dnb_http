Looking for real power? Let's talk circular saws, model 100 routers, and orbit finishing sanders. Thanks in part to the home improvement movement, Porter-Cable tools are picked up by handymen and women, professional woodworkers and construction workers, as well as electricians and plumbers. The company makes electric tools (tabletop saws, bench top planers, and radial drill presses), cordless tools (drills and drivers), air tools (hammers, nailers, and spray guns), and related accessories. Its lines are sold primarily through retailers, such as home centers, mass merchants, hardware stores, and lumber yards, including Home Depot and Lowe's , and online dealers. Porter-Cable is owned by Stanley Black & Decker .

The company sells its products through online dealers and as well as retailers. It serves professional woodworking, commercial/residential construction, plumbing, and electrical markets. Home centers within the US and mass merchants accounted for 18% of its parent's total sales in 2013.

Within Stanley Black & Decker, Porter-Cable is counted as one of about a dozen material trademarks that comprise its parent's Construction & Do-It-Yourself (CDIY) business segment. The CDIY segment accounts for 50% of Stanley Black & Decker's total revenue.

CDIY's net sales increased 6% in 2013 compared with 2012 on new product launches. These new products included its 12-volt lithium ion power tool products introduced under the Porter-Cable and sister brands DEWalt and Black & Decker Gyro.

Porter-Cable has its strategy fixed on organic growth through the launching of new products.

Porter-Cable's brand reputation extends back to 1906. Its business was founded by R. E. Porter, G. G. Porter, and F. E. Cable.
