Bonney Forge makes high quality forged and cast steel valves, fittings, and branch connections. In the US the company provides ferrous and non-ferrous branch connections, engineered specialty forged components, flanges, fittings, and piping systems and pressure vessels through its WFI International and RP&C subsidiaries. Bonney Forge also supplies forging and machining in Europe though its Italian subsidiary, Alco di Castronno. The company has expanded its reach through a joint venture based in Shanghai, China, to produce and sell cast steel valves in international markets.

The company is headquartered in Mount Union, Pennsylvania, and has other facilities in Houston.

The company serves multinational companies in the oil and gas, petrochemical, power generation, chemical manufacturing, and general industrial piping industries.

Bonney Forge was founded in 1908 as a manufacturer of hardware for horse-drawn wagons. It started making forged steel fittings and valves in the 1950s. John Leone purchased the company from Gulf & Western in 1984.
