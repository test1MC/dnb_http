Forget ringing cash registers -- the sound of sliding plastic is music to Ingenico's ears. The company makes electronic payment and credit authorization systems, including point-of-sale terminals, portable systems, and PC-based systems for offices. Ingenico also makes online payment systems and smartcard readers. Its products are used for banking, health care, hospitality, retail, and vending applications. The company has more than 32 million terminals in some 170 countries; Europe and the Americas account for the majority of sales. Ingenico has added a number of transaction services including management of terminals, connectivity, and transactions. Company director Jean-Jacques Poutrel formed Ingenico in 1980.

Ingenico is the ultimate middleman, transferring money from customer to merchant. The company's Smart Terminal product group offers a wide range of payment systems including NFC, QR code, and biometrics and security systems such as EMV, PCI, and DSS.

The company's Ingenico Payment Services unit provides merchants with a range of payment services: centralized transaction management, transaction processing, and loyalty services.

It's ePayments division offers data analytics, fraud management, and cross-border commerce services.

Ingenico operates a manufacturing plant for some of its products and outsources the rest of manufacturing.

The Europe and Africa market is Ingenico's largest segment, accounting for 35% of revenue in 2015, followed by the Asia/Pacific region at 20%, North America, 15%, and Latin America, 10%. When considered as single country markets, the US and China are Ingenico's biggest sources of revenue. The ePayments segment brought in 20% of revenue.

Ingenico posted robust revenue and profit growth in 2015.

Revenue leaped to €2.2 billion in 2015, a 37% increase from €1.6 billion in 2014. Ingenico benefited from positive foreign exchange rates, and higher sales from payment terminals business and payment services. Driving product growth were Ingenico Payment Services and the integration of GlobalCollectmwhich was acquired in 2014.

Net income increased 34% to €230 million from €171.65 million in 2014, riding the higher revenue as well as more finance income.

Cash flow from operations hit €337 million in 2015, compared to €294.7 million in 2014.

In 2016 Ingenico acquired Think&Go NFC, a start-up provider of connected screens. The companies have partnered on contactless payment technology to turn digital advertising displays into points-of-sale.

The acquisition of 70% of Lyudia in 2016 positions Ingenico to develops its market in Japan as the country moves to EMV payments in the next few years.
