Cloverdale wants to cover Western Canada and the Pacific Northwest of the US -- in paint. The paint and coatings manufacturer maintains some 60 retail stores and a network of more than 120 dealer outlets across a broad swath of North America. It not only wants to paint houses -- inside and out -- but bridges, buildings, factories, and boats. Cloverdale has expanded by acquiring US-based rival Rodda Paint and the Canadian Guertin Coatings; both companies continue to operate as independent subsidiaries. It also acquired Canada-based Serif Coatings and Chemicals. Cloverdale Paint, which was founded in 1933, has four manufacturing facilities.

Rodda Paint Co. operates its own brand and stores in the US. It is headquartered in Portland, Oregon, where it operates a manufacturing plant. Rodda has branches in Washington, Oregon, Northern Idaho and Alaska.
