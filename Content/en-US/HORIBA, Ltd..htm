HORIBA's work is purely instrumental. The company manufactures and sells an array of devices that measure, analyze, and diagnose. Among its products are instruments and systems that measure and analyze automotive emissions, air pollution, and water quality, as well as semiconductor contamination. It also makes scientific and medical diagnostic analyzers. This equipment is used in laboratories for R&D, production, and other applications. Having made strides to become a global company over the years, HORIBA does business in Asia/Pacific, Europe, and the Americas.

HORIBA has major offices, branches, and subsidiary operations in Asia/Pacific (China, Japan, Korea, India, Singapore, and Thailand); Europe (Austria, France, Germany, and the UK); the Americas (the US, Canada, and Brazil).

HORIBA's worldwide operations are divided into five business segments that focus on different markets: automotive, process and environmental, medical, scientific, and semiconductor. Semiconductor is its leading segment, generating about one-third of the group's annual revenues.

The company was started in 1945 when Masao Horiba set up the HORIBA Radio Laboratory in Karasuma-gojo in Kyoto to conduct nuclear physics research. In 1953 HORIBA, Ltd. was established and began manufacturing measurement instruments.
