SABIC Innovative Plastics US (SABIC IP) is among the world's largest producers of high-performance polymers used by manufacturers of electronics, office equipment, computers, and automotive products. The SABIC -owned company's units include Specialty Film & Sheet, which makes the polycarbonate sheet and film under the Lexan brand name; Polymershapes, a plastic film, sheet, and tube distributor; and Resins, a producer of all manner of plastic resins (the starting point in making most plastic materials). SABIC IP is a top producer of engineering thermoplastics.

SABIC Innovative Plastics, the immediate parent of SABIC IP, has operations in more than 35 countries.

SABIC Innovative Plastics' engineering thermoplastics portfolio includes thermoplastic resins, coatings, specialty compounds, sealants and adhesives, films, and sheets. The company's products are used for a range of applications in automotive, bath and spa, electronics, healthcare, building and construction, packaging, textiles, transportation, and outdoor vehicles industries.

SABIC Innovative Plastics also makes polypropylene compounds and Stamax long glass-fiber polypopylene composites, after its parent company acquired the petrochemicals business of Netherlands-based DSM . The Stamax products are used in the automotive industry.

The company also provides color and aesthetics services.

SABIC Innovative Plastics serves companies in a range of markets, including Healthcare, Transportation, Automotive, Electrical, Lighting, and Consumer Electronics.

Demand for SABIC Innovative Plastics' engineering thermoplastics (ETPs) is on the rise. Automotive demand for ETPs is strong, particularly in China and other emerging markets reflecting a strong growth in automobile sales. The company is also seeing demand for its polymers in Brazil, the Middle East, and Eastern Europe.

In 2014 SABIC Innovative Plastics' polycarbonate glazing products were highlighted during Abarth's official launch of its Abarth 695 Biposto. The vehicle's front fixed windows are made from SABIC's LEXAN resin and EXATEC coating technology.

In 2013 the company also introduced new grades of LNP THERMOCOMP compound (which enable mobile device OEMs to embed antennas onto the frame of portable devices).

To provide North American customers with improved access to a wide range of plastics and associated materials, that year SABIC Innovative Plastics and SABIC IP opened new branches of its Polymershapes plastics distribution business in Guadalajara, Mexico; Mexico City; and Austin, Texas. Globally, Polymershapes serves more than 35,000 customers in a variety of industries, including oil and gas exploration, power generation, semiconductor production, and aircraft manufacturing.

SABIC, Saudi Arabia's largest public company, owns SABIC IP through its SABIC Innovative Plastics Holding B.V. unit.

SABIC IP was formed in 1930 and became GE Plastics. It changed its name to SABIC Innovative Plastics US in 2007 when GE sold GE Plastics to SABIC for $11 billion.
