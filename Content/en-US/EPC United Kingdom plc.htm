EPC United Kingdom knows how to have a blast. Known as EPC-UK, it consists of five units: explosives (ammonium nitrate, detonators and blasting accessories, nitroglycerin, slurry explosives), additives (fuel additives), blasting services (environmental impact analysis, consulting, drilling), transport (logistics, vehicle fleets, hauling), and EDSL (facilities for ammunition, explosives, assembly). A top commercial blasting company in the UK, it is part of Paris-based EPC Groupe (Société Anonyme d'Explosifs et de Produits Chimique). Its explosives are used in quarry shotholes, civil engineering tunnels, and deep mines.

EPC-UK's parent and has 40 subsidiaries in 20 countries around the world, and is active in Europe, Africa, and the Middle East.

The parent company produces more than 145,000 tons of explosives a year. EPC-UK offer global logistics services to customers in the civil, governmental, and military sectors. Its products include the Euronel system of detonators used for surface blasting and long-delay blasting for tunnels and underground. EPC-UK also uses Daveydets, electric detonators manufactured by Davey Bickford in France, for instantaneous and short-delay detonation. Its electronic detonators include products like the Hotshot and Daveytronic.

The company serves a range of industries, including mining, cement, aggregates, civil engineering, and defense. EPC-UK's customers includes Aggregate Industries , Nammo, Hand on Heart, Hanson , QNJAC, Lafarge , The National Skills Academy, and The Institute of Quarrying.

The company's strategy is to become a full-service provider, and EPC-UK has expanded from its main three units (explosives, blasting services, and additives), to include another two units (transport and EDSL). Its additives division markets more the 10,000 tons of 77%-80% sulphuric acid per annum. In 2014 EPC-UK's the additives unit at Bramble Island commissioned a pilot facility to recover a waste 77% sulphuric acid stream.

Formerly named Exchem, EPC-UK was founded in 1905. EPC Groupe was established in France in 1893.
