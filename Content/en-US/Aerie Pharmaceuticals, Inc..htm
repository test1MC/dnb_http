Aerie Pharmaceuticals wants to help everyone get a bird's eye view. The clinical-stage company is developing new medications to treat glaucoma, a type of ocular disease that causes vision loss and sometimes blindness. Aerie Pharmaceuticals has two product candidates in development, both of which are once-daily eye drops. The drug candidate AR-13324 should be in Phase 3 registration trials in 2014, while the drug PG324 combines AR-13324 with latanoprost, the most commonly prescribed glaucoma drug. PG324 is expected to begin Phase 2 trials in 2014. Founded in 2005, Aerie Pharmaceuticals went public in 2013. It raised $67 million in its IPO and plans to use the proceeds to fund further clinical development.
