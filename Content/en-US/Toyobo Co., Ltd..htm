Toyobo is one of Japan's top makers of fibers and textiles, including synthetic fibers (polyester, nylon, acrylics) and natural fibers, such as cotton and wool. The company's textiles are designed for clothing, home furnishings, and for industrial uses. Its textiles include spandex yarn for apparel, polyurethane fiber for pantyhose, and yarns for airbags and tire cords. Toyobo also manufactures plastic films (industrial and packaging) and resins. Biochemical products (reagents and instruments), medical products (fiber membranes for artificial organs), and purification devices round out the company's primary manufacturing operations.

The company has operations in Spain, Germany, Saudi Arabia, and Brazil.

Toyobo operates in five segments:

Film and Functional Plastic (packaging films, industrial films, industrial adhesives, engineering plastic, and optical functional materials);

Industrial Material (textile materials for automobiles, super fibers, functional filters, and non-woven cloth);

Life Science (biotechnology products, aquatic films, pharmaceuticals, and medical films and devices);

Clothing Textiles (clothing, apparel products, clothing textiles and fibers); and

Real Estate (real estate selling and leasing).

The company serves a range of markets, including Life Science, Environmental, Electronic and Information display, Automotive, Lifestyle, and Safety.

The company's net sales increased in 2014 after experiencing a slight dip in 2013 following an upward trend since 2010.

In fiscal 2014 Toyobo's net sales increased by 4% due to higher sales from Films and Functional Polymers and Life Science, partially offset by decreased sales from Industrial Materials.

The net income of the company has seen an upward trend since 2010.

In fiscal 2014 net income increased by 7% due to higher sales, partially offset by increased selling, general, and administrative expenses. (including R&D expenditures, which increased along with the costs of the start-up of new film manufacturing equipment).

Toyobo is expanding beyond its traditional fibers and textiles base. It has moved into more lucrative industries, including contract pharmaceuticals manufacturing, blood glucose monitoring devices, environmental systems, and electronics materials, among other things. The company is also continuing to reduce its fiber production and narrow its focus to produce more functional textiles such as those used in sportswear, work uniforms, and bedding linens.

In 2015 Toyobo's European subsidiary, TOYOBO Europe GmbH, signed a sponsorship agreement with the Parma Football Club, which is one of Italy's Serie A teams. Toyobo presented the Parma club with training mats containing BREATHAIR, which is a three-dimensional spring structure. Going forward, Toyobo will leverage this new relationship to expand sales of BREATHAIR.

In 2014 Toyobo established a new sales and marketing office in Mexico (scheduled to become the Toyobo Group's Central American Office)

Streamlining its US operations, in 2013 the company merged New York-based subsidiaries Toyobo Specialties (USA) and Toyobo America, and renamed the expanded surviving company as Toyoba U.S.A.

In 2013 Toyobo acquired Spinreact, which has a distributing network in 90 countries, giving it access to Spinreact's product portfolio, including biochemical, immunology, and serology IVD reagents. Toyobo will distribute its lineup of biochemical IVD reagents as well as urine analysis and gene analysis systems through Spinreact's sales network in Africa, Central and South America, Europe, the rest of Asia, and elsewhere.

Toyobo was founded in 1882 as Osaka Boseki and took the Toyobo name in 1914.
