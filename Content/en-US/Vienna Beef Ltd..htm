Don't confuse the products of Vienna Beef with those soggy little wieners packed in jars. Its beef wieners are the hot dogs Chicago calls its own. The company first unveiled its frankfurters (made from a secret Viennese recipe) at the 1893 World's Fair in the Windy City. In addition to sausages and deli meats, Vienna Sausage also makes pickles (Chipico Pickles), soups (Bistro Soups), and halal foods (Minaret). Vienna Beef's products are available worldwide. The company sells to outside distributors, grocery stores, restaurants, sports and other entertainment venues, club stores, and hot-dog stands.

From dual corporate offices in Chicago and Los Angeles, Vienna Beef steers its other US operations, including its Chicago factory store and Chicago Pickle Company (Chipico) offices in Los Angeles and Palmetto, Florida. The company's products are available worldwide.

Formerly Vienna Sausage Manufacturing, the company's major vendor partners includes Mike Ditka Premium Meats, DeVanco Foods, Alpha Baking , and Turano Bakery .

Vienna Beef sells its products through supermarkets, quick-serve restaurants, hog dog stands, sports arenas, warehouse clubs, and all Chicagoland Supermarkets, as well as online.

The company has export deals with the UAE (Bahrain, Kuwait, and Qatar), Hong Kong, and Mexico. The company's developing markets include Lebanon and Oman.

Vienna Beef counts on baseball to hit its own home run. With an eye on selling an extra 1 million hot dogs -- the typical figure for each baseball season -- Vienna Beef beat out Sara Lee 's Ball Park franks in 2011 for top dog at Wrigley Field. The move marks Vienna Beef's return to Wrigley after nearly 30 years. Sara Lee had been the hot dog supplier to Wrigley Field since 2008. Thankful that the field's existing partner contracts were up for renewal, Vienna Beef is now banking on well-attended major league ballparks in Chicago.

In recent years, Vienna Beef has expanded its core product line. It has spiced up its dogs by rolling out a Jalapeno Cheddar Frank. Chasing after the hamburger slider craze, the company has also produced its own version of Pups, mini hot dogs served in multi-bite buns.

Unique to its business, Vienna Beef sells its products through hot dog carts and stands. To ensure that its training is consistent for prospective operators of company carts and stands, Vienna Beef enlists the help of Mark Reitman, Ph.D. (professor of hot dogs), as part of its Hot Dog University. Reitman heads up informational courses at the site of the Vienna Beef plant, where he discusses condiments, tongs, and the best way for a cart or stand operator to make money.

In 2013, Vienna Beef bought a 104,445-sq.-ft. hot-dog facility once owned by Sara Lee on the South side of Chicago. The purchase of the plant means that Vienna Beef could relocate from the North Side if the city goes ahead with a major two-year road reconfiguration project around its North side plant and office.

The wife of chairman emeritus Jim Eisenberg, Elin Eisenberg, died in 2010 at the age of 73. Elin's grandfather Sam Ladany, an immigrant of Austria and Hungary, founded Vienna Beef after introducing the Chicago-style hot dog in 1893 at the Columbian Exposition in Chicago.
