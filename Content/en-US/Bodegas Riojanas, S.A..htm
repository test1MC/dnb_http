Bodegas Riojanas brings full-bodied reds to dinner tables in Spain and abroad. From growing grapes to aging wine and bottling it, the company produces and distributes high-end wine from Spain's famed Rioja region. It owns more than 300 hectares (nearly 750 acres) of vineyards and two wineries (in Cenicero and San Vicente de la Sonsierra) that together house some 30,000 barrels and more than 4.5 million bottles. Bodegas Riojanas' top labels, which are sold throughout Europe and the Americas, include Cava, Rias Baixas, Rioja, and Toro. The founding Frías-Artecho and Artecho-Nieto families still help run the company, which was founded in 1890.

Bodegas Riojanas began its operations in Cenicero at the heart of Rioja Alta. Its newer winery is located nearby in the town of San Vicente de la Sonsierra, which is also located in Rioja Alta.

The company exports its products to Switzerland, Germany, Denmark, and Mexico, as well as other regions.

Bodegas Riojanas operates through subsidiaries Bodegas Torreduero SA and Bodegas Riojanas USA Corporation.

The original Cenicero winery has grown from boasting an initial capacity of 500 barrels, 22 vats, and 50,000 bottles to nearly 26,000 barrels and 4.5 million bottles. Bodegas Riojanas' current processing capacity is more than 3 million kg of grapes each harvest.

The company's San Vicente de la Sonsierra property, which houses 5,000 oak barrels, can process 1.5 million grapes per harvest.

The wine maker's best sellers MONTE REAL Crianza, MONTE REAL Reserva de Familia, PEÑAMONTE Cosecha, PEÑAMONTE Crianza, PEÑAMONTE Rosado, and VIÑA ALBINA Cosecha Semi-sweet.

Bodegas Riojanas generates about €16.74 million in net income.
