SI Group has come a long way since it first came to life in 1906 by bringing good varnishes to life for General Electric . A leading global developer and manufacturer of phenolic resins, alkylphenolic resins, alkylated phenols and other chemical intermediates, SI Group makes friction material resins for use in the manufacture of brake linings and clutch facings. The company's other products include alkylphenols, phenolic resins, and electronic chemicals used in the production of semiconductors, imaging products, packaging, rubber compounds, agrochemicals, dyes, fuel additives, and flavoring agents. SI Group operates 20 manufacturing plants in 10 countries around the world.

The company has manufacturing plants in Brazil, China, France, India, Korea, Singapore, South Africa, Switzerland, the UK, and the US, and research and technology facilities in Brazil, India, China, Switzerland, and the US.

SI Group develops and manufactures chemical intermediates, specialty resins, and solutions for industrial and consumer goods. It offers contact cement, pressure sensitive adhesives, reactive adhesives, solvents and others.

The company's markets include resins, antioxidants, fuel and lubricants, plastic additives, industrial resins, health and wellness, adhesive resins, surfactants, engineering plastics, pharma, and specialty.

The company generates $1.6 billion in annual sales.

SI Group's strategy includes deploying its resources efficiently, effectively, and safely to meet the needs of its customers. The company also strives to monitor its operations and allocate capacity based properly across its global network, and to regularly make strategic acquisitions to grow, as well as develop its own facilities through organic growth.

In 2015 the company completed a more than 1,000 metric ton expansion at its Newport, Tennessee facility. As a leading worldwide supplier of Biphenol, the expansion is the third of a robust multi-year growth plan to expand SI Group's position in the global marketplace.

That year it also planned to expand of its affiliated Rio Claro, Brazil manufacturing location with a new, state-of-the-art formaldehyde plant. The expansion is expected to bring another 60,000 metric tons per year of capacity on line.

In 2014 bought antioxidants, ibuprofen, and related assets from fellow chemical maker Albemarle Corporation. The purchase, SI's largest ever, includes plants in the US (South Carolina) and China and makes SI one of the world's biggest makers of ibuprofen.

Founded by Howard Wright in 1906, the company was established to develop insulating varnishes for GE's early electrical devices.Descendants of Wright still own the company, which was called Schenectady International until 2006.

In 2012, SI Group announced plans to invest $30 million to build an additional phenolic resin manufacturing site in the Nanjing Chemical Industry Park, in China. The new facility is expected to produce phenolic resins in lump, pastille, liquid, and powder forms to serve the rubber, abrasive, friction, impregnation, and refractory markets. That year it also formed a Russian joint venture to market products in the friction, abrasives and rubber resin market segments.
