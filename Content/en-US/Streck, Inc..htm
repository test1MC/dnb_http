When a vial of your blood goes to the lab, a Streck product might very well be there to meet it. Streck makes more than 30 hematology, chemistry, and immunology products for use in clinical laboratories. The company specializes in hematology control products, which are used to diagnose blood-related problems. Products include cell stabilization kits, urinalysis control tools, blood collection tubes, and molecular diagnostic tests. The company also provides thermometers to monitor refrigerators, freezers, and incubators/room temperatures. Streck sells its products worldwide under its own name, under private labels, and through licenses. Dr. Wayne Ryan founded Streck in 1971.

Streck conducts research, development, manufacturing, and administrative operations at its 200,000 sq. ft. headquarters facility in Omaha, Nebraska. The company's products are sold in the US and about 60 international countries around the globe.

Streck's Quality Management System complies with the FDA Quality System Regulation and is registered as compliant with ISO 9001, ISO 13485, and SOR/98-282 (Canadian Medical Devices Regulations). In addition, Streck makes more than 30 products that have been declared compliant with 98/79/EC, which is the European Union's In Vitro Diagnostic Medical Devices Directive.

The company sells its products and services through sales representatives and a network of distributors.

In addition to providing laboratory supplies, Streck helps its customers maintain profitable and thorough lab operations by offering regulatory, accreditation, and quality control programs, including a free inter-laboratory quality control program known as STATS. In addition, it works to develop new product offerings to ease simplification of use and increase processing times.

Following its strategy of product expansion, in 2014 Streck introduced UA‐Cellular Complete, a urinalysis control with both chemistry analytes and cellular components to more closely mimic a patient sample. UA‐Cellular Complete is assayed for the Siemens CLINITEK Atlas and Sysmex UF1000i combined system. Streck also offers UA‐Cellular for UF, designed specifically for the Sysmex UF1000i urine analyzer, and UA‐Cellular for IQ, designed specifically for the Iris Diagnostics iQ automated urine analyzers.

In 2013 the company introduces CD-Chex CD117, the first and only commercially available flow cytometry control for CD117; introduced LBC‐Chex, the first and only lamellar body count control on the market.

In 2012 Streck launched CD‐Chex Select, the first and only commercially‐available flow cytometry control with the selected group of CD markers used for immunophenotyping, and introduces A1c-Cellular Linearity, the only commercially-available Hemoglobin A1c linearity/calibration verification material with intact red blood cells.

In 2011 it launched the Philisa thermal cycler, which improves DNA strand replication techniques, reducing chemistry-based amplification time to less than 15 minutes.
