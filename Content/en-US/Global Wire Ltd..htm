Global Wire knew how to make contacts. The company made non-ferrous wire and conductors, varying from ultra thin to heavy copper and copper alloy wire. Global Wire targeted companies in the automotive, electronics, telecommunications, and aviation industries. Founded in 1964, the company began as an Israeli joint venture between US investors and a UK wire manufacturer. Global Wire was purchased out of bankruptcy by First Israel Turnaround Enterprise (FITE) and Technological Development and Automation Ltd. (TEDEA) in 2005. International Wire Group bought Global Wire's US operations in 2008. Global Wire subsequently shuttered one Israeli factory and sold its remaining plant to Hod Assaf Industries.
