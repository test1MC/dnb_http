MTI MicroFuel Cells (MTI Micro) is developing direct methanol fuel cell (DMFC) systems for use in wireless communications devices, such as smartphones. The company's first product -- the Mobion rechargeable fuel cell power pack -- is integrated with a portable radio-frequency identification (RFID) reader made by Intermec . Founded in 2001 as a subsidiary of Mechanical Technology , MTI Micro has development agreements with DuPont and the Duracell battery business of Procter & Gamble . The company has received funding from the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) and other government agencies.
