Hayes and Finch has made quite a holy cash cow out of the business of holy sacraments. The company manufactures and sells items belonging inside a church (besides the willing souls and the people attached to them): altars, altar linens, altar breads, baptismal fonts, candles, candlesticks, cassocks, clerical garments, crucifixes, cottas, funeral palls, hymn boards, incenses, kneelers, lecterns, pews, pulpits, stoles, surplices, vestments, wines, even collection plates. Hayes and Finch's products are sold through the company's catalog, web site, and through retail outlets.

The company manufactures, supplies, and delivers quality products and services that meet with the special needs and expectations of the church market.

Hayes and Finch sells its merchandise through the company's catalog, web site, and through retail outlets throughout the UK, Ireland, Canada, and the US.

In 2014 Hayes and Finch moved into the commercial sector, launching a new personalized memorial grave light designed to burn outdoors for 3-4 days at the graveside or memorial place of a loved one.

In 2013 Alan Gow stepped into the non-executive Chairman's role, following Simon Finch's retirement after 50 years service to the company.

The company, founded in 1882 by William Hayes and James Finch, is still owned by the Finch family.
