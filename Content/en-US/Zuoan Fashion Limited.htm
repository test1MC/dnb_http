You say rive gauche, I say Zuoan. The company, whose name means "left bank" in Chinese, designs and sells trendy casual apparel for urban men between 20 and 40 years old. It outsources most of the production of its clothing, footwear, and accessories but makes its most exclusive designs in-house to control quality and unauthorized access. Zuoan's third-party distribution network includes about 1,050 stores selling company wares exclusively in nearly all the country's provinces. It has also begun to open company-owned stores in select locations and has about 30 in seven provinces. Fashion designer James Hong, chairman and CEO, started Zuoan in 2002; the company filed to go public in early 2011.
