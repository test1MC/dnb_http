Dyson Group is zany about zirconia. The company develops performance materials (metal composites, fibers) and thermal products (ceramics, refractories). It specializes in the production of zirconia and tin oxide ceramics that are used by the aerospace, automotive, steel, telecommunications, and electronics industries, among others. (Zirconia is resistant to high temperatures and possesses optimal erosion resistant properties.) Its Dyson Technical Ceramics division makes tin oxide electrodes and firebricks (used in wall arches) for the glass industry.

Dyson is stationed in Sheffield, UK and has a manufacturing unit based in West Hunwick, UK. The company's products are sold in 37 countries across six continents. Europe is its largest market, representing 70% of net sales. Other major markets include Africa and the Asia/Pacific (15%) and the US (15%).

Dyson's revenues decreased 40% from 2013 to 2014 primarily due to decreased sales in Europe and the Asia/Pacific. The company was also negatively affected by a reduction in tin oxide electrode and zirconia nozzle sales.
