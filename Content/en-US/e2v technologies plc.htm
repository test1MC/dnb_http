ev2's products gather information from Earth to Mars. The company makes radio frequency (RF), microwave, and semiconductor-based products that are used in a wide range of sophisticated applications, including TV signal transmission, cancer radiotherapy, and high performance imaging. It makes components, subsystems, and service-level products. Industries e2v serves include defense, space, industrial, dental, medical, scientific, telecommunications, and others. Its imaging products, for example, are have been used in the Hubble Telescope, NASA space missions (including the Mars rovers), and observatories around the world. About a third of the company's sales are in the US.

e2V reports its operations in three segments.

Imaging, which provides imaging sensors, cameras and sub-systems, accounts for 44% of revenue in fiscal 2016; RF Power, which produces components and sub-systems, generates 34%; and Semiconductors, which produces chips for high-speed data converters, microprocessors, memory, analog and logic functions, 22%.

From its base in Chelmsford, UK, e2v sends its products around the world. About a third of revenue comes from the company's customers in the US with European customers supplying another 27% and Asia/Pacific clients, about 24%. The company's revenue from UK customers is 14% of the total. The company maintains nine engineering facilities and six sales offices.

Healthcare, aerospace, and defense are e2v's biggest markets. Customers include BAE Systems , the European Space Agency , and Airbus .

In 2016 (ended March) e2v's revenue rose 5% to £236.4 million from about £225 million in 2015. Imaging revenue jumped 44% higher with increased business from professional imaging and space Imaging, which had strong demand for applications in automatic data collection, machine vision sensors, and optical inspection CMOS cameras. Sales in China rose 14% from year to year.

Net income increased 25% to about £30 million in 2016 from £23.8 million in 2015, on the revenue increase and a decrease in administrative costs.

e2v's cash flow from operations fell to £22.5 million in 2016 from £40.7 million in 2015 because of changes in working capital as a result of increase in income taxes paid.

In 2016 e2v acquired Signal Processing Devices Sweden, a maker of analogue-to-digital processing technology, for £12 million. The transaction was made to accelerating e2v's semiconductor growth.
