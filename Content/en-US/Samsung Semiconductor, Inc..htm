Samsung Semiconductor, Inc. is the North American subsidiary of silicon powerhouse Samsung Electronics . The company markets Samsung's many semiconductor product lines, including its DRAM, SRAM, and flash memory chips; solid-state drives; liquid-crystal display panels and screens; LED backlight technology; and system LSI products (application-specific integrated circuits, image sensors, display drivers, and logic chips). Samsung Semiconductor's components are used in a wide range of applications, including computers, consumer electronics, mobile devices, and industrial equipment. Samsung Semiconductor was established in 1983.

San Jose, California-based Samsung Semiconductor sells products through sales offices, distributors, and sales representatives located throughout the US. The company also serves the Canadian and Latin American markets through distributors and sales representatives.

Samsung Semiconductor contributed 23% revenue to its the parent company Samsung Electronics Co. for 2015.

Parent Samsung Electronics is investing heavily in its operations in its US operations in California and Texas.

The South Korean giant in 2015 opened a 68,000-square-foot fab facility in San Jose to manufacture leading-edge chips and well as to perform research and development of new semiconductor manufacturing technologies.
