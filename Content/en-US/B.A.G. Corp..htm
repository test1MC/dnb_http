B.A.G. Corp. has business in the bag, literally. The company makes Super Sack woven polypropylene bulk containers and related products. Its line eases handling, shipping, and storage of bulk dry-flowable goods, such as chemicals, minerals, and dry food ingredients. Super Sack containers, marketed to outperform conventional flexible intermediate bulk containers (FIBC), include Clean Seal contamination-free sacks, a square-shaped baffled MaxSack, and options for static control and hazardous materials. Along with affiliates FIBC Recycling and Winzen Film + Fiber, B.A.G. operates in the US, selling direct, as well as abroad through manufacturing and warehouse partners. It is a subsidiary of Super Sack Bag.

The company is based in Dallas and has manufacturing locations in Juarez, Mexico; Romania; India; Vietnam; and China.

The Super Sack offers savings in shipping costs, a hot button during economic tough times; its weight is about 84% lighter than a similar size corrugated cardboard box with wooden pallet.

The company's major clients include Du Pont , Starbucks , Bayer , Invista , and Eastman .

B.A.G. (for Better Agricultural Goals) was established in 1969 by Robert Williamson, who envisioned the conventional flexible intermediate bulk container as an efficient and safe way to load crop dusting products into a plane.
