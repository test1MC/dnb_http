ASSA ABLOY Inc. has a secure future. Operating through ASSA Inc., the company manufactures a range of high-security products for industrial, institutional, and residential applications. It produces cylinder locks, padlocks, deadbolts, and high-security auxiliary locks under brands including Corbin Russwin and Sargent. Advanced lock systems combine mechanical and electronic elements to boast user accountability and system control. ASSA is one of the largest suppliers of high-security lock cylinders to the institutional and government markets in North America. Customers of ASSA have included Boeing , the Baltimore Ravens' stadium, and the US Supreme Court . ASSA is a subsidiary of Swedish lockmaker ASSA ABLOY AB .

The company has sales, service, manufacturing, and distribution locations across the US, Puerto Rico, and Canada. Its headquarters is located in New Haven, Connecticut.

ASSA ABLOY Inc. and its ASSA subsidiary are part of the North America unit of the Swedish parent's mechanical locks, lock systems, and fittings division, which accounts for about 35% of annual sales. North American operations account for more than 35% of the parent organization's revenues, with the majority of sales coming from institutional and commercial customers.

The company markets its products through sales representatives and distributors. Customers include commercial, institutional, industrial, and military organizations.

Parent ASSA ABLOY AB reported that 36% of its 2014 revenues came from North America, include 31% from the US.

With customer security as a focus, the company is working to expand its high-tech lock offerings.

In 2015 ASSA acquired California-based Quantum Secure, the leading provider of products and services to help enterprises manage identities and meet compliance requirements in highly-regulated industries. The acquisition provides the company's customers with a robust, policy-driven software application to help them achieve their identity management goals.

It integrated its Internet protocol-enabled locks with new transactional software from Blackboard in 2012 to improve offerings for educational campuses; the locksets utilize Ethernet and WiFi connectivity to allow for single-console security team monitoring of critical functions (including alarm notifications).

ASSA acquired California firm LaserCard for some $80 million in 2011 to expand its offering of secure identity products.

ASSA originally opened in Naperville, Illinois as a marketing arm of its Sweden-based family. Since 1984, the company has matured to manufacture a full slate of security systems. Its marketplace presence expanded into door pulls and trim hardware in 2008 when ASSA acquired Rockwood Manufacturing. The ASSA parent's roots trace back to 1881. Founded by blacksmith August Stenman, ASSA ABLOY began as a small hinge manufacturer in Eskilstuna, Sweden.
