When health care supplies are on the line, Medline Industries goes toe-to-toe with the big guys. With more than 350,000 products, the family-owned company's catalog includes hospital furnishings, durable medical equipment, housekeeping supplies, and exam gloves and garments. The firm manufactures and distributes health care products to such customers as hospitals, long-term care facilities, physician practices, and home health providers. It also acts as a distributor for other manufacturers' products. In addition, Medline offers inventory and supply chain solutions for health care providers. Products are marketed by its more than 1,200 sales representatives through some 50 distribution centers in about 25 countries.

Medline strengthens its relationships with clients by providing a range of consulting, logistics, reporting, cost control, regulatory compliance, and business support services and tools.

The company operates a truck fleet of more than 280 MedTrans vehicles traveling 14 million miles annually.

Medline operates across 90 countries; it has offices and 19 manufacturing facilities in more than 25 countries.

The company manufactures a large number of the products it distributes; it also acts as a distributor for other products through partnerships with companies such as 3M . In addition, Medline forms supply alliances with group hospital purchasing organizations such as Premier and Novation . It is a primary supplier for more than 450 hospitals and health care networks in the US, as well as 10,000 nursing homes and numerous surgery centers and home health agencies. Medline expands its distribution capabilities by opening new warehouses and adding capacity to its 200-vehicle delivery fleet.

Medline reported sales of $5.8 billion in 2013.

New and improved products are a constant for the company. Some of these come from the company's own development efforts and others from manufacturers who distribute through Medline. In 2014 it entered in to several distribution with health care companies for product distribution. For instance, it partnered with Value First, a group purchasing organization specializing in aging services. Another deal was struck with Devon Medical Products: Medline will distribute Devon's line of CircuFlow pneumatic compression therapy pumps for the treatment of lymphedema.

Along with organic product growth, Medline purchases other companies and product lines that are in line with its strategic objectives. The company has grown rapidly in recent years through this steady pace of acquisitions.

The company has been focused on geographic expansion across Europe, Australia, and Asia. In 2014 it established Medline Ireland in anticipation of growth in Ireland's hospital market. It has tripled its distribution and warehouse facility space in Germany and France, and has opened new manufacturing facilities in Slovakia and Japan. The company also opened its international headquarters in The Netherlands. New warehouses have also been built or are being built in Germany and Ireland.

Medline built a new distribution center in Arkansas in 2013; another distribution center is expected to be complete in North Carolina in 2015.

In early 2014, Medline acquired Professional Hospital Supply, a private manufacturer and distributor of health care products, for an undisclosed amount. The purchase boosted the company's operations to encompass some 350,000 medical and surgical products. Smaller acquisitions that year include the purchase of Amendia's anterior cervical fusion distraction pin business and the purchase of multiple cleaning and disinfectant technologies from Medtrol.

In 2015 the company acquired the Compat line of enteral feeding pumps and administration sets in the US and Canada from Nestlé Health Science, making it the second-largest provider in the market.
