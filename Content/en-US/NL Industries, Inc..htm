NL Industries is looking to paint a bright future. The company owns 30% of Kronos Worldwide , one of the world's largest suppliers of titanium dioxide (TiO 2 ), which maximizes the whiteness, opacity, and brightness of paints, plastics, paper, fibers, and ceramics. Kronos produces more than 40 different grades of titanium dioxide. Majority-owned (87%) subsidiary CompX International makes components such as security products (locking systems), ball bearing slides, and ergonomic computer support systems. Valhi , which is 94%-owned by Contran Corporation , in turn owns 83% of NL Industries. Through trusts, billionaire Harold C. Simmons controls Contran, Valhi, and NL Industries.

Kronos Worlwide operates four TiO 2 plants in Europe (one in each of Leverkusen, Germany; Nordenham, Germany; Langerbrugge, Belgium; and Fredrikstad, Norway). In North America, it has a TiO2 plant in Varennes, Quebec; and a 50%-owned joint venture plant in Lake Charles, Louisiana.

CompX International operates three manufacturing facilities in the US: in Mauldin, South Carolina; Neenah, Wisconsin; and Grayslake, Illinois.

The US accounted for 95% of NL Industries' total revenues in 2013.

Holding company NL Industries operates in the component products industry through majority-owned CompX, and in the chemicals industry through its non-controlling interest in Kronos.

CompX makes engineered components that are sold to a variety of industries including recreational transportation (including boats), postal, office and institutional furniture, cabinetry, tool storage, healthcare, gas stations and vending equipment.

Kronos is a leading global producer and marketer of value-added TiO 2 , used in imparting whiteness, brightness, opacity and durability to a wide range of customer applications and end-use markets, including coatings, plastics, paper, inks, food, cosmetics and other industrial and consumer products. Kronos produced 474,000 metric tons of the product in 2013.

CompX sells directly to large OEM customers through its factory-based sales and marketing professionals and through distributors. its customers include San Mateo Postal Data (13% of total sales) and Harley-Davidson (12%).

Kronos, along with its distributors and agents, sells and provides technical services for its products to more than 4,000 customers in 100 countries (mainly in Europe and North America). It sells its products through its direct sales force operating from six sales offices in Europe and one sales office in North America. Kronos also uses sales agents and distributors to sell its products in specific geographic areas.

NL Industries' revenues increased by 11% in 2013 thanks to higher demand for high security pin tumbler locks within the Security Products business, and from a growth in Marine Component sales (outside of the high-performance boat market) through gains in market share.

The growth of its Security Products' sales was supported by the company's ongoing push to diversify products and customers. Marine Components' net sales increased as a result of higher sales in the ski/wakeboard boat market and other non-high performance marine markets.

The company has incurred a net loss of $55 million in 2013 compared to a net income of $75 million in 2012 due to higher corporate expenses and other costs, and an equity loss from its Kronos operations.

Kronos' expenditures for R&D activities were $18 million in 2013; Kronos expected to spend $21 million on research and development in 2014.

NL Industries' routinely compares its liquidity requirements and alternative uses of capital against the estimated future cash flows it expects to receive from its subsidiaries and affiliates. It uses this process to raise additional capital, incur debt, repurchase indebtedness in the market or otherwise modify its dividend policies, consider the sale of its interests in its subsidiaries, affiliates, business, marketable securities or other assets, (or any combination of these actions) in order to increase liquidity, reduce indebtedness, and fund future activities.

To raise cash to pay down debt, in 2012 the company sold CompX's Furniture Components operations to a competitor for $58 million.
