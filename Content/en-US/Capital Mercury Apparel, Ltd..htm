Capital Mercury Apparel is playing the name game. Traditionally a maker of uniforms and private-label clothing, the employee-owned company shifted its focus to concentrate on more upscale, branded merchandise. Capital Mercury, founded in 1960, makes a variety of men's and women's clothing, including shirts, sweaters, dress wear, swimwear, and sportswear. Its Bill Blass Premium, Arnold Palmer, and NYNE brands are sold in department stores, and it makes other labels for Wal-Mart stores. Agreements with the likes of designer menswear labels Robert Stock and Wilke-Rodriguez -- as well as a deal struck with Jones Apparel Group -- have brought the company upscale licensing deals.

Based in New York, Capital Mercury sells its items nationwide.

The company boasts offices in Hong Kong, Korea, and Bangladesh. Its manufacturing is done in Mexico and Central America. Within the US, Capital Mercury has a pair of warehouses in Arkansas that help to serve one of its primary customers, Wal-Mart . It gets its products in the hands of retailers through a network of distributors.

Capital Mercury specializes in making woven shirts, outerwear, sweaters, and camp shirts. It has expanded its portfolio of products in recent years to extend its reach into new niches of the industry. Tapping into its expertise in uniforms, Capital Mercury launched a line of fashion scrubs called Bill Blass Healthcare.
