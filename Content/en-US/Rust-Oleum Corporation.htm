Rust-Oleum has been fighting the spread of iron oxide and other corrosion for several decades. The company makes paints, seals and other coatings that resist moisture and corrosion. Designed for materials that are exposed to the elements, including light fixtures, patio furniture, wheelbarrows, mailboxes, and window trim, Rust-Oleum products come in multiple colors and textures. It also makes paints for indoor furniture, epoxies for garage floors and truck beds, and oil finishes for wood. Key brands include Stops Rust, High Performance, NeverWet, Painter's Touch, Universal, Varathane, Zinsser, and Watco. Rust-Oleum is a subsidiary of chemicals and consumer products heavyweight RPM International .

Rust-Oleum operates manufacturing plants in the US (Maryland, Massachusetts, New Jersey, West Virginia, and Wisconsin), Belgium, and Canada.

A flagship brand of RPM International , the company has grown from a limited line of 12 rust-preventative paint products in the 1990s to become a global leader in terms of brand recognition and market share in the consumer small project paints and related products space with hundreds of proprietary products. It offers a broad line of rust-preventing coating products for the Do-It-Yourself and professional markets, as well as specialty products, deck and fence restoration products, and metallic and faux finish coatings.

In 2013 Rust-Oleum and DAP Group (which make up RPM's consumer segment) accounted for 37% of RPM's total revenues.

New products introduced in recent years have included Rust-Oleum Ultimate Polyurethane, formulated using a custom blend of hybrid oils; Rust-Oleum Premium Deck and Fence Stain; and new interior and exterior wood care lines. In addition to product innovation and diversification, the company has grown geographically through acquisitions.

In 2014 it acquired Krud Kutter, Inc., a marketer of specialty cleaning products for both residential and industrial markets which sells it products at home centers, hardware stores, mass merchants, and industrial supply outlets.

That year Rust-Oluem also bought Citadel Restoration and Repair, Inc., a producer and marketer of premium concrete and wood deck floor coatings. The company's Citadel brand is marketed to professional contractors, while its Rock Solid brand is targeted to the DIY market. Citadel also offers a premium line of Rock Solid exterior wood care products that outperforms conventional stains.

In 2013 the company acquired assets of XIM Products, a producer of specialty primers and coatings based in Westlake, Ohio. XIM offers a range of primers offering adhesion to difficult-to-paint surfaces, as well as other problem-solving coatings. The deal added the Peel Bond, UMA, and TILE DOC brands to Rust-Oleum's product portfolio.

Rust-Oleum was founded in 1921 by a Scottish-born sea captain named Robert Fergusson who discovered that fish oil prevented rust. RPM International acquired Rust-Oleum in 1994.
