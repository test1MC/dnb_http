Let's cut right to the point: construction materials and mining is the lifeblood of Knife River Corporation. The company mines aggregates and markets raw construction materials including crushed stone, gravel, and sand. Other construction materials include asphalt, liquid asphalt cement, and ready-mix concrete. Knife River also provides integrated construction services for public and private projects such as site development, highway construction, and marine construction. The company primarily operates in the central, southern, and western US. Knife River is a subsidiary of MDU Resources .

The company has administrative, manufacturing, and processing facilities in 13 states: Alaska, California, Hawaii, Idaho, Iowa, Minnesota, Montana, North Dakota, Oregon, South Dakota, Texas, Washington, and Wyoming.

Knife River makes up MDU's construction materials and contracting segment, which reported more than $1.6 billion in revenues in 2012. The segment accounts for about 35% of the parent company's annual revenues, which totaled nearly $4.1 billion in 2012. Knife River's results showed growth in both 2011 and 2012, following a dip during 2008 and 2009 due to the economic recession's impact on the construction industry.

Knife River's strategic initiatives include improving health and safety programs for its employees, partners, and clients. Initiatives include fleet safety, electrical, driving, and workplace hazard education and training programs. The company is also working to implement sustainability measures in its operations, including asphalt and cement recycling programs.
