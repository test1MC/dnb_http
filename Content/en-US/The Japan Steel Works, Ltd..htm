At The Japan Steel Works (JSW), steel is only the tip of the iceberg. JSW's industrial and plastics machinery division manufactures general machinery such as deck cranes, magnesium alloy injection molding machines, and film and sheet machinery. The company makes steel castings and forgings, steel plates, pressure vessels, and steel structures. It also manages and sells real estate. JSW's customers range from shipbuilders and manufacturers to companies in the chemicals, petrochemicals, and utility industries. The company gets approximately all of its sales from Japan.

The company operates three manufacturing plants in Japan: Hiroshima, Muroran, and Yokohama. The Muroran plant (its original factory) includes a 14,000-ton hydraulic press and a 100-ton electro-slag remelting furnace. The plant's products include cast and forged steel products, environmental preservation systems and equipment, steel sheet and pipes, towers, and industrial machinery.

JSW has three operating segments. The Forging and Energy segment makes iron and steel products, chemical machinery, nuclear power-related machinery, metallic mold, petroleum refinery, petrochemical, general chemical products, wind generated power machinery, pressure containers, clad plates, and steel pipes. The Industry Machinery segment manufactures plastic injection machines, resin manufacturing and processing machinery, fluid machines, hydraulic equipment, hollow molding machines, electronic equipment, and display manufacturing equipment, and magnesium alloy injection. JSW's last segment, Real Estate and Others, is engaged in real estate leasing and development businesses; it also provides operational support to other segments.

Founded in 1907, Japan Steel Works was the largest private weapons manufacturer in Japan at the end of World War II.
