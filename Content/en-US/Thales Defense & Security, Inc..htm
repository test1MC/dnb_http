Thales Communications has an ear for high-end radio gear. The company makes secure radio equipment -- specializing in small lightweight radio systems. Its software-based radio products include MBITR (multi-band inter/intra team radio for government programs), high-frequency RF systems, JEM (Joint Tactical Radio System Enhanced MBITR), and Thales 25 (handheld radio for both commercial and government customers). Thales Communications tailors its products for tactical, naval and maritime, and aerospace applications. It serves the US military, federal law enforcement agencies, and military forces of other allied nations. The company is a subsidiary of French aerospace and defense electronics manufacturer Thales .

Outside of Maryland, where the company has four locations (including its development and manufacturing facilities), the company operates from maintenance and repair facilities in Kuwait, Iraq, and Afghanistan to support its products in the field.

In 2013 the company completed the acquisition of the Visionix Helmet Mounted Display (HMD) and InterSense motion tracking businesses formerly owned by Gentex Corporation. The new company, Thales Visionix, Inc., is operating as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Thales. This acquisition is a strategic fit that complements Thales' global portfolio of Helmet Mounted Sight and Display systems for rotary and fixed wing platforms. It gives Thales the ability to offer highly capable HMD technology amidst rigorous budget constraints within the market.

Thales Communications moved to expand its wideband technology capabilities in 2012 when it bought privately-held Tampa Microwave, based in its namesake's city in Florida. Tampa designs, develops, and manufactures tactical satellite communications (SATCOM) terminals and satellite simulator test equipment. The company operates as a subsidiary of Thales Communications and brings improved capabilities in delivering wideband communications between parties that are visually obstructed.
