For consumers and manufacturers, The Tranzonic Companies protects and serves. The company makes and distributes personal care items (including feminine hygiene and adult incontinence products), cleaning supplies, safety products, and industrial textiles nationwide. It also makes sanitary napkin dispensing machines. Tranzonic repackages and distributes industrial wiping materials, cleaning chemicals, and disposable work clothing through its CCP Industries business unit. The company's customer base includes retailers, medical and surgical supply distributors, and industrial and personal care products companies. Investment firm Silver Oak Services Partners bought Tranzonic in 2013 from Linsalata Capital Partners.

Investment firm Linsalata Capital Partners, which had held Tranzonic for about 15 years and rebuilt its executive suite, sold Tranzonic in 2013 to fellow Illinois private equity investment firm Silver Oak Services Partners.

Based in Ohio, Tranzonic makes and distributes its products nationwide for customers and distributors that serve the away-from-home market of the consumer products industry.

As part of its business, 90-year-old Tranzonic operates through two segments: HOSPECO and CCP Industries.

HOSPECO focuses on making cleaning and protection products, supplying them to distributors that serve the away-from-home market. The business unit specializes in feminine hygiene items, toilet seat covers, air care products, safety items, non-woven and woven wiping products, and other restroom essentials.

CCP Industries distributes cleaning, containment, and protection products to end-user customers. It distributes several products, such as non-woven wiping cloths; safety, sorbent, and restroom supplies; and hand care, chemicals, and cleaning supplies. It directly serves a targeted set of away-from-home customers.

Tranzonic operates a primary converting and distributing facility in Knoxville, Tennessee, with a secondary facility in Miami and Reno, Nevada.

To better serve its customers and retain its status as a leading supplier to the away-from-home market, Tranzonic regularly expands its portfolio of products. It also works to leverage the latest product technology, as well as tap into the most recent information technology capabilities.

Under new ownership in 2013, Tranzonic aims to grow through organic investments in sales and marketing and product development, as well as via add-on acquisitions.

A leader in the away-from-home market, Tranzonic boasts a diverse customer base. The company serves industrial and personal care products companies, as well as retailers and distributors of medical and surgical supplies. Its HOSPECO unit sells its products through distribution while CCP sells directly to end users through a field salesforce.
