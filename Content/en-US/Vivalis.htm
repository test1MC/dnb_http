Valneva is a biotech company researching and developing innovative vaccines. It has two travelers' vaccines on the market: Ixiaro, for the prevention of Japanese encephalitis, and Dukoral, for the prevention of cholera (and in some countries, E. coli-related diarrhea). Candidates in development include treatments for Clostridium difficile and Lyme borreliosis. At the hear of Valneva's research is the EB66 cell line, which is derived from duck embryonic (ES) cells -- traditionally, vaccines have been made from chicken eggs or chicken embryo fibroblasts (CEF). In 2013, France's Vivalis and Australia's Intercell merged to form Valneva.
