Cambridge Broadband makes wireless broadband transmission gear used in telecom base stations and customer premises equipment. It also offers related software, which allows wireless operators to manage their networks. The company serves more than 70 telecommunications service providers (including seven of the top ten global operators) as well as some of the leading mobile firms. Cambridge Broadband sells its wares under the VectaStar brand name, which is based the point-to-multipoint (PMP) architecture. Investors include Accel Partners , Amadeus Capital Partners , Adara Venture Partners, Samsung Ventures Europe, and TVM Capital.

Cambridge Broadband has offices in Dubai, Kenya, South Africa, Nigeria and the UK, as well as a manufacturing facility in China. Its products have been used in about 45 countries.

The company's customers have included such firms as Airtel , Antenna Hungaria, etisalat , Level GST, Vodacom , Globitel, and JT. Cambridge Broadband sells through a number of Value-Added Reseller (VAR) partners, including GC International Corporation, WiConnect, ReGro Technology, and NEC .

In 2014 Cambridge Broadband launched VectaStar wideband products to provide 600Mb/s backhaul capacity for macro and small cell next generation networks. To serve the growing demand for high speed corporate network services, that year Cambridge Broadband also signed an agreement with GlobalCom Data Services to double the size of its VectaStar network.

The company has a strong presence in Africa, where its equipment operates in 14 countries. Cambridge Broadband has moved into the US. It has worked with First Communications in Ohio and TelePacific in California.

High-tech veterans Peter Wharton and John Porter co-founded Cambridge Broadband in 2000.
