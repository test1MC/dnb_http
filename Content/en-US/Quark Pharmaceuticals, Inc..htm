Quarks may be the basic building blocks of matter, but it's a fundamental unit of biology -- genes -- that Quark Pharmaceuticals is interested in. The drug development company is using cutting-edge RNA interference (RNAi) technology to develop small-interfering RNA (siRNA) molecules, or drugs that inhibit targeted genes from producing proteins associated with certain diseases. The company's two products in late-stage clinical trials are QPI-002 (for the treatment of delayed graft function, or DGF) and QPI-2007 (for acute non-arteritic anterior ischemic optic neuropathy, or NAION).
