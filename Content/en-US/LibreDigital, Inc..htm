LibreDigital speaks for the trees. The company digitally distributes content from newspapers, books, magazines, and other print media. Its software lets subscribers download publications that can be read on a computer desktop, laptop, or mobile handheld device without connecting to the Internet. LibreDigital also offers Web-based reading; its customers and partners include Apple , Barnes & Noble , Hachette Book Group , HarperCollins , Kaplan , The New York Times , Simon & Schuster , and Sony . It has offices in the US and the UK. The company changed its name from NewsStand in 2008 to reflect an expanding set of products to a broader set of publishers. LibreDigital was acquired by R.R. Donnelley in 2011.
