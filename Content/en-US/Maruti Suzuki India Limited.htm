-East meets South in Maruti Suzuki India (MSIL). The New Delhi-based company is a subsidiary of Japan-based Suzuki , and India's largest OEM of passenger cars. It touts a 17 brand line with more than 150 options, including the family car Maruti 800, premium hatchback Ritz, and popular compact A-Star. The company runs two production sites: Its Gurgaon facility, integrates three plants; and the Manesar facility is dedicated to first-to-market rollouts. MSIL produces 1.5 million a year. Revving up its bottom line, MSIL exports Suzuki models to Latin America, Africa, and Southeast Asia, and sporadically to Europe.

The company sells its cars across India and exports to about 125 countries.

MSIL has a national service network spanning more than 1,420 Indian cities and towns and sales network that operates in about 880 cities.

The company's key rivals, India's Tata Motors and South Korea's Hyundai Motor , are persistently chipping away at the MSIL market dominance. MSIL is shoring up against the competition by launching a series of new products, and improving production capacity. Recent models look to freshen up the manufacturer's offerings to attract new drivers, as well as plug alternative fuel opportunities.

In 2012 MSIL agreed to merge Suzuki Powertrain India Limited (SPIL) into MSIL. SPIL supplies engines as well as transmissions for vehicles to MSIL. Suzuki holds a 70% share in SPIL; MSIL, 30%. The merger will enable MSIL to put all its diesel engine capacity under a single management to improve synergies and costs.

In 2006 MSIL's production and sales operations were merged with, and took the name of Maruti Udyog. In 2007 the Indian government divested its 10% stake in the company. The company subsequently changed its name to Maruti Suzuki India.
