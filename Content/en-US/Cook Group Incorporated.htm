Cook Group, through flagship subsidiary Cook Incorporated , makes catheters, wire guides, stents, and other devices used in minimally invasive procedures. Other medical offerings include urological and gynecological devices, biological materials, endoscopic accessories, cardiac rhythm management, and vascular products. Its industrial parts manufacturer Sabin makes plastic and metal parts used in Cook's medical products, while its K-Tube company manufactures stainless steel tubing used in a variety of industries. The company, one of the world's largest privately held medical device firms, was founded by William Cook in 1963.

Cook Medical operates through divisions covering aortic intervention (endovascular treatment of aortic aneurysm), critical care (airway, feeding, and vascular access), endoscopy (gastrointestinal), interventional radiology (venous access, drainage, biopsy), lead management (cardiac), otolaryngology (head, neck, throat), and peripheral intervention (radiology, cardiology, vascular surgery). It also has units that cover surgical, urological, and women's health products. Overall, the company makes 16,000 products serving more than a dozen hospital lines.

The manufacturing of Cook Medical's products is conducted through operating subsidiaries including Cook Incorporated, Cook Biotech, Cook Vascular, and Cook Urological. Cook Polymer Technology (formerly Sabin) manufactures and supplies other Cook companies with plastic components; it operates from two facilities in Illinois and Canton. Cook Medical houses its development and design think-tank at its MED Institute.

With an eye towards reinvesting in its hometown of Bloomington, Indiana, Cook Group runs a number of non-medical businesses, including a real estate development firm (CFC) that preserves important Bloomington buildings, the Grant Street Inn, and the French Lick Resort. The Cook Aviation unit provides fixed base operator services (fueling and aircraft care as well as concierge services) in Bloomington, while Star Travel Services provides corporate travel management and event planning.

While most of Cook Group's operations are based in the US, it also has manufacturing and distribution facilities in Australia, China, Hong Kong, Ireland, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, and Thailand. The company's medical products are sold in 135 countries around the globe.

Cook Medical's Asia-Pacific Distribution Centre (APDC) in Shanghai, China, is the first point of distribution to Asian markets. Its European Distribution Center in Baesweiler, Germany, streamlines distribution activities across Europe.

Cook markets its medical products through local sales agents and independent distribution firms. Customers include medical clinicians, doctors, hospitals, distributors, and supply chain management firms.

Most of Cook Group's growth efforts are targeted towards developing new medical products. Recent R&D efforts include the development of new arterial stents, bone biopsy products, ureteral access, and nonvascular access systems. In 2014 the company expanded its percutaneous kidney stone extractor line and introduced a new microcatheter.

Founder William Cook served as CEO of the company until his death in 2011. His son, Carl Cook, was named as his successor.
