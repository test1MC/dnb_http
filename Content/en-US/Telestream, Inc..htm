At Telestream the name says it all. The company provides hardware and software to enable video streaming across the Internet. Its products include video encoding and distribution via Episode Encoder, Launch for sending video clips, and FlipFactory for exchange between incompatible systems. Telestream sells to news, broadcast and cable, and video production companies, as well as to the education and government markets. Customers have included ABC , the BBC , and Technicolor . It sells directly and through partnerships with systems integrators and distributors. Founded in 1998 by television industry veterans CEO Dan Castles and CTO Shawn Carnahan. In 2015 private equity firm Genstar bought Telestream from another private investment firm, Thoma Bravo .

Telestream's roster of customer includes broadcast and cable networks, CBS , CNN , FOX ; satellite and cable TV providers, Direct-TV (owned by AT&T ) and Time Warner ; and companies in non-broadcast industries. The company sells through distributors, an online store, and resellers.

Telestream is looking for expansion through technology and geographically.

The company launched Telestream Cloud, a software-as-a-service (SaaS) offering that it acquired through its purchase of PandaStream. The product builds on Telestream's position and history in transcoding. The platform makes the PandaStream encoding capabilities available under the Telestream brand.

Telestream beefed up its slaes operations to pitch its services Europe and the Asia-Pacific region. The company reports that overall sales of its Enterprise Products and Services in EMEA and APAC regions have increased nearly 50% in the last two years.

Telestream made the PandaStream deal in 2015.
