Kyocera Tycom manufactures carbide cutting tools, such as countersinks, drills, drill bits, end mills, scoring tools, routers, router bits, and end mills, while also providing resharpening services. The company caters to the industrial, electronics, medical, and aerospace sectors. Its primary market focus is the printed circuit board industry. In addition to its home in Southern California, Kyocera Tycom maintains operations in Canada, China, Hong Kong, Japan, and other parts of the US. It is also the US distributor for sister subsidiary Kyocera Advanced Ceramics cutlery and kitchen tools. Founded in 1987 as Tycom Corporation, the company became a subsidiary of San Diego-based Kyocera International in 2001.
