To paraphrase William Butler Yeats, brothers Carlos and Edgar Garcia took their "little bag of dreams, unloosed the cord, and became wrapped round," in bags. The company manufactures five types of industrial kraft bags used to hold a breadth of products, from animal feed to fertilizer, baking mix, and beans, and single beers. The lineup of tubular-style bags includes pinch bottom open mouth, pasted valve stepped end, and a long durable sleeve, or end bag for holding hundreds of can lids. Despite 2009's weak economy, along with California's soaring costs and tightly regulated business environment, Endpak relocated and expanded its manufacturing plant. Opened in 1993, the company is owned and run by the Garcias.
