Reichhold's products have clearly taken hold. It is one of the world's top suppliers of unsaturated polyester resins for composites and a leading supplier of resins used in coatings. Its makes gel coats and bonding agents for the composites industry (to make tubs and showers, in marine applications, and to provide corrosion-resistance). It provides coating resins (powder coating, epoxy, and radiation-cured resins) for the automotive, aerospace, industrial maintenance and other markets. It also provides latex, rosin, and other resins for the graphic arts market.

Heavily in debt, in 2014 the company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection.

Reichhold operates in the Americas, Asia/Pacific, Europe, and the Middle East, and has 20 manufacturing plants (17 operated by Reichhold) and five technology centers in 12 countries.

The company offers complete line of resin products, gelcoats, and bonding pastes. Reichhold's two primary businesses include the Composites Business (unsaturated polyester and vinyl ester resins for composites applications) and the Coatings Business (from resins for producing low VOC architectural paints, to resins for high-performance industrial applications).

Reichhold's primary distributors include Advanced Plastics, Composites One , and Nexeo Solutions. Composites brands include POLYLITE, HYDREX, DION, NORPOL, and ENVIROLITE. Coating products are sold under BECKOSOL AQ, UROTUF, and EPOTUF brands.

The company's products are used in custom applications by the anti-corrosion, high speed train, industrial construction, graphic arts, infrastructure, marine, wind power, and other industries.

In 2013 Reichhold's Technology Center in Sandefjord, Norway was granted 7.1 mill NOK over three years for the RenWind project under the Norwegian Research Council's clean energy program. The Center is looking to develop environmentally friendly and cost-efficient high performance, no-emission vinyl hybrid resin systems for the production of windmill blades for wind turbines.

The company has been expanding globally through joint ventures, and is targeting growth markets in Asia. Fast-growing China is a key growth market for Reichhold. In 2012 the company opened a new plant in Tianjin, China, to make composites, including unsaturated polyester resins, vinyl ester resins, and gel coats.

In 2012 Reichhold and Umicore teamed up to launch a user-friendly alternative curing technology for unsaturated polyester resins.

Reichhold opened a new manufacturing facility in India in 2009, expanding its reach into markets on the Indian subcontinent. The plant makes unsaturated polyester resins for automobile, chemical equipment, engineering stone, glass-reinforced pipe, wind turbine, and other industries.

Reichhold is owned by a group of investors, including its own executives, who leveraged a buyout from DIC Corporation in 2005. The company was founded in 1927 as an automobile paint supplier by German immigrant Henry Reichhold.
