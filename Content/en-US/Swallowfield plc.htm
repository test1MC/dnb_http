Swallowfield makes a living out of being clean. The company is a contract manufacturer of toiletries, cosmetics, and household goods for major department and specialty retailers in the UK. It formulates as well as produces global brands and private label products for such customers as Marks & Spencer and PZ Cussons . Swallowfield owns production facilities in the UK and Czech Republic, and has an expanding presence in China via a venture with Jahwa, IE (a subsidiary of one of China's largest toiletries players). Founded in 1876, Swallowfield was once a subsidiary of Cadbury ; it was spun off in a management buyout in 1986 and later floated on the UK stock market. Peter Gyllenhammar owns almost 30% of the company.
