Breathe deep: Greenheck Fan does a heck of a job. The employee-owned company, one of Wisconsin's largest manufacturers, makes industrial ventilation equipment, including fans, ventilators, and dampers. Precision Coils, Valent, Accurate, and other affiliates, supply louvers, heating and cooling coils, air-handling systems, and related offerings. Greenheck Fan operates plants in the US as well as Asia and Mexico, and it is supported by a distribution network in the US and a global sales force. Products are used primarily behind walls or on the roofs of offices, hotels, malls, schools, and industrial plants.

The company operates through domestic distribution centers located in California, Florida, Kentucky, North Carolina, Minnesota, Texas, and Wisconsin. It has international centers stationed in Mexico, Indiana, and China.

Greenheck's ventilation products are sold through US and Canada from a network of 130 manufacturer representatives and an additional 70 more around the world. Some of these representatives include Air-Tech of Pensacola, Stinebaugh & Company, Air Components, Norman S. Wright Mechanical Equipment, and CFM Company. Greenheck's fans are used in all types of commercial, institutional, and industrial buildings in applications from comfort ventilation to manufacturing processes.

Greenheck grows by expanding and opening new facilities. In 2013 it announced plans to invest $30 million to expand its operations in Shelby, North Carolina, and to establish a new manufacturing operation near its existing Kings Mountain facility. This new plant will accelerate growth of its centrifugal, vane axial, and industrial fan business.

The company made an international move to expand in late 2013 with the establishment of Greenheck India Private Ltd, located in Bawal, India. The new location in India will serve customers in both the Indian and Middle Eastern markets.

In 2012 Greenheck Fan threw its hat into the social media arena when it launched its new Toolbox App that allows engineers and contractors to make quick calculations. The app allows users to to input, measure, and calculate fan values. It also allows them to measure HVAC duct and is available on Apple and Android products.

Brothers, Bob and Bernie Greenheck founded the company as a small sheet metal shop in 1947.
