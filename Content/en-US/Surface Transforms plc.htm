Surface Transforms provides carbon ceramic materials for carbon-based life forms. The company manufactures high-performance carbon fiber ceramic products used for the aerospace, defense, and transportation industries. Surface Transforms' products include aerospace components; armor plating; ceramic brakes and clutches for automobiles, trains, and aircraft; and rocket motor systems. Surface Transforms' customers include Roxel UK , Dunlop Aerospace , and the UK Ministry of Defence. It is increasingly looking for more commercial applications of its products and to expand its customer base internationally.

Surface Transforms' offices and manufacturing site are located in Ellesmere Port, Cheshire. It also has a sales office in Germany.

The manufacturer serves a number of industry markets, such as aerospace components, aircraft brakes, automotive brakes and clutches, motorsport brakes and clutches, rail brakes, and rocket motor systems.

The company's primary customers for retrofit products include Porsche , Ferrari , Nissan , and tuning companies.

Surface Transforms' distributors are based in UK, Italy, France, Switzerland, Portugal, Eastern Europe, and Scandinavia.

In 2014 Surface Transforms' net revenues increased by 20% thanks to the rebound of sales to a major European brake manufacturer customer (which suffered supply chain problems in 2013) and a repeat of license income to a US clutch manufacturer, offset by reduced sales from its retrofit road car products.

The company's net loss increased by 16% in 2014 due to higher research costs, as the result of a growth in demand from Original Equipment Manufacturers.

In 2014 Surface Transforms' cash outflow increased by 28%.

Surface Transforms is focused on building revenue streams from a variety of different markets. Its primary strategy is to establish well engineered products which will sell into the automotive retrofit market.

The company was formed in 1992 to exploit the potential of new composite manufacturing material technologies developed by ICI.
