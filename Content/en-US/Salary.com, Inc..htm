Think you're grossly underpaid? Check out Salary.com to find out for sure. (Just refrain from doing so while you're at work.) The company's website provides the Salary Wizard, which offers employee compensation data and analysis geared toward individuals, managers, and businesses. Other tools include a Cost of Living Wizard, Benefits Wizard, Performance Self Test, and Job Assessor. The firm provides employer-reported data on more than 4,000 job titles. Salary.com also operates Salary.com for Business (at http://business.salary.com), a separate site that offers articles, tips, and tools for companies. Most revenue comes from advertising. Salary.com is a division of human resources firm Kenexa Corporation .

The company has about a dozen salary tools, including Mom Salary Wizard and Dad Salary Wizard, a handful of job search tools, education and college tuition planners, and advice info on education, salary negotiation, and job searching. It also offers compensation and talent management software.

Kenexa acquired Salary.com for some $80 million in 2010. It made the deal for Salary.com's compensation management solutions that complement Kenexa's own suite of talent acquisition and retention services, and were later rolled into Kenexa. Salary.com was also attractive for its customer base. The more than 3,700 subscribers to its compensation and talent management products included major corporate clients, such as CVS Health , News Corporation , and In-n-Out Burgers .

While the acquisition helped Kenexa grow, the deal also benefited Salary.com, which faced some troubles as a result of the recession -- business declined as customers reduced their hiring. Despite the economic downturn, Salary.com expanded through multiple acquisitions. Having started during the dot com boom as an online salary wizard, the company eventually grew to offer a plethora of HR-related services. As it increased its offerings, its share price plummeted.

Salary.com was founded in 1999 by Kent Plunkett. It filed an IPO in 2007.
