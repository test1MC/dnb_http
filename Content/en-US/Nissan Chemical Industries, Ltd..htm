Nissan Chemical Industries offers a wide range of industrial and specialty chemicals, electronic materials, agrochemicals, and pharmaceuticals. Nissan Chemical's chemical products include melamine, ammonia derivatives, silica products and electronic coatings, and acids. Its smallest segment, pharmaceuticals, includes anti-hypertensive, anti-inflammatory, and lipid-lowering drugs. The company's agricultural unit makes herbicides, insecticides, and fungicides and owns the rights to sell Monsanto 's Roundup brand of herbicides in Japan. Sales within its home country account for the bulk of Nissan Chemical's business though it also sells in France, Korea, the US, and China.

Nissan Chemical operates in Japan, France, Korea, and the US.

Its major segments include Chemicals, Performance Materials, Agricultural Chemicals, Pharmaceuticals, and Advanced Materials.

Chemicals: Basic Chemicals (industrial chemicals such as melamine, sulfuric acid, nitric acid and ammonia as well as fatty alcohol); Fine chemicals (antimony pentoxide, melamine-based flame retardants, Phenylphosphonic Acid and its derivatives); and Custom Chemicals (research and development of active pharmaceutical ingredients).

Performance Materials: (Display materials such as LCD displays and anti-reflective coating for displays); Semiconductor materials (anti-reflective coating semiconductor material); and Inorganic materials (nanoparticle control technology).

Agricultural Chemicals: (Proprietary agricultural chemical products).

Pharmaceuticals: (Ketoprofen, efonidipine hydrochloride and others).

Advanced Materials: (Organic materials, inorganic materials, and electronics materials).

Expanding its semiconductor materials portfolio, in 2013 Nissan Chemical acquired Germany's Thin Materials AG (TMAT). Nissan Chemical will use TMAT's advanced processing and material development technologies for 3D packaging.

The company was founded in 1887 as Japan's first chemical fertilizer manufacturer.
