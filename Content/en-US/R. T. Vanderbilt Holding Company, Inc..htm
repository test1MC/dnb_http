You needn't be a Dow or a DuPont to be successful in the chemical industry. But it can't hurt to be a Vanderbilt. R. T. Vanderbilt today is based in Connecticut, operates throughout the US, and sells its more than 800 products around the world. In addition to manufacturing rubber and numerous chemicals for the adhesives, agriculture, ceramics, cosmetics, paper, and petroleum industries, R. T. Vanderbilt also mines kaolin clay (used in pigments, paints, and paper) and through subsidiary Dixie Clay Company. The company also acts as a chemical distributor.

The company's subsidiaries operate chemical manufacturing plants in Connecticut and Kentucky, and mining and minerals processing facilities in Arizona, California, Kentucky, New York, Nevada, North Carolina, and South Carolina. It sells its products in some 80 countries worldwide.

A 2013 a reorganization created five wholly owned subsidiaries; which include Vanderbilt Chemicals, LLC; Vanderbilt Minerals, LLC; Vanderbilt Global Services, LLC; Vanderbilt International, SARL; and Vanderbilt (Beijing) Trading, Ltd.

Vanderbilt Chemical has rubber chemical manufacturing plants in Connecticut and Kentucky.

Vanderbilt Minerals, primarily through (Dixie Clay, Gouverneur Mineral and Standard Mineral) has a number of mining locations (smectite clay for VEEGU magnesium aluminum silicate) in Nevada and California.

Vanderbilt Global Services provides support to the company's operating businesses.

Switzerland-based Vanderbilt International provides marketing and technical support services to Vanderbilt Chemicals and Vanderbilt Minerals in Europe, Africa, Middle East and Russia.

Vanderbilt (Beijing) Trading provides marketing and technical support services to the chemicals and minerals businesses in China.

The company sells more than 60 categories of minerals and chemicals to 12 different industries: adhesives, agriculture, ceramics, cosmetics, household products, paint, paper, petroleum, pharmaceutical, plastics, rubber, and wire and cable.

In 2013 the company reorganized of its North American operations to improve operational efficiencies while focusing on growing its chemicals and mining businesses.

That year R. T. Vanderbilt completed a $30 million expansion project at its Murray, Kentucky, chemical facility, increasing capacity and adding automated packaging capabilities to its VEEGUM (a liquid thickener for pharmaceuticals) product line.

That year Vanderbilt Chemicals announced plans to cut commercial production at its chemical plant in Bethel, Connecticut by the end of 2014. To cut costs, production will shift to the newly expanded plant in Murray, Kentucky. (In 2012 and 2013 R. T. Vanderbilt spent $13.5 million to upgrade the Murray plant.

R. T. Vanderbilt was founded in 1916 in New York City by Robert Thurlow Vanderbilt. A Vanderbilt (High B.) still leads the company.
