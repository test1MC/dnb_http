Dialogic serves as a gateway to Internet protocol (IP)-based communications. The company provides hardware and software systems used to build and integrate computer telephony networks over IP connections. Its products include software (PowerMedia), computer signaling boards that handle multimedia and message processing, and media gateways (I-Gate) for delivering voice calls over digital lines with a level of quality comparable to analog landline phone systems. Dialogic sells its products worldwide to enterprises and service providers through resellers, distributors, and systems integrators including Advantech and Syntellect .

Based in Milpitas, California, the company operates about 30 locations in More than 20 countries which including offices in France, Slovenia, Spain and Brazil; development locations in Canada, India, the UK, Israel, China, Russia, Singapore, Malaysia, Australia, Hong Kong, Japan and the Netherlands. The US market represents 46% of Dialogic's revenues, followed by Europe (35%), and Asia/Pacific (19%).

The company's products are offered through distribution partners such as TEMs, VARs and other channel partners with more than 15 locations in 10 countries worldwide to more than 540 customers in more than 120 countries. Sales and marketing expenses totaled to more than $33 million in 2013 and $42 million in 2012.

Dialogic reported a 17% decrease in revenues in 2013 due to a decline in demand for legacy products, as well as project timing and associated revenue recognition of next generation products and a slight decline in services revenues.

It posted a loss of $53.9 million in 2013 (compared to $37.6 million loss in 2012) due to lower revenues and impairment of goodwill and indefinite-lived assets expenses of $31.8 million (absent in 2012) partially offset by a decrease in costs of revenues of $51 million for 2013, as well as a decrease in research and development expenses of $27 million.

In 2013 the company's cash flow from operating activities was up $1 million due to its net loss, partially offset by adjustments for non-cash items ($50 million), a $10 million decrease in operating assets, and a $11 million drop in operating liabilities.

The company is investing in research and development activities and expanding in eight different locations within five countries to further expand the designing, developing, and enhancing of products (for which it spent about $27 million in 2013). Dialogic sees its largest market growth occurring in countries such as India, Brazil, Russia, and China.

In 2014, Myanma Posts and Telecommunications selected Dialogic's intelligent call control solutions to modernize and expand its domestic core switching infrastructure. In the same year DollarPhone also expanded its prepaid and wholesale offerings with enhanced Dialogic ControlSwitch.

In 2013 ZRG International deployed Dialogic's PowerMedia HMP software media server to offer unprecedented flexibility in contact center and computer telephony integration to its customers throughout Pakistan, Bangladesh, Africa, and the Gulf region.

That year, APEX Voice Communications and Dialogic collaborated to develop APEX's WebRTC service delivery platform.

The company has used a string of acquisitions to build its product line, expand its customer base, and relocate to the US. In 2010 Dialogic Corporation, a privately-held company based in Montreal, acquired California-based public company Veraz in a stock-swap transaction. The combined publicly-traded company took the name Dialogic Inc., but adopted Veraz's headquarters in San Jose as its home base. Shareholders of Dialogic own about 70% of the merged company, while former Veraz stockholders own the rest. Dialogic made the acquisition to increase its focus on the US market, expand its client list, and transition from private to public status.

Dialogic previously purchased certain assets from NMS Communications -- including boards, servers, and gateways designed to help mobile service providers offer advanced services -- for $28 million in cash in 2008. The company bought VoIP gateway maker EAS Group the previous year.

The company was founded in 1984 as Eicon Networks. It adopted the Dialogic name in 2006 after acquiring Intel 's media and signaling business, which was formerly known as Dialogic .
