Pride Mobility Products takes satisfaction in helping people get up, get down, and get all around. The company makes motorized power chairs, scooters, and lifts for the elderly and disabled. Its power chairs, scooters, lifts, and rehab solutions are sold under brands including Jazzy, Pride, and Quantam Rehab. Offerings include manual wheelchairs, Pride chair lifts that help users get up from a sitting position, and vehicle lifts and ramps used to transfer a chair or scooter into the car. The company sells through distributors around the world. Pride Mobility is owned by the Meuser family, led by chairman and CEO Scott Meuser.

The company's Quantum Rehab division designs and manufactures high-end complex rehabilitation solutions such as power chairs and power positioning seating systems incorporating technologically innovative, intelligent features. Its Q6 Power Chair Series offers users optimal seating and positioning functionality, power options, and advanced electronics, including the exclusive TRU-Balance Power Positioning Systems, the innovative Q-Logic Drive Control System, and Synergy Cushions and Backs.

Pride Mobility has operations in Australia, Canada, Italy, the Netherlands, New Zealand, the UK, and the US. Its US locations include regional distribution centers in Florida, Nevada, Pennsylvania, and Texas, and a manufacturing center in Pennsylvania.

To increase distribution of its products, Pride Mobility forms sales and marketing partnerships with other companies. It also is expanding its capacity for production, delivery, and support services within the Quantum Rehab division.

In 2015, the company introduced the luxury Oasis lift chair, part of the Infinity Collection Lift Chair line. It also launched the new iLevel elevation technology for its Q6 Edge 2.0 power chair.

Like others in the power wheelchair industry, Pride Mobility is dealing with government regulations aimed at reducing Medicare costs, such as reduced reimbursement rates and stricter coverage standards for mobility products. To help its dealers cope with the changes (particularly the Medicare reimbursement cuts), Pride Mobility offers a number of services to its buyers, including reimbursement consultation and alternative payment plans. It has even inked deals with companies like Creek Ridge Capital, a vendor leasing company, to offer private-label leasing programs to its dealers. In 2014 it partnered with Navitas Lease Corp. to offer financing.

Pride Mobility acquired Texas-based Stealth Products in 2014. Stealth Products provides rehabilitation technology components and accessories such as head and body supports, chest straps, and joystick mounts.

Pride Mobility was founded in 1986.

To expand into pediatric mobility products, in 2011 the Quantum Rehab division acquired Kids UP, a maker of seating and mobility products for children.
