Like an old hippie but a lot more active, P&H Mining Equipment can dig it. The surface mining machinery unit of Joy Global manufactures and services excavating and drilling machines. Its principal product lines include electric mining shovels, rotary blasthole drills, and walking draglines. P&H also offers field welding, machine overhaul, equipment maintenance, and turnkey equipment erection/relocation services, along with more than 250,000 P&H parts. Its factory in Milwaukee provides contract manufacturing services, as well. The company works in tandem with sister company Joy Mining and has equipment in use at some 90% of the world's surface mines.

P&H has operations in Australia, Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, South Africa, and the US in addition to sales offices in India, Mexico, Peru, Russia, the UK, and Venezuela.

P&H makes up its parent company's Surface Mining Equipment segment, which was able to increase its net sales by 29% from 2010 to 2011. It was helped by an increase in aftermarket and original equipment sales and by LeTourneau, a previous acquisition. Aftermarket sales were up in all regions led by higher sales in North and South America. The segment's net income climbed by 26% as a result of the higher sales volumes due to favorable manufacturing overhead absorption and cancellation charges.

P&H welcomed LeTourneau Technologies, after Joy Global paid more than $1 billion for the earthmoving equipment manufacturer from Rowan Companies in mid-2011. LeTourneau, which operated two business segments -- mining and drilling products -- made wheel loaders used in the mining and oil and gas drilling industries. It also designed offshore jack-up drilling rigs and manufactured the primary components for the rigs, as well. The acquisition added to P&H's existing portfolio and moved it into a new area of resource extraction.

The company was founded by Alonzo Pawling and Henry Harnischfeger in 1884 as Pawling & Harnischfeger. In 1911 Pawling sold his stake in the company to his partner and Pawling & Harnischfeger became Harnischfeger Corporation. Joy Mining Machinery joined P&H Mining Equipment in 1995 under a new holding company, Harnischfeger Industries, Inc., which filed for Chapter 11 in 1999. When Harnischfeger emerged from bankruptcy protection in 2001, the company changed its name to Joy Global Inc.
