Tri-Arrows Aluminum is targeting the North American and Latin American aluminum markets. Formerly known as ARCO Aluminum, the aluminum sheet maker is now controlled by Japan's UACJ Corporation. Through Logan Aluminum , its 45%-owned joint venture with Novelis , Tri-Arrows manufactures aluminum sheet used mainly by the beverage and automotive industries. Logan Aluminum's offerings include automotive sheet, building products, distributor sheet, food and beverage can stock, and rigid container sheet. The company is a part of rolled aluminum products, which accounted for 78% of the parent company's total revenues in 2014.

In 2015, the company, in conjunction with Logan Aluminum , broke ground for a multi-year $240 million Tri-Arrows funded expansion with an additional $50 million investment under consideration. The expansions will create 190 new employment opportunities at Logan Aluminum. The investments include a new aluminum recycling/ingot casting facility along with numerous projects to provide expanded capabilities and increased capacity at the manufacturing facility. The installation of new production equipment, improvements to existing production centers and the casting expansion will increase the total area by 348,000 sq. feet.

Oil giant BP , which acquired ARCO Aluminum as part of its purchase of Atlantic Richfield Company in 2000, sold the company in 2011 for $680 million to a Japanese consortium which became UACJ in 2013.

The consortium consisted of Sumitomo Light Metal Industries (40%), Furukawa Sky Aluminum (35%), Sumitomo Corporation (20%), Itochu Corporation (2%), and Itochu Metals Corporation (3%).
