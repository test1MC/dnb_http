BBI Diagnostics Group (formerly BBI Holdings) puts gold to work diagnosing patients in doctors' offices and clinics. The firm produces gold reagents (colloids and conjugates) which it uses to develop and manufacture non-invasive diagnostic assays and tests for health care and research professionals. Its BBI International and BBI Enzymes divisions also provide contract manufacturing services, as well as raw antibodies (proteins), enzymes, reagents, and other research supplies, while BBI Healthcare makes point of care tests (such as glucose tests) for consumers. BBI Detection develops and sells tests for environmental and biodefense applications. Parent Alere is selling BBI to Exponent Private Equity for $164 million.

The company operates in the UK.

Consumer Diagnostics provides 4% of total revenues to its parent company. Net product sales and services revenue from the segment increased 6% to $108.6 million in 2014.

In 2015, BBI Solutions launched a new recombinant NGAL protein, a marker for the early diagnosis of acute kidney injury. It also launched a next-generation FAD-dependent Glucose Dehydrogenase for the monitoring of blood glucose.

After enjoying a licensing agreement together, Alere (then known as Inverness Medical Innovations) acquired BBI Holdings in 2008. It then merged BBI's consumer health business with Cedar Health, a division of its BioStat subsidiary; the combined operations became known as BBI Healthcare.

Prior to being acquired, BBI itself expanded through a steady process of acquisitions. It was first formed by Cardiff University researcher John Chandler in 1986 as Biocell Research Laboratories, later becoming known as British Biocell International.
