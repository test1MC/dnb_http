Skyjack takes business to great heights. The company makes aerial work platforms - its scissor lifts allow workers to perform their duties at heights up to 56 feet, and they are drivable at full height. For more challenging work environments, the company offers rough-terrain models with four-wheel drive. Its boom lifts provide work heights ranging from 45 to 71 feet. Skyjack also sells new, used, and reconditioned booms, scissor lifts, and similar industrial equipment. It additionally provides reconditioning services for other companies. Machined components (primarily automotive) maker Linamar acquired the company in 2002; Skyjack is one of Linamar's industrial segments.

The company has production facilities located in Canada and the US, and also in Hungary; it has support offices throughout Europe and Asia.

Skyjack continues to innovate on the product side by continuing to develop and launch new boom products and telehandlers as it achieved growth in the European and Asia markets in 2014. By also investing in new machines, Skyjack's customer base has expanded. The rental and infrastructure industries, which have always been solid in central Europe, are key to Skyjack's overall global development.
