Printpack serves food, non-food, and medicinal markets with its array of packaging products. Its flexible packaging is found on salty snacks, confections, baked goods, and cereal, as well as tissues and paper towels. Other packaging includes plastic film, aluminum foil, metalized films, and paper with specialized coatings, as well as cast and blown monolayer and co-extruded films. Its sustainable lineup is marketed under the Natura brand. Blue-chip clients have included Frito-Lay , Georgia-Pacific , General Mills , and Quaker Oats. The company traces its historical roots back to 1956.

Printpack caters to both consumer goods and industrial manufacturers demanding attractive, functional, less expensive packaging. It offers thermoformed structures to stand-up pouches, extruded films to nine-color rotogravure printing, and myriad wraps. The diversity of Printpack products and end uses mitigates the economic ups and downs associated with serving a limited market. The company also defines itself as an eco-leader in its industry.

Printpack operates through more than 20 manufacturing facilities in China, India, Mexico, and the US.

The company serves over 60 markets, including the food, medical, and non-food sectors.

In early 2016, Printpack reported annual sales of about $1.2 billion.

Like most companies in its field, Printpack grows by augmenting its manufacturing capacity. In early 2016, it announced it is spending more than $25 million to expand its rigid packaging operations in Newport News, Virginia. Along with the expansion, Printpack stated it is undertaking a recapitalization program at other facilities to expand its manufacturing capabilities by adding more advanced technology and equipment that will improve its graphics.

Printpack was founded in 1956 by J. Erskine Love, Jr. Love began with one employee (himself) and a used cellophane bag machine, which operated out of a rented basement space.
