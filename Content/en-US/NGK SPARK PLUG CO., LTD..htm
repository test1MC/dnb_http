Workers at NGK SPARK PLUG make a lot of ceramic products. More than half of the company's sales come from automotive parts such as spark plugs (ceramic and metal plugs used in automobiles, motorcycles, and aircraft engines) and automotive oxygen sensors. Most of the rest comes from its Communications Media unit (semiconductor and electronic components) and Technical Ceramics segment (medical products, such as artificial bone and oxygen concentrators, as well as industrial components and cutting tools). NGK SPARK PLUG gets more than half of its sales outside Japan. NGK, which was established in 1936 as a spinoff from NGK Insulators, is developing a conductive-ceramics-based micro-fuel cell the size of a sugar cube.
