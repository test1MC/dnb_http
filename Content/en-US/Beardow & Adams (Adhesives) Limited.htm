Beardow & Adams is constantly seeking out and creating sticky situations for itself. The company, known as Beardow Adams, is Europe's largest hot melt adhesives manufacturer, whose products are used in such activities as bookbinding, woodworking, adhering labels to cans, bottles, and refrigerators, and sealing up the tops of cereal boxes. Hot melts are also used in the assembly of products ranging from consumer appliances to automobiles. Beardow Adams' products are distributed worldwide. Outside the UK, the company sells its products through a distributor network.

Beardow Adams, one of the few manufacturers in the world that exclusively makes hot melt adhesives, is best known for its BAMFutura products (think hot glue guns on an industrial level). The company touts the packaging benefits of its BAMFuture products, which it says are almost odorless and provide high-quality bond strength and a high resistance to oxidation.

Holding company Beardow Adams Group of Companies has six manufacturing units and 14 production lines. It companies include Beardow & Adams (Adhesives) Limited (UK); Paniker SL, Spain; Svenksa Lim Landskrona, (Sweden); BeardowAdams, Inc., US; and Collodin, Germany. The company operates in Asia Pacific, Europe, the Middle East and Africa, and the Americas.

The company sells its products and services to more than 10,000 manufacturers through a sales team and a distributor network.

Industries served by Beardow Adams include automotive, paper derivatives, product assembly, packaging, woodworking, and footwear.

The company's strategy for growth has been to expand internationally and by involving its distributors by providing training and support. It also continues to develop new technology for its specialist hot melt operations. The global hot melt adhesives market is expect to grow from $6 billion in 2014 to $8.6 billion by 2021.

In 2014 Beardow Adams acquired Klebstoffwerke Collodin GmbH (which makes and develops environmentally friendly solvent-free adhesives) from its previous owner, Paul Joseph Nauth.

In 2011 the company launched a new generation of BAMFutura hot melt adhesives for bottle and jar labeling. The new film-wrapped EcoBlocks are loaded into a hot melt tank in which the outer film melts with the adhesive. The clear adhesives can be bonded to paper and plastic labels or plastic or glass containers. Most of the new applications are used on soft drinks and bottled water.

Beardow Adams was established in 1977 by chairman and managing director Bob Adams and his partner, Len Beardow.
