Harvard International isn't a study abroad program for bright students but rather a distributor of consumer electronics. Its Goodman brand offers set-top boxes (STBs) for standard and high definition TV and satellite broadcasts, digital video recorders, and video-on-demand devices. Its iLuv line includes accessories for Apple's iPod, iPad, and iPhone and Samsung mobile devices. Goodman and iLuv electronics are sold in the UK while Australian subsidiary Bush Australia sells Grundig, Joh Bailey, Bush, and Pevo brand electronics. Founded in 1963 as a confectionary wholesaler, the Harris family (including CEO Daniel, son of the founder John Harris) controls the largest portion of Harvard International's stock.
