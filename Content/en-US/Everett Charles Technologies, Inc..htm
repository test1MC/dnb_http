Everett Charles Technologies (ECT) makes printed circuit board and semiconductor test equipment with related components, such as contacts, probe card blanks, and spring probes. The company also offers circuit board test software and pre-owned equipment. ECT operates three business groups: Contact Product, Fixture Services, and Capital Equipment. The company has developed board test products since 1965. ECT is a subsidiary of Xcerra ), a maker of automated test equipment used to test semiconductors during the manufacturing process, after a 2013 acquisition.

ECT operates manufacturing facilities in the US, Japan, Taiwan, and in certain European countries.

ECT's subsidiaries include Multitest, a semiconductor test handling equipment designer, and atg Luther & Maelzer, a supplier of electrical testing equipment for the printed circuit board industry. Equipment and systems produced are branded as Multitest, ECT Interface Products, and Harbor Electronics.

Strategy

ECT maintains a product flow to meet current market demands. In 2015, it introduced the HC500F high current probe designed for industrial test applications including transportation, automotive, power grid, military, and PCB test. Those are fast-growing areas where performance is critical.
