Pacific Coast Feather Co. aims to give its customers the lowdown on down. The company makes and markets basic bedding, including pillows, comforters, duvet covers, sheets, and feather beds. Pacific Coast Feather Co. sells its products under a variety of brand names, such as Pacific Coast Feather, Down Around, BestFit!, EuroRest, and Dockers . It also sells items under the Spring Air , Sleep Sense, and Chattam & Wells labels. Primarily, products are sold by major department store chains nationwide. Pacific Coast Feather Co. also peddles its products online through the likes of Amazon.com , Costco.com , and jcpenney.com . The company has been owned by the Hanauer family since 1924.

Based in Seattle, Pacific Coast sells its pillows, comforters, mattress pads, feather beds, and other items nationwide.

Pacific Coast is the #1 brand of down and feather bedding in the US. Its products are found in high-end retailers and luxury hotels.

The company is one of the top suppliers of premium down pillows to leading US hotels.

With the downfall of Pillowtex (2003), Pacific Coast Feather became the largest pillow maker in the US. The company also holds rank as the nation's biggest down comforter manufacturer.

To extend its reach, Pacific Coast partnered with mattress giant Spring Air International in late 2013. As part of the alliance, Pacific Coast designs, makes, and markets a collection of top-of-bed accessories for consumers that are marketed under the Spring Air, Sleep Sense, and Chattam & Wells names.

Pacific Coast in 2014 bought an industrial facility in Gastonia, North Carolina, for $2.2 million in cash. The property will serve as the company's new mattress pad production operation to replace its Wayne, Nebraska, plant, which was destroyed by a tornado.
