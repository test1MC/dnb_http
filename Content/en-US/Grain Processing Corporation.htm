Grain Processing Corporation is pretty corny. The company makes corn-based products for the food and beverage, personal care, pharmaceutical, and industrial manufacturing sectors. Its offerings include corn-syrup solids, corn oil, maltodextrin and other starches used for sweetening, and ethyl alcohol. The company's products are used in the manufacture of food, pharmaceuticals, personal-care products, beverages, paper, corrugated boxes, textiles, and wallboard, as well as for animal food and pet-care products. Its products are sold worldwide. Founded in 1943, Grain Processing is a subsidiary of Kent Corporation (formerly Muscatine Foods), which also also owns several animal-feed manufacturing companies.

Headquartered in Iowa, Grain Processing has production sites in Iowa and Indiana, an office in Switzerland, and an office and laboratory in China and Mexico.

The company offers co-development and manufacturing services including chemical, wet and dry, and solvent processing, as well as facilities for spray drying, fermentation, extrusion, filtration, and analytical instrumentation.

Grain Processing sells its products through sales agents and distributors around the world. It serves pharmaceutical/nutraceutical, personal care, alcohol, food, and other industrial markets.

Grain Processing was formed in 1943 to build and operate a government industrial-alcohol plant as part of the WWII synthetic-rubber program.
