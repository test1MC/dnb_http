Mechdyne provides computer hardware and software that makes virtual worlds visible. The company operates through two subsidiaries, Fakespace Systems and VRCO. Fakespace creates three-dimensional display systems used by scientists, manufacturers, government agencies, researchers, and businesses involved in the energy and entertainment sectors; applications range from museum exhibits to military prototyping. VRCO develops software that transforms data into three-dimensional visual representations. Mechdyne has offices in Canada, the UK, and the US. The company was established in 1996 by Christopher Clover, Mechdyne's president and CEO. Electrohome Ltd. sold its 26% stake in Mechdyne back to the company in 2008.
