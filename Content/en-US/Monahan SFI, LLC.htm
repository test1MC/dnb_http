After a dramatic resurrection, Monahan SFI (formerly Specialty Filaments) still wants to give dirt and bacteria the brush-off. The company, operating as Monahan Filaments, makes extruded filaments, molded products, abrasives, and resins that are used in brushes and brooms. Its products are used by automotive, cosmetics, and consumer products manufacturers to make tooth- and paintbrushes, carwash components, brooms, vacuum cleaners, and floor polishers. After filing for Chapter 7 bankruptcy protection and planning to liquidate its business, broom and mop maker The Thomas Monahan Co. bought the company in 2007 and restarted operations. The company changed its name to Monahan SFI the following year.
