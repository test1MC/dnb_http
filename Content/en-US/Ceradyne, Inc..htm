A bull in a china shop wouldn't stand a chance against Ceradyne's ceramics. The company's advanced technical ceramics products combine hardness with light weight and the ability to withstand high temperatures, resist corrosion, and insulate against electricity. Some uses of Ceradyne's materials include armor for military helicopters, missile nose cones, body armor and helmets for soldiers, diesel engine components, ceramic industrial products, solar glass products, and orthodontic brackets. The 3M Company subsidiary sells to contractors and OEMs, and the US government and government agencies represent more than a third of its annual sales.

In late 2012 3M Company acquired Ceradyne in a deal worth $860 million to add Ceradyne's advanced ceramics technologies portfolio into its own diversified lineup of products, which includes everything from films and Post-in notes to sponges and dental products.

The company sells its products in North America, India, Germany, and China.

Ceradyne's operating divisions include Advanced Ceramic Operations, Ceradyne Armor Systems, Inc., Ceradyne Boron Products LLC, Ceradyne Canada ULC, Ceradyne Ceramics India PVT.LTD., Ceradyne Diaphorm, Ceradyne VIOX, ESK Ceramics, Max Pro-Police & Armor, Minco, Inc., Thermo Materials, Tianjin Technical Ceramics, SemEquip, Inc. and Semicon Associates.

ESK is a provider of groundbreaking ceramic materials. Minco makes and markets fused silica powders for a wide range of industrial applications. Ceradyne Thermo Materials produces fused silica ceramic products. SemEquip, Inc. is the technology leader in the development of cluster ion implantation sub-systems and advanced ion source materials for the manufacture of logic and memory chips.

Ceradyne's nuclear products offer a full range of thermal neutron absorbing materials including BORAL Composite, BORTEC MMC, BorAluminum, BoroBond, Enriched Boron, and Boron Chemistries for criticality control in wet and dry used and fresh fuel environments.

The company offers its services to nuclear power, oil and gas, solar energy, automotive, defense, and other sophisticated technology industries.

Ceradyne operates as part of the 3M Energy and Advanced Materials Division within 3M's Industrial and Transportation operating segment. To expand its business, the unit is also looking to diversify outside the defense sector.

In 2011 Ceradyne expanded by purchasing Washington-based VIOX Corporation, a specialty glass company that serves the electronic, industrial, and healthcare markets. Among VIOX's products is specialty glass for polycrystalline silicon photovoltaic technology. Moreover, the acquisition was a natural plug-in; many end users of VIOX products are also Ceradyne customers.

VIOX is only one of several acquisitions Ceradyne has made to increase its business in the non-defense sector. Other important acquisitions to boost non-defense sales have included ESK Ceramics and Minco. Additionally, Ceradyne has increased its non-defense revenue base by expanding its operations in Canada and China.

The company was founded in 1967.
