ACH Food Companies is ACH-ing to help the cook. The company manufactures and sells a variety of cooking oils and other food ingredients, such as cornstarch, syrup, and sauces. Its lineup is led by well-known brands, including Mazola (the #1 corn oil brand in North America), Argo corn starch, Capullo canola oil, Fleischmann's yeast, and Karo corn syrup. The company also offers private-label and custom manufacture services. Operating through two divisions, consumer products and commercial products, it caters to the retail, and industrial food and food service industries in the US, Canada, and Mexico. ACH is a subsidiary of UK food giant Associated British Foods .

The company's oils are processed and packaged in Summit, Illinois, and in Mexico City, Mexico. ACH also has test kitchens and product development teams in Illinois, and Memphis, Tennessee, as well as marketing arms in Canada, and Puerto Rico.

ACH Food Companies boasts a brand-rich product portfolio that focuses on brands that are #1 or #2 in their categories. The cornerstone of the portfolio is cooking oils, including North American market leader Mazola and Capullo, the leading premium brand of canola oil in Mexico.

In late 2016 the company sold its spices and seasoning business to B&G Foods for $365 million in cash. Brands sold include Spice Islands, Tone's, Durkee, and Weber Seasonings. Prior to the sale, ACH Food's spices and seasonings had ranked second in North America.
