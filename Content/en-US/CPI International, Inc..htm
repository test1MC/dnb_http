CPI International makes broadcast and wireless components for the defense, communications, medical, and scientific markets, among others. Its some 4,500 products include satellite communications amplifiers, radar subsystems, X-ray equipment, modulators and transmitters, and integrated microwave assemblies. Its radio-frequency (RF) and microwave components are used inÂ military hardware, including Aegis-class cruisers and destroyers, Patriot missile systems, and fighter aircraft. In addition to sales of components, the company provides replacement and spare parts, repairs, and upgrades to its installed equipment. CPI serves clients worldwide. In 2011 investment firm Veritas Capital took the company private.

Veritas has extensive expertise investing in the technology and defense markets. The $525 million deal gives CPI the capital needed to develop new products and to continue to grow in its target defense, communications, medical, and scientific markets.

CPI operates through divisions focused on such product groups as power products, power grid devices, communications and medical products, and microwave products. Its Malibu Division makes antenna systems, with the Econco and Satcom divisions providing rebuilt power tubes and amplifier products and systems for satellite communications, respectively.

CPI serves clients in about 90 countries from sales and service offices across Asia, Australia, Europe, and North America.

Sales to the US government, including the Department of Defense , historically make up around a third of CPI's sales. Its products also go into civilian communications equipment, industrial gear, medical systems, and scientific instruments.

The company paid nearly $10 million in 2012 to acquire Codan Limited's Satcom business, a supplier of solid-state RF subsystems for satellite communications services to commercial and government clients. Codan Satcom has operations in Australia and a microwave business in Pennsylvania.

CPI was once part of the Electron Devices group at the old Varian Associates; it was spun off from Varian in 1995. Varian engineers invented the klystron in 1937, which played an important role in the development of radar during WWII and is still used in high-power microwave equipment. CPI International was formerly known as Communications & Power Industries, which remains the name of the company's main operating subsidiary.
