Cats relaxed just a bit when Franz Thomastik and Otto Infeld invented steel violin strings. Thomastik-Infeld, the company they founded in 1919, still manufacturers steel and nylon strings for bowed and plucked instruments including aoud, cello, guitar, mandolin, and violin. In all the company produces more than 3,000 types of strings annually. The company also manufactures tailpieces and rosin for members of the violin family. The company sells its products worldwide through various retail outlets; John M. Connolly & Co . is the exclusive US importer of Thomastik-Infeld strings.
