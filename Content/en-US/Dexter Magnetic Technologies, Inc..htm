Combining magnetism and technological dexterity, Dexter Magnetic Technologies manufactures magnetic products for a wide range of applications, including aerospace/defense, automotive, consumer, data storage, electronics, industrial controls, medical, petrochemical, research, security, semiconductor, and telecommunications. Its Magnetic Products segment offers permanent magnet materials, electromagnets, cores, bobbins, and thermistors, as well as engineering, fabrication, and assembly support. The company's Electronic Components segment is a global distributor of power electronics and electro-magnetic components for leading manufacturers. The company was founded in 1951.

Dexter Magnetic has specialized fabrication/assembly facilities, each with the capability to design, machine, magnetize, and test products. Permanent magnets are made from varying materials including alnico, ceramic, neodymium iron boron (Nd-Fe-B), and samarium cobalt. Dexter's branded NeoForm combines Nd-Fe-B and epoxy to create a compound that can be formed into shapes. Magnetic cores include ferrite, powder, and amorphous.

The Electronic Components business unit is a global distributor of magnetic products manufacturered by companies which have included EPCOS , Fair-Rite, Ferronics, Magnetics Inc., Miles-Platts, RTI , GE Industrial Sensing , TDK (Europe only), and Toshiba , to name a few.

Dexter Magnetic Technologies was founded as Permag Corp. in 1951. It was sold to the Dexter Corporation in 1985 and renamed as Dexter Magnetic Materials Division. The company was sold to Loctite in 2000 and spun off as Dexter Magnetic Technologies in 2001.
