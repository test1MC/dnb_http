Fanatics (formerly known as Dream) caters to sports fanatics. The company owns and operates or franchises about 300 online and offline US stores under the Field of Dreams and FansEdge banners. This network of stores sells athlete-signed balls, jerseys, photos, plaques, and other collectibles, which it licenses from the NFL , MLB , NHL , NBA , NCAA , and NASCAR . Dreams also reaches shoppers through its FansEdge catalog and online at both FansEdge.com and ProSportsMemorabilia.com. The company's Mounted Memories unit makes and distributes sports memorabilia. Its Greene Organization handles special appearances, endorsements, and other off-field activities for athletes.

Dreams became a wholly-owned subsidiary of fellow Florida-based sports licensing company Fanatics in 2012. As part of the deal, valued at $183 million, Fanatics acquired all the outstanding shares of Dreams for $3.45 per share. The aggregate transaction value took into account $25 million in outstanding debt.

Based in Florida, Dreams operates retail stores in the Midwest, as well as online.

Dreams is a vertically integrated seller of sports-licensed products. It operates under several major brands, including the flagship FansEdge name. The company's operations include manufacturing, online retailing, e-commerce services, catalogs, and brick-and-mortar stores. Among its more than 200,000 products are items licensed from the NFL, NHL, NBA, MLB, NCAA, and others.

Through multiple channels, Dreams distributes, retails, and sells sports licensed products, memorabilia, and acrylic display cases. Besides its business of selling sports products through brick-and-mortar and online stores, Dreams provides athlete representation and corporate marketing of individual athletes.

Its primary markets are Chicago and Las Vegas.

With Dreams now under the Fanatics umbrella, the pair is working to invest in a broader variety of products and technologies related to mobile and e-commerce. Together, they also enjoy a regional fulfillment infrastructure. Its sales growth has been driven by e-commerce-related activities, which account for 80% of its sales.

Dreams has transformed itself into a vertically integrated sports products company. It has focused on building its brand through acquisitions of large and small competitors, boosting sales through its stores, expanding its network of franchised locations, and driving traffic to its websites. In addition to its own e-commerce channels, Dreams markets products through syndicated partner sites, including those for retailers ( JCPenney , Wal-Mart ), professional and college sports teams ( Philadelphia Eagles , University of Miami ), content providers (NBC Sports, Football.com), newspapers (Baltimore Sun, Houston Chronicle), and athletes (Dan Marino, Dick Butkus).

The company kept itself busy pursuing a pair of acquisitions in 2010. Dreams acquired Kansas-based Collegiate Marketing Services (CMS), whose main asset was a contract to manage the retail merchandise operations for The University of Texas ' athletics program. The deal included the Longhorns' on-campus operations at Darrell K Royal-Texas Memorial Stadium and the official online store LonghornsLTD.com. Also in 2010 the company acquired 51% ownership of Comet Clothing, which owns the rights to the Zubaz brand of casual sportswear.
