Otter Products' products keep your precious electronic devices safe and dry. The company, which goes by OtterBox, makes more than 250 models of protective cases for cell phones, smart phones, tablet computers, and other portable electronics from Apple , LG Corp , BlackBerry , Samsung , and other manufacturers. It outsources production and sells its impact and water resistant Defender, Reflex, Commuter, and Impact cases and watertight boxes at Best Buy , Target , and other retailers. OtterBox was formed in 1998 by CEO Curt Richardson and his wife Nancy, who came up with the name after being inspired by otters' water-repellent skin and playful and creative attitudes.

Otter Products operates two subsidiaries. OtterBox makes protectors that are more for everyday use for phones, tablets, and other mobile devices. LifeProof, which the company acquired in 2013, makes cases for more extreme uses such as outdoor sports. Its LIFEACTÍV line include FRĒ, NÜÜD, and FRĒ Power cases. It also makes apparel for men and women.

Headquartered in Fort Collins, Colorado, Otter has other offices in San Diego; Boston; Cork, Ireland; and Hong Kong. It has established distribution in 14 countries throughout the APAC region.

Otter's products are distributed through retailers including Verizon, Best Buy, AT&T, Walmart, Sprint, Target, T-Mobile, and Amazon.

The company distributes its products in other countries through distribution partners such as Ingram Micro (which distributes its products in China).

Otter maintains a steady pace of new products and product extensions. In one case, it returned to its roots by introducing a drybox, a watertight box that can protect a phone, a wallet, and other valuables for up to 30 minutes in 90 feet of water. The company has shifted most of its products for extreme environments such as ski slopes to its LifeProof line of products.

Otter, in trying to expand beyond cases, has added charging and audio products to its lineup. They include Bluetooth earbuds and USB charging cables and a car charger.

The company stepped up its marketing game, signing Peyton Manning, the retired Denver Broncos quarterback, as its spokesman. Previous spokesman, Ollie the Otter, was demoted to second-string spokesman.
