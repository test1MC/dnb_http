If olives ring your bell, tune into Bell-Carter Foods. As the largest table olive producer in the US, the California company manufactures and markets canned and jarred black and green olives and olive spreads. Bell-Carter's products, which are sold under the Lindsay and Bell's labels, also include capers, cocktail onions, and pimientos. Selling more than half of all California olives, the company peddles its products in grocery stores and other food retailers throughout the US, including Walgreen , Sam's Club , and Costco , as well as in selected Canadian stores. Bell-Carter, which generates about 90% of its revenue from the sale of olives, also serves foodservice customers and has import and export operations.

Based in the Golden State, Bell-Carter sells its California olives and other items to more than 50 countries. The company has its headquarters in Lafayette, California and its production facility in Corning, California.

The company, which boasts a 63% share of the black olive market in the US, enlists the help of California growers to keep its business thriving. Farmers cultivate two main olive varieties: Manzanillo and Sevillano. Lindsay Olives produces black and green ripe olives and imports Kalamata olives, Spanish olives, cocktail onions, capers, and pimientos from four continents.

The company also owns and operates Bell-Carter Packaging, which provides packaging-support solutions for companies of all sizes. It offers ongoing and short-term packaging and repackaging services on a contract basis. Bell-Carter boasts a vast network of distribution centers in all the nation's major markets.

Bell-Carter relies on grocers and other US food retailers, such as Walgreen, Sam's Club, and Costco, to get its products in the hands of consumers. The company also peddles its olives and related products in selected Canadian stores. Diversifying its customer base, Bell-Carter also serves foodservice customers and operates import and export units.

Its strategically placed distribution centers in major markets allows Bell-Carter to ship its products to customers within 48 hours of receiving the order. The company ships from single pallets to full railcars.

Bell-Carter is investing in its primary product, olives with a campaign to make over its image. While it wants to retain its image of tradition, tapping its long history of olive-making, it's pushing to become a snacks provider with hopes of having its products sit on the store shelf alongside chips, nuts, popcorn, and other savory items. One move the company has already made is to sell its olives in snack cans with pull-top lids.

Two new product lines under the Lindsay brand -- Lindsay Naturals and Lindsay Recloseables -- focus on specific and previously unaddressed consumer needs.

Bell-Carter is owned by the fourth generation of the families that founded the company.

The company was founded in 1912 by Arthur and Henry Bell and was operated by the third and fourth generations of Bell descendants until Ken Wienholz, chief executive, arrived in 2007 as COO.
