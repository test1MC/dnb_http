Business is shipshape at NASSCO Holdings, dba General Dynamics NASSCO. The General Dynamics subsidiary operates the only major construction shipyard on the US West Coast; it maintains and repairs all facets of large, ocean-bound military and commercial surface ships. NASSCO provides new ship construction, overhaul, and upgrade for the US Navy and, less so, commercial customers such as TOTE and Shell . Its work includes dry cargo carriers, ferries, oil tankers, and ships for US and NATO combat support. NASSCO serves as a key homeport for the Navy's Pacific Fleet. Formerly National Steel and Shipbuilding Company, it was founded in 1905 as a machine shop. NASSCO is part of General Dynamics' Marine Systems group.

To its advantage, NASSCO operates as the only full-service maintenance and repair shipyard on the West Coast.

Most of NASSCO's business depends upon the ebb and flow of major Navy construction contracts, entailing large, multi-ship programs that span several years.

NASSCO works to develop and maintain a reputation for delivering ships ahead of schedule.

The company's commercial business benefits from the Jones Act, which requires that ships that carry cargo between US ports be built in US shipyards, as well as increasing environmental regulations requiring emission controls on tankers. As ship construction gains steam, NASSCO may increase in headcount.
