Evonik Cyro's scope includes the manufacturing of all manner of acrylics. The company is a part of Evonik Methacrylates, the performance polymers unit of German specialty chemicals company Evonik Degussa , a unit of Evonik Industries AG . Evonik Cyro produces acrylic sheets and film and methyl methacrylates. Among the markets it serves and end uses of its own products are: signage, sporting goods, jet and helicopter windows, hockey rink protective walls, office equipment, medical devices, and flat panel displays. The company sells its acrylic products under the Acrylite and Plexiglas brands.

The company has plants in Jayhawk, Kansas; Wallingford, Connecticut; Fortier, Louisiana; Chester, Pennsylvania; Osceola, Arkansas; and Sanford, Maine, and a head office in Parsippany, New Jersey at which it manages its operations in North America.

The company operates as a part of its parent's North American business. It produces and supplies acrylic monomers, polymers, and sheets and offers molding compounds, multi-skin sheets, rods and tubes, bonding agents and cleaners, and wave profile sheets.

Evonik Cyro markets PMMA molding compounds and sheet materials in the Americas under the Acrylite trademark. Everywhere else in the world, the trademark is Plexiglas. It serve a range of markets and applications, including point-of-purchase displays, store fixtures, conservation glazing, greenhouses, aerospace/aviation, aquarium, architectural, automotive, skylight, hockey rink, lighting, museum, picture framing, sign, tanning bed, and trade show exhibits.

Revenues from North America increased by 15% in 2015 and accounted for about 18% of Evonik Industries AG 's total revenues that year.

An innovative company, in 2013 Evonik Cyro introduced Acrylic LED Whites, a new line of acrylic molding and extrusion compounds specifically designed for backlit LED lighting applications. Growing is capacity, that year it brought a polymer additives plant on stream in Jayhawk, Kansas, and raised the capacity of precipitated silica production at its Chester plant by 20,000 metric tons.

In 2012 the company launched ACRYLITE Hi-Gloss, a co-extruded acrylic sheet with two homogenous layers that prevents cavities. That year, Evonik Cyro teamed up with marine cover fabric manufacturer CY4EZ to develop ACRYLIT Heatstop Resist with UV filtering OP3, a customized specialty acrylic for boat windows that blocks 98% of UV light and limits heat penetration.

The company streamlined its North American operations in 2009, cutting back its manufacturing and laying off some 56 workers, mostly at its Sanford, Maine facility. Another 30 workers lost their jobs in 2010, as the cell cast production line was completely shut down. The move was prompted by falling demand in the auto, building, and retail display sectors. The plant continues to manufacture acrylic sheet and molding compounds.

Formerly known as Cyro Industries, the company was founded in 1976.
