CIRCOR International is overflowing with valves and other highly engineered products used in the energy, industrial, and aerospace markets. The global diversified company designs, manufactures, and markets more than 10,000 products, namely a wide variety of valves used in flow control applications by the oil and gas, petrochemical, power generation, and processing industries. It also makes landing gear, sensors, and controls for military and commercial aircraft. Products are sold directly and through about 1,000 distributors in some 100 countries around the world.

CIRCOR operates nearly 20 major manufacturing facilities in the US, Canada, Brazil, Morocco, India, China, and parts of western Europe. The US accounts for 45% of its total sales.

CIRCOR's business is divided into two segments segments: Energy; and Aerospace and Defense. Its largest segment, Energy, primarily serves large, international upstream and midstream oil and gas projects and the North American energy market, which use CIRCOR's ball valves and other products for drilling, production, separation, liquefaction, gathering, and transmission applications. Pipeline construction and services companies are also customers, buying CIRCOR's products for measuring the transfer of oil and gas in pipelines and for cleaning and maintaining pipeline integrity.

Meanwhile, products made by CIRCOR's Aerospace and Defense segment are geared at most of the major military and commercial aircraft manufacturers and aircraft engine manufacturers, such as Boeing , Airbus , Embraer , and Bombardier . It also markets its aerospace products to tier one suppliers, including Parker-Hannifin , Eaton , Goodrich , SAFRAN , and Triumph , some of which are also key competitors of CIRCOR.

CIRCOR targets the oil and gas, power generation, aerospace, defense, food and beverage, general industrial, metals and mining, and petrochemical sectors. Its energy and aerospace and defense products are sold through over 950 distributors to 7,000 customers in more than 100 countries around the world.

After experiencing several straight years of steady growth, CIRCOR saw its revenues decline 2% from $858 million in 2013 to $841 million in 2014. Its profits, however, jumped 7% from $47 million to peak at $50 million during that same time period due to a decrease in impairment charges.

Energy segment sales dropped in 2014 due to lower shipment volumes within large international projects. It also experienced declines from its control valves businesses and unfavorable foreign currencies. Aerospace and defense segment revenues decreased due to declines in California business related to its landing gear product line.

In terms of longer-term growth, CIRCOR looks to open new facilities in the emerging markets of Brazil, China, India, and parts of the Middle East. In 2014 its energy segment opened a new sales office in Rio De Janeiro, Brazil. Located at the heart of the Brazilian oil and gas market, the new office offers immediate support of its customers, including Petrobras , Brazil's largest oil and gas producer.
