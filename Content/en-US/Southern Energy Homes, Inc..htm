Southern Energy Homes has built a business focused on providing Southern comfort. A subsidiary of Clayton Homes (itself a subsidiary of Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway ), the company builds manufactured homes available in single or double sections. The company's homes range range from fewer than 1,000 sq. ft. to more than 2,500 sq. ft. The home builder operates its business under several brands names, including Southern Estates, SE Texas, Southern Homes, and Giles Industries . It sells its homes primarily through dealers in the Southern and South Central US. Southern Energy Homes has expanded through its nearly $50-million purchase of fellow manufactured home company Cavalier Homes in 2009.

Southern Energy Homes was acquired by Clayton (a subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway ) in 2006, the same year it bought Giles Industries. The $18.5-million deal included Giles Industries' SunRay RV affiliate, a maker of towable travel trailers.

Alabama-based Southern Energy Homes, as its name suggests, operates across the nation's Southern and South Central regions.

Southern Energy Homes designs and builds homes that are move-in-ready and serve most price segments. Besides building and selling manufactured homes, Southern Energy is focused on operating a trucking business, a finance business, and a small insurance business.

To support its business, the company boasts five manufacturing facilities.

Targeting homeowners and retailers, Southern Energy Homes markets its products through about 300 independent home display centers and dealers. It sells its homes in some 20 US states under four brand names.
