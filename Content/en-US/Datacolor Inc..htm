Datacolor takes its cue from hue. The company makes instruments and software that control color measuring, matching, and quality control for use in textile, paint, automotive, printing, photography, and home theater applications. Its Spyder brand targets the consumer market. Datacolor also offers a variety of services, including calibration of spectrophotometers, on-site consulting, product training, and education in color theory. The company sells directly to consumers online and through resellers. It also integrates its products with those of partners such as Lectra , Fongs , and Lawer. Established in 1970, Datacolor is a subsidiary of Datacolor AG (formerly Eichhof Holdings), a Swiss firm.

The company is based in Lawrenceville, New Jersey, and has operations in Europe, the Americas, and Asia.

Datacolor sells to textile and apparel, paint and coatings, automotive, plastics, microscopy, photography, videography, digital imaging, home theater, ink, printing, paper, and leather industries.

The company is part of Americas, which contributed 29% of the revenue of the parent company in 2015.

As digital samples as replace physical samples in the design to production process, Datacolor has developed software to ensure consistent measurements between instruments, evaluate color samples, and track approvals.

In 2016 the company launched of the Datacolor spectrophotometers. The line is designed for paint retailers and hardware stores, and contains color measurement technology.
