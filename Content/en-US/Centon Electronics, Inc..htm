Centon Electronics designs and manufactures memory products including memory modules, flash and embedded memories, solid state drives, and custom memory products. Its products are used in computer equipment, printers, and consumer devices such as cameras and smartphones. The company partners with the likes of Intel , AMD , and NVIDIA to develop products to coincide with new product launches and ensure compatibility. Centon was founded in 1978.

Centon markets a line of consumer accessories under the OTMâ„¢ Essentials brand. The line goes beyond USB drives to sell covers for mobile phones, cases for laptop computers and tablets, power banks, headphones, and mousepads.

The company is headquartered in Aliso Viejo, California. The company has manufacturing lines in the US that it bought from Hitachi in 2012. It also makes products through contract manufacturers.

Centon sells its products to consumers through retailers such as Amazon , Staples , Wal-Mart , Frys , and Sparco as well as through distributors to OEMs and system builders.

The company is wooing consumers' business through a diverse product mix and marketing support efforts. Centon makes customized promotional flash drives with company, college, and sports team logos. It has Major League Baseball - and National Football League -licensed USB flash drives in three styles for every NFL team. Companies can create branded flash memory promotional products through the company's Design!t Flash unit. Centon has also released the entire Webster's New World College Dictionary on a flash drive, and added MP3/MP4 players to its product lineup.

Centon Electronics was formed as in 1978 as a distributor of electronics. In the 1990's, the company made the switch from distributor to memory manufacturer.
