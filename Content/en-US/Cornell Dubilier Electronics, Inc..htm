Cornell Dubilier Electronics (CDE) makes capacitors, including aluminum electrolytic, clad mica, film, pulse, and high-voltage capacitors. Capacitors are used in virtually all electronic devices to store, filter, and regulate electrical energy. CDE's products are used aerospace, lighting, motor, power conversion, telecom, and welding equipment. Arrow Electronics , Digi-Key , Future Electronics , and TTI are among the company's distributors. The company also offers contract manufacturing services. Cornell Dubilier was formed in 1933 from the combination of Dubilier Condenser Company and Cornell Radio. The company has manufacturing facilities in Mexico and the US, and a sales office in China.
