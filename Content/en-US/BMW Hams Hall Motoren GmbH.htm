BMW Hams Hall puts the vroom in your Beamer. The company produces four-cylinder gasoline engines, crankshafts, cylinder blocks, and cylinder heads that are later installed in the BMW 1 Series, 3 Series, and MINI models at plants in Austria, Germany, South Africa, and the US. Opened for production in 2000, BMW Hams Hall was BMW's first engine production facility to be built outside of Germany or Austria, and is now one of three in the UK that manufactures for BMW Group's BMW and MINI premium brands. The company employs more than 650 workers and has a capacity for 400,000 engines.
