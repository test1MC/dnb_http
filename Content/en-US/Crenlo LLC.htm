Farmers, construction workers, and air traffic controllers owe their safety and comfort to the cabins made by sheet metal fabricator, Crenlo. The company manufactures operator cabs and rollover structures for construction, agricultural, and commercial equipment. Its big vehicle OEMs are John Deere , Caterpillar , and Case New Holland . Cabs are custom designed, steel frame enclosures that attach to an OEM's machines. Crenlo's lineup includes modular enclosures built under its proprietary Emcor name. Emcor electronic equipment enclosures, racks, and consoles house equipment for commercial, computer, broadcast, security, and defense markets.

KPS Capital Partners bought Crenlo from diversified industrial giant Dover in 2011. KPS Capital, through the newly formed International Equipment Solutions (IES), paid Dover $230 million for Crenlo and Paladin Brands Holdings. Paladin is a manufacturer of attachment tools for construction, demolition, forestry, material handling, recycling, and utility vehicles.

Crenlo maintains its own fleet of trucks, trailers, and rail vans to distribute its products. Its business activities are primarily in the US.

The company divides its operations across two businesses. Crenlo Cab Products is a manufacturer of operator cabs and roll over protective structures (ROPS) in North America. The company operates manufacturing plants located in Rochester, Minnesota, comprising two plant sites which offer nearly 600,000 sq. ft. of manufacturing capacity.

Crenlo's enclosure business designs, manufactures and sells enclosures, consoles and custom parts to protect and store electronic equipment for technology-intensive environments.

IES, Crenlo's new parent company, has plans to expand sales globally utilizing the Crenlo and Paladin products especially in the Chinese, Indian and South American markets.
