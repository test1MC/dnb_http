On land, snow, or water BRP wants to put you in the driver's seat. The company designs, makes, and markets powersports vehicles and propulsion systems. Its portfolio of brands includes Ski-Doo and Lynx snowmobiles, Sea-Doo watercraft, and Can-Am all-terrain and side-by-side vehicles. BRP also makes Evinrude and Rotax marine propulsion systems as well as engines for motorcycles, karts, and recreational aircraft. The company has operations in Canada, the US, Austria, Finland, and Mexico. BRP is a spinoff of Canadian train and plane maker Bombardier Inc , which sold its recreational products business in 2003. The Canadian firm went public in 2013.

BRP went public in May 2013 selling 12.2 million shares priced at C$21.50 each. The proceeds from the offering were used to retire debt. (Prior to its IPO the company was owned by Boston-based Bain Capital Partners LLC , members of the Bombardier and Beaudoin families, and Caisse de Depot et Placement du Quebec , Canada's second-largest pension fund manager.)

In its first year as a public company BRP reported revenue of C$3.2 billion in fiscal 2014 (ended January), a 10% increase versus the prior year. Year-round products surpassed seasonal ones for the first time. Net income of C$59.7 million in 2014 was about half of what it was the prior year.

BRP's strategy for growth includes growing its year-round products business and expanding its dealer network. The company exited the sport boat business in 2013.
