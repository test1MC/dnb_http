Southwire is certainly able to provide cable. One of the world's largest cable and wire manufacturers, Southwire produces building wire and cable, utility and industrial power cable, cord products, telecommunications cable, and copper and aluminum rods. The company provides engineering, machining, and fabrication services, as well as electronic inventory management. It also offers copper products, such as bare copper and line wire, to the semiconductor market. Southwire customers include building contractors, utility companies, and OEMs in the areas of automotive, electrical, appliances, and industrial equipment.

Southwire divides its operations across seven distinct business segments: Energy, Electrical, Industrial, OEM, Tools and Assembled Products, Engineered Products, and International. Each focuses on a specific group of customers.

Based in Carrollton, Georgia, the company operates about 15 plants across nine states in the US. It also has customer service centers spread throughout Canada and the US.

Southwire targets the commercial, industrial, retail, mining, electrical wholesale outlet, and utility construction markets. Customers include utilities, rural electric cooperatives, municipalities, contractors, and utility distributors. Its products are sold through its online stores, sales offices, product catalogs, and as well through its mobile applications.

Over the past 60 years the company has positioned itself as a one-stop shop for customers. Southwire continues to focus on developing its core operations and product portfolio. Further, the company deepens its market presence by fostering a nationally-linked distribution network. Over the years strategic acquisitions have also fueled Southwire's growth and diversified its product portfolio.

Southwire grows through the use of acquisitions. In late 2016, it obtained Texas-based United Copper Industries, a manufacturer of building wire. The deal augmented Southwire's manufacturing capabilities in the company's core building wire products segment and enhanced its geographical footprint.

Around that same time period, Southwire also purchased Texas-based Sumner Manufacturing, a manufacturer of material lifts, jack stands, welding tools, material carts, and other tools and equipment for the construction market. That deal strengthened the company's position in the contractor equipment space and rapidly expanded its presence in the industrial supply and rental channels.

Southwire was founded in 1950 by Roy Richards Sr. In 2014 the company made a significant move to achieve growth through the purchase of Coleman Cable , a wire maker with similar products and facilities in the US and Canada. Southwire purchased Coleman for almost $785 million, including $295 million of debt.
