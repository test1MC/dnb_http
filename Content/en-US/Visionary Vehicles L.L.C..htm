Visionary Vehicles has modified its vision. It was originally formed to bring low-priced, Chinese-made cars to the US through a partnership with China's Chery Automobile ; the deal fell apart when Chery backed out. Malcolm Bricklin -- Visionary Vehicles' founder and CEO and the idea man who brought Americans the Subaru and the Yugo -- is looking to build the first new car company that offers a full line of plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEV), including an electric luxury sedan (EVLS). The Bricklin EVX/LS could hit showrooms by 2011. The plug-in hybrid would get nearly 100 miles to the gallon, seat five, and cost about $35,000.
