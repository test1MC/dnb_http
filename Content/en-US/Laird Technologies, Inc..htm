Laird Technologies feels that the shield is mightier than the sword. The company manufactures electromagnetic interference (EMI) shielding materials such as custom metal stampings, signal integrity components, thermal management products, wireless antennas, and radio frequency modules. It also produces metalized fabric and conductive tape and offers product engineering and testing services. The company invests heavily in research, development, and engineering. It markets its many products to a wide variety of companies and industries. Laird Technologies is an operating unit of Laird PLC .

Outside of the US, the company has manufacturing plants, sales, engineering, and R&D facilities in Brazil, Canada, China, the Czech Republic, Germany, Hong Kong, India, Japan, Korea, Malaysia, Mexico, Singapore, South Africa, Sweden, Taiwan, and the UK.

Laird Technologies has two divisions: Laird Performance Materials (LPM) includes EMI shields, signal integrity products, and thermal management products; and Laird Wireless Systems (LWS) which sells telematics, wireless M2M, infrastructure antennas, connectivity products, and wireless automation and control products.

Laird Technologies serves a variety of industries including automotive, IT, industrial and instrumentation, medical, mining, railroad, telecom, and transportation businesses.

The company uses partnerships to grow its footprint. Its key distribution partners have included Apple , Cisco , Ford , Nokia , LG , Samsung , and Sony Ericsson .

Laird Technologies also uses acquisitions to expand its product lines and geographic reach. In 2013 it acquired Nextreme Thermal Solutions, the US- based developer of thin-film thermoelectric technologies. In 2012 Laird Technologies acquired wireless module maker Summit Data Communications and EMI solutions provider Emerson & Cuming Microwave Products.
