A faulty oven thermometer may burn some cookies; faulty or inaccurate industrial temperature control can cost millions and endanger lives. Labfacility makes complex temperature and process control equipment far removed from "place this under your tongue" thermometers. Products include highly precise temperature sensors, transmitters, thermocouple connectors, cables, instrumentation, controllers, and calibration equipment. Labfacility primarily serves customers that require strict temperature control to carry out production or research. Industries served.include aerospace, automotive, and pharmaceutical. Clients include BAE Systems , GlaxoSmithKline , and Honeywell .

Labfacility has two manufacturing facilities in the UK and exports its products (through distributors) to more than 85 countries. More than half of the company's products are sold outside the UK.

Labfacility serves customers in industries such as aerospace, automotive and motorsport, education, environmental, energy, and food.

The company's major worldwide clients include BAE , Cromwell Tools, GKN Aerospace , GlaxoSmithKline , Goodrich Power Systems, H J Heinz , Honeywell Control , ICI, Invensys , Jaguar Land Rover , Siemens Plc, together with various Minstry of Defence, research and university establishments including Cambridge, Cranfield, Oxford, and Guys & St Thomas NHS Trust.

The company's products are featured in the worldwide catalogs of Premier Farnell/Element 14 and RS Components.

Focused on organic growth, Labfacility is expanding its product lines, investing in its facilities, and building out sales channels.

In 2015 the company launched a mineral insulated thermocouple with molded on Mini K IEC plug, an iCelsius Bluetooth temperature sensors for Apple and Android devices, and a low cost digital pen-type thermometer.

In 2014 Labfacility launched the L300 Pt100 temperature alarm on-off controller, which can be used with a PC to provide accurate monitoring and alarm or on-off control of up to 8- zones simultaneously.

The company was founded in 1971.
