LifeScan is sweet on diabetes patients. A subsidiary of health care giant Johnson & Johnson , the company is a leading supplier of blood glucose monitoring systems that help diabetics monitor and manage their disease. Many of the company's products -- including meters, test strips, lancing devices, and software sold under the OneTouch brand name -- allow users to test with tiny amounts of blood taken from the forearm or palm rather than using painful finger pricks. In addition to products for home use, LifeScan sells glucose monitoring systems (branded SureStep and OneTouch Flexx) that are used in hospitals and other institutional settings. It also makes insulin delivery pumps through its Animas unit.

LifeScan, part of J&J's Medical Devices segment, has manufacturing facilities in the US (California), Puerto Rico, and Scotland. The company, along with subsidiary Animas, markets products around the globe through a direct sales force and through distributors.

More than 10 million customers use LifeScan's OneTouch products to help manage their diabetes.

J&J's Medical Devices segment provided 37% of its parent's revenue in 2014. The diabetes care part of Medical Devices, of which LifeScan is a part, accounts for 8% of the segment's revenue.

LifeScan works to keep its edge in a competitive diabetes care market, as well as to attract and retain customers, by conducting extensive product development efforts. LifeScan has recently launched new Animas products, such as the Ping glucose management system that combines the OneTouch and Animas technologies to allow for remote monitoring and operation of the insulin pump. The company has also developed new, high-tech versions of its OneTouch meters, such as its OneTouch Ultra line featuring smaller monitors and faster results. In 2015 it introduced the OneTouch Verio Blood Glucose Monitoring System to help users easily understand their test results. In addition, LifeTouch has introduced products in new markets to expand its geographic presence.

LifeScan also partners with other providers to develop and market product variations. It has a cross-promotional agreement with ResMed , a manufacturer of sleep apnea diagnostic and treatment devices, to market each others' products to type 2 diabetes patients who also suffer from sleep disorders. LifeScan also partners with Apple to provide a system for diabetes management on Apple's iPhone that allows patients and caretakers to keep track of glucose levels and view historical data.
