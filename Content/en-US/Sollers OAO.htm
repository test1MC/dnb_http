Sollers is Latin for "clever, inventive, and skillful" and Russian for "Let's make a deal on a four-wheel drive, UAZ off-road vehicle, light truck, or minibus." Formerly named Severstal-Avto, Sollers is a leading automobile and engine manufacturer of four-and eight-cylinder petrol engines for off-road vehicles, class E vehicles, buses, light trucks, and special-purpose vehicles. The fast-growing company owns majority stakes in the Ulyanovsky Automobile Plant (UAZ) and Zavolzhsky Engine Factory (ZMZ). Sollers produces cars for Ssangyong Motor Company and Isuzu . The firm was founded in 2002 by steel manufacturer Severstal. Sollers general director Vadim Shvetsov owns 58% of the company.
