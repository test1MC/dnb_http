Cardiome Pharma has a heart for those with cardiovascular ailments. The company is in the business of developing new therapies to treat atrial arrhythmia and other heart conditions. Cardiome Pharma's lead drug candidate is vernakalant hydrochloride, which treats acute and chronic atrial arrhythmia (abnormal heart rhythm). Through a collaboration with Merck , intravenous vernakalant is marketed as Brinavess in the European Union, Latin America, and other countries and regions, and the companies are seeking US approval of the drug. Cardiome was also developing an oral version of the drug; however, after Merck backed out of that portion of the partnership in 2012, Cardiome halted the program and cut 85% of its staff.
