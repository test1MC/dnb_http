Known to audiophiles, Klipsch Group delivers the primo sound. Through its Klipsch LLC division, the company makes high-end audio products and sound systems for personal, professional, and commercial use. The company's consumer products include headphones, computer speakers, wireless audio devices, and home theater systems. Its professional cinema and commercial loudspeakers are used by businesses the likes of Regal Entertainment and Hard Rock Cafe . The company's brands include Klipsch, Energy, Jamo, and Mirage. Klipsch is a subsidiary of consumer electronics giant VOXX International .

Klipsch operates an advanced Engineering and Technology Center. The company's diverse product portfolio encompasses home theater, professional cinema, installed whole-house contracting, commercial, computer speakers, wireless speakers, and headphones. In addition to the Klipsch brand, the Klipsch portfolio includes Jamo, Mirage, Magnat, Heco, and Energy.

Klipsch is part of VOXX International's Premium Audio segment.

Based in Indianapolis, Indiana, Klipsch serves customers throughout the US, the UK, Canada, and Europe. The company's Klipsch Group Europe is headquartered in Paris.

Through distributors, Klipsch sells its products under OEM agreements with both domestic and international subsidiaries of auto makers including Volkswagen , Audi , BMW , Daimler , Peugeot , Ford Motor Company , General Motors , Toyota , Kia , Mazda , Subaru , Nissan , Porsche , and Bentley .

In fiscal 2015, VOXX International's Premium Audio revenue declined 12% to $165.8 million. (The segment accounted for 22% of the parent company's total revenues.) Premium Audio, including Klipsch, had lower earnings on products including soundbars and Bluetooth speakers due to lower sales prices; headphones sales were hurt by competition. Although the company saw growth in certain high-end items and custom installations, currency exchange rates offset those gains.

The company has expanded its portfolio of audio products in recent years to meet demand generated by the popularity of smartphones and other mobile devices. It offers headphones featuring oval-shaped earbuds, which are styled to fit more comfortably inside listeners' ears. Klipsch also makes on-ear headphones and sets with mic and remote systems (which can be used with mobile phones). Other products include the LightSpeaker, a multi-room wireless audio system equipped with a dimmable LED bulb that can be installed in recessed lighting fixtures.

In 2015 the company introduced an ultra-portable Bluetooth speaker; it also launched the powerful minimalist speaker R-15PM.

In 2011 consumer electronics powerhouse Audiovox (now VOXX International ) paid nearly $170 million to acquire Klipsch. Today the company operates as a subsidiary of VOXX, which was enticed to purchase Klipsch for its ongoing R&D efforts and diversified following in both the home and professional audio segments. Buying Klipsch gave Audiovox another world-class brand name, boosted its distribution network in the US and abroad, and provided entry into the high-end residential and commercial installation markets.

Klipsch was founded in 1946 by audio engineer Paul W. Klipsch (designer of the Klipschorn loudspeaker).
