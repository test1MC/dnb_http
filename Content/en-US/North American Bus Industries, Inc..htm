Before you can get on the bus, someone has to build it, and that's where North American Bus Industries comes in. The company designs, manufactures, and sells buses for public transportation and shuttle bus systems. Its NABI line makes bus bodies and assembles and the installs mechanical components at the company's Alabama plant. Buses range from 31 feet to 60 feet in length, and are produced using state-of-the-art manufacturing processes and equipment, including the bus transportation industry's first robotic paint system. NABI buses run on diesel fuel, natural gas, and hybrid-electric power. The company is part of New Flyer Industries.

In 2013 Canada-based bus giant New Flyer Industries acquired North American Bus Industries from an affiliate of Cerberus Capital Management, L.P . for about $80 million.

North American Bus Industries operates a bus manufacturing plant in Anniston, Alabama, a parts distribution facility in Delaware, Ohio, and a service center in Jurupa Valley, California.

To save costs, in 2013 North American Bus Industries shifted the manufacturing of the NABI brand from Hungary to the US.
