All for the Family would be the name of a TV series about Crown Media Holdings. It owns and operates the Hallmark Channel, a cable network that specializes in family-oriented programming. It features third-party programs including such TV series as Golden Girls , Little House on the Prairie , and Matlock , as well as made-for-TV movies, feature films, and miniseries, which includes original programming. The channel reaches more than 87 million US homes through cable providers such as Comcast and Cox . Crown Media also operates the Hallmark Movies & Mysteries channel, a 24-hour channel that primarily offers feature films, miniseries, and lighter mysteries; it reaches nearly 64 million homes. Hallmark Cards, which already owned most of the company, acquired the rest in early 2016.

Headquartered in Studio City, California, the company has administrative offices in Colorado and New York; it has advertising sales offices in Georgia and Illinois.

With it programming mix geared towards the over-50 crowd, Crown Media is able to deliver a very specific demographic of viewers to advertisers.

The company reported more than $478 million in revenue for fiscal 2015. That was an increase of 15% compared to its fiscal 2014 revenue. Crown Media's net income was about $86 million in fiscal 2015, which was a 9% decrease compared to its net income during the prior fiscal period.

Crown Media ended fiscal 2015 with $77.5 million in cash on hand from operations (an increase of 12% compared to fiscal 2014 cash levels).

Crown Media has developed a variety of original programming aimed at attracting viewers in its target demographic and giving it more control over costs.
