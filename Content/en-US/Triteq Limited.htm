Triteq helps its customers unit form and function in products for the medical, avionic, and consumer sectors. The company designs and manufactures electronic components and equipment primarily for customers in UK. It assists its clients across the development process: from the design phase to prototype building to project management, and finally, manufacturing. Products Triteq has designed include a medical infusion pump, LED laser scanner, a fiber-optic security strap, a solar power vehicle tracker, offender monitoring device, a radio-frequency identification (RFID) PET scanner, and a cervical cancer detection device.

Besides design work, Triteq offers manufacturing and services that include printed circuit board assembly, production engineering, testing and evaluation, and contract manufacturing.

The company works with partners, including fellow electronics manufacturing services firm Custom Interconnect, Isis Innovation (a technology transfer company owned by the University of Oxford ), Microchip Technology (provider of microcontrollers and analog chips), Bluegiga Technologies (Bluetooth modules and Bluetooth-based servers), MediWales, Diabetes UK, ecoConnect, and Cranfield University.

More than a third of Triteq's business comes from customer referrals. In the past, clients have included dispensing systems specialist BevStar (in which Triteq assisted in the planning and design of a compact dispenser) and security product supplier On Guard Plus Limited (Triteq helped with the design of a criminal justice tracking device). More recently Triteq helped Eykona develop a 3-D imaging system. Triteq's customers include the University of Warwick , Altran , NUI Maynooth, Rayner, INK Instruments, and Zilico.

Triteq expanded its medical applications business by working with Fourth State Medicine to develop a hand-held device for use in non-invasive cosmetic surgery and chronic wound care. Triteq designed the pen-like device to accommodate different power supplies for the different plasmas it uses.
