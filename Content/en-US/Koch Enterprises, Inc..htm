Koch gets straight A's for diversification; it's a private holding company active in automobile parts manufacturing, metals recycling, wholesale distribution, and equipment design and construction. Subsidiaries include Audubon Metals (processes aluminum), George Koch Sons (engineers, installs, and services auto finishing systems), Koch Air (distributes Carrier HVAC equipment), Gibbs Die Casting (parts for making cars lighter), Brake Supply (repairs brakes and hydraulic systems for auto and mining equipment), and Uniseal (makes structural adhesives, thermoplastics, and sealant systems for industrial and auto markets). George Koch founded the company in 1873.

Koch has seven subsidiaries operations in Brazil, China, Hungary, India, the UK, and the US.

The company operates in five industry markets: automotive parts manufacturing (37% of total sales), equipment design and construction (9%), metal recycling (28%), and wholesale distribution (26%). Its newest market is information technology systems.

Ever since bottoming out during the Great Recession, Koch has seen steady growth over the last four years. Its net revenue reached $943 million in 2012, one of the highest totals it has generated in the company's history.

Koch's business strategy centers around owning diverse businesses which are in different stages of maturity and perform in complementary business cycles. This strategy has enabled it to maintain a strong financial position and to continue growing over many years.

A large portion of Koch's revenues are from manufacturing automobile parts for such major auto OEM suppliers as Visteon and Delphi . Koch has continued to focus on developing greener, lighter, more cost-effective vehicle-related components. Its investment has helped to sustain its competitive position, and improve the profit margins of core lines.
