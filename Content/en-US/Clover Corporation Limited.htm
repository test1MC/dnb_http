Clover Corporation hopes to strike it rich in oil and keep its bean counters busy. They'll do it with tuna fish and soybeans. Through its subsidiary, Nu-Mega Ingredients, the Australian company manufactures omega-3 fatty acids derived from from tuna fish. It sells this oil-based ingredient, which is said to benefit the human cardiovascular system, to food and pharmaceutical manufacturers in Australia and other countries. The company's Future Food Ingredients 50-50 joint-venture with Austgrains, manufactures non-GMO Nu-Soya, a range of soy-based ingredients used for commercial baking, as well as in dairy,  meat-, and egg-replacement products. Investment firm Washington H. Soul Pattinson owns 28% of Clover.
