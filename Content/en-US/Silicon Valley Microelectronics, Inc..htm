Silicon Valley Microelectronics (SVM) distributes silicon wafers and provides related services to Silicon Valley -- and the rest of the world. SVM has the capability to efficiently deliver large orders to clients in the global semiconductor industry. Wafer services include a wide range of films, dicing, thinning, laser cut down, polishing, lithography, test patterns, and more. Its subsidiary Vista Solar specializes in the installation of photovoltaic solar panels for commercial properties. SVM sells its products in Asia, Europe, and the US. Owner and CEO Patrick Callinan founded SVM in 1990 as a distributor of ceramic packages for electronic devices.

Silicon wafers from SVM come in a variety of processes including thermal oxide and lithography. While wafers are available in a range of diameters and grades, the emphasis is on 100mm, 200mm and 300mm test device quality wafers. The company's wholly owned subsidiary Vista Solar provides photovoltaic solar integration services to residential and commercial customers in the greater San Francisco Bay Area.

The company's customers., who are around the world, include semiconductor manufacturers, semiconductor foundries, solar cell manufacturers, LED manufacturers, solar module makers, research and development organizations, and universities.
