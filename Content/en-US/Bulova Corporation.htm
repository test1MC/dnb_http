Bulova is working to keep perfect time in the watch industry. It sells watches, clocks, and timepiece parts under such brands as Accutron and Wittnauer (luxury), Bulova, and Caravelle (lower-priced), as well as licensed Harley-Davidson and Frank Lloyd Wright styles. The company's watches range in price from an entry-level $150 to nearly $5,000. Bulova also sells miniature collectible clocks, mostly under its own name, and has expanded its licensing efforts to include items such as eyewear. Bulova sells its products primarily through department and jewelry stores. Founded in 1875, Bulova is a wholly owned subsidiary of Japan's Citizen Watch .

New York-based Bulova sells its products mostly in North America, but also in Brazil, China, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Spain, Switzerland, Taiwan, Turkey, and the UK.

The watchmaker offers men and women automatic, sports, classic, and crystal watches. Additionally, it provides eyewear for customers of all ages. Its special-markets division changed its name in 2013 to Bulova Corporate Sales.

Bulova relies on a network of retailers in the US and internationally to sell its watches and other items. Its eyewear is sold through optical retailers, private practitioners, and distributors in North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

It also boasts sponsorship muscle. In 2013 the company partnered with Manchester Unlimited, an English Premier League football club, for a sponsorship through 2016 as the club's Official Timekeeper and global partner.

Bulova is always looking to distribute its watches through department and jewelry stores and specialty retailers. In 2014 the firm opened its first office in Germany, from which it offers a broad selection of watches.

It also works to regularly roll out new products, such as the 2013 launch of its 80-watch Caravelle New York fall collection and the debut of the company's Bulova Time alarm clock in 2012.
