Randall-Reilly Publishing (formerly Randall Publishing Company) doesn't need a CB radio to reach truckers. The company publishes business-to-business magazines and operates Web sites that cover the trucking, construction, and industrial markets. Titles include Truckers News and construction magazine Top Bid . Randall-Reilly also publishes the Who's Who Among Students publications. The company also has holdings and activities devoted to custom publishing, Web site creation, trucking conventions, and telemarketing. Additionally, it owns a database and research group, and its Award Company of America subsidiary sells plaques, trophies, and ribbons. Investcorp owns the company.

Randall-Reilly provides data services, media advertising, and custom marketing services.

The company serves than 4,000 customers in the trucking, construction, and agriculture industries, as well as in the banking sector. Clients include Caterpillar , Chevron , PACCAR , Schneider National , and Volvo .

In the midst of the economic recession that hit the publishing world particularly hard, the company sold its struggling Modern Woodworking magazine to TCC Media Group in 2009. It made the sale in order to focus on its core competencies.

Former owners Wachovia Capital Partners, a private equity and mezzanine investing subsidiary of banking giant Wachovia Corporation , sold the company to Investcorp, a provider and manager of alternative investment products, in 2008 for some $180 million. Wachovia had purchased Randall Publishing from the family and estate of H. Pettus Randall III in 2005. As a result of that acquisition, the company changed its name to Randall-Reilly Publishing.

Randall-Reilly was founded in 1934 by H. Pettus Randall, Jr.
