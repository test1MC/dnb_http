B. Braun Medical is the US arm of German medical supply firm B. Braun Melsungen . The company's products and services include a wide range of traditional and needleless IV systems and accessories, pharmaceutical devices, dialysis machines, critical care products, and vascular access and interventional product lines, as well as continuing education and training programs. B. Braun's customers include hospitals, outpatient surgery centers, and home care services companies. Its network of CAPS (Central Admixture Pharmacy Services) compounding pharmacies provides IV admixtures and solutions to hospitals, clinics, and home care providers throughout the US.

B. Braun Medical operates through four divisions: Hospital Care, Original Equipment Manufacturing, Outpatient Markets, and Renal Therapies. B. Braun Medical's affiliates include Aesculap, Inc. and Aesculap Implant Systems, LLC; these operations distribute and sell surgical instruments and implants throughout Pennsylvania. In addition, B. Braun Interventional Systems sells and distributes cardiovascular/interventional products, while Central Admixture Pharmacy Services (CAPS) offers admixture pharmacy services nationwide.

B. Braun Medical operates two manufacturing facilities in the US (one in Irvine, California, and the other in Allentown, Pennsylvania) and, through the assistance of its parent's network, has operations in more than 60 countries.

The company markets some of its primary product lines through websites designed to target specific buyers. It operates a website devoted to products, support services, and information about products for ambulatory surgical centers. B. Braun Medical also runs a separate website designed to market its home infusion therapy products.

Customers include physicians, outpatient and inpatient care providers, and pharmacies.

B. Braun Medical focuses on improving patient outcomes, reducing the number of its medication errors, and preventing the spread of infections. Regularly rolling out new products is part of this strategy. Additionally, the company's expansion efforts are highly tied to partnerships such as distribution agreements.

In 2015, B. Braun Medical acquired German firm Lauer Membran Wassertechnik to boost its portfolio of dialysis water treatment systems. That purchase has helped the company expand its dialysis products and services in the US.
