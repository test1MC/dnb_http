Working to help companies move their goods efficiently, Rehrig Pacific makes reusable plastic containers and pallets, crates, bins, and other storage and transport products. It serves such industries as agriculture, bakery, beverage, dairy, and environmental and material handling. Rehrig Pacific's lineup includes flame-retardant, medium-duty, specialty, and nest-able features. All contain 100% recycled material. The company operates plants in the US and Mexico and maintains offices in Brazil, the UK, Europe, and Hong Kong. Subsidiary Rehrig Pacific Logistics offers tracking and recovery services for returnable packaging.

From its headquarters in Los Angeles, Rehrig Pacific boasts a global reach. While it operates half a dozen plants in the US and one in Mexico, the company has sales offices in Brazil, Europe, the UK, and Hong Kong. It also maintains a global network of licensees.

In 2012 the company's logistics division rolled out an upgraded Container Asset Recovery Tracking System (C.A.R.T.S.) software program -- developed by the company's Environmental Services Group -- targeted to municipalities and private waste haulers. The software helps customers with Rehrig Pacific's RFID technology to track container collection data in an effort to alleviate container loss and identify non-paying accounts. In 2013 more than 2.8 million of Rehrig Pacific's RFID enabled containers exist in more than 70 different North American locations.

The company caters to customers through manufacturing and service locations in the US and Mexico, as well as through sales offices in South America. Rehrig Pacific maintains a network of sales representatives nationwide and in Canada.

Rehrig Pacific was formed in 1913 as a manufacturer of wooden milk crates. In the 1930s, the company became a metals manufacturing company and made crates from wire until the 1960s, when it moved into the plastics business.
