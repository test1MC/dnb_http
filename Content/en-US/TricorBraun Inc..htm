TricorBraun (formerly Kranson Industries) doesn't have a genie in a bottle, but it has a bottle for just about everything else. The company is one of the largest distributors of rigid packaging (glass and plastic) in the US. TricorBraun's slew of bottles, sprayers, tubes, closures, and dispensers, from more than 800 suppliers, serves the needs of the personal care, food and beverage, health care, and household and industrial chemical industries worldwide. TricorBraun's top customers have included Unilever , Estee Lauder, Andrew Jergens, and Procter & Gamble . Its TricorBraun WinePak arm has supplied winemakers with bottles since 1982. The company is owned by investment firm CHS Capital .

TricorBraun has about 40 facilities worldwide, including in Canada, China, India,Mexico, the US, and Europe.

The company focuses on sourcing and supplying rigid packaging and components for range of markets. It also operates a design unit, TricorBraun Design & Innovation Group,

TricorBraun serves the cosmetics, food and beverages, healthcare, industrial and household chemicals, pet and vet, and personal care markets.

The company has evolved by successfully acquiring and integrating about a dozen businesses (including Northwestern Bottle Company, Texberry Container, Caliber Packaging, W. Braun Company, Packaging Plus, Packaging West, Smith Container, and Cal-West Tool & Mold) that has expanded its portfolio of rigid packaging products and services, and its marketplace presence.

In 2014 the company developed and introduced anew lightweight, low-profile tube and closure which improves sustainability, logistic efficiencies, and cost savings.

Growign globally, that year the company opened TricorBraun-India in Mumbai, which will serve as sales office and distribution center, and as a global sourcing facility for markets across India.

To cut costs and improve operating efficiencies, in 2013 TricorBraun moved its Philadelphia branch office to Plymouth Meeting, Pennsylvania, and its Dallas branch office to Lewisville, Texas.

Looking to beef up its customer base on the US East Coast, TricorBraun acquired Pennsylvania-based Penn Bottle and Supply in early 2011. Founded in 1920, Penn Bottle and Supply brought about $50 million per year in revenues to TricorBraun.

To appeal to more customers, TricorBraun launched a new online store in 2012 that allows people to buy rigid packaging. To meet customer demand, that year it also expanded the size of its engineering facility in Chicago.

TricorBraun was founded in 1902 by Samuel Kranzberg as a St. Louis used-bottle company.
