Meritor makes axles, brakes, drivelines, suspension systems, undercarriages, and aftermarket transmissions for commercial truck, trailer, off-highway, construction, military, bus, and specialty vehicle manufacturers. It also makes U-joints, shafts, clutches, and ABS and stability control systems. The company divides its operations across two primary segments: Commercial Truck & Industrial and Aftermarket & Trailer. Meritor does business around the globe through operations in roughly 20 countries, including the US, Canada, China, France, India, Mexico, and Sweden.

Meritor divides its activities into Commercial Truck & Industrial, and Aftermarket & Trailer.

Commercial Truck & Industrial is the company's biggest earner, and makes and sells drivetrain systems and components, including axles, drivelines, and braking an suspension systems for customers in North America, South America, Europe and the Asia/Pacific region. It also includes aftermarket operations in the Asia/Pacific region and South America.

The Aftermarket & Trailer segment supplies replacement parts to commercial and industrial aftermarket customers in North America.

The company's Commercial Truck & Industrial segment is its most lucrative, representing around 75% of total sales. The Aftermarket & Trailer segment generates the remaining 25%.

Meritor WABCO is the company's 50/50 joint venture with Belgium-based WABCO . The partnership supplies safety and control systems, including anti-lock braking, automotive traction control, and collision avoidance, for commercial vehicles. The company has other joint ventures located in India, Mexico, and Turkey.

Based in Troy, Michigan, Meritor has segments and joint ventures operating across North America, Europe, South America, and the Asia/Pacific region. North America accounted for about 60% of the company's revenue in 2015.

Meritor targets customers in the commercial truck, trailer, off-highway, defense, and aftermarket sectors. The ten largest customers account for around 75% of sales, and include Volvo (some 23% of sales), Daimler AG (18%), and Navistar (9%).

It designs, engineers, markets and sells products principally to OEMs (original equipment manufacturer), dealers and distributors.

Meritor's revenue has been on a downward trend in recent years. Revenue fell 9% to $3.2 billion in fiscal 2016, as lower production in the North America Class 8 truck market dragged on the Commercial Truck & Industrial segment. The strength of the US Dollar against the Euro and the Brazilian Real also weighed on growth. The sales decrease was partially offset by new business wins and higher market volumes in India.

Net income rocketed 795% to $573.0 million due to a non-cash income tax benefit of $438 million relating to a valuation allowance reversal. The absence of restructuring costs also contributed to the healthy figure.

Cash from operations rose 110% to $204.0 million, due to higher net income and changes in working capital.

Meritor aims to grow through acquisitions and growth initiatives. In 2015, the company acquired most of Sypris Solutions's trailer axle beam and carrier manufacturing facility in Morganton, North Carolina.

Meritor acquired a manufacturing facility in Morganton, North Carolina, in 2015.

Meritor was spun off from Rockwell Automation in 1997 and known as Meritor Automotive until 2000, when it acquired Arvin Industries and changed its name to ArvinMeritor. After 11 years as ArvinMeritor, the company changed its name back to Meritor, Inc. in 2011. Both companies can trace their roots to auto parts makers founded in the early 20th century.
