American Buildings Company can put a roof over your head, quite literally. It manufactures metal buildings and roofing products for industrial, institutional, and commercial construction applications. American Buildings Company's products range from multi-story buildings and customized self-storage units to metal roof and wall panels. Its heavy fabrication capabilities include building custom-engineered steel mills, sports stadiums, and large aircraft maintenance hangars. American Buildings Company is a unit of steelmaker Nucor Corporation .

The company has manufacturing plants in Eufaula, Alabama (also the location of its headquarters, as well as in Carson City, Nevada; El Paso, Illinois; and LaCrosse, Virginia. It has general and account management offices, as well as service centers, across the US.

The company's operations are divided into three divisions: Roofing, self-storage systems, and components. Its Architectural Metal Systems subsidiary makes up the roofing division; the unit manufactures metal standing seam roof systems, as well as traditional roof and wall panels. The self-storage segment offers single-level, multi-level, and climate-controlled storage facilities, while the components division offers panels, doors, windows, trim, and hardware. American Buildings Company's sister entity, ABC Transportation, provides distribution and delivery of the company's products.

In addition to traditional building systems, the company offers products that promote energy conservation and hurricane resistance. Its GoldBuild offerings meet federal standards for energy efficiency. Some roof and wall panels are coated with SmartKote paints, which use a cooling technology to reduce energy consumption; the SmartKote system was developed for American Buildings Company by BASF . The company has also modified its standing seam roof product to meet evolving building codes in hurricane- and wind-prone states, such as Florida and North Carolina.

The company's buildings and components are marketed through a network of more than 800 authorized builders and dealers in the US and Canada.

American Buildings Company conducts research and development programs towards the introduction of new technologies and products. Its TALON technology aims to speed the design state to reduce expenses, while its building information modeling process allows project design replication for customer life cycle management.

American Buildings Company got its start in 1947 as a small regional fabricator in Columbus, Georgia. American Buildings Company and its former parent, MAGNATRAX , were acquired by Nucor in 2007.
