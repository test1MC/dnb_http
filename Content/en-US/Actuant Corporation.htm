A diversified industrial company, Actuant makes electrical and industrial devices, such as wire connectors, switches, transformers, and tools (for wholesale and retail distribution) under such brand names as Acme Electric , Gardner Bender, Hydratight , and Kopp. Actuant's engineered solutions product line includes Power-Packer brand drive technology used in the hydraulic cab-tilt systems of heavy-duty trucks and automotive convertible tops. The company also offers cable, synthetic rope, pipeline connectors, and rental equipment for the energy industry. Customers have included such heavy hitters as Caterpillar , BorgWarner , BP , Petrofac , and Shell.

Actuant is organized into three operating and reportable segments: Industrial, Energy, and Engineered Solutions. The Industrial segment specializes in the design, manufacture, and distribution of branded hydraulic and mechanical tools to the maintenance, industrial, infrastructure, and production automation markets.

Energy provides joint integrity products and services and customized offshore vessel mooring services to the global oil and gas, power generation, and other energy markets. Engineered Solutions provides highly engineered position and motion control systems to OEMs in various on and off-highway vehicle markets; it also caters to a variety of other industrial and agricultural markets.

Actuant has established operations in more than 30 countries, including Brazil, China, Hungary, India, Kazakhstan, Mexico, South Korea, Turkey, and the United Arab Emirates, among others. The US generated 42% of the company's total sales in 2016. Other major markets include the Netherlands (12%) and the UK (10%).

The company sells to retail outlets such as Home Depot and Ace Hardware . In addition to retail and wholesale distributors, Actuant caters to OEM and MRO (maintenance, repair, and overhaul) customers engaged in automotive, medical, military, oil and gas, power generation, and infrastructure activities.

Actuant's hydraulic and mechanical tools are marketed through the Enerpac, Simplex, Precision-Hayes, Milwaukee Cylinder, and Larzep brand names. Customers include Bechtel , Chevron , Subsea 7, Aker Solutions, FMC Technologies, Expro, Honeywell , and Technip.

Actuant's revenues have decreased the last two years. From 2015 to 2016, revenues dipped by 8% from $1.25 billion to $1.15 billion. The company also suffered a net loss of $105 million in 2016 mainly due to about $187 million it paid in impairment charges.

The company's core sales were down in 2016 as a result of challenging end market conditions in the global oil and gas markets. Changes in foreign currency exchange rates also unfavorably impacted sales comparisons by $45 million, while fiscal 2016 acquisitions added $19 million of sales. Additionally, fiscal 2016 and 2015 results both included impairment charges related to the write-down of acquired goodwill, intangible assets and long-lived assets, which reduced operating profit margins.

Acquisitions has been central to Actuant's growth strategy. In 2016 it acquired the Middle East, Caspian, and North Africa (MENAC) operations of Four Quest Energy for $60 million. Based in Dubai, UAE, FourQuest MENAC is an expert in the provision of pipeline and process services across the Caspian, the Middle East, and the North Africa regions. The deal strengthened Actuant's position within the integrity and maintenance services in the energy space.

From its founding in 1910 as American Grinder and Manufacturing, a maker of tool sharpeners, the business diversified into water and oil pumps for military vehicles during WWI, and later it expanded into hand tools. Renamed Blackhawk in 1925, the company sold off its hand-grinder line, exited the pump industry, and moved into hydraulic equipment in 1927. In 1955 Blackhawk sold its original line of hand tools.

After taking the name Applied Power Industries in 1961, the enterprise became Applied Power, Inc., in 1973. It bought hydraulic-equipment makers Toyo Hydraulic Equipment and Electro-Hydraulic Controls in the 1980s. Applied Power went public in 1987, and a year later it bought Gardner Bender (established in 1959) from the Gardner family. In 1989 the company acquired Barry Wright Corporation (now Barry Controls).

During the early 1990s Applied Power expanded into Mexico, Asia, and Europe. In 1997 it purchased electronic-enclosure maker C Fab Group of Dublin, Ireland, and Versa Technologies, a US conglomerate of industrial equipment makers. In 1998 Applied Power bought other enclosure makers, including ZERO, Vero Group, AA Manufacturing, Premier Industries, Rubicon Group, and certain assets of Performance Manufactured Products, Product Technology, and Brown Manufacturing. These acquisitions were primarily responsible for the doubling of Applied Power's sales in 1998. In 1999 the company announced that it was considering selling its industrial business.

In 2000 Applied Power switched gears and spun off its electronics enclosures business to shareholders as APW Ltd. Former Applied Power chairman and CEO Dick Sim became chairman of the remaining industrial group -- which was renamed Actuant Corporation the following year -- as well as chairman and CEO of APW. Barry Controls was sold to Hutchinson Worldwide, a unit of TOTAL . APW Ltd. filed for bankruptcy two years later.
