Kemira is hoping this whole "paperless office" concept doesn't take hold. The company produces many of the chemicals used in making the printed page: its bleaching chemicals whiten paper, its coating chemicals make papers firmer and easier to print on, its inks are used to print on papers, and its titanium dioxide is a white pigment used in papers and inks. Kemira also makes water-treatment chemicals, chemical solutions for municipal and industrial operations, and extractions and process solutions for oil and mining work. It maintains research and development centers in Finland, China, Brazil, and the US. Kemira began in 1920 as Finland's state-owned sulfuric acid and phosphate plant.
