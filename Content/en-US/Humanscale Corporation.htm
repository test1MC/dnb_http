Humanscale weighs in big in the ergonomic office furniture industry. The company is a leading manufacturer of office products -- such as keyboard supports, monitor arms, foot rests, glare filters, chairs, and desk lamps -- that are easy on the body. Its products are sold through more than half a dozen showrooms in the US and in France. It also sells its products online and provides products for use in healthcare environments. Additionally, contract furniture dealers and retailers in the US and more than 50 other countries stock Humanscale's products. The company operates manufacturing facilities in the US, Australia, China, India, and Ireland. Humanscale was established in 1982.

Humanscale's offices are located in Atlanta; Denver; New York; Los Angeles, Costa Mesa, and San Francisco, California; Houston; Philadelphia; and Parsippany, New Jersey. Based in New York, it has international offices in Europe and the Asia/Pacific.

The company sells its products worldwide through dealers and online. Humanscale sells its products in the US and Canada through retailers.

The company boasts more than half a dozen showrooms, as well, across North America located in the cities of Boston, Chicago, Dallas, Denver, New York, and Toronto. In Europe, Humanscale has showrooms in Italy, the UK, France, Ireland, the Netherlands, and the Czech Republic. Its presence in the Asia/Pacific region spans Hong Kong, Singapore, the Middle East, North Africa, Shanghai, India, Australia, Malaysia, Beijing, and New Zealand.

Humanscale Healthcare, a company division, develops ergonomic products for healthcare environments. Aiming to minimize work-related health risks and boost caregiver comfort, products include such items as mobile technology carts and wall-mounted computer stations.

In 2014 Humanscale is focused on the UK. To this end, the company rolled out a series of ergonomic healthcare IT products. It's aiming to capture more market share in the region and support care and patient engagement.

The company's sleek and pricey chair designs have taken a front-row seat on both the big screen and the small screen. The Freedom chair, which was Humanscale's entry product into the task seating market, has been the company's first of many innovative designs to not only win awards but to be spotlighted by Hollywood. The company's chairs have received numerous design awards and have been spotlighted on such shows as 24, Ugly Betty, and Heroes, as well as the films The Bourne Ultimatum and Mission: Impossible III . Humanscale sold its millionth Freedom chair in 2009.

The company does its part to be environmentally friendly, manufacturing products that are composed of recycled materials, utilize fewer parts, and weigh less (so less material is needed).
