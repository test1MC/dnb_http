Carbs are a good thing to GlycoMimetics. The biotechnology company uses carbohydrate chemistry to develop drug candidates for rare diseases. Its lead drug candidate is designed to treat vaso-occlusive crisis (VOC), a painful condition that affects people with sickle cell disease. If approved, it would be the first drug to treat the cause of VOC and potentially reduce dependence on narcotics for pain management. Pfizer bought the rights to develop and commercialize the VOC drug. In addition, the company is developing a drug to assist people with acute myeloid leukemia (AML) during chemotherapy. GlycoMimetics went public in 2014. It raised $56 million and plans to use the proceeds to further fund clinical trials.
