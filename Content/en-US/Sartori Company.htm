Sartori makes Italian (parmesan, gorgonzola, asiago), Hispanic, blue-veined, and other aged specialty cheeses. Brands include Sartori, SarVecchio, Dolcina, BellaVitano, and Signature Blends. The cheese maker sells its products primarily as ingredients to other food manufacturers, as well as to retail food outlets and food service operators, and has partnerships with food distribution companies throughout the US. Sartori was founded in 1939 as S&R Cheese by Italian immigrant Paul Sartori and is still owned and operated by the fourth generation of the Sartori family, including CEO Jim Sartori.

Wisconsin-based Sartori operates in its home state and in Colorado. It sells its products nationwide and exports them to Italy and Japan.

Besides selling products online, Sartori sells its cheeses through leading conventional and specialty supermarket chains across the US, including Earth Fare , Kroger , Publix , Whole Foods , and Albertson's .
