Champion Laboratories wants to keep your machine cool and clean. It manufactures oil, fuel, and air filters for motor vehicle OEMs and aftermarket. Other products include breather filters, cabin air filters, and transmission filters. It also makes engine management systems, driveline components, and lighting systems for the marine, mining, construction, industrial, and agricultural markets. Its primary brand names include Champ, Luber-Finer, and Kleener. Champion Laboratories has 10 manufacturing and distribution locations in North and South America, Europe, and Asia. The company is owned by UCI International (formerly United Components), which is owned by New Zealand investment firm Rank Group Limited .

Champion Laboratories' six manufacturing centers in the US, Canada, France, and the UK produce about 2,500,000 filters per week. Parent company UCI generated about 85% of its revenue from the US in fiscal 2012.

Additional brand names include Encore, Mopar, TRD, Mobil, PetroClear Filters, Royal Purple, and STP, to name a few. Champion Laboratories also makes private label filters for its customers.

The filtration product line contributed almost 40% of the total sales of UCI in fiscal 2012.

Champion Laboratories started as Kleen Pak Manufacturing Company, a filter manufacturer, in West Salem, Illinois in 1955. In 1959 it developed an oil filter that revolutionized the industry and won it the business of many OEMs. Collaborations, co-branding, and acquisitions followed, making the company a success.
