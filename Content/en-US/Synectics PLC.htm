Synectics builds, installs, and maintains security and surveillance systems for customers in difficult environments such as public spaces, oil and gas installations and infrastructure settings. Its products -- stationary and mobile -- integrate information capture, storage, and analysis that can be easily retrieved and examined. The company works to adapt its offerings for the specific needs of customers, which include casinos, shipyards, and petroleum manufacturers.

Synectics works in two segments, each accounting for about half the company's revenue.

The Systems division provides specialist electronic surveillance systems, which are based on the company's proprietary technology. The systems are designed for reliability and flexibility. The systems segment particularly targets oil and gas, gaming, infrastructure, high security, transport, and public space applications.

The Integration and Managed Services (IMS) division delivers end-to-end security and surveillance products and mobile systems for transport operators. It also offers managed services for facilities and security related services.

Based in the UK, the company's biggest market is the UK-Europe which generates 69% of revenue. The company gets some 13% of revenue from both North America and the Asia-Pacific region.

Synectics reaches customers by working with global integrators, technology partners, and resellers. It also sells directly to end users. Some of its customers are Martin Linge, Penn National Gaming casinos, the British Museum , South Yorkshire Police, Deutsche Bahn , and Skoda Transportation .

The slowing oil business and a problem project combines to cut revenue 25% in 2014 from 2013. Oil and gas companies put off purchases and delayed projects to deal with the lower prices for oil. Synectics also took a hit from poor performance on a large surveillance system integration and installation contract. That project not only cost the company money, it took management attention from developing new business. North America was the only segment to grow in 2014, with sales rising about 70% there.

The lower revenue resulted in a net loss for the company in 2014.

The company has introduced new products such as improved HD cameras with capture more information and with more resolution. Synectics is targeting the Asia-Pacific region for growth. It its operations in the area in regional headquarters in Singapore from which it can provide focused, local support for customers and partners in gaming, oil and gas, critical infrastructure, and public space. The Singapore location includes a training facility and engineering lab.
