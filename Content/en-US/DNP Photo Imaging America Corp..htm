DNP Photo Imaging America develops and markets systems used to reproduce, correct, and reorder film and digital photos. The company's kiosks and countertop photo systems are used by consumers at retail locations such as drug and grocery stores. Its professional digital minilabs are used in photo labs to process film and digital images. Formerly called Pixel Magic Imaging, the company was established in 1992. In 2004 two Japanese firms, Dai Nippon Printing and Altech ADS, purchased a controlling stake in Pixel Magic. The company changed its name to DNP Photo Imaging America in 2007.
