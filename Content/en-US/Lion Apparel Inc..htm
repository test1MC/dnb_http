Lion Apparel would like to control the lion's share of its industry. The company makes men's and women's logistics and safety garments. It produces firefighters' personal protective equipment, including helmets, and it offers training equipment for first responders. Lion Apparel supplies fire departments under the BullEx and Haagen brand names in several countries for government organizations such as the Bundeswehr (the armed forces of Germany), the Department of Defense Troop Support, the US Navy , and the US Marine Corps . It claims to serve seven of the 10 largest fire departments in North America.

Lion Apparel has subsidiaries and international operations in countries such as Australia, Canada, France, Germany, Spain, and the UK. In the US, it caters to the major metropolitan areas of Chicago, Baltimore, Dallas, Detroit, Houston, Philadelphia, Phoenix, New Orleans, San Diego, San Francisco, and Seattle.

Lion Apparel sells its products through the Internet and a network of dealers internationally and n the US. It counts seven of the 10 largest fire departments in North America as its customers. It serves three primary markets: emergency services, military, and personal equipment management.

Lion Apparel increases its product portfolio and attracts additional clients through the use of acquisitions. In 2012 it bought BullEx Digital Safety and HAAGEN Fire Training Products, two providers of fire and safety training systems and centers. The Albany, New York-based BullEx and The Netherlands-based HAAGEN companies were integrated into a new division called the LION Training Resources Group. In 2011 Lion Apparel snapped up Wisconsin-based Safeguard America, a maker of the FireGuard brand of protective structural firefighting gloves and apparel.

Lion Apparel was founded all the way back in 1898 by William Lapedes.
