No need to ask how the meal will be prepared at Weber-Stephen's company parties. The firm is one of the nation's top makers of barbecue grills, including portable, charcoal, and gas versions. Its portfolio includes several brands, such as Spirit, Genesis, Summit, Performer, One-Touch, and Weber Q. Portable models include Smokey Joe and Go-Anywhere. Founded in 1951, the company also markets fireplaces and grilling accessories. Weber-Stephen's products are sold through home improvement stores, hardware retailers, department stores, and mass merchandisers. The founding Stephen family sold a majority stake in the business to BDT Capital Partners, an investment fund led by former Goldman Sachs banker Byron Trott.

Based in Illinois, Weber-Stephen helps consumers in more than 70 countries get their grill on.

The company makes and markets products that are used in more than 85 million American households. Weber-Stephen relies on home centers, hardware stores, department stores, patio stores, and other retail outlets in the US, Canada, and countries worldwide to sell its grills and accessories.

Internationally, Weber's products are distributed through two channels: importers and Weber-Stephen Products LLC subsidiaries. Its subsidiaries are located throughout Europe, Australia, Asia, and Mexico.

Some of its dealers include Zimmer Hardware, Ace Hardware of Palatine, Palatine True Value, and Home Depot.

Weber-Stephen regularly rolls out new products. The company introduced the Spirit E-210 gas grill in 2013, as well as the Jumbo Joe portable grill. Previously, it introduced the larger-capacity Weber Q 240 Electric Grill in 2012, as well as redesigned gas and charcoal grills, including a new Spirit line -- a two-burner 200 series and a three-burner 300 series.

The company has benefited from the popularity of football game tailgates and backyard parties on Fourth of July and Labor Day. Weber-Stephen's grill owners have also been firing up on Thanksgiving and Christmas to celebrate traditional holidays in a new way. Latching on to expanded interest in grilling, Weber-Stephen released the companion cookbooks Weber's On the Grill: Chicken & Sides and Weber's On the Grill: Steak & Sides . The titles followed up Weber's Way to Grill . All three books were written by chef Jamie Purviance. Weber-Stephen also posts grilling recipes on its website.

As outdoor kitchens also become ubiquitous, Weber-Stephen has rolled out several new models in recent years to offer consumers more options and update its brand. Many models feature the look of stainless-steel or bright colors.

Additionally, Weber-Stephen looks to cater to smartphone users by launching mobile apps for its product lines for iPhone and iPod.

The company operates factories in Palatine and Huntley, Illinois. It boasts subsidiaries in Europe, Australia, Asia, and Mexico.

The founding Stephen family retained ownership of The Weber Grill Restaurant business, which operates four Weber Grill Restaurants in Indianapolis and the Chicago area. The eateries specialize in classic American fare, serving up steaks, barbecue, and hamburgers that have been prepared on industrial-sized Weber kettle grills.
