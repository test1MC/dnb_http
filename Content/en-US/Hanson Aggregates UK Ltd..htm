Hanson Aggregates UK has built a sturdy business with broken stone, which is widely used in making construction materials.  Operating as a subsidiary of Hanson , itself a subsidiary of German aggregate leader HeidelbergCement , Hanson Aggregates represents one of the country's largest construction materials companies. As part of its business, it produces crushed rock, sand and gravel, dimension stone, ready-mix concrete, decorative and building aggregates, mortar, and other items. The company counts several facilities across the UK among its operations and maintains the largest fleet of marine aggregate dredgers in Europe through its Hanson Aggregates Marine.

With operations across the UK, Hanson Aggregates delivers an average of 750,000 tons of aggregates, asphalt, and cement, in addition to 80,000 cubic meters of ready-mixed concrete and transports its products by rail, water, and road. The company boasts more than 250 concrete plants, 80 quarries, 20 wharves, 10 recycling plants, and 10 distribution depots. Bringing in more than £1.2 billion in 2011 turnover, the company made more than £40 million in capital investments that year.

Principal markets for Hanson Aggregates UK include England and Wales, as well as Scotland's central belt.
