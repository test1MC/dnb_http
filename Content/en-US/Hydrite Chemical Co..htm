Whether you need cleaner water or cleaner food, Hydrite Chemical has the solution, usually in the form of a chemical product. In addition to water treatment and food and dairy sanitation chemicals, Hydrite offers expertise in custom organic chemical processes, including esterification, polymerization, and the distillation of monomers. The company also manufactures sulfur chemicals (liquid sulfur salts) and serves the pulp and paper, waste management, and food processing industries. It also offers its clients compliance management services. Hydrite operates manufacturing plants and distributes its products throughout the Midwestern US and in California. President John Honkamp and his family control the company.

The company has operations in 14 locations in California, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, and Wisconsin.

Hydrite's assets include more than 7,000 products, 850,000 sq. ft. of warehouse space, 7 million gallons of bulk storage,80 production vessels, and a fleet of 255 transportation units, including tractors, van trailers, tankers, and railcars.

In addition to industrial chemical distribution, the company makes and supplies ingredients used to make food and beverages more flavorful, fluoride to treat water to help prevent tooth decay, other chemicals used to enhance water quality, and the cleaners and sanitizers used in the food industry to keep the food supply safe.

Its chemical product line include inorganic and organic coagulants, flocculant polymers, odor control, defoamers, filamentous bulking control, formulated microorganisms for biological treatment, and a wide portfolio of commodities.

The company also offers offers expertise in chemical distribution, food and dairy sanitation, food ingredients, organic processing, liquid sulfur salts, water treatment, foam control, and compliance management.

Hydrite is the North American distributor of a number of chemical makers, including Italmatch Chemical (for red phosphorus products), Arkema , Eastman Chemical , Occidental Chemical , CITGO Petroleum , and Ineos Phenol . The company serves markets such as Agriculture; Biofuels; Graphic Arts; Food Processing; Industrial; Oil and Gas; Paints, Coatings, Inks and Adhesives, and Pulp and Paper.

The company grows through the acquisition of complementary businesses and through organic developments.

Hydrite was founded in 1929.

Expanding its geographic market and asset base, in 2011 the company acquired Choice Chemical, giving Hydrite a base in north Central California, and allowing it to expand its product offerings in the dairy, food, and beverage industries.

In 2011, to provide a reliable supply of high quality ammonium thiosulphate fertilizers to agricultural producers across the Upper Midwest, Hydrite signed a long-term agreement with Nebraska-based fertilizer producer, Kugler Co.

In 2011 Hydrite bought a 133,000 sq. ft. facility in Milwaukee, Wisconsin to help expand its capacity for manufacturing of chemical products of the food industry.

Expanding its product portfolio, in 2013 Hydrite and Arkema teamed up to launch a unique whey decolorization product (Luperox A40FP-EZ9), a liquid version of benzoyl peroxide, which has many benefits compared to the dry counterpart that is currently available.
