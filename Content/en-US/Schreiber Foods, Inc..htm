Want cheese with that, Schreiber Foods asks. The company is a major supplier of cheese used in hamburgers and other dishes served by US fast food chains and restaurants, schools and universities, hospitals, and other foodservice operators. Schreiber produces some 575 private-label processed and natural cheeses, as well as dairy ingredients, for grocers, club stores, wholesalers, and food manufacturers. It also offers its own brands, including American Heritage and Cooper, for retail sale. Schreiber has expanded its presence to 20 countries by acquiring or forming joint ventures with smaller cheese makers. Boasting $5 billion in annual revenue, Schreiber is believed to be the world's largest employee-owned dairy company.

Schreiber's natural cheeses include cheddar, Swiss, semi-soft, soft/fresh, soft ripened, string, pasta filata or Italian, Edam and Gouda, and hard cheese. It also produces and supplies processed cheeses and cultured products such as cream cheeses and yogurts.

In addition to cheese and dairy ingredients, Schreiber Foods offers customer support services, such as category management, risk management, redistribution, vendor managed inventory (VMI), packaging and design, and supply chain management. Green Bay Machinery, a subsidiary, manufactures extruders and other equipment for processing cheese. Schreiber Foods' Capri Packaging division produces flexible film and other packaging products.

Wisconsin-based Schreiber Foods serves around 20 countries from its more than 20 production and distribution facilities around the world. A little more than half of its facilities are in the US, in the states of Missouri, Wisconsin, Utah, Pennsylvania, California, Texas, and Arizona. Its international facilities are in Horbranz, Austria; Rio Azul, Brazil; Sofia, Bulgaria; Benesov, Czech Republic; Wangen and Lindenberg, Germany; Baramati, India; Leon, Mexico; Castelo Branco, Portugal; and Santa Cruz de Tenerife, Spain.

Schreiber Foods supplies products mainly to customers in the retail, foodservice, and food manufacturing industries, and is a leading supplier of cheese to fast food chains and restaurants, schools and universities, hospitals, and other foodservice operators. It makes private-label dairy ingredients and processed and natural cheeses for grocers, club stores, wholesalers, and food makers. Schreiber Foods' brands include American Heritage and Cooper, which it sells in retail outlets.

While privately owned Schreiber Foods doesn't report its financial results, the dairy giant claimed to ring up $4.8 billion in sales in fiscal 2014, and above $4.5 billion in recent years. Forbes ranked it as the 84th largest private company in the US in 2014.

Schreiber Foods has been expanding in Europe by opening and acquiring new facilities (especially yogurt plants) in new markets. In 2015, one year after its entry into the European yogurt market with plant openings in Bulgaria, the Czech Republic, Portugal, and Spain, the company purchased three European yogurt plants in Zvolen, Slovakia; and Noblejas and Talavera, Spain from French dairy firm Senoble International. The Slovakia facility manufactured its own private label dairy products and its own brand (Zvolensky), while the two facilities in Spain manufactured dairy desserts and yogurts for local retailer Mercadona, helping to deepen Schreiber's existing relationship with the retailer.

The company prides itself in being innovative, launching measures that have cut waste and production times. In 2015, for example, the company explained that it had developed an "interleaf" method for stacking cheese slices (without paper in between slices), which reduced paper use by 400,000 pounds per year and made it easier for customers to grab the cheese. It also said it was the first in the industry to shorten packaging for 8-ounce shredded cheese by two inches, which used 10% less packaging per bag, freed up valuable retail shelf space for customers, and cut packaging changeover times in half at its plants.

Schreiber Foods strategically locates its US distribution centers (which make up more than half of its global facilities base) to ensure that it can ship its dairy products anywhere in the continental US in one day. The company also pursues joint ventures as a way to win new business and add local capacity.

In the past, the company has pursued acquisitions that have taken advantage of Americans' appetite for healthier options. In 2010, Schreiber Foods expanded its European presence after acquiring a 51% stake in a joint venture with Austrian cheese maker Rupp. Known as Schreiber & Rupp, the venture catered to the Continental market for private-label cheese and food manufacturing. In 2011, Schreiber Foods acquired the customer-brand yogurt business of Dean Foods , which included production plants in California, New York, and Wisconsin, deepens its relationship with customers, including Subway , Safeway , Weight Watchers , and Wal-Mart .

Schreiber Foods is privately-held and employee-owned.
