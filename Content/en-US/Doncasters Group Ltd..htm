Eyes are cast up on Doncasters, a manufacturing services provider for the gas and steam turbine industries. Using alloys and metals that are difficult to shape and form, the company makes airfoils, casings, rings, and exhaust and combustion components, and other engineered products for use in aircraft engines. Other products include automotive turbochargers, and dies and tooling for the forging and casting industry. Doncasters also offers electromagnetic shielding services and manufactures precision components for equipment and vehicles used by the US armed forces. The company has served such customers as Boeing , GE Aircraft Engines , and Rolls-Royce .

Doncasters has 33 manufacturing sites across the UK, continental Europe, the US, Mexico, and China. The US represents 44% of its net sales. Other major markets include the UK (17%), other European countries (27%), and other countries around the world (12%).

The company's power systems division provides components, which it casts, forges, machines, and fabricates, to the industrial gas turbine, and aerospace markets. Doncasters' fastener systems segment makes fasteners (critical engineered and tension control, ceramic ferrule, weld stud) which are used in transportation (automotive, marine, aerospace, agriculture), commercial construction (bridges, highways, parking structures, machinery), power generation, and industrial applications (appliances, automation).

Doncasters' revenues declined 8% from 2013 to 2014. This was fueled by reductions in sales within its power systems division, which reflected lower market demand. Specialized engineering divisional sales were also lower in 2014 due to lower demand for metals from certain customers and weaker petrochemical market demand.

Doncasters traces its beginnings in 1778 in Sheffield, England, when Daniel Doncaster established crucible steel-making for the making of hand tools. Today it is owned by Dubai International Capital, a Dubai-based international investment company.
