Brown Jordan International (BJI) prefers that its customers take a seat -- inside or out. The firm designs and makes upscale indoor and outdoor furniture, including chairs, tables, sofas, and loveseats, for home and commercial use. Its brands include Brown Jordan, Casual Living, Charter, La-Z-Boy (under license), Lodging by Liberty, Wabash, and Winston. It also makes private-label brand furniture for Better Homes and Gardens and Hampton Bay. Residential products are sold by mass merchants ( Home Depot and SAM'S CLUB ); commercial lines are sold to hotels, restaurants, health care facilities, and schools. In 2017 private equity firm Littlejohn & Co. acquired the company.

The Florida-based company has operations in 24 countries, including the US, China, Mexico, India, Thailand, Indonesia, and Vietnam. It has an operating base in Shanghai, and a design, research, and development center in Hangzhou, China.

The company operates through more than half a dozen subsidiaries, such as Brown Jordan (outdoor furniture), Casual Living Worldwide (contract furnishings, direct imports, national accounts, and specialty retail), Charter Furniture (upholstery seating), Wabash Valley (outdoor furniture and accessories), Winston Furniture (home and garden furniture), and Winston Contract (commercial outdoor furnishings).

BJI is a multichannel marketer, making and distributing outdoor furniture to mass market national accounts, specialty retail, and contract markets. Its indoor furniture is marketed to the hospitality and lodging markets. Some customers include The Home Depot , Wal-Mart , Costco , Target , Canadian Tire , Crate and Barrel , and American Furniture .

The company focuses on developing new products for its commercial and residential customers.

Buoyed by the recovery in the housing market, the furniture maker has a multimillion-dollar capital investment plan to expand its manufacturing capacity. The effort aims to meet increasing demand for residential and commercial furniture in North America. The investment is focused on its plant in Haleyville, Alabama, where BJI has been working to add robotic welding equipment, automated buff and grind, and high-speed powder-coated paint capabilities.

In September 2014, BJI acquired Tropitone Furniture Co., which caters to the hospitality and retail furniture markets.

The company was founded in 1945.
