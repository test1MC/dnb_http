Calsonic Kansei manufactures automobile parts and modular systems. A module clusters a number of components (for instance, a dashboard, instrument panel, glove box, and radio) into a single part in order to simplify and lower the cost of automobile final assembly. The company's module offerings include front-end and cockpit modules. Other automotive products include exhaust and air-conditioning systems, as well as keyless entry systems. Calsonic Kansei also manufactures parts for the automotive aftermarket. In recent years the company has divested non-automotive offerings to focus on the auto industry.

With its headquarters in Japan, the company has its management operations in the Americas, Europe, China and Asia, along with its development operations in Japan, the US, Mexico, UK, France, China, Thailand, and India, and over 60 sites throughout the world.

Calsonic Kansei's largest customer is Nissan Motor , contributing 84% of its net revenue.

In 2014 the company established a new plant in Bawal plant (Haryana, India) owned by Calsonic Kansei Motherson Auto Products Limited (CKM), which is 51% owned by the company to manufacture compressors for automotive air conditioners. The facility supplies compressors in India where demand for these products is steadily growing.

Calsonic Kansei was established in the 2000 merger of Calsonic Corp. and Kansei Corp. Calsonic dates back to 1938, when it was founded as Nihon Radiator Manufacturing. Kansei was established in 1956 as Kanto Seiki.
