This US manufacturer is the ultimate chain store. U.S. Tsubaki is a maker of metal and plastic drive chains, attachment chains, engineering class chains, sprockets, and power transmission components. It supplies automotive OEMs including Toyota , General Motors , Ford , Honda , and Nissan . The company's roller chains are also used in food packaging and oilfield drilling equipment. In addition to product sales, the company offers its customers training, technical support, design support, and logistics services. U.S. Tsubaki is a subsidiary of Tsubakimoto Chain Company of Japan , a global leader in the manufacture of chain and power transmission components.

U.S. Tsubaki operates service centers in Dallas; Wheeling (near Chicago), Illinois; Atlanta; Los Angeles; and Philadelphia. The company has manufacturing plants in Anoka, Minnesota; Chicopee and Holyoke, Massachusetts; Milwaukee; and Sandusky, Ohio, and markets its products to more than 70 countries.

U.S. Tsubaki offers a full lineup of engineering class chain, including Drive, Roller Conveyor, Steel Bushed, Welded Steel, Drop Forged, Bar and Pin. Its premium roller chain products include the self-lube LAMBDA series, corrosion-resistant Neptune, and fatigue-resistant super chain. The company's Power Transmission components include Shock Relays, Torque Limiters, Couplings, Gear Motors, POWER-LOCK keyless locking devices, and Disco variable speed motors. Its KabelSchlepp Cable and Hose Carriers products include Dynamic Cable and Hose Carriers, Cables for Motion, and Chip Protection products.

North America accounted for 24% of U.S. Tsubaki's parent company's total revenues in 2014.

To keep up with demand, in 2013 the company spent about $7 million expanding its Holyoke plant.

Tsubakimoto Chain Company was founded in Japan in 1917. It began making roller chains in 1923. Its US subsidiary was established in 1971.
