Kestrel-BCE makes PVC-U (polyvinyl chloride unplasticized) and PVC-UE (polyvinyl chloride unplasticized expanded) building products for a range of residential and commercial projects in the UK. Through its Kestrel Building Products unit, it manufactures and distributes some 800 products for window installation and roofline applications, including soffit and fascia boards, cladding, siding, plantation shutters, and trim. Clients include architects, distributors, homebuilders, social housing, local authorities, registered installers, and consumers. Kestrel-BCE's products are also available in France, Germany, the Netherlands, and in South America.

Kestrel-BCE's PVC-U and PVC-UE products, manufactured with a calcium organic formulation, are used in sustainable building projects. Its products are also used in more than 40,000 homes each year.

Stabilizer and additives are needed to make a thermoplastic material like PVC stronger for use in window and door profiles. The three main stabilizer technologies used in the UK and Europe for PVC-U and PVC-UE extrusions are lead, tin, and calcium organic. Kestrel makes environmentally-friendly PVC-U products that use 100% calcium organic stabilizers, with no lead and no tin.

The company offers a wide range of U-PVC, PVC-UE Roofline and Cladding products, making more than two million meters of profile a month.

Kestrel-BCE has annual sales of more than £30 million ($45.4 million), and has invested more than £10 million ($15.1 million) within the last five years in new manufacturing technology and equipment.

In 2012 the company, once part of the Latium companies, became part of Epwin Holdings following the merger of Epwin Group and Latium.
