IdaTech is developing fuel cell systems and fuel processing systems for both stationary and portable electric generation. And no, they don't do it by sticking electrodes into potatoes. Fuel cells use hydrogen and oxygen to produce electricity without combustion and resulting pollutants. The company has offices in Germany and Malaysia and is developing fuel cells for the US Army and the US Navy , and for commercial and industrial power backup applications. Founded in 1996, IdaTech was acquired by IDACORP in 1999. IDACORP sold the company to Investec in 2006. In 2012 Ballard Power Systems acquired key assets of IdaTech by giving Investec $7.7 million worth of Ballard common shares.
