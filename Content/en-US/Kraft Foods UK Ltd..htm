Philadelphia Cream Cheese may be named for a city in the US and Americans do "schmeer" it on their bagels -- but people eat it in the UK too, thanks to Mondelez UK (formerly Kraft Foods UK). Along with the range of Philadelphia spreads, the Mondelez International (formerly Kraft Foods) subsidiary also sells Kenco and Tassimo coffee, Terry's Chocolate Orange candy, and Vegemite. While most of its products are manufactured elsewhere, Kraft Foods UK still makes instant coffee at its facility in Banbury. The coffee produced there is sold under the Maxwell House, Kenco, and Mellow Bird's brand names.

The company closed its York confectionery plant in 2005 and transferred production to alternative sites in Western and Central Europe.

Parent Kraft spun off its North American grocery business in 2012 and renamed its global snack food business as Mondelez International .
