Mitsubishi Electric & Electronics USA projects big things in the US. The subsidiary of Mitsubishi Electric makes and markets a diverse line of consumer, commercial, and industrial electronics products. Mitsubishi Electric US's product lines include automation equipment (motion controllers, servo systems, and uninterruptible power supplies), automotive (electrical components), elevators and escalators, heating and air conditioning (air purifiers and compressors), home theater (audio-video systems and stadium displays), power products (air-, hydrogen-, and water-cooled generators, along with photovoltaic solar cells), semiconductors, and wireless communications devices.

Mitsubishi Electric US distributes its products from more than 50 locations throughout the country.

Mitsubishi Electric US' companies include Mitsubishi Electric Power Products, Mitsubishi Electric US (Elevator/Escalator Division, Heating & Cooling Division, Photovoltaic Division, Visual & Imaging Systems Division, Semiconductor Division), Mitsubishi Electric Automotive America, Mitsubishi Electric Factory Automation and Mitsubishi Research & Development Laboratories.

The Visual and Imaging Systems Division of Mitsubishi Electric US markets data wall display systems, industrial printers, and digital photo printers. Mitsubishi Electric Power Products serves the North American power systems, rail transportation. and water treatment industries.

The company's products come in a variety of sizes. Mitsubishi Electric US has developed a projector that fits in the palm of a hand, as well as the 71x72 ft. high-definition display used at the Atlanta Braves ' Turner Field.

North America accounts for about 8% of Mitsubishi Electric 's annual revenues.

In an effort to drive revenues, Mitsubishi Electric US' semiconductor division has announced plans to expand sales efforts in North and South America for its industrial TFT LCD display products.

In 2013 Mitsubishi Electric Visual and Imaging Systems Division launched VS-60HS12U Slim Cube. The rear-projection LED display cube measures only 20.5 inches deep, which is approximately 26% slimmer than Mitsubishi Electric's similarly sized, conventional rear-projection LED display cubes. That year this unit also formed a new business relationship with WYNIT Distribution, through which WYNIT will offer authorized resellers the CP-D707DW, CP-9550DW, CP-9810DW, CP-3800DW and CP-D70DW dye-sublimation photo printers for private, professional and industrial use.
