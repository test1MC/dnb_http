The Fiskars brand is fairly frisky in its old age. Fiskars Brands is the North American manufacturing and marketing subsidiary of Finland's Fiskars Corporation . The Fiskars parent is the second-oldest incorporated business in the world, having celebrated more than 365 anniversaries. While the company's signature consumer item is orange-handled scissors, Fiskars Brands makes garden tools, craft tools, and school and office supplies. Fiskars Brands also makes ergonomic cutting tools for the garden as part of its PowerGear line, scissors and shears for sewing and quilting under the Gingher name, and outdoor cutting tools by Gerber. It serves consumers worldwide in 60-plus countries.

Based in Wisconsin, Fiskars Brands represents the North American operation of its Finnish parent company. It markets and distributes products in North America, as well as in South America, Europe, and Asia Pacific. Altogether, Fiskars Brands enjoys an extended reach to more than 60 countries.

Fiskars Brands, which sells its products through online and brick-and-mortar retailers, serves several sectors, such as schools, offices, crafting, gardening, outdoor recreation, and housewares.

Fiskars Brands keeps its company current by updating its products portfolio and operations.

In 2014 Fiskars Brands unit Gerber Legendary Blades agreed to pay a civil penalty of $2.6 million to settle allegations that it knowingly failed to immediately report to the US Consumer Product Safety Commission a safety hazard associated with Fiskars Brands' Gator Combo Axe. The company also agreed to establish and maintain a compliance program with internal recordkeeping and monitoring systems to keep track of information about product safety hazards.

In 2012 Fiskars Brands expanded its distribution channels by launching an online store for garden, school, office, and craft products.

The US company was founded in 1984. Parent Fiskars was established in Finland in 1649.
