Consumer paper products and bottled water prevail at First Quality Enterprises (FQE). A diversified group of about 10 companies, FQE manufactures and distributes adult incontinence products, baby diapers and training pants, tampons, and other consumer paper products (such as bath tissue and paper towels), as well as bottled water. Its brands include Prevail, Femtex, Opal, Cuties, First Quality, and Pureau, and the firm makes private-label goods for others. FQE distributes its products to institutions (such as long-term care and assisted living facilities), businesses, and retailers in the US and abroad. The company, established in 1988, is managed and owned by several generations of the founding Damaghi family.

New York-based First Quality Enterprises (FQE) has manufacturing plants in Pennsylvania and Georgia. The consumer goods maker's First Quality Nonwovens (Wuxi) Co. is located in Jiangsu Province, China and focuses on the Chinese and Asia-Pacific markets. FQE International's products are sold worldwide through distribution channels in Central and South America, the Caribbean, Europe, South East Asia, the Pacific Rim, and the Middle East.

First Quality Enterprises (FQE) manages and reports in two operating businesses: the Family Care Products business and the Rolled Goods business, with principal manufacturing operations located through the eastern half of the US and in China. Group companies include First Quality Tissue, First Quality Baby Products, First Quality Hygienic, and First Quality Water & Beverage, among others.

From its start as a maker of adult incontinence products, FQE has steadily expanded its product lines and operations over the years. As the population ages and demand for adult incontinence products grows, First Quality Tissue in 2014 announced the addition of two TAD paper machines with an estimated annual capacity of 140,000 tons, which are expected to come online in 2015 and 2016. In 2013 the tissue and bottled water company increased the manufacturing and warehousing capacity in its Absorbent Hygiene division in Lewistown, Pennsylvania. To quench a growing thirst, the company is adding another high-speed manufacturing line at its Lock Haven, Pennsylvania bottled water plant. The company in late 2010 began expanding its baby products manufacturing facilities in Georgia, and opened a tissue and towel manufacturing center in South Carolina in late 2011. The tissue and towel center is part of FQE's plan to invest $1 billion and create 1,000 jobs in Anderson County, South Carolina, by 2020. Each of FQE's businesses is managed separately but overseen by the Damaghis.

Amid tough economic times in the US and internationally, FQE faces unyielding competition from makers of name-brand consumer goods, such as Kimberly-Clark and Procter & Gamble . Many of its rivals have lowered their prices, eroding (and sometimes eliminating) the cost difference with store-brand products.
