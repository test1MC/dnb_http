Fulcrum Microsystems looks for leverage from clockless chips. The fabless semiconductor company designs networking switch chips using its proprietary asynchronous, or clockless, technology. Fulcrum claims that by dispensing with the internal clocking function found in most chips, its devices achieve higher performance and greater energy efficiency, for applications in computing, data storage, and networking. In 2003 the company licensed its Nexus terabit crossbar switch technology to PMC-Sierra . It has worked with such vendors as Ixia , Luxera, and Spirent Communications . Fulcrum was founded in early 2000 by Caltech engineers and was bought by Intel in 2011.
