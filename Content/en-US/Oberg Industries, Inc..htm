With a product range to die for, Oberg Industries manufactures high precision stamping dies and die components such as progressive dies, primary and secondary scroll dies, punches, and die inserts. The family-owned company pioneered the use of tungsten carbide components in the manufacture of high-speed stamping dies. Oberg also provides insert molding, design support, plastic injection, and custom designed automation equipment. It operates more than 70 stamping presses with capacities from 5 tons to 400 tons, and some 35 machining centers. The company serves such industries as automotive, aerospace, medical device, and consumer products.

The company has offices in the US and Germany, with international representatives in Australia, Brazil, Colombia, Denmark, France, Germany, Malaysia, Mexico, Spain, Sweden, and Thailand. It has manufacturing plants in the US, Costa Rica, and Mexico.

Oberg markets its products through distributors across the US and internationally.

The company focuses on increasing its global manufacturing capabilities, technologies, and capacity; boosting innovation in engineering and design; and reinvesting in its employees through training and improvement.

Oberg expanded its Costa Rica operations in 2012 to better serve customers (particularly in the medical device market) who are targeting Central and South America for growth.

The company was founded by Donald Oberg in 1948.
