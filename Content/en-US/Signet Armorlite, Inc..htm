Signet Armorlite does its best to help its customers to see clearly. The company makes plastic ophthalmic lenses and originated the plastic eyeglass lens in North America. Glass lenses and molds are made by its Crossbows Optical unit, located in Northern Ireland. It operates a lens technology center, Signetek, in California. Signet holds the exclusive rights to produce and distribute Kodak lenses worldwide and is an authorized distributor of 3M adhesive optical supply items in the US. Signet distributes lab supplies, such as blocking pads and lens protectors. The company is a subsidiary of French ophthalmic products maker and supplier Essilor International.

Signet has operations in the US, Canada, South America, Europe, and Asia.

Its glass lenses and molds are designed and produced by its Crossbows Optical unit, which is based in Northern Ireland. The company is an authorized distributor for 3M Optical Supplies in the US, and it also distributes Kodak products.

Signet sells its own products worldwide through distributors. The company distributes its products in Europe through two distribution centers and four optical laboratories in Germany, Spain, the Netherlands, and the UK.

North America accounted for 35% of parent (ophthalmic products maker and supplier) Essilor International 's 2013 revenue of some €5 billion ($7 billion).

Signet's growth strategy is based on innovation and product development to increase market share. As part of this strategy, in 2014 Signet introduced KODAK Unique HD Progressive Lenses that utilize point-of-wear measurements that allow the prescription to be highly customized to the patient's unique visual needs.

In September 2013 the company renewed a 17-year licensing agreement with Eastman Kodak Co . The company's partnership with Kodak dates to 1993.

Armorlite, founded in 1947 in Burbank, California, and Signet Uptown, which began as a wholesale lab in San Diego in 1960, merged their operations in 1983 to form Signet Armorlite.
