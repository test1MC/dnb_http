Inotera Memories makes semiconductor memory chips in two facilities in Taiwan. The company, founded in 2003, makes high-density and high-performance commodity DRAMs, which are used in PCs and consumer electronics. Inotera originally was a joint venture between Qimonda and Nanya Technology , and the three companies benefited from sharing technology. Nanya accounted for 50% of Inotera's sales, and Qimonda accounted for 40%. Qimonda's was sold to Micron Technology and in late 2015 Micron bought the 67% of Inotera that it didn't own from it joint-venture partner Nanya Technology Corp. for $4 billion.
