AMSEC helps keep the Navy ship-shape. The company supplies naval architecture and marine engineering services to the Navy and commercial maritime industry. AMSEC technicians provide design, engineering, logistics, technical support, waterfront maintenance support, training, C4I (command, control, communications, computers, and intelligence) installation and support services, and maintenance services for virtually any shipboard system on ships, from aircraft carriers to submarines. AMSEC has roughly 35 locations in the US and overseas and is a subsidiary of Huntington Ingalls Industries , one of the largest naval shipbuilders in the world.

AMSEC's facilities are located in Canada, Japan, the UK, and the US.

AMSEC's ship-systems services continue to concentrate on US Government customers (the US Navy and US Coast Guard ) under long-term contracts. Among its contract wins, AMSEC has teamed with several contractors to perform ship alteration and engineering support for long-time customer Norfolk Naval Shipyard; mechanical and electrical services for the Naval Surface Warfare Center's fleet; and, submarine maintenance, repair, and modernization support for Pearl Harbor facilities. These services encompass a full range of hull, mechanical, and electrical systems; command, control, and communication systems; information technology; and engineering and design.

AMSEC has an agreement with Hanjin Heavy Industries and Construction Philippines to collaborate in providing maintenance, repair, and logistics services to the Navy and other customers in the western Pacific region. The partnership allows AMSEC to benefit from Hanjin's state-of-the art shipyard at Subic Bay, Philippines.

The company supplies the US Navy, the US Coast Guard and the commercial maritime industry.

AMSEC is a part of the Newport News segment of Huntington Ingalls Industries ; that segment accounted for 61% of the parent company's revenues in 2013. The Newport News segment's product sales increased by $126 million in 2013 as a result of higher volumes in aircraft carriers and submarines.

In 2014 the company was awarded a contract to provide engineering, technical, repair, and logistics support to the US Navy's Carrier Engineering Maintenance Assist Team and Surface Ship Engineering Maintenance Assist Team programs.

In 2013 AMSEC secured a contract with the US Navy's Space and Naval Warfare Systems Command. The contract (with a specific focus on submarine and surface ship new construction) is in support of design, acquisition, production, integration, testing, installation and configuration management of certified C5ISR capabilities (command, control, communications, computers, combat systems, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance).

The company was founded in 1981.
