MaineToday Media, Inc. plans to be around tomorrow and the next day. The company formed in 2009 to keep four Maine newspapers in print -- the Portland Press Herald, the Maine Sunday Telegram , the Kennebec Journal , and the Morning Sentinel . Altogether the papers have a circulation of more than 225,000. With it came the papers' Web sites, niche print publications, and real estate (five offices, two parking garages, and a printing press). MaineToday Media bought the publications from Blethen Maine Newspapers , a subsidiary of The Seattle Times Company . The company was formed by Richard L. Connor, the publisher of The Times Leader in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania, with backing from HM Capital Partners .
