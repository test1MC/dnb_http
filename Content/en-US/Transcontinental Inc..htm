TC Transcontinental publishes from coast to coast. The company is the largest printer in Canada. It prints items such as books, catalogs, directories, consumer magazines, and other documents from more than 30 plants. TC Transcontinental also publishes newspapers and magazines, including sports ( The Hockey News ), consumer ( Canadian Living ), and business ( Les Affaires ) titles; offers multimedia production services; and delivers content through more than 250 websites. The family of company founder Remi Marcoux controls about 70% of TC Transcontinental's voting rights.

The company is headquartered in Montreal, Quebec. It has customers all over North America.

The company operates two segments: TC Media and TC Transcontinental Printing.

TC Media provides media and interactive marketing solutions in Canada including a wide range of print and digital publishing products in French and English: newspapers, educational books, consumer magazines, trade publications, retail promotional content, mass and personalized marketing, mobile and interactive applications, and through door-to-door and digital distribution services.

TC Transcontinental Printing is the largest printer in Canada and the third largest in North America.

The company's revenue decreased $2 billion in fiscal 2013 down to $1.8 billion in fiscal 2014. The drop primarily resulted from lower advertising revenues. TC Transcontinental posted a net income of $105.1 million in fiscal 2014. That was an imrpovement compared to the net loss of $23 million the company claimed in fiscal 2013). The improved net income picture was due to decreased operating expenses.

During fiscal 2014 TC Transcontinental's operating cash decreased to $335 million compared to $416 million in fiscal 2013.

TC Transcontinental is focused on developing its local news offerings. Meanwhile, the company has been shedding some of its less profitable assets. It sold its consumer magazines produced in Montreal and Toronto to TVA Group during 2015. In 2014 the company sold the assets of its Côte-Nord papers Le Plein Jour de Baie-Comeau, Le Port-Cartois (Port-Cartier) and Le Nord-Est (Sept-Îles) to Gestion PJNE inc.

TC Transcontinental made two strategic acquisitions during fiscal 2014. The company acquired the assets of Capri Packaging, a flexible packaging supplier in the US. TC Transcontinental also purchased 74 Quebec weekly papers owned by Sun Media Corporation.
