Océ (pronounced "oh-say") makes a wide range of imaging equipment. Its products include copiers, printers, imaging systems, and related supplies for the office, printing, and engineering markets. The company's key product lines are a series of low- to high-volume copiers, wide-format systems for commercial printers, and copiers and plotters designed specifically for engineering applications. It also provides consulting, document management, and support services. Océ primarily sells directly, but it does sell a small percentage of its products through distributors and original equipment manufacturers. In 2010 Tokyo-based rival Canon acquired the company for about €730 million (almost $1.1 billion) in cash.
