Pelican Products works to make "virtually indestructible" gear. The company designs and makes technically advanced flashlights and high-impact watertight cases. Its plastic- and metal-encased flashlights are sold in the US through specialty retailers, such as Grainger and REI . The military is Pelican's top customer and uses cases to secure electronics, medical supplies, and weapons. Pelican operates through some 20 countries and boasts more than five manufacturing plants worldwide. Its Peli Products unit does business in Africa, Europe, and the Middle East. The company was founded by husband and wife team Dave and Arline Parker in 1976.

To maintain its capacity, Pelican Products operates seven manufacturing plants globally. The company operates in Africa, Europe, and the Middle East through its Peli Products unit. It also counts India among its operations after having opened its first office in New Delhi in 2011 to serve this emerging economic region.

California-based Pelican Products operates its business in roughly 20 countries, including Australia, Canada, China, Spain, Japan, Korea, Portugal, Singapore, India, and the US. The company maintains 28 offices and more than five manufacturing facilities worldwide.

In the US, Pelican Products sells its items through specialty retailers. With its global reach and deep pockets, the military represents the company's top customer.

New products and acquisitions are key to Pelican's growth strategy. In 2017 it introduced its new Pelican 20QT Elite cooler, a bear-proof certified container that features an injection-molded construction and decreases the weight by up to 30% from previous models.

Former acquisition Pelican BioThermal (formerly Minnesota Thermal Science) provides thermal protection packaging used for shipping products including vaccines, tissue samples, blood supplies, clinical trials, and diagnostics. In 2016 Pelican BioThermal announced it is expanding its manufacturing capabilities, growing from 58,000 sq. ft. to 72,000 sq. ft. The expansion will double the company's manufacturing capacity in Plymouth, Minnesota.
