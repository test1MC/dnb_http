TEL NEXX (formerly NEXX Systems) knows that good things come in small packages. The company designs and sells equipment used in the packaging phase of semiconductor manufacturing. (Semiconductor wafers are packaged before they are shipped off for installation in electronic devices.) TEL NEXX produces three packaging systems -- the Apollo, Stratus, and Nimbus -- to create the next generation of semiconductors used in smart phones and other electronic devices. The company's systems cost up to $3 million, and the majority of its customers are in Asia. NEXX Systems filed to go public in 2010, then was acquired by Tokyo Electron in 2012 and changed its name.
