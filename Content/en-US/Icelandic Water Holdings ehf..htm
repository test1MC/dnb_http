The name alone makes Iceland seem like an ideal place to find cold, refreshing drinking water. Perhaps that's part of the reason why Icelandic Water Holdings, producer of Icelandic Glacial bottled water, uses Iceland's underground Ölfus Spring as its primary source. The company's bottling facility, which is situated next to the spring, can roll out up to 30,000 bottles per hour. The firm exports its bottled water to North America and the UK via third-party distributors, selling most products in US grocery chains like Safeway , Kroger , and Publix . A privately owned company, Icelandic was founded in 2004. A-B InBev owns a 20% stake; South Africa's Bidvest agreed to buy a nearly 15% share in 2011.
