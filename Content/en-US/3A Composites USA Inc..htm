3A Composites USA is a big supporter of the arts; graphic arts, that is. A manufacturer of lightweight composite materials, the company makes aluminum composite panels and extrusions, heavy-duty foam boards, and PVC boards used to create 2-D and 3-D graphic arts signs and displays (i.e. kiosks, large decorative props displayed at events, detailed replicas of statues, buildings, and other structures). It sells its products under the FiberMate, Fome-Cor, Sintra, Gator, Dibond, and Profiles brands and distributes its products across the Americas. 3A Composites USA is a subsidiary of 3A Composites which is owned by Schweiter Technologies .

The company makes substrates for the graphic display market in Central America (including the Caribbean), North America, and South America. It has manufacturing facilities in Benton and Glasgow, (in Kentucky) and a manufacturing plant and sales and marketing offices in Statesville, North Carolina.

3A Composites USA's products are sold to fabrication and distribution partners. Its leading brands and broad product range give it access to the leading distribution organizations in each of its markets. In some cases, such as the wind power sector, products are supplied directly to OEMs. Other customers include advertisers, event organizers, and retailers.

The company sells its products through distributors such as Mac Papers , Piedmont Plastics, and N. Glantz & Son.

3A Composites contributed about 90% of its parent company's total revenues in 2012 (about 29% of which came from its American operations). 3A Composites' revenues decreased by 3% due to the unfavorable global economic environment, but revenues derived from the American segment increased by 5%.

The company pursues market expansion through product innovation, although moves to expand existing retail sales areas with new display products made only sluggish headway due to low US GDP growth.

In 2014 3A Composites USA launched the SMART-X graphic display board, the first heavy-duty 100% recyclable foam board available in the Americas.

That year 3A Composites USA introduced FiberMat recyclable rigid print media (wood fiber manufactured into a high-density fiberboard) at the 2013 ISA International Sign Expo. It also launched the Fome-Cor Bright White graphic display board (the brightest whitest surface available to create graphics) at the Specialty Printing and Imaging 2013 SGIA Expo.

Consolidating its team of business managers, marketing, sales, and administrative functions from the graphic display, architectural, construction, and industry and transportation market segments, in 2012 the company moved its administrative headquarters from Mooresville, North Carolina to its existing facilities in Statesville, North Carolina.

3A Composites' name refers to three powerful company brands which preceded it: Alcan, Alusuisse, and Airex.
