Whether or not you are commuting or on a tour, you might have ridden in a coach model bus manufactured by Motor Coach Industries International (MCI). MCI is one of North America's leading manufacturers of intercity coaches serving charter and tour operators; line-haul and scheduled-service operators; transit agencies; and conversion companies. The company's luxury buses are up to 45-feet long and offer such comforts as full kitchens and steam showers. MCI also offers 24-hour technical support and repair services; it stocks more than 40,000 parts. The company was acquired by New Flyer Industries in 2015 for $455 million.

MCI operates seven sales and eight service centers in the US and Canada. It operates manufacturing plants in Winnipeg, Manitoba, and Pembina, North Dakota.

The company manufactures its various coach models to fit the needs of its passengers. Luxury coaches can be outfitted with LED lamps, panoramic windows, WiFi system, LCD wide-screen monitors, and a conversion coach shell. On the other side of the legal fence, the MCI D Series, J4500, and ISTV models are designed for use by US and Canadian federal agencies. Options include containment barriers and cells, shatter resistant windows with bars, sirens, weapons lockers, and bullet resistant material. In addition to making buses, the company is a leading supplier of aftermarket parts for most bus models through its MCI PRO support segment.

MCI's 2015 purchase by New Flyer Industries will give it access to a larger network of resources and cash to help it take advantage of the increase in leisure and travel spending in the US and Canada.

MCI was founded in 1933 as Fort Garry Motor Body and Paint.
