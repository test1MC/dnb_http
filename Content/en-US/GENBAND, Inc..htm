GENBAND ensures a smooth migration to next-generation networks. The company provides software and hardware that 600 telecommunications providers use to deploy voice and multimedia services. Designed for fixed, wireless, and cable operators, its products include network function virtualization software, media gateways, call controllers, and application servers. Communications service providers such as PAETEC and Axtel use GENBAND's products to build and upgrade their networks. Its chief offerings include its Kandy software platform, Nuvia Cloud uniform communications systems, and video conferencing. The company serves a customer base spanning more than 80 countries.

GENBAND markets its products directly, as well as through a global network of channel partners that includes DV Consulting and Belam in Europe, Baud Telecom Company and Emircom in Middle East and Africa, Anixter International and Eastern Independent Telecommunications Ltd. in Americas and ASAFF and Dawn in Asia-Pacific.

GENBAND’s product lineup has shifted in tune with the networking technology industry to emphasize software products that manage many functions previously controlled by hardware switches. The company’s Kandy platform for network function visualization (NFV) allows customers to make network upgrades with software and thus reduce spending on buying and installing hardware. The company also has moved into cloud computing-related products and services for customers who want mobile and web applications to include add voice, video, messaging, and collaboration capabilities.

In 2016, the company fended off a patent infringement suit brought by Metaswitch.

The company maintains partnerships with companies that use GENBAND software and hardware and resell them to their customers. Among GENBAND’s partner companies are F5 Networks, Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Enghouse Interactive, and Samsung.
