It was in Bordeaux, France, in 1755 that a certain Marie Brizard discovered a recipe for anisette, a licorice-flavored liqueur. Over the centuries the Marie Brizard brand has been known for its exotic liqueurs that come in such flavors as wild strawberry, blackberry, Madagascar vanilla, and banana cream. Marie Brizard et Roget International also produces spirit brands Cognac Gautier, San José tequila, and Old Lady's gin; as well as nine brands of wine, three brands of vodka, and three brands of whisky. The company sells its products in more than 120 countries. Marie Brizard is owned by the ailing French distiller Belvédère , which is selling assets (including Marie Brizard) to reduce debt and exit bankruptcy.
