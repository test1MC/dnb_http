From Alphabet to Z, Groupe Zannier wants to keep French kids in fashion. Zannier designs and sells children's apparel under a dozen brands, including 3 Pommes, Alphabet, Z generation, DIM, Catimini, Kenzo, and Absorba. The company operates in more than 120 countries worldwide, though France provides about half its sales. While clothing represents more than 90% of revenues, Zannier also sells shoes and watches and licenses its brands for other products. The company distributes through retailers, wholesalers, and its own network of 900 stores (company-owned, franchised, or affiliated).

The country has a presence in 120 countries, operating in more than 900 stores around the world including over 500 in France. The Zannier Group is also organized through subsidiaries based in Belgium, Spain, the US, the UK, Italy, and Portugal; key locations reside at the epicenter of the great fashion capitals of Milan, New York, Barcelona, and London.

Siblings Roger and Josette Zannier started a small clothing manufacturing business in 1962 to serve local shops in France's Loire region. Within a few years they started serving French clothing wholesalers and were specializing in children's apparel. By 1976 the Zanniers had expanded their operations to include sales to hypermarkets.
