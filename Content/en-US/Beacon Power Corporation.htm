Beacon Power beckons companies seeking backup power. The development-stage company's flywheel energy storage systems provide uninterruptible electric power for communications networks, computers, industrial manufacturing, and other power generation applications. Beacon Power's flywheel systems draw electrical energy from a power source, such as an electric power grid or a fuel cell, and then store it. The power can then be delivered as needed when a primary energy source either fails or is disrupted. The company also makes photovoltaic power conversion systems (solar inverters). Beacon Power entered Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in late 2011 and was acquired by private equity firm Rockland Capital in 2012.

In 2005 the company agreed to acquire NxtPhase T&D, a Canadian corporation. The companies were unable to consummate the stock-swap transaction by the end of the year and called the deal off, with Beacon paying NxtPhase a fee of $150,000 to be relieved of any possible liabilities.

In 2003 Beacon Power expanded its energy storage product lines through the acquisition of Advanced Energy Systems, a maker of solar power inverters. Beacon Power has launched a line of solar power products for electronic applications.
