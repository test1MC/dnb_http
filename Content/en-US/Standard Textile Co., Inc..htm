Meeting industry standards is not enough for Standard Textile. The company designs, manufactures, and distributes decorative and workwear textiles for health care, hospitality, and institutional use. Health care products include bedding, surgical gowns, and incontinence care items. Hospitality goods include custom towels, blankets, and woven sheeting. Standard Textile's workwear line offers coveralls and sport jackets. Bedspreads, flame-resistant drapery, and window treatments are part of its decorative products mix. The company, which has more than 70 patents, manages its own R&D, manufacturing, distribution, and technical services. The privately held company is led by its founder's grandson, Gary Heiman.

Standard Textile markets its products to countries worldwide. The company has established almost 25 plants and distribution centers in 12 countries in North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia. Its largest manufacturing plant is near Shanghai and produces 15% of the company's fabric.

The company conducts its European operations in more than 10 countries, and has offices in Belgium, China, France, Germany, Israel, Italy, Spain, and the UK. Its Arad Towels subsidiary is prominent in Israel's textile industry, producing 4.5 million towels per month for hotels, medical centers, nursing homes, and the retail market.

The company serves customers across across the healthcare, hospitality, decorative products and industrial apparel segments, through a global network of distribution locations and sales offices.

Standard Textile's emphasis on Research and Development gives it an edge in the institutional textile industry. To further accelerate innovation, in 2013 the company began construction on Phase I of an Innovation Center.

To incorporate new innovations in terry towel finishing, in 2014 it also expanded its existing manufacturing operations in Union County, South Carolina. With an investment of $5 million, the expanded plant will facilitate new partnerships and opportunities in Standard Textile's yarn spinning, yarn preparation, and weaving processes.

The company often partners with other companies to extend its reach to other markets. In 2012 Standard Textile teamed up with SK Textile (a custom fabricator of drapery, bedding and accessories), to open a new manufacturing plant in Las Vegas, Nevada. That year it extended its partnership with SK Textile to promote Standard Textile's Circa Modular Bedding System at the International Hotel, Motel & Restaurant Show and the Boutique Design shows in New York.

In 2011 Standard Textile and Diversey , a major provider of commercial cleaning, sanitation and hygiene services, formed a joint venture to serve the lodging and health care industries. The joint venture, Proteus Solutions, offers a laundry system marketed under the brand Proteus Activator Washing System.

Standard Textile got its start in 1940. It has been under the control of the Heiman family since its inception.
