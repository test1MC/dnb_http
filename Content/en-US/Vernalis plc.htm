No, no, no, not "Frodo." It's pronounced "Frova," and though it's not a fictional hobbit, it is magical for those suffering from migraines. Manufactured by Vernalis, Frova is sold by Endo International to treat migraines. Frova is Vernalis' only marketed drug, but the development company has a pipeline full of candidates focused on treatments for disorders of the central nervous system, oncology, and other ailments. Vernalis operates by performing early stage research and development and then partnering with other pharmaceutical companies to help usher the candidate to market. Some of Vernalis' partners include Servier , GlaxoSmithKline , and Novartis . Vernalis counts on royalties from Frova and other licensing fees for its income.
