Schlage was the name behind premium mechanical and electronic security locks since 1927. The company supplied both residential and commercial markets, including schools, hospitals, hotels, airports, and homeowners, in North America and parts of South America. In addition to door knobs and deadbolts, Schlage offered card readers, biometric access-control and video systems, as well as devices that enable cell phones and PCs to remotely unlock doors and manage home functions. Many products were sold through Home Depot , Lowe's , and other retailers. Schlage was integrated into industrial products manufacturer Ingersoll-Rand .
