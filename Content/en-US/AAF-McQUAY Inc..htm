For a cooler way, go with AAF-McQuay. Part of Daikin Industries , the company makes commercial air-conditioning and air-filtration products and systems for office buildings, schools, and manufacturing plants. Its AAF International division makes air-filtration products under the AAF and American AirFilter brands, while its J&E Hall brand manufactures and sells refrigeration products. Daikin Applied (formerly McQuay International) manufactures efficient HVAC systems and sells chillers, air handlers, heat pumps and other climate-related systems and parts. Altogether, AAF-McQuay boasts more than 50 subsidiaries across the Americas, Europe, and the Middle East, with production facilities in nine countries.

Under the Daikin brand, AAF-McQuay also manufactures, imports, and sells fluoropolymers and other chemicals to industrial customers across several sectors, including those in the automotive, electronics, and textile industries.

Based in Kentucky, the company and its 50 subsidiaries operate across the Americas, Europe, and the Middle East with production facilities in nine countries (including Taiwan, China, Malaysia, and India). Its parent company, Daikin Industries , serves more than 75 countries.

AAF-McQUAY serves customers in the institutional, industrial, and commercial markets.

Daikin has big ambitions to become the top air conditioning, heating, and ventilation company in the world. AAF-McQUAY is part of its parent's larger goals of doubling sales by 2015. In order to get there, the company has made investments to improve facilities and drive innovation and sales. In late 2014, Daikin announced plans to push its Goodman brand (acquired in 2012) to expand sales in North America, utilizing Goodman's sales network to introduce Daikin's superior-efficiency ductless air conditioners.

To cut manufacturing costs, the company in 2013 announced a restructuring effort to increase high-end product manufacturing operations and make its supply chain operations more efficient. The program included the construction of a new R&D center near its headquarters, as well as the closure of an Indiana manufacturing plant (balanced by expansions at other regional plants).

In mid-2013, the company's AAF International division began growing its Asian filtration business by investing in facility expansion efforts in China, India, Malaysia, Singapore, and Taiwan.

Reflecting its focus on production growth in Asia, AAF acquired Thailand-based filter distributor Chaimitr Engineering in 2013. The purchase established AAF as a leading filter provider in the country and other areas of southeast Asia.
