JELD-WEN's business strategy is open and shut. A leading maker of windows and doors, the company offers aluminum, vinyl, and wood windows; interior and exterior doors; garage doors; swinging and sliding patio doors; and door frames and moldings. Flagship brands include FiberLast exterior doors and Aurora patio doors. Tracing its roots to 1960, the company operates more than 90 manufacturing and distribution sites in some 20 countries, mainly in the Americas, Asia, Europe, and Australia. Canadian private equity firm Onex owns 81% of the company.

The company's door manufacturing locations reside in Australia, Asia, Canada, and Europe; window manufacturing plants are located in Australia, Canada, and the UK. It also has a distribution facility in France. North America represents its largest market, but other major markets include Europe and the Asia/Pacific.

JELD-WEN's products are sold globally through distribution channels such as retail home centers, wholesale distributors, and building products dealers.

JELD-WEN represents Onex's Building Products division, which accounted for 18% of Onex's revenues for 2014.

Former chairman Richard Wendt and his siblings founded JELD-WEN in 1960. The name JELD-WEN is an acronym created from the initials of the company's founder's parents and sister. The Wendt family and other JELD-WEN employees continue to own a stake in the company following its 2011 investment from Onex.
