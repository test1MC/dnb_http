SCA Tissue North America aims to wipe out the competition. The company produces paper products for washroom, tabletop, tissue, personal care, and health care facility applications. It also makes wiper and soap dispensers for industrial use. It is one of the largest producers of AFH (away-from home) tissue products in North America, supplying restaurants, schools, and convenience stores, Its foodservice products include napkins (under the Tork brand), paper towels, and dispensers. Other products stem from its packaging and forest products divisions. SCA Tissue is a unit of Sweden-based Svenska Cellulosa Aktiebolaget (SCA), one of Europe's leading tissue products providers.

Based in Neenah, Wisconsin, the company has a dozen locations along with its sales force in North America. SCA Tissue's network includes manufacturing, administrative, and sales operations in Canada, Mexico, Central and South America, and the US.

In the face of a often-slow economy, SCA Tissue is relying on the staying power of its customers purchasing "green" products and services. The company offers an assortment of branded items featuring 100% recycled paper; it is the first American papermaker to earn an environmental certification for its processes and products. Simultaneously, SCA Tissue looks to expand its use of lower-cost recycled paper for tissue manufacturing.

SCA Tissue goes head-to-head with its paper rivals in marketing convenient, high-performance products. The effort is accompanied by a new structure for all products launched under the Tork brand lineup. Continuing to innovate, SCA Tissue rolled out a color-coded cloth touted to improve hygiene programs and reduce the risk of contamination, proving useful for the foodservice sector. Additionally, it is introducing a line of products that bridge the feminine and incontinence care market.

SCA Tissue sells its products under the global brands TENA and Tork. Regional brands include Lotus, Libresse, Tempo, and Libero. It serves foodservice and hospitality, health care, industrial, office, education, transportation, recreation and event center, and retail businesses, as well as janitorial/sanitary companies and the automotive industry. The company sells its products through distributors.
