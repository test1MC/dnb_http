Kyocera Communications (formerly Kyocera Wireless) keeps the kinetic connected. The company is a supplier of mobile phones that use the CDMA (Code Division Multiple Access) mode, as well as GSM (global standard for mobile communications). In addition to mobile handsets sold under the Kyocera and Sanyo brands, it makes a range of other accessories including wireless network cards for PC's, cell phone batteries, and headsets. Clients have included wireless service providers Cricket and Sprint . San Diego-based parent company Kyocera International is the holding company for the North American operations of Kyocera Corporation , the Kyoto, Japan-based manufacturer of technical ceramics and electronic components.
