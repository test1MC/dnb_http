The Alibaba Group is a treasure trove of e-commerce websites in Asia. The holding company owns Alibaba.com , a leading online marketplace for importers and exporters in China and Japan. The Alibaba Group also wholly owns Alibaba Cloud Computing, which supports its technology platform; Taobao and Taobao Mall, China's largest online retail websites; and Web portal China Yahoo! Altogether, Alibaba Group websites count millions of registered users. The group was founded in 1999 by Jack Ma and is majority-owned by Softbank (30%) and Yahoo! (20%). It went public in 2014. Alibaba Group acquired Chinese online video provider Youku Tudou for $4.6 billion in 2016.

Alibaba Group raised about $21.8 billion through its initial public offering. The company was given a market valuation of $168 billion. About 60% of the proceeds went to company executives and early investors.

As China's biggest e-commerce player, Alibaba charges advertising fees to merchants looking to promote themselves.

Alibaba is more profitable than many of its American Internet counterparts. The company generates strong revenue with large operating profit margins.

Alibaba has made itself the place for online commerce in China. It offers the services of eBay , PayPal , Amazon , and more under one umbrella.

To continue spreading its umbrella, the company acquired Youku Tudou , for about $4.6 billion in 2016.
