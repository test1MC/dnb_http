Robertet bottles eau de profit and essences of jasmine and ginger. The French company makes fragrance and flavor ingredients for use in the food, perfume, and aromatic-product industries. They are used around the world in perfumes, essential oils, and cleaning products, as well as for flavorings by food and beverage companies. Robertet creates traditional and new products. It develops new extraction methods and tries to spot and develop trends in areas like perfumery, aromatherapy, and aromachology (the study of the effects of smells on human behavior). Robertet has operations in Asia, North and Latin America, Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. The Maubert family owns a controlling interest in Robertet.

The company operates three business segments: natural raw materials, perfume compositions, and food flavorings. The three segments produce for example, essential oils, cleaning products and flavous for the beverage and food industries

It has industrial facilities in more than 11 countries, comprising production, R&D, and design centers.

The company is based in Grasse, Alpes Maritimes, France, and has factories in France (Grasse), Turkey (Senir), South Africa (Rustenburg) and Belgium (Antwerp).

The company serves across more than 50 countries in the world's main business regions.

Robertet's markets are the beverage, dairy, sweet (confectionery, bakery, etc), pharmacy and culinary markets.

Net sales increased by around 12% to €435 million, from €390 million in prior year. Net income in 2015 was in the region of €36 million, and increase of 22% and outperforming expectations.

The Group's recent acquisition of SAPAD attests to its investment in organic products.
