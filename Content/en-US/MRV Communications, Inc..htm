MRV Communications puts the buzz in optical communications. MRV supplies the switching, routing, Ethernet, optical transport, and console management equipment used in voice, data, and video traffic by telecommunications carriers, data centers, and labs. It also provides network system design services, as well as integrated network products and services. It sells to cable operators, networking services providers, Internet and telecom companies, and governments worldwide. About two-thirds of sales are outside the US, mostly in Europe.

MRV Communications' sales are evenly divided between the network equipment segment (optical networking and Internet infrastructure products) and the network integration (value-added integration and support) segment.

MRV Communications has its primary US operations in California and Massachusetts; its primary overseas offices are located in Italy and Israel. The company has additional locations in Argentina, Australia, Germany, the Netherlands, Russia, Thailand, the Philippines, Poland, Taiwan, and the UK. In 2014 the largest markets were Europe (more than 60% of revenue) and the US (30% of sales).

MRV Communications has a global sales and marketing organization that includes 90 sales, tech support, and management professionals. The company has field sales offices in a dozen countries that provide system sales and installation and support and follow-up services to end users. MRV's customers FastWeb S.p.A. and Telecom Italia S.p.A. accounted for 24% and 16% of revenue.

The company reported $1.2 million in advertising and trade show expenses in 2014.

MRV Communications posted $172 million in revenue, a 4% gain, in 2014 from 2013. The revenue boost came from the Network Integration segment, in which sales jumped 13% on the strength of its Tecnonet unit. Network Equipment sales dropped 4% in 2014 from 2013. The company recorded a loss of $12.16 million in 2014, almost doubling up on its loss in 2013. The company had higher operating costs and taxes in 2014. The outflow of cash slowed to $1.4 million in 2014 from $4.1 million in 2013.

MRV has completed a number of asset divestitures in recent years to streamline its operations. It continued in 2015 with the sale of Tecnonet, its Network Integration subsidiary based in Rome, to Maticmind for about $21 million. MRV had begun investing in Tecnonet in 1998 and upped its stake in the unit in subsequent years.

Competition in the networking equipment markets is intense, with MRV going up against such heavyweights as Alcatel-Lucent , Cisco Systems , Huawei Technologies , and NEC . The company must continually develop, introduce, and market new products to remain competitive. Product development and engineering expenses totaled about $21 million in 2014.
