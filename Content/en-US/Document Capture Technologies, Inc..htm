Like The Lorax , Document Capture Technologies speaks for the trees. The company makes digital scanners used to upload paper documents into electronic data. Its line of USB-powered portable image scanners, sold under the TravelScan and DocketPORT brand names, are used in bank note and check verification devices, ID card and passport scanners, barcode scanners, and business card readers. A handful of customers, including Brother Industries , NCR , and Newell Rubbermaid account for more than half of sales. Subsidiary Syscan, Inc. develops contact image sensor (CIS) modules used in fax machines and scanners. Investor Richard Dietl owns more than 35% of the company; Hong Kong-based Syscan Imaging Ltd. holds around 15%.

Once known as BankEngine Technologies, the company provided online transaction processing services. BankEngine exited the brutally competitive telecom industry in 2003 and acquired optical imaging technology company Syscan the next year. The company changed its name to Syscan Imaging following the acquisition and became Sysview Technology in 2006.

The company was developing HDTV products using liquid crystal on silicon (LCoS) rear-projection display technology. In 2005 it expanded the business through its acquisition of Nanodisplay, a firm developing an LCoS chip utilizing carbon nanotube technology. In late 2007, however, Document Capture Technologies terminated its R&D activities in high-definition displays and laid off the staff working on that effort.
