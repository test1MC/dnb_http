INOVYN ChlorVinyls Limited is one of world's top makers of chlorine-based chemicals and Europe's largest PVC maker. The company operates its business through the trade name INOVYN. A major chlor-alkali producer in Europe, the company produces chemicals for products such as detergents, diapers, nylon seat belts, PVC pipes, and pharmaceuticals. It is also a top producer of chlorinated paraffins and has the world's largest trichloroethylene plant. Its PVC products include ethylene dichloride (EDC) and vinyl chloride monomer (VCM). Brands include Cereclor, Methoklone, Pevikon, Norvinyl, and Solvin. The company is wholly owned by INEOS group.

The company has four product groups: Organic Chlorine Derivatives, Chlor Alkali, General Purpose Vinyls and Specialty Vinyls. Annual production volumes are in excess of 40 million tons.

Organic Chlorine Derivatives mainly works in the production of 1,2,3 trichloropropane, for use as a chemical intermediate in the production of crop protection and adhesives. The chlorine produced by the Chlor Alkali division is used as an intermediate in the manufacture of vehicle parts, including fabrics, electronics, glass and safety equipment. General Purpose Vinyls produces vinyl under the Norvinyl and Solvin brands, which have numerous applications, including in blister packs, credit and ID cards, bottles for household chemicals, and drainage pipes. Specialty Vinyls makes products for automobiles, furniture and the fashion industry, including artificial leathers, dashboards and printing inks.

The company has assets across 18 sites in 10 countries over Europe, including Belgium, France, Germany, Italy, Norway, Spain, Sweden and the UK. INOVYN has manufacturing, sales and marketing operations in ten countries across Europe

The company's products are used in industries such as automotive, building and construction, paints and adhesives, healthcare and medical, personal care, pulp and paper, textiles, and water treatment.

The company has turnover of more than €3.5 billion.

In response to poor European demand, the company has updated its chloromethanes operations strategy. In 2016, production will be focused on INOVYN's chloromethanes assets in Tavaux (France) and Rosignano (Italy), which will result in closure of the company's third chloromethanes asset in Runcorn (UK).

In 2015, the company planned major investment in a large scale potassium hydroxide (KOH) production facility at its Antwerp/Lillo site, Belgium, which consists of a 160kT KOH plant using the latest technology and will be on line in 2017.

The company divested the chlorine plants and EDC/ VCM plants at Tessenderlo, Belgium (excluding the chlorotoluenes business that will remain with INEOS); the PVC plant at Mazingarbe, France; the PVC plant at Beek, the Netherlands; the PVC and VCM plants at Wilhelmshaven, Germany; and the EDC plants at Runcorn, UK. In addition, the membrane chlorine plant at Runcorn was placed in a 50-50 Joint Venture between INOVYN and International Chemical Investors Group (ICIG), a privately held industrial company.

Parent company INEOS bought out Solvay's 50% stake in INOVYN for €335 million. Solvay's exit from the business was initially planned for July 2018.
