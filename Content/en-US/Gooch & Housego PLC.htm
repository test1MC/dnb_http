Business at Gooch & Housego (G&H) requires a touch of light, it not a light touch. The company's products are designed primarily for use in light measurement instrumentation and crystal manufacturing. It makes precision optics, such as coatings, lenses, and prisms. G&H was founded in 1948 to make precision glass and crystalline materials. G&H also offers acousto-optic devices, such as frequency shifters, tunable filters, and fiber-coupled modulator. The company does much of its business in North America.

The industrial business segment is G&H's biggest money maker, accounting for 59% of revenue. The unit makes lasers for the semiconductor and microelectronic industries and metrology and telecommunications.

The Aerospace and Defense unit brings in a quarter of the company's revenue. It transfers fiber optic and semiconductor laser technology to uses in defense applications.

G&H's Life Sciences division products, 11% of revenue include Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) for retinal imaging to detect glaucoma and macular degeneration, and laser surgery, prostate surgery, scar correction, and cataract surgery.

Scientific Research segment, which covers academic and government funded research, accounts for 5% of sales.

G&H's geographic markets and percentage of revenue they account for are: the US, 45%, Europe, 21%, the UK, 19%, and Asia-Pacific, 15%.

In 2015 increased 12% compared to 2014 on higher life science sales, up 22%. The unit benefited from sales of electro-optic products for the laser surgery market and of fiber optic assemblies for OCT. Revenue retreated in the Scientific Research unit because of softness in the general research market

The revenue increase helped drive net income 38% higher in 2015 from 2014.

G&H is moving and expanding its Silicon Valley location, its base for acousto-optic R&D, design and device manufacture. It moves from Palo Alto to a Fremont that's 50% larger. The space can accommodate customer growth and absorb operations from G&H's closed Melbourne, Florida facility.

In Cleveland, G&H is significantly renovating its electro-optic device and crystal-based precision optics manufacturing plant. The renovation is to improve process flow and product quality (using Lean manufacturing and continuous improvement techniques), as well as increase capacity.
