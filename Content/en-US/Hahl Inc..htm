Fishermen, seamstresses, and landscapers are among those who can put Hahl's products to use. The company, the US subsidiary of Lenzing 's plastics unit, makes extruded monofilaments, made from nylon, polyester, and other engineered resins, that have varied uses such as fishing line, lawn-trimmer line, and sewing thread. Hahl's manufacturing facility is located in South Carolina. Austrian plastics and fibers company Lenzing acquired the company, formerly called Glassmaster, out of Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in 2007. Hahl now operates as a much-stripped-down company, forgoing many of its former operations that included automation controls and composite materials.
