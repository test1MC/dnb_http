Innophos Holdings adds a dash of its phosphate products to food, beverages, toothpaste, detergents, and asphalt. Innophos manufactures specialty phosphates used in consumer products, pharmaceuticals, and industrial applications. Customers use the company's phosphates to improve the quality and performance of a broad range of products, from electronics and textiles to pharmaceuticals, water, and detergents. Innophos divides its business into three segments: specialty salts and specialty acids; purified phosphoric acid; and granular triple super phosphates (GSTP), a fertilizer.

Although North America does account for about three-quarters of its sales, Innophos sells globally. The company has manufacturing plants in the US (Nashville, TN; Chicago Heights, IL; Chicago (Waterway), IL; Geismar, LA; Ogden, UT; North Salt Lake, UT; Paterson, NJ; and Green Pond, SC); Canada (Port Maitland, ON); China (Taicang); and Mexico (Coatzacoalcos, Veracruz, and San Jose de Iturbide, Guanajuato).

Innophos ranks #1 in North America in specialty salts and specialty acids (ahead of ICL Performance Products ) and purified phosphoric acid (ahead of PCS Phosphate ).

The company's customers include consumer goods manufacturers, distributors, and specialty chemical manufacturers, including major consumer goods manufacturers in the food, beverage, cleaning products, and pharmaceuticals markets.

The company's revenues grew by 6% in 2012 thanks to higher selling prices increases. Specialty Phosphates revenues went up 5.1%, partially offset by lower pricing in GSTP and other non-specialty phosphate products, with fertilizer market prices well below 2011 levels. Product volumes increased 3.5% due to the effects of acquisitions in the Specialty Phosphates segment. Higher fertilizer sales were also a factor.

Innophos reported net income of $74.2 million in 2012 (14% down on 2011) as the result of increased costs of goods sold.

The company grows both through acquisitions and organically.

In 2012 the company bought New York-based Triarco Industries for $46 million. Triarco makes custom ingredients for the food, beverage, and dietary supplement industries, specializing in botanical and enzyme based ingredients that provide important nutritional benefits and are often formulated with specialty phosphates and bioactive minerals.

To complement its Kelatron business, Innophos acquired Utah-based AMT Labs in 2012 for $27 million. AMT specializes in mineral ingredients essential to the human diet that it manufactures in various forms (including chelates) to be easily digested. With the combination of AMT and Kelatron, Innophos became a leading US producer of high-growth bioactive mineral ingredients.

Growing its footprint in Asia, in 2011 the company formed a new subsidiary in China, Innophos (Taicang) Food Ingredients Manufacturing Co., Ltd.

In 2011 the company acquired Utah-based Kelatron, which makes specialty bioactive mineral nutrients for nutritional and dietary supplement producers. Innophos expects Kelatron's micronutrient products to complement its own range of macronutrient products. The company has been targeting acquisitions that add specialty ingredient technologies to complement its own offerings.

Bain Capital took Innophos public in 2006. The private equity group paid down some of the company's debt and gave itself a healthy dividend with the proceeds from the offering.

The company was formed in 2004 when Bain Capital bought Rhodia 's North American specialty phosphates business.
