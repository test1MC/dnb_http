Retrotech is an engineering firm that designs, makes, installs, and modernizes stand-alone and integrated automated storage and retrieval systems (AS/RS) for distribution centers, plants, and warehouses. Its products include automated guided vehicles, automated racking equipment, overhead cranes and hoists, stacker cranes, and warehouse management software. Retrotech owns Woodson Company, a food and beverage AS/RS software and services provider. Customers have included such big names as Proctor & Gamble and Cargill Meats . Founded in 1985, Retrotech specializes in automated pallet handling systems. It provides consulting, maintenance, system design, and technical support services.

More competition has appeared as European and Asian suppliers are joining the AS/RS scene. Retrotech considers it a competitive advantage that it is not a manufacturer, but a supplier of automated systems. Since customers are not bound to the company's proprietary machinery or software; rather, Retrotech can objectively recommend the appropriate equipment for the customer's needs. Additionally, the company does not have to worry about the high overhead of operating a manufacturing facility.

Retrotech is focusing on expanding its footprint in automation products and services. In 2014 it broke ground on a $5.5 million construction of a large technical facility in Henrietta, New York. The larger location will allow the company to further expand its workforce and accommodate the increasing needs of its customers.
