Grupo Simec gives new life to scrap steel. It makes non-flat, special bar quality (SBQ) steel, and structural steel products at its minimills in Guadalajara and Mexicali, Mexico as well as plants in the US. The company's billets are used to manufacture angles, channels, flat bar, I-beams, rebar, and structural angles. Subsidiary Procarsa makes electric resistance welded (ERW) steel pipes and other tubular products. Simec's customers include construction contractors, metal fabricators, and steel service centers. It sells its steel products throughout Mexico and exports to the US. Canada, Central and South America, and Europe are also buyers of Simec's steel products. Industrias CH owns around 85% of the company.

Simec is a producer of special steels in Mexico and the US, as well as in the rebar, merchant, and structural shapes in Mexico. In the US and Mexico Simec owns and operates 14 manufacturing facilities with a combined annual crude steel installed production capacity of 4.5 million tons and a combined annual installed rolling capacity of 3.8 million tons.

In 2012, ICH along with Simec, invested in San Luis Potosi, S.L.P., Mexico, which allowed the existing facility to reach an annual capacity of 1 million tons of steel billet to produce rebar, wire rod, and wire rod derivatives.

The company acquired the Mexican steelmaking facilities of Industrias Ferricas del Norte in late 2004. In 2006, Industrias CH and Grupo Simec spent more than $225 to acquire Republic Engineered Products , a US maker of SBQ steel. In another two years, Simec spent $850 million to buy Mexican rebar producer Corporacion Aceros DM, which goes by the name Grupo San. The acquired company gave Simec a base in Central Mexico, and diversified the company's steel production.
