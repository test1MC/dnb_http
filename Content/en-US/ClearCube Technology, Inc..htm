ClearCube aims to free up some precious space in tiny cubicles. The company makes desktop virtualization computing systems that reduce the need for bulky desktop PCs. Providing centralized computing for businesses looking to cut IT network complexity and costs, ClearCube offers systems featuring thin client desktop PCs that rely on network servers and network management software (Sentral). Users access data and software housed on servers via small, streamlined PCs, minimizing investments in software and maintenance. The company serves government and commercial customers worldwide. Acer Technology Ventures America , Austin Ventures , and TVM Techno Venture Management , among others, have invested in ClearCube.

Austin, Texas-based ClearCube sells its products throughout North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.

ClearCube serves several sectors, such as healthcare, defense, government, digital signage, command and control centers, engineering, financial services, and training and education.

The company sells directly to customers and through partnerships with resellers and systems integrators the likes of CDW , CompuCom , and IBM . Its customers include government agencies, including the US Army , as well as commercial entities in financial services ( CBOE ), healthcare ( CHRISTUS Health ), energy ( Atmos ), and hospitality ( Starwood Hotels & Resorts ).

Looking to boost the profile of its brand overseas, ClearCube inked a deal with Russian IT services provider iCore in 2011. The move gave ClearCube access to new distribution channels in Eastern Europe. Further extending its reach, ClearCube added reseller IT Works in Scandinavia that year.

Closer to home, the company formed a partnership with IT and physical infrastructure products maker Belkin International in 2011 to develop a secure computing product for public sector agencies. Dubbed ClientCube, the system enables government employees with a need to access a variety of computing environments in the course of their duties to switch safely between physical and virtual computing environments, simplifying network access protocols.

As part of its effort to expand, the company in 2011 acquired Dallas-based cloud computing specialist Network Elites. The deal improved ClearCube's ability to add application and data hosting services to its desktop virtualization systems.
