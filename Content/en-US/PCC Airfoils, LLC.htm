Sometimes it pays to be vain in the vane business. PCC Airfoils, a subsidiary of Precision Castparts , manufactures geometry blades, vanes, and vane segments used in the high-temperature turbines of commercial and military jet engines and power generation equipment. The company's products use both directionally solidified and single crystal casting to engineer the vanes, or airfoils, to withstand the increasingly high temperatures demanded by the aerospace industry. The temperatures are increasingly high as a result of the more and more common demand for higher performing and more fuel efficient aircraft.

PCC Airfoils has manufacturing plants in the US (about eight are concentrated in the state of Ohio and one each in Oregon, North Carolina, and Georgia), Mexico, and the UK.

PCC Airfoils' ceramics group makes ceramic cores and custom blended ceramic particulate systems used in the manufacture of investment cast blades and vanes for turbine applications, including injection molded wax gates and runners. It makes products such as custom blended ceramic shell systems, injection-molded silica and alumina cores for investment cast airfoils, slurry cast cores for land-based turbine casting applications, and ceramic extrusions.

The company belongs to the Investment Cast Products segment of the parent company. This segment contributes about 25% to its parent's net revenue. This segment's sales decreased 1% from 2013 to 2014 due to a double-digit decline in regional business jet and military shipments.

PCC Airfoils was founded in 1986.
