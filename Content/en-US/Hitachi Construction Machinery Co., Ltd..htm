Hitachi Construction Machinery (HCM) doesn't mind boring operations. The company makes construction equipment, recycling and tunneling machinery, and ultrasonic measuring instruments for detecting damage and monitoring cracking in welded auto parts, pipes, and other items. HCM also provides maintenance and leasing services. Products include excavators, wheel loaders, dump trucks, bulldozers, aerial platforms, wood- and soil-recycling machines, crushers (for recycling), crawler cranes, foundation works machines, crawler carriers, road maintenance machinery, and tunnelers. The company gets more than half of its sales in Asian markets.

Japan accounts for 27% of its net sales. The remainder of sales are sold in India, China, Asia, Africa, Europe, Russia, the Middle East, and the Americas.

The company operates through two primarily divisions: construction machinery (85% of net sales) and mining machinery (15%).

HCM's revenues increased 2% from 2014 to 2015. The growth was attributed to increased demands North America markets resulting from a revival in housing starts and a spike in pipeline construction.

The company also experienced increased demands in Africa and the Middle East in 2015. In the Asia-Pacific markets, the company observed positive gains from its Indian and Oceania businesses with a rise in demands for infrastructure investment, such as coal and quarries.

From 2014 to 2015, HCM's net income decreased by 28% due to a rise in costs, especially in employees' salaries. In addition, the company's operating cash flows increased by 32% to 2015, due to increased cash inflows from inventories and accounts receivable, along with lower cash outflows.

HCM has grown through the use of acquisitions. In 2014 the company acquired KCM Corporation , a provider of sales and after-sales services for construction machinery and transport equipment. The deal strengthened the company's wheel loader business by combining the technologies of the two companies and increasing production efficiency.

The Hitachi conglomerate, which spun off HCM in 1970, owns just over half of Hitachi Construction Machinery.
