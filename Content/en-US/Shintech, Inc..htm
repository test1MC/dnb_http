Shintech is the largest producer of polyvinyl chloride (PVC) in the US. PVC is a plastic resin used in a wide range of consumer goods and industrial materials. Known for its durability, it is often used as a substitute for wood. Through manufacturing plants in Texas and Louisiana, Shintech serves customers primarily in North America, but it also exports its resin products worldwide. The only other product the company makes is caustic soda used by the chemical industry and also found in consumer products, such as paper, soap, synthentic textiles, and food. Shintech is the US subsidiary of Japanese diversified chemical giant Shin-Etsu Chemical Co.

The company is located in Texas. The parent company (Shin-Etsu Chemical Co., Ltd.) has manufacturing bases in US and Europe as well as Japan, and has customers located across the globe.

Shintech company operates as a PVC/Chlor-Alkali Business segment of the parent company.

Shintech has an annual PVC production capacity of 2.95 million tons having completed 12 major capital investments. It completed another expansion in 2015, adding about 300,000 tons of VCM capacity, about 200,000 tons of caustic soda capacity, and about 300,000 tons of PVC capacity.

Still bouncing back from a recession that resulted in sluggish demand for its PVC products in the US housing market, Shintech is bolstering its sales and marketing efforts and moving forward with an expansion of its Louisiana production facility that will roughly double its PVC output, bringing its total annual production capacity to about 2.6 millions tons. Scheduled for completion in 2011, the company intends to outfit the facility with more integrated capabilities to produce its main raw material, vinyl chloride monomer, in house. Historically, Shintech has sourced most of its vinyl chloride monomer needed to make PVC from Dow Chemical .

Shintech began operations in 1974 with annual production capacity of 100,000 tons of PVC.
