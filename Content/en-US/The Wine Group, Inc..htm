The cat's out of the bag about bag-in-the-box wine. The Wine Group's Franzia, which comes in a box, dominates US boxed wines. One of the largest wine producers in the world, The Wine Group (TWG) offers bargain and premium table wines (bottled and boxed). Its brand names include Corbett Canyon, Almaden, Foxhorn, and Fish Eye. It imports wine as well, including Mistrane from France and Austin Vale from Australia. TWG also operates Underdog Wine Merchants, which offers offbeat wines such as Pinot Evil, Big House, Herding Cats, and Tempra Tantrum, which are designed to appeal to younger consumers. Once part of Coca-Cola , TWG is a partnership formed in 1981 to buy Franzia Brothers Winery, Mogen David, and Tribuno.

The company markets its products in North America, Western Europe, and Asia.

TWG is the world's third-largest wine producer by volume. It produces almost 60 million cases of wine a year, much of it sold in 3- and 5-liter boxes.

TWG boasts that it's the most cost-efficient and carbon-efficient vintner in the world.

Expanding its production and product portfolio, in 2015 the company acquired Benziger Family Winery and the Imagery Winery in Glen Ellen in the Sonoma Valley. Benziger produces 139,000 cases of wine annually.
