Much like the Amazon, Braskem is a South American giant, the continent's largest petrochemical company. Braskem's chemical plants are located mostly in Brazil. The company produces olefins like ethylene and propylene that are then used to make polyolefins like polyethylene, polypropylene, and PVC. That latter group of chemicals are then used to make all manner of plastic materials and fibers. Braskem also makes aromatics like benzene and intermediate chemicals like caprolactam, which are used to make nylon. In 2014 Basic Petrochemicals accounted for 45% of its total sales.

The company operates 29 plants in Brazil, five plants in the US, and two plants in Germany. Brazil accounted for 57% of 2014 sales, while other major markets and other countries (including Argentina, the UK, Germany, Mexico, Italy, Netherlands, Singapore, Switzerland, Colombia, Spain, Chile, Peru, Uruguay, Japan, Poland, Paraguay, France, Bolivia, Canada, South, Korea, and Venezuela) accounted for 23%.

Chemical Distribution consists of Quantiq's operations related to the distribution of petroleum-based solvents, intermediate chemicals, special chemicals, and pharmacons.

Braskem's petrochemicals are sold in Brazil, the US, Europe, South America, and Asia. Its 10 largest customers accounted for 52% of the Petrochemicals unit's total net sales revenue in 2014.

Polyolefins are sold in South America and Europe through regular direct sales, local, and independent distributors and agents. Net sales revenue to the 10 largest customers of this segment accounted for 19% of Polyolefins' total net sales revenues in 2014.

The company's 10 largest Vinyls Unit customers accounted for 45% of Vinyls unit's total net sales revenues during 2014. This product is sold directly to the customers and via distribution centers.

Net sales revenue to the 10 largest customers of USA and Europe Unit accounted for 50% of USA and Europe Unit's total net sales revenue during 2014. Products are sold via a direct sales force, distributors, resellers, and traders.

The Chemical Distribution segment distributes more than 1,000 products and has relationship with more than 100 domestic and international companies. Distributors include Wacker Chemie , Lubrizol , and ConocoPhillips .

In 2014 Braskem's net revenues increased by 12% (in local currency) as a result of higher net sales revenue in the Polyolefins segment, an increase in USA and Europe segment, and a growth in Basic Petrochemicals sales.

Revenues from the Basic Petrochemicals segment increased primarily as a result of higher sales of basic petrochemicals (excluding condensate, fuels, intermediates and utilities) to third parties, and a rise in net sales revenues generated by the sales of basic petrochemicals to other business units.

Polyolefins sales increased primarily as a result of higher sales of polyethylene and polypropylene.

Revenues from the USA and Europe segment increased primarily due to higher prices of sales due to the translation of the currencies of the countries in the USA and Europe segment into reais resulting in foreign exchange gains attributable to fluctuations in the relative value of these currencies against the real, rather than operational gains, and an increase in the spot prices of the Northeast Asian market of polypropylene. Sales volume of polypropylene by the USA and Europe Unit increased due to stronger demand from US and European economies.

Braksem's net income increased by 43% due to higher net sales and other operating income.

Cash from operating activities grew by 54%.

The company sells to grow via expansion of new markets through acquisitions and adding new facilities, and by pursuing new business opportunities by developing new and specialized products and technologies.
