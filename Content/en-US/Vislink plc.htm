Vislink designs and manufactures microwave data transmission and broadcasting equipment through its subsidiary companies. The company specializes in fixed microwave earth station, portable satellite, and wireless camera systems. Its Pebble Beach Systems unit sell software for automation and content manage for broadcasters. Vislink sells products under the brand names Advent, Link, MRC (Microwave Radio Communications), and PMR (Pacific Microwave Research). Vislink, which primarily serves broadcasters, also makes sales to customers in the security, military, and closed-caption television markets. Clients have included Fox , Univision , the National Football League , and the BBC .

The Vislink side of the operation provides advanced communications to the broadcast, surveillance, and defense industries. It generates 81% of the company's revenue.

Pebble Beach Systems develops and sells software that manages acquisition of content as well as file-based workflows, archiving and multi-channel playout at large and small installations. It generates the other 19% of revenue.

North America is Vislink's biggest market where customers account for 32% of revenue, followed by Europe with 21%, and the UK with 12%. Three other world regions account for about 11% of revenue each.

Vislink sells to three core markets: Broadcast Communication Links; Surveillance, Public Safety and Defense Communication Links; and Broadcast Software.

In 2015 revenue slid by £4.1 milion to £58 million. Sales of Vislink Communication Systems division were down for the year and an increase for Pebble Beach Systems division was unable to completely counteract the drop.

The company lost about £900,000, compared to net income of £3.7 million in 2014 because of lower sale and higher costs.

Cash outflow amounted to £500,000 in 2015, compared to some £7.7 million of cash generated in 2014.

In 2015 Vislink completed a restructuring intended to make the company more flexible and responsive to changing markets. The company substantially reduced costs and consolidating manufacturing operations and outsourcing the manufacture of established products to third parties. That move should lower its fixed-cost base and increase manufacturing flexibility.

In 2016, Vislink launched a new wireless communications product designed for Electronic News Gathering. The product provides secure, bi-directional, high speed data and video, radio network with concurrent connections to each receive sector, all on a single channel within the broadcaster's BAS spectrum.
