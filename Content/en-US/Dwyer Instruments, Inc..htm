Dire straits? Out of control? Or business as usual? Whatever the circumstance, Dwyer Instruments comes in handy. The company makes a slew of industrial controls and instrumentation. The short list includes valves, pressure gauges, flow meters, air velocity transmitters, temperature controls, data loggers, current transformers, and signal converters. Customers are engaged in HVAC to food, pollution control, and chemical businesses. Dwyer also makes a series of wind meters and infrared thermometers, sold via the Web, for outdoor sports such as fishing and golf.

Dwyer Instruments operates plants in Kingsbury, Michigan City, Wakarusa, and Wolcott in Indiana. It also operates manufacturing facilities in Anaheim, California; Fergus Fall, Minnesota; and Kansas City, Missouri. It also has warehouse operations to Australia and the UK, and maintains a Spanish and Chinese language website.

Dwyer offers more than 5,000 controls and gauges online and through a variety of catalogs. Some of its brands include the Magnehelic differential pressure guages, Photohelic pressure switch/guages, Rate Master flowmeters, and Hi-Flow valves.

Dwyer makes a variety of control and instrumentation products for the HVAC, chemical, food, pharmaceutical, pulp and paper, oil, gas, and pollution control industries. It sells its products through online stores and various product catalogs and through distributors located in 50 countries across the world.

Its investment in innovation has resulted in a series of instrumentation and control firsts, including the annual introduction of scores of new products. Customer alliances have also fueled development of non-mercury replacement alternatives for products normally containing mercury.

The company has supplemented its growth with acquisitions, buying brands such as as Love Controls, Mercoid Controls, Proximity Controls, and W.E. Anderson Controls.

Dwyer Instruments was founded in 1931.

Tapping international demand, Dwyer Instruments expanded its warehouse operations to Australia in 1987 and the UK in 1990.
