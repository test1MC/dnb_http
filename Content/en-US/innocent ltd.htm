innocent's products are as pure as its name suggests. The UK company, majority owned by Coca-Cola , makes all-natural fruit drinks. It boasts no added water, sugar or other sweeteners, concentrates, or preservatives in its products, which include juices, smoothies, and thickies (yogurt and fruit); it also offers fruit tubes (pureed fruit and juice in squeezable packaging). innocent also makes veg pots -- microwaveable lunch servings that contain vegetables, herbs and spices, a whole grain, and a sauce. Selling more than 2 million smoothies every week, innocent's products can be found at European coffee shops and retail food outlets. It was deemed the official smoothie and juice of the London 2012 Olympic Games.

In addition to the UK, innocent has operations in Austria, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, the Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, Switzerland.

The company is a part of European region, a subset of the international segment of Coca-Cola . In 2015 innocent sold 750 million portions of fruits and vegetables.

Coca-Cola's international segment accounted for 54% of its 2015 revenues.

The company says that its drinks are meant to provide an easy way for customers to do something healthy for themselves amid the hectic demands of modern life.
