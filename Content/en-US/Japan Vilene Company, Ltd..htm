Japan Vilene might just be a clothes horse's best friend. From power suits to ski wear, what's responsible for keeping the shape and structure of such garments is interlinings, one of the main products manufactured by the non-woven textiles company. Japan Vilene also makes belt, bra, and kimono sash padding for the apparel industry. Other products serve the medical (surgical bandages and gowns), industrial (photocopier cleaning rolls), and automotive (air filters and floor mats) industries. Top shareholders are Dainippon Ink and Chemicals and Freudenberg Beteiligungs; each owns about 23% of the company. Founded in 1960, Japan Vilene primarily serves customers in Japan through six domestic offices and factories.
