Coleman Natural Foods raises some mighty healthy critters. The company serves up vegetarian-fed chicken, pork, and goat that's natural, organic, and antibiotic- and GM-free. It also offers chicken sausage and ready-to-eat and frozen convenience meat products. Coleman Natural's brand names include Hans All Natural, and Coleman in organic and natural varieties. The poultry-and-pork producer sells its products through grocery retailers, warehouse chains, specialty and natural food stores, and food service operators (including schools) throughout the US. The company is owned by poultry-giant Perdue .

The company works with family farmers throughout the US.

Among the retailers that carry Coleman poultry and pork products are Kroger -owned chains, H-E-B , Shaw's , and Whole Foods . Its food service products are distributed exclusively by SYSCO .

Catering to those who prefer not to eat food genetically modified foods (GM foods), in 2014 Coleman added new Non-GMO verification labels for its organic line of poultry products. Its Rosie Organic and Draper Valley Farms organic products now carry labels identifying them as GMO-free products. In 2014 parent Perdue moved production from the older Booth Creek plant in Fredericksburg, Pennsylvania (which it closed) to modern plants with the capacity to better serve customers for its Coleman and customer-owned organic and no-antibiotics-ever brands.

To serve the country's growing ethnic populations (including Hispanics, Muslims, and Caribbean Islanders) many of which consume goat as a regular part of their diets, the food manufacturer sells Natural, Pasture-Raised Rocky Mountain Goat meat that's sold in the frozen meat section at Whole Foods .

Coleman Natural's acquisition activity has been non-existent in recent years. In fact, the company sold its beef business to Meyer Natural Angus , located in Loveland, Colorado, several years ago to narrow its focus on its expanding poultry, pork, and prepared-foods businesses.

Perdue purchased Coleman Natural Foods in June 2011. Post-sale, Coleman operates independently from Perdue.

The company was founded in Colorado in 1979 by Mel Coleman.
