For Venture Corporation Limited, contract manufacturing is an unlimited adventure. Through some 40 subsidiaries, the company offers electronics manufacturing services (EMS), original design manufacturing (ODM), and supply chain services (through its e-Fulfillment division). EMS, which accounts for most of its sales, makes printed circuit boards, fiber-optic switches and transceivers, and provides box-build (complete system) assembly for companies such as Broadcom and HP . Its ODM companies specialize in printing and imaging equipment (VIPColor), plastic tools (Univac), electronic systems, and components. Almost all of the company's sales come from Asia.

About 30% of sales come from making electronics for the retail sector such as handheld point-of-sale terminals and label makers. Test and measurement equipment accounts for another 30% of its business, followed by networking and communications gear such as RF devices (20%), printing and imaging equipment (10%), and computer peripherals (10%). In 2013 it designed and built the world's first mobile all-in-one portable printer for HP.

The group has manufacturing plants in China, Hungary, Malaysia, and Singapore. Other offices are located in Spain, the Netherlands, and the US.

Venture Corporation owns a couple of subsidiaries that maintain US sales locations. VIPColor Technologies makes and sells label printers to consumer product manufacturers and retail stores, while Univac, located in the same California office, sells custom-designed machines that manufacture plastic tools and parts. Venture Design Services, Inc. developed MineTracer, a wireless communication and tracking system used in coal mines. MineTracer is only sold in the US.

Venture recognizes that the key to establishing long-term relationships with its clients is to be more involved in product design. It has collaborated with customers to develop products for mobile payments, data capture devices, and life science equipment. It has also partnered with suppliers to learn more about fluidics and photonics in order to enlarge its supply base for such strategic components.

The company signed a deal in 2012 to handle the back-end manufacturing for optical and laser component maker Oclaro . Production of Oclaro products is gradually being shifted from an Oclaro site in Shenzen, China, to a Venture facility in Malaysia through 2015.
