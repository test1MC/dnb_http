Wise Alloys is quite sagacious when it comes to aluminum. The company is a leading maker of aluminum can sheet, which is used primarily to produce packaging for beverages and food. The aluminum can sheet made by Wise Alloys is transformed into more than 16 billion beverage cans per year, representing about 15% of the total market. Along with aluminum can sheet, Wise Alloys makes wide aluminum sheet, which is used in architectural applications and to make roofs for truck trailers. Though a part of the former Reynolds when it was founded in 1948, Wise Alloys has been a part of Wise Metals Group since 1999.

The company's Alloys Plant casts molten aluminum into sheet ingot, which is rolled into coils of aluminum sheet about 1/100th of an inch thick; it has a capacity to make 950 million pounds per year, which assist with the manufacturing of beverage cans, ends, tabs, food containers, and trailer roof stock.

The Element 13 complex melts and alloys beverage cans in preparation for the recasting into aluminum ingots. The facility has nine furnaces and a capacity to melt more than 600 million pounds of scrap per year.

The company serves the packaging industry (beverage and food containers) and the truck trailer sector.

Wise Alloys uses services and products from hundreds of vendors. These include industrial controls, lubricants and solvents, tools, and consumable and renewable supplies. The company employs competitive bidding for the purchase of supplies and services.

Wise Alloys has strategic alliances with the Georgia Technological Institute For Research; R.J. Lee; and Chemir/Polytech.

In 2015 the company announced plans to refinance its existing asset based lending facility, and engaged in discussions with both current and new lenders.
