Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics earns an A+ in Chemistry. The Carlyle Group subsidiary develops and markets diagnostic tools, including VITROS instrumentation systems that use clinical chemistry and immunoassay technologies to diagnose a range of maladies such as cancer, heart disease, and infectious diseases. It also markets laboratory automation systems under the enGen brand. In addition to making clinical diagnostics products, Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics is a leading maker of instruments and reagents used by blood banks and hospitals to screen blood donations for disease and contaminants, as well as blood typing systems that guarantee patient-donor compatibility for transfusions.

In 2014 The Carlyle Group acquired Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics from Johnson & Johnson for $4 billion. The deal allows Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics to expand and accelerate its investment in research and development activities.

The company operates through two primary divisions: clinical laboratories and transfusion medicine.

The clinical laboratory division focuses on producing in vitro diagnostic tests under the VITROS brand, which are sold to small, midsized, and large clinical and research laboratories, hospitals, and doctors' offices.

The company's transfusion medicine division handles the development and commercialization of donor screening instruments and reagents that test donated blood for infectious diseases (such as HIV/AIDS and hepatitis). The division also produces blood typing equipment, which is used by client blood banks to verify patient-donor compatibility.

A third, smaller Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics division makes immune globulin, a therapeutic plasma product used to treat Hemolytic Disease of the Newborn (HDN), under the RhoGAM and MICRhoGAM brands. It also makes a FETALSCREEN FMH (fetal maternal hemorrhage) screening test, which helps in the detection and prevention of HDN.

In addition, Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics provides training, education, and support services including online data analysis, efficiency consulting, and field engineering.

Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics markets and sells its products worldwide, with facilities throughout the Asia/Pacific region, Europe, and North America. The company has more than 2,500 employees worldwide.

The company sells its products to hospitals, labs, and blood centers.

In 2014 the company entered into a strategic partnership with Astute Medical, under which Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics became the exclusive commercial partner for the distribution of Astute's NephroCheck Test and Astute140 Meter in certain markets in Europe and (pending FDA clearance) the US. The following year it entered an agreement with Quotient Limited, through which it will distribute and sell that firm's MosaiQ transfusion diagnostics platform.

In 2013 Ortho-Clinical Diagnostic sold Therakos to the Gores Group . It also launched ORTHO Workstation, a next-generation semi-automated blood typing system, and announced the availability of ORTHO Sera outside of North America.

Ortho Clinical Diagnostics has been building a solar array at its Raritan, New Jersey, corporate headquarters. The program is expected to decrease green house gas emissions through the production of renewable energy.

The establishment of Ortho-Clinical Diagnostics dates back to 1937, when J&J formed the Ortho Products Division; in 1997, it merged with Eastman Kodak 's Clinical Diagnostics Division.
