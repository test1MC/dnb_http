Arista Networks is ready for companies to flip the switch to cloud-based network switches. The company designs and sells high-speed network switches that enable large-scale Internet companies, cloud service providers, and data centers to run faster networks on an updated IT infrastructure platform. Its cloud networking products consist of the Extensible Operating System (EOS), a set of network applications and its 10/40/100 Gigabit Ethernet switches (the 7050, 7150, 7250X, 7300, and 7500 Series), which are manufactured by contractors Jabil Circuit and Foxconn . EOS supports such virtual networks as VMware NSX and Microsoft System Center. Arista Networks went public in mid-2014, raising $226 million in its IPO.

The company doesn't have any specific plans for its IPO proceeds other than paying back Singtel Innov8, the venture capital arm of Singaporean telecom SingTel . The remaining net proceeds will be used for general corporate purposes, which could be product development, sales and marketing activities, or working capital.

Arista Networks operates from two US offices in the Bay Area. International offices are located in Canada, India, Ireland, and Singapore. The US accounts for about 80% of sales, although it counts customers in more than 50 countries worldwide.

Its products are sold through a direct sales force and channel partners such as distributors, value-added resellers, system integrators, and OEMs. Its boasts a blue-chip customer base of 2,500 companies, including tech firms such as eBay , Facebook , Microsoft , and Yahoo! ; financial services organizations such as Citigroup and Morgan Stanley ; and other media and service providers such as AOL , Comcast , and Netflix .

Overall sales jumped 87% year-over-year to $361 million in 2013. The company sold double the number of new switch ports, mostly to existing customers aiming to further evolve their data centers, even as its average selling price fell 10%. Even as it invests heavily in R&D, Arista Networks has been profitable.

As a player in the software-defined networking (SDN) space, Arista Networks is focused on making its technology more agile than its competitors. Legacy networks aren't programmable and can be difficult to integrate with third-party applications for network management and automation. However, Arista Networks' cloud networking platform doesn't have the same limitations of legacy network architectures.

The company's stated growth strategy is fourfold: it plans to continue to invest in R&D; expand it sales organization and channel partners (which now number about 600) to grow international sales; upsell existing customers; and expand strategic relationships with product integrations alongside companies such as Aruba Networks , F5 Networks , Microsoft , Palo Alto Networks , Riverbed , Splunk , and VMware .

Arista Networks was founded in 2008 by former employees of industry giant Cisco .
