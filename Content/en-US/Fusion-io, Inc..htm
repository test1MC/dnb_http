Fusion-io wants to help businesses access data in a flash. The company's ioMemory hardware modules offer storage and processing using an array of flash memory delivered through its proprietary data-path controller and virtual storage layer (VSL) software. VSL allows ioMemory to function as data storage within a server, allowing data access without traditional storage devices. Fusion-io's products are used in sectors such as education, energy, entertainment, financial services, government, and life sciences, among others. The company's three biggest clients have been Facebook (29% of sales), Apple (15%), and HP (17%). Fusion-io has operated as a subsidiary of flash storage maker SanDisk following the 2014 acquisition.

California-based SanDisk bought Fusion-io for $1.1 billion in June 2014.

Headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah, Fusion-io has a sales and marketing office in San Jose, California and other spots in the US and around the world.

Fusion-io has sold products through its direct sales force, OEMs (including Cisco , Dell , HP, and IBM ), and other channel partners to financial services, Internet, technology, education, retail, manufacturing, energy, life sciences, and government. Its 10 largest customers accounted for 84% of revenue in 2013 (ended June).

Since the acquisition by Sandisk, Fusion-io's financial results have not been reported. The last full year of results were reported for 2013 when revenue hit a company high of $432 million from about $360 million in 2012. Higher volume of shipped products means higher revenue.

The company's net loss expanded by about $5 million to $39 million in 2013. Fusion-io fed its sales growth by increasing sales and marketing spending (nearly $260,000 in 2013, up from $120,000 in 2012) and research and development costs. It also higher legal fees because of litigation.

With Fusion-io on board, SanDisk can accelerate efforts to create a flash-transformed data center for customers. Fusion-io will benefit from SanDisk's vertical integration. (Fusion-io develops software in-house but outsources manufacturing, to Alpha EMS, Jabil Circuit , and Foxconn .)
