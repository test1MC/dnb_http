Folks at Scholle are encouraged to think inside the box -- or in this case, the bag. The company, also known as Scholle Packaging, pioneered the bag-in-box technology used to package and dispense a variety of consumer products, from food and beverages to agricultural chemicals and liquid cleaning products. Its flexible packaging unit offers performance-based packaging products to multinational companies around the world. Scholle also owns JW Automarine, a UK-based company that manufactures inflatable buoyancy products, and Scholle Chemical, which makes nitrocellulose (a synthetic resin), as well as ink and lacquer bases and protective and specialty coatings for medical and printing applications.

The company operates manufacturing plants and sales offices throughout the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia/Pacific.

Scholle operates through its primary subsidiaries, each of which serve a different and diverse customer base. Its largest segment, Scholle Packaging, makes bags for bag-in-box products for the food and beverage and industrial markets. Scholle Chemical, based in the Atlanta area, sells its products to the mainly to the medical and printing industries.

UK-based JW Automarine is a fabricator of underwater lifting bags, gas and liquid storage bags, and inflatable buoyancy products geared at aid and first responder agencies, armed forces, diving and salvage contractors, farmers, forestry workers, and oil companies. JW Automarine has a worldwide networks of sales and service agents.

In addition, its Flexpack subsidiary designs, manufactures, and provides stand-up pouch packaging, barrier films, and performance packaging for the Latin American market. Its products are used for applications such as processed meats, sauces, fresh pasta, dairy products, nuts, automotive fluids, healthcare, and pharmaceutical products.

With a footprint spanning over 60 countries, Scholle offers its products and services in three different markets. These include food (dairy, edible oil, and flavorings), beverage (coffee and tea, dairy and alternatives, juice, smoothies, and soft drinks) and non-food (antifreeze, automotive additives, automotive transmission fluid, bactericides, brake fluid, coolants, degreasers, and detergents).

It serves customers worldwide through a network of sales and service agents. Clients include Shell, Don & Sons, Manitowoc , and SAS.

Scholle identifies expanding its performance packaging business as a key part of its strategy for growth. Consistent with this effort, it sold its Vacumet Plastics division, a supplier of metallized plastic films, in 2012 to India's Polyplex.

As far as product development is concerned, Scholle's direction and strength over the years has benefited from an increasing number of technology partnerships. It has engaged branding and graphic design firms ( MOD/Michael Osborne Design ), box designers and corrugate suppliers ( International Paper and Klabin ), and companies that provide contract packing, dispensing equipment ( Lancer ), shipping containers ( Chep ), and various filling and processing equipment.

In late 2013, Scholle acquired IPN, headquartered in The Netherlands. IPN specializes in plastic components and packaging for the food, non-food, and health care markets. Many of IPN's patented products are major components in packaging used by leading global brands and their retail and professional consumers.

In early 2013 it acquired a controlling interest in Brazil-based packaging manufacturer Flexpack, whose standup pouch products complement Scholle's core bag-in-box offerings, especially in the emerging Latin American market.

Scholle, founded in 1945, has evolved from what was historically a chemical business that specialized in nitrocellulose-based products. Scholle's concept of bag-in-a-box packaging (a flexible spouted bag held inside and supported by a rigid outside container) traces back to 1955. Its success helped transform the company to a vertically integrated producer of flexible packaging and advanced material products -- from spouts and taps to bag-in-box and buoyancy products.
