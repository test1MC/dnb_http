You can be sure that the folks at EFCO Corporation don't throw stones considering that they build glass houses -- or at least, glass windows, sliding doors, and curtain wall systems. The company also manufactures aluminum window units, entrances, and storefronts; its products are for commercial architectural applications. Company projects include historical replication work and other custom design work as well as new construction. EFCO is a subsidiary of residential window manufacturer Pella and has service centers and distribution facilities in Arizona, Colorado, Illinois, Missouri, Nevada,

EFCO is stationed in Monett, Missouri, and has service centers in Illinois, Missouri, Nevada, and Virgina. It has five distribution centers in Arizona, Colorado, Texas, and Utah.

EFCO projects have included Pfizer Clinical Research Center in Connecticut, Ritz Carlton Hotel & Residences New York City, Memphis Public Library, Beverly Hills Public Works, Georgetown University's White-Gravenor building, and FedEx World Headquarters.

EFCO's products mainly support community and commercial architectural applications and are sold through more than 25 independent representatives across the US.

George Eberle and William Terry Fuldner founded the company as EFCO Product Company in 1951; it was incorporated as EFCO Corporation in 1953.
