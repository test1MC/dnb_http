Amada is an armada of metal working machinery manufacturers. Through about 80 subsidiaries, the company produces metalworking machinery and metal machine tools such as metal cutting and stamping, sheet metal, structural steel, and machine tooling equipment. Its metalworking machinery group makes laser machines, punch presses, press brakes, and mechanical presses. Its Amada Metal Machine Tools subsidiary makes cutting machines and machine tools including metal-cutting bandsaws, lathes, and grinders. To help customers operate all this machinery, Amada also offers computer software and peripherals for controlling industrial metal working machinery. The company was formed in 1946.

Japan is the company's largest market, representing 47% of total sales. Other major markets include North America, Europe, and Asia, each generating 17% of sales. Other regions account for the remaining 2% of sales.

Amada divides its operations across several product lines and divisions: Sheet-Metal Processing Machines (80% of sales), Bandsaws (12%), Machine Tools (5%), and Presses (3%).
