Wolverine Tube helps refrigerators and A/Cs chill out. One of North America's largest makers of copper and alloy tube for commercial products, Wolverine sells to OEMs. The company makes a range of custom tubing, including industrial (for residential A/C units), technical (for heat exchangers), copper alloy (for utilities), and fabricated (for automotive, electrical, and other applications) products, as well as wholesale tube for standard A/C and refrigeration. It also provides thermal management products for electronics and military/aerospace applications. Wolverine Tube nets most of its sales in North America.

Wolverine's manufacturing facilities are located in Ardmore, Tennessee; Shawnee, Oklahoma; and Shanghai. It also has a sales and marketing office in Apeldoorn, the Netherlands.

Wolverine's copper and copper-alloy tubes and components are sold to equipment manufacturers in the heating, ventilating, and air-conditioning industry. Its custom-engineered components provide a thermal management offering for its customers in multiple markets including HVAC, refrigeration and appliance, electronics cooling, power generation, petrochemical, and chemical processing. Its products are sold directly to original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).

Wolverine traces its historical roots back to 1916.
