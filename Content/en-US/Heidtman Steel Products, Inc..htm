Steel life is an art at Heidtman Steel Products, a provider of steel processing services such as blanking, leveling, and pickling. One of the largest privately held flat-rolled steel service networks in the US, the company processes more than 5 million tons of steel annually. Services include hot-rolling, cold-rolling, and steel coating, a full-range of slitting capabilities, and galvanizing. Heidtman Steel operates plants in five mainly Midwestern states that serve automotive, bus, and truck manufacturers, as well as furniture and appliance makers. The company is an approved steel supplier to Caterpillar and Ford .

The company has almost 10 direct and joint venture state-of-the-art facilities, including seven wholly owned processing centers in Cleveland and Toledo, Ohio; Butler, Indiana; Granite City, Illinois; and Erie, Michigan; plus two joint venture processing centers in Monroe, Michigan, and Delta, Ohio. It also has 10 processing facilities that it shares with affiliates.

Looking to ramp up its activities, Heidtman Steel participates in several joint ventures. The company's investments include Fulton County Processing with VanPoppel Enterprises and Kelco Metals, and American Roll Coating and Cleaning Co. (Arcco) with Universal Metals. Together, the company and its affiliates operates nine steel processing plants.

The company's associate members include Metal Service Center Institute, Association of Steel Distributors, Precision Metal Forming, Outside Processing Council, American Wind Energy Association, Great Lakes Wind Network, and National Truck Equipment Association.

The company serves customers in a range of industries including automotive, truck, and bus manufacturers, agriculture, service centers, lawn, garden, appliance, furniture, and HVAC makers, and other steel service centers. Its hot rolled, cold rolled, and coated steel products and first operation blanks are marketed through its Toledo blank location. Heidtman Steel has five regional sales offices across the US.

Heidtman Steel is moving into advanced and ultra-high strength steels as the mills develop new products.

In 2014 its Fulton County Processing joint venture expanded its pickling capacity at its facility in Delta, Ohio. The $20 million project will add a new 4 tank Butech Bliss/Nelson push/pull pickling line and 82,000 square feet and include additional storage and rail car capacity.

Heidtman Steel Products was formed in 1954 by William Heidtman and offered leveling and shearing services during its first few years of operation. It introduced steel slitting equipment in 1971 and flat rolled steel service in 1979.
