Explosive growth is the obvious aim of Dyno Nobel, the explosives market leader in North America, and the second largest explosive supplier in Australia (which is the world's third-largest explosives market). The company manufactures explosives in Australasia, Asia, North America, and South America. It sells its finished products to mining, construction, and industrial quarry companies, among other industries around the world through a network of distributors and through its joint venture partners. Dyno Nobel is owned by Australian agrochemical maker Incitec Pivot.

Based in Salt Lake City, Utah, Dyno Nobel has manufacturing plants in Australia, Canada, Indonesia, Mexico, Papua New Guinea, Turkey, the US, Africa, and South America.

Dyno Nobel is organized into two groups, Dyno Nobel Americas (DNA) and Dyno Nobel Asia Pacific (DNAP). DNA supplies industrial explosives and blasting services to the mining, quarrying and construction industries in Latin America, the US and Canada. Additionally, it supplies nitrogen based products to agricultural and industrial chemical markets. DNAP supplies industrial explosives and blasting services to the mining industry in Australia, Europe, Indonesia, and Papua New Guinea for surface and underground mining in the thermal coal, metallurgical coal, iron ore and other metals sectors.

Dyno Nobel makes a wide range of commercial explosives, including ammonium nitrate, bulk explosives, package emulsions, dynamite, detonators (electric, nonelectric and electronic), cast boosters, and detonating cord, as well as surface and underground loading systems and Portable Modular Emulsion Plants. It also offers services including blast design, shot loading, shot service, vibration control, airblast, flyrock and NOx reduction, through DynoConsult, a specialist consulting division. Dyno Nobel emphasizes its safety performance as a provider of innovative explosive products and services.

Dyno Nobel Albania Sh.p.k (owned by Nitromak Dyno Nobel Turkey, which serves the explosives materials market in Europe and Turkey) is engaged into manufacturing and supplying explosive products, blasting services, and consultancy services for all types of blasting applications.

Dyno Nobel Transportation transports more than 1.76 billion pounds of material a year.

Dyno Nobel serves customers in a range of industries including mining, quarry, construction, pipeline, geophysical exploration, and explosives, as well as the iron ore and other metals markets. In order to enhance its global expertise to meet customer needs, the company maintains partnerships with companies such as Boren Explosives, Brandywine & Supply Co., Buckley Powder, and Kentucky Powder Company.

The company operates under the Explosives segment of parent Incitec Pivot . The segment accounted for about 57% of the parent company's revenues in 2013.

DNA's revenues grew by 3% in 2013 primarily due to increased volume, partially offset by a decline in the Agriculture and Industrial Chemicals business (as a result of decreases in urea and urea ammonium nitrate prices), partially offset by higher volumes from the St. Helena (US) plant.

That year it experience lower earnings, driven by the negative impact of lower urea and urea ammonium nitrate prices in the Agriculture and Industrial Chemicals business, and a downturn in the coal sector (which reduced ammonium nitrate sales volumes).

DNAP's revenues increased by 25% in 2013, primarily due to increased volumes. Its earnings increased due to higher profit from the Moranbah (Australia) plant, and an increase in earnings from DNAP's joint venture business in Moura (Australia), driven by a stronger production performance.

Taking advantage of abundant natural gas supplies in North America, in 2013 the company broke ground on a $850 million ammonia plant in Louisiana. Dyno Nobel plans to begin ammonia production at its plant in 2016.

In 2012 Dyno Nobel formed Dyno Nobel International to expand the company's operations into new areas including Albania, Chile, China, Finland, Mexico, and Romania and elsewhere in Europe, Africa, Central America, and South America.

Incitec Pivot bought Dyno Nobel from Orica for about $3 billion in 2008.

The current company -- composed of the former Dyno Nobel's Australian, US, and Canadian operations -- began trading on the Australian Stock Exchange in 2006.

Private equity firm Industri Kapital, which had owned Dyno Nobel prior to a 2003 restructuring, sold it to Australia-based Orica and Macquarie Bank in 2005. The Macquarie consortium retained the Australian and North American businesses and Orica the other assets. Orica paid $685 million for Dyno Nobel's operations in Europe, Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and Latin America.

Formerly based in Norway, Dyno Nobel was founded in 1865 by an associate of Swedish dynamite inventor and prize giver Alfred Nobel.

Founder Alfred Nobel changed the world with his invention of dynamite and the detonator in the mid-1800's and changed it again when he founded the world famous Nobel Prizes. Dyno Nobel traces its roots back to William Bickford's invention of Safety Fuse in 1831 and Alfred Nobel's legacy of safety and innovation.
