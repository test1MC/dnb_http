Kate Spade's story is one of simplicity and growth. The company, launched in 1993 by designer Kate Spade and her husband Andy, has expanded from its handbag niche to the current manufacture of stationery, various functional bags, and licensed products -- with lines of homewares as well as clothing, beauty items, eyewear and shoes. Women's items are sold under the kate spade name; men's products carry the JACK SPADE moniker. Owned by Kate Spade & Co. (formerly Fifth & Pacific Companies), the firm distributes products in Asia, Canada, Europe and the US, and it sells through company-owned specialty and outlet stores, upscale department stores, and its website.

The US is kate spade's primary market, home to about 90 kate spade and jack spade stores. (California and New York are major markets for the clothing and accessories chain.) The retailer also has 30 -plus kate spade concession stores in Japan, three in Canada, and more than 25 others outside of North America.

The company's brands include kate spade, kate spade saturday, and jack spade. It operates retail stores under the kate spade and jack spade banners in the US and abroad. It sells merchandise online. It also sells goods wholesale and licenses its products.

Founded in 1996, the Jack Spade men's clothing and accessories brand is another focus of growth for kate spade's parent company Fifth & Pacific.

The kate spade brand rang up $461.9 million in sales in 2012, a 48% increase compared with 2011. Comparable direct-to-consumer sales rose 29% over the same period. Sales productivity increased from $934 per average square foot in 2011 to $1,016 in 2012. All of the company's operations posted sales increases. (Kate spade's 2011 sales were about $313 million, an increase of nearly 70% vs. the prior year.)

Given kate spade's recent strong financial performance, it's easy to see why Fifth & Pacific is staking its future on the brand. Indeed, it has ambitious growth plans for both the kate spade and jack spade brands. Growth initiatives for kate spade include the establishment of operations in the UK and the formation of a joint venture in China with E-Land Fashion China Holdings. The partnership will market and distribute small leather goods and other fashion products and accessories under the kate spade brand in China. To boost sales kate spade is also adding stores, looking to increase apparel sales, and accelerating the growth of its men's brand jack spade. The company is also looking to boost online sales substantially. During recent years, kate spade has expanded its products portfolio to include lifestyle items as the company looks to extend its reach beyond its iconic handbags. To this end, kate spade rings up sales in fashion accessories, jewelry, luggage, apparel, and fragrances. The retailer also offers footwear, eyewear, and baby gear, among other items. Under the jack spade trademark, the company peddles briefcases, travel bags, and small leather goods.

In October 2012, kate spade's wholly-owned Japanese subsidiary, Kate Spade Japan (KSJ), acquired the 51% stake held by Sanei International Co. in KSJ for about $47.6 million.
