With Calix's access equipment, your bandwidth runneth over. Calix (Latin for "cup") markets broadband access equipment for communications service providers (CSPs). Its products increase the capacity of fiber-optic and copper lines to deliver next-generation telecommunications services, such as Internet protocol television (IPTV) and VoIP, as well as mobile broadband and high-speed DSL Internet. The company also supplies Web-based network management software that handles provisioning, testing, and troubleshooting. In 2014 more than 19 million ports of its Unified Access portfolio have been deployed at a growing number of CSPs worldwide, whose networks serve more than 100 million subscriber lines.

Calix has operational locations in the US (California), as well as in Canada, the Caribbean, and China. The US accounts for about 90% of annual revenues. Calix serves CSPs in 80 countries around the globe.

Calix depends on Flextronics International and other contract electronics manufacturers to produce its products. It also outsources order fulfillment and delivery operations. Internally, the company employs test engineers, supply chain management personnel, and new product introduction professionals.

The company's products are based on four core unified access platforms: the B6 Ethernet service access nodes, the C7 multi-protocol access platform, the BLM1500 gigabit passive optical network (GPON) access terminal, and the E-Series Ethernet service access platforms and nodes. It also provides related terminals, gateways, management systems, and software applications.

Calix employs a direct sales force to market its access systems and software; it also uses a limited number of resale partners. Among its more than 1000 customers around the world some of the largest US incumbent local exchange carriers. Overall, the company serves 85% of all local exchange carriers in the US market. Top customers include CenturyLink , Frontier , Windstream , TDS , and Cox Communications . In 2014 CenturyLink accounted for 23% of Calix's total revenues.

The company business model includes working with a small number of select channel partners in North America, more than 70 international channel partners (who are part of its Fiber Forward Partner Program), and a global reseller relationship with Ericsson .

In 2014 Calix's revenues increased by 5% due to stronger bookings and shipments across all territories as the result of a growth in customer demand. It also recognized revenues derived from contracts funded by the Broadband Stimulus Programs under the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009.

The company has seen continuous losses since 2006. In 2014 Calix posted a net loss of $20.8 million compared to loss of $17.3 million in 2013, due to higher operating costs and a higher provision for income tax.

After experiencing three years continuous cash flow growth, in 2014 the company's operating cash inflow decreased by 7% due to higher net loss and a change in working capital items as a result of a change in deferred revenues, prepaid expenses, and other assets.

Calix's growth strategy lies in introducing new products in keeping with its focus on access systems and software for CSPs, primarily through internal research and development programs. R&D expenses totaled some $80.3 million in 2014. Other hallmarks of Calix's strategy are direct customer interaction, expanding product adoption with its existing customers, and building partnerships and alliances with companies such as Cisco .

The company is also working to grow in new markets, such as high-growth regions in Africa, Asia, Australia, Europe, and Latin America. It has already established a strong presence in Canada and the Caribbean. It also targets selected new fiber access application markets, such as the mobile backhaul and cable business services markets.

In addition to organic growth methods, Calix continues to look for acquisitions that will allow it to expand into new markets and increase customer adoption of its products.

In 2015 the company announced that the RS Fiber Cooperative (partnered with Hiawatha Broadband Communications to bring a gigabit broadband experience to 10 cities and 17 townships across four counties in south central Minnesota using Calix systems and software.

In 2014 Calix launched its Fiber Forward Partner Program, a highly selective program for Channel Partners that matches the world-class access innovation of Calix with local market leadership by local Partners to deliver compelling new broadband services for communications service providers worldwide.

In 2012 Calix acquired Ericsson 's fiber access assets to expand its international customer base and bolster its selection of unified access products used to build fixed line broadband networks. Concurrently, the two companies announced a global reseller agreement, through which Calix began acting as as Ericsson's preferred partner for broadband access products worldwide.

In 2011 the company acquired Occam Networks in a cash and stock deal worth $171 million. The deal added Occam's expertise in IP and Ethernet networks to Calix's offerings, as well as adding more than 300 communications service provider clients.

Just before it went public in 2010, the company shortened its name from Calix Networks to Calix.

The company was founded in 1999.
