Corning Optical Communications (formerly Corning Cable Systems) believes that fiber is an important part of an industrial diet. The company makes fiber-optic cables and networking products for voice, data, and video network applications. It manufactures cables, copper connectors, and related hardware; it also provides network design, installation, and maintenance services. The company offers its products and services, including Evolant, Vascade, LEAF, SMF-28, Corning SMF-28 Ultra Fiber, LANscape, FlexNAP, and ClearCurve brands, to both public and private networks and equipment manufacturers. Corning Optical Communications is a segment of Corning Incorporated .

Corning Optical Communications operates through more than 30 global locations. Parent Corning operates manufacturing facilities worldwide, with its optical fiber manufacturing facilities located in North Carolina, China and India; its cabling operations include facilities in North Carolina, Germany, Poland, China and smaller regional locations and equity affiliates. Along with manufacturing operations for hardware and equipment products which are located in North Carolina, Texas, Arizona, Mexico, Brazil, Denmark, Germany, Poland, Israel, Australia, and China.

The company operates under two product categories - carrier network and enterprise network.

The carrier network consists of products and solutions for optical-based communications infrastructure for services such as video, data and voice communications; whereas the enterprise network product group primarily consists of optical-based communication networks sold to businesses, governments and individuals for their own use.

Corning Optical Communications' carrier network contributed more 77% of the company's total revenues in 2014, with the rest generated by the Enterprise network. The company accounted for more than 25% of annual sales of the parent company Corning's total revenue in 2014.

To keep pace with the growing demand for mobile bandwidth, Corning has a full complement of operator-grade distributed antenna systems, including the recently developed Optical Network Evolution wireless platform, which is the first of its kind, which provides virtually unlimited bandwidth, and meets all of the wireless service needs of large-scale enterprises at a lower cost than the typical distributed antenna systems product.

The company has maintained a steady revenue growth over the last three years.

In 2014, Corning Optical Communications' revenues increased by 14% due to higher sales of carrier network products in North America and Europe, partially offset by a $52 million drop in optical fiber sales in China due to a slowing economy in the region.

Corning Optical Communications works to constantly expand its product portfolio through frequent new product launches.

One of the important operations of the company is patent protection, as Corning Optical Communications has an extensive portfolio of patents relating to its products, technologies and manufacturing processes. It licenses certain of its patents to third parties and generates revenue from these licenses
