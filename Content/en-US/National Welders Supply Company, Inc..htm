If you use that blow torch for something tougher than completing the perfect crème brûlée, then National Welders Supply Company (doing business as Airgas National Welders) has what you need. Airgas National Welders supplies the Southeastern US with all manner of industrial gases -- high-pressure commercial argon, low-pressure acetylene for fuel, liquid cryogenic nitrous oxide, and medical-grade carbon dioxide. The Airgas company also does consulting, supplies welding and cutting machines for sale and rental, and serves a wide variety of industries ranging from agriculture to furniture to pharmaceuticals.

The company serves customers across the Southeastern US (Georgia, North Carolina, South Carolina and Virginia) through its more than 50 sales and service centers.

Airgas National Welders offers a range of products and services including: The manufacture and distribution of cryogenic, industrial, medical, specialty, and refrigerant gases; Robotic, laser and cutting machine automation; Safety supplies; Technical process application for the metalworking industry; Welding equipment rental; and Welding and industrial supplies.

The company carries a complete line of welders, welder accessories, and welding consumables from Lincoln Electric , Miller Electric , Thermal Dynamics, and other major manufacturers.

Airgas National Welders operates as a part of the Distribution segment of parent company Airgas . The distribution segment contributed about 90% of Airgas' sales in 2013.

The company serve a wide range of industries, including agriculture, airlines, automotive, chemical, construction, defense, e electric and gas utilities, electronics, fabrication, food and beverage, food processing, furniture, manufacturing, medical, metal working manufacturing, pharmaceutical, pulp and paper, restaurant, and transportation equipment. It also serves wholesale distributors and federal, state, and municipal government bodies.

Its sales and service facilities convert bulk liquid oxygen, nitrogen, argon and carbon dioxide into pure or blended high pressure packaged gases. Its showrooms stock a large supply of welding, safety. and construction products.

Related sales and service units include Bulk Cryogenic Gas, Construction, Medical, MicroBulk, Specialized Equipment and Engineering, and Safety products.

The parent's Distribution segment sales increased by 4% in 2013 (3% organic sales, thanks to higher prices; and 1% incremental sales as the result of acquisitions).

In 2007 the company sought to buy 17 packaged gas facilities from Airgas, which had just bought a large portion of Linde 's US packaged gas operations and decided to sell those locations in the Carolinas and parts of Virginia and Georgia to National Welders. The facilities generated about $50 million in sales. Airgas decided it made more sense to take full control of National Welders and did so through a share exchange.

Airgas National Welders (as National Welders Supply Company) was founded in 1941 by James A. Turner. It operated as a joint-venture between the Turner family and from 1996 until 2007.
