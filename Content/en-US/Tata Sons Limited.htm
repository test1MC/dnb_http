Tata Sons is the ultimate doting parent. As the primary holding company of India's expansive Tata Group, the firm holds the bulk of the shares in the nearly 100 independent companies that make up the Tata Group. Tata Sons is divided into two divisions: Tata Quality Management Services and Tata Financial Services. The quality management unit works with Tata Group companies to improve efficiencies and other processes. Tata Financial provides in-house financial services, planning, negotiations, debt placement, mergers and acquisitions, and other advisory services to Tata companies. Tata Sons owns the Tata name and trademarks. The company is controlled by philanthropic trusts that were endowed by the Tata family.

The Tata Group has operations in more than 100 countries on six continents; its companies export products and services to 150 countries.

Tata Sons oversees group company operations in seven business area: information systems and communications, engineering, materials, services, energy, consumer products, and chemicals

Tata Sons got 67% of its revenues from outside India in fiscal 2013. The Engineering segment contributed 39% of net sales, followed by the Materials sector, with 26%.

Tata Sons is focused on aggressive growth. The company has achieved its ambitions by cutting costs and holding off on some acquisitions. Tata Sons invests heavily in new technology in order to grow business in India and abroad. The company helps the group enter new lines of business through investing in areas such as supercomputers, carbon footprint reduction, and materials manufacturing (including lightweight steel).

In 2014 it opened an office in Singapore to strengthen Tata Sons' presence in the Asean region and facilitate innovation, R&D, and technology support of Tata Group companies' growth in the region.

In 2013 the company teamed up with Singapore Airlines to create a new airline in India that will help further stimulate demand for air travel. Tata Sons will own 51% of the new company and Singapore Airlines will own the rest.

In 2012 the company's Tata Global Beverages and Starbucks formed a joint venture to open Starbucks cafés across India. Also that year Tata Communications completed the first wholly-owned cable network ring around the world; Tata Chemicals launched India's first iodine plus iron fortified salt; and Tata Steel expanded its aerospace activities in China.

In 2013 the company's Tata Technologies unit bought Cambric, a leading US-based engineering services company, while Tata Consultancy Services acquired IT services firm Alti (to help drive its long-term growth push in France).

Earlier examples of Tata making investments in innovation include its minority stake in Praj Industries, which makes machines for ethanol manufacturers. The investment moved Tata into the alternative energy field. Its Tata Motors company also unveiled the Nano, an affordable fuel-efficient car that sells for about $2,500.

Tata Sons, motivated by a dip in the stock markets and a change in India's takeover regulations, bought back shares of some of its companies in 2011, incrementally hiking its stake in Tata Chemicals (more than 25%), Tata Steel (more than 28%), and Tata Motors (more than 28%). It also bought shares of Trent (more than 26%). The move is part of the company's strategy to consolidate its holdings.

Brand Finance, a UK-based consultancy firm, values the Tata brand at $21.1 billion (34th among the top 500 most valuable global brands in the BrandFinance® Global 500 2014 report).

The company traces its roots to 1868, when Jamsetji Nusserwanji Tata founded a private trading firm.
