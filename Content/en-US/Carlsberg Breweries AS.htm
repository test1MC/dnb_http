They brew it here; they brew it there; they brew this Danish beermaker's brands almost everywhere. Carlsberg Breweries makes more than 500 beer brands at some 80 breweries worldwide. Selling big-name brands Carlsberg, Tuborg, and Baltika, as well as smaller regional and local brands, Carlsberg Breweries is Denmark's dominant brewer. It's also the fourth largest brewery worldwide; its beers are available throughout Europe and Asia. To diversify, the company also makes and sells Coca-Cola in Denmark and Finland. Additionally, Carlsberg's parent company and Heineken own Irish brewer, Scottish & Newcastle (now called Heineken UK ). The philanthropic Carlsberg Foundation controls 74% of the company's voting power.

The company sells the majority of its beer outside of Denmark and maintains a licensing agreement with Japan's Suntory . It also has an agreement with Mexico's Grupo Modelo , maker of Corona beer, to distribute Modelo's products in Asia and Europe.

Carlsberg products include many number of regional brands, including Koff (Finland), Okocim (Poland), Pripps (Sweden), Ringnes (Norway), Tetley's (the UK), Feldschlösschen (Switzerland), Mythos (Greece), and 1664 (France).

Carlsberg Breweries was created in 2001 when Carlsberg A/S and Orkla ASA combined their brewing operations. However, Orkla sold its interest in the brewery to Carlsberg A/S in 2004, making Carlsberg the sole onwer of the brewery.

Like other global brewers, Carlsberg Breweries is focusing on growth in Central Europe and China. In 2004 the company purchased Germany's Holsten-Brauerei AG and that same year invested in the Wusu Brewery in northwestern China. The company has other investments in China's Gansu, Guangdong, Qunghai, Xinjang, Tibet, and Yunnan provinces.

Following its decision to end a licensing deal with Labatt , the company opened its own Canadian and US subsidiaries in 2004.

Also in 2008 the company sold its mineral water brands, Kildevæld and Kurvand, in Denmark and its Hyvää Päivää brand in Finland to The Coca-Cola Company for $225 million to concentrate on growing its beer business.
