BASF Canada is a veritable one-stop chemical shop. The company, a subsidiary of chemical giant BASF 's North American unit , has five primary divisions that make nearly every basic chemical under the sun. Its agricultural products and nutrition unit makes pest and plant control products and ingredients for cosmetics, food, and pharmaceuticals. The company's performance products unit produces chemicals for paper, textiles, and automobiles. The functional solutions division makes coatings and pigments. Through its plastics unit, BASF Canada also is a major producer of polyurethanes and styrenics. The company's smallest unit, chemicals, makes petrochemicals, inorganic chemicals, and intermediates.

BASF Canada has 11 production facilities and offices located across Canada.

The company operates six manufacturing facilities in Alberta, British Columbia, Ontario, and Quebec.

BASF sold its white expandable polystyrene (EPS) business in North and South America to Mexico-based Alpek . This 2014 divestiture comprised customer lists and current assets in addition to production facilities in Canada.

In 2007 the parent company sold its animal nutrition unit, and so BASF Canada discontinued two production sites in the country.

BASF Canada began operations in 1954.
