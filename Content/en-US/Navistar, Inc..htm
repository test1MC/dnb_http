Navistar, Inc., a primary operating subsidiary of Navistar International , manufactures medium- and heavy-duty trucks bearing the International brand name. In addition to hauling freight, uses for the company's heavy-duty trucks range from emergency vehicles to garbage trucks. Formerly known as International Truck and Engine, the company also makes MaxxForce brand diesel engines and IC Bus brand school and commercial buses. Navistar started building school buses in 1907 and is still one of the industry's largest manufacturers. The company was formed in 1902.

Navistar sells its products around the globe. The company operates twelve manufacturing and assembly facilities, ten distribution centers, and product development and engineering facilities at Lisle, Illinois; Melrose Park, Illinois; Madison Heights, Michigan; and Columbia, South Carolina.

In addition to making trucks for domestic use, the company is also a major supplier of trucks to the US military and is expecting to generate as much as $2 billion each year in the government sector.

The company sells and distributes its products through independent dealer network as well as through direct shipments. Commercial trucks are distributed through nearly 760 retail outlets in the US and Canada, 87 outlets in Mexico, and 360 international locations.

As part of its global growth strategy, Navistar is expanding its operations in China. The company uses strategic partnerships and product enhancements as part of its efforts to grow its business. In 2014, Anhui Jianghuai Navistar Diesel Engine Co., Ltd., the company's joint venture with Chinese truck maker Anhui Jianghuai Automobile Co. Ltd. , began engine production at its new one million sq. ft. facility in Hefei, China, with a capability of producing 200,000 Euro IV and Euro V compliant engines per year.
