UCM Group makes synthetic mineral crystals by melting and then cooling natural magnesite and zircon, which are then crushed and graded according to the customer's specifications. The material is then sold to customers who use them in insulation products, steelmaking, automotive products, and ceramic components. The company operates three divisions -- UCM Advanced Ceramics, UCM Magnesia, and UCM Zirconia, all of which have offices in the UK and the US. UCM Group is an indirect subsidiary of France-based Imerys , the world leader in industrial minerals, which acquired the company in 2007.
