The North American branch of France's Arkema , industrial chemicals producer Arkema Inc. manufactures many vinyl products, such as PVC, chlorochemicals, and assorted vinyl compounds. Serving a broad range of industrial end-users, its other products include industrial chemicals and plastics (acrylics, hydrogen peroxide, and fluorochemicals) and performance products (additives, agrochemicals, organic peroxides, and technical polymers). Arkema Inc. operates 24 manufacturing facilities in the Americas.

Arkema Inc. operates 34 sites in the US, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil.

The company has two research and development facilities in North America. Its corporate R&D center, located in King of Prussia, Pennsylvania, is responsible for the Americas and coordinates R&D between the U.S. and Arkema R&D in France. Its second US facility is in Cary, North Carolina and concentrates on the company's coating resins business.

Arkema Inc. operates three business segments: Industrial specialties, Coating solutions, and High performance materials. The company also has two research and development facilities in North America. Its corporate R&D center coordinates R&D between the US and Arkema R&D in France. Its other facility concentrates on the company's coating resins business.

Arkema serves customers in a range of markets, including adhesives and sealants, automotive, coatings, consumer goods, oil and gas, and photovoltaics. It has clients in Air conditioning and refrigeration; Chemical and plastics; Construction; Consumer goods; Electronics and electrical; Food and agrochemicals; Oil and gas; Packaging and paper; Paints, coatings and adhesives; Renewable energies; Transportation; and Water and the environment.

North America contributed 35% of the parent's company total sales in 2015.

Growth remained solid in North America sustained in particular by demand in the automotive market and some improvement in the construction market. On the other hand, industrial export-oriented markets were negatively affected by a stronger US dollar.

The company is reorganizing to become more efficient.

Arkema, Inc. is a subsidiary of Arkema .

Arkema was created in 2004 when TOTAL broke up its former chemicals subsidiary, ATOFINA , into two units: Arkema and TOTAL Petrochemicals .

In 2012 Arkema Inc. divested its tin stabilizer business to performance chemicals and plastics maker PMC Group. The divestment included organometallic products, such as Thermolite tin stabilizers, Fascat catalysts, and fine chemicals.
