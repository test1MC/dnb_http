Rodda Paint, one of the largest paint manufacturers in the Pacific Northwest, makes paints and other coatings that it sells in its more than 40 stores located throughout Alaska, Idaho, Oregon, and Washington. (It also sells through independent dealers in California, Montana, and Nevada.) Art Rodda founded Rodda Paint in 1932. In 2004 Rodda joined forces with an even larger regional paints maker, Canadian company Cloverdale Paint . The two companies merged operations but kept using separate names in their respective countries and maintained separate management teams.

The company has more than 40 branches in Washington, Oregon, Northern Idaho and Alaska and has a manufacturing plant in Portland, Oregon.

Rodda Paint manufactures and distributes paints and coatings for industrial, commercial, and residential painters. It offers interior paints and coatings, such as drywall primers and sealers, interior primers.

The company sells its products through a store network and independent dealers.
