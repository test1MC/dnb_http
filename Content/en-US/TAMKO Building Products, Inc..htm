TAMKO Building Products is ready if you're in the mood to raise the roof. The company manufactures residential and commercial roofing products, waterproofing membranes, railing and decking systems, roofing cements and coatings, and driveway sealants and crack fillers. Products include Lamarite composite shingles, MetalWorks steel shingles, and the Heritage Series of laminated asphalt shingles. Founded in 1944 by E.L. Craig, TAMKO is controlled by his family. His daughter, Ethelmae Humphreys, remains the company's chairman, while her son, David Humphreys, has been its CEO for nearly two decades.

Joplin, Missouri-based TAMKO has offices in Missouri, Maryland, Alabama, Texas, and Colorado, and distributes its products throughout the US and in Canada.

TAMKO mainly sells to homeowners, architects, and builders.

As part of its operations, TAMKO boasts more than a dozen manufacturing facilities and 12 TAMKO-operated warehouses.

In addition to its roofing products, the company's brands include EverGrain and Elements composite decking, TAM-Rail railing systems, and Awaplan rolled roofing material.

Customer-focused TAMKO offers clients a call center with live support from trained professionals. As an added bonus, it allows mixed truckload shipments and offers an extensive selection of color choices and distinctive palettes, such as America's Natural Colors and Classic Heritage Colors.

TAMKO seeks to become a vertically-integrated operation, producing as many of its raw materials as possible for its manufacturing plants.

It regularly rolls out new products and services, as well. In 2014 TAMKO introduced the QuickVent continuous ridge vent and EverGrain and Envision Composite Decking, following the 2013 debut of the expanded Heritage series of laminated asphalt shingles. In recent years TAMKO has rolled out new apps to better serve its mobile customers. In 2014 it launched the TAMKO Pro App for TAMKO Pro Certified Contractors and in 2012 the company made its interactive Shingle Styles app available on iPads.

To further its vertical integration, in 2011 TAMKO acquired a newly-built asphalt processing plant in West Virginia. The plant supplies asphalt coating and saturant to TAMKO's shingle manufacturing plant in nearby Frederick, Maryland.
