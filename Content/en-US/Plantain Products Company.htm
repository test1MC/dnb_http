Banana chips are so yesterday -- today's snack of choice is made from plantains instead. Plantain Products Company produces plantain chips, cassava chips, and other snack foods, such as tortilla chips and pork rinds. It also makes a dried plantain soup mix. The company's plantains and cassava are imported from Costa Rica and processed in the company's 20,000-sq.-ft. facility in Tampa, Florida. Distribution is concentrated on the East Coast and in Florida. The company's Chifles brand name comes from the Spanish term for plantain chips. Plantain Products Company is family-owned, founded by Segundo Argudo and his wife Peggy in 1965.
