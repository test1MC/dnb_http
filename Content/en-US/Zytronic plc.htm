Zytronic is in touch with its technology. The company make touch screens for information kiosks, ATM's, and ticketing and gaming machines. Its touch screens also can be found in military, computer, telecommunications, and medical applications. The company's projected capacitive technology embeds the sensing element behind the first surface of the touch substrate, which produces a more rugged screen. The UK-based company developed its touch screens working from its expertise in producing specialized laminates used in electronic equipment. The biggest market for Zytronic's products is Europe, which accounts for 25% of sales.

Zytronic makes its touch technology products in three factories at one site in Blaydon upon Tyne, UK. The company's in-house glass processing facilities include automated cutting, edge grinding, polishing, and drilling equipment complemented by bending and thermal tempering ovens and silk-screen printing equipment.

Zytronic also makes optical filters that make it easier to read information presented on electronic display. Its range of filters provide anti-vandal protection and minimize electromagnetic emissions and interference from electronic displays. The filter products and specialized laminated products go to the defense, rail, road, and automotive industries.

Beyond the European market, Zytronic's biggest markets are the Asia-Pacific region and Hungary, 16% of sales each, the US, 15%, South Korea, 13%, the UK and the Americas (not including the US), 7% each.

Zytronic its end customers in touch with its products through a network of channel partners around the world. Its marketing efforts include print and digital efforts that include YouTube, LinkedIn, and Twitter. In 2015 the company changed agencies to better coordinate traditional and digital marketing in the key markets of the US and China.

Revenue rose 5.2% in 2015 from 2014 on higher gaming market sales in the Americas and Korea. Sales in the US were off for the year.

Net income rose 19% on the higher revenue while accounting for higher costs, which included expenses associated with an acquisition.

Cash flow from operations also was higher in 2015 from 2014.

Zytronic has added a sales office in Taiwan and recruited resellers in the US and China to increase its penetration of those markets. It is investing in research and new manufacturing equipment to increase production of touch sensors to the ultra large category, those as big as 65 inches.
