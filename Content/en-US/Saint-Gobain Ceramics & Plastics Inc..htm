Saint-Gobain Ceramics & Plastics is one of the North American units of France-based parent Compagnie de Saint-Gobain 's High Performance Materials division, along with Saint-Gobain Abrasives and Saint-Gobain Reinforcements. Its business lines include the grains and powders unit, which makes colorants and proppants (used in fracking), and Saint-Gobain crystals, used in retail scanners, medical imaging machines, and microchips. The ceramics line consists of refractories that line glass furnaces, filters for diesel engines, and powders used in cosmetics. Its high-performance plastics line includes products used in everything from medical tubing to sealants in race cars to coated fabrics.

Saint-Gobain Ceramics & Plastics has a production network in Europe, North and South America, and Asia and operates research and development facilities in France and in the US.

The company operates as a part of Compagnie de Saint-Gobain 's High Performance Materials business, under the Innovative Materials segment. Saint-Gobain Ceramics & Plastics' products include technical ceramics, chemical process products, high-performance plastics, fusedcast refractory products, silicon carbide products, and special ceramic grains.

The parent company reported that Performance Plastics sales grew in all markets in 2013 (except solar), particularly in transportation, medical equipment, habitat, and energy, lifted by new products and a push to co-develop products with customers. Ceramic Materials reported very weak demand in the glass furnace refractories segment, particularly for Liquid Cristal Display (LCD) glass, as well as in the solar and metallurgy segments (not offset by a favorable market for catalyst substrates and zirconium-based abrasives). However, energy markets reported a significant growth in volume.

The High Performance materials business contributed about 48% of the Innovative Materials segment's revenues in 2013. The Innovative Materials segment accounted for 22% of Compagnie de Saint-Gobain's total revenues that year.

Looking to further develop a product line, in 2013 Saint-Gobain Ceramics & Plastics and Nano Labs entered into a mutual confidentiality agreement (for two years) to co-develop diamond powders, nano diamond slurries, and compounds, and related capabilities.

Company Background

The company was formerly known as Saint-Gobain Industrial Ceramics and changed its name to Saint-Gobain Ceramics & Plastics in 2000.
