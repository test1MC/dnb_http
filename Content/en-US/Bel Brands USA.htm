Bel Brands USA spreads its products far and wide but never too thinly. For US consumers, the company makes and markets the #1 branded snacking cheese, Mini Babybel natural cheese. Its variety of cheese products extend from Kaukauna aged cheddar spreads to The Laughing Cow processed wedges. Bel Brands USA's other brands include WisPride, Boursin, Merkts, and Owls Nest spreadable cheeses, as well as Price*s pimento spreads and Connoisseur gourmet spreads. The company's cheeses are available in cups, wedges, tubs, balls, and logs. These products are distributed nationwide to grocery stores and retail chains, such as Kroger and Wal-Mart . Bel Brands USA is a subsidiary of Paris-based Fromageries Bel .

Based in Illinois, Bel Brands sells its dairy products worldwide.

Bel Brands is known for its five global brands -- The Laughing Cow, Mini Babybel, Boursin, Leerdammer, and Kiri -- as well as 20 local brands.

Its portfolio is packed with more than 350 SKUs.

The company operates manufacturing plants in Kentucky and Wisconsin, with a third scheduled for completion in mid-2014 in South Dakota.

Bel Brands markets its products through retail grocery stores and warehouse clubs.More than 90% of retail grocery stores and warehouse clubs sell its products. Typical customers include behemoths the likes of Kroger and Wal-Mart , which enjoy an extended reach.

Parent company Fromageries Bel has reported that its Boursin business continues to exhibit strong growth potential. Mini Babybel is also logging sales increases, albeit at a slower pace, following several years of extraordinary growth for the top brand.

Indeed, in fiscal 2013 Bel Brands reported more than $325 million in annual sales. During the reporting period, the Americas and Asia/Pacific regions generated 15% of the parent company's revenue.

Through its parent's research, development, and production departments, the company works to improve packaging design and existing product quality and consistency. To continue to push for profits for its cheeses, Bel Brands in 2012 launched a Smooth Sensations cream cheese version of its popular Laughing Cow product.

Building a new production plant in South Dakota was necessary to keep up with demand for its Mini Babybel cheeses. Bel Brands is investing some $100 million to add this 17,000-sq.-ft. manufacturing facility to its existing footprint. Situated on a 48-acre land parcel, the South Dakota plant will initially produce 22 million pounds of Mini Babybel cheese each day and ultimately 1.5 million of the cheeses daily.

Bel Brands also moved to Chicago in 2011 to a 27,200-sq.-ft. space to provide the company with elbow room to grow.
