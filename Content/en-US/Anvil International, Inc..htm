In the hot red glow of industrial competition, Anvil International hammers out its profits by making a comprehensive range of pipe fittings, pipe hangers, and related products and services. Anvil International (Anvil) manufactures cast iron fittings, couplings, pipe fittings, pipe hangers, seamless pipe nipples, and valves. The company also provides basic design services, such as fabrication drawings, to extended design services for air handling units, commercial piping, oilfield piping, and single-line routing systems. Anvil has almost a dozen manufacturing facilities in the US and Canada. The company is a subsidiary of Atlanta-based Mueller Water Products .

Headquartered at Exeter, New Hampshire, Anvil operates manufacturing plants in Pennsylvania, Tennessee, Texas, and Rhode Island. In total, it operates through 15 facilities, out of which seven are distribution centers, with two residing in Canada. About 6% of the company's total sales came from Canada in 2013.

Anvil's primary markets are HVAC/industrial, plumbing, fire protection, mining, oil field, offshore, process industrial piping, power plant, and waste water treatment. It sells its products through resellers and more than 5,000 independent distributors nationwide, supported by a sales force of some 130 sales and customer service representatives and nearly 25 independent sales representatives.

Anvil's net sales increased 5% in 2013 due to an additional $13 million in shipping volumes during the year.

Anvil buys other businesses to fortify its core fabrication equipment operations. In 2012 it acquired North Alabama Pipe Corporation (NAP), a provider of pipe fabrication and fire protection fabrication equipment.

Anvil International's history dates back to the 1850s, when Frederick Grinnell (who subsequently invented the sensitive valve automatic sprinkler) acquired control of the newly formed The Providence Steam & Pipe Co. The company subsequently became the Grinnell Supply Sales division of Tyco International in 1999. Sold by Tyco in 2000, the manufacturer was renamed Anvil International in 2001.
