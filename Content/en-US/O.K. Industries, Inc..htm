O.K. Industries makes sure its chickens taste better than OK . The vertically integrated company heads up a hatchery operation, which produces eggs, chicks, and broilers. Its poultry-processing business, O.K. Foods, markets its fresh, frozen, and fully cooked chicken to foodservice providers and food processors in the US. The company's chicken commodity products, which include leg quarters, wing joints and tips, skin, and paws, are available on four continents. O.K. Foods also sells its TenderBird-branded products to restaurants, retail food outlets, wholesale clubs, and the military, as well as products under private labels. O.K. Industries is owned by Mexican-based Industrias Bachoco .

Industrias Bachoco bought O.K. Industries for about $95 million in cash in 2011.

O.K. Industries operates its business nationwide and sells its poultry products internationally.

Part of the Bachoco USA subsidiary, O.K. Industries boasts more than 800,000 square feet of modern processing facilities. As a vertically integrated chicken producer, the company controls all aspects of the supply chain from the family farms to processing.

The company serves several sectors, such as foodservice providers, food processors, restaurants, retail food outlets, wholesale clubs, and the military.
