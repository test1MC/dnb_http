Westrock-Solvay has risen, phoenix-like, from the ashes. The company, opened in 1994 by Southern Container , built its containerboard manufacturing plant on the site of an abandoned soda ash chemicals plant in a suburb of Syracuse, New York. The papermaker recycles corrugated boxes into liner board and corrugated medium (the layered product for making corrugated boxes). The company's New York plant has a production capacity of some 880,000 tons per year. In 2008 RockTenn (now Westrock ) acquired Southern Container, along with Solvay. The acquisition established WestRock as one of the largest manufacturers of containerboard in North America.
