When the power is on, ON Semiconductor semiconductors help its customers use less of it. The company designs and manufactures energy efficient, low-cost, high-volume analog, logic, and discrete semiconductors, with more than 50,000 devices in its catalog. These components perform vital power and signal control and interface functions in nearly every sort of electronic gear, from networking routers and wireless phones and digital cameras to household appliances and automotive braking systems. It sells directly to OEMs including Cisco , GE , Honeywell , Samsung , and Siemens , and to distributors such as Avnet and Arrow Electronics . Two-thirds of ON Semiconductor's sales come from the Asia/Pacific region.

The company is organized into four segments:

-- Standard Products includes MOSFETs, logic chips, and small signal chips, among others. It accounts for about 35% of sales.-- Application Products, which makes up about 30% of sales, is responsible for analog switches, linear light sensors, low-voltage power management chips, and tunable capacitors.-- The Image Sensor group, which makes CCD image sensors, CMOS image sensors, and linear light sensors, brings in about 30% of revenue.-- Systems Solutions makes microcontrollers, flash memory chips, motor driver ICs, and audio tuners and accounts for 15% of sales.

ON Semi has three manufacturing plants in the US - in New York, Idaho, and Oregon. Outside the US, plants are in Belgium, Canada, China, Czech Republic, Japan, Malaysia, Philippines, and Vietnam. The company manufactures silicon wafers and supplies to other facilities from Roznov, Czech Republic. Distribution centers are in China, Japan, Philippines and Singapore.

The company generates 32% of revenues from Singapore, followed by Hong Kong, 25%, the US, 16%, the UK, 14%, Japan, 8%, and the remainder from other countries.

Sales and Marketing

The company has about 450 direct customers in 70 countries. Automotive electronics is On Semi's biggest end market, accounting for 33% of sales. Customers include Bosch , Continental Automotive Group , and Valeo .

The industrial electronics market, in which customers include Dahua Technology, Delta Electronic, and Emerson Electric Co., generates 19% of the revenue.

Consumer electronics is about 16% of business for the company. Customers include GoPro , LG , and Microsoft . Another 14% of sales come from the computer market where customers include Apple and Lenovo . Communications is another double-digit market at 15%. Philips and Samsung are among the customers.

ON Semi serves 530 OEMs indirectly through distributors and electronic manufacturing service providers. The company generates 54% of sales through distributors, 39% through direct sales to OEMs, and 7% through direct sales to electronic manufacturing service providers.

ON Semi's revenue increased 10.5% in 2015 to $3.5 billion from $3.2 billion in 2014. For the second straight year the image sensor group drove revenue gain, adding $411 million to the company's revenue, a 134% growth rate. The other segments were flat or had lower sales in 2015.

Higher revenue and lower spending on restructuring left ON Semi with a 9% profit increase in 2015. The number hit $206 million, up from $189 million in 2014.

Cash flow from operations slowed to $470 million in 2015 from $481 million in 2014 because of a change in working capital.

Strategy

ON Semi wants to make its biggest market, automotive electronics, even bigger. The company's automotive products do jobs throughout vehicles including reducing emissions, improving fuel economy and safety, enhancing lighting, and controlling brakes and other systems. To move faster in a fast growing automotive market, ON Semi paid $400 million to buy Aptina Imaging, a provider of CMOS image sensors for automotive and industrial markets. Image sensors are increasingly used in Advanced Driver Assistance Systems. The acquisition helped drive image sensor sales 134% higher in 2015.

In 2016, On Semi introduced next generation products in two of its lines. In imaging, it added the Optical Image Stabilization (OIS) / Auto Focus (AF) driver to its portfolio. It brings a still camera and video to smart phones, tablets, notebooks, and wearables. Also in 2016 the company launched a new series of insulated gate bipolar transistors (IGBTs), which use its proprietary Ultra Field Stop trench technology.

Mergers and Acquisitions

ON Semi concluded its purchase of Fairchild Semiconductor in September 2016. The $2.4 billion acquisition will help ON Semi diversify with Fairchild's products for power conversion, industrial, and automotive markets.

The $8 million acquisition of AXSEM in 2015 added a range of low-power radio frequency (RF) chips for wireless connectivity to support advanced functionality in the Internet of Things (IoT).

In another move to strengthen its image sensor business, ON Semi acquired Truesense Imaging, a provider of image sensor devices for machine vision, surveillance, traffic monitoring, medical and scientific imaging, and photography. The $95 million cash deal expands ON Semi's technology portfolio and adds more than 200 new customers.

ON Semiconductor and Freescale were both spun off from Motorola in 1999, with ON Semi making analog, standard, advanced logic chips, as well as discrete small signal and power components. Investment firm TPG Capital owned 91% of ON Semi and Motorola held the rest. The next year ON Semi went public, raising more than $500 million in its IPO. Also that year it bought Cherry Semiconductor for about $250 million, which expanded it into multiphase controllers, drivers, and automotive/industrial power management components. In 2005 and 2006 it opened engineering centers in South Korea and Taiwan.

In 2008 ON Semi acquired AMIS Holdings , the parent company of AMI Semiconductor, for around $613 million in stock. The merger bolstered ON Semi's portfolio of analog and power management chips. Previous shareholders of ON Semi owned approximately 74% of the combined company. Also that year the company acquired Catalyst Semiconductor for about $115 million in stock. The purchase added analog and mixed-signal devices to its product portfolio, along with EEPROMs and flash memory devices.

In 2010 ON Semiconductor acquired California Micro Devices for $108 million in cash. Cal Micro made semiconductor protection devices for telecommunications and electronics markets applications, and its products enhanced ON Semiconductor's protection and lighting product portfolio. Also that year it bought Sound Design Technologies (SDT) for about $22 million in cash. The Canadian firm designs ultra-low-power semiconductors for hearing aids and audio processing applications. SDT became part of ON Semi's Medical Division in Ontario, Canada.
