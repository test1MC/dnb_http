Working on the Railroad: Gunderson has been practicing the lyrics since 1960. A subsidiary of The Greenbrier Companies , Gunderson is one of the leading manufacturers of railroad freight cars. Customers include major railroads, short-line railroads, railcar leasing concerns, and ocean shippers. Gunderson also offers railcar repair and refurbishing services and manufacture of ocean-going barges. The company's main facility is located at a deepwater port on the Willamette River, enabling the largest side-launch ways on the West Coast. The marine facilities also leverage steel plate burning and fabrication capacity which offers additional flexibility for its corporate parent's railcar production.

Gunderson's customers include railroads, shippers, leasing firms, and ocean shipping businesses.

In mid-2013 The Greenbrier Cos. laid off 200 workers at Gunderson's Northwest Portland plant. The company has been stung by delays in regional legislation related to a coal export proposal throughout 2013.

The company traces its historical roots all the way back to 1919, when Chester E. (Chet) Gunderson started the Wire Wheel Sales and Service Company of Portland, Oregon.
