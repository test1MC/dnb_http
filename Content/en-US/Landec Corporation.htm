Landec's products don't turn into pumpkins at midnight, but the changes are nearly as sudden and much more practical. The company has developed a technology that allows polymers to change physical characteristics when exposed to temperature changes. Its BreatheWay permeable membrane packaging allows oxygen and carbon dioxide to enter and escape from sealed fresh-cut produce packages to keep produce fresh. It's used primarily by subsidiary Apio, which grows and packages fresh vegetables. Landec's Lifecore Biomedical subsidiary is a leading supplier of premium hyaluronan-based biomaterials for the ophthalmic and orthopedic markets. Landec has licensing deals with Air Products and Chemicals and Monsanto .

The company has operations in the US, Asia, and Canada. It has facilities located in California, Minnesota, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Florida, South Carolina, and New York.

Landec has three operating segments namely, Food Products Technology (80% of net sales), Food Export (13%), and HA-based Biomaterials (7%).

Sales to its top five customers accounted for 46% of its revenues in 2015. Costco and Wal-Mart represented its two largest customers from its Food Products Technology segment, generating 21% and 11% of sales, respectively.

Landec's revenues peaked at a record-setting $539 million in 2015.The historic growth for 2015 was fueled by a 19% spike in Food Products Technology segment sales. This was driven by an increase in unit volume sales resulting primarily from new salad kit products which typically have a higher price per unit than historical offerings.

The company's net income, however, has declined the last few years, dropping 29% to $14 million in 2015. The decline was attributed to a spike in selling, general, and administrative expenses associated with the promotion of its new salad kit products and the addition of incremental headcount.
