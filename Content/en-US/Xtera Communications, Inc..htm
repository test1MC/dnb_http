Xtera Communications tries to provide extra performance to network infrastructures. The company makes fiber-optic transmission equipment used in both the terrestrial and undersea sections of telecommunications networks. Its Nu-Wave systems increase the bandwidth capacity of fiber-optic strands, enabling converged communications. Xtera also offers load balancers and traffic management devices for wide area networks. The company serves wholesale telecommunications carriers and other service providers; clients have included Pacnet . Xtera has attracted investments from a wide range of private equity firms, including Accel Partners , ARCH Venture Partners , New Enterprise Associates , and Sevin Rosen .

Xtera sells its products through distribution partners in the Americas, Europe, Africa, and Asia/Pacific.

In 2011 it made an effort to grow its Internet protocol network business by expanding its value added reseller program in North America, hoping to replicate some of its overseas success.

The company has pursued an acquisition strategy that has helped to expand its product portfolio, customer base, and geographic reach.
