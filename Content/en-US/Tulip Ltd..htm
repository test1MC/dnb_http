More fried breakfast than fragrant blooms, Tulip is one of the UK's largest meat groups. The company specializes in pig production, and rears, slaughters and processes its own pigs. It produces a wide variety of pork products, including bacon, deli meats and cooked meats. The company's brand names include Adams, Cherry Tree Farm, Alf Turner, Danepak, Plumrose, Celebrity, its namesake brand Tulip, and Parkam. The meat processor also markets SPAM, Stagg and Skippy peanut butter products in the UK for US-based Hormel. It is a subsidiary of Danish Crown group.

The company operates five business segments: Business to Business, Retail Own Label, Foodservice Solutions, Wholesale, and Exports.

Tulip produces over 4,700 own brand products, and produces around 135,000 tons of pork products to the UK market and exports some 60,000 tons to over 40 countries worldwide each year.

The company's Foodservice division works with clients to help them develop new products and recipe ingredients.

BQP, which is part of Tulip's subsidiary, Dalehead Foods, has 250 farmers who are largely based in East Anglia.

The company is UK based, and has 17 sites across the United Kingdom (Ashton, Bodmin, Boston, Bromborough, Bury St. Edmunds, Coalville, Corsham, Kings Lynn, Linton, Redruth, Ruskington, Spalding, Stradbroke, Wednesbury, Westerleigh, and Tipton).

Tulip's products are sold business to business, wholesale, in retail and food service markets. BQP, the pig production division of Dalehead Foods (acquired in 2004), is a supplier of British upmarket supermarket Waitrose.

The company supplies most of the UK's major supermarkets, including Waitrose, Marks & Spencer, Sainsbury's, Tesco, Asda, Aldi, Lidl and Morrisons.
