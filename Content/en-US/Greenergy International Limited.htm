Green is the theme for Green ergy International, which is the UK's largest independent low-emission fuels supplier. The company focuses on developing low-carbon fuels that blend biofuels (biodiesel or bioethanol) with mineral fuel. Green ergy sources globally, but also buys rapeseed for biodiesel production from UK farmers. It supplies its industrial and commercial clients with biodiesels made from waste oils and other virgin oils. Among its branded products is GlobalDiesel, which is sold by UK retail giant Tesco . The company supplies 15 billion liters of petrol, diesel, and biofuel a year and delivers more than 50% of all biofuel sold in the UK.

The company locations in Canada, the UK, the US, Dubai, and China.

In the UK Green ergy owns two biodiesel production facilities (one at Immingham and one at Seal Sands on Teesside) and has biofuel sourcing offices in Brazil and the US.

Green ergy operates two of Europe's largest waste-based biodiesel manufacturing facilities and a pre-processing facility allowing itto upgrade lower-quality waste oils to make them suitable for biodiesel production.

The company supplies fuel to oil companies and supermarkets, independent gas station owners and major end-users of fuel, such as bus and logistics companies.

The company supplies fuel from more than 26 terminals across the UK.

In 2015 Green ergy' s revenues decreased by 10% as a result of falling oil prices.

Its net income decreased by 71% that year, mainly due to lower net revenues and changes in exceptional items, which included the material items of income or expense and higher finance costs.

The company's operating cash inflows increased by 312%, mainly due to changes in working capital.

To keep up with its growth, Green ergy is investing in expanding its fuel storage terminals. It also offers carbon emission tracking and reduction services

The company added terminals in Plymouth in 2009, Teesside in 2010, and Cardiff in 2011.

Green ergy was founded in 1992. Tesco holds a minority stake in the private company.
