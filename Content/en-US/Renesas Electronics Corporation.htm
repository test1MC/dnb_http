Renesas Electronics makes microcontrollers, the tiny devices that regulate operations in products from computer printers to microwave ovens to automobiles. The company, formed when NEC Electronics and Renesas Technology combined chip operations in 2010, also turns out a wide array of microprocessors and power devices. Its products are mostly used in automotive (in the auto factory controlling robots as well as in the auto controlling a variety of functions), industrial and, consumer electronics applications.

More than half of the company's revenue comes from its general purpose business segment and 41% from automotive.

Some 42% of Renesas's revenue comes from Japan with 16% from China and another 18% from other Asian countries. The company's European and North American markets, which posted sales gains in 2015 (ended March) account for 14% and 8% of revenue.

The company has sales operations in Asia, North America, and Europe. Its manufacturing and engineering facilities are in Japan and Asia.

The company's major customers have included Ryosan Company , contributing 14% of revenue, and Sanshin Electronics Company , 9%.

In 2015, Renesas's revenue dropped 5%, which the company attributed to consolidation of customers. Net income rose, however, with lower expenses for the year and a lack of costs associated with issuing shares of stock.

Renesas reorganized some of its manufacturing operations to increase production efficiency and transferred some manufacturing assets in Japan to Sony Semiconductor. The company also reorganized the design and development operation of Renesas Electronics Corporation and of its wholly-owned subsidiaries Renesas Solutions Corporation, Renesas System Design Co., and Renesas Engineering Services Co. And Renesas absorbed its Renesas Mobile Corporation subsidiary as considers other measure for the mobile unit, which has left the 4G wireless business.

In 2017 Renesas closed its acquisition of Intersil for $3.2 billion. Intersil's power management chips complements the microcontroller business of Renesas and furthers the push by Renesas into the market for automotive electronic components. Intersil operates as a fully owned subsidiary of Renesas.
