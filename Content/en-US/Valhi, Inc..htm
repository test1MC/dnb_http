Valhi keeps it interesting by pursuing a variety of things. The company's NL Industries unit, operating through subsidiary Kronos , is a leading maker of titanium dioxide pigment, which is used to whiten and add opacity to fibers, paper, paint, and plastic. Other subsidiaries include CompX (ergonomic computer support systems and office security products), Tremont (titanium metal products for the aerospace and other markets through its stake in Titanium Metals ), and Waste Control Specialists (operator of hazardous-waste treatment facilities in Texas). It also has real estate assets.

Valhi operates worldwide through its 17 fully owned and six majority owned subsidiaries. Its global operations span several geographical segments, including North America, Europe, and Asia. Europe accounted for 46% of the company's revenues in 2014; North America, 40%; Asia, 12%; and other countries, 2% .

The company operates in four segments: Chemicals (titanium dioxide); Component Products (computer and office equipment); Waste Management; and Real Estate Management and Development. Valhi subsidiary Kronos is among the world's largest makers of titanium dioxide, behind DuPont , Tronox , and Huntsman . Other companies making up Harold Simmons' portfolio include wire-maker Keystone Consolidated Industries .

The Chemical segment (titanium dioxide, a whitening pigment used in a wide range of applications such as coatings, plastics and paper, as well as many specialty products such as inks, food and cosmetics) accounted for 87% of the company's 2014 revenues.

The company sells its chemical products via distributors and agents, and provides technical services for their products to 4,000 customers in 100 countries, primarily in Europe and North America.

The Waste Management segment's target customers are industrial companies, including nuclear utilities, chemical, aerospace and electronics businesses and governmental agencies, including the Department of Energy , which generate low-level radioactive, hazardous waste.

CompX sells directly to large OEM customers through their factory-based sales and marketing professionals.

The company's revenues fluctuated each year in the last five years.

In 2014 Valhi's revenues decreased by 2.4% was mainly due to the Chemicals segment's net sales decline of 5% as the result of lower titanium dioxide prices caused by competitive market pressures, changes in level of supply and demand as well as changes in raw material and other manufacturing costs. Sales volumes remained stable, as slightly higher sales in Europe were offset by lower sales in certain export markets.

Sales from other segments increased due to higher demand in component product segment, a rise in disposable volumes in waste management segment, and revenues from real estate.

Net income was $53.8 million in 2014 (compared to a loss of $151.8 million in 2013) due to a decrease in selling, general, and administrative costs, the absence of loss on prepayment of debt, and a benefit from income tax.

Cash from operating activities decreased by 43% due to changes in changes in receivables, inventories, payables and accrued liabilities and higher net cash paid for income taxes.

The company pursues a diversified growth strategy, buying and selling business to enhance its long term profitability.

Valhi's titanium marketing strategy is aimed at working directly with customers to monitor the success of its products in their end-use applications, evaluate the need for improvements in product and process technology and identify opportunities to develop new product solutions for their customers. It also focuses its sales and marketing efforts on those geographic and end-use market segments for higher selling prices, including continuously reviewing and optimizing its customer and product portfolios.

In 2013 the company purchased an additional ownership interest in and became the majority owner of Basic Management, Inc. (which provides utility services to certain industrial customers, and owns real property in Henderson, Nevada) and The Landwell Company (commercial, industrial and residential land holdings in Henderson).

Bringing new waste sites online, in 2012 the company commenced full scale operations at the Compact LLRW disposal facility and the Federal LLRW disposal location, both in Texas.

To raise cash, that year Valhi's CompX unit sold its furniture components business for $58 million.

Chairman Harold Simmons and his family control about 93% of Valhi and thus control all of the company's subsidiaries.
