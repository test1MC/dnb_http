Going up? ThyssenKrupp Elevator looks to elevate the elevator operations of its parent company, global industrial giant ThyssenKrupp AG . Through divisions spread across five continents, the company makes, distributes, and services passenger and freight elevators, escalators, and moving walkways. It also offers handicapped accessibility products, such as stairway lifts, platform lifts, and home elevators. The company's products are used in such prominent buildings as the Sydney Opera House, London's City Hall, and New York's high-rise Time Warner Center. ThyssenKrupp provided over 70 elevators and 12 escalators for the One World Trade Center, under construction at the site of the former Twin Towers.

ThyssenKrupp's elevator business is organized across four business units. Three units concentrate on the regional elevator and escalator business (Europe/Africa, Americas and Asia/Pacific). The other one operates globally as Access Solutions (passenger boarding bridges, chair, service and platform lifts, as well as home elevators).

The company accounted for 17% of the total net sales of its parent company in 2015. Elevator Technology's sales were 12% higher compared to the previous year as a result of stronger demand for new installations and positive exchange-rate effects.

As part of the growth strategy, the company continues to invest in the international expansion of its service network and production sites.
