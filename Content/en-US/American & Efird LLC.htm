A stitch in time may save nine; it may also secure the bottom line of American & Efird (A&E). The company manufactures and distributes industrial sewing and embroidery threads, and engineered yarns. Its products are used by both consumers and OEMs of apparel, automotive materials, home furnishings, and medical supplies. In addition to threads and yarns, the company distributes cords, notions, twines, zippers, and related supplies made by other companies such as YKK . A&E holds a handful of manufacturing plants and distribution hubs in the US, and multiple international operations and ventures worldwide.

A&E operates 23 manufacturing facilities and some 75 distribution locations in more than 50 countries spanning Asia, Europe, and the Americas. More than half of the company's revenues are generated outside of the US, in Asia, Africa, Europe, Oceania, and Latin America.

A&E divides its operations into four segments: Industrial Sewing Thread, Embroidery Thread, Consumer Products, and Technical Textiles. The company has more than 10,000 shades and offers a global color card that provides 1,024 popular shades.

The company maintains a close ties to its market via an in-house sales force, commissioned agents, and distributors that sell to some 7,300 domestic and 6,000 international customers. It also sells to consumers through US online retailers, such as Sew True and eQuilter.com.

The thread-maker's sales are driven mainly by demand for its industrial sewing thread used in apparel manufacturing. Although the industrial thread portfolio is used in a diversity of applications, A&E's customer base is competitive and consumer-sensitive. Going forward, A&E is aggressively expanding its foreign production and distribution activities by purchasing other manufacturers, entering joint ventures, and launching start-up businesses.
