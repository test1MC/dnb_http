InnerWorkings has inserted itself into the nuts and bolts of the corporate printing world. The company procures, manages, and delivers printed products (brochures, catalogs, and other promotional materials) to companies in the advertising, consumer products, publishing, and retail industries. InnerWorkings' proprietary software application and database, PPM4, matches customers' jobs with printing companies' equipment and capacity. The InnerWorkings system submits a job to multiple printers, who then bid for the business. Approximately 10,000 suppliers participate in the company's network, which includes includes printers, graphic designers, paper mills and merchants, digital imaging companies, and binders.

The company has about 70 global offices in more than 35 countries around the world.

InnerWorkings operates three segments based on geographic regions: North America, Latin America, and EMEA. The North America segment includes operations in the US and Canada; the Latin America segment includes operations in Mexico, South America, and Central America; and the EMEA segment includes operations in the UK, continental Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia.

InnerWorkings serves industry such as retail, financial services, hospitality, consumer packaged goods, not-for-profits, healthcare, food and beverage, broadcasting and cable, and transportation. The company served more than 7,000 clients during fiscal 2014. Its enterprise clients accounted for 79% of revenue and its middle market clients accounted for 21% of revenue during the year.

The company's net revenue has enjoyed steady growth in recent fiscal years. Its revenue increased by 12% to $1 billion in fiscal 2014, compared to $890 million in fiscal 2013. The increased revenue was the result of increased sales in North America.

InnerWorkings suffered a net loss of $8.66 million in fiscal 2013 due to high operating costs and restructuring costs. In fiscal 2014 the company posted a net income of more than $44 million due to higher annual revenue and the absence of goodwill and restructuring charges.

The company uses multiple partnerships to expand its business. It has agreements with companies such as Newsday , Sanofi , and Young & Rubicam Mexico.
