MEGA Brands want to ensure that today's kids grow up to be creative blokheads. The company makes plastic building blocks and other construction and magnetic toys, as well as puzzles, and games for preschoolers on up. Its toy brands include Mega Bloks, Mega Games, Mega Puzzles, and Magnext. Its Rose Art and Board Dudes businesses make and sell art materials, including crayons, markers, paints, and modeling dough, and presentation boards and writing instruments. While North America accounts for about two-thirds of the company's sales, its products are sold in more than 100 countries worldwide. US toy giant Mattel acquired MEGA Brands in 2014 for $460 million.

Mattel completed its purchase of MEGA Brands in April 2014 for $460 million (C$17.75 per share in cash). The toymaker was attracted to MEGA Brands' construction product line, second only to LEGO . Mattel plans to maintain MEGA Brands' headquarters in Montreal.

The company operates in two segments: Toys, which contributes 75% of revenue and includes Mega Bloks and Mega Puzzles, and Stationery and Activities, which comprises ROSE ART arts and crafts sets, BOARD DUDES presentation boards, and WRITE DUDES writing instruments.

MEGA Brands is headquartered in Montreal with operations in the UK, Spain, Belgium, France, Mexico, Switzerland, and Australia. North America accounts for about 70% of sales.

In the US, its products are available at Target , Toys "R" Us, and Wal-Mart Stores , among others.

MEGA Brands' 2013 revenue was $404.7 million, 4% less than the previous year. A 5% drop in global toy sales was offset by a 1% increase in the Stationery business. The company cut expenses and got a lucky break on currency conversions to post a 25% uptick in net income, from $16.6 million to $20.8 million.

Like most toy makers, MEGA Brands relies heavily on licensing deals. In 2014 it inked deals with Universal Pictures for construction toys based on the Despicable Me franchise and with Nickelodeon for SpongeBob SquarePants products.
