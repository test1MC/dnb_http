American Labelmark wants to take some of the danger out of hazardous materials. The firm's Labelmaster division sells books, training manuals, and protective equipment (such as steel salvage drums and spill kits) to companies that transport hazardous materials, helping them navigate the maze of government rules. The Chicago-based firm also makes labels and signage for hazardous materials and infectious waste transportation, along with labels concerning workplace safety and ergonomics. Its Reg-Trieve software division sells compliance materials on compact disks. American Labelmark also offers regulatory information online.

Among Labelmaster's best-known products are the company's Duo-Flip and Spacemaster systems, which can be seen on the backs of tankers and trailers carrying hazardous materials. Many of the placards display identification codes that are meaningless to most of us -- especially when glimpsed at 70 mph -- but vital to those in the know.

The Chicago-based firm serves customers in the US and globally.
