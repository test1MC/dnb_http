Fleury Michon provides a full menu. The company offers processed meats, ready-made meals, and other convenience food products for the supermarket and the foodservice sectors. As well as hams, poultry, roasts, and charcuterie (deli meats), it makes surimi (imitation crab). Fleury operates the Graine d'Appétit chain of restaurants and vending kiosks. France accounts for most of the company's sales but it also sells ready-meals in Canada, Italy, Spain, and the US. Star chef and company culinary advisor Joël Robuchon develops many Fleury Michon recipes. While publicly traded since 2000, the majority of Fleury Michon stock is held by chairman Yves Gonnord.

Fleury Michon operates three segments: French Supermarkets, International Operations and Services. French Supermarkets is the largest by revenue, bringing in 87% of total sales; International Operations and Services account for 6% and 7% of revenue respectively.

The company sells 37,500 Fleury Michon branded products.

Fleury Michon is headquartered in Pouzauges, France, and has a presence in 8 countries and 15 production sites, including 7 outside France.

The company sells about 381 million units every year to customer groups including airlines, businesses, and hospitals/healthcare.

Revenue nudged up 1% in 2014 to €707.2 million due to strong increases in demand from Italy and Spain. French Supermarket revenue rose 1.7%, outperforming the market. Services grew 14.5% due to favorable sales trends in delivered meals, airline catering and healthcare meals.

Net income increased 12% to €18.2 million due to higher sales and lower employee benefits and income tax.

Cash from operating activities increased 34% on prior year to €52.1 million.

Fleury Michon aims to expand its brand geographically through joint ventures, acquisitions and the sale of technology.

The company has reorganized to reduce siloing and promote cross-functional activities, with the goal of generating synergies and increasing competitiveness.
