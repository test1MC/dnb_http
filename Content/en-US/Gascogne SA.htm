Gascogne (formerly Groupe Gascogne) is clearing paths to success in France's Landes Forest. The company's core businesses include wood, paper, packaging, and laminates. Gascogne's products include bags, boxes, wood pallets, pressure-treated wood, sealing systems, paper for envelopes, and aluminum/paper composites. The company serves clients -- ranging from construction and insulation to chemicals and paper processing businesses -- in more than 70 countries through some 20 subsidiaries and nearly 20 production plants. French diversified group Électricité et Eaux de Madagascar (EEM) owns about 30% of Gascogne. The company was incorporated in 1925 as Papeteries de Gascogne.
