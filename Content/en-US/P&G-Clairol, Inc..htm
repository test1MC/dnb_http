Clairol is big on hair care -- mostly focused on covering up the gray and helping to keep non-blondes blond. A unit of consumer products behemoth Procter & Gamble , P&G-Clairol specializes in making and marketing hair-coloring products, hair spray, shampoo, conditioner, and hair-styling items for both women and men. Some of its brands include Nice 'n Easy, Natural Instincts, and Age Defy. Besides all-over color, the beauty company also sells highlighting products for touches of hair color. Clairol markets its products in the US and abroad. One of P&G's larger acquisitions (behind Gillette ), P&G-Clairol is part of its beauty segment.

Based in Connecticut, P&G-Clairol serves consumers in North America, the UK, Australia, and New Zealand.

Clairol offers several hair-coloring options, including permanent, demi-permanent, and semi-permanent hair colors.

The company operates as part of P&G's Beauty segment, which generated some 24% of its parent's fiscal 2013 revenue. P&G's hair care and color business accounted for 11% of sales.

P&G-Clairol sells its hair care products through retail outlets and online through retail partners.

To cater to more customers, P&G-Clairol has been working to broaden and deepen its products portfolio, such as creating a brand extension for its Nice 'n Easy line called Perfect 10. The company continues to expand its hair-care items to target a more diverse customer base.

Banking on its existing brands and morphing them to attract new audiences also allows P&G to sidestep pricey research and development costs, which it already incurs for several of its other brands. Additionally, P&G-Clairol is not one of P&G's billion-dollar brands, so investing further in a second-tier brand is not prudent.

While its parent does not report separate financials for P&G-Clairol, the consumer products company reports that its Beauty segment's net sales dropped some 2% in fiscal 2013 to $20 billion. It lost more than half a point in global market share of the hair care and color category. The company logged continued low single-digit increases in developing regions thanks to innovation and market growth, despite low single-digit drops in developed regions from reduced shipments.
