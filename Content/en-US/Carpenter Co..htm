It's a cushy job for Carpenter Company, making polyurethane foam and chemicals and polyester fiber used as cushioning by the automotive, bedding, floor covering, packaging, and furniture industries. The company started out making foam rubber; it now also manufactures air filter media, expanded polystyrene building materials, accoustical non-wovens, and a tire fill product that can be used as a replacement for air in off-road construction vehicles. Carpenter also sells consumer products -- which include craft fiber products, furniture cushioning foam, mattress pads, and pillows -- through retailers.

Carpenter has 19 foam-producing plants in the US and globally. The company maintains 56 sales or manufacturing locations in the US (in 16 states), Canada (in five provinces), and Europe (in six countries).

The company's products include urethane foam, molded foam, furniture cushioning foam and fiber application, fiber, air filtration media, bedding, consumer products, and "in place" packaging foam. Carpenter's M. H. Reinhart Technical Center (in Richmond, Virginia) serves as the core research and development laboratory for Carpenter's engineers, scientists, and technicians.

The company sells its products through retailers and distributors. Its customers include Chrysler , Ford , GM , and Toyota .

Growing its global footprint, Carpenter has a polyol manufacturing technology license agreement with Saudi Arabia. It is partnering with a joint venture between Saudi Advanced Industries Company and National Industrialization Company which in 2014 built a polyol manufacturing plant that country. Polyol demand in the Middle East is expected to grow at 8% to 11% a year.

With more than $1 million dollars invested in testing equipment, the Reinhart Technical Center provides critical R&D support as well as customer application support across the complete line of company products.

In 2012 Carpenter announced the introduction of a Quick Response Code (a matrix barcode first designed for the automotive industry) to help improve its product marketing and sales activities by supply prompt and accurate data about shipment and sales.

Carpenter (founded in 1948 by Rhodes Carpenter as a distributor of Goodyear latex products) is owned by chairman and CEO Stanley Pauley. The founder brought the flexible urethane foam manufacturing process from Germany to the US in 1956.
