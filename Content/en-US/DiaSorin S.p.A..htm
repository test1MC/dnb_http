DiaSorin gives the tests no one wants to take. The company produces in vitro diagnostics, or tests using blood, urine, and other patient samples. Its 260 immuno assay kits, in conjunction with its ETI-MAX 3000 and LIASON machines, are used by hospital and private labs working in infectious disease, endocrinology, bone metabolism, autoimmunity, hypertension, and oncology. Operating through two dozen subsidiaries, including six manufacturing and research facilities in Germany, Ireland, Italy, South Africa, the UK, and the US, DiaSorin has sales organizations in more than 60 countries. The firm was formed in 1968 as part of Italian medical device company Sorin , which spun DiaSorin off as a public company in 2007.
