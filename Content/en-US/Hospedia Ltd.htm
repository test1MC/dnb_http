Hospedia provides the health care industry with electronic bedside consoles that combine communications and entertainment functions. The company's touchscreen products give patients access to a telephone, a radio, a television, and internet access from their hospital beds. Hospedia primarily serves hospitals in the UK, but also does business in mainland Europe. The company, founded in 1994, has installed about 70,000 devices in 160 hospitals. Its products are used by many health care providers in the UK National Health Service including Plymouth Hospitals, as well as Royal Bournemouth and Christchurch Hospitals. Hospedia was taken private by investment firm Marlin Equity Partners in 2010.

Hospedia's products go beyond the bedside. Its Clinical Solutions package of products provides hospitals with software and hardware to digitally manage patient care, reducing time and administration expenses. The tools are used in acute, community, mental health, and social care settings. The company's Patient Engagement Solutions connect patients with their health and care at the bedside through surveys, menu display and meal ordering, translation tools, and healthcare education.

The company has a partnership with GE Capital for capital expenditure funding and to expedite installation.
