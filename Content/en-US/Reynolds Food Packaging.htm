Reynolds Packaging (formerly Reynolds Food Packaging) has food wrapped, sealed, served, and stored. It offers a comprehensive array of packaging products for the restaurant, agricultural, supermarket, and food processor markets. Products are made from aluminum foil, plastic film, and molded plastic; they include containers, platters, lids, wraps, bags, and baking cups and liners. Custom-made products are also available to its customers, which include bakeries, caterers, delis, and fast food joints. Reynolds Food Packaging's has operations in the US, Canada, Mexico, Spain, and the UK. Parent Reynolds Group Holdings is part of Rank Group Ltd., an investment firm controlled by New Zealand billionaire Graeme Hart.

Parent Reynolds has locations in Argentina, Bahrain, Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Costa Rica, Egypt, Germany, Hungary, Japan, Mexico, Nepal, Peru, the Philippines, Russia, South Korea, Spain, the UK, the US, and Venezuela.

The company makes plastic and aluminum closures; capping equipment; papers and foils for baking, cooking, and grilling; and products ranging from private label food and disposer bags, packaging closures, and soil stabilization materials to specialty stretch films.

Ranked as one of North America's top 20 film and sheet makers, Reynolds Packaging keeps pace with the market's evolving appetite with a stream of new consumer and foodservice products. Its launches include a 100% recycled aluminum foil wrap, Micro Smart microwaveable plastic containers and lids for prepared meals, and nonstick foil for use in foodservice kitchens. Reynolds Packaging serves customers worldwide in the soft drink, juice, bottled water, milk and dairy products, beer, food, liquor, pharmaceutical, personal care, automotive fluids, and other markets.

Looking to lighten its debt load and free up capital, in 2013 Reynolds was reviewing its ownership of debt-laden companies Evergreen Packaging, Closure Systems International, and SIG Combibloc.

Reynolds Packaging originated as a division of aluminum products company Arconic (formerly Alcoa).

Reynolds Packaging was formed by the combination of Ivex Packaging and Reynolds Foodservice Packaging -- both Alcoa acquisitions. Alcoa bought Reynolds for its strength in foodservice packaging and its line of aluminum products. Reynolds also produces stock and custom items including foil, film, lids, and bags for the foodservice, supermarket, and food processor markets. Alcoa purchased Ivex Packaging for its strength as a specialty plastic packaging maker (plastic containers, microwavable packaging) for food processors and retail supermarkets. Alcoa combined the operations into one company in 2003 but sold it in 2008.

(In 2008 Alcoa sold Reynolds Packaging and its other packaging and consumer businesses to Rank Group for $2.7 billion.)
