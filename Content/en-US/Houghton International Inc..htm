It might not have been called the Rust Belt if they'd used Houghton International's products more often. The Hinduja Group company is the world leader in industrial fluids, providing both high-tech fluid products and fluid management services. Houghton manufactures oils and specialty chemicals for lubrication in several major industries: metalworking, automotive, and steel as well as offshore deep-water oil drilling. Its products range from aluminum and steel rolling lubricants to rust preventatives to fire-resistant hydraulic fluids. Its Fluidcare system helps manufacturers reduce costs through chemical management and recycling.

Based in Pennsylvania, Houghton maintains sales offices and representatives in about 50 countries and 14 manufacturing facilities in 12 countries. In addition to Houghton's Valley Forge R&D facility, the company also operates five research labs in Europe (Germany, France, Italy, Spain, and the UK). Houghton's labs in China, Thailand, Brazil, and Australia provide local technical support and new product deployment.

Operations

The company makes oils and specialty chemicals for lubrication in metalworking, automotive and steel manufacturing, and offshore deep-water oil drilling. Its products range from aluminum and steel rolling lubricants to rust preventatives and fire-resistant hydraulic fluids.

Houghton serves customers in a broad range of industries, including aerospace, automotive, appliances, defense, food and beverage, machinery and equipment, marine, metalworking, packaging, offshore oil production, spring and wire steel, and wind energy.

The company is focusing on expanding its customer service operations and growing its worldwide network. In response to the global push for 'greener' solutions, Houghton has developed more than 10 new proprietary formulations to meet government requirements and customer needs.

To enhance its service capability, in 2014 Houghton acquired Envirotek Management Services. This purchase complements its FLUIDCARE Chemical Management Service offering and will help Houghton better serve its North American customer base. That year it also bought Henkel 's North American Steel Mill business, given it key resources in sales, technology and business development to continue to serve its existing and new customers in the North American steel industry.

The company is owned by Gulf Oil Corporation Ltd (part of India's Hinduja Group), which acquired the company from AEA Investors in 2012 for more than $1 billion.

In 2011 Houghton acquired Shell Metalworking and Metal Rolling Oils. Shell is a specialty fluid manufacturer in the global metal working and metal rolling fluids marketplace. The deal enables Houghton to provide its global technology and application capabilities to a broader customer base, expanding its ability to service customers in Europe and in rapidly growing markets such as India, Brazil, China and Russia.

In 2010, as part of a settlement of a Federal Trade Commission complaint, Houghton sold the aluminum hot rolling oil (AHRO) business of subsidiary D.A. Stuart to Quaker Chemical Corporation . The FTC had claimed that Houghton's acquisition of D.A. Stuart in 2008 put it in control of almost 75% of the AHRO market in North America, allowing it to fix prices in the absence of competition. Houghton retained the rest of D.A. Stuart's business lines.
