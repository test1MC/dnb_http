GEA PHE Systems North America (formerly FlatPlate) makes brazed plate heat exchangers for refrigeration and hydronic heating applications in residential, commercial, and industrial process installations. The company's refrigeration division supplies heat exchangers used for evaporators and condensers, while its hydronic heaters are used in boilers and hot water heating. The company also holds advanced heat exchanger patents for fluid chillers. GEA PHE Systems North America also makes replacements for out-of-date shell and tube heating systems. The company is a subsidiary of Triton Advisers Limited.

GEA PHE Systems North America operates in 20 countries.

GEA PHE Systems North America offers a range of plate heat exchangers in three product groups: Gasketed (EcoFlex); Fully Welded (EcoWeld); and Brazed (EcoBraze). The specialty manufacturer's heat exchangers consist of four to 200 stainless steel plates with a special heat transfer surface which are copper or nickel brazed in a vacuum furnace.

It also operates EcoServe, which is dedicated to after-sales and service for plate heat exchangers of all brands.

The company's products are used by customers in a range of industries, including HVAC, power, chemical industry, refrigeration, marine, renewable energy, food, and paper.

Triton Advisers Limited is looking to develop GEA PHE Systems North America as a standalone entity.

Founded in 1851, Ahlborn Company made the first plate heat exchanger in 1930. FlatPlate Inc., founded in 1992, quickly established a leadership position in brazed plate heat exchangers. GEA Group acquired Flatplate in 2005.
