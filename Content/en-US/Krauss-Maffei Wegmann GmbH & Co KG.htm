Heeding General MacArthur's words, Krauss-Maffei Wegmann (KMW) is not retreating -- it is advancing in another direction. The company that once supplied arms solely for the Bundeswehr develops defense products for the world market. KMW's core business is manufacturing and overhauling wheeled and tracked armored vehicles, from heavy combat tanks (LEOPARD) to light-armored vehicles (Mungo). It welds assemblies from high-strength steel for aircraft, too. KMW also makes IT parts for weapons systems. Manufacturing and repair parts-subsidiaries in Greece and the US serve foreign military markets. In 2011 Siemens sold its 49% stake for an undisclosed amount to the Wegmann Group, which already owned a 51% stake in KMW.
