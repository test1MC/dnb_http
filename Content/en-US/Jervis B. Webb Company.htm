Jervis B. Webb Company, operating as Daifuku Webb, has spun a global web of custom-engineered material handling systems and maintenance support services. Through facilities located around the world, the firm makes equipment used in multiple markets including automotive production, bulk and baggage handling, automated newsprint handling, beverage bottling, product distribution, and furniture manufacturing. Products include conveyors, automatic guided vehicles (branded under the SmartCart name), automated storage and retrieval systems, cleanroom systems, and loading vehicles. Webb is part of the Daifuku Webb division of Japanese-based material-handling firm Daifuku Co., Ltd .

Webb-designed baggage conveying systems can be found in more than 50 airports around the globe. Jervis B. Webb and the Daifuku Webb division share a headquarters location in Farmington Hills, Michigan. The company has domestic locations in Michigan and South Carolina and international locations in India and Canada.

Daifuku Webb accounts for about 18% of the global Daifuku parent organization's revenues. Its revenue increased in 2014 due to acquisitions, increased production, and organic growth.

Moving beyond North America, Daifuku is looking to leverage technology developed by Webb to expand into emerging markets in Asia (China and India) and the Middle East. The company is also growing by moving into related industries, such as automated warehouses for airline components.

To achieve its expansion goals, Daifuku is looking to acquisitions. For instance, in 2013 Daifuku Webb acquired Wynright Corporation to expand its warehousing and distribution operations; Wynright makes intelligent material handling systems (including through its Automotion subsidiary).

Jervis Bennett Webb founded the company in 1919. He is credited with inventing the forged rivet-less chain conveyor that helped revolutionize mass production.
