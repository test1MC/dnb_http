Hobart makes a variety of commercial foodservice equipment including Baxter brand baking equipment (ovens and proofers); Traulsen brand refrigerator/freezer equipment and prep tables; and Advansys conveyor and door-type warewashing equipment, as well as waste equipment. A short list of Hobart products includes bar code scanners, fryers, griddles, rotisseries, food processors, meat saws and slicers, scales, and wrapping equipment. Its service unit provides installation, maintenance, and repair for all of its equipment. A subsidiary of Illinois Tool Works , Hobart also services the equipment of sister companies, including Vulcan .

Hobart manufactures its products at facilities in Georgia, Kentucky, Ohio, and Texas. International locations reside in Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, Italy, and the UK.

Hobart's Advansys line of low-water, energy-efficient warewashing systems uses less than a gallon of water per rack, and cleans pots and pans, as well as dishware and utensils. The ventless design saves on energy costs, because there is no fan to operate. To promote more efficient foodservice equipment, Hobart launched its Sustainable Kitchen. Supported by the Hobart Center for Foodservice Sustainability, the products from this unit are designed to save water and energy, as well as reduce waste.

Hobart's sales network spans more than 200 locations with 1,700 service representatives worldwide. The company primarily caters to the food institutional and restaurant, service, and food retail markets.

Hobart is part of the Food Equipment segment of Illinois Tool Works (ITW). This segment contributed 14% of ITW's net revenue for 2013. The segment's revenues also increased by 6% in 2013 primarily due to additional sales from acquisitions and an increase in base revenues.

Product development and enhancement drive the company to design new technology for its customers, which are mostly institutions and restaurants. The company is expanding its product line to include more energy-efficient items such as solid door refrigerators and freezers, as well as its Advansys technology.

The company has been in business for more than a century. It started as Hobart Electric Manufacturing Company in 1897. Illinois Tool Works acquired Hobart in the 1990s.
