You are traveling into the Forth Dimension. The company's name celebrates its four-dimensional (i.e., time-based) video display devices, as well as its location on Scotland's scenic Firth of Forth waterway. Forth Dimension Displays (FDD) supplies high-resolution, near-to-eye microdisplay used in night vision systems, video camera viewfinders, medical imaging systems, and flight training simulators. The company is also adapting its ferroelectric liquid-crystal-on-silicon technology for use in gaming and other home entertainment applications, as well as in digital film printing and data storage. FDD has a support office in Germany, and sales offices in California and Japan. FDD is owned by Kopin .

Besides its facility in Scotland, FDD also is present in Germany, Japan, Sweden, Israel, and Asia.

FDD contributes about 11% of its parent's revenue. The unit's 2015 revenue was $3.5 million, a 1% increase over its 2014 revenue.
