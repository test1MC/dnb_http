Kimberly-Clark de México (also known as Kimber) has the Latin American consumer paper market tightly swaddled. A 48%-owned affiliate of Kimberly-Clark Corporation , the company claims Mexico's top spot in making disposable consumer paper products including KleenBebé and Huggies diapers, Kleenex tissues, and Kotex feminine care products, as well as toilet paper, paper towels, and napkins. The firm was folded into the Kimberly-Clark consumer products business in 1955; it merged with Scott Paper's Mexican Cirsoba unit in 1995. (Both companies then divested certain operations.) In addition to serving its domestic market, Kimber exports throughout Latin America.
