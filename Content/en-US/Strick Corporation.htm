Traffic jams might occasionally cause you to get stuck behind Strick. The company makes standard dry van trailers -- the kind that make up the back half of the tractor-trailer rigs that haul freight on interstate highways throughout the US. Besides making standard trailers, Strick customizes its trailers to accommodate specific product hauling needs, including trailers with dual side doors, recessed floors, ceiling tracks, drop frame, and second deck storage options. The company's trailers are sold via a network of independent dealerships in the US and Canada. Strick makes its trailers at a plant in Monroe, Indiana.
