Unsure exactly how contactless card payments work? Just ASK. The French company specializes in the design, development, manufacture, and market of contactless communication technology -- typically found in bank cards and passports. The company supplies cards, paper tickets, smart labels, terminals, services (personalization, operating systems), inlays, and shielding solutions. It works mainly in the public mass transit sector. Nations using ASK's contactless passport technology include the US and the UK, and ASK company has delivered contactless ticketing systems for major events including the 2006 Winter and 2008 Summer Olympics. ASK was founded in 1997. In 2016, ASK agreed to merge with Paragon's Data Identification division.

ASK's range of smart cards have functions including memory storage for ID purposes, contactless payment, Ultra-High Frequency chips with a range of up to 5 meters, and smart objects applications. Contactless payment cards need corresponding card readers and couplers, and ASK supplies these too, as well as development kits. The company's ticket solution is C.ticket, a one- or limited-use paper ticket market with a silver ink antenna.

It also offers personalization services, including database management, alphanumeric data printing, and color photos. Additionally, it offers operating system services, security services, and near-field communication technology that allows smartphones to make contactless payments.

It also offers a shielding product that prevents cards being unlawfully scanned.

ASK offers its products in over 100 cities in around 25 countries. The company's products are manufactured at its production center and headquarters in the South of France. Regional offices are located in Paris and Singapore. Its subsidiaries ASK IntTag and ASK Smart are located in the US and China.
