John Bean Technologies Corporation (dba as JBT Corporation) keeps food cold and jets in the air. JBT Corporation manufactures industrial equipment for the food processing and air transportation industries. Its JBT FoodTech segment makes commercial-grade refrigeration systems, freezers, ovens, canning equipment, and food processing systems for fruit, poultry, meat patties, breads, pizzas, seafood, and ready-to-eat meals. JBT AeroTech manufactures and services ground support equipment (plane de-icers, aircraft tow vehicles, and cargo loading systems), airport gate equipment (Jetway brand), and military equipment.

JBT owns production facilities spanning almost 25 countries. Its principal production facilities are located in the US (California, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Florida), Spain, Mexico, Brazil, Belgium, Italy, Sweden, the UK, South Africa, and China. The US alone accounts for about half its sales. Other major markets include the EMEA region (28%), Asia/Pacific (12%), Latin America (8%), and Canada (2%). JBT sells its FoodTech products to more than 100 countries.

JBT FoodTech accounted for 64% of its sales in 2014, while JBT AeroTech generated 36%.

Since its inception, the company has delivered more than 40,000 pieces of food processing equipment which includes more than 8,000 industrial freezers, 2,200 industrial citrus juice extractors, 3,000 industrial sterilization systems and 8,500 coating systems. JBT has also delivered the largest volume of cargo loaders (more than 9,500), passenger boarding bridges (more than 7,900), and aircraft deicers (more than 4,700). The company has also sold more than 2,200 mobile passenger steps, more than 2,000 cargo transporters, and more than 1,800 tow tractors.

JBT sells and markets its products and services predominantly through a direct sales force, supplemented with independent distributors and sales representatives. The company also educates its customers about products through newsletters, websites, seminars, trade shows, user groups, and conferences.

Customers include AdvancePierre Foods , Agrosuper S.A., Ajinomoto Co., Ardo N.V., Aujan Industries, Bonduelle Group, Brasil Foods S.A., Campbell Soup Company , Air Canada , Air China , and Air France KLM .

In 2014 JBT's revenues increased by 5% due to higher sales in the US and Latin American markets and recurring revenues driven by growth in aftermarket parts and service.

JBT FoodTech's revenues grew from acquisitions and growth in protein processing and liquid foods processing equipment. AeroTech's revenues increased due to a growth in fixed equipment products, driven by higher investment in airport infrastructure in North America; and mobile equipment products, driven by demand for our military air conditioning units.

Over the last five years, the company's income has been trending opposite to revenues. In 2014 JBT's net income decreased by 7% due to increased expenses (include restructuring expenses) partially offset by higher revenues.

In 2014 operating cash flows increased by 27% due to higher inflows from inventories, accounts payable, and trade receivables; offset by an increase in outflows of advance payments, decreased net incomes, and higher pension contributions.

The company is pursuing organic growth initiatives and strategic acquisitions. It is investing in the profitable aftermarket business, and building a dedicated sales and service network which will capitalize on global installed base of equipment. JBT is also looking for opportunities in emerging markets, with a major focus on Asia, where it is establishing a direct presence.

In 2014 and 2015 the company began to restructure its operations by consolidating back office operations in the US and Europe, consolidating smaller operations, enabling JBT FoodTech and JBT AeroTech to operate shared facilities.

In 2014, the company formed the JBT Equipment Finance LLC, a wholly-owned captive equipment finance unit, to complement its financing products in the US and a number of other countries.

Enhancing its core operations through acquisitions is a key growth strategy for JBT. In 2014 it purchased the ICS Solutions business from Stork Food & Dairy Systems. ICS Solutions -- with locations in Amsterdam and Gainesville, Georgia -- provides engineering, installation, and servicing of high-capacity food preservation equipment.

It also spent $54 million to buy Wolf-tec, Inc., a New York-based innovative equipment manufacturer providing processing solutions for poultry, beef, pork, and seafood. The deal enables the company to better meet customer needs through an expanded portfolio of protein processing equipment and solutions, and further entry into beef, pork, and seafood processing markets.

In 2014 JBT also acquired Sweden-based Formcook, a regional leader in designing, manufacturing and servicing custom-built industrial cooking and forming technologies for the food processing industry, for about $2 million.

John Bean Technologies was spun off from FMC Technologies in 2008. JBT Corporation takes its name from John Bean, a California inventor who founded Bean Spray Pump Company in 1884, the company which eventually became Food Machinery Corporation (FMC) though a series of mergers and acquisitions in the 20th century. (The original Bean Spray Pump technology was adapted to make a plane de-icer in the 1960s.)

In mid-2012 JBT's FoodTech division bought container sterilization machinery belonging to H.G. Molenaar & Co (Pty) Ltd., a firm in South Africa. The deal enabled JBT to offer global customers an even broader range of equipment for the canned food industry.
