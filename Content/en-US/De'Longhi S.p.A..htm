De'Longhi knows a house is not a home without its appliances. The company makes and markets a variety of home appliances, including air conditioners, heaters, humidifiers, blenders, espresso makers, toasters, vacuums, and irons. De'Longhi's brand names include Ariete, Braun, Kenwood, and RotoFry, among others. It operates in about 50 countries through its two divisions: Household and Professional. The professional group makes Climaventa and DL Radiators cooling and heating systems for commercial clients such as shopping centers, hospitals, and hotels. Europe accounts for more than two-thirds of De'Longhi's sales. Kenwood Appliances in the UK is a subsidiary of De'Longhi.

Although the company is based in Italy, which generates some 19% of its sales, other regions contribute to De'Longhi's bottom line. Europe, including Italy and the UK, accounted for some 68% of 2013 sales.
