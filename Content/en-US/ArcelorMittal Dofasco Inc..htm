Very much a can-do kind of company, what ArcelorMittal Dofasco does is steel. In 2011 ArcelorMittal Dofasco produced 3.5 million tons of steel used in cans, cars, roofs, and more. The company makes hot-rolled, cold-rolled, galvanized, and tinplate steels, as well as tubular steel products. The automotive sector accounts for a significant amount of ArcelorMittal Dofasco's total sales. Most of the rest of its customers are in the packaging, distribution, and construction industries. Its primary manufacturing facility is in Hamilton, Ontario. Since 2006 Dofasco has been a part of global steel giant ArcelorMittal .

The company produces hot rolled, cold rolled, galvanized, Extragal (specialty galvanized products), Galvalume (specialty coatings), tinplate, chromium-coated and pre-painted flat rolled steels, as well as tubular products. ArcelorMittal Dofasco sells this broad portfolio of steel products to customers in the automotive, construction, energy, manufacturing, pipe and tube, appliance, packaging. and steel distribution industries.

Parent Arcelor Mittal has mining, steelmaking, and tube manufacturing plants in New Brunswick, Quebec, and Ontario. Dofasco's steelmaking facility in Hamilton, Ontario is strategically located adjacent to water, rail, and highway transportation routes.

ArcelorMittal Dofasco was created in 1912, when C.W. Sherman founded the Dominion Steel Casting Company to manufacture castings for Canadian railways. Later named the Dominion Foundries and Steel  Company (the origins of the Dofasco moniker), the company merged with its subsidiary, Hamilton Steel Wheel Company, in 1917. The name was officially changed to Dofasco in 1980.
