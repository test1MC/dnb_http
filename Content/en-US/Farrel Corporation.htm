Before the rubber meets the road, tire makers meet with Farrel, maker of rubber and plastic production machinery. The Harburg-Freudenberger Maschinenbau-owned company's products include batch mixers, extruders (used to make hoses and sheathings), mills, and pelletizers under brand names Banbury, Technolab, and Intermix. It also provides related services, such as equipment upgrading, consulting, rebuilding, remanufacturing, and spare parts. Farrel sells its machinery to petrochemical, plastic resins, and plastic compounder firms. Rubber-making equipment customers include tire makers, automotive parts manufacturers, and footwear producers.

Farrel has operations in Ansonia, Connecticut, in the US; Rochdale, the UK; Barcelona, Spain; and Singapore.

Farrel leads Harburg-Freudenberger Maschinenbau's Mixing Group's business unit. Farrel's sister company, Pomini Rubber & Plastics (based in Italy) is a heavy machinery manufacturing company.

The two companies (as Farrel Pomini) provide a full portfolio of services, including new processing equipment design and engineering, spare parts, remanufacturing and rebuilding, and process engineering as well as field service and technical support.

The company has sales offices in the US (Akron, Ohio; Charlotte, North Carolina; Deer Park, Texas; and Libeznice, the Czech Republic). It also has licensees in Australia and China.

Its global sales representatives include ABC Plasticos S.A. DE C.V (Mexico), Carst & Walker Pty. Ltd. (South America), Kata Industries Ltd. (China), McCabe Engineering Pty Ltd (Australia), MEGAPLASTIC SRL (Argentina), and Terra Engineering (Europe).

The company started as the Farrel Foundry in the 1840s. It produced chilled iron rolls, which were a necessary component in rubber processing machinery.
