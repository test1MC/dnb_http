The spry triple centenarian Okaya & Co. holds its own against its bigger, younger cousins, Japan's mammoth sogo shosha (general trading companies). Founded as a hardware shop in 1669 in the Teppocho district of Nagoya, Okaya has evolved into a global trading company over the centuries. Its current operations and trading interests include iron and steel, nonferrous metals, chemicals, building materials, electronics, machinery, food products, and more. The company has around 24 offices throughout Japan and another 45 locations in countries around the world.

The company has offices in Japan (Toyota, Kariya, Kita-Kanto, Shizuoka, Hamamatsu, Hokkaido,Tohoku, Niigata, Hokuriku, Chugoku, Kyushu, Hitachi, Higashi-Hiroshima, Yahata) and in 20 other countries.

Okaya is engaged in a broad range of businesses including domestic and overseas transactions in iron and steel, metals, machinery, tools, electronics, chemicals, foods, and a number of other products.

The company's net revenues increased between 2010 and 2014). In 2014 Okaya's net revenues increased by 10%.

Net income has followed a similar trend as net revenues over the last five years. Okaya's net income increased by 22% in 2014 over 2013.

In 2014 the company established a trading company (Okaya Mexico S.A. de C.V.), in Mexico. Okaya plans to expand this business as the sales operations site of its group in the Latin America region, an area where companies from many countries are entering, especially in the auto industry.

That year it also set up a farm production corporation (Makishima Farm Co., Ltd) in Miyagi Japan, as a joint venture company with Sunfresh-Matsushima Co., Ltd. to produce and distribute tomatoes.

In 2014 Okaya set up Okaya Malaysia Sdn. Bhd in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, as itd base for future trading growth in the region.

To enhance its sales of processed food, in 2014 the company announced plans to acquire the Nishifu Meat Co.
