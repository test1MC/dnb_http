ZS Pharma is out for blood. The company developing a drug to treat hyperkalemia, a life-threatening condition whereby patients have too much potassium in their blood. Hyperkalemia can increase the risk of heart attacks; it occurs most frequently in patients with chronic kidney disease, heart failure, and diabetes. ZS Pharma gets its name from zirconium silicate, the chemical compound used to trap potassium ions and reduce toxicity in the blood. ZS Pharma has plans to apply for regulatory approval for its ZS-9 drug, an oral tablet, by 2016. It hopes zirconium silicate can be used for other ailments. ZS Pharma, which went public in 2014, was acquired by AstraZeneca for $2.7 billion in late 2015.
