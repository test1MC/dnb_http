Need high-grade industrial sensors or automatic identification products? Call in SICK. The North American subsidiary of German-based SICK AG , makes a wide range of factory and process automation products, including bar code scanners; laser measurement system sensors; and photoelectric, luminescence and contrast sensors, which are used to count, inspect, measure, determine volume, and sort products during various phases of production, distribution, and sales. SICK targets the automotive, packaging, electronics, postal, consumer goods, food and beverage, robotics, and material-handling markets. Established in 1976, the company operates and distributes its products throughout the US, Canada, and Mexico.

With the parent company operating worldwide, SICK is the American arm serving the US, Canada, and Mexico. The company is headquartered in Minneapolis.

SICK specializes in safety control devices, which keep operators safe from hazardous automated machinery, and products and machinery safe from damage. The company makes such safety products as laser scanners, light curtains, multiple light beam devices, camera systems, switches, and sensors, which will cause a device shutdown in the event of a disruption.

The company's sales teams and distributor network serves a range of industries which include automotive, packaging, electronics, food and beverage, consumer goods, logistics, parcel, and material handling.

Like its parent, SICK grows by launching new products that meet its customers' growing demands. In 2014 it launched a low-cost OD Mini short range distance sensor; the CLV600 IP 69K Housing bar code scanners; CQ4 capacitive proximity sensors; the CLV610 fixed-mount 1D bar code scanners for warehouse and distribution center applications; the AHS/AHM36 absolute encoders; and the G10 photoelectric sensor with Q-Lock mounting.
