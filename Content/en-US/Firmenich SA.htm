According to Firmenich International, Prunella is "fruity" while Dorinia is "floral" -- all part of the business of creating chemical flavors and fragrances. With operations worldwide, Firmenich produces flavors and fragrances for perfumes, cosmetics, foods and beverages, and household products. Its customers include world's leading multinational makers of beauty, household, and fabric care products, as well as manufacturers pharmaceuticals, food, and drink products. With a 14% global market share Firmenich ranks among the world leaders in flavors and fragrances along with International Flavors & Fragrances and Givaudan .

Firmenich operates 26 manufacturing sites worldwide and maintains R&D facilities in France, China, Switzerland, and the US. It has a presence in 64 countries worldwide.

The company's products include perfume for fine fragrances, body care items, and home care products, and flavors for beverages, sweet goods, and savory foods.

In fiscal 2012 the company reported a 1.1% jump in revenues (in local currencies) led by moderate increases in all geographic regions except Western Europe. Ingredient sales also declined as the result of strong competition.

Firmenich's strategy is to grow through organic growth and it makes a heavy investment in research and development activities. The company is committed to invest 10% of its annual revenues in innovation activities.

Looking for greener products, in 2013 the company teamed up with Amyris , a leading renewable chemicals company, to develop and commercialize renewable ingredients for the flavors and fragrances market.

In fiscal 2012, Firmenich filed 27 patent applications for new ingredients, processes, and delivery systems. It also opened a new R&D center in Shanghai that year.

In 2011 the company opened two new production sites, a flavor plant in the US, and a perfume ingredients plant in India. It also opened the Naturals Innovation Center in France, designed to strengthen the company's expertise in natural ingredients through developing new distillation and extraction techniques.

Firmenich lists a 2005 Nobel Prize for Biology among its numerous awards for research.

The company was founded in Geneva in 1895.
