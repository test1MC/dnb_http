Master Lock's founder figured that if battleships and bank vaults were made of laminated steel, why not locks? The world's largest padlock manufacturer invented and patented the laminated steel padlock in 1924. Today, it continues to manufacture and market padlocks, as well as steering wheel locks, cable locks and U-locks, window and door locks and alarms, electronic access control solutions, and other products for the home and security applications in the automotive, consumer goods, commercial, education, and government markets. Master Lock is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Fortune Brands Storage & Security, itself a subsidiary of Fortune Brands Home & Security .

Master Lock sells products in Europe, Australia, Asia, the Middle East, Central America, South America, the Caribbean, and South Africa.

The company makes products for home, automotive, campus, power sports, bike and storage security needs of consumers, and commercial and industrial clients.

Master Lock sells its products through hardware and other retail outlets, home centers, and more than 70 wholesale distributors throughout North America, and via online stores. It sells lock systems primarily to locksmiths, industrial, and institutional users, and original equipment makers (OEMs).

The parent company's Security & Storage segment's revenues decreased by 1% in 2013, due to a decline in revenues from storage products primarily due to the timing of promotions by the business's largest customer. The Security & Storage segment contributed 14% of the total revenues of Fortune Brands Home & Security in 2013.

Master Lock attempts to keep its brand name familiar to consumers by launching new products.

In 2014 Master Lock introduced the S- series padlock line, an all-new line of aluminum safety padlocks specifically designed for safety lockout/tagout purposes. It also introduced the 1652 Series Built-In Combination Lock, featuring an all-new button-less combination change process that will save a considerable amount of maintenance time. That year it also has a new product line extension with the new S2394 Universal Miniature Circuit Breaker Lockout.

In 2013 the company entered a new market with the introduction of the Multi-User lock.

Master Lock is also continuing to expand its presence in Europe.

In 2013 the company's Master Lock Canada unit acquired N4 Systems, Inc., whose Field ID unit is a leading provider of safety management software.

In 2012, Master Lock upgraded its Vault Service, offering extra security measures plus convenience for digital storage, allowing consumers to store and organize a wider range of personal information, save passwords, set reminders for key dates, upload documents for easy access, and retrieve cloud-based backup master codes for Vault enabled Master Lock electronic products.

In 2010 it began offering the latest ProSeries resettable combination padlocks and a free Web-based storage site for passwords and PIN numbers called Master Lock Vault. It also expanded its line of TSA -accepted travel locks with a combination model that allows users to set their own passwords with letters instead of numbers.

Master Lock is a part of Fortune Brands Home & Security, which was spun off from Fortune Brands, now Beam, in 2011 so that Beam could focus on growing its spirits business.

Master Lock was founded in 1921 by locksmith and inventor Harry Soref, and during prohibition it operated out of the abandoned Pabst Brewery in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. The company's colorful history includes providing padlocks for many of the establishments that served outlawed booze during prohibition, as well as for New York City federal agents to lock down many of those same establishments. Additionally, Soref had advised Harry Houdini about keys that could be used to help the magician escape from handcuffs in his stage acts.
