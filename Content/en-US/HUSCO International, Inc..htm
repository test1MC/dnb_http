HUSCO International is venturesome about valves. The manufacturing company designs, develops, and makes hydraulic and electro-hydraulic controls and control valves for makers of off-highway equipment, as well as passenger cars and light trucks. HUSCO's products include proportional flow control valves and fuel system pump controls that end up in agricultural machinery, construction equipment, and material handling equipment. HUSCO management provides support from the conceptual stage to the production stage of product development in order to maximize product value and performance. Its customers have included such notable names as Deere & Company , Komatsu , Caterpillar , and Chrysler .

HUSCO has three facilities in the US and owns locations in China, Brazil, India, and the UK. For more of a regional presence, the company has association agreements throughout North America, as well as in Australia, South America, Korea, Japan, and South Africa.

In addition to designing products to improve engine efficiency and reduce emissions, HUSCO provides suspension systems, transmission controls, and convertible top controls. These off-highway control products are experiencing growth in China and India, where the company has expanded its facilities.

HUSCO products are protected by more than 35 patents with an additional 150 inventions under development being considered for future patents.

The company serves customers in the construction, agricultural and automotive sectors.

The geographic diversity of HUSCO's supply chain allows it to adapt its sourcing strategy in response to currency fluctuations and other policy changes while maintaining low supply chain costs. To secure continued growth, the company is leveraging a global manufacturing footprint, and developing new technologies that differentiate its products in the marketplace.

Responding to increased regional demand, in 2013 HUSCO began made a multi-year $45 million capital expansion project in Wisconsin that is expected to generate over 150 permanent new jobs by the end of 2015.

HUSCO was founded in 1950 as Hydraulic Unit Specialty Company.

The modern HUSCO story began in 1983 when Agustin Ramirez, Jr. was assigned to develop a strategic plan for HUSCO, then a subsidiary of Koehring/AMCA. Ramirez recognized the tremendous growth potential that could be unlocked by separating HUSCO from AMCA and allowing the company to market its products to other major OEMs of off-highway equipment. As a result Ramirez led a management buyout of HUSCO in 1985.
