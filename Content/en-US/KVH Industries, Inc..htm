KVH Industries makes communications products for people (or boats or armies) on the go. The company's mobile satellite communications products include antennas and compasses for yachts and commercial ships (CommBox) and mobile satellite TVs, telephones, and high-speed Internet antennas (TracVision and TracPhone) for automobiles, boats, small planes, and RVs. It sells to retailers and distributors, as well as boat and other vehicle manufacturers. KVH's guidance and stabilization products -- sold mainly to US and allied governments and defense contractors -- include digital compasses, fiber optic gyros for tactical navigation, and guidance systems for torpedoes and unmanned aerial vehicles. The company has branched out to transmit various kinds of content, including educational services.

The company's satellite TV products are compatible with the DIRECTV and DISH Network in the US, Bell TV ( BCE ) in Canada, and various other regional satellite TV providers elsewhere.

KVH uses outsourced components in its manufacturing process, but the company assembles, stores, and distributes its own products. Its mobile satellite communications products are made in Middletown, Rhode Island, while its navigation and fiber optic gyro products are made in Tinley Park, Illinois and CommBox is made in Norway, a hub of the marine industry.Third-party manufacturers make its printed circuit boards, injection-molded plastic parts, machined metal components, connectors and housings.

More than half of the company's revenue comes from services while products account for the rest. It terms of products, its mobile offerings generate 75% of sales with 25% from guidance and stabilization products.

KVH generates about two-thirds of sales in the Americas, but the company has offices in Denmark, India, Japan, Norway, the Philippines, Singapore, and the UK. International sales increased by $10 million in 2014.

Products are sold through a network of distributors and retailers worldwide. Major customers include Saudi Arabian National Guard (12% of sales), General Dynamics Land Systems -Canada, and Kongsberg Defence & Aerospace AS.

KVH's sales continue to rise every year, and overall sales grew 6.3% in 2014 to $172.6 million. Service revenue increased 27% in 2014 on more airtime sales for the mini-VSAT Broadband service. The company's revenue also was boosted by contributions from its Videotel and Headland Media acquisitions. The company's net income plunged to $41,000 in 2014 from $4.5 million in 2013 as it spent more on sales and marketing and research.

Not content with providing communication, KVH is intent on providing content to transmit through its products. Carriers throughout the telecommunications landscape (and seascape) are making similar moves to provide more than the communications infrastructure.

KVH began its content delivery service, IP-MobileCast in 2014. Using multicast technology content and data files are transmitted to vessels and vehicles equipped with TracPhone V series of V-IP series terminal.

The company also began transmitting educational content for the maritime industry through its Videotel acquisition. Content includes certification and refresher courses required by international regulations. By the end of 2014, more than 100 million training hours of Videotel content had been delivered to more than 250,000 registered crew members.

Examples of content delivered included safety training on the Ebola virus for crew members and the broadcast to ships at sea of the 2014 World Cup final.

KVH bought Videotel in 2014 for about $50 million. Videotel's offerings include video, animation, e-Learning computer-based training, and interactive distance learning courses.

In mid-2013 KVH bought Headland Media Limited, a UK-based media and entertainment services company. Headland Media provides commercially licensed news, sports, movies, and music content sold to the maritime, hotel, and retail markets. The acquisition supports KVH's strategic vision of extending its maritime broadband service to include additional content.

The KVH in the company's name comes from the founder's last name, Kits van Heyningen.
