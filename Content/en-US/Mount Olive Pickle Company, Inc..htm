Mount Olive Pickle Company makes pickled products preferred by persnickety people. The company's pickles are the #1 selling brand in the southeastern US and a top seller across the nation. Every year, it produces more than 110 million jars of condiments -- offering some 80 styles of processed and fresh-pack pickles, relishes, and peppers. The company procures its yearly supply of more than 160 million pounds of cucumbers and peppers from farmers in North Carolina, as well as nine other states and Mexico. Mount Olive Pickle Company, which boasts more than 1,200 brine vats, was founded in 1926 by Lebanese immigrant Shickrey Baddour.

The company operates from facilities in North Carolina and sells in all 50 US states. Its cucumbers come from Alabama, Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Maryland, Michigan, North Carolina, Ohio, South Carolina, and Texas, as well as Mexico. North Carolina ranks third in the nation for its agricultural production of pickling cucumbers.

It also imports brined cucumber and pepper products from India and Greece.

Year-round, Mount Olive Pickle Company runs two production shifts. The company is situated on 110 acres, with some 675,000 sq. ft. devoted to production, office, and warehouse space.

Of the 160-million-plus pounds of cucumbers and peppers the company receives, about 53 million pounds are sourced from independent growers in North Carolina.

The company markets its products across the US via a direct sales force that manages a network of food brokers.

Mount Olive Pickle Company works to stay on top of the latest in horticulture, food science, and engineering by forming relationships with research universities the likes of North Carolina State University . Through partnerships, it has remained a leader in cucumber and pickle production development, waste management methods, and in salt reduction in the brining process.
