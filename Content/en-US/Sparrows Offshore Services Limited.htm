"Cranes" might be a better avian moniker for Sparrows Offshore, one of the world's largest crane management and engineering services contractor to the offshore oil and gas industry. Sparrows offers offshore platform crane operations, maintenance, management, training, and turnkey services. It operates primarily in the North Sea, although it also does work in Southeast Asia, the Middle East, the Caspian Sea, Australasia, and West Africa. The company, which has more than 30 offices worldwide, has a regional headquarters and engineering center in Singapore to serve the growing Asian market.

Sparrows offers offshore lifting and mechanical handling services ranging from lifting study through crane procurement and installation, operation and maintenance, lifting services, training, rigging equipment, and crew management. It also designs, supplies, installs, and maintains mechanical handling systems. Sparrows' engineering services include lifting and design, jacking, flare tip replacement, hydraulic engineering, and on-site machining services. In addition, the company sells new and used cranes, and rents various lifting equipment, including cranes.

Sparrow's business units include Engineering and Operations, Fluid Power, Manufacturing, Rental, and Training. It also operates Sparrows Baricon, which provides design and build services for subsea pipe and cable laying systems on a turnkey basis.

The company has operations in 31 locations across Europe, Africa, the Middle East, Asia, Australia, and the Americas. It has training centers in Aberdeen (UK), Houma and Houston (US), Abu Dhabi (UAE), Singapore, Luanda (Angola), Macae (Brazil), Batam (Indonesia), and Port Harcourt (Nigeria).

Sparrows leverages its strong reputation and long track record to secure new and repeat business with engineering and oil and gas companies around the world.

In 2017, the company secured a contract to provide crane management services at the high-profile Kraken Field in the UK North Sea. In 2016, it won a five-year contract to deliver cranes and maintenance services at Statoil 's Mariner field, one of the North Sea's largest new developments in more than a decade.

In 2016, Sparrows formed a partnership with SapuraKencana Technology to provide offshore and onshore crane services including manufacturing, supply, operations, maintenance and overhaul of offshore and onshore cranes for the oil and gas industry in Malaysia. That year Sparrows was also awarded a one-year contract extension with a long-standing client to provide crane maintenance, operation and inspection services in Angola.

Historically, the company (incorporated in 1965) began to gain momentum in 1973 when Sparrows was the first contractor to send specialist crane operators to work on offshore platforms in the UK's then-fledgling North Sea oil and gas industry.
