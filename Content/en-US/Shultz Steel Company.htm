Forging is an honorable profession for Shultz Steel, which fabricates impression forgings, open die forgings, and seamless rolled rings. The company primarily serves the aerospace (commercial and defense airframes, engines, helicopters, and missiles) and commercial land-based gas turbine markets. Shultz Steel's aerospace products include bulkheads, disks, engine mounts, flap and slat tracks, landing gear structures, nacelles, shafts, and windshield frames. It also makes extruded cases, nozzles, and stationary shells for missiles. Shultz Steel's gas turbine engine products include disks, rings, and shafts.

The company can handle impression die forgings up to 13,000 lbs, while open die forgings can be handled up to 50,000 lbs. Its VAR (vacuum arc re-melting) furnace allows for the re-melting of steel and steel alloys into aerospace quality ingots.

The company was founded in 1947 by Gordon Shultz, who had had previously founded and operated two other successful forging companies before establishing Shultz Steel.
