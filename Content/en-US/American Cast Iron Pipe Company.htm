American Cast Iron Pipe Co. (ACIPCO) operates one of the largest ductile iron pipe casting plants in the world. Its divisions -- including American Ductile Iron Pipe, American Flow Control, American Steel Pipe -- make ductile iron pipe and fittings, cast steel tubes, fire hydrants and fire truck pumps, and valves for water treatment and energy production. It also make specialized products for the water utility industry and electric-resistance steel pipe for the oil and natural gas industries. The company has been exporting pipes and related products since 1915.

ACIPCO makes specific pipe products for gravity sewers, pump stations, sewer force mains, treated effluent lines, trenchless applications, wastewater treatment plants, water distribution, water transmission, and water treatment plants. Products include ductile iron pipe, spiral-welded steel pipe, fire hydrants and valves for the waterworks industry, and electric-resistance-welded steel pipe for the oil and gas industry. Its operating subsidiaries include AMERICAN Castings, LLC; AMERICAN SpiralWeld Pipe Company, LLC; AMERICAN Valve & Hydrant Manufacturing Company; Intercast SA (Brazil); Specification Rubber Products Inc.; and Waterous Company.

AMERICAN SpiralWeld makes spiral-welded steel pipe to meet or exceed the requirements of the applicable American Water Works Association and ASTM International standards.

The company has manufacturing plants in Alabama, Minnesota, Oklahoma, South Carolina and Texas. It also has a plant in Brazil. The company's products can be found in water and sewer systems across Africa, Asia, the Middle East, the Caribbean, and North and South America.

ACIPCO makes products for a range of industries, including fire protection, infrastructure, manufacturing, mining, oil and gas, and power. The ACIPCO International Sales unit is responsible for the export marketing of a wide portfolio of products, including ductile iron pipe, steel pipe, valves, and hydrants.

The company has found a bump in demand in recent years from US water utilities, as urban governments replace their old infrastructure with the company's zinc-coated ductile iron pipe. ACIPCO's zinc-coated pipe (introduced in the US in 2015) extends the life of iron pipe and protects millions of feet of cast and ductile iron pipe around the world. In 2016, Danville (Indiana) laid its first zinc-coated ductile iron pipe, and Washington Suburban Sanitary Commission , a major US utility, updated its specifications so that all ductile iron pipe installed in its system will be zinc-coated and use V-Bio enhanced polyethylene encasement.

In addition to original pipe production, to save costs, ACIPCO also recycles structural steel and iron for water and sewer infrastructure.

In 2013 the company announced plans build a new spiral-welded pipe facility in Flint (Michigan) to support the Karegnondi Water Authority project by supplying 60 miles of spiral-welded pipe, 17 miles of ductile iron pipe, and 24 large diameter Flex-Ring gate valves.

In 2011 the company recycled scrap metal from the decommissioned NASA launch pads at Cape Canaveral, Florida. ACIPCO claims that 90% of its ductile iron pipe is made from recycled material.

In 2011 and 2012 ACIPCO lobbied the US government to lift the cap on private activity bonds to encourage municipalities to upgrade their water infrastructure as a way to both drive economic growth and provide the company with new product demand.

John Joseph Eagan founded ACIPCO in Birmingham, Alabama, in 1905, and in 1922 placed all of the company's stock into a trust for employees.
