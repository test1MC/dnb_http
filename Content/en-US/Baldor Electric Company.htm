Electricity drives Baldor Electric's sales -- and its products. The company manufactures industrial AC and DC electric motors, controls, and speed drives that power products ranging from material handling conveyors to fluid handling pumps. Other products include industrial grinders, buffers, polishing lathes, and generators. Baldor Electric sells to OEMs primarily in the agricultural and semiconductor equipment industries and to independent distributors for resale as replacement parts. Baldor Electric, a subsidiary of ABB , has customers in some 70 countries.

Baldor has about two dozen plants in Canada, China, Mexico, the UK, and the US. It generates most of its sales from the US market.

The company generates revenue both from direct sales to end users as well as from indirect sales through distributors, third-party channel partners, wholesalers, machine builders and OEMs, system integrators, and panel builders. It has 50 sales offices in North America and nearly 25 offices serving international customers.

Customers and markets include machinery manufacturers, process industries such as pulp and paper, oil and gas, and metals and mining companies, rail equipment manufacturers, discrete manufacturing companies, utilities and renewable energy suppliers (particularly in the wind and solar sectors), as well as customers in the automotive industry.

Baldor operates under the Discrete Automation and Motion division of ABB. This division contributed about 24% of ABB's total 2013 revenue. Its revenues climbed 5% in 2013 due to higher demand for robotics components and additional revenues due to ABB's acquisition of Thomas & Betts .

Baldor is focused on organic growth through new product development, especially in the area of energy-efficient motors. On average, it releases 250 new products per year.

Parent company ABB is looking to boost its presence in North America and strengthen its position in the market for energy-efficient products, which is expected to grow as new energy efficiency legislation goes into effect in the US. Baldor's electric motors and mechanical transmission product lines, along with its established industrial customer base, fit well with ABB's energy-efficient drives and complementary motor products. Baldor also aims to expand internationally using the global customer base and strong channel sales organization of the Swiss conglomerate.

Electrical engineer Edwin Ballman and master machinist Emil Doerr founded Baldor in 1920 in St. Louis. The company took its name from the last names of the founders.
