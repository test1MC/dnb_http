While not a home to hip hop royalty, BelAir Networks may seem like a prince to customers who want to freshen up their data networks. The company's wireless networking gear, based on the Wi-Fi standard, is designed to fill gaps in wired networks by enabling users to connect wirelessly. Its products are intended to extend the reach of internal e-mail, Web, instant messaging, and VPN services. BelAir sells in part through partnerships with other manufacturers, including Nomadix and Airpath. It serves such industries as telecom and hospitality, as well as state, local, and federal government agencies. Customers have included The University of Georgia and Omni Hotels . BelAir was acquired by Ericsson in 2012.
