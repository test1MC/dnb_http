ChromaDex can talk a blue streak about the health benefits of blueberries. The company markets pterostilbene, a plant-based chemical found in blueberries that is said to lower cholesterol and reduce the risk of cancer. Sold under the brand pTeroPure, the phytochemical is used in about 20 different nutritional supplements, including Nutraceutical's Solaray Super Resveratrol with Pterostilbene. ChromaDex also launched its own line of supplements in 2011 called BluScience that are sold at GNC and Walgreens . Biotech investor Philip Frost, the chairman of Teva Pharmaceuticals , owns almost 20% of ChromaDex.
