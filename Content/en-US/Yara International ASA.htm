Yara International has its feet firmly planted in fertilizer. The global chemical company converts energy, natural minerals, and nitrogen into fertilizers for farmers and other products for industrial and environmental use. With sales in 150 countries, Yara's main activities are large-scale ammonia and fertilizer production in Europe, though more recently it is adding capacity in regions with low-cost gas supplies. It is the largest producer of ammonia in the world. Yara also provides local customer service and agronomical support, helping farmers improve crop yields and quality.

The company has production facilities on six continents, operations in more than 50 countries, and it sells its products in some 150 countries.

Yara operates through three operating segments -- Upstream, Downstream, and Industrial. The Upstream unit commercial produces nitrogen-based products, the base for the company's fertilizers and industrial solutions. Downstream provides worldwide sales, marketing, and distribution of a complete range of fertilizer and crop nutrition programs. The Industrial segment develops and markets environmental goods and services and essential products for industrial applications.

The company's Supply & Trade unit is responsible for optimization of energy, raw materials, and third party sourcing; ammonia trade; shipping; and logistics.

Yara's global distribution and marketing network includes more than 200 terminals, warehouses, blending plants, and bagging facilities, located in more than 50 countries.

The company's net revenues have been growing over the past few years. Yara's net revenues increased by 12% in 2014 due to an 11% increase in fertilizer sales volumes and higher premiums and a 5% increase in industrial sales volumes.

Yara's net income increased by 30% in 2014 due to higher net revenues and a change in inventories.

The company's cash inflow decreased by 30% in 2014 due to changes in working capital as a result of changes in inventories, changes in receivables, and prepaid expenses and other current assets.

Yara, which has a strong production and marketing base in Europe, is increasing its activities in North America, Latin America and Australia significantly, while also expanding its footprint in Africa and Asia.

To raise cash, in 2015 Yara agreed to sell its stake in GrowHow UK Group Limited for $648 million.

In 2014 Yara acquired control of Brazil-based Galvani. The deal complements Yara's downstream presence in Brazil (which has included the purchase of Bunge 's fertilizer division), and further strengthens Yara's position in the world's fastest growing agricultural region.

That year it also bought OFD Holding Inc, which has production facilities in Cartagena, Colombia and distribution companies across Latin America. The enterprise value is $377 million.

To expand its emissions control business, in 2014 Yara acquired a majority 63% stake in Green Tech Marine, a leading sulphur oxide (SOX) scrubber supplier to the marine industry. Combining GTM with Yara's existing nitrogen oxide (NOX) portfolio will help the maritime industry clean up their emissions.

Yara was spun off in 2004 by Norwegian aluminum giant Norsk Hydro , which produced the world's first chemical nitrogen fertilizer.
