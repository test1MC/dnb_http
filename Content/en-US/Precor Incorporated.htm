At the core of this company's operations is an innate desire to help customers train their core -- to help its bottom line. Precor makes a variety of fitness equipment, including treadmills, cycles, climbers, stretch trainers, elliptical fitness crosstrainers, and strength-training systems. They're used at home, in fitness facilities, hotel fitness centers, and corporate environments. The company boasts offices in the US, as well as Austria, Benelux, China, France, Germany, Japan, Spain, Switzerland, and the UK. Its international division manages distribution through a dealer network spanning more than 100 countries. Precor is a subsidiary of Finnish fitness firm Amer Group Plc .

Precor sells its fitness equipment worldwide.

The company serves fitness facilities, hotels and individuals in 90-plus countries across the Americas, Europe, Asia Pacific, the Middle East, and Africa.

Precor generated about 17% of its parent's 2013 revenue from the sale of fitness equipment.

Precor's portfolio of products includes both home and commercial fitness equipment. Its brand names include EFX Elliptical, Adaptive Motion Trainer (AMT), and Preva Networked Fitness.

Its products are sold to gyms, hotels, municipalities, health clubs, corporate wellness programs, the government, fire departments, hospitality and spas, law enforcement, medical and rehabilitation facilities, multi-family housing, and parks and recreation.

Precor has been concentrating on its commercial business -- consisting of clubs and institutions -- as it generates 88% of revenue. The consumer channel comprises the rest of its revenue. Also, Precor's looking to Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, as well as the Asia Pacific region.

To help set itself apart from the competition, the equipment manufacturer has been focused on catering to customers who want a more personal workout experience and one that keeps exercisers motivated. For instance, Precor's 880 line allows users to store their age, weight, and workout history to suggest the next workout. Also, in 2014 the company launched its Experience Series of commercial treadmills, complete with updated features and the latest technology. The company works to leverage the latest technology to provide reliability, ease-of-use, and solid construction.

Precor's also seeing gains in the hotel market through an agreement with Hilton Worldwide and it's making strides in home-gym equipment. Sales of Precor products in the consumer market rose in 2007, with new elliptical crosstrainers and treadmills selling more swiftly for people who work out at home.
