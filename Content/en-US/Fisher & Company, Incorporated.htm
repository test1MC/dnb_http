Fisher & Company invites drivers to have a seat and buckle up. Operating through Fisher Dynamics and Fisher Dynamics Metal Forming, the company manufactures automotive seating components, including armrests, headrests, latches, and seat recliners. Its metal forming business offers a myriad of safety-critical parts, from stampings to blankings and broachings. Fisher products are sold worldwide to automotive OEMs Chrysler , Ford , and GM , as well as auto parts makers Lear , Delphi , and Visteon . Fisher is led by family members of its founder, Alfred J. Fisher.

Fisher & Company has manufacturing plants in the US (Michigan and Tennessee), China, and Mexico. It also maintains customer and engineering offices in Germany, India, and Japan.

Fisher & Company works through two main operations. Fisher Dynamics is a leading supplier of seat mechanisms for the automotive industry and designs and makes recliners, latches, and specialty mechanisms for passenger vehicles. It makes about 28 million assembled seat mechanisms a year.

Fisher Dynamics Metal Forming is a top manufacturer of high-strength, tight-tolerance stampings, complex fine blankings, and precision broachings, and annually makes 140 million of these components.

The company serves car manufacturers and seating suppliers including Abbot, Buick, Cadillac, Chevrolet, Chrysler , Ford , Herreshoff, Oldsmobile, Packard, Regal, and Studebaker.
