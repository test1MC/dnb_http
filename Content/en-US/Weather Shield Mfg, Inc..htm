To keep bad weather at bay, Weather Shield offers impact-resistant windows, doors, and other related products to homeowners, architects, and contractors. The company custom-builds wood and vinyl windows and entry and patio doors with hurricanes, storms, and the environment in mind. Its products come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and finishes and are sold through a network of more than 2,000 dealers worldwide, with North America as its largest market. Product lines include EnduraShield, ProShield, and Weather Shield. Founded as Weather Shield Windows & Doors in 1955, the company is owned and managed by the family of founder Edward "Lee" Schield.

Based in Wisconsin, Weather Shield is a global supplier of windows and doors that are designed to withstand the wrath of Mother Nature.

As part of its business, the company operates manufacturing plants in Wisconsin. It also enjoys an extended reach through an extensive dealer network that gives it access to international customers in Canada, Australia, Vietnam, the Bahamas, Belize, the Cayman Island, Japan, Mexico, Panama, and Puerto Rico.

Weather Shield rolled out both its Signature Series and Premium Series of aluminum-clad wood windows and patio doors in 2013. The Signature Series is a mid-priced product line that consists of casement, awning, picture, double hung, sliding, direct-set, and specialty-shaped windows, as well as both hinged and sliding patio doors. The series represents its second of three new product lines being launched as part of its ReInvented campaign. The first product, the Aspire Series of vinyl-clad wood windows, debuted in mid-2012. The Premium Series, also launched in 2013 alongside the Signature Series, boasts several options and comprises architecturally-inspired aluminum-clad wood windows and patio doors. They're available in double hung, casement, awning and sliding windows; and hinged, sliding, telescoping, and bi-fold patio doors.

To boost the value of its products, Weather Shield uses special coatings and protective glazings. The company also incorporates some recycled materials, such as pre-consumer vinyl and glass, into its production.

Weather Shield has been able to grow its business and reach into new geographic markets through its extensive dealer network. Leveraging this network, the maker of windows and doors sells its products worldwide.

Weather Shield serves both residential and consumer markets. It sells its products through Wisconsin dealers in Colby, Merrill, Wausau, Stevens Point, and Fifield.
