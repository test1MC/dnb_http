The sound of yard work sounds good to Echo. The company is a top developer and manufacturer of professional, hand-held outdoor landscaping equipment, serving both the commercial landscaper and residential homeowner markets. Echo, a subsidiary of Japan's Yamabiko Corporation , offers a full line of trimmers and brush cutters, power edgers, power blowers, chain saws, hedge clippers, power pruners, and sprayers, as well as attachments, engine drills, pumps, and safety accessories sold worldwide through more than 30 distributors. Founded in 1972 as Kioritz Corporation of America, Echo engineered the world's first hand-held power blower.

Based in Illinois, Echo's business extends from North America to Europe, Latin America, Africa, the Middle East, Oceania, and Asia.

In 2009 the company merged with outdoor power equipment maker Shindaiwa.

Echo has grown to become one of the world's top developers and manufacturers of professional-grade, hand-held outdoor power equipment. It's known for making and marketing chain saws, grass trimmers, leaf blowers, hedge trimmers, edgers, brush cutters, chippers, shredders, engine drills, power pruners, pumps, sprayers, tillers, cultivators, accessories, and parts.

The company serves both the commercial and homeowner markets. It sells its products in North America through 10 distributors managing 6,600 independent dealers. Echo products are sold through Home Depot in the US, Canada, Mexico, and Puerto Rico. They're sold via a variety of store channels with the help of two dozen Latin American distributors.

For professional users, Echo launched an enhanced Fleet Program, which provides them with financial incentives for purchasing multiple Echo- and Shindaiwa-branded products from local dealers.

The Official Chain Saw of The Professional Bull Riders, Echo also markets its brand through nearly a dozen events for Ford .

Echo's former parent Kioritz and Oregon-based Shindaiwa merged to form a new holding company, Yamabiko Corporation, which triggered the Echo and Shindaiwa business combination. The union of Echo and Shindaiwa creates a more efficient organization with lower costs and to boost competitiveness in the North and South American markets.

For more than 15 years, Echo has been a supplier to Home Depot . The Echo and Home Depot partnership has been a boon for both of the companies as they target the professional and residential markets. In 2013, Echo expanded its chain saw lineup and released the ECHO PB-760LN, a powerful, low-noise backpack blower.

To gain ground in the commercial turf and consumer lawn and garden markets and boost sales, Echo in 2014 formed a US dealer alliance partnership with Kubota Tractor Corporation . As part of the alliance, the Echo and Shindaiwa names will become the preferred brands of hand-held outdoor power equipment within the Kubota dealer network.

To expand its portfolio of products, Echo has acquired several professional equipment makers including Kwik Products, an Arizona-based manufacturer of distributor of trimmer heads and trimmer line, and Fargo, North Dakota-based TerraMarc Industries. Operating as a subsidiary of Echo under the name Crary Industries, TerraMarc makes Bear Cat chippers, Lockwood potato planting and harvesting implements, and Crary agricultural equipment.
