This Swan Hunter provides engineering, design, and management services for the shipbuilding and offshore oil and gas markets and additionally supplies staffing for shipbuilding. Swan Hunter's services include 3D-modeling for shipbuilding specifications and decommissioning of offshore platforms and structures. Among Swan Hunter's major projects is a design services contract preliminary to the construction of the world's largest offshore vessel for the Allseas Group. The company additionally offers office and storage space rental. Swan Hunter traces its origins to the purchase of a shipyard at Walker-on-Tyne by a 23-year-old Quaker, John Richardson.

Swan Hunter (NE), which dates back to 1842, once built, repaired, and converted ships at its Wallsend shipyard on the Tyne river and its Port Clarence yard on the Tees River. The first to build tankers of more than 250,000 tons on a slipway, the Wallsend shipyard constructed ships in a floating dock that minimized risks during launches. The Port Clarence yard specialized in structures for the offshore industry and for other shipbuilders.
