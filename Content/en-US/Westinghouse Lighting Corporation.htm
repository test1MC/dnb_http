And George Westinghouse said "Let there be light." Following in the great man's footsteps, Westinghouse Lighting Corporation (WLC) makes more than 5,000 electrical and lighting products including light bulbs, lighting fixtures and hardware, door chimes, and wall plates. The Energy Star certified manufacturer also makes ceiling fans for indoor and outdoor use under brands such as Builder and Industrial Plus. Its decorative electrical lineup extends from lighting hardware to glassware. The company's global distribution facilities dot Mexico, Germany, Canada, Panama, and China, catering to residential, commercial, and industrial markets.

WLC has major locations in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania; Chino, California; Montreal, Canada; Dusseldorf, Germany; Colon, Panama; and Mexico City.

The company offers hundreds of ceiling fans, lighting fixtures, lighting hardware and accessories, as well as a full line of light bulbs for both consumer and commercial indoor and outdoor applications. It sells sells its products to both retail (including online retailers like Amazon.com ) and business-to-business markets and through its joint venture with Combi, WLC sells products into Canada through distributors and retail partners.

WLC sells its products through distributors in 128 countries on six continents.

Beefing up its services in Canada, in 2013 WLC teamed up with Combi Inc to form joint venture Westinghouse Lighting Canada. Combi remains Westinghouse Lighting's representative and third-party logistics provider while Westinghouse Lighting Canada provides products and services directly to Canadian customers while positioning WLC for growth in that country's distribution and retail markets.

Responding to the growing adoption of LED lighting, in 2012 Westinghouse began offering both Professional Series and General Purpose LEDs.

WLC traces its roots to entrepreneur and inventor George Westinghouse, the man who illuminated the 1893 World's Fair, and whose championing of alternating current (AC) (instead of direct current (DC) system) led to the electrification of US homes. Formerly known as Angelo Brothers, the company changed its name in 2003. Six years earlier, Stan Angelo, an executive for his family's lamp business, purchased the rights to the Westinghouse brand for lighting and ceiling fan products. Chairman Stan Angelo and his brother, president and CEO Raymond Angelo, lead Westinghouse Electric.
