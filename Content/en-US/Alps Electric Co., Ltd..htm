Alps Electric makes a wide range of electronic components (some 40,000) for a wide range of products. The component it produces include sensors, switches, actuators, and potentiometers that go in vehicle navigation systems, touch screens, digital cameras, environmental systems, and other products. Some of the end-products it makes include smart phones, autos, keyboards, printers, and remote controls. Its US-based subsidiary Alpine Electric makes mobile audio equipment and electronics.

The company's electronic components segment, which brings in 47% of revenue, makes components for safety, energy efficiency, and comfort in vehicles and, for consumers, components for TVs, PCs, and digital cameras. Automotive infotainment, which generates 39% of sales, includes components that enhance the human-machine interaction in vehicles; and, logistics, about 11% of sales, handles transportation, storage, and forwarding services.

About 40% of the company's sales are evenly split between Japan and America. China and Germany accounted for nearly 16% and 11% with the rest of the world making up the rest. Alps has 50 sales offices in 14 countries. It makes products at seven manufacturing facilities in Japan and 14 more in eight countries. Research and development and design is conducted in three facilities in Japan and five facilities in three other countries.

The financial fortunes of Alps hiked to a higher altitude in 2015 (ended March) after several uneven years. Revenue rose 25%, with each segment posting higher sales. The company benefited from higher auto sales in the US and Europe, which has Alps components on board as well as continued strong sales of smart phones. Net income recovered into positive territory in 2015, pushed by stronger sales. Almost as dramatic was the upswing in cash flow from operations, which rose 133% from 2014 to 2015.

Alps is in the middle of a three-year business plan to reach higher financial ground. It has laid out milestones for sales in the automotive and smartphone markets and it wants to establish new business in environmental and energy as well as to build core businesses. Products for low-carbon, high-efficiency energy use include reactors for hybrid and electric vehicles and in solar power generation systems.
