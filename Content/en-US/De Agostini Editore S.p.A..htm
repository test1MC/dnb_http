Italian publishing giant De Agostini Editore has a library of titles large enough to fill the Roman Coliseum. The company prints non-fiction books, children's books, magazines, and scholarly tomes such as atlases, reference books, textbooks, and educational materials. It also creates collectibles (games and toys), cards, and DVDs. Its Digital De Agostini distributes content via the Internet and operates digital TV services. De Agostini Editore sells its periodicals primarily at newsstands and through mail order; its materials are available in more than a dozen languages across about 30 countries, mostly in Europe and Latin America. The company is a subsidiary of media holding group De Agostini .
