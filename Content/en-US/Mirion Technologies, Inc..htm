Mirion Technologies provides a myriad of radiation detection, measurement, and monitoring products and services to the nuclear power, defense, and medical industries. The company operates several divisions including health physics (nuclear detection and monitoring devices), radiation monitoring systems, sensing systems, dosimetry systems (used to measure doses of radiation), and imaging systems (radiation-tolerant cameras). Customers include nuclear power plants, hospitals, and military agencies. The company serves clients worldwide from offices in Asia, Europe, and North America. Investment firm American Capital has majority ownership in Mirion.

Mirion gets most of its sales from the nuclear market, including sales to mine, fuel manufacturing facility, nuclear reactor, fuel reprocessing, military, weapons, and waste storage installations. The company touts that its products are installed in about 90% of active nuclear reactors around the world, including all of those operating in the US.

It also serves the medical market, where increased use of nuclear and radiological diagnostic and therapeutic procedures is driven by an aging global population and by emerging opportunities in markets such as India and China. Safety concerns have increased demand for dosimetry and radiation detection and monitoring products by hospitals, clinics, and medical imaging facilities. In the defense market, Mirion's growth has largely been tied to civil defense, military, and other government spending initiatives to combat worldwide security threats.

Mirion operates about a dozen facilities in Canada, China, Finland, France, Germany, the UK, and the US.
