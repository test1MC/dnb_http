Start with a strong dose of analytical expertise in organic compounds, add more than a pinch of synthesis, and mix in high quality manufacturing operations. These are the key ingredients that ABITEC says make up its business. Enzyme producer ABITEC's services range from vegetable oil refining to customer-specific ingredients synthesis. In addition to emulsifiers for the food and flavor industries, the company makes oleochemical-based ethoxylates for industrial uses, emollients and emulsifiers for the personal care segment, and excipients for pharmaceutical companies. It is a part of the ingredients unit of Associated British Foods , which is controlled by the Wittington Investments.

The company is headquartered in Columbus, Ohio, and has manufacturing sites in Wisconsin and Illinois.

ABITEC provides bioavailability enhancers, carriers, solubilizers, emulsifiers, energy sources, structured lipids, nutritional powders, and tablet lubricants for functional food, supplement, and nutrition markets. One of ABITEC's main product lines is CAPTEX, medium chain triglycerides.

ABITEC's functional excipients can be incorporated into a broad range of drug formulations and dosage forms including; soft-gelatin capsules, tablets, solid microemulsions, and self-microemulsifying drug delivery systems (SMEEDS).

The company serves a range of markets, including Contract and Toll Manufacturing, Pharmaceutical, Nutritional Sciences, Specialty Chemicals, and Personal Care, Candles & Cosmetics.

ABITECH has introduced new INJECTA parenteral grade lipid excipients at CPHI to enable improved solubilization and permeation for injectable APIs, andexpanded its portfolio of CAPMUL bioavailability.

ABITEC also develops and produces customized products (the manufacture of new and established oleochemical molecules) for US-based chemical manufacturing companies.

The ABITEC name is derived from AB(Associated British) I(Ingredient) TEC(Technologies).
