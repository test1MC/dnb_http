Brewer Science brews up a rich mixture of innovative materials, processes, and equipment for the fabrication of cutting-edge microdevices used in electronics (such as tablet computers, smartphones, digital cameras, televisions, and LED lighting). Its chemicals include anti-reflective coatings and specialty polymers and resins. Brewer Science also makes baking and curing equipment used to make wafers and photo masks. Its customers include companies in the aviation, automotive, diagnostic, communications, and computer manufacturing industries. The family owned company operates from locations in eight countries and gets more than half of its sales from customers outside of the US.

The company has operations in China (Hong Kong and Shanghai), France, Germany, Korea, Japan, Taiwan, the UK, and the US.

Brewer Science processes technology, advanced materials and equipment for the reliable fabrication of cutting-edge microdevices used in electronics, such as automotive sensors, cell phones, computers, displays, flash drives, LED lighting, logic devices, and memory devices.

Key target markets for Brewer Science include advanced packaging, compound semiconductors, displays, processing equipment, LED and energy devices, semiconductor microelectromechanical systems, printed electronics, and sensors.

Brewer Science focuses on product development in niche markets, and on rolling out new products.

In 2014 it introduced the first aqueous, surfactant-free, ready-to-use semiconducting CNT inks for microelectronics and printed electronics applications.

A year earlier, Brewer Science installed a scale-up reactor to increase production by ten-fold of its CNTRENE C100 family of electronics-grade CNT materials (used in chemical and biological sensors and nanotube-based nonvolatile random access memory device applications).

In 2013 the company broke ground on a new facility at the Rolla National Airport which will be used to produce advanced materials for manufacturing next-generation smartphones, tablet computers, and other microelectronic devices. It also got the approval of a $1 million grant from the Economic Development Administration, a bureau of the US Department of Commerce , to help fund the project.
