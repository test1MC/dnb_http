This company can print a lot more than restaurant supply catalogs. Fry Communications prints catalogs, magazines, and other commercial publications with print runs from 6,000 to 5 million. In addition to digital prepress, printing, and bookbinding, the company provides a range of services, including mailing and distribution, content management, graphic design, and website development. Customers include book and periodical publishers, educational organizations, financial institutions, marketing firms, health care providers, and government agencies.

Fry Communications operates from six facilities in Pennsylvania.

In 2012 "Fry Family Network member" Godengo, the largest provider of content management systems for magazine publishers, acquired Texterity, a leading provider of digital magazines, mobile applications, and a virtual newsstand. Fry Communications hopes the combination of Godengo and Texterity will provide a next-generation unified platform for Web, mobile, and app publishing.

In 2011 Fry Communications acquired a majority of Aysling Digital Media Solutions in order to expand its presence in the digital media markets. Aysling is a provider of digital content creation and distribution services. The deal also expanded Fry's US presence -- Aysling has offices in Ann Arbor, Michigan and Orlando, Florida.

Fry Communications was founded in 1934 as a publisher of weekly guides.
