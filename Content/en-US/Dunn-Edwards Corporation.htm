If you want to paint the town red, it's a "Dunn" deal. With a manufacturing facility in Arizona and a head office in California, Dunn-Edwards makes numerous paints and coatings, as well as paint-related products ranging from ladders to spray equipment. The company has more than 115 retail outlets and caters to architects, designers, home builders, and property managers in the Southwest; it also serves do-it-yourselfers, though professionals account for 90% of sales. Among the places coated with Dunn-Edwards paint are the Staples Center in Los Angeles and Caesars Palace in Las Vegas. In late 2016 the company agreed to be acquired by Nippon Paint .

Dunn-Edwards operates stores throughout Arizona (where it has a manufacturing plant), California, Nevada, New Mexico, and Texas; it also has 60 authorized dealers in the US Southwest. Its international presence includes authorized dealers in China, Guam, Korea, Lebanon, Lithuania, Mexico, the Philippines, and Singapore.

The paint manufacturer supplies architectural and industrial coatings, producing up to 12,000 gallons of paint in 2-4 hours.

The company serves a range of customers, including Painting Contractors, Architects and Designers, Builders and Developers, Property Managers, and Education and Government Agencies.

Dunn-Edwards strives to be environmentally friendly and reduce or eliminate harmful ingredients from its paints. Following its Greener by Design philosophy, the company participates in such initiatives as energy efficiency, recycling, and emissions reduction.

The company also formulates its paints for the climate of the US Southwest, and regularly introduces new brands and paint variations.

In 2015 Dunn-Edwards enhanced its designer-preferred Perfect Palette color system with the Then, Now & Forever collection of 300 new colors.

In 2013 the company introduced a new line of interior, low odor, Zero volatile organic compound (VOC) paint called Spartazero for use in residential and commercial projects, schools, hospitals, hospitality, and other locations where low odor products are preferred.

In 2012 it added an Eggshell sheen to its popular Contractor's Edge Interior Maintenance line used in the apartment maintenance repaint market.

It introduced two new lines of paint products in 2011, Spartawall Interior Acrylic Paints and Spartashield Exterior 100% Acrylic Paints for use on commercial and residential projects requiring a more durable, longer lasting paint.

In 2011 Dunn-Edwards opened what it billed as the world's first LEED-certified paint manufacturing facility, in Phoenix, Arizona. The green building received a gold certification rating from the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) by the US Green Building "Council. The $40 million, fully automated plant -- designed to be one of the most efficient in the coatings industry -- houses such operations as manufacturing, product development, distribution, retail, and quality-control laboratories.

The company was founded in 1925 by Frank Dunn. In 1938 he partnered with his friend (and former painting contractor) Arthur C. Edwards, to form the Dunn-Edwards Corporation.

Dunn-Edwards is owned lock, stock, and paint bucket by the employees and the Edwards family.
