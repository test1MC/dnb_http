The semiconductors of Sandbridge Technologies help wireless signals bridge thin air. The company designs baseband processors for wireless handheld devices. Sandbridge's multi-threaded Sandblaster instruction set architecture is said to have the capacity for software-defined radio baseband processing and advanced multimedia operations, without increasing battery consumption. The company's SB3000 flexible baseband processor contains four Sandblaster cores. In 2008 Sandbridge brought out the software-programmable SB3500 processor, capable of replacing two or more chips in a handset design. Cadence Design Systems , IBM , and Lucent Technologies veterans founded the fabless semiconductor company in 2001.
