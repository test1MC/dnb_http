If you want to see the biggest salt mine in the world, you should go to its owner, North American Salt. The company, one of the world's largest salt producers (and a subsidiary of Compass Minerals International ), manufactures a variety of salts, including agricultural, food, and industrial grades. North American Salt has production and mining sites in the US and Canada, including the largest global salt mine, which is located in Goderich, Ontario. Products include mined rock salt for highway and consumer deicing projects and high-grade branded and private label mineral products for use in homes and businesses.

The company produce salt in the US (Ogden, Utah; Lyons, Kansas; Kenosha, Wisconsin; Duluth, Minnesota; Chicago, Illinois; and Cote Blanche, Louisiana) and Canada (Goderich, Ontario; Amherst, Nova Scotia; and Unity, Saskatchewan).

North American Salt produces millions of tons of salt and other minerals a year for use in snow and ice removal, animal nutrition, water conditioning, and pool, industrial, and food applications.

Formerly known as IMC Salt, the company was incorporated in 1988.
