Gillig manufactures heavy-duty buses for mass transit systems. The company offers two main models, the BRT and the Low Floor, in multiple lengths and widths. It also builds the Trolley, a replica of an old fashioned cable car. The company produces mostly diesel-powered buses and, to a lesser extent, a variety of alternative-fuel models, including one that uses a hybrid diesel-electric engine made by General Motors . The bus maker has the capacity to turn out as many as 1,300 vehicles annually. Gillig is a subsidiary of private equity firm CC Industries.

Gillig has its headquarters in Hayward, California. It has its manufacturing facility and parts warehouse located in Hayward, California, within the San Francisco Bay Area.

The company supplies original equipment, premium aftermarket parts, and supplies for other major brands including Chance, Flx, MCI, NABI , Neoplan, New Flyer , Orion, and RTS.

The company traces its historical roots back to 1890.
