Worthington Cylinder makes tanks to contain industrial and medical gases. A subsidiary of US steel manufacturer Worthington Industries , Worthington Cylinder makes steel and aluminum pressure cylinders used to hold liquid propane gas, refrigerants, and acetylene. The company also makes products designed for consumer usage, including scuba tanks, Balloon Time (helium for party balloons) tanks, Flame Saver (propane tanks with a built-in reserve) tanks, and Worthington Pro Grade torches. It is also offers cylinders for liquid nitrogen, oxygen, argon, and carbon dioxide. The company has manufacturing plants in North America and Europe and sells its products in more than 70 countries.

It has a global network of 17 manufacturing facilities in the US (California, Kansas-2, North Carolina, North Dakota, Oklahoma, Ohio-5, and Wisconsin), Austria, India, Poland-2, Portugal, and Turkey.

Worthington's Pro Grade torches use MAP-Pro gas (higher flame temperature than propane), propane, oxygen, or propylene, and are used primarily by plumbing and refrigeration repair professionals. The company's consumer products line includes propane tanks used for grilling and camping. Additionally, it makes manufactured products such as automotive aftermarket stampings, metal framing, metal ceiling grid systems, and laser-welded blanks.

Worthington Cylinder primarily sells its tanks to gas manufacturers and distributors. It has 4,500 customers and also sells its products through retailers such as Lowe's , Wal-Mart , ACE , Sears , Rona , and Orgill . Distributors have included Western Outdoor Wholesalers, Diversco Supply, Tri-State Distributors, and NRG Distributors.

Worthington Cylinder operates under the Pressure Cylinders segment of its parent. This segment contributed about 30% of Worthington Industries' total revenues in 2015.

Expanding its portfolio, in 2014 the company launched its cryogenic liquid cylinder line. The cylinders ranging from 180 - 265 liters and are used to store liquid nitrogen, oxygen, argon and carbon dioxide for industrial gas markets. Worthington has dedicated a new production area at its Westerville, Ohio facility to produce the new cylinders.

Worthington Cylinder also grows through acquisitions by its parent, which allows it to participate in the expanding North American oil and gas markets, as well as global growth in alternatives for gas and diesel to power vehicles, which include compressed natural gas (CNG), liquefied natural gas (LNG), autogas (propane) and hydrogen.

The company was established in 1972 when its parent company purchased Lennox Industries' cylinder division.
