Parkview Metal Products -- named for the company's original location, which had a view of Chicago's Wrigley Field -- stamps light-gauge metal to produce the outer shell, or housing, for computers, microwaves, and other consumer electronics. Production includes fastening, welding, assembly, and hardware insertion operations. The company's technical center provides engineering, tool building, and prototyping services. Parkview runs both low- and high-volume metal stamping jobs and has adopted just-in-time order fulfillment to minimize excess inventory. The Leutwiler family, including CEO Nels Leutwiler, owns Parkview.
