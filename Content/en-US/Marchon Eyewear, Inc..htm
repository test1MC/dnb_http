Marchon Eyewear believes fashion and glasses go hand in hand. One of the world's top eyewear designers and manufacturers, Marchon distributes eyeglasses and sunglasses in more than 100 countries. It sells its eyewear collections under the Marchon brand and those of more than 20 designers and brand names, including Calvin Klein , Kors , Sean John , and NIKE . Marchon is the first manufacturer of frames for Google Glass under the DVF/Made for Glass name. It also makes googles for action sports. The company manufactures eyewear in Italy and China. Founded by three friends in 1983, Marchon is owned by vision-care provider Vision Service Plan.

New York-based Marchon Eyewear has design centers in design centers in New York and Italy, with manufacturing taking place in Italy and China. It also has regional and local offices in Australia, Brazil, Canada, France, Germany, Greece, Hong Kong, Italy, Japan, Mexico, the Netherlands, New Zealand, Portugal, Switzerland, Turkey, United Arab Emirates, the UK, and the US.

In 2014 Marchon is focusing on opportunities in Europe and the Middle East. It recently restructured its sales organization to support expansion in those areas. The company retail division focuses on high-end department stores and boutiques, as well as duty free shops.

Diving into a niche market, Marchon in May 2012 acquired Dragon Alliance, a leading goggle and eyewear brand in the action sports market catering to skiers, snowboarders, surfers, and moto rider. The purchase strengthened Marchon's presence in the youth lifestyle and action sports niches. Later in 2012 the company purchased the Swedish eyewear firm Scandinavian Eyewear, thereby broadening the distribution of its proprietary and licensed brands in the Nordic region. Scandinavian Eyewear's proprietary SKAGA brand is a leading eyewear brand in Scandinavia.
