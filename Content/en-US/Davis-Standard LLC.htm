Davis-Standard's technology has got your products covered. The company designs and manufactures extrusion systems and process controls for the wire, cable, plastic, and rubber industries. Extrusion products include foam, fiber, pipe, and sheet extruders; its conversion products provide liquid coating and laminating, blow molding, and industrial films. It operates through subsidiaries Black Clawson Converting Machinery, Brookes, Harrel, Clipper Machines, among others. Davis-Standard is owned by ONCAP, a private equity fund owned by Canada-based investment firm Onex .

Davis-Standard has three manufacturing and technical facilities in the US, in addition to subsidiaries in China, Germany, and the UK. Sales offices reside in France, Russia, the Philippines, and China. Research and development centers are located in Pawcatuck, Connecticut; Bridgewater, New Jersey; and Fulton, New York.

A short list of its subsidiaries includes Black Clawson Converting Machinery, Brookes, Harrel, Clipper Machines, Killion, and Egan, among others. These businesses serve customers in the agricultural, automotive, building, flexible packaging, medical, and communications markets.

The company's specialty systems business has benefited from the government's stimulus initiatives over the years. Davis-Standard is creating new materials and processes for solar energy projects, which are funded through the stimulus package and are proving to be growth areas for the company.

Davis-Standard claims to generate $300 million in annual sales.

Davis-Standard is boosting its operations through geographic expansion, acquisitions, and partnerships. In 2012 the company penetrated the most populous country in the world when it opened a new facility in Suzhou, China. To better support customers in the Asia region, in 2014 it opened the Spare Parts and Service Center at Suzhou plant in 2014.

In 2013, Davis-Standard and Guangdong Jinming Machinery teamed up to enhance the blown film product line, global footprint and service capabilities of both companies.

For greater operating efficiency, in 2013 Davis-Standard moved its manufacturing work out of the Egan blown and cast film factory in Bridgewater, New Jersey to Davis-Standard's Black Clawson plant in Fulton, New York.

In 2012 it purchased Circonix Technologies, a provider of drive control system retrofits, in a deal that gave Davis-Standard the ability to upgrade drive and mechanical systems (including non-company brands). The move also extended the company's penetration in the aftermarket services market.

In 2005 Davis-Standard acquired Merritt Extruder Corporation of Hamden, Connecticut and moved its operations to Davis-Standard's headquarters in Pawcatuck, Connecticut.

The company was founded in 1848 as a manufacturer of cotton gin machinery and began manufacturing extruders a century later.
