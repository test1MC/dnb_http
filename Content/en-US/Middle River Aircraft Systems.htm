Everyone knows that planes can reach great speeds -- and that's great when a plane is flying, but how in the world do pilots stop large planes on runways? Middle River Aircraft Systems (MRAS) knows that stopping a large aircraft takes more than simple brakes; it takes jet engine thrust reversers. The company, which is a subsidiary of General Electric , makes thrust reversers -- which divert jet thrust backwards, thus slowing the plane -- for GE and Pratt & Whitney aircraft engines, as well as specialized aerostructures, such as nacelles and flight control surfaces. MRAS also provides aircraft overhaul and repair services.
