Keeping systematic pressure on the competition, Compressor Systems manufactures, sells, and services compression equipment used by international oil and gas customers for well head compression, gas gathering and storage, liquid recovery, gas transmission, and pressure maintenance. Compressor Systems sells equipment primarily in the Great Plains, Intermountain West, and Southwest, and has sales and service offices in Alabama, Arkansas, Colorado, Kansas, Louisiana, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Texas, Utah, and Wyoming. It also has international operations in Australia and Mexico. The company is owned by co-founder Johnny Warren's Warren Equipment Company .

The company operates through business units in the Gulf Coast, Mid-Continent, Northern Rockies, Permian Basin, Southern Rockies, and South Texas as well as in Australia and Mexico.

Compressor Systems fabricates, sells, rents and services natural gas compressors. It also operates Compressor Systems Australia Pty. Ltd., Compressor Systems de Mexico, Pump Systems International (which has 130,000 sq. ft. of fabrication capability and more than 400 tons of crane capacity), and Rotary Compressor Systems, Inc. The company's sister companies include Ignition Systems & Controls and North American Power Systems.

CSI gas compressor packages feature Caterpillar and Waukesha engines with Ariel reciprocating compressors and Frick rotary screw compressors.

The company distributes parts for F.W. Murphy Power Controls, Mercer Valves, Kingtool, and Ariel Compressors through its sister company Ignition Systems & Controls. The company's manufacturers (for part and services) include Aeroquip, Chart Coolers, and Caterpillar Engine and Parts.

In 2011 Compressor Systems Australia, Pty. Ltd. acquired Queensland-based Roma Diesel Service. The deal strengthened Compressor Systems Australia's position in the coal seam methane gas market.

Compressor Systems was established in 1971 by the owners of Treanor Equipment Company, a Caterpillar dealership in Odessa, Texas, to boost the sales of gas engines to the compression industry. The original owners included two members of the Treanor family and Johnny Warren.
