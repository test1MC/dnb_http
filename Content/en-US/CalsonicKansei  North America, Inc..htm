CalsonicKansei North America's business tends to run hot and cold - which is a good thing. The Nissan -affiliated company makes car air conditioning and heating systems, thermal components, cockpit modules, consoles, exhaust systems, radiators, cooling fans, and other related automotive components. The company also offers services including engineering, testing and development, as well as testing labs. CalsonicKansei North America is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Japan's Calsonic Kansei Corporation (which itself is about 40% controlled by Nissan Motor Co.).

CalsonicKansei North America has about 10 factories in Kentucky, Michigan, Mississippi, and Tennessee, as well as in Mexico.

The company's product lines include Climate Control, Electronics, Cooling, Engine, Exhaust, Interior, and Systems Integration. Its research and testing facility in Detroit helps to ensure that its engine cooling, climate control, and exhaust systems deliver optimal performances.

The majority of the company's products are purchased by Nissan . Other customers include General Motors and Honda Motor Co .

Like most companies in the manufacturing sector, CalsonicKansei North America grows by adding to its manufacturing capacity and investing in its equipment and infrastructure. In late 2013 it announced plans to invest $110 million and add 1,200 new manufacturing jobs across its facilities in Lewisburg, Shelbyville, and Smyrna, Tennessee. It is investing some $50 million at its Lewisburg facility, which manufactures electronic components for meters, inverters and dash electronics, and plastics components for interiors.

The investment in Lewisburg includes the construction of a new 300,000 sq. ft. warehouse facility; it is investing nearly $58 million at its Shelbyville facility, which manufactures exhaust units, catalytic converters and manifolds. Around $2 million will go toward its Smyrna facility, which is located within the Nissan plant and places Calsonic products into the Nissan vehicles.

The company began operations in 1976 at a plant in Irvine, California.
