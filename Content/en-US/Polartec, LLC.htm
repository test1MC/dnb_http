What keeps arctic explorers warm on the tundra and marathon runners dry in the long run? Polartec's high-tech fabrics do. The company -- which introduced Polarfleece more than 30 years ago -- makes more than 300 types of all-season synthetic textiles used primarily in the manufacture of performance apparel and outerwear under the eponymous Polartec brand. Its textiles are fundamental to active garments produced for retailers such as L.L. Bean , The North Face , and Timberland , as well as the military. Polartec is owned by private equity firm Versa Capital Management.

Polartec has operations in France, Italy, Spain, the Netherlands, and the US.

Polartec is a premium producer of innovative textiles and supplies advanced fabric innovations. Its products range from advanced lightweight wicking fabric to insulation and weather-protection textiles.

Among the applications for Polartec fabrics, the US military apparel is a particularly promising outlet. Polartec's fabrics for hostile climates are used by all branches of the armed forces. The company has delivered to the US Marine Corps stationed in Afghanistan and Iraq a new type of jacket that keeps out rain, wind, sand, and snow -- all while making the wearer less visible to enemy night-vision goggles. Polartec also helped to develop advanced cold weather clothing for the US Army , available through specialized equipment and logistics support provider ADS .

Polartec manufactures more than 300 different fabrics under the brand Polartec, including Polartec Power Dry, Polartec Power Stretch, Polartec Classic 100, 200 and 300, Polartec Thermal Pro, Polartec Wind Pro, Polartec Windbloc, and Polartec Power Shield.

The company's products are used by leading apparel brands, and the US and other militaries, as well as the flame resistance, work wear, contract upholstery, and commercial markets. It also sells its fabrics through retailers, including online retailers.

It is also a partner of the Outdoor Industry Association (which has 4,000 manufacturers, distributors, suppliers, sales representatives, and retailers as members).

Polartec stays afloat by offering more than just performance fabric. It is repositioning its signature brand, carving out sales for its fabrics by introducing new technologies and applications that deliver an end product with improved insulation, protection, and performance characteristics. The company holds more than 200 patents on fabric construction, thermal insulation, weather protection, wicking fabrics, and high loft fleece.

A technological alliance has been made between Polartec and Finetex EnE, a filtration materials maker. Polartec gained exclusive global rights to market Finetex's micro-porous membrane technology combined with Polartec's lamination engineering. The results are sold under the Polartec Power Shield Pro nameplate and offer an array of water resistant and breathable soft shell fabrics.

Polartec also grows by rolling out new products. In 2013 it introduced Polartec Alpha synthetic insulation, a new fabric product with an emphasis on durability, versatility, breathability, and warmth. Adopters of the new product include Eddie Bauer , Marmot, Mountain Equipment, Trangoworld, and The North Face .

Growing its network of clients, in 2013 the company signed deal with Quick-Med Technologies to allow it to develop a range of products using Polartec materials and technologies.

The company began its life as Malden Knitting in 1906, later changing its name to Malden Mills Industries. In 2007 it became Polartec.
