Primex Plastics Corporation's talents lie between the sheets -- the custom-extruded plastic sheets that the company manufactures. Primex's products are based on a family of synthetic resins and sheet molding compounds, including polystyrene (HIPS), polyethylene (PE), polypropylene (PP), polyester (PETG), and ABS (acrylonitrile butadiene styrene). The sheets are used in a myriad of products, from food and medical trays to cosmetics packaging, truck caps and bedliners, outdoor signs, paddle boats, and portable toilets. Primex operates in conjunction with subsidiaries O'Neil Color & Compounding , Pace Industries, Woodruff, and its UK arm Primex Plastics Limited. Privately held ICC Industries owns Primex.

The company has manufacturing plants in Georgia, Indiana, New Jersey, Nevada, and Wisconsin. It also operates a UK subsidiary, Primex Plastics Ltd.

Primex operates more than 100 plastic sheet extruders at its plants. Subsidiaries include Dover Chemical, O'Neil Color & Compounding Corporation , Pace Industries, and Woodruff Corporation.

Primex serves customers in a range of industries, including appliance, automotive, construction, cosmetic, food packaging, graphic arts, health care, textile, toy, transportation, and pharmaceutical.

Primex is striving to cut waste and improve resource utilization in both process and finished products. The company looks to recapture manufacturing byproducts or to divert items from the waste stream whenever possible.

Primex was founded as a thermoplastic sheet provider in 1965.
