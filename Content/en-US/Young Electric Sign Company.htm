YESCO makes signs shine. Young Electric Sign Company (YESCO) custom designs displays and signs for companies of all sizes and in a variety of industries. Products include neon signs, billboards, custom spectaculars, LED video displays, and electronic message centers. The company also provides maintenance and repair services for its products and retrofit services which reduce energy costs. It boasts more than 2,000 sign installations across the US and around the world. YESCO has locations in about 27 states and one location in Canada. In early 2015, the company was acquired by electronics giant Samsung .

YESCO's other artistic services include providing illustration for books, magazines, and stamps; creating murals and dioramas for zoos and aquariums in the US and Japan; providing videography; glassblowing to create custom chandeliers and light fixtures; and creating 3-D designs out of metal, wood, plexiglass, and high-density foam.

Founded in 1920, YESCO found a niche in the 1930s with neon signs, and really lit up as Las Vegas boomed. The company is responsible for many of the signs that grace "Sin City" including the marquees at New York New York and Caesars Palace as well as iconic neon cowboy Vegas Vic and the "Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas" sign.
