Soft drinks, candy, and potato chips are the major food groups for Taghleef Industries. Doing business as TI Films, the company produces oriented polypropylene (OPP) films used to make flexible packaging, as well as labeling for plastic, metal, and glass containers. It sells its OPP films to converters (companies that print text and graphics on the films) and to end users in the food and beverage, automotive, personal products, pet food, and medical industries, among others. Taghleef Industries also makes apertured, or porous, films used as facing material for products such as adhesive bandages.

Taghleef has distribution centers in Germany, the UK, France, Portugal, South Africa and El Salvador, and representative offices in China, Malaysia, and India.

Taghleef serves 500 customers with more than 80 different product groups.

Amin Khoury, who began his career as a research scientist with Wyeth Labs, founded Applied Extrusion Technologies (AET) in 1986. In 2012 AET was acquired by Taghleef Industries (TI Films).
