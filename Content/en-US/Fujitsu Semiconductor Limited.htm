Fujitsu Semiconductor makes an iron-clad case for its memory chips. Its ferroelectric random access memory (FRAM) chips contain a layer of iron and offer an long lasting and low power alternative to conventional flash memory devices. The subsidiary of Japanese conglomerate Fujitsu Limited, the semiconductor unit produces other types of circuits and operates fabrication plants that serve the parent company and other electronics companies. Manufacturing units (organized mainly by the size of wafers they produce) are Mie Fujitsu Semiconductor, Aizu Fujitsu Semiconductor, and Aizu Fujitsu Semiconductor Wafer Solution, and Aizu Fujitsu Semiconductor Manufacturing. In 2015, Fujitsu Semiconductor joined with Panasonic and the Development Bank of Japan to spin off Fujitsu's large-scale integration circuit business into a company called Socionext.
