The Real Good Food Company takes what larger food firm have pushed off their plates. Since 2003 it has bought half a dozen smaller manufacturers that once belonged to high-end convenience food makers looking to sell off non-core operations. Real Good Food's sugar products are made by subsidiary Napier Brown and distributed to industrial, retail, and foodservice customers. Its Renshaw business is known in the UK for its sugarpaste and marzipan. Yeast-dough products and other baking ingredients come from the company's Haydens Bakery unit. Garrett Ingredients is a top UK supplier of dairy and bakery ingredients and ice-cream mixes. Real Good Food's large retail customers include Waitrose and Marks and Spencer .
