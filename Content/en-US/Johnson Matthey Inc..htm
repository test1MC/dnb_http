According to Johnson Matthey's math, its component parts adds up to serving the precious metals, catalysts, coatings, and pharmaceutical industries in the US. The company also provides contract research and development for the pharmaceutical industry. Its Emission Control Technologies segment makes catalytic systems and emission controls for a range of industries, including automotive manufacturing. The Precious Metals segment is engaged in supplying fabricated precious metal alloys, chemicals, and catalysts, as well as medical device components, and colors and coatings. The company is the North American unit of UK chemicals and catalysts maker Johnson Matthey plc .

Johnson Matthey Inc. operates in the US.

There are no separate financials for the company; however, parent Johnson Matthey plc reported that its revenues from the US increased by 1% in 2015 thanks to a growth in production. That year the parent company derived about 35% of its revenues from North America, with the US accounting for 25%.

In North America Johnson Matthey plc completed a learning needs analysis to identify business priorities and common requirements. This formed the basis for a regional learning and development strategy it implemented in 2013 and 2014.

In 2012 the Medical Device Components business of Johnson Matthey Inc. (Johnson Matthey Medical) announced a partnership and exclusive supply agreement with Microfabrica Inc. , a leading provider of sub-millimeter scale, high precision metal components for a range of industries. This partnership is expected to significantly expand the metal component choices Johnson Matthey Inc. can offer its customers at the level of millimeter and sub millimeter scale.

The company was founded in 1935.
