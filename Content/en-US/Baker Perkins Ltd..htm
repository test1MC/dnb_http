Through its assortment of machinery products, Baker Perkins does its best to ensure your sweet tooth gets something to bite into. The company makes food production equipment for bakery, biscuit and crackers, breakfast cereal and snack, and confectionery manufacturers. Among its products are mixers, dividers, sandwiching machines (for Oreo-like cookies), cookers, toasters, provers (for bread-baking), ovens, and injection machinery (to shoot filling into cookies or crackers). Baker Perkins manufactures equipment from its two locations in the UK and US.

The company operates from two sites -- Peterborough, in the UK, and Grand Rapids, Michigan, in the US. It sells its products across North America, South America, Africa, Europe, and Asia.

Baker Perkins serves a range of industries, including Bread, Biscuit, Cookie and Cracker, Confectionery, Cereal, Snack, Dry Pet Food, Industrial Extrusion, and Customer Services. It sells equipment to customers throughout the world, from UK baker Warburtons to Greek confectioner Hermes, and Vietnamese candy maker Bibica Corporation. In addition, the company runs supply centers across the UK and US, providing aftermarket parts and services for its machinery.

Baker Perkins' products are sold through regional representatives located worldwide, which include Hirshberg Brothers & Co Machinery; Techquip Developments; Agroexport Alimec; TEA Bakery Systems; and 3P Solutions Private Limited.

Responding to significant orders from customers in Europe and Australasia, in 2014, the company re-launched its Cook Master Cereal Cooker range, including an enhanced control system for all Baker Perkins Cook Master machines.

Baker Perkins has also launched an expandable line for snack and cereal manufacturing that offers process flexibility, volume processing, and reduced maintenance. Its line is touted to make popular consumer products while extending in stages to produce a range of high-specification foods that appeal to health-conscious consumers.

The company was founded in 1920 by the merger of Werner, Pfleiderer & Perkins, and Joseph Baker & Sons.
