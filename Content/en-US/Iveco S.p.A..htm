Whether it's beer from Bavaria or vino from Venice, European goods often get from point A to point B with a little help from Iveco. A subsidiary of Fiat Industrial , Iveco is a manufacturer of commercial vehicles, fire and rescue vehicles, buses, specialty vehicles, and engines. Brands include Iveco (light-, medium-, and heavy-duty trucks), Iveco Motors (marine, automotive, and industrial engines), Iveco Magirus (fire trucks), Astra (construction vehicles), and Irisbus (buses). Iveco is a leader in Western Europe, China, and is looking to grow in Latin America. Iveco vehicles are not sold new in the US. The company sells its products through a network of more than 700 dealers.

Iveco is, meanwhile, angling to expand its footprint. Like most automotive and transport concerns, the rising Chinese economy beckons. The company has entered the market, striking a deal with the Beijing Public Transportation Corporation (BPTC) to deliver 1,400 natural gas fueled bus engines. Beijing purchased the cleaner-burning engines in an effort to improve air quality in preparation for the 2008 Olympic Games.
