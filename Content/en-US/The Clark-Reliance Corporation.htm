Power and process industry customers rely on The Clark-Reliance Corporation to manufacture level measurement and control products and alarm, separation, and energy conservation equipment. Products include instrumentation and controls; filtration equipment and elements; and glass kits, shields, gaskets, valve rebuild kits, relays, and separators. The family-owned company operates globally, supplying boiler and fluid control products to clients in the power generation, petroleum refining, chemical processing, pulp and paper, and food processing industries.

Clark-Reliance is based in Strongsville, Ohio with plants in Norcross, Georgia, and Zelienople, Pennsylvania.

The company's operating units are Clark-Reliance Instrument and Controls, Clark-Reliance Filtration Equipment, and Clark-Reliance Filter Elements.

Clark-Reliance Aftermarket Filter Elements makes filter, separator, and coalescing elements for applications in the electric power generation, natural gas conditioning, refinery, petrochemical, chemical, biodiesel production, ethanol, methanol, butanol processing, metal working coolants, and oil and gas industries. Clark-Reliance's aftermarket services include providing engineered replacement elements for more than 80 brands of filter cartridges/elements, separator elements, and coalescer elements.

Clark-Reliance Filtration group provides application assistance. Its products and services include turbine lube oils, natural gas filtration, natural gas separators, natural gas coalescing, plant water, fuel filtration, final product filtration, and condensate return.

Clark-Reliance's Instrumentation and Controls Group companies include Clark Steam Specialties, Jacoby-Tarbox, Jerguson Gage and Valve, Magne-Sonics, and Clark-Reliance.

The company sells its products through an international network of sales representatives. It has sales offices in North America (Canada, Mexico, and the US) Central America, the Caribbean, South America, Europe, Asia, the Middle East, Africa, and the South Pacific.

Clark-Reliance's ICON Management Systems is an instrumentation, controls, and filtration representative dedicated to serving marine customers, shipyards, the military, the Department of Defense , and their primary contractors.

Clark-Reliance has historically enhanced its operations through acquisitions. It has acquired about 10 companies since 1960 and continues to pursue this growth strategy.

Clark-Reliance traces its long history of innovation to 1884 when the newly formed Reliance Gauge Column Company of Cleveland produced and marketed the first alarm water column ever used by boiler operators. This device subsequently became standard equipment for major boiler makers across the US.

In 1968 Reliance Gauge Column Company merged with Clark Manufacturing Company (which had been founded in 1908), forming The Clark-Reliance Corporation.
