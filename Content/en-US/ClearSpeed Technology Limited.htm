ClearSpeed Technology hopes customers find a clear advantage in its chips. The fabless semiconductor company offers specialized microprocessors designed to handle math-intensive computing functions more rapidly -- and with less energy consumption -- than standard processors. Its chips are programmable and process data in parallel; the company also offers related development tools. The company touts its products for heavy-duty number-crunching chores in areas such as biomedical research and sonar equipment. ClearSpeed sells directly and through resellers and distributors.

ClearSpeed's products are the CSX700 processor and the Advance e710 processor.

The company pitches the CSX700 for running sophisticated data-parallel signal and image processing. Its efficient use of power makes it ready for remote applications. The processor is manufactured by IBM.e2v Technology PLC .

The Advance e710 is built for performance, reliability, precision, and accuracy for applications in financial services and science. It can be quickly installed into standard servers.

Founded in 1997 as PixelFusion, ClearSpeed was delisted in 2009 and taken private in 2011.
