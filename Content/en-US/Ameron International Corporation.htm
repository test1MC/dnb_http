Oil, water, and other liquids need a place to flow and that's where Ameron International comes in. The firm (aka NOV Ameron) designs, makes, and markets fiberglass-composite pipes for transmitting oil, chemicals, corrosive liquids, and other specialty materials. Ameron also makes and sells concrete and fabricated steel products used for water transmission and wind towers. Additionally, its infrastructure unit supplies ready-mix concrete and aggregates, box culverts, and sand for construction projects in Hawaii. The firm's pole products division makes concrete and steel poles for lighting and traffic signals. Ameron is a subsidiary of major oilfield equipment maker National Oilwell Varco .

Ameron operates in North America, Europe, South America, and Asia, with some 25 manufacturing locations around the globe. The company also has affiliates in the Middle East.

Ameron is a leader in pipe technology and a major supplier of highly-engineered concrete and steel pipe systems in the US, Mexico, and Canada. It operates as part of National Oilwell Varco 's Distribution & Transmission segment. The Distribution & Transmission segment provides pipe, maintenance, repair and operating supplies and spare parts to drill sites and production locations, pipeline operations, and processing plants worldwide. In addition to its comprehensive field location network, which supports land drilling operations throughout North America, the segment supports major land and offshore operations in all the major oil and gas producing regions throughout the world.

Ameron also manufactures a wide selection of street and highway lighting and traffic poles, as well as lighting poles for airports, parks, sports facilities, parking lots and residential and commercial developments.

The company's Water Transmission Group is a leader in pipe technology and a principal supplier of highly-engineered concrete and steel pipe systems in North, Central, and South America.

Ameron's Colombia-based American Pipe & Construction International subsidiary is a major supplier of concrete and steel pipe products in Central and South America.

Ameron Saudi Arabia, Ltd. (a 30%-owned affiliate) manufactures concrete pipe in Saudi Arabia and in Egypt (through a joint venture).

Ameron's Pole Products division supplies spun-cast, prestressed concrete and steel poles throughout the US from plants in California, Washington, Alabama, and Oklahoma.

Ameron Hawaii is a multinational manufacturer of highly-engineered products and materials for the chemical, industrial, energy, transportation, and infrastructure markets.

Ameron's customers include developers, contractors, shipbuilders, oil companies, and government agencies.

Its key market segments include the water and wastewater, wind energy, construction, and infrastructure sectors.

The company operates through its parent company's Distribution & Transmission segment. In 2013 the Distribution & Transmission segment's revenues increased by 30%, primarily due acquisitions, offset to a degree by lower North American drilling activity.

The company's operations are sensitive to downturns in core market segments including wind energy, construction, water, and infrastructure industries. Strong demand for onshore oilfield piping in recent years (especially in North and South America) has helped spur sales of its fiberglass products. However, sales of construction-related and wind tower products have been weak during certain periods due to lower demand.

Despite poor market conditions in some regions and sectors, Ameron is continuing to expand geographically and grow in areas that promise future increases in demand such as Brazil, China, and the Middle East. Each would serve the shipbuilding and onshore oil pipeline business.

At home in the US Ameron is looking to capitalize on the country's need to update its infrastructure. The company built a new steel pole plant in Oklahoma in 2012; the facility serves the US transportation market by constructing products such as light, signage, and traffic signal poles.

Ameron was acquired by oilfield equipment maker National Oilwell Varco for some $777 million in 2011.The deal was one of several acquisitions National Oilwell has initiated as it prepared for an expected boom in offshore drilling activities. Ameron's fiberglass pipes and other products expanded National Oilwell's offerings for the offshore drilling industry.

Ameron International was founded in 1907, and it merged with another company in 1929 to form American Concrete Pipe Co. The enterprise diversified into construction products in 1942 and became known as American Pipe and Construction Co.

By the 1960s American Pipe and Construction Co. had further diversified into protective coatings, ready-mix concrete, and resin piping. The company shortened its name to Ameron, Inc., in 1970, and over the next 20 years, it grew to have a worldwide presence.

Ameron adopted its current name in 1996 to reflect its international focus. That year the company bought Imperial Chemical's Devoe marine coatings unit in a move that helped it become one of the nation's leading providers of marine and offshore coatings. In a similar move, it bought the maintenance-coatings unit of Valspar Corporation in 1997, significantly increasing its product finishes business.

Bargain-basement gasoline prices and Asia's economic crisis hurt results in 1998, but the acquisition of Croda Coatings later in the year enabled the company to post a sales gain. The same year Ameron sold its 50% ownership in Gifford-Hill-American (concrete pipe). In 1999 the company established Bonstrand Ltd. (fiberglass pipe), a joint venture company in Kuwait. Bonstrand began operations in 2000. In 2001 Ameron International's Performance Coatings and Finishes unit inked a deal with Duron, the third-largest paint manufacturer in the US, to distribute Ameron's products using Duron's company stores.

In 2002 Ameron announced it would become PPG Industries' distributor of its PITT-CHAR XP fire protective coatings, primarily used in fireproofing off-shore oil rigs. The company sold its 25% stake of Amercoat Mexicana in 2003. In 2006 Ameron sold its protective coatings and finishes business to PPG .

In 2009 the company sold its pressurized gas systems business to electronics manufacturer AMETEK .
