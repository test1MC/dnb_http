If Ralph Waldo Emerson were writing today, he might put to paper, "Build a better valve or gasket, and the world will beat a path to your door." Greene, Tweed & Co. (Greene, Tweed) manufactures next generation technology seals, gaskets, fiber optic connectors, and custom engineered plastic components for use in aerospace and defense, medical and pharmaceutical, nuclear, and chemical industries. The company's products are also at the heart of the petrochemical, oilfield OEM, industrial hydraulics, semiconductor, and solar markets. Greene, Tweed's facilities are located in the Americas, Asia, and Europe. It answers its door to customers such as Airbus , Boeing , Bayer , and the DoD , among others.

Stationed in Kulpsville, Pennsylvania, Greene, Tweed has locations across more than 10 countries in the Americas, Asia, and Europe. Its manufacturing facilities reside in the US (Pennsylvania and Texas), Taiwan, Switzerland, and the UK. It also has technical centers in Japan, Switzerland, and the US.

The company operates through four segments. Its Aerospace segment collaborates with leading aircraft manufacturers to developed high-performance thermoplastic composite solutions and advanced processing techniques. Its Oilfield segment delivers solutions for critical oilfield customers. The company's Petrochem & Power segment serves leading pump and compressor manufacturers while its Semi & Solar segment serves the semiconductor and solar markets.

Customers are beating a path to Greene, Tweed to select products from the company's head-spinning lineup. It has developed bonding, coating, and encapsulation capabilities, under brand names including OpTegra, Enduro, ProTechna, and TechnaLoc, to make its products better able to withstand high temperatures, vibration, friction, and aerospace fluids.

Greene, Tweed caters to the aerospace, petrochemical and power, oilfield, semiconductor, and solar industries worldwide. In addition, the company serves the defense, medical, pharmaceutical and industrial markets.

The company began as a small distributor in New York City during the Civil War. Started by brothers J. Ashton and John W. Greene, along with partner Henry Tweed, the small company distributed wholesale hardware and mill supplies.
