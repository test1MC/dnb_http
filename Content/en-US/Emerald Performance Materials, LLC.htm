Not really what most people would consider green, Emerald Performance Materials makes additives, dyes, fine chemicals, silicones, and specialty polymers. The company operates through four business groups, including CVC Thermoset Specialties (epoxy resins), Kalama Chemical (ingredients for food and beverage preservatives, as well as pharmaceutical additives), polymer additives and nitriles, and specialties (including Carolina Chemical, Hilton Davis, and Foam Control). Emerald serves the consumer products industry, food and beverage companies, plastics and adhesives makers, and lubricants producers. Based in Ohio, it operates eight manufacturing facilities globally. Investment firm Sun Capital Partners owns Emerald.

Emerald Performance Materials' Specialties group makes colorants and defoamers for the food and personal care industries, as well as for use in coatings, graphic design materials, and fibers. Its three plants focus on colorants, food defoamers, and industrial defoamers, respectively.

The company has seven plants in the US and one in the Netherlands with an additional sales office in Hong Kong. It has been expanding its operations in Rotterdam and Washington. Emerald sells its products globally.

Emerald has been growing by expanding existing distribution agreements and adding production lines to its plants. The company also makes occasional strategic acquisitions, including the one in 2011 that added its Rotterdam operations.

Sun Capital formed Emerald Performance Materials from businesses the private investment group had bought from Lubrizol 's Noveon unit in 2006.
