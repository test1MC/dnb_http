Once a century-old textile maker, Leeds Group weaves together a web of businesses that import and sell fabric. Its primary trading arm, Hemmers Europe, based in Germany, imports, warehouses and wholesales cloth to European retailers and boutique wholesalers, for apparel and home furnishings applications. It sells about 14.5 million linear meters of fabric a year. Europe is by far its largest market, generating 95% of net sales. The group also holds a Shanghai trading business, Hemmers China. It is looking to further expand into the Asia/Pacific (2% of sales) and North America (3%).

From 2014 to 2015, Leeds' revenues climbed by 2% mainly due to 31% growth from Hemmers China. Throughout 2015, Hemmers China experienced a larger volume of deliveries to customers in the EU and, to a lower extent, customers in Australia.

The company has recently launched online sales systems in both Germany and China. In addition, plans are in place for an expansion of its in Nordhorn, Germany, facility.
