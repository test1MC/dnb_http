Oxford Instruments is a student of nanotechnology. The company develops analytical instruments and superconducting materials for analyzing and manipulating matter at the atomic and molecular level. Its products include spectroscopes and X-ray fluorescence devices, which customers use for chemical analysis and for deposition and etching processes during semiconductor, telecommunications, and optoelectronics manufacturing. The company also supplies superconducting materials and related equipment for chemical and biochemical analysis and scientific research.

Oxford Instruments gets more than 90% of its sales outside the UK.

The company's revenue increased in fiscal 2014 compared to the previous year. However, Oxford Instruments' net income decreased in fiscal 2014 compared to the prior period. Oxford Instruments' cash on hand also decreased in fiscal 2014 compared to fiscal 2013 levels.
