Showa Denko is one of Japan's leading producers of chemicals and industrial materials. The company's sectors include petrochemicals (olefins, organic chemicals, plastic products), aluminum (aluminum cans, sheets, ingots, foils), electronics (semiconductors, ceramic materials, hard disks), chemicals (industrial gases, ammonia, agrochemicals), and inorganic materials (ceramics, graphite electrodes). The petrochemicals segment is its largest division, accounting for more than a third of sales. Showa Denko has more than 160 subsidiaries and affiliates.

Showa Denko is stationed in Japan and performs its operations in China, Germany, Portugal, Korea, Shanghai, Taiwan, Indonesia, Thailand, Singapore, Philippines, Vietnam, Malaysia, the UK, and the US.

The company divides its operations across several divisions: petrochemicals, chemicals, inorganics, aluminum, and electronics. The Advanced Battery Materials Department makes lithium-ion battery components.

In 2013 Showa Denko acquired a controlling stake in Sinosteel Sichuan Carbod Co., a China-based maker of graphite electrodes. It renamed the operations Showa Denko Sichuan Carbon.

Showa Denko was formed in 1939 when Nihon Electrical Industries and Showa Fertilizers combined.
