Frontier Silicon has been a pioneer on the digital audio broadcast (DAB) frontier. Founded in 2001, the fabless company designs chips, DAB receiver modules, and platform software that its customers integrate into digital audio systems, Internet radio, and networked audio products. Frontier Silicon has added Internet radio operating system software and a wireless device remote control application to its software portfolio. Its customer base includes such global audio bigwigs as Bang & Olufsen , Bose , Denon , Harman , Philips , Pioneer , Sony , and TEAC . Frontier Silicon has offices in China, Hong Kong, Ireland, Japan, the UK, and the US. Frontier Silicon has been owned by wireless communications chip provider Toumaz Limited since 2012.

Frontier Silicon has collaborated with radio broadcasters, in addition to consumer electronics manufacturers, to continue to develop digital radio technology. The company, which claims to supply around 70% of the DAB market, works with a number of development partners that include Factum Electronics, Silicon & Software Systems, Fraunhofer Institut, IMI-Speedy, and Wongs Electronics Company.

In 2015, Frontier Silicon has signed a co-development agreement with Google . Google's Cast-for-Audio technology will be integrated into Frontier's next generation connected audio solutions. The results of the agreement should boost Frontier Silicon's connected audio business in 2016.

Frontier Silicon's digital radio module supporting the HD Radio standard has been on the market for two year. The company worked with iBiquity , the developer of HD Radio technology, to create the product based on its Verona digital radio module, of which more than six million units have been sold. With the new Frontier Silicon module, receiver manufacturers are able to sell US versions of best-selling digital radio devices, which to date have only been sold in Europe and the Asia Pacific region.

Frontier Silicon provides support to some 13,000 Internet radio stations through its online radio portal. It is also involved with organizations that develop international standards for digital broadcasting, including digital multimedia broadcasting (DMB), which enables TV and radio programming to be sent over the air to mobile devices.
