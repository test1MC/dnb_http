Advanced Circuits can turn on a dime - and things much smaller as a contract manufacturer of quick-turn printed circuit boards (PCBs) and prototypes. The company offers standard and custom manufacturing and will check board designs to make sure they can be manufactured efficiently. Engineering students can receive sponsorships for projects and low-cost prototypes. The company offers lead-free solder finishing on all prototype boards. Customers include NASA , Thermo Fisher Scientific , and Microfabrica . Advanced Circuits was founded in 1989.

Based in Aurora, Colorado, Advanced Circuits also has operations in Maple Grove, Minnesota and Tempe, Arizona.

Advanced Circuits says its gets about third of its new customers from referrals of current customers. The company gives incentives for referrals in the form of $25 gift cards to restaurants -- once the new customer has placed its first order. Customers of Advanced Circuits include Mann Electronic Solutions, Capital Products Design, and Sandia National Laboratories .

It's been several years since Advanced Circuits made an acquisition, but its has been active in the past in buying companies that extends its capabilities and market reach.

In 2012 Advanced Circuits acquired Universal Circuits (UCI), based near Minneapolis. With its Minnesota facility meeting certain Department of Defense clearance specifications, UCI has served military, aerospace, and medical equipment manufacturers through more than four decades.

In 2010 the company bought Circuit Express of Tempe, Arizona, for more than $15 million. Circuit Express also does much of its business in quick-turn PCB production, primarily for military/aerospace customers.
