Texas Instruments (UK) (formerly National Semiconductor (UK)) is a key part of its parent's international presence. The company, which is a subsidiary of chip maker Texas Instruments , began producing microchips in 1970. Through its wafer fabrication facility in Greenock, Scotland, it produces analog semiconductors, power management chips, LED drivers, and other devices used in mobile consumer electronics, medical imaging instruments, sensing and detection equipment, and communications gear. The United Kingdom the unit's second largest European market, after Germany.

The UK unit became part of TI when TI acquired National Semiconductor in 2011.

Texas Instruments (UK) is part of Europe, Middle East and Africa region of the parent company, which contributed about 17% of revenue in 2015.
