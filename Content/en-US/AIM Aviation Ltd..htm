AIM Aviation aims for high-altitude interior decorating. Formerly operating as AIM Group plc, the company develops, makes, and installs civilian and military aircraft interior components and equipment. Airline passengers can readily see its bar units, lavatories, sidewall panels, and video-control centers. They can feel its premium seat modules. Less visible (and tactile) offerings include ducts, panels, insulation, and tail cones. AIM also provides repair services. Customers have included Airbus , Boeing , British Airways , and Delta . AIM is backed by funds managed by TowerBrook Capital Partners, a leading international investment management firm, and Lloyds Development Capital, the private equity arm of Lloyds .

AIM maintains eight sites across UK and New Zealand. It sells its products throughout Asia/Pacific, the Middle East, and the Americas.

In addition to supplying commercial airlines, the company serves the military, marine and rail markets.

For military aircraft AIM provides stowages, workstations, doors, aviation racking, flooring, troop seating, and other features. AIM Aviation also serves the military market by providing radar panels, missile containers, refueling probe fairings, medical stretcher assemblies, and other military-specific applications for air- and land-based programs that include the Typhoon, C130, C17, Merlin Helicopter, and Foxhound armored vehicle.

In the marine market the company provides deck heads, sidewall panels, overhead bins, partitions, and other interior items with an emphasis on weight saving for such craft as fast ferries and super yachts. The rail industry is served with interior and exterior applications that include train and station toilets installations, access ramps, interdeck floors, and catering units.

AIM serves major airlines and OEM's including Boeing , Airbus , the UK Ministry of Defence, and regional aircraft manufacturers.

In 2014 the company opened its new manufacturing facility in Byfleet, Surrey, adding 31,000 square feet of production and testing facilities .In response to growing demand, that year, in tandem with the Manchester Airports Group , the owner of Bournemouth Airport, AIM announced plans for the construction of a new 160,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing unit at Bournemouth Airport's Aviation Business Park, which will provide AIM's Cabin Interiors Division with 30% additional capacity.

Enhancing the company's European, Middle Eastern, and North American operations, in 2014 AIM acquired New Zealand-based Altitude Aerospace Interior, from Air New Zealand, and changed its trading name to AIM Altitude.

In 2010 AIM was created to acquire the AIM Group plc's UK subsidiaries.

The subsidiaries that AIM acquired from AIM Group included Jecco, Henshalls, and AIM Composites. AIM Group PLC's US subsidiaries (AIM Aerospace, Inc., AIM Aerospace Auburn, Inc, and AIM Aerospace Sumner, Inc.) were not included in the sale and continue to operate as US companies.

Jecco (founded in 1926) serves such airlines as Emirates , Virgin Atlantic , Etihad , and Qatar, with aircraft interior features that include premium bars, lounge and cabin areas, and mood lighting systems. Henshalls provides galley systems for the Airbus A320 and A330 and Boeing B737 platforms of such airlines as China Southern , China Eastern , and easyJet . The Composites unit serves companies in aerospace and related markets, including its sister companies Jecco and Henshalls. The Composites unit's trademarked Chameleon Decorative Finish uses a water transfer process to apply up to 500 decorative finishes to interior components.
