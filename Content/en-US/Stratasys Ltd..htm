New product ideas can materialize before your very eyes, thanks to Stratasys. The company produces a range of 3D printers, materials, and production systems that are used by manufacturers to create models and prototypes as part of the new product design process. They are also used for production of finished goods in low-volume manufacturing. Stratasys additionally offers services, such as producing manufacturing tools for aerospace/defense applications and on-demand prototyping of production-grade parts. The current incarnation of Stratasys Ltd. formed in 2012 by the merger of 3D printing companies Stratasys, Inc. and Objet. The company maintains dual headquarters in Minneapolis and Rehovot, Israel.

Stratasys generates 72% of revenue from its products and the rest from services. Besides printers, products include the consumable materials used to make objects. The company's subsidiaries include MakerBot and SolidScape. MakerBot makes several models of desktop 3D printers for various markets. SolidScape makes non-toxic thermoplastic modeling materials.

Customers in the US supply 59% of revenue for Stratasys. Europe accounts for 21% of revenue with Latin America responsible for some 18%. The company makes few sales to the Asia market, which, combined with the "other" category, accounts for 2% of sales.

With a broad range of printers, Stratasys has a broad roster of clients that include large industrial companies to university research labs. Among its customers are Medtronic , Nestle , Volvo Construction Equipment , Logitech , and the DeRisi lab at the University of California, San Francisco .

After five years of virtually printing money with rising sales, Stratasys posted a 7% drop in 2015. Sales fell to $696 million for the year, compared to $750 million in 2014. Product sales plunged 18% in 2015, hurt by weaker sales of the MarkerBot line. Services revenue increased 39%.

Profit had risen with sales until 2013, when Stratsys reported a loss. Losses have deepened since then, sinking to a loss of $1.3 billion in 2015, following a loss of $119 million in 2014. Besides the revenue decrease, the company took a large impairment charge on technology assets in 2015.

The company's cash flow from operations hit a negative $22 million in 2015 after it generated about $14 million in 2014.

Stratasys made three acquisitions in 2014 to extend and expand its products and services.

With the acquisition of GrabCAD, Stratasys added collaboration tools and improved access related to 3D CAD content.

After buying Solid Concepts and Harvest Technologies, the company combined them with its RedEye business to establish one additive manufacturing services business unit.
