Knock, knock Who's there? Therma-Tru. Therma-Tru, who? A part of Fortune Brands Home & Security , that's who. Therma-Tru manufactures fiberglass and steel exterior doors for residential and light commercial use. The company's patio and entry door systems feature wood-grain that resembles mahogany and oak. Smooth-surfaced fiberglass doors that resemble steel are sold under the Smooth-Star brand. Therma-Tru also offers decorative glass and hinges. Its Tru-Defense doors are built to withstand damaging weather and other harsh conditions. All doors are made with polyurethane foam cores for increased insulation. Therma-Tru products are sold in the US and Canada by distributors, lumberyards, and home centers.

The company is stationed in Maumee, Ohio, and has manufacturing plants in Indiana and Mexico; it has a research center in Ederton, Ohio. It sells its products in the US and Canada.

Therma-Tru, along with Fypon and Simonton, are all brands comprising their parent's Door business segment, which accounts for more than 10% of the parent's total revenues.

The company focuses on developing products for customers, designers, installers and consumers. In 2014 sales to The Home Depot and Lowe's collectively accounted for 11% of net sales for the Door segment. The company sells its products through distributors, lumberyards, and home centers.

The company is a part of the Door segment of Fortune Brands Home & Security . The segment contributed 10% to the parent company's revenues in 2014.

In order to cope with the sluggish housing industry, Therma-Tru has been introducing new products and increasing the production of its contemporary and Craftsman style door glass packages. The company sees a trend of customers preferring more energy efficient and simplistic glass designs and plans to launch additional Craftsman style products and simplistic glass packages. Therma-Tru rolled out its new Stone and Timber brands by Fypon, catering to architects, builders, remodelers, and homeowners.

In 2015 the company introduced a variety of decorative new door glass options and door styles for the year. It also expanded its Villager and Homeward decorative glass products along with its Pulse Door Lines. In 2014 the company launched the DoorWays App for iPhone and iPad. It also introduced an online Education Center for builders, contractors, dealers, and fabricators that provides easy access to product information. That year it also expanded its Fiber-Classic MMahogany Collection which included a 2-Panel Square Top door, a 2-Panel Plank Soft Arch door, a 2-Panel Craftsman door, a Flush door and a Flush sidelite and also added Mahogany-grained fiberglass options to its popular Pulse series of contemporary entry doors.

Therma-Tru was founded by David Welles Sr. in 1962 and acquired by Fortune Brands in 2003. Fortune Brands spun-off Fortune Brands Home & Security in 2011.
