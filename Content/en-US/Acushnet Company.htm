Acushnet stays teed off all the time. The company is a top maker of golf balls, clubs, shoes, gloves, and other golfing equipment and accessories. Its Titleist golf balls and FootJoy golf shoes and gloves are #1 sellers in the US. Acushnet also makes value-priced Pinnacle golf balls, Titleist golf clubs, Scotty Cameron putters, and Vokey Design wedges, as well as golf bags and outerwear. Products are sold worldwide through golf pro shops, specialty sporting goods stores, and mass merchants. PGA players who have used Acushnet's equipment include Adam Scott, Davis Love III, Geoff Ogilvy, and Mark O'Meara. Global Fila brand owner Fila Korea bought Acushnet from spirits maker Beam Inc. (formerly Fortune Brands ) for $1.2 billion in mid-2011.

Fila Korea, which makes sport and leisure footwear and apparel and owns the global Fila brand, led the transaction alongside Mirae Asset Private Equity. With Acushnet under its umbrella, Fila Korea is looking to leverage the company's iconic golf brands for expansion throughout Asia.

From its headquarters in Fairhaven, Massachusetts, Acushnet has operations in Canada, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia. It covers the Eastern US through from its Massachusetts office and Western US from an office in California. It serves several countries, including the UK, Austria, Hungary, Slovenia, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, the Netherlands, Germany, Switzerland, the Czech Republic, France, Belgium, Luxemburg, Portugal, South Africa, Australasia, Australia, New Zealand, China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Japan, Korea, Thailand, Singapore, and Malaysia.

Being purchased by Fila Korea couldn't have been better for Acushnet. The company has been shifting its focus to foreign markets, particularly in the Asia/Pacific region, as the US recovered from the economic downturn. International markets account for about 45% of total revenue. Indeed, the US recession has had a stinging effect on the golf supplier, which pared down its operations in recent years amid plunging demand. To this end, Acushnet in 2010 sold its Cobra Golf business to PUMA , which viewed the golf category as having growth potential despite challenging economic factors. The deal included the Cobra Golf brand, its inventory, intellectual property, and endorsement contracts.

Product innovation and brand loyalty are the key components of Acushnet's success. The company relies on aggressive protection of its technology patents and effective marketing, often using endorsements of tour and club professionals.

It's speculated that the company is aiming to go public in 2016 in an effort to expand further. Fila Korea intends to maintain a 33% equity position afterward.

Acushnet sells its products globally through retailers, including golf pro shops, specialty sporting goods stores, and mass merchants. It also markets its popular portfolio of brand names through celebrity athlete sponsorships. PGA players who have used Acushnet's equipment include Adam Scott, Davis Love III, Geoff Ogilvy, and Mark O'Meara.
