When the coworkers or kids need some hand-washing help, Hillyard stands ready to clean up. The company manufactures and distributes a slew of cleaning solutions and custodial supplies. Its products range from carpet and gym-floor cleaners to toilet-bowl detergents and air fresheners. Hillyard also owns its own network of distributors; across the US and Canada, each distribution point offers a full line of supplies. The company serves educational institutions, government agencies, retail stores, and restaurant chains, among other markets. Its larger customers include manufacturing and consumer products giants, such as Georgia-Pacific , 3M , and Rubbermaid . Hillyard was established in 1907 by N. S. Hillyard.

Based in St. Joseph, Missouri, Hillyard's products are sold in the US and Canada.

Hillyard's products are sold nationwide by Hillyard Distributors and dealers the likes of Brady Industries, Morcon Industrial Specialty, Ashford Services, Abilene Maintenance Supplies, and CC Distributors.

It serves several markets, such as education, industrial, commercial, government agencies, retail, hospitality, healthcare, religious, services, contract cleaners, restaurants and clubs, recreation, and transportation.

Vertically-integrated Hillyard is one of North America's largest distributors of cleaning equipment and supplies. In addition to manufacturing and distributing cleaning products, the company offers training programs for maintenance staff. The company's Cleaning Cost Analysis Program (CCAP) identifies, defines, and measures labor/staffing costs, cleaning frequencies, standardization of tasks, and proper utilization of supplies and equipment for customers.

Spanning several generations of family ownership, the company continues to evolve with market demand. The rise in popularity and marketability of green cleaning has spurred Hillyard to expand its portfolio of products and affiliations. It partners with the green industry's Green Seal, The US Green Building Council, and The Healthy Schools Campaign.
