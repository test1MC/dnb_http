Alpine prefers to be on top of the electronics market. Alpine Electronics of America, a subsidiary of Japanese electronics manufacturer Alps Electric , makes, markets, and distributes mobile electronics equipment to customers in Canada and the US. The company produces amplifiers, subwoofers, speakers, monitors, CD and DVD players, rear seat entertainment systems, and security and navigation systems designed primarily for use in automobiles, but also in boats. The company's other products include headphones and adapters designed to connect Apple 's iPod products to car stereos. Alpine Electronics, which was formed in 1978, operates research and development facilities in Asia, Europe, and the US.

California-based Alpine Electronics has R&D and manufacturing facilities in Japan, China, Europe, and North America.

America accounted for 37% of its Japanese parent company's sales in fiscal 2014 (ended March), or about $9.8 billion.

Alpine's specialty is mobile electronics systems: a good fit for an increasingly mobile and wired world. To grow, the company is focusing on expanding sales in North America chiefly by selling more CD players, and sound systems that combine high-quality speakers and amplifiers. It 's also focusing on increasing the number of its products that feature built-in Bluetooth wireless technology, and expanding its lineup of SIRIUS XM -ready units.

The service its products, Alpines in 2013 established a subsidiary to repair and provide other after-sale services for Alpine's OEM car electronics products in North America.

In late 2014 the company moved out of the car and entered the crowded and trendy headphones market with Alpine Headphones that the company says deliver the "feel" of the music, not just the sound.
