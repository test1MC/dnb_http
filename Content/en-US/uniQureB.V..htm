uniQure is unique in that it has developed the first gene therapy with EU approval. Its main product, Glybera, received approval in 2012 and is being launched in collaboration with Italian pharma firm Chiesi Farmaceutici. The drug treats lipoprotein lipase deficiency, a rare fatal disease, by restoring a missing protein in a single dose. The company's pipeline includes four candidates for other orphan (rare or neglected) diseases including hemophilia B and Sanfilippo B; it intends to focus on orphans but may eventually branch out. uniQure was formed in 1998 and went public, raising $92 billion, in early 2014. It's using the funds on US operations.
