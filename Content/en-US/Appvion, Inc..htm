Appvion (formerly Appleton Papers) has an app for that -- paper. The company manufactures specialty coated paper products, including carbonless and security papers, and thermal papers. It is the world's #1 producer of carbonless paper. Sold under the NCR Paper brand, carbonless paper is used in multi-part business forms (such as invoices). Appvion makes security papers for government documents and coated products for point-of-sale displays. Its thermal papers are used in coupons, gaming and transportation tickets, and medical charts. Its Encapsys segment makes microencapsulation (a microscopic wall around a substance) materials. Appvion is the primary operating subsidiary of Paperweight Development Corp.

Appvion (from "applied innovation") operates through 10 distribution facilities in Appleton, Wisconsin; Camp Hill, Pennsylvania; Monroe, Ohio; Kansas City, Kansas; Ontario, California; McDonough, Georgia; and Peterborough, Ontario, Canada. Third-party logistics services are contracted through distribution facilities located in Portland, Oregon; the Netherlands, and the UK.

Over the years, Appvion has realigned its business to focus on coating formulations and applications (carbonless papers) and security technologies, thermal papers, and a segment that makes microencapsulation materials, called Encapsys. The carbonless papers segment generates 42% of the company's total sales, while thermal papers makes 50%. Encapsys accounts for the remaining amount.

The company's papers are sold to merchant distributors that stock and redistribute those products worldwide. They are also sold to printers and converters that cut, print, or further process the paper for different end uses.

Appvion's largest customer accounted for 8% of its total sales in 2014. Each of the company's three segments depends upon a large concentration of customers for their revenues: five customers accounted for 41% of the entire revenue in the carbonless papers segment, while 41% of thermal papers' revenue was generated from five of its largest customers for 2014 as well. The largest customer in the Encapsys segment generated 73% of that segment's total 2014 revenues.

In 2014 the company's revenues remained flat to a positive 0.3% change, due to an increase in the sales from the Encapsys segment resulting from higher sales volume and a favorable product mix; offset by a decrease in other segments.

That year Appvion reported a net loss of $92.8 million (compared to an income of $17.3 million in 2013) due to higher costs, and increase in selling, general, and administrative expenses resulting from a mark-to-market loss and related to its retiree benefit plans. The company also recorded legal expenses resulting from Fox River settlement matters and anti-dumping and countervailing duties claims.

In 2014 operating cash flows increased to a cash inflow of $47.2 million (from a cash outflow of $22.7 million a year earlier), due to increased cash inflows from accounts receivable, accounts payable, and accrued pension; offset by higher cash outflows in inventories and a major net loss.

The company is focused on product development along with enhancement of its current portfolio of products, using its existing technical and manufacturing capabilities and targeted at new markets and customers. It also looks to improve the productivity and cost structure of its manufacturing operations and boost the efficiency of its support services.

In 2015 Appvion added a 20 lb. bond to its lineup of treated high-speed inkjet papers, expanding its portfolio of specialty papers, one that includes market-leading products in the carbonless, thermal and security segments.

In 2014 the company launched AlphaÂ® Free, its first thermal receipt paper made using vitamin C, in place of traditional phenol-based thermal developers like BPA, BPS or controversial phenol substitutes. That year it also added a treated high-speed inkjet paper to its portfolio of specialty papers. ideal for transactional documents such as bills and statements, direct mail, promotional materials, and catalogs and books.

To meet the demand for PVC digital printing substrates, in 2014 the company expanded its product offering of quality specialty substrates with the launch of Appleton Digital PVC.

In 2013 the company changed its name from "Appleton," the site of its first plant) to the more dynamic "Appvion."

Appvion is owned by Paperweight Development Company, which is 100% owned by Appvion employees.

The company got its start as Appleton Coated Paper Company in 1907. In mid-2012 Appvion agreed to be acquired by Hicks Acquisition Company II, a blank check company formed in 2010 to make acquisitions, in a deal valued at $675 million. However, the deal was called off months later, citing unfavorable market conditions.
