Hannibal sought to conquer Rome on the back of an elephant. With more modest aims, Hannibal Industries would just like conquer a niche market by selling steel tubing, primarily in the western US. Hannibal Industries manufactures a variety of welded steel tubing. The employee-owned company offers tubing in round, rectangular, square, and other shapes. Hannibal Industries produces more than 100,000 tons annually from its six high frequency tube mill lines. The company supplies its products to Steel Service Centers and OEMs. Its Hannibal Material Handling division manufactures pallet and cantilever racks.

Hannibal Industries has operations in Oregon, Southern California, Washington, and in Western Canada.

Hannibal Industries manufactures steel automotive tube on state-of-the-art equipment with the ability to create special finishes and chemistries to allow for tight radius bends and extreme flaring.

The company's storage and rack products includes Structural Pallet Rack, Roll-Formed Selective Pallet Rack, Hybrid Systems, Cantilever Systems, Pushback Systems, Pallet Flow Systems, Case Flow Systems, and Drive-In Systems. Its engineering and design services include system design, seismic engineering, permit administration, in-house installation, and custom fabrication.

Among other products, Hannibal Industries provides exhaust tubing in custom specifications for automotive, motorcycle, and truck stacks. It also manufactures steel products for a range of other industries including agriculture, construction, furniture, material handling, medical, and recreation.

The company's diverse product line enables it to provide turnkey storage systems to its various clients.

In 2015 the company added new facility in Houston as part of its Southern expansion strategy.

In 2014 Hannibal Industries teamed up with Twinlode formed a strategic partnership to provide material handling services to major multinational bottling companies.

In 2013 the company reported that global demand for its patent pending TubeRack product had allowed it to move into the material handling markets in Saudi Arabia and Thailand. To keep up with this new demand, the company ramped up TubeRack production at its Los Angeles steel mills.

Updating it truck fleet, in 2013 the company purchased six cleaner burning diesel tractors as a part of an initiative to reduce vehicle emissions.

Growing its product portfolio, in 2012 Hannibal Industries launched a new line of custom automotive OEM exhaust tube capabilities, providing exhaust tubing that is flash controlled, cut-to-length with chamfered ends, and with a bright finish.
