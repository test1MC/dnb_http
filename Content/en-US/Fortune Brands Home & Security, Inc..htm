Formerly a unit of Fortune Brands (now Beam Suntory ) Fortune Brands Home & Security (FBHS) holds the keys to the kitchen and the garden shed. With about 45 plants, the consumer products manufacturer makes and sells kitchen and bathroom cabinets, faucets, entry doors, trim, and padlocks. Its well-known brands include Moen faucets, MasterBrand Cabinets , SentrySafe, and Therma-Tru entry doors, along with Master Lock , American Lock, and Waterloo Industries padlocks and other security products. Most of the company's products are the top sellers in their respective markets.

Illinois-based Fortune Brands Home & Security (FBHS) rang up almost 85% of its sales in the US in 2015. Canada is the company's largest international market, representing about 10% of sales. China and other countries account for the rest. Other major markets for FBHS include Europe, Southeast Asia, South America, and Mexico. The company operates 31 US manufacturing facilities in 17 states and has 13 international plant locations (seven in Mexico, three in Asia, and three in Canada).

The company operates four business segments. The largest, Cabinets (47% of sales), ranks as the #1 maker of such cabinetry in North America. FBHS's Plumbing and Accessories business (31% of sales) manufactures Moen, Cleveland Faucet Group, and Waste King brand faucets, the leading brands in North America and China. The firm's Doors unit (10% of sales) is a leading maker of doors and vinyl windows, while its Security business (12% of sales) is the #1 maker of padlocks in North America and Europe.

The company's two largest customers are Home Depot and Lowe's Companies , together accounting for about 30% of annual sales. The company sells directly through its own sales force and indirectly through independent manufacturers' representatives. Other sales channels include kitchen and bath dealers, wholesalers catering to builders or professional remodelers, industrial distributors, and other retail outlets, including mass merchants. Sales to all US home centers in the aggregate were approximately 30% of net sales in 2015.

FBHS' end market included repair & remodel, the largest market accounted for 49% of sales followed by new construction 23%, international 15%, security 9% and commercial 4%.

Advertising costs amounted to $195.4 million, $200.4 million, and $197.1 million in 2015, 2014, and 2013, respectively

The improving US home products market, market share gains, growth overseas, and acquisitions have collectively driven FBHS's sales up in recent years. Indeed, since 2011 the company's net sales grew at a compounded annual rate of around 12% till 2015 with the exception of 2014, when the company reported 4% decline due to the disposition of its Simonton Windows business.

In 2015 FBHS' net revenue increased by 14% due to higher sales from all segments.

Cabinets segment revenues increased by 22%, mainly due to additional revenues from an acquired company Norcraft Companies (Norcraft, a manufacturer of kitchen and bathroom cabinetry); higher sales volume (including the impact of new product introductions); a favorable mix; and an increase in prices.

There was also a 15% increase in Security segment, and 6% each in the Plumbing and Doors segments.

The US market grew by 18% due to increases in new home construction and repair and remodel activities as a result of the acquisitions of Norcraft (Norcraft) in 2015, and John D. Brush & Co., Inc. (SentrySafe) and Anaheim Manufacturing Company (Anaheim) in 2014, higher sales volume primarily resulting from U.S. home products market growth, price increases. The growth was partially offset by 5% decline in Canada.

In 2015 FBHS' net income increased by 99% due to higher revenues and income from discontinued operations as a result of the sale of Waterloo tool storage business in 2015, compared to a loss from discontinued operations in 2014.

Operating cash flow increased by 62% in 2015.

FBHS is focused on expanding internationally. Its Moen faucet brand is expanding in China, India, and South America, while Kitchen Craft is growing its brand in Canada. Master Lock continues to expand in Europe and Asia Pacific as well as Therma-Tru's has made inroads in Canada. In the US, the company is building its Cabinets business through acquisitions.

In 2015, Moen opened a manufacturing facility in China as part of its continued international expansion.

FBHS is also focused on expanding its product portfolio. Its Norcraft Companies expands regional market presence and enhances frameless cabinetry. In 2015, MasterBrand Cabinets launched new cabinet door designs, color palettes and features in a range of styles; exclusive laminate door & finish options. Its Moen brand introduces hand showers featuring the Magnetix magnetic docking system, a new line of garbage disposals, Spot Resist finish, touchless Motionsense electronic faucets and pull-out and pull-down faucets with Reflex self-retraction. Its Therma-Tru released a portfolio of on-trend door and glass collections.

To raise cash to pay down debt, in 2014 FBHS sold Simonton to Ply Gem Holdings for $130 million. In 2015, it also sold Waterloo Industries, its tool storage business for $14 million.

Growing its portfolio, in 2015 the company acquired Minnesota-based Norcraft for $649 million to increase regional market penetration and enhances its frameless capabilities.

in 2014 the company's Master Lock division acquired SentrySafe, a Rochester, New York-based maker of personal safes (with estimated annual sales of $150 million), for about $118 million.

In 2014 FBHS bought Anaheim Manufacturing, which markets and sells garbage disposals, for $30.6 million

Formed in 1988 as a Fortune Brands subsidiary, the company was spun off in 2011.

In 2013 FBHS' Kitchen & Bath Cabinetry business acquired WoodCrafters , a manufacturer of bathroom vanities and tops, for about $302 million. The purchase expanded the company's bathroom cabinetry products offering.
