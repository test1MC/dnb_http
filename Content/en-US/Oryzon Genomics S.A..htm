Oryzon Genomics is a clinical stage biopharmaceutical company with a focus on developing experimental therapies for cancer and neurodegenerative diseases. The firm is a specialist in epigenetics, which is the study of changes caused by modification of gene expression instead of alteration of the genetic code. Its lead program, ORY-1001, is a selective LSD1 inhibitor which is being studied for the treatment of acute myeloid leukemia. Another program under development is ORY-2001, which is being studied for its ability to treat neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. Oryzon went public in late 2015.

Oryzon raised some €96.5 million in its initial public offering. It plans to use funds raised to further develop its product pipeline.

Oryzon has locations in Barcelona and in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

In fiscal 2015, Oryzon revenues totaled €4.3 million, a 68% drop from the previous year. It netted a modest loss that year, a drop from 2014 when it netted more than €6 million.

Oryzon focuses on developing first-in-class compounds through mid-stage clinical trials, after which it decides whether to continue development itself or outlicense those activities.
