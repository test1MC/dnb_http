STOP! That familiar sign that prompts motorists to brake at intersections was the first transportation management item made by Econolite Control Products. The company now manufactures and distributes much more sophisticated traffic control equipment, including loop traffic control systems, vehicle detection systems, traffic control cabinets, and signal display equipment. Econolite boasts about 5,000 arterial systems in operation, controlling 100,000-plus intersections, and more than 90,000 wide-area video vehicle detection systems set at intersections and on freeways. Econolite began in 1933 when company founders developed a stop sign with a built-in flashing red light, controlled by a bi-metal sun valve.

Based in California, Econolite serves customers internationally, with operations in Africa, Asia, Australia, Canada, the Caribbean, Europe, Mexico, the Middle East, and South America.

To boost its revenue, Econolite leverages its portfolio of traffic flow and safety products and provides training for the effective use of its products. Distribution agreements with other developers of transportation systems have enabled Econolite to expand its lineup, as well as its customer base. For example, the company serves as an exclusive development partner and distributor of Image Sensing Systems ' autoscope products.

In 2012 Econolite inked a channel partner agreement with Traffic Systems and Technology (TS&T) of Manassas, Virginia. As part of the alliance, TS&T represents Econolite's products in northern Virginia, Maryland, and Washington, DC, in an effort to extend the company's reach into the mid-Atlantic region.
