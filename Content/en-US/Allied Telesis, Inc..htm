Allied Telesis makes Ethernet networks appear out of thin air. The US subsidiary of Japan-based Allied Telesis Group, it sells and services a wide range of IP networking equipment made by its parent company. Products include media converters, multi-access platforms, network interface cards, routers, and switches. It also offers network software and wireless equipment. Allied Telesis serves customers in the education, enterprise, government, healthcare, and retail markets, among others. Customers have included Agilent , Stanford University , and the US Department of Homeland Security . Allied Telesis counts General Bandwidth , and Kasenna among its strategic partners. The company also provides R&D services for its parent organization.

The company's major alliances and associations include IP Triple Play Alliance, IBM , and PSA Security Network. The company sells through distributors including Anixter , Graybar , Ingram Micro , Synnex , and Tech Data .

As it seeks additional growth areas, Allied Telesis is targeting the education and federal government markets as well, while extending its range of services to include network diagnostics and design. The company is also pursuing new relationships with systems integrators to widen its distribution network.

In 2016, the company added a security measure for fiber optic cable to its portfolio. Its Active Fiber Monitoring technology can detect when a cable is being tampered with, and will raise an alarm to warn of a possible security breach.

The company's IE510 Series of Gigabit stackable switches was released in late 2015 to handle increasing amounts of data being transmitted. The series of products serve as core or distribution switches for industrial applications. Paired with the company's IE200 Series, IE510 switches can connect devices CCTV cameras, industrial automation systems, and building management systems.

In 2013 Allied Telesis partnered with the Potawatomi Business Development Corporation (PBDC) to offer its integrated infrastructure solutions to enterprises seeking to migrate their IT infrastructure and applications to remote data centers and to the cloud. Allied Telesis will soon release its new 'Data-Center-in-a-Box' solution, which will combine robust network, storage, and compute capabilities in a single, compact footprint. The solution will enable any size of IT organization to afford the modern infrastructure required to deliver next-generation applications needed to drive a competitive advantage.
