Physio-Control makes an integrated line of noninvasive emergency cardiac defibrillators and vital-sign assessment devices, as well as data management software. The company's LIFEPAK branded products are used both in and outside hospitals for the early detection and treatment of life-threatening events including trauma, heart attack, and the acute heart rhythm disturbances of bradycardia (slow heartbeat), tachycardia (rapid heartbeat), and ventricular fibrillation (chaotic heartbeat). Its LUCAS chest compression device is used to assist with CPR duing cardiac arrest. In 2016 Physio-Control was acquired by Stryker Corporation for $1.28 billion.

The company was owned by private equity firm Bain Capital until 2016, when it was sold to medical equipment supplier Stryker Corporation for $1.28 billion.

While Physio-Control's primary products are its external defibrillation devices, the company also offers medical assistance, equipment leasing, and liability protection, as well as data management software, technical support, and training services.

Physio-Control is a leader in the US emergency medical services (EMS) market, holding a 70% market share; it also holds about 50% of the US in-hospital market. The company sells its products in over 100 countries and is a global leader in the external defibrillation market.

Physio-Control's main customers are hospitals, schools, governments, businesses, and other public facilities, including paramedic and firefighting organizations. Selected customers for the company have included Bochumer EMS, CEGA Air Ambulance, Geisinger Valley Medical Center , RasGas , Skippack EMS, and Holy Spirit Health System .

Physio-Control developed the external defibrillator technology in 1961 and began manufacturing portable, battery-powered defibrillators in the late 1960s; since then the company has sold some 650,000 models.
