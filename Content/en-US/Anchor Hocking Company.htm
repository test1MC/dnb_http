The Anchor Hocking name is nearly synonymous with glassware. In existence since 1905, the company is the nation's second-largest supplier of glassware. It makes and markets beverageware, cookware, serveware, storage items, and giftware. With US manufacturing plants located in the Ohio Valley, the company also serves as a third-party manufacturer for other companies. Anchor Hocking's products are sold through catalogs and US retailers, such as Wal-Mart and Target , as well as directly to foodservice and specialty markets. Private equity firm Monomoy Capital Partners, which focuses on middle-market investments, owns and runs Anchor Hocking as an operating subsidiary of EveryWare Global .

Global glassware marketer Anchor Hocking makes its products at company facilities in the US. It operates manufacturing plants in Ohio and Pennsylvania, with specialty glass, distribution, and customer service functions conducted at both sites.

Anchor Hocking is an operating subsidiary of EveryWare Global , formed in 2013 under parent Monomoy to steer the complementary Anchor Hocking and newly acquired Oneida businesses.

Anchor Hocking markets its glassware across a variety of price points through multiple channels, including retail, business-to-business, and hospitality. Anchoronline.com is the company's online presence.

Anchor Hocking relies on several US retailers to get its products in the hands of consumers. They include Wal-Mart , Target , Kmart , Crate and Barrel , Ace Hardware , True Value , Kohl's , Joann's, Michaels , Hobby Lobby , Fred Meyer , Meijer , Publix , Sears , JCPenney , Old Time Pottery , and Bed, Bath & Beyond . In Canada, Anchor Hocking sells its products through Canadian Tire , Wal-Mart Canada , Zellers , Loblaw , Home Outfitters, and London Drugs .

While the company makes most of its revenue through its retail efforts, Anchor Hocking distributes glass products for the foodservice industry through licensing agreements. It has extended its reach into foodservice glassware through its partnership with Germany's Stölzle, a manufacturer of crystalline stemware and barware for hotels and restaurants. As part of the agreement, Anchor Hocking markets the Stölzle line of foodservice glassware in North America from its Ohio location. It also provides World Kitchen, maker of the Corelle and Chicago Cutlery product lines, with distribution services in the US, Canada, and Mexico through an agreement that became effective in 2011.

Anchor Hocking is banking on extending its portfolio of products. Thanks to Monomoy's purchase of flatware and dinnerware supplier Oneida , the glassware giant aims to gain market share in the tabletop niche alongside its sister company as the two leaders work together to reposition their lines for other parts of consumers' homes.

Monomoy bought Anchor Hocking for $75 million. The 2007 deal, which included Monomoy's assumption of some $20 million in ordinary course liabilities, plucked Anchor Hocking from Global Home Products LLC , which had filed for bankruptcy protection in 2006. Global Home bought Anchor Hocking from Newell Rubbermaid in 2004 for $310 million.
