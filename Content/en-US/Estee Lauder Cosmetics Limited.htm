Estée Lauder's reach to the UK some 40 years ago has benefited even the most striking European beauties. Estée Lauder Cosmetics develops about 15 brands of makeup, skin care, and fragrance products, such as Estée, Beautiful, Darphin, White Linen, and Knowing. Its operations span major and niche cosmetics markets in the UK, Estée Lauder's second largest market. Since the 1960s, Estée Lauder has had a presence in department stores throughout the UK and Ireland. The company's distribution operations are based in Hampshire, where it leases a property with more than 200,000 sq. ft. Estée Lauder's products are sold through several stores, including Boots the Chemists , Harrods , Selfridges , Pure Beauty Store, Debenhams , and House of Fraser .

The company operates through department stores in the UK and Ireland selling its parent's wares. It manufactures and distributes from its own UK facilities.

Estée Lauder Cosmetics owns its 225,000-sq.-ft. manufacturing facility in Petersfield and leases its distribution building in Hampshire that spans 203,000 sq. ft. The facilities serve all of the UK and Ireland. The UK subsidiary represents a core market for parent Estée Lauder along with the US, France, Italy, and Japan.

The company operates in its parent's Europe, the Middle East & Africa segment, which contributed 38% of the total revenue in 2015. Segment sales decreased 2%, or $77.3 million, to $4.1 billion, driven by approximately $285 million of unfavorable foreign currency translation due to the strength of the U.S. dollar. However the parent company says that sales grew double digits before the currency conversion.

The company has been reaching out to smaller cities and looking for interesting ways to market its products like participating in art and pop-up events while looking for ways to more directly reach consumers.
