As Mexico's leading chicken and chicken egg producer, Industrias Bachoco doesn't care if the chicken or the egg came first. The company, founded in 1952, operates poultry production and distribution facilities primarily throughout Mexico. Bachoco breeds, processes, and markets chicken (accounting for about 90% of its 2013 sales). It also produces and distributes eggs, swine, and animal feed and offers turkey and selected value-added beef products. (It raises no turkey or cattle, it only processes them.) Altogether, it boasts more than a thousand facilities across nine production complexes (including one in the US) and 64 distribution centers. The Robinson Bours family owns a 73% interest in the company.

In 2005 the company acquired Grupo Sanjor, a Yucatan Peninsula poultry company. It acquired Grupo Libra, which produces broilers at its northeastern Mexico operation the following year. The following year, the company acquired Del Mezquital, another broiler producer in 2007. Del Mezquital is located in the Mexican state of Senora, which is in northern Mexico, close to the US border.
