OMRON is a proponent for components. The company's industrial automation division focuses on electronic controls such as sensors, timers, relays, counters, and power supplies. Its electronics components division offers trigger switches, laser radars, counterfeit detectors, and components for amusements. OMRON'S social systems and healthcare units produce airline check-in equipment, ATMs, credit card authorization terminals, cash dispensers, ticket vending machines, traffic control systems, digital blood pressure and body fat monitors, hearing aids, and thermometers. OMRON was established in 1933 and officially incorporated in 1948.

OMRON's largest market remains Japan, which brings in 46% of net sales. Other major markets include China (19%), the Americas (14%), Europe (13%), and other countries in Southeast Asia (8%).

OMRON divides its operations across several divisions: Industrial Automation (35% of net sales), Electronic and Mechanical Components (17%), Automotive Electronic Components (15%), Social Systems Solution and Service (10%), Healthcare (10%), and Other (13%).

From 2013 to 2014, OMRON's revenues increased 19% due to significant growth across all its segments. Specifically, Automotive Electronic Components grew by 30%, while Industrial Automation increased by 11%. Other segments achieving growth for 2014 included Electronic and Mechanical Components (14%), Healthcare (25%), and Other (36%).

OMRON is focused on extending its reach into emerging markets. It is intent on selling its automation products in China and its industrial automation, health care, and automation electronics products across Asia and South America. In 2012 it opened a new head office in the north Indian city of Gurgaon as a springboard for selling its wide array of products in India.
