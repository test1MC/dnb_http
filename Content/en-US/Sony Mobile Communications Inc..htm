Sony Mobile Communications makes and markets the Xperia line of smartphones and tablets based on Google 's Android mobile operating system and related services and applications. The wireless products are sold throughout the world. Xperia users can have access to video, streaming music, and play games from Sony's PlayStation Mobile. The company sell several models of phones which include the Z and X series. Formerly known as Sony Ericsson Mobile Communications, Sony bought out Ericsson's 50% stake in the joint venture for about â‚¬1 billion in 2012, at which point the business was renamed Sony Mobile Communications.

Sony Mobile has research and development operations in China, Japan, Sweden, and the US (in California).

Xperia is sold in Europe, the Middle East, Africa, the Americas, and Asia Pacific. It is eyeing emerging markets as a source for growth over already crowded markets such as the US. There, its only relationship is with carrier T-Mobile . However, the Sony Mobile has established an e-commerce platform in eight countries in Europe as well as the US.

Sony Mobile generated $3.2 billion in sales for Sony, which resulted in profit of just more than $200 million in profit in 2016 (year ended March). Mobile sales dropped 15% in 2016 from 2015, but profit rose 133%. The company sold fewer units in 2016, but spent less on research and development and sales and marketing.

Sony Mobile is squared off against such global mobile phone stalwarts as Apple , HTC , and Samsung . To help boost its profile in what is now the world's largest smartphone market, the company expanded its partnership with Chinese retailer Suning to increase its presence across China, where it offers jointly developed phones tailored for consumers there.
