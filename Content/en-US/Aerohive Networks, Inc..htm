Aerohive Network will not stop until everyone is deploying wireless mobile-centric networks. Aerohive's 802.11n and 802.11ac enterprise wireless LAN access products are included in a suite of wireless infrastructure equipment with cooperative control technology that provides the benefits of controller-based wireless LAN technology (without requiring a controller or an overlay network). Aerohive has historically targeted distributed enterprises and the K-12 and higher education sectors as customers, who hold licenses to its products in addition to software subscriptions and services.

The Americas account for 65% of the company's total sales, while the EMEA region accounts for nearly 25%. Customers in the APAC region account for the remainder.

In March 2014 the company launched an IPO and raised $75 million in proceeds, which it intends to use for general corporate purposes, including working capital, sales and marketing activities, research and development activities, general and administrative initiatives, and capital expenditures.

Aerohive sells its products through a network of certified resellers and distributors to 13,100 customers in a wide variety of industry verticals. It has typically focused on distributed enterprises, K-12, and higher education. Within distributed enterprises, it has a market-specific focus on the health care and retail industries as well as and state and local government agencies. It also serves the manufacturing, utilities, transportation, finance, and other professional services.
