Gerber Products Company doesn't strain to make money. It does, however, strain to make its food products. Gerber's more than 200 baby offerings include the ubiquitous glass jars of baby food. The company, which had its beginnings in 1927 in Dorothy Gerber's kitchen, also makes baby formula, cereal, juices, and Gerber Graduates toddler food, along with baby bath and skin care products, bottles, teethers, pacifiers, breastfeeding accessories, spill-proof cups, and infant toys. Its Gerber Life Insurance Company offers various whole- and term-life products. As part of its focus, Gerber works to feed, soothe, and amuse babies worldwide. Global food behemoth Nestlé owns the company.

The world's largest food company, Nestle, acquired Gerber from Novartis for more than $5 billion in cash. Besides bringing into its fold one of the most enduring brand names, the move made Nestlé the largest baby-food manufacturer and supplier worldwide.

Based in New Jersey, Gerber sells its products in more than 80 countries and labeled in 16 languages.

The baby food maker's marketing campaign, The Gerber Generation, works to promote a healthier generation of babies. Online retailers, such as Amazon.com, Diapers.com, and Target, sell its products.

The company's life insurance business insures babies, their parents, and their grandparents in the US, Puerto Rico, and Canada.

Gerber is part of the Nestle Nutrition segment, which contributed 11% of fiscal year 2013 net revenue, has seen both organic and internal growth. The segment's infant nutrition business -- via its Wyeth Nutrition acquisition -- drove sales, particularly in infant formula and infant cereals.

Focused on early childhood nutrition, Gerber sells the Good Start brand of infant formula. The first of its kind, the line is a partially hydrolyzed 100% whey protein routine formula for infants.

Gerber is known for its glass baby-food jars, which have become part of American life. But while the food maker wants to hold onto its heritage, it opted to transition many of its products to plastic containers, citing cost savings. Indeed, the company provides its baby food varieties in plastic containers and is the only major baby-food company to do so. In response, consumers have been largely positive.

To cater to those who want to make their own baby foods, Gerber sells organic baby food under brand names Organic 1st Foods and Tender Harvest.
