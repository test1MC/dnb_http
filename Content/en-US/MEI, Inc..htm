There's plenty of cash flow at Crane Payment Innovations CPI). The subsidiary of Crane Co. makes bill and coin acceptors, coin changers, and control boards that operate those and other cash handling machines. Its machines are used around the world in retail, gaming, financial services, transportation, and vending. Uses include self-checkout and kiosks to add minutes to a cell phone contract. Customers include Dairy Queen , Dunkin Donuts and Gold Country Casio and Hotel. CPI's brands include Cashcode, Currenza, Conlux, Money Control, and Telequip. CPI was created when Crane combined Crane's Payment Solutions and merchandising systems businesses with MEI Conlux Holding Group, which Crane acquired. CPI systems handle more than 2 billion transactions a week.

Crane acquired MEI in 2013 for $804 million. The acquisition was Crane's fourth payment-related acquisition in the last 10 years and the biggest deal in its history.

CPI's machines take and process cash and cashless payments and the company also has products for back-office cash handling. Coin payments are handled with coin dispensers, coin acceptors, coin hoppers, and other types of coin validators. For bill payment, the company integrates security validation technology in its bill acceptors and bill recyclers. The machines are used for casino gaming, quick-sale vending, on-street parking, and other applications. PCI also integrates its machines into payment and processing systems such as PayLink systems.

CPI is based in Malvern, Pennsylvania and has offices in Canada, Germany, and the UK. Its products are in use in 120 countries.

Among CPI's customers are Borgata Hotel, Casino and Spa in the US, Inversiones Doral in Peru, Jamp s.r.o. in the Czech Republic, and K + H Automatendienst GmbH in Germany.

Crane Co.'s payment and merchandising technology sales rose 87% in 2014 from 2013. The sales received a big boost from the MEI acquisition and were also helped with strong sales at Merchandising Systems.
