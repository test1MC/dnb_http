On the banks of the Kymijoki, you'll find the Myllykoski plant, and a paper trail that goes back more than 100 years. Founded in 1892, Myllykoski Paper makes supercalendered and coated paper and lightweight coated paper, used in magazines, catalogs, advertising inserts, and brochures. The company and subsidiary Rhein Papier collectively operate 7 paper mills and produce some 3 million tons of paper annually; more than 95% is exported to Europe and the US (through subsidiary Madison International Sales). The paper maker was owned by holding company Myllykoski Oyj (65%) and Finnish paper producer M-real (35%). In mid-2011, rival UPM-Kymmene took over the business in a deal valued at €900 million ($1.3 billion).
