Mercian wants you to drink up. The company makes and distributes alcoholic beverages, including wine, sake, mirin, and shochu (a liquor distilled from sweet potatoes or rice). Its best known label is Chateau Mercian. The firm also makes livestock feed from the by-products of alcohol production. To focus on its beverage business, Mercian has spun off its pharmaceutical and chemical businesses. Mercian is a wholly owned subsidiary of Kirin Holdings , which acquired the remainder of Mercian's shares in 2010 following a scandal in which the winemaker padded earnings by booking fictitious transactions within its feedstuffs division. Mercian later sold its fish feed unit to Tokai Seapro.
