X-Rite has an eye for color. Its products help manufacturers, retailers, distributors, printers, and graphic designers achieve a consistent color appearance. Using spectrophotometers and colorimeters, its color measurement products check that fabrics, paints, and plastics are the correct shade. Its color standards product line uses densitometers to measure optical and photographic density and control color for processing textiles, film, and inks. Products include paint matching systems for retailers, sensitometers for manipulating photographic film exposure, and color formulation software for PCs. X-Rite has been a subsidiary of Danaher since the 2012 acquisition.

X-Rite operates as a standalone company within Danaher's product identification group, and maintains its own leadership and branding. Access to new markets and complementary technologies enables the company to expand; X-Rite also benefits from Danaher's global presence, scale, and resources.

In addition to the US, the company has offices and subsidiaries in China, the Czech Republic, France, Germany, India, Italy, Japan, Russia, South Korea, Spain, Switzerland, and the UK. It generates the majority of its sales outside the US.

X-Rite markets its products across a wide range of products and industries, including automotive, food, health and beauty, medical, paint and coatings, plastics, and video and displays. It sells through its owns sales force as well as independent distributors.

Financial Performance

X-Rite and its subsidiaries are part of Danaher's product identification division. The division supplied 16% of Danaher's revenue in 2015.

The company focuses on new product development and organic growth, as well as partnerships and acquisitions.

In 2016 X-Rite and its Pantone subsidiary Pantone partnered with SmallHD to bundle and support the X-Rite OEM i1Display Pro to manage color calibration for a daylight-viewable, full-HD display. The models are 1703-HDR, 2403-HDR, and 3203-HDR.

That followed the 2015 release of the Judge AC light booth, which was designed to help industrial and commercial printers better simulate colors under a range of lighting conditions.
