Has workplace pressure made you feel like exploding? Control Components Inc. (CCI) designs, makes, and services corrosion-resistant valves and related components. They're used in power plants (solar, fossil, nuclear), pulp and paper facilities, by oil and gas companies, and for combined heat and power (CHP) applications. Its products include safety and pressure relief valves, fluid and gas control valves, and ventilation components. The company boasts that its trademark DRAG technology cuts downtime related to valve failures on supercritical boilers. CCI operates manufacturing and service facilities in North America, Europe, and Asia. The company is a subsidiary of valve and metal components maker IMI .

CCI is located in California and has manufacturing and sales locations in 18 countries spanning the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, Asia, and Australia. It has representative offices in 60 countries.

Under IMI's direction, the company serves as the operational hub for power plant certification and approval, as well as for assembly and test services. Through the IMI Severe Service division, CCI provides custom-engineered valve, actuation, and control products for a variety of critical in-plant processes.

CCI, which boasts a diverse portfolio of 70-plus service technologies, has installed 100,000 severe service valves worldwide.

CCI counts Archer Daniels Midland , Alstom Power , and Electricite de France among its customers. It sells its products through distributors and sales representatives worldwide.

It serves several sectors, such as fossil power, nuclear, oil and gas production and transportation, and renewables.

The IMI Severe Service division, which CCI is a part of, contributed 41% of IMI's total revenue in 2013. The division generated about 20% of its revenue from its business in North America.

Founded in 1961 by Richard Self, CCI became a key business of UK-based IMI in 1981.
