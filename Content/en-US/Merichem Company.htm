Merichem gives companies in the upstream, midstream, and downstream sectors of the energy industry (and other process industries) a way to clean up their act. Boasting more than 1,000 licensed process units worldwide, Merichem is the leader in licensing patented process technologies and providing proprietary equipment for hydrocarbon treatment and related management services that remove sulfur and other impurities from hydrocarbon liquids and gases during exploration, development, production, refining, and other process activities. The company also produces phenol and cresylic acids at its plant in Texas and naphthenic acids (used in paint driers and wood preservatives) at another in Alabama.

The company's US manufacturing operations are in Alabama, Illinois, and Texas; international operations include sales offices in China and Japan.

It addition to its Refining, Oil and Gas Production clients, Merichem also serves customers in major industries including Chemicals; Food and Beverage; Industrial Gases; Metals and Mining; Petrochemicals; Power Generation; and Water and Wastewater.

Merichem Process Technologies licenses its technologies to customers for whom it then designs, procures, and fabricates equipment to remove hydrogen sulfide, mercaptans, naphthenic acids, carbon dioxide, and other impurities from light and middle distillate hydrocarbons in refineries.

Merichem Gas Technologies provides hydrogen sulfide removal systems for a wide range of operations. It either serves as the contractor to provide design, procurement, fabrication and installation of equipment, or sells a license, engineering, and equipment only.

Merichem Caustic Services has two business lines: naphthenics and caustics. It provides environmentally responsible caustic recycling services for the petroleum and petrochemical industries. Merichem Caustic Services also produces catalysts used by Merichem Gas Technologies and Merichem Process Technologies.

Expanding globally, in 2012 the company registered new trademarks in China and India. That year it also launched a single vessel MERICON III unit for caustic neutralization and environmental control in Turkey.

The company was formed in 1945.
