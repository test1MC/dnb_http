Florida Crystals leans heavily on its cane. One of the top US sugar producers, owner Fanjul Corp. company processes some 3 million tons of sugar a year. It farms sugar cane on some 68,000 acres and operates two sugar mills, a rice mill, a sugar refinery, a packaging and distribution center, as well as a renewable power plant. Its products include certified organic, granulated, powdered, and brown sugars, and agave nectar, which are sold under the C&H, Florida Crystals, Jack Frost, and Redpath brands. With the Sugar Cane Growers Cooperative of Florida , Florida Crystals owns American Sugar Refining , the world's largest care sugar refiner and holder of the Domino and Tate & Lyle sugar brands.

Along with its subsidiaries, the company has operations in the US (California, Florida, Louisiana, Maryland, and New York), the Caribbean, Europe, the Middle East, India, and Asia. An expert in raw sugar procurement, the company's reach spans more than 40 countries.

Florida Crystals' renewable energy facility (fueled by biomass) is the largest of its kind in North America and provides clean energy that powers the company's sugar production operations (as well as tens of thousands of homes), which helps the firm reduce its use of fossil fuels.

The company's sugar processing yields 374,000 tons of raw sugar and 18 million gallons of blackstrap molasses a year.

Florida Crystals boasts a bulging portfolio of brands: Domino, C&H, Redpath, Tate & Lyle, Wee-Cal, and namesake Florida Crystals.

The company, which sells its products through retailers and online, is expanding its distribution into local grocery and natural food stores, industrial and pharmaceutical channels across the US. Florida Crystals makes the world's first carbon-free certified sugar.

Florida Crystals relies on several retailers to get its products in the hands of consumers. It sells through Kroger , Sprouts , Farm Fresh, Earth Fare, Whole Foods , Foodlion, Tunie's, Navarro Drug , Ocean State, and Wal-Mart , among others.

Florida Crystals' growth is fueled in large part by American Sugar Refining 's Domino Foods, which drives sales, marketing, and logistics for its owners' sugar products. Domino distributes to customers in the industrial, grocery, foodservice, and specialty and export markets.

In 2014, the company launched a real estate division, through which it bought a Channel District property in downtown Tampa for $3.8 million and plans to build 270 luxury apartments on the former Amazon Hose and Rubber site.

Domino has enjoyed a supply agreement with China's Sunwin International Neutraceuticals, a major supplier of stevia extracts, through an agreement inked in 2011. Domino and its affiliated companies use stevia extracts in making low calorie sweeteners, such as Domino Lite, as well as market the extracts to food and beverage industry customers in North America and Europe.

Florida Crystals and the Dante B. Fascell Port of Miami also teamed up in late 2011 to develop the South Florida Intermodal Logistics Center on 850 acres owned by the sugar company. The center serves as a distribution facility, where freight from South Florida ports can be transported by road and rail, and stored and routed. It will also expand Florida Crystals' distribution capacity nationwide.
