Paragon Technologies produces automated order picking systems and other order fulfilling products used by manufacturing, assembly, and order distribution customers. Also known by its major brand name SI Systems, the company supplies customers with horizontal transportation and conveyor systems, related computer software, and other products and services used for improving productivity. Customers are located primarily in the US and have included Caterpillar , engine giant Cummins , General Motors , and contact lens manufacturer Vistakon (a subsidiary of Johnson & Johnson ). The company was founded in 1958.

The company has its headquarters in Easton, Pennsylvania, and has customers located throughout North America. It also targets Canada, Latin America, Asia, and Europe.

Paragon's equipment and systems are sold primarily through a nationwide network of authorized material handling integrators and dealers and also direct to major OEM accounts. Its automated material handling systems are marketed, designed, sold, installed, and serviced by its own staff or subcontractors.

Paragon's balance sheet has fluctuated over the years. After declining sharply in 2014, Paragon's revenues soared 73% to $11.6 million in 2015. After suffering net losses from 2012 to 2014, the company posted positive net income of almost $1 million in 2015.

In order to grow, the company is focused on building a strong distributor base and bringing additional hardware and software technologies to the integrator market. It also plans to continue to increase and invest in its sales and marketing efforts.
