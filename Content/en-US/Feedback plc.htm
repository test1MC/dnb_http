Feedback has gained a new image in medical imaging. With the acquisitions of Cambridge Computed Imaging (CCI) and TexRad, the company has refocused from technical training tools to provide medical practitioners and researchers with medical imaging tools. Its products are used by research institutions and medical centers around the world. Some 70% of Feedback's sales are to customers in the UK. To make way for the imaging business, Feedback sold interest in some of its previous pursuits.

In 2014, Feeback bought CCI and TexRad and shifted to medical imaging as it core business.

The CadRan product from CCI allows clinicians to display, review and manipulate images using in-line measurement and analysis tools to assist diagnosis and quantify treatment response.

TexRad instruments provide for stratification of clinical diagnostic images for prognosis, risk, and treatment. TexRAD analyzes the textures in radiological scans and has the potential to assist clinicians in assessing the prognosis of patients with cancer. It has been used retrospectively on a wide range of conditions including colorectal, breast, lung, prostrate, esophageal, head and neck, and renal cancers.

About 70% of Feedback's sales are in the UK with Europe accounting for 20%, and the rest of the world, 10%.

Institutions where Feedback products are in use include Evangelische Lungenklinik Berlin Hopsital in Germany, Velindre Cancer Centre in Cardiff, University of Tokyo Department of Radiology at the Institute of Medical Science in Japan, CHU de Reims in France, Seoul National University Bundang Hospital in South Korea, and Peking University Medical College Hospital in Beijing.

In its second year as a medical imaging company, Feedback's sales soared. Revenue jumped more than 5,000% to about £390,000 in 2016 (ended May) from £7,250. Feedback posted a loss of £1.1 million in 2016, compared to a net loss of £600,00 in 2015. The company had higher operating costs in 2016.

With the CCI and TexRad acquisitions in 2014, Feedback moved into medical imaging. It had provided electronic and electrical equipment, much of which is designed for training applications at educational institutions and companies. In 2016, Feedback sold its 50% equity interest in Stone Checker Software to Free Association Books for £46,000.
