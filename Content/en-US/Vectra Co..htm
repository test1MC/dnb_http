Vectra (formerly the OM Group) has two specialty technology divisions. Vacuumschelze is a leading global manufacturer of advanced magnetic materials and related products. It serves markets that include battery materials, semiconductors, ceramics, chemical, and defense. It also makes magnetic technologies products for electronic equipment, auto, and alternative energy markets. EaglePicher Technologies makes up its battery technologies segment, which serves the defense, aerospace, and medical markets. The company was acquired by Apollo Global Management in 2015. In 2017 Vectra sold its Borchers specialty chemical additives division to affiliates of The Jordan Company, L.P.

The company operates two businesses: Vacuumschelze and EaglePicher Technologies.

Vacuumschelze is a top global manufacturer of advanced magnetic materials and related products. It develops, makes, and distributes high-performance, industrial-use magnetic materials, and related products and systems with exceptional magnetic and/or physical properties.

EaglePicher Technologies provides integrated power solutions (batteries and related devices) for defense, aerospace, commercial, and medical customers.

Vectra has operations in North America, Europe, and Asia/Pacific, with the majority of its operations located outside of the US.

The company supplies a wide range of global markets, including aerospace and defense, automotive, industrial equipment, and medical.

Vectra has followed a strategy of growth through continued product innovation and strategic acquisitions.

In 2016 Eagle Picher won a contract to make batteries for Orbital ATK 's first space commercial servicing vehicle for its Mission Extension Vehicle program.

That year the European X-Ray Free Electron Laser (the brightest X-ray source in the world) was under construction in Hamburg. Vacuumschelze is supplying magnets and pole pieces for undulators related to the project.

As part of its strategy to exit its Advanced Materials business to focus on its core assets, in 2013 OM Group sold the downstream portion of the business, including its cobalt refinery assets in Kokkola, Finland, for $435 million.

The company made another tactical acquisition with the 2011 purchase of VAC Holding of Germany (Vacuumschelze), a manufacturer of advanced materials and specialty magnetics, for about $996 million. VAC is a global leader in several segments, including chemicals and metal-based materials; the deal gave OM Group a significant boost in the alternative energy market.
