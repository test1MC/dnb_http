No, she won't teach you how to dance the merengue, but she will bake you some French cookies. Miss Meringue produces meringues (an airy confection made with flour, eggs, sugar and egg whites) in Mini, Classique, and Chocolette versions. They are sold in grocery stores and national retailers throughout the US, as well as via the company's Web site. Miss Meringue's other prepackaged cookies include coconut macaroons and, that other traditional French treat ( tres bien !), Madeleines -- in both vanilla and chocolate flavors. The company was founded in 1997 by chef Jacques Pautrat and co-founder Roland D'Abel de Libran.
