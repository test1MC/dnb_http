United Microelectronics Corporation (UMC) found its calling in the silicon foundry business. Behind only neighbor and archrival Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing , UMC is a leading silicon foundry, or contract semiconductor manufacturer. Its production services include design, engineering, manufacturing, packaging, sorting, and testing. UMC operates through offices in Taiwan, Japan, Korea, China, Europe, the US, and Singapore, and continues to add capacity and advanced production technologies. Taiwan is its largest market, generating 33% of net sales. Other major markets include Singapore (24%), the US (12%), and China (9%).

Founded in 1980, United Microelectronics Corporation (UMC) was one of the first companies involved in Taiwan's high-tech renaissance. The company, which originally designed and produced its own integrated circuits (ICs), was a spinoff of Taiwan's government-backed Industrial Technology Research Institute (ITRI). Robert Tsao, a founder of the company, became its president in 1981; he served as chairman from 1991 to 2006.
