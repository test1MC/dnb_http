A top US poultry processor, Wayne Farms is too busy to cross the road. The company processes more than 2.5 billion pounds of chicken every year. It produces fresh, frozen, boneless, bone-in, and fully-cooked whole chickens and chicken parts. A vertically-integrated company, the subsidiary of Continental Grain Company operates hatcheries, feed mills, and grow-out farms, as well as processing facilities, all in the Southeastern US. Its Wayne Farms brand is sold to customers in the retail food, food-manufacturing, and food service sectors in the US and abroad. Its Dutch Quality House and Platinum Harvest brands specialize in further processing chicken products for the restaurant industry.

The Georgia-based company operates nationwide, selling its poultry products globally.

Wayne Farms boasts about a dozen production facilities across Alabama, Arkansas, Georgia, Mississippi, and North Carolina.

In addition to the US, the company markets its chicken in American Samoa, Armenia, Bahrain, China, Cuba, Curacao, Guam, Iraq, Korea, the Marshall Islands, Mexico, New Caledonia, Okinawa, Oman, Palau, Russia, Saipan, Singapore, Tahiti, and the United Arab Emirates.

Wayne Farms supplies poultry products to food service, industrial, and retail customers. About 10% of its production is exported.

The poultry processor builds its business and boosts its overall capacity through partnerships with others. Through a joint venture with Salm Partners inked in late 2013, Wayne Farms makes chicken sausage products. Also in 2013, the company invested $3 million to renovate and expand its processing operations in Laurel, Mississippi.

Hardly chicken feed, Wayne Farms has annual sales of more than $1.9 billion.

Adding to its holdings, Wayne Farms in 2012 acquired an idled poultry feed mill in Atkins, Arkansas, to use as a grain storage facility. It also purchased a Dothan, Alabama, poultry complex that added a processing facility, hatchery, and feed mill to its US footprint.

Headquartered in Oakwood, Georgia, Wayne Farms' operations date back to 1895, when its was known as Allied Mills and produced commercial animal feed.
