Products from STIHL Incorporated create a lot of buzz. The company, which is the US arm of Germany-based STIHL International, makes chain saws, edgers, leaf blowers, saw chains, trimmers, and other landscaping tools and components. STIHL Incorporated markets its products, which are protected by more than 1,000 patents, through a dozen corporate distribution centers and independent wholesalers. Its tools are sold by some 8,000 independent tool and hardware retailers (you won't find them at big box home stores) throughout the US and exported to more than 90 countries. The US-based marketing and manufacturing unit was established in 1974.

Outside of the US, the company has operations in Africa, Asia, Australia, Europe, the Middle East, and South America.

STIHL makes 260-plus variations of handheld outdoor power equipment, selling them in the US and internationally.

STIHL, which sells its products through independent service dealers, is seeing rising demand for electric lawn and garden equipment as consumers shift away from traditional gas-powered products. Electric equipment is generally perceived as more environmentally friendly and typically carries a smaller price tag than its gas-driven counterparts. To service this growing consumer group, STIHL is working to lower the emissions of its gas-powered gear while adding more corded electric products to its lineup.

To boost its revenues, the company has been broadening its base of retail customers and expanding its product lines. Fellow outdoor equipment maker John Deere offers STIHL products through its network of more than 500 stores.

It regularly rolls out about a dozen new products each year. Its STIHL FS 94 R professional trimmer debuted in 2014. In 2013, it introduced Dorocut mowing heads with string cutting, the M-Tronic version of the MS 362 and MS 261 chain saws, a new backpack sprayer, and the new GS 461 STIHL Rock Boss to its industrial lineup, among other products.
