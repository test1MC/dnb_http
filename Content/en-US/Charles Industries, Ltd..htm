Charles Industries is in charge -- of a variety of charging and surge protection products. Charles Industries manufactures telecommunications products, industrial chargers, and charger components, such as transformers, power inverters, and capacitors. Its telecommunications group produces enclosures to protect and support migration to wireless technologies and has product lines to support telecom and utilities operations. The marine and industrial group makes marine battery chargers, power cables and adapters, power management systems and other related products.

Charles has four facilities in Illinois and one in Missouri.

The divides its operations across three product categories: industrial, telecommunications, and marine.

Charles targets the telecommunications, utility, government, marine, and industrial markets. The company's distributors include Anixter , Graybar , KGP Logistics , and Power & Tel .

Charles often enhances its product portfolio through acquisitions. In 2012 the company acquired the molded high density polyethylene (HDPE) below grade enclosure product line from NewBasis, a company based in California. Charles' acquisition of the line complemented its existing enclosures and expanded its applications for current telecommunications, cable, and broadband customers. In addition, it allowed Charles to introduce new products and accelerate growth in its newest markets: wireless, utility, and vertical markets.

Charles was established in 1968 in the basement of the Charles family home.
