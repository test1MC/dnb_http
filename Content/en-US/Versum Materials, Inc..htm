Versum Materials makes the chemicals and components that go into semiconductors, displays, LED technology, and micro-electro-mechanical systems (MEMS). It operates through two segments: Materials; and Delivery Systems and Services (DS&S). Materials, its largest segment, makes specialty chemicals, materials, and gases used to make semiconductors and used in the semiconductor manufacturing process. DS&S makes, installs, and operates chemical and gas delivery and distribution systems to deliver its gases and chemicals directly to its customers' manufacturing tools.

Versum divides its operations into two segments: Materials (74%) and Delivery Systems and Services (DS&S; 26%).

Versum operates through roughly 35 facilities in the US, Europe, Korea, Taiwan, Singapore, Japan, and China. The Asia/Pacific is the company's largest market, representing about 61% of its net sales. Other major markets include the Americas (30%) and Europe (9%).

About 80% of Versum's customers stem from the semiconductor industry, the majority of which represent large global companies.

The company traces its historical roots back to the 1960s, when it was a part of Air Products , an expert in process intermediate chemicals. It was spun off from Air Products in mid-2016.
