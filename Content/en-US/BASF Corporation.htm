BASF Corporation, the North American subsidiary of the world's largest chemicals company, BASF SE , makes nearly every chemical under the sun. Like its German parent, it manufactures ingredients for several markets: chemicals (inorganics, petrochemicals, and intermediate products), performance products (paper, personal care, and pharma chemicals), functional solutions (catalysts, industrial coatings, and construction chemicals), and agricultural (fungicides, herbicides, and insecticides). In 2013 it opened a $33 million facility expansion in Research Triangle Park, North Carolina. BASF Corporation is responsible for about a fifth of BASF SE's total sales.

BASF Corporation has more than 100 production and research and development sites across North America. In US the company has manufacturing plants in Decatur, Alabama; Orange, California; Atlanta, Georgia; Fenton and Neosho, Missouri; Orville, Ohio; Carrollton, Texas; and Memphis, Arkansas. In Canada it has plants in Abbotsford, Blackie, Cornwall, Georgetown, and Toronto. It's North American site includes Geismar, Louisiana; Freeport, Texas; Port Arthur, Texas; Wyandotte, Michigan; Tarrytown, New York; Iselin, New Jersey; Research Triangle Park, North Carolina; Altamira, Mexico; and Windsor, Canada. It also has locations in Costa Rica, Puerto Rico, Mexico, the Dominican Republic, and Panama.

The company operates in four business segments: Chemicals, Performance Products, Functional Materials and Solutions, and Agricultural Solutions.

The Chemicals segment includes petrochemicals, monomers, and intermediates. Its portfolio ranges from solvents, plasticizers, glues and electronic chemicals to starting materials for detergents, plastics, textile fibers, paints and coatings, as well as for crop protection products and pharmaceuticals.

Performance Products includes Dispersions and Pigments, Care Chemicals, Nutrition and Health, Paper Chemicals, and Performance Chemicals. Its portfolio ranges from vitamins and food additives to ingredients for pharmaceuticals and for hygiene, home and personal care items. Other products in this segment improve processes in the paper industry, oil and gas production, mining and water treatment.

Functional Materials and Solutions includes Catalysts, Construction Chemicals, Coatings, and Performance Materials for the automotive, chemical, and construction industries. Its portfolio comprises automotive and industrial catalysts, battery materials, automotive and industrial coatings, and concrete admixtures as well as construction products such as tile adhesives and decorative paints.

Agricultural Solutions include Crop Protection products which guard against fungal diseases, insects and weeds, increase the quality of agricultural products and secure crop yields.

BASF Corporation produces plastics, including polyurethanes, and styrenics and engineering plastics; and coatings, such as automotive OEM coatings, automotive refinishes, and industrial coatings, as well as associated membranes. Although diversified, basic chemicals are BASF Corporation's core business. The company operates one of the world's largest naptha steam crackers (for making propylene and ethylene) in Port Arthur, Texas, in a joint venture with Total Petrochemicals . In North America, BASF is one of the leading producers of butanediol and its derivatives, which are used to produce engineering plastics, polyurethanes, solvents, electronic chemicals, and elastic fibers.

BASF Corporation serves customers in a range of industries, including Agriculture, Automotive, Chemicals, Coatings, Construction, Health and Beauty, and Packaging.

In 2015, BASF SE 's technology competencies were clustered into three main global platforms, each headquartered in a different strategically chosen global region, Advanced Materials & Systems Research in Asia (Shanghai); Process Research & Chemical Engineering in Europe (Ludwigshafen); and Biosciences in North America (Research Triangle Park, North Carolina).

In 2016, BASF collaborated with TopBuild Home Services' Environments For Living program to develop the new 'BEYOND' program, which allows builders to offer homeowners the extended benefit of a ten-year limited heating & cooling energy use and comfort guarantee.

In 2014 the company formed a strategic partnership with Heritage Plastics to bring ecovio biopolymers (compostable polymers) manufacturing to North America to enable BASF to begin making ecovio biopolymers outside of Europe and better serve in the rapidly developing North American market.

In 2014 (and for the next five years) BASF planned to invest more than $5 billion in North America, 25% of its global capital expenditure over the period. By 2020, 25% of BASF's research activities will be conducted in North America.

In 2016 the company bought Albemarle 's Chemetall Surface Treatment business for $3.2 billion.

In 2012 the company moved to become a global leader in biological seed treatment when it acquired Iowa-based Becker Underwood for more than $1 billion. Becker Underwood is a major provider of technologies for biological seed treatment and seed treatment colors and polymers, as well as products in the areas of biological crop protection, landscape colorants and coatings, turf and horticulture, and animal nutrition.

In 2011, production of methyalmines started up at BASF's chemical complex in Geismar, Lousiana. The facility produces chemical intermediates that are further processed into a range of products, including petrochemicals, solvents, plastics, pharmaceuticals, and computer circuits.

That year the US operations of specialty chemicals supplier Cognis Corporation were transferred to BASF Corporation, following Cognis's acquisition by the parent company at the end of 2010.

BASF Corporation is a subsidiary of BASF SE. BASF set up its first office in the US in 1873.
