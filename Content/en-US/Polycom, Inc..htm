Polycom's vision is a world united by video. The company makes video-conferencing and immersive telepresence (which combines digital audio, video, and content sharing) systems that let users collaborate as if they were in the same room. Its products combine camera, microphone, network connection, and external audio and video devices. The company also offers PC-based phones that transmit both voice and video. Polycom's software enables users to manage conferencing locations and connect with them using ISDN and Internet protocol connections. It partners with the likes of Microsoft and IBM to develop open standards-based products. Polycom was sold to Siris Capital Group for $1.8 billion in 2016.

Siris Capital bought Polycom, taking the company private. It ceased trading as a public company in September 2016. Polycom spurned previous $1.8 billion offer from Mitel . Upon conclusion of the deal, Siris installed a new CEO at Polycom.

The company divides its products into three segments - unified communications (UC) Group Systems, the UC Platform, and UC Personal Devices. UC Group Systems consists of its immersive telepresence, group video, and group voice systems products. It accounts for about 65% of sales. The UC Platform includes servers, virtualization management, recording and streaming, and remote access technologies that constitute its RealPresence Platform. UC Platform products account for almost 20% of sales. Finally, personal devices, which include desktop video devices and voice products, make up the remaining 15% of sales.

Polycom does not manufacture its own products, but instead relies on contract manufacturers in low-cost countries to keep costs down. Celestica , which has facilities in China and Thailand, makes the majority of its products. Polycom also uses Flextronics in Mexico, and Askey Computer Corporation, Foxconn , and VTech in China.

Polycom has more than 75 offices in about 30 countries. About half of its sales come from North and South America. Asia/Pacific and the EMEA region each account for 25% of sales. International sales made up 58% of revenue in 2014.

The company counts more than 415,000 customers around the world. Its products are sold through a network of strategic, service provider, and channel partners. Distributor ScanSource accounts for almost 17% of sales. Other wholesale partners include Anixter , AT&T , BT , Digital China , and Dimension Data .

Sales for Polycom were down 2% in 2014, the third straight year of declining revenue. One reason for the drop was that Cisco Systems stopped reselling a product it had bought from Polycom. A reduction in operating costs enabled the company to net income of $42 million in 2014 after a net loss of $18 million in 2013. Cash flow from operations increased to $205.4 million in 2014 from $168.4 million in 2013.

Polycom reached agreements with Microsoft to extend its relationship with Microsoft Lync and provide video services for Microsoft's Skype. For Lync, Polycom introduced its CX5100 unified conference station for 1080p HD video and a 360 degree of all participants in a room. In the Skype-related deal, Microsoft and Polycom extended Skype for Business collaboration to small and midsized businesses with the Polycom Roundtable Service. It includes voice, video, and content collaboration.

Polycom put acquisitions on hold in 2014. In 2013 it bought Sentri, a managed services provider for Microsoft Lync. The acquisition will help it deliver advanced services for multi-vendor unified communications that offer video, voice, data, and networking.
