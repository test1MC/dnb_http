Dynamic Homes makes and markets modular homes in a dynamic fashion. Over 90% of the homebuilder's manufactured single-family homes are custom built, and are sold primarily in the upper Midwestern US through a network of authorized local builder/dealers. Dynamic Homes constructs about 500 homes a year that range in size from 560 sq. ft. cabins to 2,380 sq. ft. two-story custom homes. Custom options include appliances, floor coverings, lighting, roofing, and siding. The builder also makes modular apartments, nursing homes and senior living facilities, motels/resorts, small office buildings, and large commercial complexes. Founded in 1970, Detroit Lakes, Minnesota-based Dynamic is owned by Ho-Chunk, Inc.

Dynamic Homes builds mostly custom-built homes from a range of styles, including rambler, split-entry, split-level, and two-story homes, as well as Cape Cod homes, chalets, cabins, and cottages. Its auxiliary products include garages, wood basements, and retail products.

As of early 2016, the majority of the homebuilder's revenue came from residential modular home sales, while the rest came from commercial and auxiliary product sales.

The builder's properties are marketed through authorized independent builders in Minnesota, North Dakota, South Dakota, Wisconsin, Iowa, Nebraska, and in Canada.

Through its investment company Ho-Chunk Inc. , the Winnebago tribe of Nebraska purchased Dynamic Homes and took it private.
