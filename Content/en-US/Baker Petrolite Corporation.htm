You can't just dig a hole and expect the oil to come right out. Baker Petrolite's products, petrochemicals, and additives, play a big part in getting all that crude out of the ground. A business unit of oilfield equipment maker Baker Hughes , Baker Petrolite manufactures specialty chemicals for oil and natural gas production -- fluid stimulants for oil drillers, as well as hydrate, corrosion, paraffin, and scale inhibitors. (The range of inhibitors are designed to reduce drag in pipelines.) It also makes additives for refineries, herbicides for farmers, and polymers for everyone from candle makers to the toner and ink industries.

Baker Petrolite provides oil/water separation technology and in solutions to control corrosion, deposition, bacteria and hydrogen sulfide in producing wells and production facilities. It makes oil and gas production products (reservoir souring controllers, wax and asphaltene control agents, foamers, scale and corrosion controllers, emulsion breakers, and water clarifiers); protection chemicals (aquatic herbicides); and polymers for a wide range of end uses.

The company's polymers anti-blocking agents are used for a wide variety of applications, including control of surface tackiness of injection, rotational, and blow molded parts; and easy separation between layers of stacked sheets and wound film rolls; and non-clumping and free flow of pellets during packaging, shipping, and transfer.

The company serves customers in the hydrocarbon recovery and processing, pipeline transportation, refining, petrochemical, aquatic herbicides, and polymers industries.

There are no separate financials for Baker Petrolite, which operates as a part of the parent company's North America segment. However, parent Baker Hughes reported that North American revenues were flat in 2013 despite rig counts declining 7% (primarily due to lower gas prices driving down onshore rig activity) as increased revenues from stronger Gulf of Mexico drilling and completion fluids, pressure pumping, completion systems, and wireline services product lines compensated for the decline in onshore drilling. The North American segment contributed about 49% of the parent company's total revenues that year.

The company, formerly known as Baker Performance Chemicals, was incorporated in 1930.
