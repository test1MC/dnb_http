Ergo, Ergotron puts ergonomic mounts on display -- or maybe it's the other way around. The company is a designer, manufacturer and marketer of innovative, ergonomic mounting and mobility products for computer monitors, notebooks and flat panels. Ergotron makes mounting hardware that enables computer displays, laptops, and TVs to be affixed to desks and walls. It also offers computer carts and ergonomic accessories such as wrist rests, extension arms, and mouse holders. Its products are sold under the brands Neo-Flex, OmniMount, StyleView, TeachWell, and WorkFit. Ergotron is a subsidiary of global diversified company, Nortek .

The company sells its products in Asia, Europe, and North America, and has a manufacturing plant in China.

Ergotron operates under Nortek 's Ergonomic and Productivity Solutions segment, which also includes OmniMount. The segment accounts for about 11.5% of its parent's total sales.

Retailers include Amazon.com , Best Buy , ePlus , Newegg , and Office Depot . Reseller partners include Dell , Hewlett-Packard , and Workspace Solutions. The company has sales teams in Phoenix, Amersfoort, London, Tokyo, and Singapore.

Sales for the Ergonomic and Productivity Solutions business were $294.4 million in 2014, up from $274 million in 2014. Sales of Ergotron-branded products rose in 2014, driven by volume increases in ergonomic sit-stand, device management, and healthcare carts.

As manufacturers slowed production of personal computers, Ergotron shifted toward products for mobile devices. In 2014 it introduced Dock Locker, which physically secures Microsoft Surface Pro 3 devices and Surface Pro 3 docking stations and protects them from damage and theft. In 2014, it released the Samsung Solutions Exchange, which has syncing and storage capabilities for the Samsung Tab Active tablet.

Nortek, through Ergotron, acquired Anthro Corporation, which designs and makes technology furniture products, for about $50 million in 2015. Anthro's products include charging carts (for electronics, including tablets, laptops and other mobile devices) and height adjustable desks and technology carts.

Established in 1982, Ergotron was bought by Nortek for $280 million in 2010.
