At one time, Taiwan Tobacco & Liquor Corporation (TTL) was the only company that could make authentic Taiwanese beer. The former monopoly, which was founded in 1947, transformed into a corporate entity in line with Taiwan's joining the World Trade Organization (WTO) in 2001. TTL sells beer and wine (including sake) under the Taiwan Beer and Shaohsing brands respectively and also sells Kaoliang brand spirits. The company operates three breweries and nine distilleries and sells its products in more than 40,000 outlets throughout the country. TTL's cigarette brands include Long Life, New Paradise, Formosa, Gentle, and 520. The company is owned by the Taiwanese government.
