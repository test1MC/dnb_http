When your home is your castle, it deserves the Royal treatment. Royal Group is a leading North American maker of PVC and vinyl building and home improvement products such as window profiles, fencing and decking; it also produces vinyl siding. Its Royal Mouldings division makes decorative moldings and millwork. Royal Pipe Systems makes plastic pipe and fittings for municipal, plumbing, and electrical construction uses. Other divisions include Royal Window & Door Profiles and Royal Building Products. Royal is a subsidiary of plastics and chemicals maker Axiall Corporation (formerly Georgla Gulf Corporation).

The company has its headquarters in Woodbridge, Canada and manufacturing facilities in the US (Michigan, Ohio, Tennessee, and Virginia) and in Ontario, Canada.

Royal Group operates as a subsidiary of Axiall Corporation . It is part of the Building Products segment of the parent company.

In Canada vinyl siding and accessories are distributed through company-owned, as well as independent, building product distributors. These distributors sell to professional building product installers in North America.

The company operates 18 company-owned distribution branches in Canada that sell its vinyl siding and accessories, trim molding and deck products, as well as pipe and pipe fittings. The branches also sell other products related to the exterior of the house that are not manufactured by its building products segment.

Royal Group serves, renovation, remodeling and new construction industries.

The Building Products Segment contributed 25% of Axiall's total net sales in 2015.

In 2016 Royal Building Products introduced Zuri Grooved Boards to its Zuri Premium Decking by Royal product line. This product line extension offers homeowners the complete Zuri decking experience with a true hidden fastener application with Zuri Grooved Boards that creates an aesthetically incomparable outdoor environment. In the same year, it also launched its newest online design tool, the Trim Visualizer, to provide homeowners and professionals with ideas and inspiration for choosing the right trim option based on the style of their home.

In 2015 launch new vinyl and aluminum product lines at the 2015 International Builders' Show.

In 2013 PPG Industries merged its commodity chemicals business with Georgia Gulf in a $2.1 billion deal and the company became Axiall.

Georgia Gulf bought the company in 2006 for $1.5 billion. Burdened with debt and facing the construction industry's downturn, Georgia Gulf was forced to cut costs and sell some Royal assets. It has closed several Royal Group plants since the acquisition and streamlined operations. In 2007, the company exited the window coverings extrusion business. The following year, it sold Royal Outdoor to Fenclo for $12 million.

Prior to the Georgia Gulf acquisition, Royal Group had a tumultuous decade that included investigations by US regulators and the Canadian Mounties. In 2008 former Royal executives, including the company's founder Vittorio De Zen, were charged in Canada with fraud. De Zen is fighting allegations that he and others defrauded the company for some $29 million.
