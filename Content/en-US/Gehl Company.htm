Manitou Americas (formerly Gehl Company) asks the question, "Can you dig it?" The company manufactures light-construction equipment bearing the Gehl, Mustang, and Manitou brand names. Products include mini-excavators, mini-loaders, and skid steer loaders for material handling; telescopic loaders; and asphalt pavers for building sidewalks, parking lots, trails, and driveways. It has several hundred independent dealers around the globe, though most are located in North America. Manitou Americas also offers financing to its dealers and their customers. The company is a subsidiary of French equipment manufacturer MANITOU BF .

The company is headquartered in West Bend, Wisconsin; it also has regional locations in Belvidere, Illinois; Madison and Yankton, South Dakota; and Waco, Texas. Manitou Americas serves customers in the US and internationally.

Manitou Americas designs, manufactures, and distributes a range of compact equipment. The company is part of the Manitou Americas organization, which manages administrative and personnel functions.

The company's equipment is primarily used by building contractors. Other customers include agricultural and industrial companies. Manitou Americas markets its products through a network of equipment dealers.

Manitou Americas was founded in 1859 in a Wisconsin blacksmith shop. It commemorated its 150th anniversary by creating "chromed-out" Model 7801E skid loader, weighing in at five tons.
