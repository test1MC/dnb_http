When the cookie crumbles, Burton's Foods still has plenty of pounds (sterling). Doing business as Burton's Biscuit Co., the company is one of the UK's largest suppliers of biscuits (cookies in the US). Burton's bakes up such iconic brands as Jammie Dodgers, Wagon Wheels, and, under a manufacturing agreement with Mondelez , Cadbury chocolate biscuits. Its other brands include Viscount and Maryland cookies. Burton's also makes private-label biscuits and some confectionery products. Its lineup is distributed and marketed in Europe and North America. Founded in 2000, the company is the result of a merger of Burton's Gold Medal Biscuits and Horizon Biscuits. Burton's has been owned by Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce and Apollo Management since 2009.

Burton's is based in the UK at St. Albans and manufactures its products from three main facilities in Blackpool, England; Edinburgh, Scotland; and Llantarnam, Wales. The company also operates a chocolate refinery in Moreton, a central distribution hub in Liverpool, and a subsidiary office in Blackpool. In addition to distributing throughout the UK, Burton's also sells its wares in North and South America and Russia.
