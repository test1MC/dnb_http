Clarkson Grain fancies itself a grain clerk. The company is a grain, oilseed, and ingredient supplier to the food manufacturing and animal-feed industries. It procures its raw materials -- crops (corn and soya) from farmers in the US and Canada. Clarkson operates grain elevators, conditioning and processing plants, storage warehouses, and a barge station. Its products include corn and soy flours, masa, meal, refined soy oil, lechitin, whole grains, and organic and non-GMO grains and oilseeds. They are shipped to customers throughout the world by road, rail, and water. The company was founded in 1974 by the Clarkson family. In 2012 Clarkson Grain acquired local rival US Soy , a grower and processor of soy beans.
