QuickLogic is quick to get embed. The company designs and sells logic chips that can be programmed by OEMs, as well as low-power customizable chips used to add features to and extend the battery life of mobile, consumer, and business electronics. It also offers related hardware and software, plus custom programming services. The fabless company -- it uses contract manufacturers to produce its chips -- targets smartphone, tablet, and access card manufacturers. Customers for its logic chips come from the aerospace, instrumentation, and military industries, among others. Honeywell (15% of sales) is a top customer.

QuickLogic gets about 60% of sales from outside the US.

The company primarily outsources semiconductor fabrication to TSMC , eSilicon , and TowerJazz, the integrated brand used by Tower Semiconductor and Jazz Semiconductor . Product packaging, testing, and programming is contracted out to Amkor Technology and Unisem.

Most of the company's sales come from field-programmable gate array (FPGA) devices, which it labels mature products.

QuickLogic reported revenue of $26.1 million for fiscal 2013, almost double the $14.9 million it reported for revenue in fiscal 2012. However, the company has suffered a net loss of about $12 million in both of the past two fiscal periods. Its cash flow has also suffered in recent years despite the top line revenue growth.
