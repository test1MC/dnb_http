Teenyboppers may not be acquainted with one of The Mentholatum Company's best-known products, Mentholatum Deep Heating Rub, but they can probably find another of the company's bestsellers in their medicine cabinets -- Oxy Pads. The Mentholatum Company, a subsidiary of ROHTO Pharmaceutical , makes OTC products to relieve a variety of common ailments, including acne, cold sores, aches and pains, and chest congestion. In addition to the Oxy and namesake Mentholatum brands, the firm sells its products under the WellPatch, ROHTO, Fletcher's, Red Cross, Natural Ice, and Softlips monikers. ROHTO's Mentholatum companies have operations in more than a dozen countries and sell products in more than 150 countries.

The company is an international maker and marketer of non-prescription drugs. It specializes in eye care, skincare, and internal medicines and food, among other items. The Mentholatum Company's bulging portfolio of brands includes such products as OXY Acne Solutions, pHisoderm Facial Care, Softlips Lip Protection, ROHTO Eye Drops, WellPatch Pain-Relieving Patches, Deep Heating Muscle Rub, Mentholatum Ointment, Fletcher's Laxative, and Red Cross Toothache Medication.

As part of its business focus, parent ROHTO develops products for each geographic region, such as North America, Europe, and Asia. In turn, this has helped the company achieve sales turnover growth in recent years. Additionally, The Mentholatum Company is working to develop products based on traditional Japanese remedies for Western customers, cashing in on the growing trend of Westerners making use of ancient Eastern remedies.

The Mentholatum Company is committed to strengthening its presence in Europe. To this end, the company is investing £10 million in its operations in Scotland. The effort, which includes the development of a Research & Development Center, aims to increase production capacity with the latest technology.

The company is devoting more dollars toward advertising to young people in an effort to make them lifelong customers. The Mentholatum Company is looking to lure more male teens by rebranding some of its Oxy products as a male grooming brand. Relaunched as a male skincare brand in 2008, Oxy seeks to focus on encouraging teen boys to develop an entire skincare regime.

Based in New York, The Mentholatum Company is a global provider of health and wellness products, peddling its products in 150-plus countries. It has operations in more than a dozen countries.

The Mentholatum Company was started in 1889 in Wichita, Kansas, as a maker of OTC remedies and other personal-care items.
