Cardinal Glass manufactures residential glass for windows and doors. It caters to domestic and international markets through five operating units: Cardinal CG (coated glass and optical mirrors), Cardinal FG (float and tempered glass), Cardinal IG (insulating glass), Cardinal LG (laminated glass), and Cardinal ST (solar technologies). Its value-added panels include self-cleaning coated "neat glass," smooth enough to loosen dirt and enable water to sheet off, and "LoE3," designed to cut solar heat gain. Cardinal Glass began in Minneapolis in 1962.

The company is headquartered in Eden Prairie, Minnesota, and has more than 35 manufacturing locations across the US. It also has two research and development centers in Minnesota and Wisconsin.

Cardinal Glass grows its business by opening new facilities in strategic locations. In mid-2016, the company's Cardinal IG division acquired 77 acres of land in Buckeye, Arizona, and will use use it to develop a 250,000 sq. ft. advanced glass manufacturing facility. The new location will produce energy-efficient insulating glass for residential window and door companies across the globe.

Also in 2016, Cardinal LG purchased 30 acres for the construction of a new 200,000 sq. ft. manufacturing facility in Archbald, Pennsylvania. The new location will increase the glass company's presence in the eastern US.
