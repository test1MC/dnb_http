Want welding wares? Miller Electric Manufacturing Company makes arc welding and cutting equipment under the Miller brand. Its products include MIG (metal inert gas) welders, TIG (tungsten inert gas) welders, stick welders, engine-driven welders, plasma cutters, generators, wire feeders, and helmets. Miller's welding and cutting products are sold through thousands of distributors located in more than 100 countries. It serves the aviation, fabrication, construction, manufacturing, motorsports, agriculture, and marine industries. The company, which has been owned by Illinois Tool Works since 1993, is not affiliated with Miller Electric Construction .

Based in Wisconsin, Miller sells its products worldwide.

Parent Illinois Tool Works ' welding segment produces arc welding equipment, consumables, and accessories for a wide array of industrial and commercial applications.

In addition to selling its own products, Miller Electric Manufacturing manages and supports Illinois Tool Works' other welding brands. It also provides welding guides and safety tips on its website and sells Miller-branded clothing, mugs, desktop wallpaper, and other items through an online company store. It also offers Bobcat and Trailblazer products.

The company's products are sold through more than 2,600 US distributors serving 100 countries around the globe. It caters to the construction, fabrication, aviation, education, agriculture, marine applications, motorsports, and manufacturing sectors.

In 2014 the welding segment accounted for 13% of Illinois Tool Works' net revenues.

Miller focuses on customers' welding challenges in specific market segments and applications/

In 2015 Miller expanded its respiratory protection portfolio with the addition of two new products aimed at helping companies establish and maintain a safe, comfortable, and compliant welding environment. The N95 disposable respirator and expanded LPR-100 half mask respirator expanded Miller's already strong line of powered air-purifying respirators.

That year the company introduced the new Millermatic 350P auto body aluminum repair system - designed to provide high weld quality and simplify repairs on aluminum auto body panels and beds, such as found in the 2015 Ford F-150 truck.

In 2014 Miller joined with area employers, manufacturing alliances, technical colleges, workforce development boards and the Hispanic Chamber of Commerce of Wisconsin to spearhead a collaborative job training initiative aimed at addressing a shortage of qualified welders in Wisconsin.

Miller was founded in 1929 by Niels Miller, who wanted to make a smaller and more affordable arc welder for use in rural Wisconsin.
