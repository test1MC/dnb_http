Aviall Services, the primary subsidiary of Aviall, Inc. , distributes new aviation parts and provides aftermarket services to military and government aircraft operators, commercial airlines, and general aviation and marine customers. Rolls Royce , operating under a contract with the US military, is Aviall Services' largest customer. Aviall Services, which accounts for the lion's share of its parent's sales, distributes more than 2 million new parts, components, and supplies from more than 240 OEMs. The company maintains sales and support centers throughout the US and overseas. Parent Aviall Inc. is a subsidiary of manufacturing giant Boeing .

The company operates 40 customer service centers and about 20 authorized product repair facilities throughout Asia, the Middle East, Australia, Europe, New Zealand, and North America. Boeing brought in about 45% of its revenue from the US during fiscal 2014.

Aviall Services provides product repair for aviation batteries, hoses, wheels, and brakes, as well as painting services. It also offers maintenance seminars and battery training schools. Its logistics services include stocking, automated inventory management, and order processing. The Inventory Locator Service (ILS) unit provides an online marketplace for inventory, as well as information about the company's related industries, and supports the operations of Aviall Services.

The company uses a sales force comprised of more than 250 customer service reps and 80 field sales professionals.

Aviall Services operates as a unit of Boeing Commercial Airplanes segment. The Commercial Airplanes segment contributed more than 66% of the parent company's total revenue in fiscal 2014.
