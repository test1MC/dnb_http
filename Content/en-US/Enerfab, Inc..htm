Enerfab says "tanks, a lot" to customers who buy its products. The company manufactures fabricated metal products, including storage and brewery tanks, tank heads, and piping systems. Enerfab also operates as a boiler installation and maintenance contractor serving both the industrial and utility markets, and it offers material handling services including conveyors, rotary car dumpers, scales, and barge loaders. Customers include a range of companies in the chemical, food and beverage, pharmaceutical, and utility industries. Over the years, Enerfab has produced more than 400 million gallons of juice storage tanks in the US, Europe, and South America.

Enerfab has regional offices in Atlanta; Chicago; Harrisburg, Pennsylvania; Kansas City, Missouri; Paducah, Kentucky; Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania; Appleton, Wisconsin; Charleston, West Virginia; and Somerset, Kentucky.

Enerfab serves customers in the brewery, chemical, energy, and food and beverage sectors.

A major component in the company's growth has been the production of pollution control equipment that Enerfab markets to utility companies across the midwestern US. It was helped in 2011 by new rules finalized by the US Environmental Protection Agency calling for new pollution control investments by 2015. As a result of the new rules, Enerfab expects to generate up to $1 billion in revenue between 2013 and 2016. Its pollution control systems division generates about a third of Enerfab's revenue, and the company expects that amount to grow to over 50% over the next four years as utilities adhere to government standards and demand more of its products.

Enerfab was founded in 1901 as The Bishopric Products Company to serve the brewing industry. Following Prohibition, the company began to make resinous, food-grade coatings and tank linings.
