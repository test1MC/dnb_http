Brewster Dairy is the hole-y grail of Swiss cheese. The 100 million pounds of cheese the dairy operation produces annually makes the Ohio company the #1 maker of natural Swiss cheese in the country. More than 400 dairy farms provide the cheese maker with milk. Brewster Dairy markets a variety of the holey cheeses, including regular Swiss, baby Swiss, lacey Swiss, and reduced-fat Swiss, under the Brewster Cheese brand name. Brewster Dairy's products can be found in retail grocery stores and deli shops throughout the US. As part of its business, the family-owned company also makes dairy proteins and whey powders that are used as ingredients by other food manufacturers.

Based in Ohio, Brewster Dairy markets its dairy items nationwide. It gets its milk from more than 400 US dairy farms. The company has three manufacturing sites. Its main plant and company headquarters are located in Brewster, Ohio. The company also runs Brewster West in Rupert, Ohio, and Stockton Cheese in Stockton, Illinois.

As part of its business, it operates a retail cheese store adjacent to its factory in Brewster. Combined, Brewster Dairy's operations churn out 200,000 pounds of cheese every day.

Brewster Dairy's primary products include cheese and whey. It makes and markets a variety of cheeses, such as Aged Swiss, Swiss Select, Lacey Baby, and Baby. Its whey items include Brewster Lactose and Brewster 34% Whey.

The company's powdered whey and lactose products are used as ingredients in baking, infant formula, and other dairy and non-dairy foods. Brewster exports much of its whey and protein to foreign countries.

Founded in 1933 as Stark County Milk Producers, the company took on its current name when CEO Fritz Leeman and his father, John Leeman, bought it in 1965.
