Protecting various parts of a worker's body isn't child's play for Honeywell Safety Products, which makes protective equipment such as boots, clothing, eyewear, hats, hearing protection aids, and respiratory devices for the general safety, industrial safety, fire service, and utility markets. With brands such as North Safety Products , Salisbury, Ranger, Uvex, and Miller, it offers products to the manufacturing, agriculture, construction, food processing, pharmaceutical, and automotive industries, as well as military clients. Honeywell Safety Products (HSP) is part of diversified manufacturer Honeywell International 's Automation and Control Solutions segment.

Headquartered in Smithfield, Rhode Island, HSP's principal manufacturing facilities outside the US reside in Europe and Asia; it also has operations in Canada and Australia.

HSP markets and sells its products through engineered systems distributors and security equipment distributors. It caters to the agriculture, chemical, construction, fire protection, food services, forestry, government, and military markets.

The Automation and Control Solutions segment contributed more than 40% of parent company Honeywell International's revenue in fiscal 2012 and 2013.

HSP grows by opening additional facilities in key metropolitan areas and by introducing new products. In 2013 it expanded its safety training operations by opening a new training facility in Houston. The company in 2012 introduced Uvex Mercury safety eyewear, used in a broad range of industries from construction and manufacturing to laboratories and more.
