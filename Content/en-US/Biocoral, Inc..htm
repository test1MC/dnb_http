Biocoral heals human bones with marine-life skeletons. The biomaterials company develops bone-graft substitutes derived from natural coral. The company's chief product, Biocoral, consists primarily of calcium carbonate and is very similar to bone tissue. It is used primarily by surgeons and doctors to treat and prevent fractures resulting from osteoporosis. Biocoral is also used for additional orthopedic, facial reconstruction, and oral surgery applications. The company's products are sold in Europe and Asia through its Biocoral France division. Biocoral plans to seek FDA approval to sell its products in the US.

Biocoral researches, develops, manufactures and markets bone graft (Biocoral) and other bone-growth products for orthopedic, oral and maxillofacial applications.

The company is based in France, and serves in the US and internationally.
