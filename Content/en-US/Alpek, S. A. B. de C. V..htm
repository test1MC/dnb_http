Alpek claims the high ground when it comes to producing polyester (PTA, PET and polyester fibers), plastics and chemicals (polypropylene, EPS, caprolactam), and other specialty and industrial chemicals (surfactants, ethoxilates, and glycoethers).â€‹ The company is a leading producer of PTA and PET worldwide, the #1 expandable polystyrene manufacturer in the US, and the only producer of caprolactam in Mexico. Alpek also operates one of the largest polypropylene facilities in North America. Some 90% of Alpek's products are used for packaging food, beverages, and consumer goods.

The company operates 22 plants in Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Mexico, and the US.

Alpek's operating companies include Akra-Polyester, DAK Americas , Indelpro, Polioles, Styropek Mexico, Tereftalatos Mexicanos, and Univex.

In 2015 Alpek reported revenues of $5.3 billion and EBITDA (consolidated operating income plus depreciation, amortization, and impairment of noncurrent assets) of $630 million.

Mexican conglomerate Alfa spun off Alpek via an IPO in 2012.

Alpek was founded in 1975.
