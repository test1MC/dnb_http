Pulp is anything but fiction at Millar Western Forest Products. The company is a leading producer of bleached chemi-thermo-mechanical pulp. The pulp is used by other manufacturers to make printing, writing, and specialty papers as well as paperboard, tissue, and paper towels. Through a company-owned mill in Whitecourt, Alberta, and a managed mill in Meadow Lake, Saskatchewan, Millar produces 450,000 metric tons of pulp per year. The company also produces 300 million board feet of kiln-dried spruce, pine, and fir lumber from two sawmills in Whitecourt, and Boyle, Alberta. Millar Western Industries Ltd. owns the company.

The company is headquartered in Edmonton, Alberta. Millar Western's lumber is sold into Asia and Europe, with a smaller proportion shipped to destinations in the Americas.

Millar Western sells its products through wholesale distributors and overseas via a marketing consortium, Interex Forest Products. The majority of lumber is sold within Canada, with the balance shipped to Asia and the US.
