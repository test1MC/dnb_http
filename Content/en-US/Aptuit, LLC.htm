On the big pharmaceutical industry stage, Aptuit prefers to operate behind the scenes. A pharmaceutical services company, Aptuit offers a range of support services to biotech and other drug development companies, including early- to mid-stage development clinical studies support, information systems development, project management, and consulting services. The Welsh, Carson, Anderson & Stowe portfolio company operates in North America, Europe, and the Asia-Pacific region and serves companies of all sizes. Aptuit derives its name from "aptitude" (for science) and "intuition" (for service).

The company maintains six global facilities, three in Europe (in the UK and Italy) and three in the US (Connecticut, Indiana, and Missouri), and has a strategic relationship with Laurus Labs in India.

Aptuit provides a complete set of integrated early to mid-phase drug development services. Working with more than 600 client companies over its history, Aptuit offers integrated services such as drug design, discovery, solid state chemistry, and preclinical bioscience. It also offers product formulation, active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) manufacturing, dosage form manufacturing, and sterile fill finish services. Its Aptuit INDiGO offering helps accelerate drug development programs; other specialized services include brain imaging analysis for pharma and biotech companies, as well as biodistribution studies for gene therapy development.

The company provides scientific expertise to biotech and pharmaceutical companies in need of the design, implementation, and analysis of Imaging Brain studies. It also offers fast screenings of synaptic activity on hippocampal slices for clients who are focused on the central nervous system therapeutics.

Aptuit has grown through the acquisition of facilities. Once it has established a presence in a market, the company also works to strike partnerships and contracts with local drug developers. For instance, in 2013 it entered a supply agreement to provide APIs to Aegerion Pharmaceuticals . That year Aptuit's Verona center expanded its services to include nutritional ingredient development through a partnership with Microbion and Total Quality Foods; and in 2012 Aptuit extended its strategic partnership with GlaxoSmithKline for dedicated drug development work at The Aptuit Center for Drug Discovery & Development in Verona.

Upgrading its R&D capacity, in 2013, Aptuit formed the Aptuit Scientific Advisory Board comprised of scientific experts from within the company. The board meets regularly to assess Aptuit's current and long term scientific capabilities and objectives and recommends actions.

In 2013 Aptuit launched 'Complementary Vision' for those in search of high value Chemistry, Manufacturing and Control services. These services include: API Development and Manufacture; Qualitative/Quantitative Chemical, Physical and Biopharmaceutical Characterization of activities and dosage forms; Dosage Form Design, Development and Manufacturing for preclinical and clinical dosing; and the Development, Manufacturing and Testing of Inhaled Products such as Dry Powder Inhalers.

In order to refocus on the discovery and mid-phase development of drugs, Aptuit sold its clinical trial supply business to Catalent Pharma Services for $410 million in cash in early 2012. That business included late-stage drug development services and clinical manufacturing capacity.

Aptuit purchased its Verona, Italy, facility from GlaxoSmithKline in 2010 to expand its capacity for bioanalytical services, which are used in the development of biotech medicines such as antibodies.

Aptuit was founded in 2004.Private equity firm Welsh, Carson, Anderson & Stowe holds a significant ownership stake in the company.
