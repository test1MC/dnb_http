Dot Hill Systems designs and markets RAID (redundant array of independent disks) storage devices that are used in corporate data centers and other network environments by enterprises in data-intensive industries such as financial services and telecommunications. It also makes entry-level and and mid-range storage area network (SANs) and fibre channel systems, and provides storage system and data management software. Dot Hill's products are sold under the Assured brand. Most of the company's sales are made to manufacturing partners, including Hewlett-Packard , which accounts for nearly three-quarters of sales. Dot Hill has international offices in Germany, Israel, Japan, and the UK. Seagate Technology bought Dot Hill in late 2015.

Dot Hill became a subsidiary of Seagate Technology in a $696 million deal sealed in October 2015. Seagate made the purchase to complement and expand its storage systems offerings. Dot Hill is part of Seagate's Cloud Systems and Electronics Solutions business.

Dot Hill's server OEM segment consists of computer makers that sell Dot Hill systems with their server products. The OEM segment accounts for 53% of revenue.

The rest of Dot Hill's revenue comes from the vertical markets segment, which targets data-intensive sectors. Businesses in vertical markets include media and entertainment, telecommunications infrastructure, oil and gas, Big Data analytics, and digital imaging.

Customers in the US account for 99% of Dot Hill's revenue, with the rest from Asia.

While Dot Hill sells to a number of computer makers, its biggest customer has been Hewlett-Packard . In 2013 HP accounted for 59% of revenue and 47% in 2014. Dot Hill expects the percentage of revenue from HP to continue falling but that it will remain a key customer. Other OEM customers include Dell and Lenovo . Vertical customers have been Quantum, Samsung , Stratus , Tektronix , Flextronics , and Teradata .

Dot Hill's revenue increased 5% to $217.7 million in 2014 from $206.6 million in 2013 driven by a 42% rose in vertical segment sales. The company experienced greater demand for existing products as well as for a new line of higher-density chassis models. The OEM segment, however, posted a 14% drop in sales.

Higher revenue and income from continuing operations propelled net income to a 59% jump from 2013 to 2014.

Dot Hill has added value to its hardware with continual software improvements. In 2014 it released RealStore 2.0, which extended to the company's AssuredSAN 4000 Series. In a 2015 agreement, Arrow Electronics will use Dot Hill's AssuredSAN systems in their OEM products in Europe, Africa, and the Middle East.
