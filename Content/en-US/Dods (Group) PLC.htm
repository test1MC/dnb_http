Dods (Group) knows a thing or two about parliamentary procedure. The political communications company provides information and services for policy makers in formats such as print (magazines, directories, newsletters, and books), digital (websites, monitoring, and databases), and events (seminars, conferences, and award ceremonies). Publication titles include The Parliament Magazine and Who's Who in Public Affairs , while websites include politicshome.com and theparliamentmagzine.eu. In addition, Dods's Westminster Explained unit provides Parliament and policy training. The company does business in the UK and other parts of Europe, and was formed in 2001.

Dods' subsidiaries include Vacher Dod Publishing Limited; Training Journal Limited; Dods Parliamentary Communications Limited; Monitoring Services Limited; Le Trombinoscope SAS; Total Politics Limited; and Holyrood Communications Limited.

The company operates in the UK, Belgium, and France.

In fiscal 2016, revenue climbed 7% to £19.6 million, after a dip in 2015 interrupted four years of consistent sales growth. Revenue from events and training businesses was up 16% on the year.

Net income increased 123%, in line with the increase in sales. Cash flow from operating activities more than tripled to £3.5 million.

Following an assessment of go-to-market effectiveness, Dods introduced a client portfolio approach alongside the existing product sales method. The company rejigged its events and training business to make it more specialized and scaleable.
