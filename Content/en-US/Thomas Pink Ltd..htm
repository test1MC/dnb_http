On the streets of London, 18th century men who were "in the pink" were hip to one of the most luxurious clothing brands. Thomas Pink, a subsidiary of upscale lifestyle purveyor LVMH designs, makes, and retails stylish and pricey men's and women's clothing and their accoutrements. It provides custom clothing through a network of more than 100 stores in Australia, Canada, China, France, Indonesia, India, Mexico, Panama, Philippines, Italy, Ireland, Mexico, South Africa, the UK, the UAE, and the US (where it has a presence in a dozen states). A top custom apparel maker, Thomas Pink operates the world's largest shirt shop on New York's Madison Avenue and has been focusing its efforts on expanding in the US.

Thomas Pink sells through stores, Boutiques, and also market through online.

The company is a top brand name within LVMH 's fashion and leather goods business group, which generated 35% of 2014 sales for the luxury goods company.

Thomas Pink has been responsible for robust revenue growth despite an economic downturn in recent years. The clothier has continued to boost its presence in existing locations while also looking for new areas for growth, such as entering the Mexican, Australian, and Indian markets.

The company relies on growth in its key markets -- the UK and the US -- and regularly rolls out new products. Most notably, the company has seen successes among its online boutique from sales of its White Shirt Bar and Informal shirt line. Thomas Pink logged similar sales increases with its Imperial, Informal, and Comfort Stretch shirt lines. Thomas Pink added breadth to its products portfolio by enlisting the expertise of British designer Bill Amberg to create a line of shirt dresses and a leather line.

Thomas Pink's products and services have given the apparel company a competitive edge worldwide. The shirt company offers a Personally Pink custom-order shirt service. At certain Thomas Pink stores customers make an appointment with a company tailor, either at the store or at their office or home. Thomas Pink also offers monogramming, delivery, and online services through its website for those who cannot make it to a store.

In 2012 Thomas Pink extended its reach into India by opening its first store in South Mumbai, thanks to a long-term franchise agreement with Reliance Brands, a unit of Reliance Industries .

Founded by three Irish brothers (James, Peter, and John Mullen) in 1984, the company was acquired by LVMH in 2001.
