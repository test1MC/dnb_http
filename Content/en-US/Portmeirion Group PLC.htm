Perched in the highlands of the Welsh coast, Portmeirion Group makes ceramics with themes reflecting the beauty and unspoiled nature of the surrounding area. The company also distributes glassware, cutlery, cookware, and decorative household items under the Spode, Royal Worcester, and Pimpernel brands. Products are made locally and distributed in the UK and US directly to retailers and through distributors in 60 other countries. Portmeirion also operates a US subsidiary and Canadian joint venture. Founder Susan Williams-Ellis was making ceramic items for a gift shop at the well-known Portmeirion Village when she purchased two pottery companies in 1960 and 1961 and combined them to form Portmeirion Group.

The US is the Group's largest market at some 33% of sales, followed by the UK (26%), and South Korea (18%). It has a showroom and office in New York, a warehouses in Connecticut and Guangdong, China.

Portmeirion Group distributes its products through large high street retailers and independent stores, and well as Group-owned stores and its e-commerce website. In the US, it sells through over 1,200 independent retailers, internet retailers, 'big box' retailers, and warehouse club merchandisers. In the UK, it sells through department stores, 500-plus independent retailers, 10 retail outlets and nationwide mail order companies.

In 2015, revenue grew 12% to £69 million, a continuation of a trend for strong growth in recent years. Emerging markets, particularly India, are posting strong growth figures, with the segment as a whole recording 56% growth for the year. Further, its established markets of the US and UK also both grew appreciably, at 11% and 12% respectively, due to higher demand for its products. South Korea however recorded an 18% decline.

Net income grew 14% on prior year to £6.9 million due to higher revenue, partially offset by higher finance costs. Cash from operating activities increased from £7 million to £11 million for the year.

The company's strategy of geographic expansion is proving successful: the company has recorded strong growth in emerging markets in recent years. It also regularly launches new products. It launched 359 products in 2015, and 601 in 2014.

In 2015, Portmeirion Group acquired Lighthouse Holdings for £17.5 million to expand its product offering. Lighthouse's operating subsidiary, Wax Lyrical, is the UK's largest manufacturer of home fragrances. Midlands-based Wax Lyrical sells its range of home fragrance products (mainly scented candles) via wholesale and retail to both the UK and export markets.
