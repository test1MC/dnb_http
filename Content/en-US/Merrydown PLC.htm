Eat, drink, and be merry; Merrydown will take care of the latter two. The UK-based company produces Merrydown Vintage, a hard cider, and Shloer, a sparkling grape juice. Merrydown Vintage comes in Medium and Dry varieties and is sold through retail chains such as ASDA , Tesco , Sainsbury's , and Morrison Supermarkets in the UK, as well as parts of Europe. Shloer, also offered in several flavors, is reportedly the #1-selling adult soft drink in the UK and Ireland. Merrydown is owned by consumer goods company SHS Group , which distributed Merrydown cider years before purchasing the business.

The company sells its goods in Cyprus, Germany, Netherlands, Malta, Poland, Thailand, and the Channel Islands.

Merrydown is available in many major supermarkets and off-licenses. The company's supermarket clients include ASDA , Spar , Nisa, Tesco , and Costcutter .

Since Merrydown's £36.7 million (about $70 million) acquisition in 2005, the SHS Group has invested in building the hard cider maker's presence in the adult soft drinks market. The group has introduced a series of exotic flavored offerings. The Merrydown Vintage line has expanded more slowly, but most recently launched a black current version.

Merrydown's renaissance follows a struggle with limited resources and a declining image. Seen as old fashioned, it fended off makers premium beer and alcopop (malt sweet pre-mixed alcoholic beverages), which were winning over its core consumer, young drinkers. Merrydown weathered the challenge by expanding with Shloer, a drink that is tarter than other soft drinks. Acquired from GlaxoSmithKline , the product was instrumental in positioning Merrydown as a takeover target by SHS Group.

The company began in 1946 with three friends who put up £100 each (about $167) to launch a drink based on apple wine and fermentation.
