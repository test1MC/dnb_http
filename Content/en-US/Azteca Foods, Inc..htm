Azteca Foods carries on the tasty culinary traditions of the Aztecs. The company is a food manufacturer that specializes in producing tortillas, chips, and salad shells. It offers a variety of tortillas, including pressed flour, low-carb flour, fat-free flour, and white or yellow corn products. They're sold under the Azteca, Ultragrain, and Buena Vida brands. Azteca's offerings can be found in the refrigerated section of retail grocery stores. Its foodservice division serves large customers from a storage and distribution facility in Chicago and also provides quality assurance and research and development services to its customers. The family-owned company was founded in 1970 by Arthur Velasquez.

Based in Chicago, Azteca Foods serves both national and international markets and supports local Latino communities through its participation on more than 20 not-for-profit boards. Exports comprise some 50% of its business.

Besides the US, Azteca Foods sells its products in 17 other countries across Europe, the Middle East, and the Maghreb.

Azteca Foods, which produces 100,000 flour and corn tortillas per hour, makes its tortillas and other products at a 100,000-sq.-ft. facility on the southwest side of Chicago.

Its industrial products division creates custom tortilla products, providing research and development resources, unique packaging ideas, and special product formulations.

The food firm looks to expand its product line from traditional tortillas to bake-and-fill salad shells and tortilla chips, as well as its newer line of Azteca Meal Essentials. The company's Trans Fat Free Shape & Bake Tortilla Shells leverage a patented process to customize shells in a variety of shapes for use in quiches, soups, desserts, pizza, and other applications.

While the company's committed to Chicago area consumers, Azteca Foods has grown to serve global markets.

Formed in 1970 as Azteca Corn Products Corp., the company was sold to baker Pillsbury in 1984. Under Pillsbury, Azteca's product line and brand recognition grew. In 1989 founder Velasquez and his wife bought back the assets of the business from Pillsbury and changed the name to Azteca Foods, Inc.
