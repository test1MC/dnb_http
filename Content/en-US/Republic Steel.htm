Republic Steel (formerly Republic Engineered Products) is one of the largest makers of special bar-quality (SBQ) steel used to manufacture automobiles and heavy equipment. Customers turn the company's steel into components such as bearings, crankshafts, and spark plug shells. Republic Steel operates both electric arc furnace and traditional integrated blast furnace mills. Its plants are located mostly in Ohio, though the company also has locations elsewhere in the US as well as in Ontario, Canada. The company has been a unit within Mexican steelmaker Grupo Simec since 2005, but it traces its roots back to 1886, when it was founded as Berger Manufacturing Co.

With nine plants, Republic Steel is North America's supplier of special bar quality steel, a highly engineered product used in axles, drive trains, suspensions, and other critical components of automobiles, off-highway vehicles, and industrial equipment. Its special bar quality products include hot rolled steel bars, cold finished steel bars, and bottom poured ingots.

Republic Steel is also a manufacturer and supplier of carbon and alloy rod and wire for a diverse customer base.

Republic Steel Wire Processing serves the OEM and replacement markets. Its customers includes the fastener, automotive, hand tools, appliance, off-road heavy equipment, and construction markets

In 2016, the company announced plans to idle its rolling mill operations at the Lorain steel mill as the plant continued to struggle with a decline in the energy market and weak customer demand.
