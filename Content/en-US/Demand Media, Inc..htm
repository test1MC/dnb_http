Demand Media knows that Web branding is in demand. Attracting some 125 million unique visitors each month, the company operates through a variety of Web-related enterprises that exist to help drive Web traffic to its clients' sites. Subsidiaries include domain-name wholesaler eNom and Pluck , a blog syndicator and provider of social media tools used for integrating websites. Other Demand Media websites include online how-to tutorials provider eHow, humor site Cracked.com, and Trails.com, for outdoor enthusiasts. It also produces online video and written articles through its Demand Studios business, which employs freelancers to provide content for its websites. Demand Media launched an IPO in early 2011.

In addition to its headquarters in Santa Monica, CA, the company has offices in Bellevue, Washington; Austin, Texas; Chicago, Illinois; New York City; and London, UK. In fiscal 2014 about 90% of Demand Media's revenues came from websites and customers located in the US.

The company earns more than 75% of its revenues through the sale of advertising in connection with its content and media segment. The segment includes Demand's owned and operated websites such as eHow.com, as well as more than 375 websites operated by customers such as USATODAY.com. The company produces videos through Demand Studios. The videos, primarily distributed to YouTube , earn revenue via targeted advertising.

Demand Media spent about $2.2 million on advertising and promotional expenses during fiscal 2014.

Demand Media reported $172.4 million in revenue for fiscal 2014. That was a massive decrease compared to the company's revenue in previous fiscal years. Even more concerning was the $34.7 million net loss Demand Media suffered in fiscal 2014, although that was $41.5 million less than the company lost in fiscal 2013. The net loss had an impact on Demand Media's cash on hand from operations. Its cash on hand decreased by $41.5 million during fiscal 2014 and the company ended the year with $34.7 million in cash on hand.

The company has been streamlining its operations as it tries narrow its focus and . Demand Media spun off its Rightside Group business in 2014. The company also sold off its online video arts and crafts business Creativebug to Otter Media that year.

Demand Media did add to its business during fiscal 2014 as well. The company acquired Saatchi Online and made it a wholly-owned subsidiary.
