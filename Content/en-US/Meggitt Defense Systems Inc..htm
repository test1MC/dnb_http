Meggitt Defense Systems manufactures defense systems through its varied segments. Aeromechanical makes air-to-air and surface-to-air simulation combat targets, aerial towed vehicle systems, special purpose aircraft pods, decoy deployment, and reeling machines. Ammunition Handling provides linkless loading, magazines, and autoloaders, while Electronic Systems produces live-fire training, target scoring, threat detection, and identification and location equipment. Environmental Control makes products for thermal management of equipment used in airborne and ground combat vehicles. The company sells to armed forces and law enforcement agencies worldwide. Meggitt Defense is a business unit of Meggitt PLC .

Meggitt Defense sells is products to military forces in more than 30 countries worldwide.

The company's thermal management systems address the growing need for lightweight and durable equipment to cool computer processors, which can be negatively affected by the heat they expel, as well as the heat generated by vehicles. Meggitt Defense's Auxiliary Cooling and Power Units (ACPU) are designed to reduce the dependence on vehicle engine power and cooling by more than 40%. The company has contracts with General Dynamics to supply thermal management systems on the M1A2 SEP Abrams tank, and a long-range infrared search and track system for the F/A-18E/F fighter; it has a development contract with the US Army for the Logistics Equipment (MULE) unmanned ground vehicle. So far, Meggitt Defense's thermal management systems are the only systems that have been applied practically in combat.

The company's Ammunition Handling Systems division is a leader in its market. In addition to being awarded a contract to supply a 35mm feed system for Krauss-Maffei Wegman , this division also won an $18 million order to provide mobile gun system replenishers for General Dynamics' Stryker wheeled infantry combat vehicle.

The company grows through securing defense and aerospace contracts. In 2012 Meggitt was awarded a five-year agreement from Sikorsky Aircraft to make fuel tanks, ice protection equipment, composites and interiors. Covering all Sikorsky's production military rotorcraft, including the BLACK HAWK helicopter, the contract is valued at up to $129 million.

That year the company also won a $1 billion contract to provide components for seven Hamilton Sundstrand thermal management packages on Pratt & Whitney PurePower geared turbofan engines. The engine family will power aircraft such as the new Bombardier C-Series regional jet, Mitsubishi Regional Jet and Airbus A320neo aircraft. Meggitt was awarded these contracts by Hamilton Sundstrand, which provides the thermal management sub-systems for Pratt & Whitney's PurePower engine suite.
