This M&M is more rugged than the candy-coated confection that goes by the same name. From food to defense, Mahindra & Mahindra (M&M) keeps its businesses diverse. Sectors include Automotive, After-Market (pre-owned vehicles and spare parts), and Farm Equipment (FES). Its Automotive unit makes jeeps and SUVs, as well as commercial vehicles, three-wheeler utilitarian delivery and flatbed vehicles, and scooters and motorcycles. Mahindra Defense Systems (MDS) produces armored vehicles and sea mines, while Mahindra Agribusiness exports fruits to Europe and Asia. M&M makes aircraft and related components, and is active in IT and financial services, steel processing, and infrastructure.

The company has operations in more than 100 countries across North America, South America, Europe, the Middle East and Africa, Asia, and Australasia.

M&M's segments are Automotive (automobiles, spare parts and related services) and Farm Equipment (tractors, spare parts and related services).

The company operates in the key industries that drive economic growth, enjoying a leadership position in tractors, utility vehicles, information technology, financial services and vacation ownership. In addition, M&M enjoys a strong presence in the agribusiness, aerospace, components, consulting services, defence, energy, industrial equipment, logistics, real estate, retail, steel, commercial vehicles and two wheeler industries. M&M has dedicated plants and facilities to address specialized requirements of Indian Army, Navy and Air Force and is an active participant in various 'Make in India' programs in this space.

Net income declined by 12% due to decreased sales, increased employee benefits expense and other costs. Other expenses increased mainly on account of marketing related expenses on incentives, advertisement, and sales promotion to support and enhance sales in a challenging market and for brand building.

The company has targeted diverse businesses and strategies through its more than 100 companies, and has allied itself with a number of international companies to further its product portfolio and geographic presence. It has a domestic market ripe for the picking -- less than 2% of the Indian population own vehicles. Its manufacturing footprint is rivaling the leaders of the domestic Indian market. In addition to introducing new automotive products, M&M is building out its defense interests to include aerospace.

To sustain growth, M&M is pursuing several strategic initiatives in a number of areas, including strengthening the product portfolio, refreshing and updating existing products and strengthening R&D and technology capabilities. The company is also is pursuing international expansion.

M&M is taking its farm equipment business global. While the US is M&M's largest export market, other core export markets for its tractors include Africa, Australia, China and SAARC countries (South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation -- Bangladesh, Bhutan, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka).

In 2014 Mahindra Two Wheelers Ltd. launched the Centuro N1 in Pune, India, while the agricultural division of M&M signed a joint venture agreement with HZPC, Holland, one of the leading innovative companies in the world in potato breeding, seed potato growing and seed potato trading to offer high quality seed potatoes to farmers within and outside of India.

In 2015 Mahindra Two Wheelers agreed to buy Peugeot Motocycles, which offers one of the most comprehensive range of scooters and mopeds (including the successful three wheeled scooter, Metropolis) in the European market.

In 2014 Mahindra Holidays & Resorts India Ltd. acquired a resort comprising 60 rooms situated at Manali, India, taking its resort count to 41. It also agreed to buy 19% of Holiday Club Resorts Oy, Finland. Holiday Club is a leading vacation ownership company in Europe with 32 resorts,24 of which are located in Finland, two in Sweden and six in Spain.

That year Mahindra Logistics Ltd. bought a majority stake in LORDS Freight (India), which specializes in international logistics services.

In 2014 Tech Mahindra Ltd. acquired global network services leader, US-based Lightbridge Communications for $240 million.

Company Background

M&M got its start in 1945 assembling jeeps from the US. Its Automotive sector, which is its oldest unit, was founded in 1954.

In 2009 the company launched Mahindra Australia and Mahindra China Tractor, which is a joint venture with Jiangling Motor. Also in China, M&M allied with Jiansu Yueda Yancheng Tractor Manufacturing in 2009. The company announced in early 2011 that it is is spending $6 million to buy a 38% share in EPC Industrie, a micro-irrigation company, with plans to acquire an additional 20% stake later in the year.

In the meantime, M&M is eying a future in vehicles powered by alternative fuel sources, such as compressed natural gas (CNG) and electricity, as well as a hydrogen-power and biodiesel. In 2010 M&M purchased a controlling stake in Indian electric car maker REVA (renamed Mahindra REVA). M&M will bring product development, deep pockets, and management know-how, along with 450 million rupees ($10 million); it will receive ready-made electric engines for its entire product line.

Looking to expand its SUV manufacturing worldwide, the company has moved into Korea, too. In 2011 it completed its KRW522.5 billion ($473.5 million) acquisition of a controlling stake (about 70%) in bankrupt Ssangyong Motor Co. The acquisition of the Korean car maker paves the way for M&M to establish a significant presence in southeast Asia, and a springboard to neighboring regions, as well as to pick up a string of technologies.

Earlier in the year M&M took a presence in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) market -- west and central Asia, as well as Africa -- by agreeing to enter into a joint venture with Arabia Holdings, a UAE-based real estate, logistics, and transportation company. M&M will hold a controlling interest in the joint venture known as Ras Al Khaimah Transport Investments to manufacture and sell armored vehicles (for military and commercial use) in that region.

Another important joint venture includes M&M's partnership with Renault , which dates back to 2005. The combined business named Mahindra Renault Private Limited made an Indian model of Renault's Logan sedan, but sales were disappointing; in early 2010 M&M agreed to buy out Renault's 49% stake in the venture. Renault continues to supply engines and transmissions for the Logan to M&M, which plans to reengineer the model and source parts locally.

M&M is also growing more active in aircraft and aerospace component manufacturing. Through subsidiary Mahindra Aerospace, M&M acquired a stake of about 75% in Australia-based parts supplier Aerostaff Australia and aircraft maker Gippsland Aeronautics. The deal opens the door to bidding on defense contracts in India through tie-ups with Boeing and Airbus , among other foreign contractors, which allows M&M to source from local businesses. M&M also agreed to form a joint venture with BAE Systems for defense land products (combat vehicles and artillery products) in late 2009.

And to support all of its growth and expansion, M&M formed a joint venture in early 2011 with Cisco Systems to create a platform to provide cloud computing services, which is described as an on-demand network serving a shared pool of computing resources. Through cloud computing, M&M in essence will be changing the way it does business, focusing on the Internet to provide a range of services to its customers, including virtual dealerships.
