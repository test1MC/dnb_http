Under the adidas umbrella since 2006, Reebok International Limited (UK) has been running with a new pack but is headed toward some of the same hurdles. It's the British subsidiary of Reebok International that faces some of the stiffest European competition. The company manufactures athletic footwear and activewear for men, women, and teens and serves as the center of European operations for its parent company. Its products include limited edition athletic shoes, men's and women's shirts and shorts, gloves, and sports-related items. It makes a G-Unit and (Allen) Iverson branded products. Reebok brand veteran Paul Harrington succeeded Paul Fireman as head of the company when adidas acquired Reebok.

The company provides designing, marketing, and distribution of sports, fitness, and casual footwear, apparel, and equipment.

The company is based in Stockport, UK.

Reebok has shifted its marketing strategy targets towards the fitness fanatic consumer segment - those that put fitness at the center of their lives and take part in activities such as CrossFit, with which Reebok has entered into a commercial partnership. It targets women in particular, who are engaging in weights-based fitness in increasing numbers.

In 2015, Reebok acquired Refuel (Brand Distribution) Limited for £11 million. London-based Refuel mainly markets and distributes apparel of Mitchell & Ness. In 2014, it completed the £5 million acquisition of Luta Ltd, which makes workout clothing and which aided Reebok in signing a licensing agreement with Ultimate Fighting Championship.
