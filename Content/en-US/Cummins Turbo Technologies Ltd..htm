Cummins Turbo Technologies contributes a boost of turbocharged power to the business of parent company Cummins, Inc. , a diesel engine manufacturer. Cummins Turbo Technologies manufactures a line of turbochargers for use on everything from heavy-duty trucks ("lorries" in the UK) to off-highway vehicles, marine vessels, and power generation sets; products are designed for use on diesel and natural gas engines ranging from 49 to 5,100 horsepower. In addition to providing turbochargers for Cummins' line of engines, Cummins Turbo Technologies sells to companies such as PACCAR , Volkswagen , and Fiat .

A turbocharger (turbine, compressor, shaft, and bearing system) converts waste energy to air, then compresses the air before it flows into a diesel engine. More air means more fuel can be added, and that means more engine power. Turbocharge technology increases engine power without increasing the engine size, which allows for improved fuel consumption, better performance in altitudes, and lower emissions. Cummins Turbo Technologies gives its parent company the confidence to actually look forward to tougher emissions standards, which is crucial as more environmentally friendly legislation gets passed in the upcoming years.

Cummins Turbo Technologies operates manufacturing and/or aftermarket supply facilities in Brazil, China, India, the Netherlands, the UK, and the US.

The company is part of the components business segment within Cummins, which contributed 27% of its total revenue in 2015.

The company (Holset Engineering and also known as Holset Turbochargers) got its start in 1948 when W. C. Holmes manufactured flexible couplings designed by Louis Croset. The company's original name was derived from the two men's names: "Hol"mes and Cro"set." It was acquired by Cummins in 1973 and changed its name to Cummins Turbo Technologies in 2006.
