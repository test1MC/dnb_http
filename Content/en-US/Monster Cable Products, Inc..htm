Monster makes high-end cables for connecting audio and video equipment. Monster Cable's products are made for audiophiles for use in their homes, cars, professional environments, and with computers. Its Monster Power unit offers A/C power to audiovisual gear and computer products. Professional Monster customers have included the likes of Aerosmith, Barenaked Ladies, Ozzy Osbourne, and Jennifer Lopez. Monster, which has more than 500 US and international patents to its name, makes more than 5,000 audio products.

Based in California, Monster has grown to serve professional and novice audiophiles in more than 160 countries in North America, Europe, Africa, the Middle East, Asia, Australia, New Zealand, and Latin America.

Monster's audio cables increase the clarity, dynamics and power of the audio signals that travel through them. Its Monster Power division delivers cleaner AC power to A/V components and computer products.

Monster sells its audio products through music stores, authorized dealers, electronics retailers, and online to music lovers, audiophiles, recording studios, sound professionals, musicians, custom-installers, gamers, and home theater enthusiasts.

Monster regularly introduces new products that resonate with a wide range of consumers as a way to tune out the competition. In mid-2016 it introduced its Elements Over-Ear wireless headphone, which it claims is used by DJs, celebrities, and athletes.

In 2015 it released new versions of its Rock, Bass, and Acoustic cables. It also introduced the first two-step cleaning system that cleans, polishes, and shields screens on all types of devices, including TVs, smartphones, cameras, tablets, GPS devices, game systems, and portable devices.

Monster was founded in the late 1970s by Noel Lee, a laser-fusion design engineer at Lawrence-Livermore Laboratory .
