Ampacet helps manufacturers of plastic products show their true hues with custom color and additive concentrates. Using polyethylene, polypropylene, polyamide, and polyester resins, Ampacet makes compounds, concentrates, and masterbatches that enable those manufacturers to produce consistent colors and chemical characteristics for their extruded and molded products. It also offers technical, analytical, and color matching support, as well as on-site troubleshooting and in-house prototyping. Globally, the company competes with firms such as BASF and Clariant in the color concentrates market. Its additives are used in food and industrial packaging, pipe and conduit, wire and cable, and other plastic products.

Ampacet operates 21 manufacturing facilities in 15 countries (Argentina, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, India, Italy, Luxembourg, Mexico, Poland, Russia, Thailand, the UK, and the US). The company six production plants in North America are un Cartersville, Georgia; DeRidder, Louisiana; Heath, Ohio; Terre Haute, Indiana; Kitchener, Ontario, Canada; and Querétaro, Mexico.

The company also has four major R&D centers worldwide for quality management, technical service, analytical testing, and innovative development activities.

Ampacet has a global masterbatch capacity of more than 400,000 tons. Its 20 masterbatch production sites have color matching capabilities.

It provides thousands of black and white masterbatch formulas (including those with electrical conductivity, high pigment loadings, temperature resistance, and UV resistance). Its color concentrates encompass all organic and inorganic pigments and those for pearlescent, metallic and other special effects. Ampacet maintains more than 1,000 white masterbatch formulations/

Ampacet's product applications include utility uses and high quality specialty applications such extrusion coatings, weatherable grades, products for photographic uses, and molding grades for food, non-food, and medical uses.

Its tailored masterbatches bring together commodity and specialty resins and include include antistats, antiblocks, antifogs, antioxidants, flame retardants, foaming agents, optical brighteners, process aids, purge agents, release agents, scents, slips, and UV absorbers.

The company's specific markets include agricultural and greenhouse films, construction, electronics, packaging (flexible and rigid), piping and conduit, textiles and nonwovens, toys, and wire and cable insulation. It markets its products in more than 90 countries.

It has expanded its global reach in recent years. In 2014 it opened its European headquarters serving Europe, the Middle East and Africa (EMEA), with additional production capacity and a new European innovation center located in Dudelange, Luxembourg. The expansion is a gateway to the European market and its more than 500 million consumers and the 40% of the European Union's wealth that is concentrated in a 500-km area around Luxembourg. The more than $32 million investment includes the installation of a new production line, which expanded Ampacet's EMEA capacity to produce white and black masterbatches by 60%. The site offers the possibility for further expansion (up to double the initial installed capacity).

The company is also focused on offering new and exclusive color and additive masterbatch product development assistance and on providing process efficiency technology and services.

Mergers and Acquisitions

Growing geographically, in 2014 Ampacet agreed to acquire Australia-based Allied Color and Additives.

Ampacet opened new facilities in Brazil, Italy, and India during 2011.

North American projects in 2011 included new locations in DeRidder, Louisiana; Cartersville, Georgia; and Kitchener, Ontario. Two of the lines in DeRidder were for color and additive concentrates, while the third will be used to make black masterbatches. The new line in Cartersville makes white masterbatches, while in Kitchener, Ampacet installed a new color concentrates line for custom packaging and other uses.

Ampacet was founded in 1937. Alexander bought Ampacet, then known as American Molding Powder Co., in 1954. (Ampacet is actually short for American Molding Powder acetate.) The family of the late Norman Alexander, one-time chairman of Sequa Corporation , owns the firm.
