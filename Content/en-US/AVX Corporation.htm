AVX proves that tiny parts can add up to big business. The company's largest segment, passive components, makes passive electronic components for automotive braking systems, copiers, hearing aids, locomotives, and wireless phones. The KED Resale segment specializes in products made by Kyocera (which owns a controlling stake in the company), such as ceramic and tantalum capacitors that store, filter, and regulate electrical energy in electronic devices. The interconnect segment makes electronic connectors for automotive and medical electronics applications. Asia is its largest market.

Passive components generate more than two-thirds of AVX's sales. Inside that segment, Advanced Components have 27% of total revenue, followed by Tantalum Components, 26%, and Ceramic Components, 15%. The KED Resale segment accounts for 22% of the company's revenue with Interconnect products at 10%.

The company has manufacturing facilities in some 10 countries and research and development centers in the Czech Republic, France, Israel, the UK, and the US.

It generates about 42% of sales from Asia; the Americas and Europe each contribute about half of the remaining share. Europe was the only region to grow in fiscal 2012, the result of a strong European automotive market.

It markets to the consumer, military, industrial and medical sectors with a direct sales force, as well as through electronic component distributors and third-party manufacturers' representatives. Nearly 40% of sales are handled through distributors.

AVX's revenue fell 6% in 2015 (ended March) to $1.35 billion. A 10% drop in the sales of Tantalum Components reflected softer as customers carefully managed inventory. Sales of Ceramic Components increased 5%, demonstrating opportunities in the automotive, computer, industrial and telecommunications equipment markets. In KDP and KCD Resale, sales dropped 22% on lower demand from cellular device customers.

While revenue fell, net income leaped 77% higher to $225.8 million in 2015. The company attributed the increase to its focus on selling passive components with higher margins and lower manufacturing and overhead costs. AVX's cost of sales was about $140,000 million lower in 2015 than 2014.

Cash flow from operations turned around to close 2015 at $197 million from a negative cash flow of $79 million in 2014.

AVX has spent about $100 million in the past three years to improve its manufacturing facilities and reduce the cost. In regard to cost, the company has moved and expanded manufacturing operations in lower cost countries such as El Salvador, Malaysia, Mexico, and the Czech Republic.

AVX released a new product lined aimed the fast-growing market for automotive electronics. Its SV Series of capacitor could be used for applications such as sensors for fuel pump, anti-lock brake, water pump, motor control, and controls for hybrid vehicles.
