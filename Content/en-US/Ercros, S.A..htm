Chemistry is everything at Ercros. The Spain-based chemical giant comprises four divisions: basic chemicals (caustic soda, chlorine, phosphates), intermediate chemicals (formaldehyde, glues), pharmaceuticals (erythromycin, phosphomycin), and plastics (PVC compounds, vinyl chloride). Its major clients include Bayer , BASF , ENCE , and UBE . Exports to countries in Europe and Asia account for nearly half of the company's sales. Ercros was formed in 1989 by the merger of Spain's two largest private chemical companies: S.A. Cros and Unión Explosivos Río Tinto. Over the years, acquisitions have made Ercros the top non-multinational chemical company in Spain (excluding petrochemical firms).
