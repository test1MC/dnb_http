There's plenty to die for at Gibbs Die Casting. The company manufactures aluminum and magnesium die-castings, such as air conditioning compressor, commercial refrigeration, and engine and viscous clutch parts. Its Comac Machining Division provides precision machining and assembly work. The Audubon Tool Division uses fused deposition modeling prototyping equipment and CNC electrical discharge machines to build die-casting tools. Gibbs Engineering offers on-site product design, prototype models, and production tooling services. Gibbs Die Casting serves industries, such as automobile and appliance manufacturing and alternative energy. The comopany is owned by Koch Enterprises .

Based in Kentucky, Gibbs Die Casting sells its products worldwide and has plants in Brazil, China, Hungary, Korea, and the US.

At its Kentucky headquarters, Gibbs Die Casting maintains a manufacturing facility and technical center. Altogether, Gibbs Die Casting operates eight plants (five for die-casting, four for machining, and one die building facility) with a total of more than 1.2 million sq. ft. of floor space across the US, Brazil, Hungary, and China. Producing aluminum and magnesium automotive parts, the company performs its work using about 50 vertical and horizontal die casting machines that vary from 600 to 1,600 tons.

The company's divisions include Die Casting, Machining, Tool and Die, and Engineering.

Gibbs Die Casting offers vacuum ladled, high and ultra-high pressure die casting as an alternative to forging, permanent mold, and conventional die casting.

Its Comac Machining Division's services range from basic drill and tap operations to high-precision machining and assembly work.

The company's tool division is a captive die casting tool builder.

Gibbs Die Casting's engineering department provides a full range of technical services.

Gibbs Die Casting serves several sectors, such as lawn and garden, alternative energy, small engine, furniture, and automotive. Automobile manufacturers -- who are using more aluminum and magnesium parts to create lighter vehicles -- account for about 90% of sales. Indeed, Ford , General Motors , Fiat , and Toyota are top customers.

The company belongs to the Manufacturing Division of Koch Enterprises . The Manufacturing Division accounted for about 37% of the parent company's revenues in 2012.

The trend toward lighter automobiles has led manufacturers to replace iron and steel parts with aluminum parts. To keep up with the demand for lighter-weight aluminum die castings for the auto market, Gibbs Die Casting is expanding its Comac Machining Division with new production lines at the Tech and Synchronous plants. The investment will cost the company $23 million.

Koch Enterprises has owned Gibbs Die Casting since 1969.

Gibbs Die Casting was founded in 1965 by Bob and Nick Gibbs.
