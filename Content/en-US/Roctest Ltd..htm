Roctest designs, makes, and markets measuring instruments and sensors for civil engineering, industrial, energy, medical, and health and defense applications. Roctest's instrumentation products are used in such projects as dams, tunnels, and mines. Chief subsidiaries include Telemac (measuring and monitoring equipment and inductive sensors) and Fiso Technologies (fiber-optic sensors and signal conditioners). In 2006, the company acquired Smartec, which makes monitoring systems for civil engineering projects. Roctest products are sold in 75 countries. The company was acquired in late 2010 by Nova Metrix for a reported $22.2 million.
