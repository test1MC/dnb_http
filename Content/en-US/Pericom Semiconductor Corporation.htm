The scope of Pericom Semiconductor's product line covers analog and mixed-signal integrated circuits that communicate with other devices. The company specializes in chips that control the routing and transfer of data among a system's microprocessor, memory, and peripherals. Targeting the automotive, computer, networking, and telecom markets, Pericom offers various chip product lines: interfaces for data transfer, switches for digital and analog signals, clock management chips, and telecommunications switches and component bridges. Most sales come from customers located outside the US, primarily in the Asia/Pacific region. Pericom was acquired by Diodes in late 2015.

Pericom became part of Diodes when the $413 million deal closed in November 2015. The addition Pericoms broadened Diodes' analog products line and added mixed-signal connectivity to its mix. Diodes expected combined revenue to reach $1 billion a year with Pericom products selling to markets for cloud computing and server storage, embedded automotive information and entertainment systems, and Internet of Things applications.

As a fabless company, Pericom has relied on MagnaChip Semiconductor , GLOBALFOUNDRIES , SMIC , and TSMC to handle most of its wafer manufacturing operations. It also has used subcontractors to assemble, test, and package its chips.

Its frequency control devices incorporate quartz crystal resonators that are designed and manufactured by its vertically integrated subsidiaries, PSE Technology Corporation in Taiwan and PSE Technology (Shandong) Corporation in China. The plant in Taiwan is 75,000 sq. ft., while the Chinese one is much larger, at 345,000 sq. ft.

Pericom has operated IC design centers in California, China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan; it develops FCPs in California and Taiwan. Its sales offices are located in Hong Kong, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Taiwan, the UK, and the US.

Customers have included distributors, contract manufacturers and OEMs for computer, networking, telecommunications, embedded and consumer markets. Pericom sells its products through distributors ( Avnet accounts for around 20% of sales) and a worldwide sales force to tech heavyweights such as Alcatel-Lucent , Brocade , Cisco Systems , Dell , Hewlett-Packard , Intel , and Xerox . Customers in Asia account for the majority of sales.
