Paradise and fruitcakes are holiday traditions right up there with visiting in-laws. The company is the largest US maker and seller of glacé fruits, a mainstay ingredient in fruitcakes. It sells its dried and candied fruits to home bakers through supermarkets and other retail stores and, less so, to commercial bakers and foodservice operators under brands Paradise, Mor-Fruit, and White Swan (licensed), among several. Wal-Mart is one the company's biggest customers; most sales are generated during September, October, and November. The company also makes molded plastic packaging for its products and for third parties through subsidiary Paradise Plastics. Chairman and CEO Melvin Gordon control the company.

The company operates in two segments: Candied Fruit (68% of 2015 sales) and Molded Plastics (32%).

Further sweetening its revenues, Paradise's candied fruit business includes frozen strawberries. The ruby offerings are used by commercial and institutional customers, such as makers of preserves, dairies, and drink manufacturers.

The company sells directly to its food brokers and end customers. Paradise Plastics, a wholly owned subsidiary of Paradise, markets its plastics products to industries such as medical supplies, food processing, and aerospace. In 2015 Paradise shipped about 20% of candied fruit products to manufacturing bakers and other institutional users. The company also sells through supermarkets and other retail outlets, such as Kroger , Safeway , Food Lion , and Bi-Lo. In 2015, the company also sold through Amazon and other e-commerce sites.

Aqua Cal accounted for 16% of Paradise's 2015 revenues; Wal-Mart , 14%.

In 2015 net revenue decreased by 6% due to lower sales in Molded Plastics segment, attributable to a decision by a long term plastics customer within the housing market to transition production of an injection molding part to its own facility.

Candied Fruit segment sales decreased by 2% due to consolidation within the supermarket industry which limited the number of outlets for the company to sell its glacé fruit products.

Paradise's net income increased by 5% as the result of a decline in amortization expenses and the absence of interest expenses.

In 2015 operating cash flow decreased by 23% due change in inventories.

The company is focusing on expanding its marketing efforts though online selling. Pursuant to which, in 2015, the company began selling its glacé fruit products through Amazon.

Despite competition from major corporations like Dole Food and Seneca Foods coupled with high energy costs and changing consumer preferences, Paradise is enjoying some stability.

The company is led by a management team that not only holds significant ownership of Paradise but has a family relationship. Chairman Gordon is a cousin of vice-president Eugene Weiner, and father of president Randy Gordon and executive vice-president Mark Gordon.
