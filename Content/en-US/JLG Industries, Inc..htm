Need a boost? JLG Industries' lift equipment can provide that extra reach. Since 1969 when founder John L. Grove invented the first self-propelled boom lift, the company has been providing such products as aerial work platforms, telescopic material handlers, stock pickers (to access high shelving), and power deck trailers for ground-level loading of everything from heavy construction equipment to vending machines. Products are sold under such names as JLG, SkyTrak, and Lull. The company supplies customers in the government, equipment leasing, construction, maintenance, agricultural, industrial, and military markets. Equipment manufacturer Oshkosh Corp. has owned JLG since 2007.

The company maintains manufacturing facilities in the US, Australia, China, and Belgium. JLG also operates about 20 sales and service locations in the Americas, Asia, and Europe.

JLG is the primary business of Oshkosh's access equipment segment, which is the second largest of four Oshkosh segments, in terms of revenue. The segment also includes tow truck manufacturer Jerr-Dan . In addition to its own products (including JLG, SkyTrak, and Lull-branded offerings), JLG produces and sells a line of telehandlers under the Caterpillar brand, per a licensing agreement. It also makes a line of telehandlers through an agreement with German firm SAME Deutz-Fahr.

The company has its own sales force based in Europe and other regions; it also sells through a network of some 3,500 outlets of distributors and independent rental companies. JLG provides equipment financing and leasing options through partnerships with financial institutions.

The company was founded in 1969 by John L. Grove (hence JLG Industries) and Paul Shockey. The two purchased a small metal-fabrication shop to produce an alternative to ladders and construction scaffolding. The company pioneered elevating work platforms that use telescoping booms, called boom lifts -- its main product for two decades. In 1973 JLG began making scissor lifts, platforms that rise up to 50 feet on scissor-shaped supports. The firm established overseas offices in 1977.
