Biodex Medical Systems aims to have a Rolodex overflowing with customers for its health care equipment. The company designs and manufactures a variety of products for the diagnostic radiology market, as well as equipment used for physical rehabilitation, such as treadmills, elliptical cross-trainers, and balance testing equipment. Diagnostic supplies include tables, stretchers, and stools for medical imaging, as well as radiation shields, lung ventilation systems, and storage and dosing devices for nuclear medicine applications. The medical equipment manufacturer also offers technical support and clinical training for its equipment.

Biodex is headquartered in Shirley, New York, where its research and manufacturing operations are conducted. It also has international dealer locations in several countries, including Austria, Australia, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Guatemala, Egypt, France, Greece, Hong Kong, India, Iran, Israel, Italy, Japan, Korea, Lebanon, Malaysia, Mexico, the Netherlands, Poland, Spain, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, Switzerland, Taiwan, Turkey, the United Arab Emirates, and the UK.

To support sales of its balance systems and diagnostic units, Biodex provides concussion and balance screening services, as well as concussion management programs and fall risk conditioning.

Biodex's products are marketed through direct sales representatives and international distributors. The company also reaches customers through affiliations with organizations such as the American College of Sports Medicine, American Geriatrics Society, and Radiological Society of North America.

The company is working to expand its product offerings in core therapeutic areas and new fields of medicine. Each year, the company launches one or two new product enhancements. In 2014 Biodex expanded its shielding and storage product line by introducing the Clear-Lead Mobile Radiation Barrier and the Tabletop Shield. It also added the Balance System SD to its Fall Risk Screening Program. These products follow the company's 2013 rollout of a new Atomlab thyroid uptake system, which has enhanced versatility for nuclear medicine applications, and its new line of Ultrasound Table for sonographers. In 2012 it also launched a new series of surgical c-arm tables and an improved version of its semi-recumbent elliptical cross-training machine (BioStep 2).
