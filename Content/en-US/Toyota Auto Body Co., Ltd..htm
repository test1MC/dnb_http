Even a giant company like Toyota Motor can use a helping hand from time to time. Toyota Auto Body puts together bodies for passenger cars such as the Prius, as well as commercial, recreational, and special-purpose vehicles for its parent company, Toyota Motor. Toyota Auto Body also assembles minivans, compact trucks, and specially equipped vehicles. Most of its revenues come from the assembly of van bodies. The company has four plants and one development center in Japan, three in Indonesia and two in Thailand, and one each in Malaysia, Taiwan, and the US.

Japan accounted for 55% of parent company Toyota Motor 's revenues in 2014.

In 2015 the company began production of the completely redesigned Alphard and Vellfire. In 2014 it began production of the completely redesigned Voxy and Noah, and began production of the Esquire. That year it also completed building the Toyota Auto Body Kariya Fureai Park.

Founded in 1945, Toyota Auto Body was majority-owned by Toyota Motor until late 2011, when it became its parent's wholly owned subsidiary.
