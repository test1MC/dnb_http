OCI Company makes basic chemicals (soda ash, acetic acid, etc.), fine chemicals (ingredients for rubber and glyphosate), and petro- and coal chemicals (carbon black, pitch, benzene, and xylene). OCI maintains manufacturing plants primarily in South Korea, though also throughout Asia, Europe, and North America (through OCI Chemical .) The company was formed in 1959 as Oriental Chemical Industries and changed its name to DC Chemical upon its 2001 merger with Korea Steel Petrochemical. It went back to its past in 2009, choosing a former nickname as its new corporate identity, following a period of restructuring.
