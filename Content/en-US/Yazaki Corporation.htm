Since 1941 the Yazaki family has been wired. Yazaki Corp. was handed down from patriarch to sons and is considered to be the world's largest automotive wire harness (the central nervous system for vehicles) business, holding a one-third global market share. The company comprises more than 170 companies; more than half are outside of Japan (including Yazaki North America ) in over 40 countries. Its lineup includes electrical cables, meter and auto instruments, gas equipment, air-conditioning, and solar-powered systems. Yazaki sells chiefly to auto makers, and, to a lesser extent, electric power, gas, and general construction companies. It ranks among the top 100 companies receiving the most US patents.

Yazaki operates 237 sites in 44 countries. Japan generates about 40% of its total revenue each year. Other major markets include the Americas (29%), Asia and Oceania (16%), and Europe (17%).

The company sells its products to a variety of customers including automakers, electric power companies, taxi companies, gas distributors, and construction businesses.
