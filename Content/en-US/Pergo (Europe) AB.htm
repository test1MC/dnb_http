People needed more durable flooring. Ergo, Pergo. With a brand name that has become almost synonymous with laminate flooring, the company makes a variety of flooring products with the look of hardwoods, tile, stone, and other materials. Pergo flooring products are designed to be easily installed by the do-it-yourselfer and can be found at home improvement stores, other retailers, and wholesalers. The company sells its products in North America (about 60% of sales), Asia, Africa, and Europe. Pergo is a subsidiary of Mohawk Industries , one of the largest rug and tile makers in the world.

Pergo was bought by Mohawk Industries in early 2013 for $150 million in cash.

Founded as a Perstorp division, Pergo began making tabletop laminates in 1923. In 1977 Pergo introduced laminate flooring, the product for which it is now best known.
