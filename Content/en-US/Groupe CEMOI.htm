There's nothing wonky about this chocolate factory. Groupe CEMOI has been making chocolate in France since 1814 and is the country's leading chocolate maker. Its chocolate brands are Cemoi, Petit Ourson, and Pupier. The company also makes molded and panned chocolates, cocoa powder, cookies and wafers, sugar confectioneries, fruit jellies, sugared almonds, marshmallows, meringues, and industrial and semi-finished food ingredients. Its products are available in supermarkets and to the foodservice and food-manufacturing industries. The company has more than a dozen production plants. CEMOI is run by the family of founder Georges Poirrier.

Groupe CEMOI sells products for retail; conducts the wholesale supply of chocolate, powders, fillings and glazes, decorations, and cocoa liquor and butter; and offers private label contract manufacturing.

Groupe CEMOI has 14 of its 21 factories in France. It has one each in Germany, Poland, the Ivory Coast, and the UK. It also has 3 warehouses and 3 sales offices. France accounts for some two thirds of sales.

The company is based in Perpignan.

CEMOI sells 260,000 tonnes of chocolate every year, including sales of cocoa beans. It also buys 145,000 tons of cocoa beans a year. It sells its branded products direct to consumers, as well as wholesale goods and ingredients to restaurateurs, pastry chefs, and chocolate makers.

Customers include industrialists, craftsmen, and consumers.
