Sonus Networks has found a sound place in the voice infrastructure market. The company makes hardware and software that public network providers -- including long-distance carriers, ISPs, and cable operators -- use to provide voice and data communications services to their subscribers. Service providers use Sonus' switches, session border control (SBC) products, and related network software partly to transition from older circuit-based equipment to VoIP-based systems. The company also provides installation, support, and training services. Customers include AT&T , BT Group , Verizon , and Deutsche Telekom ; Sonus operates worldwide, but generates most of its sales in the US.

The US is the company's largest market, accounting for almost 71% of sales. Europe, the Middle East, and Africa (EMEA) and the Asia-Pacific region (primarily Japan) each contributed just about 13%-14% of revenue.

In 2015 Sonus Networks' largest customer, AT&T, accounted for 13% of sales, a percentage that has declined over the past four years. AT&T accounted for 20% of sales in 2012. Other telecom customers include CenturyLink , Verizon Communications , Vonage Holdings , and XO Communications .

Sonus Networks experienced a 16% drop in sales for 2015 to $249 million. Sales of older products flagged and a major customer reduced orders.

The company reported a loss o f $32 million, compared to a $17 million loss in 2014; it extended the company's streak of profitless years to nine years.

Cash flow from operations slowed to $20 million in 2015 from $30 million in the previous year.

Sonus Network is invested R&D to expand its communications network products to address emerging Unified Communications, Internet Protocol, and cloud-based enterprise and service providers. It seeks to expand and broaden its customer base by targeting the enterprise market for Session Internet Protocol trunking and access solutions. Targeted customers include those making the transition from a telecommunications network architecture to a Cloud-based software-defined network. Besides conducting its own R&D, Sonus Networks is adding to its technology portfolio through acquisitions.

In 2015 Sonus Networks acquired the software-defined networking (SDN) business from Treq Labs Inc. for $10 million. The acquisition included VellOS, a network-aware application that uses SDN technologies to provide cloud exchange services which service providers can pitch to enterprise customers. Sonus Networks released VellOS as a products in 2015.

In 2014 Sonus Networks acquired Performance Technologies , a provider of network communications products, for about $34 million. This deal brought an integrated, virtualized Diameter and SIP-based technology for multimedia services through mobile, cloud-based, real-time communications.
