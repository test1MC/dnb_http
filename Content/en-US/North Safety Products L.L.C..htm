North Safety Products' offerings are to be found in the north, but also in the south, east, and west. North Safety, doing business as North by Honeywell, makes and distributes protective equipment, including first-aid products, head gear, clothing, and footwear. With more than a dozen product lines, the company distributes its wares in North America, Europe, and Africa, and elsewhere, and is one of the largest international developers of new safety products. North Safety is an operating subsidiary of diversified manufacturer Honeywell International.

North Safety sells its products in North America, Europe, the Middles East, Africa, and the Asia/Pacific region.

A part of Honeywell Safety Products, the company has a number of segments and products. North Respiratory Protection's range of products includes disposable respirators; limited-use respirators; reusable air-purifying respirators; powered air-purifying respirators, SCBAs and supplied air systems. North Eye, Head, and Face Protection provides specialized, proven protection against chemical splash, flying objects, airborne particles, and harmful radiation. North Hand Protection offers gloves and sleeves protect against chemical and biological exposure and abrasions, as well as electrical risks and temperature. North Fall Protection offers line of fall prevention and protection equipment, professional training, and fall arrest systems, and offers protection from a variety of work-related injuries. North Protective Clothing provides a proven line of protective wear.

North Safety serves commercial customers and homeowners, health care organizations, OEMs, retailers, security monitoring service providers, as well as industrial, fire service, data centers and telecommunication companies, and the US federal government. The company offers its services for workers on job sites, factory floors, pipelines, and platforms as well as to firefighters and medical personnel.

With a greater emphasis on occupational health and safety in the workplace, there has been an increased demand for North Safety Products' offerings and services. In 2013 the company launched the new North brand GX-8 series of safety eyewear.

In 2012 North Safety Products signed a five-year co-branding agreement with MICROmega's subsidiary NOSA (Pty) Ltd, to sell 275,000 hard hats a year in the South African market.

North Safety was established in 1973 as the Norton Company, a manufacturer of abrasives (the company was renamed in 1994). In 1998 it became a subsidiary of Norcross Safety Products. Norcross was acquired by Honeywell International for some $1.2 billion in 2008.
