Through its subsidiaries -- Bearium Metals, Carondelet, Southern Centrifugal, Wisconsin Centrifugal, Wisconsin Investcast, and others -- MetalTek makes a slew of precision cast parts. The conglomerate specializes in non-ferrous, stainless steel, heat and corrosion resistant alloys; its alloy casting methods include centrifugal, continuous, investment, and sand casting. MetalTek parts are used in virtually every industry, from aerospace to mining and defense, petrochemical, and nuclear.

The company's 10 metal casting facilities serve customers in more than 35 countries.

MetalTek serves over 5,000 customers across a range of sectors including aerospace, defense, environmental, food processing, hydro, marine, mining, nuclear, petrochemical, power transfer, process equipment, pulp and paper, and steel mills.

MetalTek traces its origins to a company that was founded in 1890.
