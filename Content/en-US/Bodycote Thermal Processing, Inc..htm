Bodycote Thermal Processing knows how to turn up the heat. The company, a subsidiary of UK-based Bodycote International plc , heat-treats metal components to improve their mechanical properties, durability, and wear resistance. Bodycote Thermal Processing processes aircraft and auto parts, and oil field, mining, and agricultural equipment. Its heat-treating processes include hardening, tempering, and annealing. The company doesn't sell parts, but its customers are primarily parts suppliers to manufacturers in the commercial aerospace, automotive, defense, and consumer products industries.

Bodycote Thermal Processing operates about 45 facilities throughout the US and Canada, with plant locations in Alabama, Georgia, Kansas, Louisiana, North Carolina, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, and South Carolina.

Bodycote reported in 2012 that its North American operations enjoyed a 25% spike in revenues (of which 6% was organic and 19% was from acquisitions). The growth was driven by increased car and light truck related sales. It also enjoyed a 25% rise in general industrial sales.

The company fortified its southeastern US operations in 2012 when its parent acquired Carolina Commercial Heat Treating for $68 million. The deal also added to Bodycote's expertise in the off-road vehicle and heavy truck markets.
