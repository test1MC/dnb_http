Deltex Medical helps doctors keep an eye, or an ear, on their patients' blood flow. The company makes and markets CardioQ, a heart monitor used on critically ill patients and patients undergoing surgery. Via proprietary software and disposable probes inserted into the esophagus, the CardioQ system uses Doppler ultrasound technology to measure and monitor circulating blood volume to detect hypovolemia, or decreased blood volume, in patients. With such information in hand, doctors can administer fluid and drugs as necessary. Direct sales in the UK account for half of the company's sales. Sales in the US and Spain account for 15% of Deltex Medical's revenues.
