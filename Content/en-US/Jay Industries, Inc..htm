Jay Industries puts the pedal to the metal to sell its services. The family-owned company offers metal fabrication, plastic molding and assembly, and metal coating services through its manufacturing divisions. Jay Plastics makes injection molded plastic components, while other units provide metals processing, such as coil slitting, tube manufacturing and bending, forming/fabricating, metal stamping, machining, welding, and coating. Its joint venture with precision machining company CIGNYS offers robotic welding, CNC machine tools, electrical discharge machining (EDM), and water jet/laser cutting.

Jay Industries has five manufacturing locations in Alabama and Ohio. Its joint venture with CIGNYS operates through four locations in Saginaw, Michigan.

Jay's Plastics also makes ferrous assemblies that are stamped, fabricated, and welded, and it provides assembly and decoration of plastic parts, such as adhesive-backed emblems and medallions, as well as light mechanical assembly of plastic and metal parts. Its products include wheel trim, sputtered lighting, and painted interior component.

Its joint venture with CIGNYS combines that company's metalworking and engineering capabilities, as well as its advanced machine tools and fabricating equipment to the partnership, with Jay Industries' concept, design, prototype, and production expertise in metals and plastics. The combined synergies of these companies offers a vertically integrated manufacturing operation, one which provides a lower cost alternative to outsourcing.

Its competencies include coil slitting, tube manufacturing, tube bending, machining, fabricating, welding and coating, 3D modeling, wire and sinker EDM, and 5-axis CNC machining centers.

The company serves the aerospace, agricultural, automotive, lawn, and defense industries and other OEM markets.

The company's partnership with Michigan-based CIGNYS was established in mid-2010. The joint venture expanded and diversified Jay Industries' operations, which had been geared primarily to the automotive industry

Taylor Dise Company, which produced radiant gas heaters, got its start in 1917. Jay Plastics was created as a division in 1968 and became a separate entity in 1989 -- the organization became known as Jay Industries in 1995.
