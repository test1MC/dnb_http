Don't say we didn't Warn you about this company: Warn Industries' products pull a lot of weight with 4x4 enthusiasts and other outdoor types. The company manufactures a line of more than 600 aftermarket and original equipment components for 4x4 vehicles. Products include winches, industrial hoists, lighting, steps, and other accessories. Warn's customers include wholesale distributors and OEMs like Ford , General Motors , Kia Motors , Renault , Yamaha , and Polaris . The company also offers products for industrial and military vehicles. Warn Industries, founded in 1948 by Arthur Warn, was acquired by Dover Corporation in 2003.

Warn operates over 400,000 sq. ft. in two Oregon manufacturing facilities and a technical facility in Livonia, Michigan.

The company's Industrial Division markets electric and hydraulic winches and hoists to commercial, industrial, and severe-duty customers. Warn also has a line of utility winches and hoists targeting professional trade workers and do-it-yourself consumers who need powerful tools for pulling, hauling, dragging, or lifting.

With customers in over 65 countries, Warn focuses on three core markets: consumer aftermarket, industrial/commercial, and original equipment. Its products are distributed through distributors and OEMs like Ford, AM General, Chrysler, Kia, Land Rover, Oshkosh, Navistar, Arctic Cat, and Bombardier. In North America, Warn has a Red Letter dealer program comprising of retail outlets that provide consumers with its products and services.

Warn operates as a part of Dover's Engineered segment, which contributed 43% of its parent's total revenue in 2013.
