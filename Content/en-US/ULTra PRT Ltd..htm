No traffic, no driving, no waiting, no parking headaches, and no crowded trains -- that just about sums up the promise of ULTra PRT. The commuter system developer supplies four-person cars to carry passengers non-stop any place on a guideway network. The cars are powered by a battery pack utilizing an average of two kilowatts of power. With a maximum speed of 40kph/25mph, they can accommodate wheelchairs and luggage in addition to passengers. Founded in 1995, the company does business as ULTra Global and its first three letters stand for "Urban Light Transport."

ULTra PRT grows its business by extending its reach into other global markets. In addition to a PRT system that is expected to be built in Amritsar in Northern India, the company is also doing a detailed feasibility study for extending its products into the Brazilian city of Florianopolis and across Malaysia, Taiwan, Thailand, Australia and Saudi Arabia.
