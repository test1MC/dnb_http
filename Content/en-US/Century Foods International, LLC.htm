Century Foods International knows where there's swill, there's a whey. The company provides dairy and vegetable proteins and nutraceuticals used for muscle-building and weight-loss powders, bars, and beverages. Century Foods' clients include food manufacturers and heath, nutritional supplement, and sports companies. The company serves customers in more than 45 countries. Century Foods provides additional services, such as development, manufacturing, testing, and packaging, from its production plants located in central Wisconsin. It provides packaging in pouches, jars, stick packs, and pails. The company, founded in 1991, is a division of Hormel Foods .

Hormel paid about $115 million to purchase Century Foods International from the family of its late founder, Michael Otten, in 2003. Hormel added the company to its Specialty Foods group.

Based in Wisconsin, Century Foods serves customers in more than 45 countries.

Century Foods serves several sectors, including the food and beverage industry and the food, sports, health, and nutritional supplement industries. Customers include some of the nation's top-selling branded food and beverage products.

The company sells its products through grocery stores and retailers. Wal-Mart is its largest customer, representing 14% of its parent's revenue in fiscal 2013. The behemoth retailer is a customer across all company segments.

The company, which boasts four manufacturing plants with a combined 400,000 sq. ft., operates as part of Hormel's Specialty Foods segment alongside Diamond Crystal Brands and Hormel Specialty Products. The segment contributed about 11% of the parent company's fiscal 2013 revenue. The US accounted for 94% of Hormel's overall revenue in 2013.

Century Foods maintains a 20,000-sq.-ft. research and development technical center to support its business as a custom private labeler of nutritional products.

Through a focus on research and development efforts, Century Foods works to extend the reach of its existing brands and expand into new branded products.
