Automatically powerful, ABB Holdings works like a robot. Operating as the UK division of Swiss firm ABB , the company makes electrical distribution components and builds power plants and substations through its power products and power systems divisions, respectively. Its discrete automation and motion division makes drives, motors, generators, and robotics. Optimizing operations for oil, gas, power, chemical, and pharmaceutical plants falls under the banner of process automation. The low voltage products division makes low-voltage circuit breakers, switches, and other products for protection from electrical overload. With more than 20 locations throughout the UK, ABB Holdings was formed in 1988.

Based in Warrington, the UK division of ABB operates some 20 locations nationwide.

As part of its business, ABB Holdings operates several divisions: power products, power systems, low voltage products, and discrete automation and motion.

The UK unit caters to both utility and industry customers and works to boost their performance while limiting the impact their businesses make on the environment.

In mid-2013 parent ABB purchased Dynamotive Ltd., based in Coalville, UK, to strengthen its service portfolio in low- and medium-voltage motors and drives. Dynamotive has expertise in designing, commissioning, and upgrading systems of drives, controls, and motors for industrial and marine applications and automotive test rigs. As a result, ABB is banking on increased sales of complete industrial motion packages, which include drives, motors, controls, and related services.
