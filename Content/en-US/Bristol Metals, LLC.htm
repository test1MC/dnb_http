Pursuing more than a pipe dream since 1946, Bristol Metals is the largest US manufacturer of stainless steel welded pipe. Its pipe is also made from corrosion-resistant nickel alloy and titanium. The line ranges from 1/2 to 120 inches in diameter, and 36 inches to 48-feet in length. Bristol grew in 2009 with the acquisition of carbon pipe maker Ram-Fab. Bristol serves diverse arenas -- petrochemical, mining, power (including nuclear), brewery, food processing, and pulp and paper. Its manufacture of large alloy pipe, rare among domestic pipe makers, enables the company to also enter markets for waste water treatment, liquid natural gas, and scrubbers for power industries. The company is a subsidiary of Synalloy .
