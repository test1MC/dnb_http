Natural plastics and a side of optical fiber furnaces mean big business for Biome Technologies. The company operates two units: RF Technologies makes and distributes graphite, zirconia, shaping, and soft and specialty glass furnaces. Engineering marvels, the furnaces, which run at a blistering 2200 degrees C, can produce enough fiber annually to circle the world more than 20 times. Another unit, Biome Bioplastics, produces plastics products (dubbed bioplastics) derived from natural, biodegradable materials. Product applications range from flexible films to compression-molded containers.

The US generates 42% of the company's net sales, while the UK brings in 17%. Other major markets include Poland (12%), China (10%), Germany (5%), and India (5%).

Supported by a network of sales agents and a direct team, the company's products cater primarily to the food, horticulture, and electronics markets.

In 2013 Biome's overall revenue decreased by 65% due to less revenue from both its Biome Bioplastics and RF Technologies divisions. Biome Bioplastics' revenue nosedived 71% due to the company's refocus away from lower-margin, single-use plastic bag markets. RF Technologies revenues fell by 57% in 2013 as turbulence in the Chinese fibre optic market continued, coupled with a drop in furnace orders in both China and also India due to weaker demand.
