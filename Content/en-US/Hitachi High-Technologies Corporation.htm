Hitachi gets high techy with Hitachi High-Tech, which makes manufacturing equipment used in semiconductor production, such as chip mounters and etching and inspection systems; analytical and clinical instruments, such as electron microscopes and DNA sequencers; manufacturing equipment for flat-panel displays (FPD), liquid crystal displays (LCD), and hard disks; and metrology and inspection equipment. The company also sells steel, plastics, silicon wafers, fine chemicals, optical components, and automotive-related devices and materials. Hitachi High-Tech generates 42% of its sales in Japan. Hitachi owns nearly 52% of the company's stock.

Hitachi High-Tech operate in five segments: Electronic Device Systems, semiconductor and LCD manufacturing equipment; Fine Technology Systems, technology that supports people and society; Science and Medical Systems, microscopes, analytical instruments; Industrial and IT Systems, chip mounters; and Advanced Industrial Products, steel and plastics, silicon wafers, optical components, and automotive-related devices and materials.

Advanced Industrial is the biggest segment, account for 42% of revenue, followed by Science and Medical Systems, 23%, Electronic Device Systems, 18%, Industrial and IT Systems, 14%, and Fine Technology, 3%.

Hitachi High-Tech does its biggest business at home with 42% of sales in Japan. The next biggest markets are Asia (excluding China), 18%, North America 14%, China, 13%, and Europe, 12%.

In 2014, revenue increased 11% and net income jumped 48% higher compared to 2013. Sales increased in all segments but for Industrial and IT systems, which was flat, and in all geographic areas. Growth was particularly strong in North America (up 36%).

Hitachi High-Tech is focusing on three areas for growth: Biotechnology and Healthcare, Social Infrastructure, and Advanced Industrial Systems and is allocating money and other resources to develop products for those markets. The company also aims to pump up overseas sales to account for two-thirds of revenue by 2020 (from 58% in 2014). As part of its international focus, Hitachi High-Tech opened a subsidiary in Russia in 2014.
