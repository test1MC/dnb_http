MinebeaMitsumi's products bear many things. The company is one of the world's leading makers of miniature ball bearings and high-precision components. It produces about 8,500 varieties of bearings and electronic devices for use by telecommunications, aerospace, automotive, and household electrical appliance industries. In addition to a diversity of bearings, MinebeaMitsumi's lineup offers machinery parts and ball-bearing spindle motors for disk drives, keyboards, and other computer-related hardware. The company's integrated manufacturing systems, ultra precision machining, and mass-production technologies reach through almost 50 subsidiaries, primarily in Asia. In 2017 Minebea acquired Mitsumi Electric and took its new name.

The company has its headquarters in Tokyo. The company has its sales offices in Japan, Asia, North and South America, and Europe. China contributes 32% of its net revenue. Other major markets include Japan (19%), Thailand (13%), Europe (11%), the US (10%), and others (15%).

MinebeaMitsumi's operations are divided across several segments. The machined components segment (36% of net sales) primarily manufactures miniature and small-sized ball bearings, and rod-end and spherical bearings, as well as fasteners and pivot assemblies.

The electronic devices and components segment (58%) primarily makes light-emitting diode (LED) backlights for liquid crystal displays (LCDs), spindle motors for hard disk drives (HDDs), information motors such as stepping motors, brushless DC motors, brush DC motors, fan motors, as well as measuring components.

Another segment (6%) focuses on dies and parts produced in-house.

MinebeaMitsumi's products cater to the key markets of PCs and peripherals (accounting for 25% of net sales), office automation and telecommunications equipment (24%), automotive (13%), aerospace (9%) and household electrical appliances (7%). Many of its clients are manufacturers of these products.

MinebeaMitsumi's revenues surged 32% from 2013 to 2014 due to growth across all its segments. Machined components sales increased in 2014 amid the global economic recovery and a higher operating rate that brought unit manufacturing costs down in addition to the positive effect of exchange rates.

In the electronic devices and components segment, growing demand in the smartphone market in 2014 buoyed sales of its ultra-thin light guide plates for high-end product applications as its customer base and market share expanded. HDD spindle motors and information motors also saw spikes in sales.

Throughout 2014 Minebea engaged in an aggressive expansion strategy and re-structural initiatives with an eye fixed on boosting growth in product categories where demand is expected to rise.

In 2015 the company acquired German measuring component manufacturer Sartorius Mechatronics T&H GmbH. With production facilities in Germany, China, and India, Sartorius MTH is a manufacturer of industrial measuring instruments and process inspection equipment that includes product lines from load cell, industrial scale, and process instrument to detection devices as well as an aftermarket business providing modifications and adjustments, repair, and process optimization services worldwide.
