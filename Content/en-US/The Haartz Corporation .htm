When weather conditions make you put up the top on your convertible, Haartz might be helping keep you dry. The company manufactures coated fabrics that are used in automotive convertible tops, as well as moldable composite products used in interior trim applications. Haartz also makes automotive vinyl roof treatments made of PVC and polyester fabric. The company's fabrics are also used for marine applications, rood gears, tonneau covers for pickup truck beds, nose covers, and spare tire covers. Haartz has facilities in Asia, Europe, and North America.

The company operates locations in the US (Acton, Massachusetts, and Bloomfield Hills and Detroit, Michigan); Beijing, China; Chakan and Gurgaon, India; Mannheim, Germany; and Tokyo, Japan.

Moving into the fast-growing Indian market, in 2012 the company launched Haartz Automotive India PVT in collaboration with Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation. It set up a manufacturing facility in Chakan (Pune) to complement existing sales offices in Gurgaon (Delhi) and Hinjewadi (Pune), India's major automotive hubs.

Haartz was formed in 1907.
