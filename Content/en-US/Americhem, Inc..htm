You know all those pretty green and blue bottles at the corner store? Americhem's responsible for that. The Ohio-based additives and dispersions company makes color and additive solutions used primarily in PET (polyethylene terephthalate) bottles used by soda and beer makers. Americhem's color and additives master batches also are used by the automotive, construction, synthetic fibers, packaging, and plastic materials industries. The company serves customers around the globe. Americhem ranks as one of North America's 30 largest makers of compounds and concentrates and specializes in color and additive masterbatches and single pigment dispersions.

Most of Americhem's operating locations are in the US, including the main headquarters, manufacturing, and R&D facility in Ohio. It operates four additional manufacturing locations in Georgia, North Carolina (2), and Texas, as well as one plant in the UK and one in China. The company also has sales offices in Mexico and Luxembourg.

The company serves the automotive fluids, food and beverage, health and beauty products, household cleaners and detergents, pharmaceuticals, and commercial and industrial container markets. Its technical capabilities include central research, design, testing, and packaging services.

Americhem uses direct sales representatives to promote its products. It also markets select products on behalf of other manufacturers. To expand its third-party offerings, the company entered an agreement to distribute flame retardant polymers on behalf of FRX Polymers in 2012.

Americhem is increasing its manufacturing capacity to keep pace with customer demand. In 2013 it added a new production line at its Liberty, North Carolina plant capable of manufacturing masterbatches with a high percentage of fillers and other additives for a variety of end-product uses.

The company also grows by introducing product enhancements. That year Americhem and LIFE Material Technologies teamed up to offer antimicrobial products for use in synthetic fibers and plastics applications.

In 2013 the company also unveiled its breakthrough mBrace softening additive. mBrace is a softening technology that can be combined with color and other additives to provide a cost-effective, multi-attribute masterbatch. In 2012 Americhem launched its nDuramax line of UV stabilizers to improve the durability and lifespan of products exposed to the sun.

In 2012 Americhem introduced four new color families for 2013 and 2014 and installed a new production line at its plant in Mansfield, Texas.

At the close of 2012 Americhem completed its acquisition of Infinity Compounding, a maker of thermoplastics based in New Jersey. The purchase expands the company's offerings to include highly engineered thermoplastics including specialty filled and reinforced compounds, which are used in electronics, consumer product, and industrial industries. In addition to broadening Americhem's technologies and the markets its serves, the purchase expands the company's global reach.

In 2011 the company expanded its facilities in Concord, North Carolina, adding three new extrusion lines and employing 25 additional workers. It also completed construction of a new manufacturing plant in Liberty, North Carolina, that year.

Americhem was founded in 1941 as a manufacturer's representative selling fillers and additives to the rubber industry. In 1959, it began manufacturing color concentrates.
