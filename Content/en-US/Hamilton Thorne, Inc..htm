If you ever need to analyze mouse sperm, give Hamilton Thorne a call. The company makes computer-assisted sperm analysis (CASA) systems used for human and veterinary fertility research. It also makes laser systems and instruments that attach to microscopes to assist researchers in the laboratory. The ZILOS-tk laser is used for biopsying embryos, and the XYClone is used in animal and stem cell research. Hamilton Thorne sells its products to physicians, medical facilities, and health care companies worldwide through distributors and resellers. Clients have included Regeneron Pharmaceuticals . The company was founded by CEO Meg Spencer (née Hamilton) in 2001. Louisa Spencer owns about 16% of the company.
