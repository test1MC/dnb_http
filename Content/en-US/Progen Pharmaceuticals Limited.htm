Shhhh... TBG Diagnostics (formerly Progen Pharmaceuticals) hopes that by silencing cancer genes, they can put the disease to sleep. But if it does show up, the company also has drug candidates in development to address certain cancers. Its lead candidate, PI-166, targets liver cancer, while its PG11047 candidate induces cell death in tumors. The company's small-molecule compounds are based on interrupting the way heparan sulfate binds with proteins, a process that is implicated in tumor growth and other diseases. TBG's subsidiary PharmaSynth provides contract pharmaceutical development and biopharmaceutical manufacturing services.
