Roper Rhodes is really into Bath's baths. Bath-based Roper Rhodes offers some 1,500 bathroom fittings and furniture items, The company's products portfolio ranges from traditional to contemporary and includes vanities, cabinets, faucets, mirrors, and toilet seats. Roper Rhodes counts on a growing group of customers that shops through more than 2,000 do-it-yourself stores, major builders, plumbing merchants, department stores, and independent retailers. The bathroom fixtures company designs its products in-house and markets them in the UK and Republic of Ireland. Roper Rhodes leverages manufacturers in China, Spain, and Taiwan to produce its goods.

The company operates in the UK and Ireland.

Roper Rhode's range incorporates contemporary and classic designs of bathroom furniture, sanitary ware, showers, taps, accessories, cabinets mirrors, bath panels and toilet seats in a wide variety of colors, materials and finishes.

The company advertises through its brochures and sells through shops.

Its product development program ensures that it is continually updating and refining its product range to reflect changes in demand and style.

In 2015 Roper Rhodes was appointed as the Principal Partner of the Holburne Museum's Canaletto: Celebrating Britain exhibition, which opened in June. Already a corporate member of the museum (located in Bath), this latest partnership represents a substantial investment to enable the museum to dedicate its top floor to the Venetian artist.

Looking to offer its customers innovative products to stay one step ahead of market rivals, Roper Rhodes in 2012 rolled out the first multi-media bathroom mirror in the UK. Marketed under the Audio-Image brand name, the mirror incorporates a variety of multi-media inputs, including FM radio reception, and media device storage.

The company was founded by chairman Brian Roper in 1979
