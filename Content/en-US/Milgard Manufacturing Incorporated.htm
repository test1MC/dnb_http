You won't hear Milgard Manufacturing say they don't do windows. Also known as Milgard Windows & Doors, the company makes windows and patio doors for the new home construction and home improvement markets. Its offerings include aluminum, vinyl, and wood clad windows, as well as wooden French doors and vinyl, fiberglass, and aluminum sliding doors. Milgard's wares are mostly sold through independent dealers to consumers, architects, and contractors. Brand names include Griffin, Cambrian, Premier, and Duraflex. Milgard is owned by home improvement products group Masco Corporation .

Milgard operates manufacturing and assembly locations throughout the western US, where it does most of its business through 12 service facilities and customer care centers. It is also active in Canada and Mexico.

The company has 3,700 dealer locations and sells to wholesalers and production and custom homebuilders. It also caters to the repair and remodeling markets and sells products through lumber yards and home centers.

Milgard operates under its parent's "other specialty products" segment, which generated 8% of total sales in 2013. This segment's revenues increased in 2013 primarily due to an uptick in sales volumes of windows in western US markets, a more favorable product mix, and increased selling prices.

Like other home products manufacturers, Milgard's revenue tends to follow the barometer of the nation's economy. Over the years, the company's sales have fluctuated as construction has slowed down, but Milgard has held strong by introducing new products and innovations. It has introduced a line of energy-efficient windows, allowing buyers to take advantage of federal energy tax credits. The company also introduced two new styles for its Style Line Series vinyl windows in 2012.

Maurice Milgard, Jr., and his son Gary founded Milgard as a glass shop in 1958. Masco acquired the firm from the Milgard family in 2001.
