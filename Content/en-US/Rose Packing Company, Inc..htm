They pack it away at Rose. The suburban Chicago meat-packing business processes fresh, frozen, and smoked pork, beef, and turkey products for retail food outlets, food service suppliers, and the military in the US and abroad. The Rose Packing Company's meat products include Canadian bacon, sausage (turkey, pork, bratwurst, Polish, Italian, chorizo), ham, pizza toppings, meatballs, various pork cuts, and boneless turkey breasts, all of which are sold under the Rose brand name. Rose operates a meat packing plant in Chicago that measures more than 200,000 square feet. The Barrington, Illinois-based company was founded by Louis Rose in 1924.

In the US, the meat-packing industry accounts for some $165 billion. Within the meat-packing industry, Rose Packing contends with such competitors like Hormel Foods , Smithfield Foods , and Pilgrim's Pride .

Rose Packing joined with Guinness, a brand of Diageo , to produce its brand of Guinness Beer Brats in 2011. This agreement enables Rose Packing to further expand its bratwurst line of products.
