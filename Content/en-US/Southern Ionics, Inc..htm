Neither iconic or ironic, Southern Ionics (SII for short) simply gets on with the job of manufacturing a number of specialty and basic chemicals, beginning with aluminum-based products (for the paper and water treatment industries) to zirconium (for makers of titanium dioxide -- the most widely used white pigment). Its core markets include manufacturers of cement, molecular sieves, pharmaceuticals, refinery and automotive catalysts, and zeolites. SII has about 10 manufacturing plants spread throughout the southeast US and Texas. President and CEO Milton Sundbeck owns Southern Ionics.

The company has its headquarters in West Point, Mississippi. It has its presence in Augusta, Georgia; Baton Rouge, Louisiana; Baton Rouge-North, Louisiana; Calhoun, Tennessee; Chickasaw, Alabama; Williamsport, Maryland; Columbus, Mississippi; Lake Charles, Louisiana; Pasadena, Texas; Tuscaloosa, Alabama; West Point, Mississippi; and Williamsport, Maryland.

Southern Ionics is the South's largest producer of sulfur solution products, including sodium bisulfite, sodium sulfite, and sodium thiosulfate. The company makes a high purity aqua ammonia solution called AQUA-CAT from anhydrous ammonia and de-mineralized water.

SII is the largest regional manufacturer of magnesium sulfate solutions. The company has developed magnesium-based products for the paper industry to protect fiber during oxygen delignification and to improve peroxide efficiency. Other magnesium products are used in the pharmaceutical industry.

The company's markets include Cement, Molecular Sieves, Pharmaceuticals, Pulp and Paper, Refinery and Automotive Catalysts, Titanium Dioxide, Water and Wastewater Treatment, and Zeolites.
