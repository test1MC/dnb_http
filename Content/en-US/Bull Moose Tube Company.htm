Although its namesake draws from General Bullmoose, a character in Al Capps' Li'l Abner comic strip, Bull Moose Tube is serious about its role in the tubular market. The company manufactures mechanical and structural tubes, and sprinkler pipe. It offers one of the industry's longest lists of sizes for myriad ends; as-welded mechanical tubing is used in greenhouses to handrails, furniture parts, and roller conveyors. Eddy brand sprinkler pipe is integral to fire protection systems, and its hollow structural sections tubing is preferred for building construction, outdoor lighting, and agricultural equipment. Bull Moose Tube is a subsidiary of the diversified UK Caparo Group , which took over the business in 1988.

Headquartered in Chesterfield, Missouri, the company has seven manufacturing plants in Arizona, Illinois, Indiana, Missouri, Ohio, and Georgia. It also has an international location in Ontario, Canada, and it operates about 20 mills in these seven locations.

Bull Moose Tube sells its products through its centralized sales and support personnel. Some applications for its tubing include construction, transportation, agricultural, fire protection, garden furniture, automotive, sports equipment and many other engineering and household products.

Bull Moose Tube's strategy for growth includes the continuous improvement and refining of its systems, processes, and procedures. The company has extended its size range by bringing in products that it doesn't make but can resell at a profit.
