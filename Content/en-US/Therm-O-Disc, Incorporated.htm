Some like it hot, but in reality most like it moderate. Therm-O-Disc, a subsidiary of Emerson Electric , keeps indoor temperatures steady with sensor, switch, and control products for air-conditioning and heating systems, home appliances, cars, and electronics. Products include thermostats, toggle and snap-action switches, time delay relays, and thermal cutoffs. Therm-O-Disc also makes products for various automotive applications (parking brakes, sunroof and wiper controls, seat heaters, and sliding doors). Parts are supplied directly to original equipment manufacturers. Established in 1947, Therm-O-Disc has operations throughout North America and in Asia, Mexico, the Netherlands, South America, and the UK.

Based in Ohio, Therm-O-Disc has offices in the US, China, Canada, Mexico, the Netherlands, the UK, Hong Kong, Japan, South America, India, and Korea.

Therm-O-Disc is a subsidiary of Emerson Electric and part of its Emerson Appliance Solutions and Emerson Climate Technologies businesses. It's recognized globally for its expertise in designing and making temperature sensors and controls.

The company serves several sectors, such as HVAC, appliance, automotive, and electronics. Its customer groups include consumer, climate, oil and gas, power and equipment, and transportation. Therm-O-Disc sells its products through North American distributors and wholesale distributors, as well as through wholesalers such as Grainger and Johnstone Supply .

Therm-O-Disc's cost-cutting efforts during the economic downturn, which included shuttering its nearly half-century-old St. Thomas plant in Canada and shifting production of thermal controls to its Mansfield, Ohio, plant and to facilities in Mexico, helped parent Emerson perform well during the recession.

More recently, Therm-O-Disc has focused on investing in technology and international expansion. Its Climate Technology segment has logged sales gains, thanks to stimulus programs that supported higher efficiency standards. The company performs its extensive performance and regulatory agency testing in its Mansfield, Ohio, and Bao'an China engineering centers.
