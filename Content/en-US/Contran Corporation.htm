Contran is a holding company that owns stakes of varying sizes in subsidiaries, including the largest, Valhi, Inc. (a publicly traded company about 93% controlled by Contran). Primarily through Valhi, the company has stakes in diversified operations in chemicals ( NL Industries and Kronos Worldwide ); waste management (Waste Control Specialists); and computer support systems, precision ball bearing slides, and security products ( CompX International ). Keystone Consolidated Industries , a maker of fencing and wire products, is also a wholly owned subsidiary of Contran. Contran is controlled by the family of late chairman Harold Simmons.

Contran's Valhi unit makes and sells titanium dioxide pigments in North America and Europe. The unit generated about 55% of its revenues from the US in 2013.

Valhi's operations include three primary segments: chemicals, component products, and waste management.

Kronos produces the chemicals, titanium dioxide pigments used in a range of applications that include coatings, plastics, paper, food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals. Kronos is the leading marketer of titanium dioxide in several countries, holding an estimated 14% share of worldwide sales.

Valhi's interests in component products are through CompX . The component products are used in office furniture, transportation, tool storage, and other industries. CompX also makes stainless steel exhaust systems for the boat industry.

Contran's waste management holdings are in Texas-based Waste ControlSpecialists (WCS). The company operates a West Texas plant to process, treat, store, and dispose of hazardous, toxic, and certain types of low-level radioactive waste.

Keystone Consolidated Industries makes steel fabricated wire products, industrial wire, and carbon steel rod, which it sells to the agricultural, industrial, construction, OEM, and retail consumer markets.

To raise cash to pay down debt and reinvest in its core businesses in 2013 Contran sold its majority-owned subsidiary Titanium Metals (the largest independent titanium manufacturer in the US), to Precision Castparts for $2.9 billion.

In 2013 Contran merged Keystone Consolidated Industries with Contran unit KYCN Acquisition Corporation. Keystone, the surviving company, became a wholly-owned subsidiary of Contran.

Trusts benefiting late chairman Harold Simmons' family (with Simmons as the sole trustee) own almost all the shares of Contran.

Contran was founded in 1944.
