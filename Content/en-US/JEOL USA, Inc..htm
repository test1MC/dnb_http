Science is instrumental to the work at JEOL USA. The company distributes analytical instruments, electron optics, and semiconductor production equipment. Its products include electron microscopes, mass spectrometers, sample preparation equipment, and silicon wafer inspection equipment. JEOL USA was founded as the US sales arm of Japan-based scientific instrument maker JEOL Ltd. in 1962. It provides sales and support of JEOL products throughout the Americas. Researchers at Boston College , Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory , The University of Southern California , and Wesleyan University , among other institutions, use JEOL equipment.

JEOL USA has about a dozen regional service centers for product support. It's one of its parent company's nearly 20 international subsidiaries. In addition to serving the US market, it oversees the sale and support of JEOL products in Canada, Mexico, and South America.

Its Peabody, Massachusetts headquarters is home to an Electron Optics and Analytical Instruments demonstration facility, the JEOL Institute, the main parts depot and service center for the western hemisphere, and a manufacturing and software development facility. It has demonstration and applications support centers in Peabody and Pleasanton, California.

The federal government is one of JEOL USA's top clients. In 2014 the company secured a contract with the US Dept. of Energy in Pittsburgh.

In order to keep pace with technological advancements and bring new products to market in a timely fashion, JEOL USA maintains product development partnerships with optical equipment maker TOPCON , the Massachusetts Institute of Technology , and microscopy specialist The McCrone Group.

JEOL is an acronym that stands for Japan Electron Optics Laboratory.
