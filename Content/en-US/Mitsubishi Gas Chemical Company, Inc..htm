Mitsubishi Gas Chemical (MGC) makes chemicals through four major units. Its Natural Gas Chemicals segment primarily produces methanol, ammonia, and their derivatives. Its Aromatics Chemicals division produces xylenes and nylon products that used as raw materials in the manufacture of plastics, coatings, and adhesives. The Specialty Chemicals unit makes hydrogen peroxide and plastic resins. Through its Information & Advanced Materials branch, MGC also manufactures materials such as plastics and circuit boards for electronics manufacturers. The company was formed in 1971 when Japan Gas Chemical merged with Mitsubishi Edogawa Chemical.
