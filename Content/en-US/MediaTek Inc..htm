MediaTek designs systems-on-a-chip (SoC) for wireless communication, HDTV and DVDs, and optical storage products. Its chips are found in cell phones, TVs, DVD players, and other consumer electronics. MediaTek is the largest supplier of chips for wireless handsets in China and Taiwan. The fabless semiconductor company outsources production of its devices to contract semiconductor manufacturers including Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) and United Microelectronics Corporation (UMC). MediaTek makes 90% of its sales to markets in Asia outside its home country, Taiwan. The company was spun off from UMC in 1997.

MediaTek's are used in a range of mobile phones from entry-level feature phone to high-end smart phones. Its chips run function such as Bluetooth, networking, GPS, NFC, and wireless charging.

In TVs, MediaTek's decoder chips and demodulator chips are used to receive and decode digital TV signals from satellite, terrestrial or cable for video as well as video-on-demand and video phone over wired and wireless connections.

MediaTek is based in Taiwan and has sales and research subsidiaries in Singapore, Mainland China, India, US, Japan, Korea, Denmark, England, Finland, Sweden, France, Holland and Dubai. In 2015, revenue from Asia accounted for 90% of the total sales, while Taiwan accounted for 5%.

MediaTek's sales were steady in 2015 from 2014 while net income dropped 44% on higher operating and financing costs. Cash flow from operations dropped 44% in 2015 from 2014.

MediaTek spends some 22% of its revenue each year on research and development for new products. The company also makes acquisitions to bring new technologies into its fold.

MediaTek made three acquisitions in 2015 to bolster its portfolio of media chip designs.

It bought Alpha Imaging Technology Corp. , which specializes in image signal processing technology and multimedia processors for portable devices and consumer markets.

In buying Chingis Tech, MediaTek got a designer of specialty memory IC products. Its chips are used in digital consumer, computer peripheral, wireless communication, networking, and industrial controls.

With power management chipmaker Richtek Technology Corp ., MediaTek expanded into more geographic markets.
