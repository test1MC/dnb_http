Meiji Co. is a leading Japanese dairy products company. Formerly Meiji Dairies, it makes infant formulas, yogurt, ice cream, cheese, butter, and dairy-based drinks, but more than half of its of sales come from its home-delivery milk service. The company also makes cheese-heavy frozen foods such as pizza, and its VAAM sports and fitness drink which contains endurance-enhancing amino acids found in hornet saliva. Meiji Co. introduced Bulgarian-style yogurt to Japan and continues to develop probiotic dairy products. The company merged with Japanese confectionery maker, Meiji Seika Kaisha, to form Meiji Holdings .

Meiji sells its products in about 50 countries, mostly in Asia. It has seven sales offices and production plants in Japan.

Post-merger, the company's strategy is to develop new products to serve the changing demographics of the Japanese population (low birth-rate and consequent increase in the age of the population). In addition, due to the lessening of family ties and continued urbanization of the populace, it will develop products that reflect the changing life-styles of the Japanese. It also hopes utilize the synergies of combined business resources, manufacturing technologies, quality analysis, pharmaceutical research, marketing techniques, and diverse sales channels.

The company is also working to expand products for China, the world's largest market. In 2014 it opened a new ice cream plant in Guangzhou to produce ice cream and frozen novelties, some based on Japanese favorites (sweet red bean and green tea), others made for Chinese consumers (sweetened condensed milk and ice cream fruit cups.)
