The wheels are turning and the trucks are truckin' at Bravo Sports. The company makes skateboard and in-line skate accessories such as boards, wheels, bearings, in-line boots, and apparel. Brand names include Kryptonics (skateboarding), Hyper (in-line skating), and Senate (skateboarding). The Kryptonics name has been around since 1965 when Kryptonics Inc. first began making plastic wheels and experimenting with urethane wheel technology. Bravo Sports also licenses character brands from Nickelodeon , Mattel , and others for its products. In addition, the company makes outdoor products such as instant canopies, chairs, trampolines, and trampoline enclosures.

Bravo Sports provides premium recreational and sporting goods at key retail price points. Its major licenses include Marvel , Nickelodeon , Disney /Pixar, John Deere , Element, Airzone, Variflex , Pulse Performance Products, Kryptonics, Darkstar , Speed Demons, X Games, Static, Ten-Eighty, and Hyper.

Some of the products made by Bravo Sports include canopies, shaded pet beds, skateboards, scooters, electric scooters, skates, ramps, rails, and trampolines.

The company maintains a varied set of key distributors.

Bravo Sports constantly strives to better match consumer value to retail offers through a calculated mix of brand equity and inventive new product development.

It has key alliances with top entertainment, action sports, and children's products brands. It constantly reassesses market trends, and expands into adjacent product categories wherd it see new opportunities.

Tony Armand, former co-CEO of the company, left Bravo Sports to join mouthguard maker Shock Doctor in May 2010. Leonardo Pais, former longtime leader of the chain's international operations, took over responsibility for both the domestic and international businesses.

The company was founded in 1987.
