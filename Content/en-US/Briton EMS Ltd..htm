Briton EMS is a key source for electronics outsourcing in Britain. The company offers contract electronics manufacturing services, specializing in low- and medium-volume, high-mix production. These services range from purchasing and logistics to printed circuit board (PCB) and full product build, assembly, and functional testing. Vendors of various types of electronic gear outsource these chores to Briton EMS to cut costs and increase efficiency. The company's engineering and design department specializes in designing hardware and software products for the instrumentation industry. Briton EMS was founded in 1979 as Briton Electronics. It has been a part of OSI Electronics since a 2013 acquisition.

Besides its manufacturing facility in Bedford, UK, Briton has access to OSI facilities in the US (where OSI is based) and Malaysia. Briton targets customers in industrial, medical, security, counter terror, agricultural, and transport infrastructure businesses and agencies. The company has worked with the 42 Technology consulting firm to develop specialized tools and products. The most recent project was to develop and make a tool for use in the wind tunnel used by the Aircraft Research Association.
