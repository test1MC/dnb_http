LANXESS Corporation's synthetic rubber products are used by the tire, auto, and industrial goods industries, while its plastics are marketed toward makers of medical and electronic communications products. The US unit of Germany-based LANXESS AG 's largest segment is its performance chemicals division, which supplies the textiles, pharmaceutical, and automobile industries, among others. The company's other units are performance polymers (the synthetic rubber operations) and advanced intermediates (basic and fine chemicals). Subsidiaries include Rhein Chemie (rubber chemicals), Sybron Chemicals (ion exchange resins), and Saltigo (fine, agro-, and pharmaceutical chemicals).

LANXESS' portfolio included basic and fine chemicals, color pigments, plastics, synthetic rubber and rubber chemicals, leather, material protection products and water treatment products. The company offers industrial intermediates and aromatic compounds, including chlorobenzenes, chlorotoluenes, cresols, nitrotoluenes, and their derivatives.

The company has operations in Baytown, Texas; Birmingham, New Jersey; Burgettstown, Pennsylvania; Bushy Park, South Carolina; Chardon, Ohio; Gastonia, North Carolina; Greensboro, North Carolina; Orange, Texas; and Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

The company serves a range of industries and industrial applications, including agriculture, polymers, pigments/dyes, paints, automobiles, tires, technical rubber goods, fuels, and construction.

The company, a subsidiary of LANXESS AG , expands through product innovation and organic growth and through selective, complementary acquisitions.

LANXESS AG was spun off from Bayer in 2004.

In 2012, LANXESS opened its first US-based compounding facility for high-tech plastics, in Gastonia, North Carolina. The facility produces the company's Durethan-brand polyamide and Pocan-brand polybutylene terephthalate, which allow automotive engineers to design lighter-weight plastic parts to replace metals in cars, contributing to greater fuel efficiency and reduced emissions. The company projects that global demand for such plastics will grow at an annual rate of 7% through 2020.

In 2011 LANXESS, through a subsidiary, acquired Argentina-based Darmex SA, a leading manufacturer of release agents and curing bladders for the tire industry. The deal made its subsidiary, Rhein Chemie, a leading provider of release agents for rubber products. It also strengthened LANXESS' standing as a leading global synthetic rubber company.
