Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) speaks Korean with a Southern drawl. A subsidiary of South Korea's Hyundai Motor , HMMA began construction of its plant in 2002. The $1.4 billion facility -- Hyundai's first North American auto plant and its most technically advanced -- turned out its first car, the Sonata sedan, in 2005 and now also produces the Elantra. The factory includes a stamping facility, paint shop, vehicle assembly shop, and a two-mile testing track. HMMA has an annual capacity of about 400,000 vehicles. They are distributed throughout the US by sister company Hyundai Motor America and exported to Canada and Puerto Rico.

HMMA produces the 2015 Sonata sedan, and 2015 Elantra sedan vehicle. (It rolled out its millionth Sonata in July 2011. It began making the Elantra in November 2010).

Engines built at HMMA include the 4-cylinder 2.0L Turbo and 2.4L Theta Gasoline Direct Injection and Multi Port Injection models and the 1.8-liter Nu 4-cylinder version.

HMMA's advanced stamping shop includes two stamping presses, which deliver a combined force of 5,400 tons of pressure press pieces of steel into car parts. All material that flows from the stamping shop into the welding shop is 100% automated.

Hyundai cars and SUVs are distributed by Hyundai Motor America via about 800 Hyundai dealerships across North America.

Hyundai entered the US market in 1986. HMMA produced the Santa Fe SUV from 2006 to 2010.
