Ricoh Production Print Solutions (RPPS, formerly InfoPrint Solutions) makes a statement in every color of the rainbow. RPPS develops and markets printing hardware and software used for mass printing. Businesses use its products to produce forms, billing statements, bulk mailings, books, and other media. Its InfoPrint and RICOH line of printers ranges from multifunction office printers to high-speed production systems for mailing operations. Its software includes print management applications, communication tools, and document design products. RPPS caters to direct mailers, publishers, legal firms, utilities, universities, and other businesses. In 2014, the assets of RPPS were transferred to Ricoh USA . Ricoh Co. is the parent company.

The Ricoh unit is based in Boulder, Colorado and serves customers throughout the US.

The company's products are used in banking and financial Services, insurance, higher education, local and federal Government, and energy and utilities.

RPPS was started as a joint venture between Ricoh and IBM. Ricoh bought out IBM's stake and made the unit a wholly owned subsidiary in 2010. The InfoPrint-IBM joint venture had been formed in 2007 when Ricoh paid $725 million for a 51% stake in IBM's Printing Systems Division. Over the next three years Ricoh gradually acquired the rest of IBM's stake in the unit. As part of the transition, RPPS moved from internal use of legacy IBM infrastructure to an Oracle -based enterprise resource planning system.
