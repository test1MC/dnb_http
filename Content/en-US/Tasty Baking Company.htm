Tasty Baking's name says it all. In addition to its popular Krimpet, the baker is known for its Tastykake single-serve cupcakes, snack-sized cakes, and fruit pies. It also makes cookies, brownies, pastries, honey buns, and doughnuts. Tasty Baking sells its goodies through convenience stores, supermarkets, mass merchants, and other retail food outlets in the US, primarily on the East Coast, but also as far west as Arizona and Nevada. ( Wal-Mart is its largest customer, accounting for about 20% of sales; Wawa brings in 10%.) Tasty Baking's customers also can order Tastykake gift packs directly from the company by phone and through its website. Founded in 1914, Tasty Baking is owned by Flowers Foods .

New marketing efforts to strengthen the Tastykake brand include a new platform called milk media featuring branded milk gallons with Tastykake messaging and instant redeemable coupons, a mobile snaptag campaign, as well as a targeted sampling box program. Tastykake advertising also included outdoor billboards, free-standing insert drops in 30 markets.

Based on retail prices, excluding discounts, Tastykake sales have climbed to $425 million from $225 million since the takeover by Flowers Foods.

Flowers Foods has expanded Tasty Baking's product line and added delivery routes from Maine to Florida and as far west as Nevada. (Prior to its acquisition, Tasty Baking was a regional brand sold in about 16,700 retail outlets in Delaware, Maryland, New Jersey, New York, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Virginia.) The sale also expanded Flowers Foods' reach to the densely-populated Northeast.

In 2013, Flowers added about a half a dozen new items to Tastykake's lineup: Tastykake Dreamies, Swirly Cupcakes, Bag Cinnamon Donuts, Six Count Cinnamon Rolls, Glazed Pies and Tastykake Almond Joy Kandy Bar Kakes . Tastykake continued expansion efforts into the west during 2013 and entered the Arizona and Nevada markets. Flowers Foods expects to extend the Tastykake brand to additional markets over the next several years.
