It's always tea time at Whittard Trading. The company operates more than 70 coffee and tea retail shops in Great Britain and serves up more tea shops worldwide. Also known as Whittard of Chelsea, the company sells its products throughout the world, including in the US, Japan, and Australia. Whittard, which was founded more than 125 years ago in London, sells 130 varieties of tea. Its other products include Arabica coffee, hot chocolate, and tea brewing equipment, china, glassware, and espresso brewing machines. PIC Private Equity's turnaround unit EPE Special Opportunities owns Whittard.

Whittard concentrates on sharing its tea expertise with the rest of the world. To this end, the company wholesales its products in more than 20 countries, including Italy, Spain, the Netherlands, and Belarus. It operates stores in Dubai, Chile, Thailand, Cyprus, China, Sri Lanka and Romania.

Particularly through franchise agreements, Whittard is focused on extending its reach across Eastern and Southern Europe, Brazil, Russia, India, the Pacific Rim, Australasia, and South Africa.

Whittard was a unit of Iceland-based Baugur until 2009, when Baugur collapsed and its assets were sold off.

The company was founded by Walter Whittard in 1886.
