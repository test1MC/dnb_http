Westlake Chemical Partners (WCP) is a limited partnership formed by basic chemical manufacturer Westlake Chemical to operate, acquire, and develop ethylene production facilities and related assets. WCP's business and operations are conducted through Westlake Chemical OpCo, a partnership between Westlake and WCP. WCP owns Westlake Chemical OpCo's general partner and controls Westlake Chemical OpCo's assets and operations: three ethylene production facilities, which convert primarily ethane into ethylene (with an aggregate annual capacity of approximately 3.4 billion pounds), and a 200-mile ethylene pipeline. The company went public in 2014.

WCP raised $270 million in an August 2014 IPO to purchase a 4.4% stake in Westlake Chemical OpCo (which in turn gave WCP another 5.6% stake, to give it an overall 10% holding).

Following the offering, WCP held a 10% limited partner interest in Westlake Chemical OpCo as well as the general partner interest. Westlake retained a 90% limited partner interest in Westlake Chemical OpCo and retained a significant interest in WCP through its ownership of its general partner and 55.7% of WCP's limited partner units (consisting of 1,436,115 common units and all of the subordinated units) and its incentive distribution rights.

In connection with this offering, Westlake Chemical OpCo entered into a 12-year ethylene sales agreement with Westlake, under which Westlake agreed to buy 95% of Westlake Chemical OpCo's planned ethylene production each year on a cost-plus basis that is expected to generate a fixed margin per pound of $0.10 Any ethylene not sold to Westlake and all co-products that are produced by Westlake Chemical OpCo will be sold to third parties on either a spot or contract basis. Westlake Chemical OpCoo also entered into a feedstock supply agreement with Westlake that will supply Westlake Chemical OpCo with all of the ethane (and any other feedstocks) required for that company to produce ethylene.

Westlake Chemical OpCo plans to expand the capacity of one of its ethylene production facilities by approximately 250 million pounds in late 2015 or early 2016.
