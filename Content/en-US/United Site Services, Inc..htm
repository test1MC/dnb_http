United Site Services (USS) is one company that hopes its profits are in the toilet. USS is a leading provider of portable toilets and restroom trailers in the US. It also offers temporary fence, power, storage, and other site services to the construction, agriculture, and special events industries. The military and federal, state, and local governments are among its other customers. With more than 250,000 portable restrooms and a fleet of delivery and service vehicles, USS has the capability to respond to emergencies and disasters. The company has provided temporary sanitation services to the Obama presidential inauguration, the Coachella music festival in California, and construction firm D.R. Horton .

The company has portable restrooms, ADA compliant and handicap restrooms at its 60 locations across the US.

USS provides portable toilet, restroom trailer, and temporary fence rental services. It has more than 11,000 temporary hand wash stations, more than 1,000 service, pick-up and delivery vehicles, hundreds of comfort station and VIP restroom trailers, as well as temporary shower trailers available in a range of sizes and configurations to suit specific needs.

The company serves the construction, agriculture, and industrial markets.

In early 2010 the company restructured its debt, leading to equity ownership by its creditors. As part of the deal, USS reduced its debt by 80% and its interest payments by 90%. Following the restructuring, fund units managed by GSO Capital Partners and Angelo, Gordon & Co and related entities control USS. The new owners appointed GE veteran Ron Carapezzi as CEO.

That year the company boosted its US-leading fleet of restroom trailers to about 300 with the acquisition of 31 new trailers.

Since its founding in 1999, USS has made acquisitions an integral part of its growth strategy. Acquiring smaller, locally-based companies in its industry gives it the geographic diversity to serve professional event planners in major metropolitan areas across the US, as well as emergency responders along the Gulf Coast, for example.
