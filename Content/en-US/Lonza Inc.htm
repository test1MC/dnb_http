From disinfecting workplaces to exfoliating dry skin, Lonza puts itself in the middle of things. It is a major part of the North American operations of its parent company, Switzerland-based specialty manufacturing concern, the Lonza Group . While Lonza doesn't actually make consumer products, the company provides chemical ingredients and intermediates for the biocide, nutrition, personal care, pharmaceutical, plastics, and water-treatment industries from a handful manufacturing and research and development locations in the eastern half of the US.

Lonza has production and research centers in Allendale, New Jersey; Cohasset, Minnesota; Mapleton, Illinois; and Williamsport, Pennsylvania; Richmond, Canada; Alpharetta, Georgia; Houston. Texas; and Walkersville, Maryland.

Lonza Allendale houses a major R&D center, with microbiology and analytical chemistry labs and activities that include formulation, process and product development for customers in the hygiene, preservation, water treatment and materials protection markets. Also, Allendale is home to the sales and marketing teams for Custom Manufacturing and Nutritional Ingredients.

Lonza's Cohasset plant produces larch arabinogalactan, an ingredient for food and dietary supplements in both human and animal nutrition, which can also be used in personal care products.

The production plant in Mapleton specializes in chemical intermediate products for hygiene, water treatment and materials protection. It manufactures more than 60 long chain alkyl chlorides, amines and derivatives, including quaternary ammonium compounds and amine oxides. The products find application as biocides, water treatment algaecides, corrosion inhibitors, fabric conditioners, hair care products and wood preservatives.

The company serves various markets such as Consumer Care, Industrial Solutions, Pharma and Biotech, Wood Protection, Water Treatment, Agro Ingredient, and BioResearch.
