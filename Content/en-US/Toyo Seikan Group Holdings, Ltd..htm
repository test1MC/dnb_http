Toyo Seikan has a can-do attitude. Operating through multiple subsidiary and affiliated companies, Toyo Seika is one of Japan's largest can makers, producing and selling containers, including bottles made from glass and plastic, such as polyethylene terephthalate (PET), PET pouches, tin cans, and paper packaging products. Beverage can-making accounts for a large portion of sales, but the company also contract-manufactures aerosol cans, packaging machinery, and product filling. Japan is Toyo Seikan's core market. Founded in 1917, Toyo Seikan was first to domestically market oxygen scavenger sachets, a packaging material used to preserve food.

The company has its headquarters in Tokyo. It operates in Japan, China, Thailand, Malaysia, Vietnam, and the US.

In 2012, in response to challenging business conditions, Toyo Seikan split its operations and created new subsidiaries to transform itself into a holding company. It made the transition in order to utilize better synergies and to bolster its competitiveness in order to sustain continuing growth. Toyo Seikan launched the subsidiaries of Toyo Seikan Kaisha Ltd, Toyo Seikan Split Preparation K.K., Toyo Glass Co Ltd, and Tokan Kogyo Co Ltd.
