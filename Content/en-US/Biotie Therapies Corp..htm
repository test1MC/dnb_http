Biotie Therapies develops drugs focusing on neurodegenerative and psychiatric disorders. Its Selincro treatment for alcoholism was approved in Europe in 2013 and is marketed by H. Lundbeck . Lead candidate tozadenant, for the treatment of Parkinson's disease, is in Phase 3 clinical trials. Biotie Therapies prefers to conduct research and product development in the earlier stages of drug development, then hand off the final stages to partner companies Lundbeck, Roche , and Somaxon . Biotie Therapies is the result of the 2002 merger of three Finnish biotech companies: Control Pharma Corporation, Biotie Therapies (founded in 1992), and Carbion. US-based Acorda is buying Biotie for $363 million.
