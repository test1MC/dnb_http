This company keeps the citizens of Washington informed, through good times and bad. The Seattle Times Company publishes the state's leading newspaper, The Seattle Times , boasting a circulation of more than 250,000. The company also publishes community papers serving such communities as Issaquah, Yakima, and Walla Walla. In addition to traditional print editions, Seattle Times Company operates online news and advertising websites, and it owns printing company Rotary Offset Press. The Seattle Times was founded in 1896 by Alden Blethen. The Blethen family continues to own 50.5% of the company; The McClatchy Company owns the rest.

Like most newspaper publishers, Seattle Times Company has been beset by declines in readership and advertising sales. It has been focused on beefing up its online content to help make up for the shortfall in print revenue and reach audience members who have switched to Internet sources for their news.
