Corning Japan oversees the sales and support of the display technologies division of global cable and materials giant Corning . Corning's display operations in Japan produce glass substrates used in active-matrix liquid crystal displays (AMLCDs). AMLCDs are used in electronic products ranging from notebook and handheld computers to flat-panel televisions and other video devices. Corning Japan was established in 1971. Initially the company supplied glass to research labs in Japan, and it began supplying display manufacturers with glass substrates in 1986. Japan is the third largest market in the world for Corning, trailing only Taiwan and the US.
