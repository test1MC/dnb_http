The films made by Transilwrap Company may not get any Oscars, but they do get rave reviews. The company manufactures and converts plastics for the laminating, printing, and specialty packaging industries, among others. Transilwrap produces its plastic films and sheeting at its manufacturing sites in the US and Canada. The company partners with printers, marketing agencies, packagers, product developers, industrial designers, and other professional to develop film technologies and products for a wide range of applications in traditional and emerging markets.

Transilwrap operates eight manufacturing facilities and nine distribution centers across North America.

The company provides specialized manufacturing and converting services (including liquid and extrusion coating, select sheet extrusion), and a wide-range of precision converting (such as slitting, sheeting, magnetic striping, security and specialty printing).

The firm makes edge reinforcing films in a variety of widths, thicknesses and lengths, in both roll and spool configurations.Transilwrap print finishing films for book jackets, posters, menus, POP displays, trade show graphics or any other lamination or commercial application that demands clarity and graphic protection. They offers specialty film solutions for the document archiving industry.Transilwrap packaging films are ideal for a wide variety of applications including frozen and refrigerated food, bakery goods, fresh produce, consumer goods, cosmetics and much more. The films are ideally suited for medical applications including face shields, medical pouches, lidstock, and thermoformables. Rigid films are also used for die cutting.

The company sells its product to companies in a range of industries, including Advertising and Promotions, Alternative Energy, Commercial Printing, Construction/Interiors, Education, Electronics, Entertainment, Food and Beverage, Health and Beauty, Horticulture, Industrial, Office Supply, packaging, Security, Sporting Goods, and Transportation. Transilwrap serves a diverse customer base for a wide range of end markets including Industrial, Packaging, Security ID, Office Products, Healthcare, Solar and Commercial Printing.

Transilwrap's growth strategy (via both internal growth and through acquisitions) is to provide customers with a strengthened service platform and new solutions through adding converting capabilities and expanding product lines to reach new markets.

It invests heavily in innovation, and has added liquid coating capabilities and pilot line development services. The company has also created custom solutions for new and emerging applications including electric car batteries, digital solar panels, and printed electronics.

The company tailor its films to meet individual requirements and engineer films with specific chemistries and attributes for application. It uses lean manufacturing methods and statistical process control to reduce costs.

In 2013 Transilwrap introduced ProPrint Plus, the newest addition to its ProPrint line of high quality printable synthetic media. This durable new synthetic media is specially made to hold vivid color print using flexography, offset lithography, gravure, screen, wide format, and UV digital printing, while offering enhanced rigidity and stiffness beyond traditional synthetic paper options.

In a major funding move, that year the company partnered with a fund managed by private equity firm The Jordan Company , which will help provide capital for Transilwrap to execute its strategic plans and help position the company for sustained, long-term growth.

In 2014 the company acquired MetPro Group, a leading global supplier of active and protective packaging systems that protect products from corrosion or physical damage during shipping and storage. MetPro uses proprietary technology in combination with specialty films and foils to develop customized products. This acquisition furthers Transilwrap's strategy of expanding its presence in important global growth markets and enhancing its value-added product portfolio.

In 2011 Transilwrap acquired Interfilm Holdings, a leading US-based distributor and converter of flexible films. The deal allowed Transilwrap to expand and strengthen its current films business and access new markets.

Adding to its printable plastics and synthetics capabilities, in 2010 Transilwrap installed a new sheet extrusion line, expanding the company's capacity for its bio-plastics, synthetics, styrene, and styrene alloy products.

The company was founded as the Transparent Sylvania Wrapping Paper Company in 1931 and was one of the first converter/distributors of film in the plastics industry. The founding Minkus and Drower families owned most of the company until a management buy-out in 2004. As management was looking for a partner to help Transilwrap grow, in 2007 private investment firm Nicolet Partners acquired a stake in the company.
