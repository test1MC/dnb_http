Enzymes from Novozymes A/S might have had a hand in brewing your beer, making ethanol for your car, or even treating your medical condition. It produces more than 700 kinds of enzymes (a 48% global market share), microorganisms, biopolymers, and proteins used by more than 40 industries in 130 countries. Its enzymes are used in detergents, foods, animal feed, and other industrial /commercial products. The firm's microorganisms are used in wastewater treatment, and its proteins and biopolymers are used in pharmaceuticals. Novozymes is a sister company to Novo Nordisk , and is controlled by the Novo Nordisk Foundation. In 2013 it bought TJ Technologies, further strengthening its position in bioagriculture.
