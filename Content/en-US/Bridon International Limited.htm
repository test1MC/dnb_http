Bridon International is playing tug-of war with its competition in the steel wire business. The company makes wire rope, fiber rope, steel wire, and strand for the worldwide construction, mining, oil and gas, telecommunications, and marine and fishing industries. Its products are used for such applications as cranes and structural suspension cable systems. The company also makes high carbon and stainless steel wire for engineering uses. Bridon International operates nine manufacturing plants worldwide. The company is owned by Bekaert (67%) and Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan (33%).

The company's manufacturing operations are located at North America, Europe, Australia and New Zealand, and Asia. Its sales and distribution centers are in North America, South America, Africa, Europe and Russia, Asia, Middle East, and Australia and New Zealand.

Bridon International has strong presence in Europe and the US with a portfolio of rope wire, strand and steel and synthetic rope. The company's sales are supported by a global network of agents and distributors.

The company's growth strategy is via acquisitions. As part of its strategy for growth, Bridon International has been investing in manufacturing to increase capacity and in research and development to enhance its technology. It is also expanding its global footprint in emerging markets.

In 2015 Bridon International acquired ScanRope (a Norway-based manufacturer of both steel and fiber deep water mooring systems) from the Parker-Hannifin Corporation . The deal strengthened Bridon International's position as the market leader in this segment.

The company was originally formed as British Ropes in 1924.

In 2011 the company launched eight new products, as well as improved some of its existing products. Among the new products are extended and sheathed round balance ropes for the mining industry designed to have more flexibility and resistance to corrosion. A new Brystar rope has been designed with enhanced rope fatigue performance for the oil and gas industry.

Despite uncertainty in the markets, especially in the Eurozone countries, Bridon expects demand to remain positive during 2012 from such sectors as oil and gas. It expects growth in emerging markets to continue, which will offset any weakness from other areas.
