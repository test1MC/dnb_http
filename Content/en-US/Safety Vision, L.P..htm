If Safety Vision has its way, rear-view mirrors will soon go the way of rumble seats. The company is a distributor and manufacturer of vehicle-mounted cameras and digital video recorders (DVRs) used for rear-vision, side-vision, and mobile surveillance. Safety Vision started out as a distributor of Clarion rear-vision camera systems and has since grown to offer several additional products such as the SafeDrive MiniDVR and a personal flashlight DVR. The company's rear vision and side-vision camera systems are used for safer backing by the trucking and delivery industries as well as by government vehicles. Safety Vision's mobile surveillance cameras are used by mass transit, rail, and law enforcement.

Headquartered in Houston, the company has satellite offices in California, Missouri, Texas, and Canada.

Safety Vision sells its products through distributors and partners like Dell , Ryder Fleet Products, Doran Manufacturing, and LSM Technologies. Clients include Del Papa Distributing, Vulcan Materials Company, Ameron Hawaii, Elgin Medi Transport, Liberty Township Vol. Fire Dept., and the Houston Metropolitan Transit Authority.

The company entered the law enforcement (as well as fire and emergency services) market by acquiring bankrupt ICOP Digital in early 2011. Safety Vision reportedly paid $800,000 for Kansas-based ICOP Digital, a manufacturer of surveillance equipment used primarily in law enforcement squad cars to capture service activities for the express purpose of improving safety, increasing visibility, protecting assets, and reducing liability. ICOP Digital operates as a subsidiary of Safety Vision, heading up its Law Enforcement group.

Throughout 2012 Safety Vision also signed deals with several bus companies for applying its portfolio of safety products to their public transportation vehicles.

Safety Vision was founded in 1993 by Bruce Smith.
