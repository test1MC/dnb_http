WhiteWave Foods rides a wave of dietary changes as consumers seek alternatives to conventional foods. The company is best known for its refrigerated Silk soymilk in the US, and Alpro brand soy products in Europe. WhiteWave also produces organic dairy products under the Horizon Organic label and dairy related foods, including International Delight coffee creamers and LAND O'LAKES-branded creamers and dairy dessert toppings (licensed from dairy co-op Land O'Lakes ). WhiteWave products are sold through natural food and grocery stores, as well as mass merchandisers and restaurants and food service businesses in the US and Canada, and parts of Europe. In 2016 French dairy giant Danone agreed to acquire the company for $12.5 billion. The acquisition is expected to close by the end of the year.

WhiteWave's coffee creamers and beverages unit was its biggest revenue producer in 2015, accounting for 27% of revenue. The unit that sells foods and beverages from plants brought in 24% of revenue with premium dairy goods generating 20% of revenue. The fresh foods and European units split the remaining 30% of revenue almost evenly.

Geographically, the company's North American business, which accounted for nearly 86% of sales in 2015, offers plant-based foods and beverages, coffee creamers and beverages, and premium dairy goods. The European business generated about 14% of revenues through sales of plant-based foods and beverages in Belgium, Germany, the Netherlands, and the UK, and less so in France, Italy, Portugal, Spain, and Sweden.

In 2014, WhiteWave poured into China through a joint venture with China Mengniu Dairy Company Limited, a Chinese dairy company. Through the JV, the companies make, market, and sell premium plant-based beverages in China. The joint venture, a 49/51 split between WhiteWave and Mengniu, sells under the Silk ZhiPuMoFang brand name with available in several major Chinese markets. They include almond milk and walnut milk beverages in single-serve and multi-pack formats.

WhiteWave Foods' largest customer is Wal-Mart Stores , which accounted for 14% of 2015 sales. The company has spent more to advertise in each of the past three years with $216.7 in 2015, $194 million in 2014and $169 million in 2013.

WhiteWave maintained robust revenue growth in 2015 with a 14% increase to nearly $3.9 billion from $3.4 billion in 2014. The three contributors to the Americas Foods and Beverages unit grew in 2015, paced by a 29% increase ($204 million) in the sale of plant-based foods and drinks. Acquisition contributed to the growth.

The revenue increase led to a rising tide of profit for WhiteWave in 2015, when it posted a 20% gain to $168 million from $140 million in 2014.

Cash flow from operations also increased in 2015, reaching $315 million, compared to $284 million in 2014.

WhiteWave brought a new product to dairy cases (or non-dairy in this case) in 2015 with the introduction of cashew milk under the Silk brand. Besides regular cashew flavor, the linen includes chocolate and vanilla.

In 2015, WhiteWave acquired Sequel Naturals, which owns the Vega brand and has been a pioneer in developing plant-based nutrition products, for some $553.6 million. This acquisition extended the Company's plant-based foods and beverages platform into nutritional powders and bars.

In another 2015 deal, WhiteWave bought EIEIO Inc., which has owned the Magicow brand and other brands, for $40.2 million. The acquisition expands WhiteWave's portfolio of bulk coffee creamer and flavor dispensing products and provided new product capabilities to support growth in away-from-home channel.
