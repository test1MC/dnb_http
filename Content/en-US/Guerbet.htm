Guerbet is one of Europe's leading makers of contrast agents used in medical imaging. Most of the company's products, such as Xenetix, Hexabrix, and Oxilan, are substances that are given by mouth, injection, or airway to make internal organs visible during X-ray and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) examinations. Subsidiaries Simafex manufactures specialty chemicals for use in pharmaceuticals, and biomedical laboratory Medex develops medical devices. Guerbet has operations in some 20 countries and is targeting growth in the US and Japan. Marcel Guerbet, grandfather of chairman Michel Guerbet, developed the first iodinated contrast agent in 1901; his descendants control the company.
