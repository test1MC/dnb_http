Mitsubishi Electric Automation (MEAU) knows how to give its industrial customers the machine touch. MEAU makes a variety of automation products for the automotive, packaging, machine tool, energy, logistics, and other industries in North and Latin America. Its product line includes everything from components like circuit breakers and motion controllers to industrial sewing equipment and robotic arms. The company operates 20 manufacturing sites in North America and operates 30 service centers throughout the region. A subsidiary of Mitsubishi Electric Corp ., MEAU was formed in 1997 from three existing automation product business units.

The company is headquartered in Vernon Hills, Illinois, and is present in about 50 locations throughout North America. It has roughly 20 service centers and service satellites.

MEAU serves the automotive, industrial sewing equipment, logistics, machine tool, packaging, and renewable energy sectors.

One strategy for MEAU will always entail the optimizing of its automation products for improved efficiency. Responding to demand within the HVAC market for a drive that can improve efficiency of air movement through HVAC systems, MEAU in 2015 introduced the F800 Series low-voltage variable frequency drive (VFD). The low-voltage F800 is specifically designed and optimized for HVAC applications, including air handlers, cooling towers, and pumps.
