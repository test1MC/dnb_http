Impinj designs semiconductors to adapt to their surroundings. The company's "self-adaptive silicon" technology allows analog circuits -- which translate radio waves into data usable by electronic systems -- to be made smaller and more efficient, and allow for chip adjustment. The company focuses on designing communications chips, particularly UHF Gen 2 radio-frequency identification (RFID) devices. The RFID chips connect an item's identity to businesses' software for solutionis that range from healthcare asset management and optimization to interactive product displays for retail stores. Impinj was founded in 2000 by chip design legend (and Caltech professor emeritus) Carver Mead and his former student, Chris Diorio.
