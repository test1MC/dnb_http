Emerging biotechnology firm Dipexium Pharmaceuticals was formed in 2010 to develop and commercialize Locilex (pexiganan acetate cream 1%), a broad spectrum, small peptide topical antibiotic for the treatment of mild and moderate skin infections in superficial wounds, including mild infections of diabetic foot ulcers. Locilex is also being eyed as a promising product candidate to treat acute bacterial skin and skin structure infections in superficial wounds (including infected burns, decubitus ulcers, and surgical wounds), and to treat methicillin resistant staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) infections in the nose. In late 2016 Dipexium agreed to merge with PLx Pharma, which is developing aspirin product Aspertec.
