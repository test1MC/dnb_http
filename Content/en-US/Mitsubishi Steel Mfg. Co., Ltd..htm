Mitsubishi Steel Mfg., part of the Mitsubishi keiretsu (group of companies working collectively) , makes special steel castings and forgings such as coil springs, hot-rolled special steel bars, magnetic materials, and vibration and shock isolators for construction machinery. Springs and processed steel products each account for around 40% of its total sales. Other products include shoe plates and assemblies and automotive suspension components. Mitsubishi Steel has 16 service centers throughout Japan and a handful of sales offices in Nagoya, Osaka, Fukuoka, and Hiroshima.

Mitsubishi Steel has its head office in Tokyo. It has 16 service centers throughout the country and sales offices in Nagoya, Osaka, Fukuoka, and Hiroshima.

Mitsubishi Steel's reportable segments include Special Steel Bars (41% of net sales), Springs (40%), Formed and Fabricated Products (8%), Machinery (8%), and Other (3%).

Mitsubishi Steel's revenues climbed 6% in 2015 compared to 2014. Its net income also jumped 5% during that same time period due to the spike in revenues coupled with dividends income and decreased deferred income taxes.

The growth for 2015 was fueled by increased sales from its Special Steel Bars, Machinery, and Formed and Fabricated Products segments. The increases were attributed to the overall recovery in demand within the construction machinery, industrial machinery, and machine tool industries.

The segments in 2015 were also helped by increased sales of special steels, precision castings, and special alloy powders in addition to a rise in power generation and related products.

In order to grow, Mitsubishi Steel is working to establish production and sales facilities for spring steel and special steel bars in Indonesia. Its Springs segment continues to make costs improvements in its businesses in North America and China. It also aims to strengthen its supply capabilities and expand its sales areas in the emerging markets by solidifying its newly established Indian joint venture.

The company traces its historical roots back to 1919 when it was founded as Nagasaki Steel Works of Mitsubishi Shipbuilding & Engineering Co., Ltd. It became independent as Mitsubishi Steel Manufacturing Co., Ltd. in 1942.
