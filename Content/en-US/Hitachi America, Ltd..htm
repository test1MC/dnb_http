Hitachi America supplies the Americas with a broad range of high-tech products for consumer and business applications. The wholly owned subsidiary of the Hitachi Ltd. conglomerate operates through several subsidiaries located in the US, Canada, Venezuela, and Mexico. Hitachi America makes and sells consumer electronics (LED and LCD TVs and accessories), LCD projectors, and surveillance cameras and other security devices through its Digital Media Division. The Industrial Systems group covers everything from AC drives and air conditioning equipment to water treatment systems. In between are compressors, elevators and escalators, motors, power generation equipment, semiconductors, and transportation systems.

Hitachi America is comprised of Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas , Hitachi Communication Technologies America , Hitachi Global Storage Technologies , and Deere Hitachi Construction Machinery , among other businesses.

The company's renewable energy unit (within Industrial Systems) focuses on energy storage for the smart grid (an electricity network using digital technology to control distribution) as it works with wind and solar farms. Hitachi America believes its storage products can address the need to store and transport alternative energy. It also makes generators and converters for the renewable energy market. Additionally, its parent company released its long-life lead acid battery for distribution in North America. These batteries have a 15- to 17-year battery life and can be recycled.

The Alliances and Sourcing Division of Hitachi America is part of a global network of procurement offices which helps suppliers build partnerships Hitachi Group companies.

Hitachi is looking for new alliance opportunities in a wide range of fields including information and telecommunication systems, power systems, environmental, industrial and transportation systems, and social and urban systems.

In 2014 the company teamed up with Hitachi Industrial Equipment Systems (HIES) to establish Hitachi Industrial Equipment Mexico. The joint venture is engaged in the sale and after-sales services of HIES products.

Hitachi is looking to Mexico as a base for automotive manufacturing throughout the Americas. In this regard, in 2014 Hitachi Automotive Systems Mexico began building a new plant in Lerma City to make automotive systems.
