Looking for lathes? Haas Automation makes and distributes such machine tools as computer numerical controlled (CNC) vertical and horizontal machining centers, which can cut cast iron, alloy and stainless steels, and high-nickel and aluminum alloys. Other products include CNC lathes, rotary tables, collet indexers, and axis machines. The company's primary markets include the aerospace, education, medical, oil, and automotive industries. Haas sells its new and pre-owned products through a worldwide network of more than 170 Haas Factory Outlets (HFOs).

Haas Automation manufactures four major product lines: vertical machining centers (VMCs), horizontal machining centers (HMCs), lathes, and rotary products. Haas claims to ship more CNC machines per month than any other manufacturer in the US. The company has sold more than 175,000 machines.

The Haas Technical Education Center Network partners with learning institutes (such as vocational schools, career centers, and community colleges) to offer CNC education.

Based in Oxnard, California, the company operates more than 170 outlets in more than 50 countries worldwide in Europe, China, India, Russia, Turkey, and the US. Products are manufactured at the company's 1-million-sq.-ft. manufacturing facility in Oxnard, California.

Haas' HFOs are complete with showroom facility, service personnel, and vehicles, and spare-parts inventories. In addition to North America, Haas operates HFOs in Europe, China, and India. These facilities are built around local factory outlets and are designed to provide sales, service, and support to regional customers.

Designing with the times, Haas recognized the industry trend in the mold-and-die industry for especially large components and structures used in aerospace and automotive applications. The company expanded the capability of its EC-1600 horizontal machining center to travel an additional 10 inches to accommodate expanded capacity.

The company was founded by Gene Haas in 1983.
