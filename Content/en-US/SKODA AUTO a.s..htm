Put away your Czech book. If you don't live in Europe, chances are you won't be buying a ŠKODA. Once the Czech Republic's state carmaker, ŠKODA is a subsidiary of Volkswagen . The company makes several core models -- Fabia (available in hatchback and combi), Octavia (in liftback and combi), Superb (luxury sedan and wagon), Yeti, Praktik, Rapid, Citico, Rapid Spaceback, and Roomster (hatchback and compact SUV variations). In addition to manufacturing plants in the Czech Republic, ŠKODA assembles cars in India, Russia, and with partners, in the Ukraine, Kazakhstan, Slovakia, and China.

Germany represents 25% of the company's total sales, while other countries in Western Europe generate 37%. Other major markets include Central and Eastern Europe (19%) and the Czech Republic (9%).

ŠKODA recorded a revenue jump from 2013 to 2014 due to a 13% spike in car deliveries. In 2014 its top selling models included the ŠKODA Octavia, ŠKODA Rapid, and ŠKODA Fabia.

Having built more than 1 million cars in 2014, ŠKODA aims to soon reach a capacity for 1.5 million cars per year. Adhering to this, the company will release a new or updated model every six months. Throughout 2014 it introduced the new ŠKODA Octavia G-TEC and ŠKODA Octavia Scout, as well as three new sporty Monte Carlo editions of the ŠKODA Citigo, the ŠKODA Rapid Spaceback, and the ŠKODA Yeti.
