XIO believes in the power of data. The company develops data storage systems and provides related support services. XIO's support services include virtualized online transaction processing, cloud hosting environments, and enterprise data center workloads. Other applications include tools for data management and data protection. It supports flash, hybrid, and hard disk drive storage. Serving more than 1,500 customers worldwide, the company has partnerships with VMware , Citrix Systems , Microsoft , Oracle , and others. Founded in 2002 as a division of Seagate Technology , XIO is now majority owned by venture firm Oak Investment Partners .

The company has offices in China, India, South Africa, South Korea, the UK, Europe, the Asia/Pacific region, and the US.

XIO markets its products directly, as well as through a network of resellers. Its customers include technology companies ( Cisco , Microsoft ), consumer products firms and retailers ( Blistex , Wet Seal ), educational institutions ( UCLA , Arizona State University ), and health insurers ( WellCare ), among others.

Many of XIO's customers are in financial services, manufacturing, health care, government, retail, and education.

The company has focused on expanding its distribution base. To that end, in 2014 XIO signed a distribution agreement with Ingram Micro Inc.

XIO has used past acquisitions to expand its product line and related services, including acquiring the assets of Daticon , a provider of litigation support and document conversion services, as well as assets and associated technology of Seagate's Advanced Storage Architecture group. The Daticon business was sold in 2008 to Electronic Evidence Discovery.
