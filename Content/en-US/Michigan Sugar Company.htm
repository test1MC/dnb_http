Michigan Sugar is the largest beet-sugar processor east of the Mississippi and one of the llargest in the US. The agricultural cooperative is owned by more than 1,000 beet-sugar farmers who in 2002 purchased the company from Imperial Sugar . Michigan Sugar manufactures both private-label and branded consumer, food service, and industrial products. Its portfolio of sugars include granulated, confectioner's, and brown sugar, along with molasses and liquid sucrose. It also produces sugar by-products, such as beet pulp, which is used for animal feed and fertilizer. Michigan Sugar's flagship brand is Pioneer Sugar, but the company also sells products to institutions under the name Big Chief Sugar.

Bay City, Michigan-based Michigan Sugar operates factories in Bay City, Caro, Croswell, and Sebewaing, Michigan. It also has three warehouse terminals located in Michigan and Ohio.

The cooperative operates four Michigan factories that produce an annual average of more than 1 billion pounds of sugar. The coop's more than 1,000 farmers grow sugarbeets on 160,000 acres of land.

Michigan Sugar is investing in its business, to the tune to more than $20 million in 2013 spent on strategic, long-term capital projects at its factories and piling grounds. In 2012, the coop sent about $13 million at its Bay County plant to update equipment.
