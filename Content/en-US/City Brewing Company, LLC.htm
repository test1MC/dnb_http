City Brewing Company has been brewing beer in the same city since its predecessor's inception in the mid-1800s. The corner of Third and Mississippi streets in La Crosse, Wisconsin, once home to the G. Heileman Brewing Company (which brewed Old Style), is today City Brewing. The company brews 40 recipes for itself and more than a dozen contract-brewing customers. Alcoholic beverages it makes under contract include Mike's Hard Lemonade and Smirnoff Ice . In addition to beer, City Brewing also manufactures and packages teas, sodas, and energy drinks. Nonalcoholic brands it makes under contract include Monster and Rockstar energy drinks.

Besides its La Crosse brewery, City Brewing runs breweries in Latrobe, Pennsylvania (a former Rolling Rock brewery) and Memphis, Tennessee (a former Schlitz brewery). From those facilities City Brewing can roll out a lot of barrels each year. The La Crosse plant has packaging capacity of more than 50 million cases; the Latrobe plant can make more than 20 million cases; and Memphis can pump out more than 60 million cases.

Beer brands made at City Brewing operations include Rolling Rock, Iron City, and Stoney's. In 2014 the company won a contract to brew new American style beers from Guinness (owned by Diageo ), including American Blonde Lager. In a contract for the Memphis plant, City Brewery will produce about 100,000 barrels a year for Craft Brew Alliance , based in Portland, Oregon. The Craft Brew Alliance's brand include Red Hook, Widmer Brothers, and Kona.
