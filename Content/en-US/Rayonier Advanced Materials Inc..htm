Rayonier Advance Materials is the world's leading producer of high-value specialty cellulose fibers (serving customers in 20 countries) with proprietary cellulose chemistry expertise and deep manufacturing process knowledge. It traces its history to 1926, when Rainier Pulp & Paper began operations in Washington State. With three companies merging in 1937 to form Rayonier , it became the #1 single largest manufacturer of dissolving pulps. In 1931 DuPont partnered with Rayonier to develop a process to purify hemlock pulp for use in the production of rayon. To get better shareholder returns, in 2014 Rayonier separated its performance fibers business from its forest resources and real estate businesses.
