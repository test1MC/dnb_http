Chip makers and others in need of advanced test equipment often turn to Advantest. The company is a top maker of automated test equipment (ATE) used in the manufacture of semiconductors. Most of Advantest's operations center around its semiconductor production equipment, including its extensive line of specialized ATE gear, as well as electron-beam lithography equipment. The balance of sales come from a variety of measuring instruments used by manufacturers of consumer electronics, telecom equipment, and other high-tech goods to test their wares.

Advantest's semiconductor and component test system segment's products accounted for 62% of revenue in 2016. The mechatronics system segment, 19% of revenue, supplies test handlers and mechatronic-applied products for handling semiconductor devices, device interfaces that serve as interfaces with the devices that are measured and operations related to nano-technology products. Services, support and others segment also accounts for 19% of revenue.

Advantest caters to semiconductor manufacturers, foundries, and test houses in Japan, Korea, China, Taiwan, Singapore, the Americas, and Europe. Customers in Asia (excluding Japan) account for 70% of sales.

Revenue ticked down 1% in 2016 (ended March) from 2015 with a 7% drop in the semiconductor and component test system business segment. It was affected by weakening sales of major end-products of non-memory devices, such as smartphones and PCs. In memory test systems, demand for DRAM also decreased due to the impact of the smartphone slowdown.

Advantest's net income fell 48% in 2016 from 2015. The company had higher selling, general and administrative expenses and it invested more in research and development. It did benefit from the weakness of the Japanese currency in relation to the US dollar.

Advantest was founded in 1954 as Takeda Riken Industry Co., Ltd. and took the Advantest name in 1985. Originally a manufacturer of measuring instruments, Advantest entered the semiconductor test equipment business in 1972. The company established a US subsidiary in 1982 and a European subsidiary in 1983.
