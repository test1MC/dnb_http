Compañía Vinícola del Norte de España (which goes by the initials CVNE and is known in English as The Northern Spanish Wine Company) has produced wine in Spain since 1879. It operates three vineyards: Cvne Haro, Viña Real, and Viñedos del Contino. The oldest, Cvne Haro, serves as the company's headquarters and is commonly referred as "La Cuné." Compañía Vinícola's brands include Crianza Reserva, Contino Graciano, Monopole White, Real de Asúa, and Viña del Olivo. In addition to Spain, CVNE's wine is distributed in some 30 other countries, including Argentina, Australia, Bahrain, Japan, and the US.
