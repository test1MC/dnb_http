PowerSecure International helps its utility, energy, and water customers stay on top of what is going on with their gas, electricity, and water. Its primary segment offers distributed generation electricity systems, backup generators, and onsite energy controls to industrial and commercial users, which help to monitor and more efficiently dispatch the flow of electricity. Other segments provide management consulting and utility engineering planning, design, and construction services, as well energy efficiency products. It also provides solar energy services through its PowerSecure Solar subsidiary. The company was acquired by Southern Company in 2016.

In May 2016, Southern Company , a major electricity distributor serving more than 4.5 million customers and about 44,000 megawatts of generating capacity, purchased PowerSecure for $425 million.

The company is based in Wake Forest, North Carolina, and has several satellite offices and manufacturing facilities, the largest of which are in the Raleigh-Durham and Greensboro, North Carolina; Atlanta; Bethlehem, Pennsylvania; and Stamford, Connecticut areas.

PowerSecure operates through the segments of Distributed Generation (33% of net sales), Utility Infrastructure (32%), Solar Energy (21%), and Energy Efficiency (14%).

PowerSecure achieved unprecedented growth in 2015, with revenues exploding 73% to increase from $257 million the previous year to peak at a record-shattering $444 million for 2015. After posting a net loss of $7 million in 2014, the company posted positive net income of almost $6 million during 2015.

The milestone growth for 2015 was fueled by a more than 300% increase from its Solar Energy division. This segment experienced a significant year-over-year increase in the number and size of solar projects, including two utility scale solar installation projects, as well as an overall spike in customer demand for solar solutions. This was driven, in part, by the scheduled expiration or reduction of federal alternative energy investment tax credits at the end of 2016, which has now been extended.

PowerSecure's recent growth strategy has been focused on its burgeoning Solar Energy segment. Indeed, this segment enjoyed growth of more than 300% from 2014 to 2015 due to a surge in the number of the amount of solar projects it is working on.
