ShoreTel's internet protocol-based telephony hardware and software offers small and midsized businesses, government agencies, and schools an alternative to standard phone service. It provides voice, video, data, and mobile communications with products that include phones and switches, as well as messaging and systems management software. The company provides a cloud-based subscription service and licenses its software for use in customers' data centers. It also offers communications service for call centers. ShoreTel generates most of its sales in the US. The company counts about 40,000 customers in the financial services, health care, manufacturing, nonprofit, and technology industries, among others.

The biggest chunk, 45%, of Shoretel's revenue comes from sales of products. Hosted and related services account for about 35% of revenue with support and services accounting for a fifth of revenue.

The company's products are ShoreTel Connect, for unified communications; Contact Center, for routing communications; and ShoreTel Summit, its platform for developers and integrators. ShoreTel Connect offers services onsite, in the cloud, and as a hybrid service. Contact Center helps customers manage incoming communications by routing calls and other incoming communications to the appropriate agent. Summit is a Communications Platform as a Service (CPaaS), which customers use to integrate communications into an application or to create a standalone communications application.

ShoreTel outsources the production of its switches and routers to contract manufacturers in the US and China.

ShoreTel has offices in the US, the UK, Europe, Canada, India, Australia, and Singapore. The company generates more than 90% of sales in the US.

ShoreTel sells its products through a network of more than 1,200 channel partners, including AT&T and CenturyLink . This network includes resellers, to whom the company sells directly, and value-added distributors, who, in turn, sell to resellers.

ShoreTel products are used by organizations such as The San Francisco Giants , Girl Scouts of America , First Republic Bank , Twitter , and Randstad .

ShoreTel's revenue held steady at about $360 million in 2016 (ended June) compared to 2015. Product revenue dropped 23% on reduced demand for premise-based systems from new customers, which, the company said, is an industry trend. That drop was somewhat balanced by increases in hosted (cloud) services services (up 20%) and support and service (up 3%). The growth came from new customers as well as current customers switching to hosted services.

Increases in research and development, sales and marketing, and administrative costs nudged the company's net loss to about $5 million in 2016, about $1 million higher than 2015.

Cash from operations dipped to $35 million in 2016 from $41 million in 2015. More cash coming in from an increase in deferred revenue and decreases in accounts receivable and inventory was offset by decreases in accrued taxes and surcharges and accounts payable.

ShoreTel is on the move to two destinations where, it hopes, sales await. The company is in the midst of shifting its products -- and its customers -- to the cloud. And it is pushing expansion to international markets.

The company has lost sales of its on-premises products but gained sales of its hosted cloud products. A key part of its cloud strategy is to switch its base of on-premise customers to cloud services. Already a number of its on-premise customers have agreed to move or have moved to ShoreTel's cloud services or added them to their setups. The company estimates that recurring revenue from cloud services should account for nearly two-thirds of its total in 2017, up from 40% in 2014.

ShoreTel has established a presence in the UK for entree into Europe and in Australia as a base for the Asia/Pacific market. It also has added offices in Canada. The company plans to push its cloud communications products and services in the new markets.

ShoreTel's acquisition of Corvisa in 2016 for some $8 million, should help its push to the cloud and overseas markets. Corvisa provides cloud-based software for call centers and has an active international sales component.
