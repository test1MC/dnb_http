Holsten-Brauerei makes Holsten Pilsener, which is sold mainly in northern and eastern Germany but also exported to nearly 90 countries. The company also distributes brands including Astra, Duckstein, Feldschlösschen, Lübzer, Landskron, and Tuborg. In the wake of Heineken ’s 2003 purchase of rival BBAG , the Eisenbeiss family, which owned nearly 50% of Holsten, announced interest in selling a stake of the company. Carlsberg Breweries purchased a majority stake in the German brewer in 2004. Under Carlsberg, Holsten is focusing on selling beer in northern Germany and has sold many of its subsidiaries, including its water and nonalcoholic beverage units. The company was founded in 1879.
