Mad Catz Interactive puts hard-core gamers in the virtual driver's seat. The company makes joysticks, control pads, headsets, memory, and other video game accessories and enhancement products under the Mad Catz, GameShark, Joytech, Saitek, and AirDrives brands. It also publishes software. Products are sold through mass-market and specialty retailers globally. ( GameStop is Mad Catz's #1 customer, accounting for about a fourth of sales). The US generates more than 50% of revenue. Most of the company's products are made by third parties in Asia and are designed to be used with Microsoft , Nintendo , Sony , and other PC game systems; it also supplies accessories for Apple and other branded audio devices. In 2017 the company filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy protection, liquidated its assets, and ceased all operations.
