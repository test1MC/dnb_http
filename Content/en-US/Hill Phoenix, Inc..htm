This phoenix rises from the cold. Hill Phoenix engineers, manufactures, and sells as well as installs industrial walk-in coolers, freezers, and refrigerated display cases. It also makes electrical distribution units featuring switchboards, load centers, and lighting control panels. Aside from the expected supermarkets, other customers of Hill Phoenix include big-box retailers, commercial and industrial refrigeration companies, and convenience stores. Hill Phoenix is a subsidiary of diversified industry conglomerate Dover . Hill Phoenix has been keeping it cool since 1887, when a New Jersey grocer developed the first cold case.

The company has locations in Chino, Pleasanton, and San Dimas, California; Conyers and Covington, Georgia; Mt. Laurel, New Jersey; and Colonial Heights, Virginia. It also has offices in Denmark, Canada, Mexico.

Although Hill Phoenix strives to increase its market share by selling directly to customers, the company also makes selective acquisitions.

In 2015 Hill Phoenix's engineered systems segment acquired Barker Company and its sister company, Barker Sales and Service (collectively the Barker Company). The Barker Company, with estimated full-year revenue of $70 million, is based in Keosauqua, Iowa, and specializes in manufacturing refrigerated, non-refrigerated, and hot display cases. The Barker Company will become part of the Hill Phoenix Specialty Products Division and will enable the company to meet increasing demand for more specialized and highly customized products in the fast-growing specialty merchandiser segment.
