Edwards Group adores a vacuum. The company is one of the largest suppliers of vacuum pump technology and semiconductor abatement systems, which are needed to create particle-free, controlled environments as part of the chip manufacturing process. Edwards' pumps are used to make semiconductors, LEDs, flat panel TVs and mobile device screens, solar cells, and scientific instruments. The company counts more than 20,000 customers in 100 different countries, including AIXTRON , Applied Materials , GE , TSMC , and Waters . Edwards has almost a dozen manufacturing plants in seven countries, but more than half of its sales come from Asia. Edwards has been part of Atlas Copco since the 2014 acquisition.

Atlas Copco bought Edwards for about $1.6 billion, which included debt. Edwards became part of Atlas Copco's Vacuum Solutions division.

Edwards operates through three platform businesses: Semiconductor and Emerging Technologies, General Vacuum, and Service (Aftermarket). It runs 15 manufacturing facilities (most in Eastern Europe and Asia) and five service centers around the world.

Edwards Group traces its roots back to 1919, when it was founded by F.D. Edwards. It was bought by the BOC Group in 1968 and known as BOC Edwards until 2007, when the BOC Group was bought by Linde and Edwards, its vacuum technology division, was taken over by CCMP Capital Advisors and Unitas Capital.
