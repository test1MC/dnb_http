Getting burned up is all in a day's work for John Zink. The company, a part of Koch Industries ' chemical technology group, specializes in designing, manufacturing, and installing equipment and systems used to cleanly burn fossil fuels. The systems help reduce air and water pollution at refineries and plants. John Zink serves the chemical processing, oil and gas, manufacturing, power generation, energy transmission, and waste management markets. The company's products include process, duct, and boiler burners; flaring systems and accessories; flare gas recovery systems; vapor recovery and combustion systems; and thermal oxidation systems. It also offers engineering, repair, and equipment rental services.

John Zink's products are distributed and used around the world. Its brands include JZ (flares and process and duct burners for oil/gas and petrochemical customers), TODD (boiler and duct burners for maritime, manufacturing, and processing sectors), Gordon-Piatt (burner parts and services), and Kaldair (flare systems). German subsidiary KEU supplies thermal oxidizers and heat recovery systems for the chemical and pharmaceutical manufacturing industries.

The company also operates The John Zink Institute, which offers practical courses on process and boiler burners, process and biogas flares, and vapor control technologies.

Affiliates of John Zink Company in 2011 acquired UK-based Hamworthy Combustion Engineering and US-based Hamworthy Combustion (USA) from subsidiaries of Citigroup -owned Citi Holdings to form John Zink Hamworthy Combustion. Hamworthy engineers and manufactures combustion equipment.
