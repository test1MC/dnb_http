Oriel Resources, formed in 2003 to explore for gold, silver, and nickel in Russia and Eastern Europe, now seeks its fame in ferroalloys. Oriel is part of giant Russian steel company Mechel , which uses ferroalloys (an alloy of iron with another element such as chromium) in its steel production. Oriel's assets initially included the Tikhvin ferrochrome smelting plant in Russia and two Kazakhstan operations -- the Voshod chrome mining and processing plant and Schevchenko nickel mine and deposit. These were added to Mechel's Southern Urals nickel plant and Bratsk ferroalloys plant in Russia to form its ferroyalloys unit. Mechel bought Oriel in 2008 and brought all its ferroalloy assets under Oriel's umbrella.
