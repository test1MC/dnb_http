Dr. Livingstone presumably had no idea that one day Stanley Electric Sales of America, a subsidiary of the Japan-based Stanley Electric Company, would be making and selling a myriad of semiconductors, subminiature lamps, and related electronic components. The parent company was named after 19th-century explorer Sir Henry Morton Stanley, known for exploring the Dark Continent, hoping that some of that aptitude would be reflected in the business activities of Stanley Electric. Similarly, Stanley Electric Sales of America explores for ways to supply North America's demand for electronics, automotive, computer, and communications products.
