ZF Group North American Operations (NAO) is the North American arm of German transmission, chassis, and driveline manufacturer ZF Friedrichshafen AG . ZF Group NAO makes driveline and chassis components for almost anything that rolls -- from passenger cars and light trucks to buses, heavy-duty trucks, and construction and agricultural equipment. Products include axles, steering systems, suspensions, and transmissions. It also specializes in transmissions for rail, marine craft, and helicopters. Aftermarket parts and service are offered, as well.

NAO has a network of 22 manufacturing sites in the US (16) and Mexico (6). NAO targets its products primarily to US automotive manufacturers, as well as German, Japanese, and Korean car makers that have operations in North America.

The company recently expanded its technical center in Michigan to accommodate research and advanced product and technology development activities. There, NAO performs ball joint and suspension link testing for torque, elasticity, wear, fatigue, and sealability. The facility also performs transmission testing and axle road testing using real world simulations.

NAO in 2014 produced 16,900 units of cars and light commercial vehicles in addition to 520 units of commercial vehicles.
