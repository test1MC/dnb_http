1105 Media has a myriad of ways to distribute business-to-business information. The company covers markets such as IT and computing, office technology, home medical equipment, and the public sector. Its products and operations include publications ( Redmond Magazine for the Microsoft IT community), e-newsletters ( Federal Employees News Digest ), websites (CampusTechnology.com), and conferences and events (The Defense Systems Conference). Its 1105 Government Information Group subsidiary covers the government information technology market. 1105 Media was founded in 2006 by Nautic Partners, Alta Communications, and publishing and marketing executive Neal Vitale.

1105 Media is based in Chatsworth, California. It has several other offices in California, Florida, Massachusetts, Nevada, Texas, Virginia, and Washington.

The company continues to launch new offerings, particularly in the events and digital media spaces. In 2011 it enhanced its government technology conferences with the introduction of the FOSE Institute. The FOSE Institute is designed to identify ideas that address government technology policy and implementation challenges. The previous year 1105 Media launched Upside, an online lead generation solution for IT vendors that provides information on more than 1 million IT professionals.

1105 Media was established when Vitale and his private equity partners acquired 101 Communications and merged it with Stevens Publishing. The acquisitive firm added Post Newsweek Tech Media to its holdings in 2006, followed by Fawcette Technical Publications; the GovSec, U.S. Law and READY Conference and Exhibition; and Federal Employees News Digest (FEND) in 2007.
