Consisting of 12 manufacturing facilities, six research and development centers, and over 10 sales and service offices, Siemens Ltda. only represents the Brazilian outpost of the Siemens empire. Providing the foundation for modern life in Brazil including power, information and communications, and transportation, the company's other activities include automation and control, medical solutions, and lighting through a number of subsidiaries, joint ventures, and associated companies. Although Siemens established its Brazilian operations in 1905, the company has operated in the Brazilian market since 1867 when it installed the first telegraph line between Rio de Janeiro and Rio Grande do Sul.
