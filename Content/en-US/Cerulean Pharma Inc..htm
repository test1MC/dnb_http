Cerulean Pharma focuses on developing its proprietary dynamic tumor targeting platform. Its nanopharmaceutical product candidates (polymers linked to anti-cancer therapeutics) dynamically target tumors by exploiting the leakiness of new blood vessels in tumors as an entry portal into tumor tissue. Its lead candidate CRLX101 has orphan drug designation from the FDA for the treatment of ovarian cancer. However, in mid-2016, CRLX101 failed a phase 2 clinical trial in combination with Roche 's Avastin. Cerulean Pharma, still hopeful for the candidate, slashed its workforce in half. The company went publin in 2014.
