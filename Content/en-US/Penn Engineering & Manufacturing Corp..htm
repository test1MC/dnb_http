Penn Engineering & Manufacturing is a real clincher in the fastener industry. The company's largest division, PennEngineering (PEM), is a leading specialty fastener manufacturer. Other subsidiaries include PennEngineering Automotive Fasteners, 3V Fasteners Company, and QRP. The company's products include fasteners, faster installation equipment, plastic inserts, small screws, quick release pins, and precision components. They are used in electronics, computer, data/telecom, medical, automotive, marine, and general manufacturing applications. Penn Engineering & Manufacturing sells through independent distributors and has manufacturing facilities in Asia, Europe, and North America.

Penn Engineering is based in Danboro, Pennsylvania; it has manufacturing facilities in Danboro, Pennsylvania; and Winston-Salem and Wilmington, North Carolina. International locations reside in Doncaster, UK; Singapore; Galway, Ireland; and Shanghai, China

Penn Engineering's specialty fasteners are marketed under brand names including PEM (for use in thin sheetmetal or printed circuit board materials), SI (for use in plastic materials), StickScrew (small screw insertion system), and Atlas (blind threaded rivets). The company's PEMSERTER fastener-insertion press installs self-clinching fasteners.

Penn Engineering serves customers in the aerospace and aircraft, appliances, automobiles, consumer electronics, food service equipment, furniture, industrial equipment, lawn and garden equipment, marine and boating, telecommunications, and trucking and trailer sectors.

It sells it products through representatives and distributors such as Aero-Space Southwest, D.B. Roberts Company, and Nappco Fastener Company to noteworthy Fortune 500 companies like General Motors.

Penn Engineering grows by acquiring new technologies, product lines, smaller companies, and joint ventures worldwide. It also introduces new products and services in anticipation of customer needs.

Penn Engineering was founded in 1942 by K. A. Swanstrom, the former president of WWII war baby Elastic Stop Nut Corporation.
