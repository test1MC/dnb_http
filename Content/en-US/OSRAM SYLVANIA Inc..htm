It's only natural for OSRAM SYLVANIA to be in the limelight. After all, the company specializes developing and manufacturing automotive lighting, general lighting, display/optic lighting, electronic control systems, and precision materials and components. It has some 22 manufacturing facilities in the US, Canada (primarily through Canadian division OSRAM SYLVANIA Ltd.), Mexico (OSRAM Mexico), Puerto Rico, and Germany. It also maintains a dozen R&D labs and a network of sales and distribution centers. OSRAM SYLVANIA is the North American business of Germany's OSRAM Licht .

OSRAM SYLVANIA serves customers in more than 140 countries.

OSRAM SYLVANIA's general lighting business unit manufactures incandescent, fluorescent, and high-intensity discharge lamps that are marketed and distributed throughout the US to industrial, commercial, and retail customers, as well as to OEM and aftermarket lamp manufacturers. The business unit has five sales channels: its industrial and commercial groups market SYLVANIA products to respective industrial and commercial customers through a nationwide network of independent electrical distributors; the consumer products channel sells SYLVANIA household lamps through retail stores to residential customers; the OEM channel sells lamps and ballasts to fixture manufacturers; and special markets focuses on specialty manufacturers, distributors, and the export market. The company sells products for homes, businesses and vehicles under the OSRAM, Traxon, ENCELIUM and SYLVANIA brand names.

Parent OSRAM AG's global automotive lighting business, is the world's largest manufacturer of automotive light sources. That business designs and develops products and manufactures and markets hundreds of lamp types for cars, trucks, and electronics.

Most of OSRAM SYLVANIA's products are marketed in North and South America under the SYLVANIA or OSRAM brand names. Its lamp and ballast products are geared at the following markets: aerospace, automotive, commercial, industrial, machine tool, mining, original equipment manufacturers, and retail. It markets its products through an authorized dealer network.

Financial Performance

The company saw marginal decline in revenues in 2013 largely due to the drop in the traditional general lighting business and OSRAM SYLVANIA's exit from the traditional maintenance business in the US.

According to the US Department of Energy , lighting is said to be responsible for about 20% of electrical energy consumption in the US. Consumers and businesses spend approximately $37 million a year to light their homes, offices, streets, and factories. However, advanced technologies can significantly reduce energy consumption and costs. LED lighting coupled with lighting management systems, for instance, can save up to 80%. Demand for such products and integrated systems is increasing due to both consumer and government outcries for better energy savings. This demand is leading to reduced usage of incandescent light bulbs and increased adoption of LED-based lighting.

In response to this demand, OSRAM SYLVANIA is busy building out its energy-efficient lighting portfolio, introducing a comprehensive SYLVANIA fixture line for commercial and residential use that includes LED modules, power supplies, controls, lighting services, and LED retrofits. It is also making improvements to predecessor products, including its high-efficiency QUICKTRONIC, T5, T5HO, and T8 PROstart electronic ballasts and longer-life OCTRON lamps.

In 2015 the company introduced OSRAM LIGHTIFY connected lighting kits (indoor and outdoor lighting products that can be controlled and automated via an app on a customer's mobile device).

In 2014 it expanded its OSRAM OPTOTRONIC Programmable LED power supplies, which include outdoor drivers, and grew its indoor portfolio, and also launched a line of ORIOSTM LED lamps.

That year Lowes introduced SYLVANIA ULTRA iQ, the first LED bulb designed to work with the Lowe's Iris home management system.

In 2013 OSRAM SYLVANIA teamed up with Comcast to offer energy efficient light bulbs that can be controlled remotely from anywhere through Comcast's Xfinity Home platform. Comcast also plans to enhance its Xfinity Home service by providing Sylvania's energy-saving LED smart light bulbs that can save up to 83% in energy costs and last up to 17.5 times longer than standard incandescent light bulbs.

Strengthening its general lighting business unit's position in light emitting diode-based technologies and lighting control software, OSRAM SYLVANIA signed an agreement with Townsend Ventures in 2011 to increase its stake in Encelium Technologies from 15% to 100%. Encelium complements the company's general lighting portfolio of energy-efficient lighting products for industrial and commercial buildings.

OSRAM AG transitioned into a stock corporation from a limited liability company called OSRAM GmbH in 2011. The year before OSRAM SYLVANIA Inc. merged into OSRAM SYLVANIA Products Inc., with the resulting entity renamed OSRAM SYLVANIA Inc.

OSRAM SYLVANIA is a unit of OSRAM Licht , a subsidiary Siemens .
