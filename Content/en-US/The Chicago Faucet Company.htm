The Chicago Faucet Company (Chicago Faucets) has something to spout off about. The company makes about 1,000 faucet components with more than 55,000 possible configurations. Six plants in the US manufacture handles, spouts, outlets, and inlets for residential, commercial, industrial, safety, and medical markets. All of the faucets are modular, interchangeable, and cast in brass. The company sells through plumbing distributors and sales representatives in the US and in most regions of the world. Geberit AG , a Switzerland-based sanitation system manufacturer, owns Chicago Faucet. The company traces its roots to 1901 when founder Albert Brown opened a small plumbing fixtures shop in Chicago.
