Grede (pronounced "grady") wants to receive a "Grade A" for supplying metal components to the auto sector. The company operates over a dozen foundries and a handful of machining facilities to manufacture gray and specialty iron castings, including lost foam and high silicon molybdenum applications. It also provides chassis and exhaust systems, mounts and brackets, brakes and pumps, and diesel engine components. In addition, it offers services, such as design, painting, machining, and testing. Customers have included such heavy hitters as Caterpillar , SMW Automotive, Mercedes-Benz , Toyota , and Volvo . In the summer of 2014, Grede was acquired by Metaldyne Performance Group .

Grede has its headquarters located in Southfield, Michigan, and it operates 14 foundries in the US and Mexico and three machining locations in the US catering to the transportation and industrial markets.

The company specializes in high-quality ductile, gray, and specialty iron castings, including lost foam and high silicon molybdenum applications. Castings include highly complex and safety critical designs and range from 1lb. to 2,000 lbs.

Grede serves transportation and industrial markets, including agriculture, construction, rail, medium and heavy truck, mining, petroleum and wind energy. Its customers include Caterpillar , Hitachi , Honeywell , Mercedes-Benz , SMW Automotive, Toyota , and Volvo .

In 2014 Grede introduced lower leaf spring support brackets for the Chevrolet Silverado using SiboDur, a new ductile iron alloy. In 2013 it won a $57 million contract with a major Japanese OEM to produce front knuckles and other chassis components.

To help meet the growing demands of its automotive and heavy truck customers in the region, in 2013 Grede expanded its NovoCast ferrous casting foundry in Monterrey, Mexico. It added a new pressure pouring system, adding 8,000 metric tons per year (or 12%) of capacity.
