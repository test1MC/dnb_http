Organic Resource Management Inc. (ORMI) turns organic waste into feedstock for biogas and other renewable energy production in Canada. The company collects non-hazardous, liquid food residuals from more than 10,000 grease traps at restaurants, grocery stores, and other food processing facilities in British Columbia and Ontario. The residuals are taken to ORMI facilities for processing and used in anaerobic digesters to create green energy for Ontario's power grid. ORMI was acquired by diversified Canadian corporation Walker Industries Holdings Limited in late 2012. At that time, ORMI was delisted and became part of the Walker Environmental Group (WEG) division.
