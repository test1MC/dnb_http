Ventura Foods is one slick operator. The company makes and distributes branded and private-label vegetable oil-based products, including bulk salad oils, shortenings, margarine, butter and butter blends, pan coatings, mayonnaise, dressings, and more. It also produces soup and flavor bases and concession items, such as popcorn popping oil. Products are sold to foodservice operators such as Sysco and, less so, other food product manufacturers and retail grocers. Core brands are Marie's Dressing, Dean's Dip, Gold 'n Soft margarine, and LouAna oils. The company is a 50-50 joint venture, created in 1996 between US agricultural co-op CHS and Wilsey Foods, a subsidiary of Japanese trading conglomerate Mitsui & Co.

Ventura Foods produces more than 3 billion pounds of finished goods each year, which generate about $2.5 billion in revenue. It's the largest maker of vegetable oil-based products in the US and the largest maker of margarine for the foodservice sector.

Ventura Foods boasts a dozen manufacturing, packaging, and distribution locations across the US.

About 60% of sales come from private-label products produced for third parties; the other 40% comes from branded products.

It produces private-label products as a contract manufacturer and uses a direct sales model to retailers, food distribution companies, and large institutional food service companies. More than half of sales are in foodservice; the remainder is split between retail and industrial customers who use edible oil products as ingredients in foods they make for resale. As part of its business, Ventura Foods offers its customers a full range of marketing programs and promotional materials, such as national flyers, point-of-sale materials, recipe brochures, and more.

Ventura Foods has identified the Asia-Pacific region as a key market and ready for expansion. In 2014, the company tapped a unit of D&L Industries to develop and produce specialty oils and ingredients to export and sell locally. The venture sources goods at the Philippine firm's plant in Quezon City.

Positioning itself as a leader in the science of food research and menu development for the food service industry, Ventura Foods enjoys a partnership with culinary arts educator The Culinary Institute of America (CIA). In 2013 the food manufacturer expanded its existing partnership with the group through the Ventura Foods Center for Menu Research and Development at the CIA's campus in California.
