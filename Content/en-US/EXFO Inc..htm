EXFO has the info on communications testing equipment for next-generation networks. Its handheld and modular testing platforms are used by telecommunications carriers, cable television companies, and public utilities to test and monitor wireline and wireless fiber-optic networks. Optical component manufacturers also use EXFO equipment to test their products. In addition to its off-the-shelf equipment, EXFO designs and builds custom testing products from its manufacturing plants in Canada, China, Finland, and the US. Serving some 2,000 customers, products are sold in about 100 countries; the Americas counts for 54% of sales. Founder Germain Lamonde holds about 95% of EXFO's voting rights.

EXFO targets specific market segments in telecom, such as portable optical products, data communications testing, and optical transport equipment.

Physical-layer is the company's biggest revenue producing product line, bringing in 64% of sales. The protocol-layer line supplies the remaining revenue.

The US generates 37% of EXFO's revenue making the company's biggest market. Europe and the UK account for 26% of revenue with the Asia/Pacific region accounting for 20% of revenue.

EXFO goes through its own sales force as well as distributors to reach customers. Its biggest customer has accounted for about 6% of revenue for several years.

In 2015 EXFO's revenue slipped 4% to $222 million from about $231 million in 2014. A 19% drop in protocol-layer sales wiped out a 9% gain in physical-layer sales.

Net income rose nearly 600% to about $5.3 million in 2015 from 2014, a gain attributed to currency exchange rates that benefited EXFO, which is based in Canada.

Cash flow from operations dropped to about $6.5 million in 2015 from about $19 million in 2014.

EXPO took steps to expand its market with new products and to ensure that its product pipeline is full. Going forward the company intends to expand its sales to tier-1 network operators and data center, cloud, and web-scale service providers.

It spent $50.1 million on research and development expense in 2015 to develop new offerings. As R&D proceeded, the company released 13 products or product enhancements during the year such as EXFO Xtract, NetBlazer 800 v2 series.

Furthermore, the company implemented a restructuring plan to align costs to what it termed a "challenging market environment." The restructuring resulted in one-time severance expenses of $1.6 million for employees laid off during the year.

Back in 2014 EXFO acquired ByteSphere, a privately held software company that specializes in global IT management and network monitoring. This deal extended EXFO's capabilities in network performance management for infrastructure monitoring through highly scalable device and network element polling.

In 2006 the company expanded by acquiring the assets of Consultronics Limited for about $19 million. Consultronics, which had operations in Canada, Hungary, and the UK, sold broadband access testing equipment. The firm became part of EXFO's Telecom division.
