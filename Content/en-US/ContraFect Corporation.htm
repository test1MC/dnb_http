Do drug-resistant hospital super bugs scare you? ContraFect is not afraid. The development-stage biotech company is working on therapeutic protein and antibody treatments for antibiotic-resistant diseases like Staph and pneumonia usually contracted in hospital settings. It uses lysins, enzymes that can eat through bacteria walls, and monoclonal antibodies (mAbs), which "disarm" bacteria and viruses so that the body's natural immunity can defeat them. ContraFect's two lead products uses lysins against Staph, and mAbs against a variety of flu viruses, respectively. The company formed in 2008 and went public in 2014, raising $36 million for drug development. It had a $57 million total deficit at the end of 2013.
