Brazil's largest tobacco company, Souza Cruz controls about 60% of its home cigarette market. The firm, established in 1903, produces cigars, cigarillos, pipe tobacco, and various cigarette brands, including Carlton, Derby, Free Fresh, Hollywood California, Dunhill, and Vogue, as well as brands of parent British American Tobacco , which owns a 75% stake. Souza Cruz directly services some 300,000 retailers across Brazil and in nearly 5,000 cities and towns. It produces nearly 80 billion cigarettes a year, selling them primarily in Latin America. The firm exports tobacco leaf to more than 50 countries on five continents. Its BrasCuba joint venture with the Cuban government sells Popular cigarettes in Cuba.
