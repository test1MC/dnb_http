Someone's in the kitchen with Dinah, and most likely it's Elkay Manufacturing. Elkay makes sinks, cabinets, drinking fountains, faucets, water coolers, and water filtration products for residential and commercial use. The family owned company sells products such as stainless steel sinks under such names as Harmony, The Mystic, and Elite Gourmet. It also offers kitchen accessories (colanders, cutting boards, soap dispensers). Elkay's cabinet brands include Yorktowne, Medallion, Schuler, and MasterCraft. Elkay is the parent of about dozen subsidiaries and one joint venture, Elkay Pacific Rim.

Elkay operates 13 production plants and more than a dozen distribution centers in North America (Canada, Mexico, and the US) and overseas in China.

The company provides its products for applications across commercial and residential, healthcare facilities, schools and stadiums, restaurants, airports, office buildings and single and multi-family homes. Elkay sells its products online, through sales representatives, distributors, and at showrooms, home centers, and hardware stores. Customers include such notable names as Home Depot and Lowe's .

Elkay's innovative products and production techniques have enabled it to become the world's largest manufacturer of stainless steel residential sinks.

In 2015 the company's Medallion Cabinetry brand launched a new finish, Appaloosa, in addition to other new products, floating shelves and five-piece drawer front modification.

In 2014 Elkay's Yorktowne Cabinetry brand launched new Iconic door and two new finishes -- Cappuccino and Peppercorn.

In 2012 the company opened a new showroom in Shanghai, hoping to take advantage of the increased interest in American-made cabinetry products in China.

In 2012 Elkay joined forces with CuVerro Antimicrobial Copper to introduce a line of antimicrobial copper sinks designed for infection control in healthcare facilities. This partnership helped Elkay to build its healthcare business and combat the industry's ongoing battle against hospital-acquired infections. The sinks take advantage of the unique bacteria-killing characteristics of uncoated copper, which are greatly enhanced in the copper alloys used by CuVerro.

The company was founded in 1920 by the Katz family to make sinks for butlers' pantries and sculleries.
