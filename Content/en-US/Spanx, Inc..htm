Spanx helps women draw the line on pantylines and men smooth their physiques. The apparel company makes footless and footed hosiery, tights, and body shapers to give wearers a slim and smooth look. Spanx sells bras, slips, panties, and lined apparel. Its Spanx for Men includes undershirts and bottoms. The company's shapewear products are sold at such retailers as Nordstrom , Saks Fifth Avenue , Lord & Taylor , and Bloomingdale's , as well as through half a dozen of its own stores. Target developed ASSETS bodyshaping hosiery with Spanx; they're also sold at www.loveassets.com. Credited with inventing footless pantyhose, Sara Blakely founded Spanx in 2000 with $5,000 in savings and patented her pantyhose.

Based in Georgia, Spanx enjoys a global reach across more than 55 countries, including the Americas, Europe, Asia-Pacific, the Middle East, and Africa.

The Spanx portfolio of products has grown to include more than 200 items. They range from slimming apparel and swimsuits to bras, activewear, and men's undershirts.

Initially selling its products through other retailers, Spanx in late 2012 opened its first brick-and-mortar store in suburban Washington. It has since added stores in Connecticut, New Jersey, Virginia, Pennsylvania, Florida, and Texas (2014).

Through an alliance with discount retailer Target , Spanx generates sales under the ASSETS brand name. The line includes a range of body-shapers, as well as a luxury extension that features slimmers, bras, and panties.

During its first decade in business, Spanx sales have reached about $150 million annually. Looking to expand its target audience beyond women, Spanx in 2010 developed a line of undershirts for men that feel like a wetsuit. Styles include V-necks, crew necks, and tanks. Springboarding from the success of its Zoned Performance and cotton compression undershirts, Spanx quickly added a line of bottoms for men. In 2011 the firm launched Mama Spanx, a new range of control underwear for pregnant women.

In recent years, Spanx has added another target audience: plus-size women. As a result, the company distributes its products to niche retailers the likes of Catherines and Lane Bryant . Spanx, which range in size from A to G (approximately a size 16), now boasts some 100 different product styles.

Spanx products are sold through more than 12,000 retail locations nationwide.

The SPANX portfolio includes brand names SPANX by Sara Blakely, ASSETS, ASSETS Red Hot Label by SPANX, and Star Power by SPANX.

Celebrity Spanx fans have included Madonna, Kate Winslet, Tyra Banks, and Gwyneth Paltrow.

Spanx received a significant boost in 2000, when Blakely appeared on Oprah as one of Oprah Winfrey's "Favorite Things" show. Spanx, with the help of money awarded to Blakely through Sir Richard Branson's Rebel Billionaire show, founded the Sara Blakely Foundation in October 2006. Since then, Blakely has donated $1 million to The Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy Foundation in South Africa.

Awards & Recognition

Blakely in 2012 was featured on the cover of Forbes Magazine as the youngest self-made female billionaire.
