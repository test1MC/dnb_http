If you're facing hellacious weather when you're headed out, you might turn to Helly Hansen (UK). The company is the British arm of Helly Hansen, a Norwegian maker of outdoor sports apparel. Its foul-weather offerings include jackets, vests, gloves, and shells to keep the wearer (man, woman, or child) warm and dry. The company also makes protective technical workwear for harsh outdoor environments. Customers include consumers (who can purchase items via its website or other online retailers), retail stores, and government agencies, such as the US Navy . Founded in 1877 by sea captain Helly Hansen, the apparel maker is owned by The Ontario Teachers Pension Plan .

The Ontario Teachers Pension Plan acquired 75% of the Helly Hansen Group from Sweden's Altor Equity Partners in 2012.

Prior to this, a number of suitors had signaled an interest in bidding for Helly Hansen, including international lifestyle apparel giant VF Corporation , US consumer product maker Jarden Corp. , French luxury and retail group RPP (which owns Gucci and Puma ), and Columbia Sportswear .
