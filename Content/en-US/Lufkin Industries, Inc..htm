Lufkin Industries is all geared up to produce oil pumps and power equipment. Through its Oil Field division it makes and services pumping units, automation equipment, foundry castings, and other equipment. It also provides computer control equipment and analytical services used to maximize well efficiency. As part of Lufkin Industries' vertical integration, the Oil Field segment operates an iron foundry to produce castings for new pumping units, and for third parties. Through its Power Transmission unit, Lufkin Industries manufactures and services gearboxes (in power levels from 20 to 85,000 horsepower) used in large-scale industrial applications. General Electric bought the company in mid-2013.

With an eye toward doubling its oil and gas revenues, GE in mid-2013 acquired Lufkin Industries in a deal valued at $3.3 billion. The behemoth is particularly interested in the Texas-based oil and gas industry provider for its rod lift, gas lift, hydraulic lift, progressive cavity pumps, and well automation and production optimization controls and software. Buying Lufkin Industries also allowed GE to broaden its fast-growing GE Oil & Gas ' artificial lift capabilities beyond electric submersible pumps.

Lufkin Industries operates manufacturing locations in Argentina (1), Canada (3), France (1), and the US (3). In also has a manufacturing plant in Romania.

The Oil Field segment makes and services artificial lift products, including reciprocating rod lift, gas lift, plunger lift, and progressing cavity pump equipment, and related products. The Power Transmission unit designs and makes speed-increasing and -reducing gearboxes and related parts for industrial applications.

Lufkin Industries' Oil Field customers include independent oil and natural gas production companies, integrated oil companies, and national oil companies. Its Power Transmission customers include owners and operators of chemical plants, refineries, and industrial manufacturers.

The company claims that about 76% of oil and gas companies in North America have used products in their operations. Lufkin Industries has seen a resurgence in rod lift unit sales in recent years due to increased development of oil and liquids-rich formation such as the Bakken and Eagle Ford Shale plays.

Prior to its acquisition by GE, the company's overall strategy was to extend its position as a leading global supplier of high quality artificial lift and power transmission products, and related aftermarket services, through organic growth and acquisitions.

In August 2015GE Oil & Gas announced plans to close its plant in Lufkin and eliminate 262 positions at the plant and at one other facility.

To grow its position as the leading provider of automation systems integrated with artificial lift technologies, in 2012 it acquired Ireland-based Datac Instrumentation and RealFlex Technologies. That year it also bought UK-based automation business Zenith Oilfield Technology, for $127.3 million.

In 2011 the company acquired Canada-based Pentagon Optimization Services, which provides plunger lift systems and well optimization products and services. The acquisition bolsters the company's presence in Canada, and introduced Lufkin Industries to the well optimization, testing, and services market. That year the company also bought privately held Canadian firm Quinn's Oilfield Supply for $311 million.

Lufkin Industries was founded in 1902.
