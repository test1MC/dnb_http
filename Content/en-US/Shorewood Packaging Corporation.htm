How many cartons would a company cut, if a company could cut cartons? A lot, if it's AGI-Shorewood. The company makes paperboard packaging for cosmetics, drugs, tobacco, and other consumer products in North America, Europe, and Asia. It provides conversion and finishing processes, such as hot foil stamping, die cutting, and folding, as well as sheet-fed, and Web printing. The company specializes in entertainment and multimedia packaging, including products for computer software and music. It also offers graphic design and art direction in conjunction with its packaging services.

AGI-Shorewood operates almost 20 lithographic, flexographic, digital, and specialty gravure print and injection-molded plastics manufacturing facilities in North America, Europe, Asia, and Latin America with eight creative services centers in the US, the UK, France, China, and Australia.

In 2014 ASI-Shorewood acquired the Bydgoszcz, Poland-based beauty and personal care folding carton business of MeadWestvaco . Bydgoszcz is a supplier of packaging to major global and regional consumer goods companies, including cosmetics and beauty brands. ASI-Shorewood aims to leverage the Bydgoszcz operation's assets to expand its footprint in the European consumer-packaging market.

In 2012 International Paper merged Shorewood Packaging with Atlas Holdings ' AGI World to create AGI-Shorewood. The AGI-Shorewood merger created one of the largest specialty packaging businesses in the world. Atlas Holdings owns 100% of AGI-Shorewood in the US and 60% outside of it. International Paper owns a 40% of AGI-Shorewood outside of the US.
