Formosa Chemicals & Fiber was established to meet the rising demand in fiber and plastic products in Taiwan. Since its founding in the mid- 1960s, the company, which is primarily engaged in petrochemical production, has expanded into specialty chemicals, plastics, textiles, nylon, and the engineering and construction of industrial parks throughout the country. It also is engaged in carpet products, and generates electricity and provides water treatment services. The company is a member of the Formosa Plastics group and is listed on the Taiwan Stock Exchange.

Formosa Chemicals & Fiber major businesses are the production and sales of petrochemical products, including PTA, PS, AN, Butadiene, SM polymer, SM, benzene, toulene, p-xylene and o-xylene, as well as nylon fiber, and rayon staple fiber. It also engages in spinning, weaving, dyeing, and finishing.

The company has diversified into petrochemical and power generation businesses with plants in Changhwa, Ilan, Dongshan, Chiayi Hsin Kang, and Yunlin Mailiao, turning itself into a major manufacturer of chemicals, fibers, petrochemical and plastic materials in Taiwan.

In 2014 The 1st Petrochemical Division contributed 29% of Formosa Chemicals & Fiber's total revenues; Plastics Division, 21%; 2nd Petrochemical Division, 15%; 3rd Petrochemical Division, 13%; Formosa Taffeta, 6%; Formosa Advanced Technologies, 2%; and Other divisions, 14%.

In 2014 the company's net revenues decreased by 6% due to lower results in all the company's business divisions.

Net income decreased by 47% due to lower net sales and higher operating costs and selling expenses.

In 2014 Formosa Chemicals & Fiber's cash from operating activities decreased by 16%.

The company anticipates strong demands for its major products in Asian markets. It is targeting the demand growth in Asian markets by undertaking process debottlenecking or capacity expansion to become a steady supplier for downstream operators in the region. For major target markets (in particular China -- 42% of 2014 sales) it plans to set up a distribution center or seek a strategic alliance to gain access to marketing channels to expand its market share.

In 2015 subsidiary Formosa Biomedical agreed to acquire 22% of a pharmaceutical company for T$600 million.

Formosa Chemicals & Fiber got its start in 1965 as a fiber plant producing wood pulp from trees harvested from Taiwan's mountainous regions.
