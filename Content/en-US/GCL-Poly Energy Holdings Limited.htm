GCL-Poly Energy didn't go into business single-mindedly. The group is one of the world's leading producers of polysilicon, the raw material used to make photovoltaic cells to convert sunlight into electricity. Through its subsidiaries, GCL-Poly annually produces more than 70,000 metric tons of polysilicon and 14 gigawatts of polysilicon wafers. In addition, the group owns or is developing several large solar farms in locations including China, South Africa, and the US; their combined capacity totals more than two gigawatts. The group earns the majority of its revenue from the sale of wafers. In late 2015, GCL-Poly sold its stakes in its non-solar power plants including a wind farm.
