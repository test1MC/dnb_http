Go ahead, tip your glass to Schott. The company manufactures and distributes technical glass, materials, and components primarily to the automotive, architectural, defense, electronics, home appliance, optics, pharmaceutical, and solar power markets. Its products are used in everything from building windows and traffic lights to military vehicles and microscopes. Schott's mirror substrates are part the world's largest solar telescope. Schott, with subsidiary Schott North America, is the headquarters and holding company of North American subsidiaries of Germany-based Schott AG .

Schott operates through more than 15 divisions and subsidiaries in the US, Canada, and Mexico, including solar power module maker SCHOTT Solar . Schott's North American business accounts for about 23% of parent Schott AG's total sales.

Schott is targeting Asia as an area of growth going forward. It plans to increase its penetration within the pharmaceutical systems (special glass tubing, syringes, vials, ampoules, and cartridges), home technology (glass-ceramic cooktop panels), electronic packaging (high tech components), and advanced materials sectors.

It also plans to withdrawal from the making of polycrystalline photovoltaics, a type of solar panel, due to unstable political conditions, increased competition within the Asian region, and unprofitable growth within this division.
