No native of nowhere, this John from Britain boasts a lineage of luxury label knitwear. John Smedley manufactures fashionable knitwear garments for men, women, and children. Its pieces, which range from sweaters and cardigans to shirts and scarves, are sold through department stores and boutiques in more than 40 countries, as well as through company's London retail outlet and a website. Founded in 1784 by Peter Nightengale (uncle of famed nurse Florence) and the first John Smedley, the company operates Lea Mills, the world's largest fine-gauge knitting plant (located at its original site). The Smedley family continues to own most of the company. For the first time since the 1950s, a family member is at the helm.
