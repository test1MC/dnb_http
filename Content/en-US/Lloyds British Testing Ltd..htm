Lloyds British Testing primarily designs, tests, inspects, manufactures, and repairs cranes and other industrial cargo-loading machinery. The company has about a dozen branches located throughout the UK. Lloyds British Testing forms the core of Lloyds British Group, an entity that provides rental, repair, and engineering services to customers in the manufacturing and transportation industries, among others. It also provides some manufacturing services of lift-related equipment such as lifting beams and frames. Lloyds British Testing also provides training and support services to customers outside the UK. In 2017 UK-based equipment rental company Speedy Hire acquired Lloyds.

Following its acquisition in 2017 by equipment rental company Speedy Hire , Lloyds British Testing is no longer a private company.

Lloyds British operates 24 locations across the world including in the UK, Egypt, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE.

The company's offers preventative maintenance, modernization and refurbishment, and new crane technical support. Its services include inspection, testing (including non-destructive testing), operator training, design and manufacture, and equipment sales.

Lloyds British Industrial Products Division specializes in the supply of a comprehensive range of lifting, materials handling, safety equipment and restraint systems, engineering products, and jacking and access equipment.

The company serves power, construction, mechanical engineering, oil and gas services and petrochemical industries, among others. Its customers include BP , Cadbury , SNCF , Wartsila , and Weatherford .

Lloyds British expands through acquisitions. In 2015 the company bought Swindon Lifting Gear Limited.

Beyond acquisitions, the company has also expanded to new geographic markets by setting up offices in Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.
