Planes, trains, and automobiles rely on Michelin North America (MNA)'s tires to make their rounds. So do airplanes; bicycles; motorcycles; heavy trucks and agriculture, off-road, and specialty vehicles (like NASA 's lunar rover). With some 19 manufacturing facilities in 16 locations in the US, Canada, and Mexico, MNA makes and sells tires stamped with the Michelin, BF Goodrich, and Uniroyal brands. Its tires are sold to consumers, the military, OEMs, and replacement centers. A major subsidiary of France-based tire maker giant Michelin , the company also produces North American road maps and travel guides.

MNA manufactures tires in Alabama, Indiana, Ohio, Oklahoma, North Carolina and South Carolina. It also has three plants in Nova Scotia, Canada and one plant in Queretaro, Mexico.

Michelin designs, makes and sells tires for every vehicle, including airplanes, automobiles, bicycles, earthmovers, farm equipment, heavy-duty trucks, motorcycles, and the space shuttle.

Two plants specialize in making semi-finished goods taking raw materials and turning them into components for the plants that produce tires; one plant produces only synthetic rubber. MNA produces finished goods at roughly 20 plants; two plants produce retreads for the trucks; and one site produces retreads for the aircraft industry.

North America-based Michelin Business Solutions and Michelin Fleet Solutions units are dedicated to supply chain and logistics services. Its Tire Centers (TCI) subsidiary operates a network of dealership and service centers, as well as retreading plants.

The company sells its products basically through its sales force, along with 150 Tire Centers (TCi) and 20 TyrePlus centers based in Mexico.

The breadth of MNA's mobility portfolio helps the company weather ecomomic downturns in a particlar segment. It is also seeking to gain revenues by offering tires for niche market. The company's Energy Saver tires, for example, feature reduced fuel consumption and emissions, compliant with government energy performance labeling requirements. In addition, Michelin North America's silica-based snow and ice tires are experiencing a sales bonanza, spurred by Quebec's mandatory winter tire regulation.

Michelin's specialty tires also serve the defense market; the US Department of Defense is one of its customers. For more than five years, MNA has supplied aircraft and ground tires to the Defense Logistics Agency . Awarded two contracts, Michelin North America procures, stores, and distributes tires to all four branches of the US military. Moreover, a majority of the tires are from other tire manufacturers, thereby maintaining the military's industrial tire pool.
