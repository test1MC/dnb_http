As the physical infrastructure for the internet grows, so grows Radiall. The company manufactures electronic components for the aerospace, military equipment, telecommunications, and automotive industries. It makes antennas and microwave components (attenuators, couplers, terminations) for wireless networks and connectors for fiber-optic and coaxial networks. The company's rack and panel connectors are standard components used by aerospace giants Airbus and Boeing ; other clients include Alcatel and Continental . Radiall also offers radio-frequency base stations for mobile networks. Some 40% sales go to the US. The company was started in 1952 to make coaxial plugs for TVs.

Aerospace is Radiall's biggest market, accounting for 43% of the company's revenue. Defense and space generates 25%, followed by telecommunications, 19% and industrial, 13%.

Raidall sells components around the world. Its biggest market is the Americas (40% of revenue). Customers in Asia account for a third of revenue with France and Europe bringing in some 37% of revenue.

The company has design offices and production facilities in China, India, France, Mexico, and the US.

In 2015, Radiall posted revenue of about €290 million, a 4% increase from 2014 as foreign currency exchange rates worked in Radiall's favor. The integration of Van-System into its business also boosted revenue. International sales rose about 5% with help from the exchange rate to the US dollar.

Radiall's net income of about €29 million was off 7% from 2014. Higher costs in 2015 included some non-recurring expenses and a higher budget for research and development.

Radiall acquired VAN-SYSTEM in 2015 to bolster its offerings for the industrial market. VAN-SYSTEM designs and makes circular electrical connectors for industrial applications including those for harsh industrial environments.
