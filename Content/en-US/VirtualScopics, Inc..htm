VirtualScopics makes medical imaging analysis tools that help clinical researchers speed up the drug development process. Its patented algorithms let researchers analyze data from computed tomography, MRI, PET, and ultrasound scans, with the aim of helping pharmaceutical, biotech, and medical device companies determine how an investigational drug is working (or not working). The firm also hopes its products can be used to develop diagnostic tools to help with disease treatment and surgery. VirtualScopics provides services for many large pharmaceutical companies, including GlaxoSmithKline and Johnson & Johnson ; its largest customer is Pfizer . In 2016 BioTelemetry paid some $15.5 million to buy VirtualScopics.
