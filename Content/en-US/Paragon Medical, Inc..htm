Paragon Medical helps keep medical equipment safe and clean. The company designs and manufactures customized polymer and metal cases and trays used for surgical instrument storage and sterilization purposes, primarily for the original equipment manufacturer (OEM) market. It also provides engineering, design, and manufacturing of surgical instruments and implantable medical device components for OEM customers. Paragon Medical specializes in equipment and supplies for the orthopedic industry. Additional services include project management, consulting, sterilization validation, and inventory management. The company was founded in 1991 and is owned by private equity firm Beecken Petty O'Keefe & Company.

In 2013 former owners Altaris Capital and Seidler Equity announced they were looking for a buyer for Paragon Medical. The two investment firms acquired Paragon in 2005 for some $25 million; the capital was used to support the company's growth activities.

By the close of 2013, Paragon Medical had been acquired by an affiliate of Chicago-based Beecken Petty O'Keefe & Company , a private equity firm that focuses its efforts on the healthcare industry.

Paragon Medical has offices and manufacturing facilities in China (Changzhou), Switzerland (Lausanne), and the US (Pierceton and Indianapolis, Indiana; Smithfield, Utah).

The company specializes in software-based design and manufacturing of custom products. Its facilities include grinding and milling, metal fabrication, polymer sheet extrusion, and custom polymer forming. Paragon's Global Design Center allows engineers and OEM teams to work together on product development. Surgical products include cutting instruments, blocks, and guides, as well as handled and cannulated instruments. Implants include screws, plates, anchors, and rods, while its cases and trays range from standard and sample cases to tracking-enabled storage systems.

Paragon Medical pursues growth through both organic and acquisitive means, with a focus on expanding its capabilities to develop and make custom instrumentation and implantable components. The company opened a new Bioskills Lab in Pierceton, Indiana, in 2011 to provide product testing and surgeon training services.

It also forms partnerships with other manufacturing firms to widen its operations. For instance the firm has partnered with Solstice Medical to combine Solstice's asset tracking and lifecycle management technologies with the Paragon surgical cases and trays. In addition, the firm partners with Aesculap to provide customized sterile packaging systems for orthopedic devices.
