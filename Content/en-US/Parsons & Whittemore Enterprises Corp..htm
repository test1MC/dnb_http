Founded in 1909, Parsons & Whittemore Enterprises (P&W) is one of the world's largest producers of market pulp, the raw material used in papermaking. It is also a supplier of bleached kraft pulp, which is used to make paper bags, butcher wrap, newsprint, strong bond and ledger paper, and tissue. P&W has operated pulp mills in Alabama and Canada. In addition, the company touts turnkey engineering and construction of pulp and paper mills, and all of their related processes. Its experience includes some 60 projects in 28 countries. Chairman George Landegger and his family own the company. Landegger's late father, Karl, came to the US from Austria and bought P&W in 1944, then a small pulp-trading firm.
