Schwebel Baking produces a noseful of wonderful aromas. As one of the fastest-growing independent wholesale bakers in America, it bakes bagels, bread, buns, rolls, pita bread, and tortillas for the consumer food industry. The company's brands include Schwebel's, Sun-Maid, Roman Meal, Country Hearth, Milton's, Vogel's, and Aladdin's. Its products are available in Ohio, Pennsylvania, and New York at retail food stores, restaurants, and more than 30 company-owned bakery outlet stores. It also licenses its brands to other producers, and customers can have bread shipped directly to their homes. Schwebel Baking was founded in 1906 by Joseph and Dora Schwebel and remains a family-owned and -operated company.

Based in Youngstown, Ohio, Schwebel Baking serves customers nationwide and boasts plants in several states, including Ohio, New York, Pennsylvania, and West Virginia.

The company produces more than 700,000 packages of bread products each year. Its operations consist of a handful of baking facilities and 30-plus distribution centers across Ohio, Pennsylvania, New York, and West Virginia, as well as logistics company SBC Transportation, Inc.

Schwebel Baking services retail and foodservice customers, providing products under the names Schwebel's, 'taliano, Millbrook, Country Hearth, Cinnabon , Sun-Maid, and Roman Meal.

Schwebel Baking has earned the largest market share in several Ohio cities, including Cleveland, Youngstown, and Columbus. And while it competes each day against large, public companies with a reach nationwide, Schwebel Baking also has a noteworthy share of the baking markets in Erie and Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

Customers purchase products through bakery outlet stores located in Ohio, Pennsylvania, and New York, as well as through grocery stores, supermarkets, convenience stores, restaurants, schools, and churches.
