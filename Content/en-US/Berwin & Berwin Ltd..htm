Founded in 1885 by Belarusian immigrant Barnett Berwin, Berwin & Berwin is one of Europe's largest suit makers. The fourth-generation, family-run firm manufactures and distributes more than 18,000 men's tailored garments per week (of which 95% are made from merino wool) and supplies major UK retailers including NEXT , Ted Baker , and Moss Bros . The company has factories in Hungary and China turning out suits, casualwear, and overcoats. Beyond Britain, the firm distributes its apparel in France, Germany, Italy, Spain, and several other countries in Europe. The firm owns nearly 30% of menswear retailer Moss Bros Group, one of its largest clients.

Berwin and Berwin makes suits and casualwear under the Baumler, Berwin and Berwin, Daniel Hechter, Lambretta, and Paul Costelloe brands.

Headquartered in Leeds, England, the company has operation in china (factory), Vietnam (Factory), Hungary (Planning and Logistics) and Germany (Design, Sales and Service). In additional, the company exporting its products to countries such as Australia and Canada.

The UK generates around 75% of sales, followed by Europe at 22%.

In fiscal 2015 (ended December), revenue fell 7.2% to £40.5 million due to a re-focusing on its high-margin business. Despite that, margins in the year still declined by 1.2% as the strengthening of the US dollar push up the cost of sales. The company was also hit hard by the collapse of Austin Reed in 2016: Berwin & Berwin supplied 75% of the company's tailoring, and was a significant unsecured creditor when Austin Reed went into administration. The company wrote off over £800,000 of bad debts following the collapse. As a result, Berwin & Berwin incurred a net loss to the tune of £1.4 million in 2015, versus a gain of £809,000 in 2014.
