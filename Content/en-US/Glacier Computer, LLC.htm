Glacier Computer designs and sells ruggedized computer systems for the industrial manufacturing and public safety sectors. Its products include touchscreen-based workstations, tablet and laptop PCs, and handheld scanners. Its computer line includes models designed for use in vehicles such ambulances, police cars, and military vehicles as well as forklifts and other industrial equipment. Glacier also provides related accessories, such as keyboards and mounting systems. The company's devices run on Android, Linux,a several Windows-based operating systems. Its customers have included FedEx , Honda Motor , and Hormel Foods . The company was established in 2000 by Ron D'Ambrosio (president) and VP John Geary, among others.

Glacier works with a team of partners to equip and run its devices. They include the Allen Group, which provides web, software, and information technology; Brother International Corporation , which make a range of home, business, and industrial products; and Datalogic , which make data capture and industrial automation products.

Glacier's operations are based in the northeastern US. It is headquartered in Milford, Connecticut and it has a facility in Amherst, New Hampshire.
