Accellent (dba Lake Region Medical) provides the nuts and bolts for the medical device industry. The company offers outsourced design, engineering, and manufacturing of custom components, subassemblies, and completed devices, primarily for medical device firms. Its customers include such industry leaders as Medtronic , Abbott Labs , Stryker , and Johnson & Johnson . Lake Region Medical specializes in equipment used for cardiac rhythm management, interventional cardiology, neurology, and orthopedics, as well as urology and minimally invasive procedures (especially in the field of endoscopy). It also offers its customers complete supply chain management. The company owns or leases about 25 locations in North America and Europe.

The company's capabilities include product design, prototyping, and precision manufacturing, as well as device assembly, packaging, and warehousing.

Lake Region Medical has manufacturing facilities around the world, including China, Germany, Ireland, Malaysia, Mexico, and the US (California, Colorado, Georgia, Illinois, Massachusetts, Minnesota, New Hampshire, New York, Pennsylvania, Texas, and Virginia).

The comany's revenue base is made up of a diversified product mix with limited technology and the risk of product obsolescence. Many of the products it manufactures have been used in the medical device market for more than a decade, such as biopsy instruments, joint implants, pacemakers and surgical instruments.

Some of Lake Region Medical's business comes from industrial customers outside the medical device industry. For those customers, the company supplies components used in fiber optics, motion sensors, high density discharge lamps, and other products.

The firm believes it can benefit from the growing demand for high-tech medical devices (driven by an aging population, globalization, and the development of minimally invasive technologies), as well as its clients' increasing reliance on outsourcing to reduce costs and increase focus on research, sales, and marketing operations.

In addition to growing revenue from new and existing customers, Lake Region Medical wants to increase manufacturing efficiencies (using Lean Sigma) and expand its service offering, particularly in the areas of design, prototyping, and supply chain services.

To remain competitive, the company has to invest in research and development to introduce new and next-generation products to its customers.

The company rebranded itself after its 2014 acquisition of Lake Region Medical. As part of the combination of the two companies, it consolidated manufacturing operations in Galway, Ireland. It also shuttered a plant in Colorado.
