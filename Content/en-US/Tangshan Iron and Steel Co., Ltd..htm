Tangshan Iron & Steel is among China's largest steel companies and is in the process of getting even larger. The company's products include wire rods, hot-rolled steel sheets, and twisted steel bars. In addition, its four subsidiaries conduct mining exploration and make aluminum zinc alloys. Tangshan is 60%-controlled by the Chinese government, which pushed forward the country's steel industry consolidation in 2008 by combining Tangshan with Handan Iron & Steel and other companies located in Hebei province. The resulting holding company, named Hebei Iron and Steel, has an annual production capacity of more than 15 million tons.

Tangshan has major products in four categories: plates, rods, lines and sections, with a total of more than 140 varieties and more than 400 kinds of specifications. The main products are hot-rolled sheet, cold rolled thin plate, galvanized sheet, color-coated sheet medium and thick plates, stainless steel plate, bar, wire rod, and sections.

The company has modernized and expanded its processing equipment. Its cold-rolled sheet project is a national "the fifteenth plan" key project, bringing together a number of the world's leading metallurgical technology features. The 1,700 mm hot rolled sheet production line is fully supported by integrated technology.

Tangshan, Hebei Iron and Steel Group's key enterprise, was founded in 1943. The formation of Hebei Iron & Steel created China's largest steel company, ahead of Baosteel . At the end of 2008 Hebei announced that its Tangshan unit would buy Handan and another company that was part of the formation of Hebei, Chengde Xinxin Vanadium & Titanium.
