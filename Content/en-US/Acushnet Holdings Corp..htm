Acushnet is a top maker of golf balls, clubs, shoes, gloves, and other golfing equipment and accessories. Its famous Titleist golf balls and FootJoy golf shoes and gloves are #1 sellers in the US. Acushnet also makes value-priced Pinnacle golf balls, Titleist golf clubs, Scotty Cameron putters, and Vokey Design wedges, as well as golf bags and outerwear. Products are sold worldwide through golf pro shops, specialty sporting goods stores, and mass merchants. PGA players who have used Acushnet's equipment include Adam Scott, Davis Love III, Geoff Ogilvy, and Mark O'Meara. Footwear and sportswear maker Fila Korea owns a majority of the company.

Acushnet launched an IPO in late 2016 and raised $464 million in proceeds. It is using the proceeds to convert debt to equity. The IPO also frees up cash flow and allows it to further invest in its business.

The company operates through the reportable segments of Titleist golf balls (36% of net sales), Titleist golf clubs (26%), Titleist golf gear (9%), and FootJoy golf wear (28%).

The US represents 54% of Acushnet's net sales. Other major markets include the EMEA (13%), Japan (12%), Korea (10%), and other countries (11%).

Acushnet sells its products globally through retailers, including golf pro shops, specialty sporting goods stores, and mass merchants. It also markets its popular portfolio of brand names through celebrity athlete sponsorships. PGA players who have used Acushnet's equipment include Adam Scott, Davis Love III, Geoff Ogilvy, and Mark O'Meara.

Acushnet's revenues decreased 2% from 2014 to 2015. This slight decline was driven by declines from its golf balls (2%) and golf clubs (8%) segments. The company also experienced declines in the regions of EMEA (7%), Japan (7%), and other countries (11%).

Product innovation and brand loyalty are the key components of Acushnet's success. The company relies on aggressive protection of its technology patents and effective marketing, often using endorsements of tour and club professionals.
