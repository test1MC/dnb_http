Ferndale Pharma Group wants to help your skin flourish. Through a handful of healthcare specialty companies, the holding company is responsible for producing and distributing prescription, generic, and over-the-counter (OTC) medications. Its products primarily treat skin maladies. Ferndale Pharma Group also produces topical pain relief creams, surgical adhesives, and adhesive removers. The company markets and distributes its products internationally to physicians, hospitals, and consumers. It also offers contract drug manufacturing, warehousing, and distribution services to third parties. Ferndale Pharma Group was founded in 1897 as the J.F. Hartz Company.

Ferndale Pharma Group comprises several businesses under a holding company structure. In the US the group operates pharmaceutical production company Ferndale Laboratories & Contract Manufacturing, skin care company Biopelle, dermatology specialty company Eloquest Healthcare, and prescription and OTC products maker Ferndale Healthcare. Ferndale boasts a global presence through its product licensing company Ferndale International, UK hospital and pharmacy provider Ferndale Pharmaceuticals Ltd., and Aestheticare, which supplies aesthetic professionals in the UK and certain European markets with skin care products.

The company sells its products in the US through a direct sales force or through physician partners. In Europe Ferndale sells its products through subsidiaries Ferndale Pharmaceuticals Ltd. and Aestheticare. Other regions of the world are served through Ferndale International, which licenses out marketing rights for the company's products to distribution partners.

Ferndale Pharma Group has expanded its product lines over the years through internal development efforts and through select acquisitions. The company has not made any acquisitions, however, since its Biopelle unit widened its offerings by acquiring the PRESCRIBEDsolutions line of anti-aging products in 2009.

The company was founded in Detroit in 1897 as J. F. Hartz Company, and it originally began selling vitamins, tonics, elixirs, and surgical instruments. It operated under the J.F. Hartz name until the mid-1950s when it changed to Ferndale.
