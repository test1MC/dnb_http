Roger Cleveland Golf Company is in the swing of things. A maker of woods, irons, and putters, the company sells its golf clubs, as well as apparel and accessories, online and through pro shops and sporting goods stores in about 25 countries. The company also offers custom club fitting at golf courses and retailers across the US. Roger Cleveland, who got his start making replicas of classic golf clubs from the 1940s and 1950s, established his namesake firm in 1979. Today the company designs and forges its clubs from sturdy lightweight materials, such as steel, graphite, and titanium. It is owned by Japan's SRI Sports, a unit of Sumitomo Rubber Industries , which owns the Dunlop Sports brand in Japan.

Based in Huntington Beach, California, Roger Cleveland Golf enjoys an international presence through divisions that extend beyond the US to Canada, France, Germany, Japan, Korea, South Africa, Spain, and the UK.

Roger Cleveland Golf makes and markets golf club equipment, apparel, and accessories. It specializes in manufacturing putters, wedges, hybrids, and irons. Accessories include headwear, bags, travel gear, gloves, towels, and umbrellas.

The company sells its products through authorized dealers and online. As a service, it provides custom club fitting onsite at golf courses and through US retailers. It's known for its wedges -- its 588 wedge is one of the best-selling golf clubs ever -- and for developing innovative equipment with advanced technology. In addition to Roger Cleveland, the company markets golf clubs under the XXIO and SRIXON brands.

Roger Cleveland Golf gives traction to its name through sponsorships. In 2013 the company partnered with the Veteran Mental Health Foundation to jointly market the Invisible Wounds Cure Challenge, which generates funds for research studies focused on Post Traumatic Stress and Traumatic Brain Injuries.

To boost its brand image, Roger Cleveland Golf has been lending its name (and its clubs) to tournaments and professional players. The company sponsors the Byron Nelson Championship, the Cleveland Collegiate Golf Championship, and the Cleveland Golf Father/Son Team Classic at Pebble Beach, among other events. Its professional tour staff includes such big-name players as Vijay Singh, Keegan Bradley, Graeme McDowell, David Toms, Jerry Kelly, and Boo Weekley.

About 10% of Sumitomo's total revenues are generated by its sports division, which includes Roger Cleveland's operations. Sumitomo acquired Roger Cleveland in 2007, and since that time the parent has focused on strengthening its golf brand in the US.

Sumitomo also aims to extend the Roger Cleveland brand to emerging markets, where the game of golf has experienced an upswing in popularity.
