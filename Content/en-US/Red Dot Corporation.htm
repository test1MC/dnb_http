Red Dot keeps drivers from turning red in the summer and blue in the winter. Founded in 1965 and owned by its employees, Red Dot manufactures heavy-duty heating and air conditioning products for the heavy vehicle industry. The company, which got its start supplying integrated heating and air conditioning systems for Freightliner , Mack , and Peterbilt trucks, sells its systems and components to makers of trucking, mining, agricultural, construction, fire and rescue vehicles, and marine vessels. Other products include condensers, hydraulic compressors, filters and pressurizers, and replacement parts.

Red Dot operates factories in Tennessee, Washington, and the UK. In addition to its factories, Red Dot has established agreements with 200 distributors in 15 countries worldwide and in other countries scattered throughout Asia, Europe, and North and South America.

Red Dot divides its operates across several divisions: Red Dot Europe, Red Dot China, its Memphis Distribution Center, and JBAR (a subsidiary located in Cleveland).

The company distributes aftermarket products through three channels. It caters to warehouse distributors through more than 200 Red Dot distributor locations in the US and Canada. It targets private-label OE parts programs, and it sells to export markets to distributors in Asia, Australia, South America, Mexico, and New Zealand.

Red Dot has expanded in the past through the use of acquisitions. In 2011 it picked up JBAR A/C, an Ohio-based designer and maker of off-the shelf heating and AC units for heavy-duty vehicles. The deal strengthened Red Dot's presence in the eastern US and Canada.

Red Dot was founded by H.G. "Harky" Runnings, who started making custom-designed, heavy-duty radiators and heaters while he was running his own radiator service shop in the late 1950s. A family-owned business for the first 35 years of its history, Red Dot became employee-owned when Runnings transferred 100% ownership of the company to the Red Dot Employee Stock Ownership Program in 2000. The company is still run by the Runnings' family -- Harky's grandson Randy Gardiner is Red Dot's president.
