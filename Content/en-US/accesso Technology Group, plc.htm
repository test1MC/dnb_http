Accesso Technology Group wants to keep the flow going for its clients. The company develops theme park ride reservation systems. The systems consist of a central computer used to schedule rides, wireless communication stations located throughout the park, and handheld devices. In the company's Lo-Queue line of products, QBots are messaging devices that beep to let customers know when they can board a ride. For patrons with mobile phones, Qsmart communicates with smartphones when a ride is available. Accesso products are used by parks throughout the world, including about 10 Six Flags parks in the US. The company was inspired by the frustration founding director Leonard Sims felt once while waiting in line for an amusement park ride.

Besides the Lo-Queue products, accesso Technology has two other main lines of business.

Its accesso Passport is designed to sell tickets at the park as well as online via a computer or over a mobile device. The system help's the company customers up-selling and cross-selling products and services using proprietary technology.

The company's accesso Siriusware provides ticketing and admissions for every point-of-sale throughout a park. With accesso Siriusware software's include Ticketing & Admissions, Memberships & Passes, Reservations, Resource Scheduling, Retail, Food Service, Gift Cards, Kiosks, and eCommerce.

Though based in the UK, accesso Technology earns just 3% of its revenue from its backyard. The company's big moneymaker is the US and Canada -- 96% of revenue -- with their wide range and high number of theme parks. The company also sells in Europe and Australia.

The company's clients include International Speedway Corporation , Columbus Zoo and Aquarium, International Spy Museum, Cincinnati Museum Center, Premier Attractions, Cedar Fair Entertainment , Herschend Family Entertainment , and StarParks .

In 2014, sales for accesso hit $75 million compared to $61 million in 2013. Sales in the US and Canada rose 24% on growth in queuing revenue in general and per guest, reflecting a move to premium services.

Net income rose to $3.7 million in 2014 from $2.8 million in 2013. It might have been higher but accesso had higher administrative expenses.

In 2014, accesso acquired VisionOne Worldwide, a US ticketing and e-commerce provider to the entertainment sector. Accesso found offerings from VisionOne that complement its own.
