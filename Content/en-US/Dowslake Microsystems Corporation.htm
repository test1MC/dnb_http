Dowslake Microsystems manufactures optical subsystems and modules used to build communications equipment and network testing equipment. The company designs, manufactures, and sells carrier Ethernet and optical transport network devices from 2 megabits per second to 40 gigabits per second. Major customers include tier-one network equipment integrators, service providers, and government agencies. Outside its US headquarters, Dowslake Microsystems has international offices in China, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Spain, and Singapore. The company was founded in 2000 by CEO Dan Yang. She is also the CEO of Rullingnet Corporation, which sells the VINCI brand tablet computer she invented for children.
