For Lippert Components, making chassis brings the company both cachet and cha-ching. A subsidiary of LCI Industries , Lippert Components is a manufacturer of chassis for manufactured and modular homes. It also makes chassis for recreational vehicles (RVs) as well as galvanized roofing and utility, cargo, and horse trailers. Lippert Components fabricates parts for more than 150 customers. The company processes more than 90 tons of hot-rolled sheet steel per day in its fabrication operations. The company often works in tandem with sister company Kinro to supply components for vehicles.

Stationed in Elkhart, Indiana, Lippert operates through nearly 35 manufacturing facilities in 12 states: Alabama, California, Georgia, Idaho, Indiana, Kansas, Michigan, Oregon, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Texas, and Utah.

The company's customers include major manufacturers of recreational vehicles such as Thor Industries and Forest River . Its manufactured housing products are sold primarily to major producers of manufactured homes such as Clayton Homes , Cavco Industries , Champion Home Builders , Skyline Corporation , other OEMs, and, to a lesser extent, distributors of aftermarket products.

The company has strategically used small, selective acquisitions to expand its product lines. To that end, in 2013 it picked up assets of Midstate Tool & Die & Engineering, a designer and builder of automated equipment, tools, and dies. The acquisition is now part of the company's Lippert, Automation, Tool and Die division.

Lippert Components was founded in 1956.
