Dogs might be man's best friend, but Graco is mom's and dad's best buddy. The company is a unit of Newell Rubbermaid and part of its Baby & Parenting segment. Graco has built a business making and marketing a variety of accoutrements that make life as a parent more enriching. While Graco has expertise in swing-making, its products portfolio has grown to include highchairs, strollers, car seats, play yards, and activity centers. It also makes potty training items to help Junior say goodbye to diapers for good. Graco also sells furniture through a licensing agreement. Its products are sold nationwide at major retailers, such as Babies "R" Us , via shops in more than 40 countries, and through its website.

Graco sells its baby and children's products worldwide.

Alongside the Aprica and Teutonia brands, Graco is part of Newell Rubbermaid's Baby & Parenting segment, which accounted for 13% of its parent's fiscal 2012 sales.

Graco markets its products directly to mass merchants and department stores, such as Amazon.com , Target , Meijer , Kohl's , and other retailers.

Graco is the core operation of the Baby & Parenting business segment. As part of Newell Rubbermaid's work to strengthen this part of its business and extend its reach into international markets, it acquired add-on businesses Aprica, a Japanese manufacturer of strollers, and German stroller maker Teutonia.

Along with acquisitions, Graco reaches new customers by launching new products and by investing in consumer-driven research and development. The company in 2013 rolled out the FastAction Fold Jogger Click Connect, its first jogging stroller, and the first-ever Glider family of swings. Previous innovations include the Graco Sweetpeace Newborn Soothing Center, a baby swing that uses patent-pending movements to soothe infants, as well as the Graco Nautilus 3-in-1 car seat, which accommodates babies through children weighing in at 100 lbs. as they transition to a backless booster seat.

Graco has also extended its reach into new product niches through licensing deals. The company has been able to expand its products portfolio with the help of LaJobi Industries, a distributor and retailer of baby furniture.

While Newell Rubbermaid does not break out separate financials for Graco, net sales for its Baby & Parenting segment came in at $736 million in fiscal 2012, representing an 8% increase from $680.4 million in 2011. North American retail sales and sustained growth in the Asia Pacific markets both contributed to a core sales increase of 10%. North American and international sales rose 11% and 9%, respectively, during the reporting period.

Founded as Graco Metal Products in 1942 by Russell Gray and Robert Cone, the firm developed the first wind-up infant swing called the Swyngomatic in 1955. In the 1950s Graco designer David Saint, who has nine children, used to watch his wife soothe crying babies in a backyard swing. One of Saint's sons, Nate Saint, designed Graco's Pack N' Play portable play yards, which debuted in 1987.
