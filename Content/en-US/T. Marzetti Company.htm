Specialty food is the specialty of the house at this company. T. Marzetti is a leading maker of specialty food products, including salad dressings, condiments, dips, and other food toppings mostly sold under the Marzetti brand name. Its other products include croutons (Chatham Village), dry and frozen egg noodles (Inn Maid, Amish Kitchen, and Reames), and frozen baked goods (Marshall's, Mamma Bella, and Sister Schubert's). The food manufacturer sells its items nationwide, primarily through retail grocers; however, it also supplies foodservice operators. Begun by Teresa Marzetti in 1896 as a small restaurant in Columbus, Ohio, the company represents the specialty foods division of Lancaster Colony .

Ohio-based T. Marzetti operates more than a dozen facilities across its home state and in Alabama, California, Kentucky, Iowa, and Massachusetts.

T. Marzetti and its foods business are part of Lancaster Colony's specialty foods segment, which accounted for 87% of 2013's overall revenue. The dressings maker has become a big part of its parent's business, thanks to several deals inked during the past couple years. The company expanded its frozen baked goods line by acquiring the Marshall's brand of frozen rolls and biscuits T. Marzetti became the supplier of salad dressings for Wendy's restaurants and the firm opened a new manufacturing plant to boost production of its Sister Schubert's frozen breads.

T. Marzetti has been focusing on product development to ensure that the company is providing its customers with specialty food items that are popular. T. Marzetti has launched new lines of refrigerated salad dressings to compete with rivals the likes of Marie's that sell their products mostly through the refrigerated section. Chasing after the popularity of Greek yogurt, T. Marzetti rolled out a Otria Greek Yogurt Veggie Dip, touting it as a low-calorie yet flavorful alternative to typical sour cream based dips.

The company is a key component of Lancaster Colony's growth strategy, which includes expanding its specialty foods business through acquisitions.
