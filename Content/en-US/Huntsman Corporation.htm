Huntsman Corporation has a long track record of stalking business opportunities in the world's chemical marketplace. The global chemical manufacturer operates its businesses through subsidiary Huntsman International . Its broad range of products include MDI (methylene diphenyl diisocyanate), amines, surfactants, epoxy-based polymers, and polyurethanes. Huntsman's chemicals are sold worldwide to a variety of customers in the adhesives, construction products, electronics, medical, and packaging industries. Huntsman operates manufacturing and research and development facilities in about 30 countries worldwide. It gets 31% of its revenues from the US.

Huntsman operates more than 100 manufacturing and R&D facilities in more than 30 countries. The company has R&D centers in Everberg (Belgium), Shanghai (China), and The Woodlands, (Texas). Other regional development/technical service centers are located in Wynyard, UK, and Duisburg, Germany (pigments); Auburn Hills, Michigan, US (polyurethanes for the automotive industry); Basel, Switzerland and Panyu, China (advanced materials and textile effects); Billingham, UK (pigments); Derry, New Hampshire, Shanghai, China, Deggendorf, Germany and Ternate, Italy (polyurethanes); Melbourne, Australia (surfactants); Mumbai, India (textile effects); and Port Neches, Texas (process engineering support).

The company's MDI production facilities are located in Geismar, Louisiana; Rotterdam, The Netherlands; and through joint ventures in Caojing, China. It operates synthesis, formulating and production facilities in North America, Europe, Asia, South America and Africa.

Pigments and Additives operate 31 manufacturing facilities, have ten titanium dioxide manufacturing plants in Europe and 16 color pigments manufacturing and processing facilities in the US. It also operates five facilities producing water treatment, timber treatment chemicals, and functional additives.

In 2015 the US accounted for 31% of Huntsman's revenues; China, 11%; Germany, 7%; Mexico, 5%; and other countries, 46%.

The company operates through five business segments: Advanced Materials, Performance Products, Pigments and Additives, Polyurethanes, and Textile Effects.

The largest segment is the Polyurethanes unit, which makes MDI, propylene oxide, and propylene glycol for automotive interiors, footwear, and furniture cushioning. It is one of the largest producers of MDI, which is used in producing rigid and other types of polyurethanes and has boasted a growth rate of 7% annually since 1992. Other segments are Advanced Materials (epoxy, acrylic, and polyurethane-based polymers), Textile Effects (epoxy resins and adhesives), Performance Products (mostly ethylene-based chemicals used in detergents, paints, and fuel additives), and Pigments (the whitening agent titanium dioxide).

Polyurethanes accounted for 37% of revenues in 2015; Performance Products, 24%; Advanced Materials, 11%; Textile Effects, 8%; and Pigments and Additives, 21%.

They company makes differentiated organic chemical products and inorganic chemical products. Its products comprise a broad range of chemicals and formulations, which it markets globally to a diversified group of consumer and industrial customers. The products are used in a wide range of applications, including those in the adhesives, aerospace, automotive, construction products, personal care and hygiene, durable and non-durable consumer products, electronics, medical, packaging, paints and coatings, power generation, refining, synthetic fiber, textile chemicals and dye industries. Huntsman is a leading global producer in many of key product lines, including MDI, amines, surfactants, maleic anhydride, epoxy-based polymer formulations, textile chemicals, dyes, titanium dioxide and color pigments.

Huntsman markets polyurethane chemicals to more than 3,500 customers in about 100 countries. Major customers include companies in the appliance, automotive, footwear, furniture and coatings, construction products, adhesives, sealants and elastomers industries. It sells more than 2,000 products to more than 4,000 customers though the Performance Products marketing groups.

The company sells to customers in the following end markets: civil engineering, consumer appliances, food and beverage packaging, industrial appliances, consumer/do it yourself (DIY), aerospace, LNG transport, electrical power transmission and distribution, printed circuit boards, consumer and industrial electronics, wind power generation, automotive, recreational sports equipment and medical appliances. It also markets multiple products to customers in multiple end-markets, including the following: consumer fashion apparel, sportswear, career and uniform apparel, military, automotive, home textiles and furnishings, carpet and other functional textiles.

Advance Materials conduct sales activities through dedicated regional sales teams and also supported by a strong network of distributors.

About 85% of titanium dioxide sales are made through global sales and technical services network.

Huntsman sells iron oxides through its global sales force. The company's ultramarine sales are predominantly through specialty distributors. It sell the majority of its timber treatment products directly to end customers via Viance.

In 2015 net revenues decreased by 11% due to declines in all segments except for Pigments and Additives.

Revenues in Polyurethanes segment decreased due to a planned maintenance outage at PO/MTBE facility in Port Neches, Texas; lower MDI due to the market slowdown in China; and lower sales into commercial construction in the US. It was also hurt by the stronger US dollar.

The decrease in revenues in Performance Products segment was primarily due to lower average selling prices and lower sales volumes. Sales volumes decreased across most product lines, including the effect of the sale of European commodity surfactants business partially offset by higher toll volumes in upstream intermediates business.

The Advanced Materials segment revenue drop was due to lower sales volumes and lower average selling prices. Sales volumes decreased globally primarily in coatings and construction and transportation and industrial markets due to the deselection of certain business and competitive pressure, partially offset by strong volume growth in do-it-yourself and wind markets in the Asia/Pacific region.

The decrease in revenues in Textile Effects segment was due to lower average selling prices and lower sales volumes. Sales volumes decreased primarily due to the deselection of certain less profitable business and challenging market conditions.

In 2015 Huntsman's net income decreased by 71% due to lower net revenues and higher restructuring, impairment and plant closing costs.

Cash from operating activities declined by 24%.

The company's business strategy focuses on the three major markets -- apparel, home, and institutional and technical textiles.

Huntsman's strategy is to increase sales to its current customers while seeking growth in emerging international markets such as India and China. Huntsman has relocated the headquarters of some of its divisions overseas to be closer to critical markets, putting its Polyurethanes operation in Hong Kong and its Textile Effects unit in Singapore. Huntsman has also expanded its manufacturing capabilities overseas by expanding its current operations and acquiring other companies. The company believes that by integrating its different product operations in large facilities close to its customers, it can cut transportation costs and exposure to cyclical prices.

In addition, the company continued its restructuring activities that include workforce reduction in Europe. The Performance Products segment announced plans for a reorganization of its commercial and technical functions and a refocused divisional business strategy to better position the segment for growth in coming years. It also planned to close the 'black end' manufacturing operations and ancillary activities at its Calais, France site, reducing titanium dioxide capacity.

In 2015 the company spent more than $850 million completing its pigments restructuring and integration, and a number of global projects, including a once-every-five-year maintenance project.

In 2014 the company began a 400,000 metric ton expansion of methylene diphenyl diisocyanate by investing in a new, world scale MDI (diphenylmethane diisocyanate) plant at its Geismar, Louisiana site. The new capacity is expected to come on-stream in 2018.

Huntsman has also spent considerable capital investing in safety measures, environmental performance and the long-term growth of the company.

In 2014 the company saved nearly $100 million via initiative include relocating manufacturing from Europe to countries such as Thailand and China, where many of its customers are expanding. As part of a comprehensive restructuring program, Huntsman planned to reduce its workforce by 900 positions. Annual cost savings are expected to exceed $130 million.

In 2016 Huntsman sold its European Differentiated Surfactants business, valued at about $260 million, to Innospec .

Expanding its portfolio, in 2014 Huntsman acquired the performance additives and titanium dioxide businesses of Rockwood Holdings for $1.1 billion. On the other end of the scale, in 2015 it picked up Tecnoelastomeri (an Italy-based manufacturer and marketer of methylene diphenyl diisocyanate based hot-cast elastomer systems and processing machines) which has $12 million in annual sales.

Subsidiary Huntsman International was formed in 1999.

The founding Huntsman family controls about 16% of Huntsman Corporation, which was founded in 2004. Its predecessor, Huntsman Holdings, was established by Jon M. Huntsman in 1970.

In 2013 Huntsman and Shanghai Chlor-Alkali Chemical announced plans to double the MDI splitting capacity of their joint venture company, Huntsman Polyurethanes Shanghai Ltd. Co. at the Shanghai Chemical Industrial Park on China. The new plant, which will allow for MDI splitting capacity at the site to increase from 240,000 to 480,000 metric tons per year, is expected to commence commercial operations in 2017.

To provide technical support to its Asian customers, in 2013 Huntsman opened a new $40 million Asia Pacific Technology Center in Shanghai's Minhang Economic & Technological Development Zone.

In 2013 Huntsman also spent $76 million to buy Houston-based Oxid L.P., a privately-held maker and marketer of specialty urethane polyols (a key component in the production of energy saving polyurethane insulation products used by residential and commercial construction companies).
