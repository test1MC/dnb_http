McBride keeps Europe (and parts of Asia) well-groomed and squeaky clean. The continent's largest manufacturer of private-label household and personal care products, McBride sells its products to most of Europe's major grocery chains. The company's array of household products includes laundry and dishwashing products, hard surface cleaners, bleach, and air fresheners. Its personal care items include shampoo, soap, shaving cream, deodorant, toothpaste, and nail and skin care products. McBride has about a dozen manufacturing facilities in a handful of European countries and operates through subsidiaries in Belgium, Italy, the Netherlands, Spain, and the UK.

The company is organized into three segments which includes the UK (UK and Ireland; 35% of net sales), Western Europe (France, Iberia, Benelux, Italy, and Germany; 56%), and Rest of the World (Poland, Czech Republic, and Asia Pacific; 9%). It operates 17 manufacturing sites and operations in 12 countries.

McBride's customers include major food retailers, such as Tesco , J Sainsbury , ALDI , and ASDA Group (owned by Wall-Mart ). The company also operates the largest private-label aerosol business in Europe, named Aerosol Products Limited. Its products are manufactured in facilities across Europe.

McBride has experienced a slow decline in its revenue the last few years. Its revenues dipped by 5% from 2014 to 2015 primarily as a result of the weaker Euro. In addition, its UK segment experienced a 5% drop in sales due to price reductions, lower private label volumes, and a decline in contract manufacturing volumes. Western Europe segment revenue also decreased by 6%, while Rest of the World sales dipped by 6% during 2015.

McBride's roots reach back to the late 1800s when small laundry owner Camille Simon developed a cleaning product from an extract of soap. The company was not established under the McBride name until 1927.
