International automotive manufacturers, and aftermarkets, have been clutching LuK products for more than half a century. LuK GmbH (which stands for Lamellen und Kupplungsbau, or disc and clutch makers) manufactures and markets clutch systems, as well as transmissions systems, torque converters, dampers, such as dual mass flywheels, and related parts for tractors, including hydraulic clutch slave cylinders. The Bühl-based company, a subsidiary of the Schaeffler Group , produces at 17 manufacturing facilities in more than 10 countries, including the US where it operates under the name LuK USA . LuK also collaborates with sister subsidiaries FAG and INA on the development and production of automotive components.
