You'll never have to take the stairs if you work at Schindler Elevator. The company is the North American operation of Switzerland-based Schindler Holding , the world's largest escalator maker and second largest in elevators after Otis Elevator . Schindler Elevator designs, manufactures, installs, and services passenger and freight elevators (low-, mid-, and high-rise), escalators, and moving walkways for almost every type of building. Its products have been installed in such notable places as The White House, The Pentagon , the Orlando International Airport , and New York City's Rockefeller Center.

Headquartered in Morristown, New Jersey, the company has two major manufacturing plants in Gettysburg, Pennsylvania; and Clinton, North Carolina. It has 250 locations (mostly concentrated in New York and Florida) across North America. Its Canadian head office is in Toronto.

Elevator Products Corporation is a division of Schindler Elevator, and Adams Elevator Equipment Company is a sister company.

In addition to its products, Schindler Elevator provides maintenance and modernization services for all brands of elevator and escalator equipment. It stocks more than 85,000 different elevator and escalator parts for immediate shipping at its national service logistics center in Chicago.

Schindler Elevator offers its products for office buildings, airports, shopping centers/retail establishments, and specialty buildings.

Demand continues to be strong for its hydraulic elevators primarily used in low-rise, less-costly building projects. On the high-rise end, the company is going the green route, developing and producing more energy-efficient products, such as the Schindler 7000 elevator for skyscrapers. Elevator energy consumption is a topic of growing interest among architects, property developers, and real estate managers.

Schindler Elevator continues to grow through the acquisition of independent elevator service companies and by organic expansion.

The company was established in Toledo, Ohio, in 1979.
