Derrick produces dry and wet high-frequency screening units primarily for applications in the plastics, oil-drilling, mining, aggregate, chemical, wastewater, and wood processing industries. Derrick's products include centrifuges, dry-screening units, fiber extractors, high-shear screens, linear motion screens, recovery systems, repulp wet-sizing screens, resin screens, and stack sizers. The company is the world leader in the design and manufacture of machinery used to screen a wide variety of materials in the range of 3/8 inch to 38 microns. Derrick is controlled by the founding Derrick family.

Derrick divides its operations across the Derrick Equipment Company and Derrick International. Derrick develops solids control systems such as pyramid screens, shakers and centrifuges, while the Slurry & Underground division makes slurry separation equipment, including plants for horizontal drilling. The mineral processing operations makes dewatering machinery, urethane screens, and stack sizers.

Derrick serves the mining and industrial, oil and gas drilling, plastics, chemical, wastewater, forest products, and civil and underground industries. It sells its products worldwide through distributors such as Quality Control services de Mexico, Petrolink, Ramde International, and Grand Rig.

H. William Derrick Jr. established the enterprise in 1951. That year Derrick teamed up with Messinger Bearing Co. to develop the first four bearing high-speed screen vibrator.
