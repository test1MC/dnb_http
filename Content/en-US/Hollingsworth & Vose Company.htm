It's tough to get past Hollingsworth & Vose Company unless you're squeaky clean -- it filters out the riff-raff. The company manufactures products for engine filtration, air and liquid filtration, and battery separators using nonwoven materials. It also makes advanced composites as well as technical, filter, and specialty papers. The company's engine and industrial filtration products are made to perform in harsh environments and are used in automotive air, automotive lubrication, diesel, fuel, hydraulic, oil, and other specialty filtration applications. Hollingsworth & Vose's battery separators are made with uniform pore structure, chemical stability, and high wicking and absorbency.

The company has plants in Germany, the UK, and the US. It also has sales offices and research centers in Asia, Europe, and North America

Hollingsworth & Vose's products are found in a range of materials and applications, including air and liquid filtration, battery separators, engine filtration, gasket materials, and specialty and industrial nonwovens.

The company serves customers in a range of industries, including aerospace, apparel and home furnishings, energy generation and storage, heavy equipment, indoor air quality, manufacturing, and transportation.

Hollingsworth & Vose has grown organically and through buying complementary technologies, and by setting up joint ventures.

In 2014, to expand capacity.it planned to purchase the Floyd Flex Building from the Floyd County Economic Development Authority in Virginia to house its new Technostat production line.

In 2013 it also added NanoWave Filtration Media production capability in its plant in Hatzfeld in Germany to support the rapid growth in demand for high performance filter media.

To help the company to grow in India, in 2014 Hollingsworth & Vose acquired Raman FibreScience Private Limited.

In a rebranding initiative, in 2012 the company changed the business name of its Evanite Fiber Corporation subsidiary (which joined Hollingsworth & Vose in 1997) to Hollingsworth & Vose in order to strengthen that unit's marketing and sales profile.

In 2011 the company established a joint venture with Nath Group to set up a manufacturing operation in India. The plan includes construction of a new mill in Maharashtra. The investment will be financed by both companies, with Hollingsworth & Vose being the majority owner and managing partner. The mill will be capable of producing water- and solvent-based engine filter media, as well as products for HVAC filtration and battery separator applications.

That year it also announced a major capacity expansion at its Suzhou, mill in China. The expansion includes adding a new paper machine capable of producing water- and solvent-based filter media to serve engine and industrial filtration applications.

Founded in 1843 as a maker of cigarette filters, Hollingsworth & Vose is still managed by the founding Hollingsworth family.
