Rolling lubricants, like rolled oats, have this Quaker as a chemical maker. Quaker Chemical rolls out specialty chemicals for industrial and manufacturing processes. Its rolling lubricants are used in making rolled aluminum products and hot- and cold-rolled steel products. Quaker Chemical also makes corrosion preventives, metal finishing compounds, hydraulic fluids, and machining, grinding, and forming compounds. Other products and services include aerospace milling compounds, metal and concrete coatings, and chemical management services. Quaker Chemical's subsidiaries and joint ventures operate worldwide, although it sells primarily to the steel, auto, and appliance industries in the US, Europe, and Asia.

Headquartered in Pennsylvania, Quaker Chemical has major facilities in the US (Batavia, Dayton, Detroit, Middletown, Santa Fe Springs, Batavia); Monterrey, N.L., Mexico; Uithoorn, The Netherlands; Santa Perpetua de Mogoda, Spain; Rio de Janeiro, Brazil; Italy (Tradate and Gorgonzola); and Qingpu, China.

The company holds a 50% investment in Kelko Quaker Chemical, S.A. (Venezuela), Nippon Quaker Chemical, Ltd. (Japan) and Kelko Quaker Chemical S.A. (Panama), and held a 32% investment in Primex, Ltd. (Barbados).

North America accounted for 44% of Quaker Chemical's revenues in 2014;Europe, Middle East and Africa, 25%; Asia/Pacific, 24%, and South America, 7%.

The company's Metalworking Process Chemicals segment includes industrial process fluids for heavy industrial and manufacturing applications. Quaker Chemical's Coatings segment includes temporary and permanent coatings for metal and concrete products and chemical milling maskants. A third segment manufactures various other chemical products.

Worldwide, the company sells directly through its own employees through distributors and agents in Asia/Pacific, Europe/the Middle East/Africa, North America, and South America. Quaker Chemical is subject to the same business cycles as those experienced by its primary customers (aircraft, appliance, automobile, durable goods, and steel manufacturers).

In 2013 the company's five largest customers accounted for 18% of Quaker Chemical's revenues; Arcelor-Mittal Group accounted for 9%.

The company has reported steady revenue growth over the past few year. In 2014 its revenues increased by 5% due to higher product volumes (including acquisitions) partially offset by a lower price and product mix worldwide.

Quaker Chemical's net income has been on a similar trend as net revenues between 2010 and 2014. In 2014 the company's net income increased by 0.3% due to higher revenues and an increase in interest income.

In 2014 the company's cash inflow decreased by 26% as the result of changes in working capital stemming from higher accounts receivable and inventories.

Quaker Chemical's growth strategy is to focus on a limited number of products and to sell to specific markets in order to maintain quality and market leadership. The company seeks growth through a balance of capital spending and strategic acquisitions. Starting in 2000, it has made a careful and calculated expansion into the global market, expanding from its customer base in North America to new opportunities in South America, Europe, and Asia.

The company is also conducting an integrated marketing campaign which encompasses an updated brand identity, logo, and tagline.

Boosting its global coverage, in 2014 Quaker Chemical acquired Binol AB, a leading bio-lubricants producer primarily serving the Nordic region; US-based ECLI Products, LLC, a specialty grease manufacturer; and the remaining 49% ownership interest in its Australian affiliate, Quaker Chemical (Australasia) Pty. Limited.

In 2012 the company expanded its European presence by acquiring NP Coil Dexter Industries, an Italian manufacturer of steel coil coating and metal finishing products, for an undisclosed amount. NP Coil Dexter's coil products will complement Quaker's steel flat rolling process.

In 2011, Quaker Chemical acquired specialty lubricant-and-grease manufacturer Summit Lubricants for $30 million. Summit has a broad range of products which are used in military applications, and should help Quaker Chemicals expand its specialty chemicals business. The company expanded further in 2011 by picking up G.W. Smith & Sons, a top US die casting lubricant provider with commercial and manufacturing operations based in Dayton, Ohio. G.W. Smith, which has net sales of about $14 million per year, will operate as a standalone subsidiary.

That same year, Quaker Chemical acquired the balance of its Mexican joint venture partner, TecniQuimia Mexicana, S.A. de C.V. Quaker paid about $12 million to acquire the 60% of TechniQuima's outstanding shares. The deal strengthens Quaker's position with its Mexican automotive and steel customers.

The company was founded in 1918 as a manufacturer and supplier of oil products, primarily for lubrication and for the textile industry. Eagle Asset Management, Inc. owns 13% of Quaker Chemical; Royce & Associates, LLC, 12%.
