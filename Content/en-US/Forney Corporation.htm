Forney Corporation is en fuego . The company designs, manufactures, installs, and services front-end combustion equipment and related components. Its products include igniters, burners, burner management systems (BMS), flame detectors, and duct burners, as well as continuous emissions monitoring systems (CEMS). Forney Corporation maintains a presence in Western Europe and Asia, in addition to its manufacturing base in the US. R. H. Forney founded the company in 1927 as the Forney Combustion Engineering Company. It was a subsidiary of industrial machinery maker United Technologies until mid-2013, when it was acquired by Graham Holdings Company (formerly The Washington Post Company).

In a move to diversify its operations, Graham Holdings Company ( formerly The Washington Post Company ) acquired Forney Corporation in July 2013. Forney is a former subsidiary of United Technologies .

Forney is headquartered in Carrollton, Texas, and has manufacturing and sales offices in China, India, Japan, Mexico, Singapore, and the US.

With field offices and sales representatives throughout the world, Forney serves the electric utilities, chemical processing, pulp and paper, and cement industries. It offers 24-hour emergency service, as well.

The company offers a line of environmental systems for flue gas and stack monitoring. CEMplicity measures up to four gases simultaneously; the Vigilant system monitors all compliance gases at the same time; Forney's custom-designed shelter system handles eight systems in one housing; and the Coal Pre-ignition Detection System is designed to monitor coal handling and processing equipment.

Forney's network of offices and sales representatives serve companies in the Combustion Products and Systems market worldwide. Its customers include power plant and industrial system businesses

The company was established as Forney Engineering Company in 1927, a manufacturer of oil and gas burners for electric utilities. It was the first company to apply electronics technology into a burner management system.
