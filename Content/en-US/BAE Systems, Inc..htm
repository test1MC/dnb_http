BAE Systems takes care of business in North America for UK-based parent BAE SYSTEMS plc -- the largest foreign player in the US defense market. BAE Systems' military operations include the design, manufacture, and maintenance of armored combat vehicles, weapons, munitions, and other defense systems. Affiliate BAE Systems also provides security information technology (IT) systems for defense, commercial, and law enforcement applications. It is a top supplier to the US Department of Defense (DoD), and adheres to the Pentagon's Special Security Agreement to ensure control of sensitive technology.

The US division has operations in 38 states and in Germany, Israel, Mexico, South Africa, Sweden, and the UK.

The US arm is responsible for the development of BAE SYSTEMS' trans-Atlantic business, providing services to the US government and DoD, and managing US-based operating groups.

Given the US budget cuts under the Obama administration, many of the major defense players, such as Raytheon and Lockheed Martin have realigned their operations similarly to encompass electronics, aerospace, and information systems and services.

BAE Systems has been transitioning to a more manageable and cost-efficient structure. Although anticipation is high that there will be less government spending for traditional weapon systems (like combat vehicles), the Obama administration has signaled a move to build more high-tech systems that allow for military responsiveness, speed, flexibility, and precision.

BAE Systems is also supporting the development of unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) -- another project earmarked for continued government funding.

In a move that would expand its services for water-borne craft, BAE Systems plans to enlarge the dry-docking capabilities at its San Diego shipyard in 2015. On the expansion punch list are improvements to ship repair, maintenance, and modernization services for the Navy , other government agencies, and commercial customers. The project could total about $100 million.
