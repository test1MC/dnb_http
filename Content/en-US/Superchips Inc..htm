High-tech hot-rodders can turn to Superchips, which sells handheld tuners designed to provide additional horsepower to computer-controlled gasoline- and diesel-powered cars and light trucks. The tuner can be plugged into the vehicle's diagnostic connector, and it will automatically update the performance settings for air/fuel ratio, spark timing, and fuel injector flow rates. Its branded Mileage XS tuners reduce horsepower for up to 20% fuel efficiency. In 2005 Superchips was acquired by Autotronic Controls (dba MSD Ignition), a leading maker of performance ignition systems. Through MDS Performance, the company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in September 2013.
