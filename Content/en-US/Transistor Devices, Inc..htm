Transistor Devices, Inc. (aka TDI Power) is plugged into the power needs of the computer industry. Founded in 1960, the company makes high-power, fault-tolerant, uninterruptible electronic power supplies and systems designed to prevent power loss in transportation equipment and computer data storage and telecommunications systems. Customers include companies in the aerospace, computer/networking, industrial, medical, military, and telecommunications industries. TDI Power operates mainly in the US, but it has a manufacturing facility in China. It also has a global network of sales representatives located around the world to peddle its portfolio of products.

Transistor Devices is stationed in New Jersey and operates through three facilities in China and the US. It opened its new China facility in 2012.

Transistor Devices formerly did business as TDI, when it was predominantly a military company. However, as the company expanded into other market sectors, it felt the need to update its corporate identity, which it did in 2008 by adopting a new logo and brand name -- TDI Power.
