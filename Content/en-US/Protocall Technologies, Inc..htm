Protocall Technologies once provided software to go, now it provides armor to go. Through its TitleMatch Entertainment subsidiary, the company offered a software-on-demand retail kiosk system that allowed customers to select and make software CDs. Offerings comprised over 1,000 movies, television shows, music, software, audio books, and video games. In May 2008 Protocall furloughed its non-executive employees and sales staff, and announced it was looking for alternatives in order to resume operations. In July 2010 the company completed a reverse merger with Integrated Armor Solutions, which makes products used to protect buildings and vehicles against IEDs (improvised explosive devices) and bombs.
