Cooper-Standard Automotive (CSA) is the world's largest maker of auto body sealing systems, as well as one of the largest of anti-vibration system (AVS) and fluid handling products. Its body and chassis lines, which protect interiors from noise, dust, and weather, include brackets, mounts, and seals sold under brands StanPro, Tecalemit, and Metzeler. CSA fluid handling products (sensors, tubes, and hoses) are used in heating/cooling, braking, fuel, and emissions systems. CSA sells to such major OEMs as Ford , GM , Fiat Chrysler , PSA Peugeot Citroen , and some suppliers.

CSA is concentrating on fuel efficiency, supplying an increasing number of small cars, hybrids, and alternative powertrains that enjoy growing demand. Another focus for the company is using its global presence, which includes operations in 20 countries, to meet the burgeoning global footprint of OEMs, which have traditionally represented 82% of CSA sales.

To better support its customers' growing global integration, CSA has been complementing its facilities in Western Europe with plants in Central and Eastern Europe. For its global operations as a whole the company has been creating a leaner manufacturing base by cutting waste, reducing costs, and boosting productivity.

CSA's operations are divided into four divisions: North America, Europe, South America, and Asia Pacific. The US accounts for 27% of net sales, while Mexico generates 18%. Other large markets include China (10%), Canada (9%), France (8%), Poland (7%), and Germany (7%). It operates in almost 80 manufacturing locations and 20 design, engineering, and administrative locations in 20 countries around the world.

CSA's major customers include Ford (26% of total sales in 2015), GM (16%), Fiat/Chrysler (12%), Peugeot (5%), and Volkswagen (5%). Other customers included Daimler, Renault-Nissan, BMW , Toyota , Volvo , Jaguar/Land Rover , and Honda .

The company has seen strong growth over the last five years, with revenues peaking at a record-setting $3.34 billion in 2015. Profits also skyrocketed by more than 160% to reach $112 million in 2015 as its operating cash flow jumped by 58% for the year.

The historic growth for 2015 was fueled by a surge in sales from the Asia Pacific due to additional revenue from a previous acquisition and improved sales volumes. This was partially offset by unfavorable foreign exchanges. In addition, North America sales increased primarily due to an improvement in sales volume and product mix.

The company is focusing on developing four core product lines: Sealing Systems, Fuel and Brake Delivery Systems, Fluid Transfer Systems (including hose, transmission oil cooling and air conditioning lines), and Anti-Vibration Systems.

Seeking more opportunities in such emerging markets as China, India, Mexico, and South America, where light vehicles sales are growing, CSA has developed a joint venture strategy that has enabled the company to enter new markets with less exposure to risk and reduced capital investment. Its JV partners have included Shanghai Automotive Industry , Guyoung Technology, Hubei Jingda Precision Steel Pipe, Nishikawa Rubber, Toyoda Gosei, and Zhejiang Saiyang Seal Products.

With about 20 design, engineering, and administration centers worldwide, the company also counts product development as another important strategy for growth. The company's research and development staff is focused on developing greener technology by creating more products made of lighter weight and recyclable materials, among other initiatives. CSA is also seeking growth in the commercial vehicle, off-highway, marine, and power sports markets.

Throughout 2015 and 2016, CSA opened four new facilities across the important automotive market of China.

In 2015 the company acquired an additional 47.5% of Huayu-Cooper Standard Sealing Systems (increasing its ownership to 95%) for $59 million. China-based Shenya primarily supplies sealing systems and components to the automotive industry.
