Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas (HiAMS-AM) makes its mark with restraint as well as speed. The company, once known as Hitachi Automotive Products (USA), manufactures an array of electrical and electronic automotive components. Its lineup includes control units for engines and transmissions, fuel injectors, drivetrains, alternators, various sensors, and infotainment radios. HiAMS-AM also makes components for hybrid-electric vehicles (starters, generators, and electric motors). The company operates manufacturing plants in Kentucky and Georgia, and sales and technical centers in California and Michigan. HiAMS-AM's corporate affiliate, Hitachi America , is a subsidiary of Japanese industrial giant Hitachi, Ltd.

HiAMS-AM operates three manufacturing facilities and one sales and research and development office, and one aftermarket sales office.

The company serves major automotive original equipment manufacturers in the US.

Parent company Hitachi earned 13% of its 2014 net revenue from its North American operations.

In 2015 HiAMS-AM introduced a wide range of aftermarket products and technologies most notably steering systems and ride control products.

Hitachi's global business strategy to respond to growing demand in the important North American market by expanding production capacity in the Americas, including Mexico. In 2014 it built a new plant in Lerma City, Mexico to manufacture automotive systems, through its Hitachi Automotive Systems Mexico, S.A. de C.V. The Mexican market is developing into a base for automotive manufacturing throughout the Americas, including Latin America. The plant will strengthen the supply for the car makers throughout the Americas using the preferential trade agreement.

That year HiAMS-AM also built a third production facility at its Monroe, Georgia plant, through an investment of an expected $80 million. Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2016.

In 2014, the company launched the Hitachi Advantage mobile application for Android and iOS devices, which will meet the growing demand for tools that inspire customers to purchase Hitachi products.
