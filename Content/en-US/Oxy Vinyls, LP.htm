PVC? That's a pipe, right? Used in plumbing or something? Turns out that it's that and a whole lot more -- and Oxy Vinyls is the country's largest manufacturer of the stuff. A subsidiary of Occidental Chemical , Oxy Vinyls develops polyvinyl chloride, or PVC, resins and vinyl chloride monomer (VCM); the latter is a raw material used to make PVC. Oxy Vinyls' products -- which are branded as OxyVinyls -- are used in plumbing pipes, but also in flooring, films, bottles, siding, outdoor furniture, and medical tubing. Its leading position in the North American PVC market puts it ahead of the likes of Georgia Gulf and Formosa Plastics .

OxyVinyls provides polyvinyl chloride resins for vinyl production applications such as the manufacturing of PVC pipes.

The company's products are used by plumbing pipe, flooring, films, bottles, siding, outdoor furniture, and medical tubing manufacturers, among others.

With an industry leading position in terms of integrated raw material supply, OxyVinyls focuses on quality and low-cost production. Its focus on chlorovinyls allows it take advantage of economies of scale.

Company Background

The company was formed through the combination of the PVC and VCM businesses of Occidental Chemical and PolyOne, North America's largest suppliers of PVC resin.
