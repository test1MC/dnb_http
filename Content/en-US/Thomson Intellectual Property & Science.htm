For some information seekers, the more technical the better. Thomson Intellectual Property & Science provides scientific and technical information and services to researchers, scientists, and information professionals in the academic, scientific, corporate, and government marketplaces. The business encompasses a number of information brands and products, including Thomson CompuMark (trademark and copyright research), Web of Knowledge (global research online), and the Pharma Knowledge Hub (pharmaceutical information). Thomson Intellectual Property & Science is part of information giant Thomson Reuters .

Thomson Intellectual Property & Science operates three businesses: Intellectual Property Assets (patent, trademark and brand content and services); Life Sciences (pharmaceutical research and development); Government & Academia (scientific search and discovery by providing access to critical citation, benchmarking, and analytics data).

The company accounted for 8% of Thomson Reuters ' revenues in 2014.

The company's target customers include business executives, docketing administrators and investment and regulatory professionals at pharmaceutical and biotechnology enterprises.

Thomson Intellectual Property & Science's revenues in 2014 increased by 3% due to acquired businesses, a 2% growth from existing operations, and higher subscriptions for Web of Science products. Recurring revenues, which comprised more than 75% of the company's total 2014 revenues, increased by 7% (5% from existing businesses), and transaction revenues dropped by 6% (including a 8% decrease from existing businesses).

The company focuses on product innovation (the continued release of new research products), aligning sales resources against high potential market opportunities, and enhancing customer service in order to drive retention.

In 2011 it launched Thomson Reuters Cortellis, a solution for the life sciences industry that specifically targets pharma and biotech professionals. Thomson Reuters Cortellis combines the company's drug research and development data with information management technology in order to provide advanced searching features, customization, and integration with systems.

The Thomson Reuters division was formerly called Thomson Healthcare & Science. However, in 2011 the parent company put the healthcare business up for sale, and began classifying it among "other" businesses in its financial results. It did so after deciding the unit lacked the scale of its competitors, and because it wanted to focus on its core businesses serving the legal and financial industries.
