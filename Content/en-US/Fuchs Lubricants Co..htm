Squeaky wheels in America should direct their requests toward Fuchs Lubricants, the US subsidiary of Germany's Fuchs Petrolub , one of the world's top manufacturers of specialty lubricants and greases. Its products, including heavy-duty greases, hydraulic fluids, industrial grease and maintenance lubricants, and metal working fluids, are used by customers in the food processing, industrial equipment, metals and mining, automotive, and pharmaceutical industries. Fuchs Lubricants also provides chemical process management services, including process control, usage reduction, and waste minimization services. The company also has units to produce lubricants for the steel industry and for mining companies.

Fuchs focuses on lubricants and related specialties, offering more than 10,000 products.

Its core products are automotive and industrial lubricants; metal working fluids; and lubricants for special applications.

To support customers, Fuchs has more than 600 ongoing research and development projects and also offers a range of technical and process-related services.

Fuchs Petrolub reports that the Americas account for about 28% of the global lubricant market (of about 36 million tons), second only to Asia-Pacific/Africa (53%).

Fuchs' more than 100,000 customers include automotive suppliers, OEMs, and customers in the mining and exploration, metalworking, agriculture and forestry, aerospace, power generation, mechanical engineering, construction and transport, steel, metal and cement, food, glass production, casting, and forging industries.

The Americas account for about 17% of the parent company's total sales.

Fuchs grows organically via product innovation and development and through acquisitions.

Expanding its portfolio in the field of of industrial specialty lubricants, in 2016 the company acquired Delaware-based Ultrachem, a producer and marketer of specialty synthetic lubricants for the compressor OEM and industrial maintenance markets.

That year, the parent company took over the worldwide white oils and food machinery specialty lubricants business of Chevron and integrated it into Fuchs. About 85% of the Chevron unit's sales were in North America.
