Materion Advanced Materials Technologies and Services is the largest business segment of parent Materion Corp . Materion Advanced Materials also does business as Materion Microelectronics and Services. It makes vapor deposition targets, frame lid assemblies, clad and precious metal preforms, high-temperature braze materials, ultra-fine wire, advanced chemicals, optics, performance coatings, and microelectronic packages. The company's precious, non-precious, and specialty metal products are used in the magnetic and optical data storage markets, as well as the solar/photovoltaic industry, and the hybrid, photonic, semiconductor, and wireless segments of the microelectronics industry.

Materion Advanced Materials is based in Buffalo, New York. It has locations in Albuquerque, New Mexico; Wheatfield and Buffalo, New York; Milwaukee; Newburyport, Massachusetts; Limerick, Ireland; Louny, Czech Republic; the Philippines; Singapore; Suzhou, China; and Taipei, Taiwan.

In addition to its manufacturing of metals and metal alloys, Materion Advanced Materials offers refining and recycling services for precious metals. The company operates a 15,000-sq.-ft. chemical refinery where material, sent by customers, is processed for reclaim; processing may involve incineration, milling, or chemical leaching, among other processes.

Materion Advanced Materials serves the wireless, LED, data storage, defense, semiconductor, photonics, military, medical, consumer electronics, science, energy, industrial components, commercial aerospace, and telecom infrastructure markets.

Its products are sold directly from its facilities in Asia, Europe, and the US as well as through direct sales offices and independent sales representatives throughout the world.

As Materion's largest business segment, Materion Advanced Materials accounts for more than two-thirds of its parent's overall sales. The segment saw an overall sales decrease of 19% from 2011 to 2012.

Parent Materion Corp. has made about one acquisition per year for the advanced materials segment. In 2011 it bought EIS Optics Limited, a Chinese company that made optical thin film filters, glass processing, lithography, and optical sub-assemblies for the the projection display, entertainment lighting, sensors, medical instruments, and gaming industries. EIS Optics operates a 97,000-sq.-ft. in Shanghai. Materion paid about $24 million for EIS Optics.

Materion Advanced Materials was founded as Williams Gold Company in the 1920s when Reginald Williams developed the first commercial application of induction melting of gold and other materials to make dental alloys. Williams Gold Company was acquired by Brush Engineered Metals in 1986. Brush changed its name to Materion in 2011.

Through WAM, Brush Engineered Materials acquired Barr Associates for approximately $55 million in October 2009. Massachusetts-based Barr is a leading manufacturer of precision thin film optical filters, which are used in the aerospace/defense, medical, telecommunications, and astronomy markets, to name a few. Barr brings an added source of revenue to WAM and expands its advanced materials technologies and products portfolio.

Brush continued its acquisition agenda through WAM, picking up New Mexico-based Academy Corporation in January 2010. Academy delivers precious and non-precious metals and refining services and is expected to provide clients an expanded global slate of manufacturing, technology, and services. The transaction is valued at approximately $23 million cash.
