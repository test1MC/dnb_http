Taking its name from the legendary King Gambrinus of Flanders (aka the King of Beer), The Gambrinus Company brews and distributes a fine libation with which to toast -- Shiner Beer. Shiner is brewed by Gambrinus' Spoetzl Brewery located in Shiner, Texas. The company also owns Portland-based BridgePort Brewery, Oregon's oldest microbrewery; Pete's Brewing Company, maker of Pete's Wicked Ale; and Berkeley, California-based Trumer Brauerei, which makes Trumer Pils in a joint venture with Austrian brewer Privatbrauerei Josef Sigl. Gambrinus brews are available nationwide, as well as in Australia, New Zealand, and Italy. Owner Carlos Alvarez founded Gambrinus in 1986.

The company has a number of brewing agreements. In addition to its Trumer Pils JV with Privatbrauerei Josef Sigl, it works with Italian joint-venture partner, Birra Menabrea to make Tappeto Volante. The brew is only available in the US in New York State; Birra Menabrea distributes it in Italy.

Gambrinus has breweries in the US in California, Texas, and Oregon, and in Italy. It distributes its beer in Australia, New Zealand, the US, and Italy.

The company builds its brands through marketing efforts, product quality, and an aggressive field sales force. It serves brewery suppliers, wholesalers, and retail partners.

Gambrinus lost its agreement to distribute brands such as Corona from Mexico's Grupo Modelo in the Eastern US in 2006, when Constellation Brands took over the account. The next year, it sold the US import rights for Moosehead Lager back to Moosehead Breweries of Canada.

With the loss of its Mexican and Canadian beer import business, Gambrinus began focusing on its domestic brands, in particular targeting the Chicago area for its Shiner products.
