If you have ever read a magazine or a catalog, then chances are you've held Blandin Paper. Blandin produces lightweight, coated publication-grade paper for use in magazines, brochures, and direct-mail advertising. Finland-based forestry company UPM-Kymmene bought Blandin in 1997 from the Canadian subsidiary of New Zealand-based Fletcher Challenge Group in order to establish a presence in North America. Blandin's magazine papers production contributes to UPM-Kymmene's largest product segment. The paper business group as a whole accounts for more than 69% of the parent company's sales.

Blandin is one of about 65 subsidiaries operated by UPM-Kymmene worldwide. It has operations in Florida, Maryland, and Minnesota.

Blandin markets its products through a direct sales force.

The paper sales business contributed almost 70% of UPM-Kymmene's total revenue during fiscal 2012. The US and Canada contributed only about 12% of Blandin's revenue in fiscal 2012. The majority of revenue in paper sales for the parent company was derieved from the European market.
