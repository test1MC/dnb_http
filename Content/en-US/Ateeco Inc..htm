Got a thing for pierogies? Thank Ateeco, the longtime manufacturer of the traditional Polish specialty dish: potato-stuffed pasta. Doing business as Mrs. T's Pierogies, the company makes and ships more than 13 million frozen Mrs. T's brand pierogies every week. Its customers include retail food, convenience, and club stores, and foodservice providers across the US, as well as US military commissaries worldwide. Mrs. T's pierogies come in more than a dozen varieties, including potato and cheddar; sauerkraut; and potato, sour cream and chives. The family-owned and -managed company was founded by Ted Twardzik in 1952; the product was named after his mother, Mary Twardzik.

Ateeco is synonymous with pierogy. The company, which maintains a plant in Shenandoah, Pennsylvania, produces more than 600 pierogies each year in 14 different varieties.

Based in Pennsylvania, Ateeco distributes its Polish pasta products nationwide, from its home state to Washington State.

Ateeco makes its pierogies available in all 50 US states. The company's Segment Solutions unit serves several markets, such as K-12 schools, colleges and universities, the US military, healthcare, lodging and catering, business and industry, and commercial restaurants.
