Cerro's dealers can get you wired, but there's no need to call the cops. Cerro Wire manufactures electrical wire and cable for residential, commercial, and industrial building projects. Cerro's lineup specializes in copper and aluminum interior building wire, aluminum URD (underground residential distribution), and overhead conductor and submersible pump cable. Its products are sold under the Cerrowire and Circle Wire brands through a sales network to wholesale electrical distributors, as well as to consumer hardware centers. Operating as a subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway 's Marmon Group , Cerro is part of the Marmon family of wire and cable companies, including Owl , Aetna, and Cable USA.

The company operates three other manufacturing and distribution facilities in Carrolton, Georgia; Crothersville, Indiana; and Ogden, Utah.

Cerro operates through two divisions. Its Electrical Distribution Division offers building wire for residential, commercial, and industrial use, while the Consumer Markets division provides data communications cable and telephone and security wire, portable cord, extension cords, and reels.

The Consumer Markets division sells through retail home improvement stores; Electrical Distribution sells through wholesale electrical distributors across North America.

The company was established in 1920 by Hyman Cohn as Circle Flexible Conduit Co. It changed its name to Circle Wire & Cable Corp. in 1935 and then Cerro Wire in 2007.
