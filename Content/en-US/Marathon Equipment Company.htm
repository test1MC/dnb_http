You don't have to run 26 miles, 385 yards to get away from your trash. Just call Marathon Equipment Company instead. The subsidiary of Dover makes waste handling and recycling equipment for all manner of public places: malls, arenas and stadiums, hotels and motels, schools, and apartment buildings. Marathon's equipment includes compactors (vertical, stationary, self-contained, etc.), containers, baling systems (auto- and manual -tie systems, horizontal and vertical), and conveying systems. Marathon Equipment offers parts and service nationwide.

Marathon Equipment operates manufacturing locations in Alabama, Nevada, and Pennsylvania. Its business is primarily within the US.

In 2013 Marathon Equipment established three new business segments to meet the evolving needs of its customers in the global solid waste and recycling industry. The new segments include Marathon Compaction Solutions, Marathon Recycling Solutions, and Marathon Renewable Energy Solutions.

Marathon Equipment sells its products under the RamJet (compactors), NEXGEN (balers and conveying systems), BlokPak (high-volume waste compactors), and TrashMinder (monitoring systems that measures a container's fullness) brands. Acknowledging the environmental concerns of the 21st century, the company has unveiled its GreenBuilt line of products, equipment that can run on solar power (with its own solar panels) and using biodegradable oil for lubrication.

The company uses national advertising campaigns and equipment exhibits at major industry trade shows to sell its products. It also leverages its internal telemarketing and an in-house literature department to respond to hauler and distributor sales material needs. Two of its customers include Greenwood Leflore Recycling and Durango Recycling Center.

Marathon Equipment was established in 1967 and acquired by Dover in 1990.
