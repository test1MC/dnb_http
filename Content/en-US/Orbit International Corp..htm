Orbit International is at home in the world -- on land, in the air, or at sea. The company's core electronics group is comprised of its instrument division, and its Tulip and ICS subsidiaries. It specializes in customized display terminals, intercommunication panels, and keyboards for military ships and aircraft, as well as rugged computing hardware for commercial customers. Orbit's power unit, led by subsidiary Behlman Electronics, makes electrical AC power supplies and frequency converters; it also reconfigures obsolete military equipment and does repairs for the US Air Force and Navy . Orbit primarily serves the US government and defense contractors, such as BAE SYSTEMS .

Orbit's Electronics Group segment, 53% of revenue, consists of Orbit Instrument (Orbit), the Tulip Development Laboratory (TDL), and the Integrated Combat Systems (ICS) divisions.

The other 47% of revenue comes from the Power Group, which is the company's Behlman subsidiary. It designs and makes commercial and custom power units.

Some 95% of sales come from customers in the domestic US market.

Most of Orbit's customers are contractors to US government agencies.

Orbit reported higher revenue and turned a profit in 2015.

Increased sales in Orbit's electronics group pushed overall revenue 5% higher to $20million in 2015 from 2014. The company reduced costs in 2015, which, combined with higher revenue, drove it to a $902,000 profit from a $2 million loss in 2014.

Cash flow from operations was steady at about $240,000 a year.
