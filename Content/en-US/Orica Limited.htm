Business is always booming for Orica, which ranks among the world's top suppliers of commercial explosives and blasting systems. The company primarily serves the mining, quarrying, and construction industries and has operations in more than 50 countries and customers in more than 100. Orica also is a top producer of sodium cyanide, emulsifiers, coolants and degreasers, and other mining chemicals. Its Minova unit is a global maker of chemical and mechanical earth control products, adhesives and support equipment, specialty bolts, and cement products used in underground mining and civil tunneling.

The company is based in Melbourne, Australia and operates in Brownsburg, Canada; Denver, US; Gyttorp, Sweden; Troisdorf, Germany; Dubai, UAE; Santiago, Chile; Singapore; Jakarta, Indonesia; Johannesburg, South Africa; and Kurri Kurri, Australia.

Orica has operations in more than 50 countries, with customers in more than 100 countries.

The company operates through Mining Services (Australia/Pacific, North America, Latin America, EMEA/Europe, Middle East & Africa & Other), Ground Support and Other.

Mining Services manufactures and supplies commercial explosives and blasting systems including services to the mining and infrastructure markets, and supplies mining chemicals including sodium cyanide for gold extraction.

Ground Support makes and supplies specialty bolts, accessories, and chemicals for the stabilization and ventilation systems in underground mining and civil tunneling works.

Other activities include the operation of the Botany Groundwater Recycling Business, non-operating assets, corporate and support costs and financial items (such as foreign currency gains/losses).

The company serves a range of markets including Surface Metal, Surface Coal, Underground Mining, Underground Construction, Construction, Quarrying, and Oil and Gas.

Financial Performance

The company's revenues was restated in 2015 due to the sale of its Chemicals business.

Net revenues decreased by 17% as Mining Services Australia/Pacific segments declined due to lower volumes, contract losses, and a decrease in service levels requested by customers as a result of cost pressures.

In 2015 Orica posted a net loss of A$1.40 billion (compared to net income of A$0.63 billion in 2014) mainly due to lower net revenues and a presence of the impairment of property, plant and equipment and impairment of intangibles in current year.

The company's operating cash inflow decreased by 19%.

Orica's strategy is to create sustainable shareholder value through customer-focused and capital-efficient supply of advanced blasting services, mining chemicals and ground support services and products.

In 2015, the company sold its Chemicals business, marking the realization of its strategy to become a pure play mining services company. The company will focus on growing its position as the world's leading supplier of blasting products and services to benefit customers in the mining, quarrying and construction sectors.
