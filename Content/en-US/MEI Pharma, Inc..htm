MEI Pharma, formerly known as Marshall Edwards, wants to stop the clock on cancer cells. The oncology company is developing potential anti-cancer compounds that block the proteins that keep tumor cells ticking. Its pharmaceutical candidates may also make tumor cells more susceptible to chemotherapy and other anti-cancer drugs. The company's development programs target ailments such as prostate, ovarian, pancreatic, and bile duct cancers, as well as melanoma. Australian biotech firm Novogen owns a majority stake in MEI Pharma but intends to spin it off to shareholders as part of a reverse merger strategy.
