Global Power Equipment Group keeps its customers in power. Through its subsidiaries, the company designs and manufactures power generation equipment for OEMs, engineering, construction, and power generation customers. Its products, sold under its Braden Manufacturing, Consolidated Fabricators, TOG, and Koontz-Wagner brands, include auxiliary parts for steam and gas turbines, electric transmission systems and controls, and custom components. Additionally, subsidiary Williams Industrial Services offers upgrades and maintenance services to industrial and utility companies and nuclear and hydroelectric power plants, while its Hetsco unit provides welding and fabrication services. The company was formed in 1998.

Global Power operates 10 facilities in the US, as well as a number of other facilities in Mexico, China, and the Netherlands. Its fabrication operations are located primarily in the US and Mexico. The US generates almost 60% of its net sales.

The company operates within three reportable segments: Product Solutions (70% of net sales), Energy Services (22%), and Nuclear Services (8%).

A significant percentage of the company's revenue comes from only a handful of customers. These customers include General Electric , Siemens Energy , Southern Nuclear Operating Company , and the Tennessee Valley Authority , each of which accounted for around 15% of the company's total revenue in 2014 (a total of 60%).

Global Power's revenue has grown the last few years, climbing 11% from $484 million in 2013 to $539 million in 2014. The growth was fueled by a 72% surge in Energy Services sales and a 8% spike from Product Solutions.

The company's net income fell 5% from $12 million in 2013 to $11 million in 2014 due to higher operating and acquisition-related expenses. In addition, its operating cash flow has fluctuated wildly the last few years; it experienced negative cash flow two out of the last three years.

Global Power is expecting growth in its services division as aging plants, including nuclear power plants built decades ago, seek to upgrade and modernize their facilities. Global Power is also confident that demand for its gas turbine and other equipment will increase as the anticipated recovery in the natural gas industry -- evident in increased gas exploration and production activity -- takes hold in coming years.

To support its growth strategy, the company has made acquisitions to bolster its product and service offerings. In 2013 the company acquired Hetsco, which provides brazed aluminum heat exchanger maintenance and repair services, for $32.5 million. Hetsco is part of the company's services division.

In 2015 Global Power acquired Siemens ' eHouse manufacturing operations, a part of its Medium Voltage Business Unit. The acquisition enhanced Global Power's manufacturing footprint and will serve as a platform to provide Global Power's customers with an integrated product and service portfolio.
