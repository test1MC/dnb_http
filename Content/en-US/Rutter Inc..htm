Rutter makes radar technologies for seacraft that offer real-time data management and can be used in harsh environment. The company's radar products help captains deal with ice, track the flow of oil spills, monitor currents and waves, and identify small objects that might interfere with operations. Rutter takes its name from "rutters," the small books in which sailors log navigational information, which reflected its former business of making voyage data recorders.

The company had been publicly traded on the Toronto Stock Exchange. But in 2014 it went private through a merger with a company called 8758875 Canada Inc. The resulting entity maintained the Rutter Inc. name.

Rutter sells to a number of seagoing industries such as petroleum, shipping, cruise lines, and coastal operations.

Rutter has increased narrowed to radar technologies and products. It previously sold the video record business and more recently sold its manufacturing operations to DAVWIRE.
