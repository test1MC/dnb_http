When Twin Rivers Technologies manufactures, it makes no bones about it. The company produces oleochemicals and other additives for food-manufacturing customers in North America and abroad. Its products include approximately 80 different types of fatty acids, glycerin, and methyl esters in food and industrial grades. Its food-grade products are made from edible tallow, cottonseed, soybeans, and sunflower and canola seeds. Twin Rivers also produces vegetable-oil-based biodiesel and ethanol. The company operates its business in the US and Canada. Twin Rivers is a subsidiary of the Federal Land Development Authority (Felda), a Malaysian government-owed agency in Kuala Lumpur.

Massachusetts-based Twin Rivers operates across the US and Canada.

As part of its business, Twin Rivers owns and operates Fore River Transportation, the short line railroad that's responsible for connecting Twin Rivers with its national rail carrier, CSX.

Twin Rivers sells to customers North and Latin America, as well as overseas. It operates terminals in Cincinnati, Ohio; Newark, New Jersey; and Houston, Texas.

Because the company's products are used in a variety of industries, Twin Rivers serves multiple customer sets. Its products are found in animal feed and supplements (excluding fatty residues), asphalt additives, Crayons, candles, waxes, emulsion polymerization, foods, candies, edible emulsifiers, oil fields, corrosion inhibitors, paint and protective coatings, inks and personal care products, and plastics (excluding emulsion and polymerization, among others).

Felda is actively investing in operations that will expand its global presence in its core plantation and related businesses, with Twin Rivers being part of this strategy. It boasts supply chain and technological expertise.
