Raritan Americas makes specialized computing products used in data centers and other corporate environments that engage in intensive IT activities. The Legrand North America subsidiary's products include PC keyboard, video, and mouse (KVM) switches which enable multiple PC servers and networking equipment to be controlled from a single set of devices and related connectivity devices, including serial console and remote management systems. Raritan sells directly and through resellers and distributors to computer hardware vendors and enterprise customers. The company's global customer base has included Cisco , Dell , EarthLink , Merrill Lynch , NASA , and Oklahoma State University . Raritan was founded in 1985.

Raritan Americas serves customers in the US and about 75 international countries, including Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, France, Germany, India, Japan, Mexico, the Netherlands, New Zealand, and the UK.

Raritan's additional products include infrastructure and power management devices, systems, and software (PowerIQ and dcTrack) used to monitor electricity consumption and heat dissipation in data centers. It also provides serial consoles for controlling networking equipment such as routers and switches, WAN devices including ISDN terminal adapters, and servers.

Raritan's sales and marketing activities are conducted through channel partners. The company serves a number of government customers, including numerous branches of the US Department of Defense .

To expand beyond its core KVM product lines as the company builds its customer base, Raritan increasingly supplies a wider range of products for data centers and other computing operations. It is capitalizing on the global boom in data centers as more enterprise information and applications move to computing environments which are accessed via the Internet.
