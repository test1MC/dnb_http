Jaguar -- the pride of the British automotive industry -- takes a passage to India. Tata Motors acquired Jaguar in 2008 for about $2.3 billion. ( Ford Motor acquired Jaguar Cars in 1989 for $2.5 billion.) Jaguar Land Rover Limited, which is part of Jaguar Land Rover Automotive plc, formerly Jaguar Land Rover, offers a line of luxury coupes, convertibles, and sedans, including the XF (a replacement for the S-Type), XJ, and XK. Jaguar continues to design, engineer, and manufacture its vehicles at eight locations, including three car assembly plants in Castle Bromwich, Solihull, and near Liverpool. Jaguar vehicles are sold in more than 100 countries.

Jaguar was founded by Bill Lyons and William Walmsley in 1922 as Swallow Sidecar Company, making motorcycle sidecars in the UK. The name was changed to Jaguar after World War II.
