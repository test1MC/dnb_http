Holophane lights things up all around the world. A company and brand of Acuity Brands , Holophane makes lighting fixtures and systems for industrial, commercial, and outdoor markets. Its largest product line, industrial fixtures, features lighting for vast indoor spaces (retail spaces, convention centers, factories, warehouses, etc.). Holophane also makes lighting products for large outdoor areas such as highway interchanges, and it offers commercial and institutional lighting for schools and offices. Its ISD SuperGlass prismatic lighting technology allows reflectors to cast more light. Holophane was founded in 1895 in London, and its founder helped start the Illuminating Engineering Society of North America.

The company's sells its lighting systems directly or through distribution centers, regional warehouses, and commercial warehouses in North America using both common carriers and a company-owned truck fleet.

As with many companies in the broadly defined construction and renovation supply industries (paints, construction supplies, household fixtures, etc.), Holophane's business has evolved recently to depend on home improvement centers more and more. For instance, Home Depot alone accounted for 13% of parent company Acuity Brands' sales in 2013.

Other clients include electrical distributors, retail home improvement centers, electric utilities, utility distributors, national accounts, government and municipalities, lighting showrooms, original equipment manufacturers, and energy service companies.

National Service Industries purchased Holophane in 1999; it created and spun off Accuity Brands in 2001.
