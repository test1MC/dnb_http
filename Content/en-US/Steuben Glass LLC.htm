For those who want to see a grown woman cry like a baby, let a Steuben Glass piece slip through your fingertips to the floor. The firm designs pricey crystal stemware, barware, vases, bowls, decorative housewares, art glass, and books through a flagship store on Madison Avenue. Steuben Glass also serves the corporate gift market. It also sells its products online and through catalogs. Steuben Glass was founded in 1903 by Frederick Carder and Thomas Hawkes. Despite its status, Steuben Glass had been unprofitable for several years. The crystal maker's longtime parent, Corning , which had run the company as part of its glass division since 1918, sold Steuben in August 2008 to Schottenstein Stores Corporation .
