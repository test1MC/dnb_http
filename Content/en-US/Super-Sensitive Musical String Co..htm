Musicians would never tell violin strings to stop being so sensitive. Super-Sensitive Musical String makes strings, rosin, and accessories for bowed instruments such as violins, violas, cellos, and double basses under the brand names Stellar, Sensicore, Red Label, and Octava. The company was founded in Chicago in 1930 by musician and machinist Ed Wackerle. Sold to Vince Cavanaugh in 1967, Super-Sensitive is still owned and operated by the Cavanaugh family.
