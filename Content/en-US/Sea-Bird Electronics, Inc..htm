This bird can tell you when the seas change. Sea-Bird Electronics makes marine instruments used to measure salinity, temperature, pressure, dissolved oxygen, and related oceanographic variables. The company's instruments -- samplers, recorders, profilers, data loggers, sensors, and pumps -- are used by research institutes, universities, government agencies, engineering firms, and naval forces. It also provides calibration services, which are necessary for the accurate operation of its instruments. Sea-Bird Electronics was incorporated in 1979. Former owners Nordeen Larson (president) and John Backes (VP) sold the company to tool and instrument maker Danaher in 2008.
