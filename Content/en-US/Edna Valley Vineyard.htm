Edna Valley Vineyard produces and distributes wine (primarily Chardonnay, but also Pinot Noir, Syrah, Merlot, and Cabernet Sauvignon) from its 1,100-acre vineyard in the San Luis Obispo area of California. Best known for its complex Chardonnay, the vineyard is the result of a partnership between Paragon Vineyard Company (owned by the Niven family, who planted Edna Valley's estate vineyard in the 1970s) and Diageo Chateau & Estate Wines . Edna Valley wine varietals are distributed nationally and can be purchased from the company's Web site and shipped to states that allow such transactions. Edna Valley Vineyard was purchase by wine collosus E. & J. Gallo Winery in 2011.
