This company wants to help shoppers get the most for their euros. Kelkoo operates leading price-comparison Web sites serving consumers in 10 European countries. The sites allow users to search for products from a number of online and offline retailers to find the lowest prices and the best deals. It lists goods and services in a number of categories, including books, consumer electronics, DVDs and videos, fashion and lingerie, music, travel, televisions, and mobile phones. Kelkoo generates revenue from fees paid by retailers to have their products indexed in its database.

Kelkoo operates localized sites in 15 countries including Belgium, Denmark, France, Germany, Italy, The Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Sweden, and the UK.

Kelkoo was started in 2000 through the merger of comparison search engine operators Dondecomprar, Shopgenie, and Zoomit. Its investors included venture capital firms such as Banexi Venture Partners, Sgam (Societe Generale), and Innovacom from France; Netjuice and BBVA from Spain; and Kistefos from Norway.
