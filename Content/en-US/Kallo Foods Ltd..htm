Healthy eating is at the heart of Kallo Foods. Its all-organic products include breakfast cereals, crackers, breadsticks, savory and sweet biscuits, dairy-free products (rice drink, soft drinks, tofu), rice and other cereal cakes, and stocks and gravies, sold under the Kallo and other labels. The company's products are available in major UK food chains and health-food stores. In addition to Kallo (rice cakes), the company's brands include Free to Enjoy (wheat- and gluten-free biscuits, crackers, and bread sticks), Just Bouillon (stocks and gravies), and Whole Earth (organic fruit spreads, peanut butter, cereal, sauces, and drinks). Kallo is a subsidiary of Dutch company Royal Wessanen .

The company produces soil association accredited health food products such as rice cakes and breadsticks and has a strong representation in supermarkets and specialist shops.

Kallo sells through major supermarkets and independent health food stores.

The company is part UK operation of the parent company Royal Wessanen . The UK segment contributed nearly 15% of total sales in 2015.

In 2008 the company acquired the So Good brand in Europe. So Good is the second-largest brand in the UK's dairy-alternative sector and offers a range of shelf-stable and chilled soy-based beverages.

Founded in the early 1980s, Kallo was purchased by the Netherlands food giant Royal Wessanen in 2003 and was made part of its Tree of Life subsidiary. (Tree of Life was sold to Kehe Food Distributors in 2010; however Wessanen retained ownership of Kallo.)
