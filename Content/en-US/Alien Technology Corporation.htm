This alien makes sure you don't get abducted. Alien Technology makes low-cost, high-volume radio-frequency identification (RFID) tags and ultra high frequency (UHF) tags using its Fluidic Self Assembly (FSA) manufacturing process. (FSA allows for large quantities of small components to be placed on a surface during a single operation.) The company also makes the chips used in the tags and readers used for detection and tracking. Alien's tags and readers are used for inventory management, logistics, security systems, supply chain management, and vehicle asset tracking. Its products are compatible with supply chain software from vendors such as GlobeRanger , HighJump , OATSystems , and RedPrairie .

The company has an RFID Academy training center (also known as the Alien Academy) in Ohio. There it has taught more than 1,500 companies about implementing RFID systems into their supply chain operations. International Alien Academies are located in Hungary and the UK.

It also has international sales offices in China, Japan, and South Korea.

For the most part, customers come from the retail, consumer goods, manufacturing, defense, transportation/logistics and pharmaceutical industries. Other applications that use Alien RFID tags and systems include self-service kiosk automation, automotive components assembly, evidence tracking in crime labs, and blood bag tracking within blood banks. The company also works with channel partners around the world to expand use of its RFID products to new industries.

Alien Technology was established in 1994 as Beckman Display, initially developing the FSA technology for information display purposes. The company changed its name to Alien Technology and redirected its focus to RFID technology in 1999.
