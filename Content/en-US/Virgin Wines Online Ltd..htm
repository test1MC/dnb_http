UK oenophiles and online-ophiles alike can shop their thirst away at Virgin Wines. The company operates a website that serves as a wine marketplace. Virgin Wines sorts its wines primarily by wine style or taste, rather than by region. The company's strategy, it believes, makes its product more accessible to the everyday wine drinker. While conducting the majority of its delivery business online, Virgin Wines boasts a staff of wine advisors that also provide phone consultation and wine tips to customers. The company is an independent entity, but works closely with the Virgin Group .

The company works with winemakers in Argentina, Australia, Chile, France, Germany, Italy, New Zealand, Portugal, South Africa, Spain, UK and US.

Virgin Wines is known for seeking out undiscovered winemakers worldwide to sell their wares. From its roots in the UK, the company has expanded its operations to include the fast-growing wine market in the US, which has leapfrogged Italy in per capita wine drinking.

To give it a marketing edge, Virgin Wines has kept the Virgin name (from its founding) and continues to work closely with the Virgin Group.

The company partnered with Domino's in 2011 in a partnership that pairs Virgin Wines with the pizza chain's gourmet range of pizzas.

Virgin Wines was founded in 2000 by by Richard Branson's Virgin Group, and in 2005 was bought by Direct Wines . In 2013, Virgin Wine's executives (supported by Mobeus Equity Partners and Connection Capital) bought the company in a management buy-out.
