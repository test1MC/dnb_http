Biomedical Acoustics Research Company (BARC) wants doctors to listen to their patients -- their bodies, that is. The research company --  which relies heavily on grants -- develops a variety of acoustic technologies for medical diagnoses and therapeutic services. It primarily focuses on gastrointestinal diseases, including intestinal obstruction, delayed gastric emptying, and gastrointestinal perforation. Its research areas also include respiratory, tracheal, optical, and vascular projects. The company's diagnostic technologies use computerized analysis techniques such as sensors and digital signal processing. BARC was founded in 1990 and is led by CEO Dr. Richard Sandler.
