Aisin U.S.A. Mfg. is the not-so secret key to Japanese auto parts-maker Aisin Seiki 's entr√©e into the West. The manufacturing subsidiary of Aisin Seiki, which is partly owned by Toyota , supplies the cars built in the US by Japan's largest carmaker. Aisin U.S.A. Mfg. makes a diverse line of components and systems, including door frame components, latching systems, seating, and trim molding. In addition to Toyota, the company supplies parts to several other Japanese carmakers -- Honda , Mitsubishi , and Nissan -- as well as to General Motors .

The company operates two plants, its largest one in Seymour, Indiana, and another plant in Marion, Illinois. The Seymour plant has 650,000 sq. ft. of manufacturing space and 100,000 sq. ft. of logistics facilities. Its location takes advantage of east-west highway and railroad arteries, enabling efficient access to major US automakers. Wooed by southern Illinois' Regional Economic Development Corporation, Aisin U.S.A. Mfg. subsequently established a 166,000-sq.-ft. manufacturing facility in Marion.
