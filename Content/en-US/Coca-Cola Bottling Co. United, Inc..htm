Coca-Cola Bottling Co. United brings icy-cold Cokes to folks in the sunny South. Founded in 1902, Coca-Cola Bottling United serves up "the real thing" in and around The Coca-Cola Company 's home turf, catering to consumers from Atlanta, Georgia, to Baton Rouge, Louisiana. The company is no slouch when it comes to production -- its brands include Coca-Cola Classic, Diet Coke, Coke Zero, Fresca, Sprite, and Cherry Coke. Coca-Cola Bottling Co. United serves accounts in Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, Mississippi, South Carolina, and Tennessee. In addition to soft drinks, the company bottles water, iced teas, fruit drinks, and sports drinks, in all more than 750 beverages from Coca-cola's vast beverage portfolio.

Alabama-based Coca-Cola Bottling Co. United has three production facilities and 19 distribution centers located in Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, Mississippi, South Carolina, and Tennessee.

Already the third-largest bottler of Coca-Cola products in nation, the company has plans to expand in Alabama, Florida, Georgia, and Tennessee under its 21st Century Beverage Partnership model with The Coca-Cola Company. Under the new model, The Coca-Cola Company and its five US bottlers, including Coca-Cola Bottling Co. United, are expanding and and entering new territories. To that end, in 2014 the company added the Oxford-Anniston market to its coverage through its takeover of Oxford Coca-Cola Bottling Co. Ultimately, Coca-Cola Bottling Co. United will cover all of central Alabama and most of eastern and southern Alabama, as well as Pensacola and Valparaiso, Florida, and West Point, Georgia.

To supply its expanded overage area, the company has spent millions on expanding its distribution and sales facilities.

Coca-Cola Bottling Co. United was founded in 1902 and was formerly known as Crawford Johnson Company. Its 102-year-old subsidiary, Chattanooga Coca-Cola Bottling Co., the world's oldest Coca-Cola franchise, was closed in 2008. Its operations were moved to the company's plant in Baton Rouge.
