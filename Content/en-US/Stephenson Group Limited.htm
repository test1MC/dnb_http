Stephenson Group has been cleaning up on soap bases and other specialty chemicals. The company manufactures specialty chemicals for use in products ranging from cosmetics to textiles. Its chemical product operations are split into four divisions: personal care, textiles, rubber and industrial, and paper recycling. The company makes fatty acid soaps, antifoams for the textiles industry, liquid dispenser hand soaps for janitorial supply, and de-inking chemicals for the paper recycling industry. The company operates a worldwide network of agents and distributors and has warehousing facilities in North America, Europe, and China. Stephenson Group is owned by holding company Thos. Bentley & Son.

The company has facilities in North America, Europe, and China that serve customers in more than 50 countries.

Stephenson Group's operations focus on using green chemistry to create environmentally friendly alternatives and organic products. Its factory in Horsforth, Leeds, in the UK is the only soap maker to have both the UK Soil Association and the ISO 14001 Environmental Standard verifying the company as a manufacturer of completely organic products.

Stephenson Group continues to expand on its experience and develop new skills. A large percentage of the company's revenues is reinvested in chemical research, logistics, business communications, and environmental initiatives.

In 2015 it installed the first fully automated batch reactor on site at Brookfoot House, Leeds. Stephenson Group launch a newly designed website, a US e-commerce site, alongside new standalone websites for Alkon Solutions, Textile Chemicals, Personal Care, and Innovation.

Stephenson Group was founded in 1856.

A new household cleaning products line was developed under the Bentley Organic brand name after the company acquired the Natural House brand in 2010. The company distributes its organic products through retailer Whole Foods .
