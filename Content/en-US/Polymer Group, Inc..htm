Avintiv's business is interwoven with nonwovens. Formerly known as the Polymer Group, the company is a leading developer and maker of nonwoven textiles and other engineered materials used by consumer and industrial product manufacturers. Its absorbent and disposable fabrics go into baby wipes, diapers, and other hygiene and medical products. On the industrial side, they are incorporated into filtration, insulation, and automotive acoustics products. The company operates more than 20 manufacturing and converting facilities worldwide. Avintiv was acquired by Berry Plastics in the summer of 2015 for almost $2.5 billion.

The company's portfolio spans products for hygiene, health care, wipes, and filtration. The hygiene group focuses on supplying nonwoven fabrics used in disposable baby care (SuperSoft brand), adult care (Reticulon), and feminine care (Reticulon and ComfortSilk) products, including diapers, pads, panty liners, and tampons. The health care group is the #1 global supplier of nonwoven fabrics used in infection control, medical apparel, and wound care. Products include MediSoft surgical gowns, PolySafe wound care gauze, tapes, and prep pads, and Poly-breathe protective apparel.

Finally, filtration products include materials for air and liquid filtration (think tea bags), for flooring insulation and flame retardant mattress covers, for automotive acoustics and trunk liners, and for car and boat covers. Its SilOnyx fabric, for instance, is designed to silence interior vehicle noise and is used in vehicles made by Ford , GM , and Honda .

The company operates more than 20 manufacturing and converting plants in a dozen countries -- Argentina, China, Colombia, France, Germany, India, Italy, Mexico, the Netherlands, Spain, the UK, and the US (in Oregon, North Carolina, Tennessee, and Virginia.

Key customers include manufacturers such as Procter & Gamble (diapers, feminine sanitary protection, household wipes), Kimberly-Clark (diapers, surgical drapes, face masks) and Cardinal Health (surgical drapes, medical accessories).

The company announced that its long-term strategic growth plans are to invest in new, state-of-the-art spunmelt manufacturing operations in South America and China and to solidify its position in these emerging markets. It expanded plants in Nanhai and Suzhou, China, and bought a company in Brazil in 2014.
