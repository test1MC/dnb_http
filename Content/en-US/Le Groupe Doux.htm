The company is among the largest poultry processors in Europe, serving both the retail grocery sector and the foodservice and food-manufacturing industries. Along with chicken and turkey, the company also processes rooster, duck, guinea fowl, quail, rabbit, goat, and kid. Its products include fresh meats and further-processed frozen entrées, snacks, and sausages sold under the Doux, LeBon, Père Doux, and Frangosul brands. With operations in Brazil, France, Germany, Spain, and the UK, Doux markets its products in more than 130 countries around the world.

Doux is vertically-integrated and oversees 14 hatcheries, 22 slaughterhouses, four processing plants, and 12 food factories, which produce some two million birds a day. Its brand include Père Doux (in France) and Doux (international) and it divides it products into whole birds and processed products, including snack products.

Its operations are located in France, Germany, Spain, Switzerland, and Brazil. It sells its products in more than 130 countries and has been moving into the Middle East.
