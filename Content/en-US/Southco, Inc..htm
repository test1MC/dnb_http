Southco holds the key that unlocks the global access solutions market. A subsidiary of South Chester Tube Company, Southco manufactures and distributes engineered access control hardware for automotive, computer, HVAC, industrial machinery, marine, mass transit, medical, military, RV, and telecom applications. Its products include cam locks, captive fasteners, drive rivets, handles and pulls, hinges, and latches, as well as electronic access products, such as key systems and readers. All told the company offers some 25,000 standardized and 50,000 customized products.

Southco has a global network of distributors in more than 60 countries. Outside of North America, Southco maintains sales, engineering, and manufacturing facilities in Europe and the Asia/Pacific region.

Southco offers its products through distributors in Canada, the US, and Mexico. Products are sold through its affiliate companies and subsidiaries across the Americas, Europe and the Asia/Pacific. Its applications cover industries such as marine, automotive, off-highway/construction, RV/caravan, industrial machinery, enclosures, HVAC, medical equipment, and network, telecommunications and computers.

Southco has expanded its operations, both domestically and internationally, primarily through acquisitions and innovations. Most of its growth to date has occurred in Asia where it has established distribution and manufacturing facilities.

In 2014the company introduced and a range of innovative products, including the release of R4-EM 8 Series Electronic Rotary Latch; three new models of AV Display Mount (with enhanced functionality and design flexibility); and non-locking versions of its AC-30 and AC-40 Actuator styles that provide quick, reliable actuation in heavy duty applications.

In 2013 the company continued to expand its presence in Asia with the opening of a new office and distribution center in Osaka, Japan; the location provides sales operations, customer service, technical support, warehouse, and distribution services across the country.

The company has also expanded its operations to other parts of the world, including Sweden, where its Mobella subsidiary (marine locks, latches, and accessories) is located, and Stuttgart, Germany, which is home to its automotive engineering facility.

Because of the gradual shift of its customer base, Southco has shut down some of its US operations and moved them to China.

Through additional acquisitions Southco has built on its marine product offerings, as well as its lock mechanism and hinge making capabilities, and products for medical applications.

In 2014 the company acquired DFCI Solutions, a US-based manufacturer of quick-access fasteners and specialty latching mechanisms.

In an effort to expand its range of engineered access hardware products and to extend its global market presence into South America, Southco in 2013 acquired Brazil-based Unikey Industrial Components, a manufacturer of latches, locks, hinges, ventilation systems and related accessories.

Southco was founded as South Chester Tube Company in 1899 as a specialty pipe manufacturer to serve the rapidly growing Pennsylvania oil industry. It was reorganized as Southco in 1945, and commenced making specialty fastener devices.
