Decathlon outfits the athlete, be it novice or pro. The company, designs, manufactures, and sells athletic apparel and equipment for some 70 sports, including cycling, hiking, hunting, basketball, swimming, running, soccer, and tennis. Its lineup includes roughly 20 private label brands covering everything from combat sports to skateboarding. The company operates a global chain of over 1,100 retail stores in around 30 countries. France is its largest market, followed by Spain. Stores also offer tennis racket stringing, ice skate sharpening, bicycle tune-ups, and other services. Decathlon was founded in 1976 by Michel Leclercq and is owned by the Mulliez family, one of the wealthiest in France.

The company's 20 brands cover everything from dance and fitness to horseback riding, hunting, golf, and darts. Decathlon's Aptonia, Geonaute, and Orao produce sports nutrition, sports-related electronics, and sport-specific eyewear, respectively.

In addition to offering what it calls its Passion Brands at its 810 stores, the company also sells limited lines of its merchandise through more than a dozen specialty online and bricks-and-mortar retailers.

Decathlon is headquartered in Villeneuve-d'Ascq in Northern France. Around 65% of the chain's sales come from France, with the remaining 35% spread across 30 other countries in Europe, Asia, Africa, the Middle East, and South America. France is home to the most stores, at 304, followed by China (184), Spain (148), and Italy (109). It sells exclusively via e-commerce in Japan and Singapore.
