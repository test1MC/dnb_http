Mag Instrument was born from a bright idea. In the 1970s, the company invented the anodized aluminum flashlight and now counts police officers, firefighters, and mechanics among its fans and target audience. Mag Instrument's products appeal to a broad audience, with flashlight models ranging in size from as long as a police night stick to the size of a pencil. The company also makes rechargeable flashlight systems and accessories. Mag Instrument's products are sold in the US at outdoor goods retailers ( Oshman's ), hardware stores ( Sears ), and discounters ( Target ), as well as internationally. Founded in 1955, Mag Instrument is still run by founder Tony Maglica.

The company's corporate office in Ontario, California, serves as not only a headquarters but also its 700,000-sq.-ft. manufacturing, engineering, and distribution facility.

Mag Instrument's products, which are designed and made exclusively in the US, are sold internationally in Europe, Asia, and South America. Exports account for about a fourth of company sales.

The flashlight maker relies on big-name retailers to get its products in the hands of consumers. Mag Instrument sells its products in several stores, including Ace , Kmart , The Home Depot , REI , Lowe's , AAFES , Sears , Target , True Value , Orchard Supply , and Wal-Mart .

To extend the reach of its products, Mag Instrument produces a line of specialty products through a partnership with Swiss Army knife maker Victorinox and consumer products behemoth Procter & Gamble . To this end, Mag Instruments sells a set that includes a Maglite flashlight, Duracell batteries, and a Swiss Army knife.

The company has adjusted its workforce in recent years to ensure it could ride out the recession. Prior to the economic downturn, Mag Instruments boasted a staff of 1,000. By 2013, the flashlight firm had 850 employees.
