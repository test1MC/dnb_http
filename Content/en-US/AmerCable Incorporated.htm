AmerCable manufactures and distributes flexible electrical power and control cables used in severe environments. It also offers brackets and related hardware. Its cables are used by mining, marine and offshore oil and gas drilling and production, renewable energy, telecom, and heavy industrial customers. AmerCable also designs and manufactures cable systems for use in communication centers. AmerCable was formed in 1899 as American Wire and Cable Company and operates facilities in Arkansas and Houston. It is owned by Nexans , one of the world's largest cable and wire providers.

The company is headquartered in Arkansas and has a manufacturing facility in Texas and a service center in Utah.

Giving it a decided competitive edge, AmerCable places great importance on the safety features of its products, since they are marketed to industries impacted by intense environmental conditions, such as humidity, salt air, heat, and cold, as well as cave-ins and explosions. The company makes its CIR (crush and impact resistant) cable with no sharp metal edges and with greater flexibility, making it safer to deal with during installation. AmerCable also insulates its cable with Gexol, its branded insulating compound.

Nexans purchased AmerCable for $275 million in 2012 in a transaction that improved its position in the mining, oil, and gas cable markets and gave it a leg-up against Italy's Prysmian SpA , its chief rival.
