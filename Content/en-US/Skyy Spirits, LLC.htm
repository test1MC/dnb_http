Don't fault Skyy Spirits (dba Campari America) if it's up bright and early to raise the American flag. The company touts its SKYY Vodka as the #1 domestically made US premium vodka. Founded in 1992 by an inventor tired of hangovers, Skyy claims that its distilling process eliminates toxins, which results in a cleaner vodka that makes the morning after less, er, shall we say, painful. Its other key brand, Wild Turkey Distillery, makes bourbon. The company, which is the exclusive US importer for Campari, Cutty Sark Scotch Whiskey, and other liqueurs and spirits, adopted the Campari America moniker in 2012. A subsidiary of Davide Campari-Milano, the company manages more than 25 brands in the US.

San Francisco-based Campari America distributes international and regional brands throughout the US, Canada, and Puerto Rico.

In addition to its importing and distribution activities, the company produces Wild Turkey Straight Kentucky Bourbon in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky. Campari America has additional marketing and distribution agreements with Suntory (MIDORI, Zen Green Tea Liqueur, Yamazaki) and Morrison Bowmore Distillers (Bowmore, Auchentoshan, Glen Garioch). It also has deals with Berry Bros. & Rudd (Cutty Sark, The Glenrothes) and C&C (Carolans, Tullamore Dew, Tipperary, Irish Mist).

Campari America accounted for about 20% of Gruppo Campari's €1.5 billion ($2 billion) in 2013 sales. US sales increased 6% in 2013 versus 2012, to €312.6 ($417.7 million), mainly due to the performance of the Wild Turkey franchise (up 16%), while Skyy's sales were essentially flat. Good performance of Skyy's infused vodkas, offset the weaker performance of the core SKYY Vodka product.

In 2012 Skyy Spirits changed its trade name to Campari America to identify more closely with its Milan-based parent company, Davide Campari-Milano , and its affiliates, collectively known as Gruppo Campari, and to reflect the full scale of the company's growing portfolio of vodkas, tequilas, whiskies, rums, and liqueurs. The company has grown through brand extensions and the purchase of marketing and distribution rights to other brands.

In March 2013, the company acquired the distribution and marketing rights to the Appleton rum portfolio in the US from Kobrand Corporation for $20 million.

The company was formed by the acquisition of Skyy Spirits, LLC in 2002 and the Wild Turkey brand and distillery in 2009.
