Biosearch (formerly Puleva Biotech) researches, develops, and markets food items and ingredients made from natural compounds designed to have positive health benefits. The company's primary products include Eupoly-3, a refined fish oil high in omega-3 fatty acids and nutritional supplements for infants made from breast milk. As part of its business, Biosearch also offers third parties its research and development and product-development services. The company's European biotech firm Exxentia, which it purchased in 2008, develops plant extracts for use in the food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries. Biosearch is owned by Spanish food giant Ebro Foods .

Biosearch's research spans several lines, including cardiovascular health, obesity, diabetes, bone health, immune function, gut health, neurodegenerative diseases, child nutrition, and women's health.

Its portfolio of products includes more than 200 items that are grouped as lipids (Eupoly-3), natural extracts (Exxentia), and probiotics (Hereditum).

The company's food and nutrition areas include Dairy Products, Child Nutrition, Juices and Beverages, Bakery, Pastry, Clinical Nutrition, Animal Nutrition, and Health.

Headquartered in Spain, Biosearch enjoys a global reach. It operates production centers in Spain in Granada, Talayuela (Cáceres), and Peñafiel (Valladolid).

Biosearch works to innovate to compete in its niche of the market. It also looks to establish strong relationships with its customers. To this end, it offers its customers such services as design concepts and product co-development. In 2016, the company received authorization for registration of a new probiotic strain from the Chinese Family Planning and National Health Commission.

Biosearch serves customers in 30-plus countries.
