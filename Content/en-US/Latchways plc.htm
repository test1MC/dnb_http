Latchways is here to help you come to grips with your fear of heights. The company makes, sells, and installs a variety of industrial safety products designed to protect people from falling while working on buildings, bridges, offshore platforms, and telecommunications towers. Its cable-based equipment can be affixed horizontally, vertically, and around corners onto most architectural structures made of brick, steel, aluminum, concrete, or wood. The company's safety services subsidiary installs the company's products in the UK. Latchways' products have been employed worldwide at such sites as the Pentagon, the Manchester Stadium, and the Sydney Harbour Bridge.

Europe (excluding the UK) represents 32% of net sales, while the UK alone generates 41%. North America accounts for 18%.

Latchways operates through two segments. Safety products (around 75% of net sales) designs and manufactures fall protection equipment for people working at great heights. Safety Services (25%) installs and supplies fall protection systems and industrial safety products.

The company's products are sold worldwide, both directly to end users and also through a network of independent, trained installers. In addition, certain products such as the PRD and SRL are sold through distributors. Major customers include the US Air Force and HCL Safety Limited.

Latchways experienced declines for 2014, with revenues dropping 9% due to lower performance of its Wingrip business and declines from wind energy projects in the European markets. This was offset by a 60% surge in North America sales resulting from a major growth in its self retracting lifeline (SRL) and personal rescue device (PRD) product ranges.

In 2014 the company's net income declined by 32% due to decreased revenues, along with higher administrative expenses and higher salary costs associated with sales and development in addition to foreign exchange losses. In addition, Latchways' operating cash flow increased nearly 10% during 2014. (Note: Growth rates or declines may differ after conversion to the US dollar.)

The company's strategy is focused on expanding through organic growth along with the acquisitions of companies. Focused on growth in the North American markets, it completed a new production facility in Devizes, UK, and established a new US operation to support its customer base there. The company also invests in its technical and sales network to prepare for future growth.
