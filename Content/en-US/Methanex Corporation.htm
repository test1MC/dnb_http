Methanex methodically makes methanol. The world's #1 producer of methanol (also known as wood alcohol), Methanex produces about 9.4 million tons per year from plants in Chile, the US, Canada, Egypt, New Zealand, and Trinidad. Methanol is used to produce acetic acid, formaldehyde, and other chemicals, which are found in products such as adhesives, paints, plastic bottles, polyester, and solvents. Methanol also is used as a blending additive for gasoline called dimethyl ether (DME). It's also used to make methyl tertiary butyl ether (MTBE), which enhances octane and creates cleaner burning fuel, although production has declined because it has been found to be a groundwater contaminant.

The company has marketing offices in North America (Vancouver and Dallas), Europe (Brussels), Asia-Pacific (Hong Kong, Shanghai, Tokyo, Beijing, and Seoul), South America (Santiago), and the Middle East (Dubai).

China is its largest market, accounting for 27% of Methanex's total revenues in 2015; South Korea (17%); South America (16%); the US (10%); Europe (10%); and Canada (7%).

Methanex's wholly owned subsidiary, Waterfront Shipping, operates the world's largest methanol ocean tanker fleet. The fleet delivers methanol to storage terminals and customers' plants.

The company's customers are primarily large global and regional petrochemical manufacturers and distributors.

In 2015 Methanex's revenues decreased by 31% to $2.2 billion due to a decline in revenues from Europe and US, driven by lower prices.

That year, the company's net income decreased by 56% to $200.6 million due to lower net revenues and an increase in depreciation and amortization expenses, primarily the result of depreciation associated with the start up of commercial operations of a methanol plant in Geismar, Louisiana.

In 2015 Methanex's operating cash inflow decreased by 63% to $296.9 million.

The company focuses on the Asia-Pacific region, which continues to lead global methanol demand growth (particularly China, South Korea and Japan). It is also investing in upgrading its global distribution and supply infrastructure (including opening two production facilities in Louisiana in 2015).

In 2011 Methanex opened a joint venture facility in Egypt (with the Egyptian government and a regional investor) to primarily serve European markets.
