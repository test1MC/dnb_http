Virtual pat-downs mean hard cash for Rapiscan Systems. The company makes an array of security inspection products, from baggage handling and cargo inspection equipment to machines that screen people at airports, sea ports, and government buildings. Offerings include metal detectors, explosive detection systems, and X-ray and gamma-ray imaging scanners. Customers have included NATO , Manchester Airport , the US Department of Homeland Security , and Department of Defense . Rapiscan Systems is the security division of OSI Systems and accounts for half of its parent's sales.

The company is supported by manufacturing plants and offices in the US, Australia, Finland, Malaysia, Singapore, the UK, India, Brazil, and the United Arab Emirates.

Parent OSI touts its security division as being the only business that provides X-ray, gamma-ray, and neutron-based technologies and the only business that provides a wide range of services by offering inspection technologies at energy levels from 200 kilo electron volts to 9 mega electron volts.

Rapiscan's business is organized into four lines: baggage and parcel inspection; cargo and vehicle inspection; hold (or checked) baggage screening; and people screening. It reportedly has installed more than 70,000 security and inspection systems around the globe.

Rapiscan's customers include the UK Customs, Hong Kong International Airport, and Transportation Security Administration, US Department of Energy , and other federal agencies. The company markets and sells its products through a direct sales and marketing staff located in North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia; it also sells through a global network of independent distributors.

Rapiscan Systems roots trace back to 1993. Its current incarnation was formed in 2005 through the combination of several OSI business units, including Ancore, ARACOR, Metorex, and Rapiscan.
