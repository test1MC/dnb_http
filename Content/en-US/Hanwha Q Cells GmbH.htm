Hanwha Q-Cells (formery Q-Cells) is evolving from solar cells to solar systems. It produces solar cells made from monocrystalline and polycrystalline silicon. It also makes thin-film solar modules (collections of solar cells) used in solar arrays for residential, commercial, and industrial installations. Through partnerships with companies such as LDK Solar and MEMC Electronic Materials , its Hanwha Q-Cells International unit builds, operates, and maintains large-scale solar power plants which are then sold by the partnerships to investors. Hanwha Q-Cells gets most of its sales from Europe. In 2012 Q-cells was acquired by the Hanwha Group, which became the 3rd largest solar cell manufacturer in the world.
