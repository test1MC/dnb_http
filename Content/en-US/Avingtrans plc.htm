Avingtrans knows all about shiny metal objects. It makes precision engineered components, which run the gamut from aircraft engine parts to medical devices to roadside poles. Operating through its subsidiaries, the company designs, makes, and sells its products through two business segments: Aerospace makes pipe assemblies and polishes aerospace components, and Energy and Medical manufactures pressure and vacuum vessels. Avingtrans' Crown International subsidiary makes its CrownPole branded poles for railside or roadside signal systems, traffic cameras, or signage. Major customers have included Rolls Royce , Messier-Dowty, Meggitt , and Siemens .

Avingtrans has its registered office in Nottingham, England. The company has a presence in Europe, Asia, North America, and the UK. Its largest market is the UK, which contributed 83% of its net sales in fiscal 2015.

The company's net sales decreased in fiscal 2015 after experiencing an upward trend since 2011. The decline due to decreased sales from its Aerospace segment, partially offset by a spike in sales from Energy and Medical.

Avingtrans is looking to grow its core segments through the use of acquisitions. In 2014 it acquired the trade and certain business assets relating to the manufacture of aerospace components from Tricorn Group 's subsidiary, RMDG Aerospace. RMDG is located in Swadlincote, Derbyshire, and manufactures a range of rigid pipe assemblies and precision components for the aerospace sector. The addition strengthened Avingtrans' Aerospace division, specifically its Sigma Components operations.
