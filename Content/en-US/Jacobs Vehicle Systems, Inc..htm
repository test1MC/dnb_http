Over four million sold -- not burgers, but brakes! Jacobs Vehicle Systems, a subsidiary of Danaher Corp. makes masses of engine retarding and valve actuation products -- for those mechanically challenged, engine braking systems -- primarily for diesel engines used in heavy-duty commercial vehicles. Its valve actuation systems tout emissions control, fuel efficiency, and performance for diesel and spark-ignited engines. Marketed under the Jake Brake brand, Jacobs' products are designed for integration into engines manufactured by such noteworthy companies as Cummins , Daimler , Doosan , Hyundai Motors, Hino Motors , and PACCAR .

In addition to its US operations which features its 260,000 sq. ft. design, manufacturing, and testing facility, Jacobs has support sites in the UK, China, France, Kora, and Japan. It primarily serves the markets of China, Europe, India, Japan, Korea, and North America.

Jacobs' customers are primarily major manufacturers of class 2 through class 8 vehicles, and sales are typically made through their direct sales personnel.

The company operates as a part of the Industrial Technologies Segment of its parent company. This segment contributed 18% of its revenues in 2013.

Jacobs aims to expand its technology into emerging markets. In 2012 the company signed its first development agreement with one of India's largest commercial vehicle manufacturers. In 2013 it signed another agreement with one of Turkey's largest commercial vehicle manufacturers.
