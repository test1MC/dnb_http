In its business, Shimadzu spends a lot of time on analysis. Shimadzu makes a wide variety of laboratory instruments (gas and liquid chromatographs, mass spectrometers), surface analyzers, testing and inspection instruments, scales, aircraft equipment (flight control and landing gear systems), medical systems (X-ray, ultrasound, fluoroscopy), and industrial manufacturing equipment (deposition systems, LCD injection systems, turbomolecular pumps). Analytical and measuring instruments account for about 60% of sales. Japan is the company's #1 market representing more then 50% of sales. The business dates back to 1875, when founder Genzo Shimadzu Sr. began making instruments for education in chemistry and physics.

Shimadzu's Analytical and Measuring Instruments segment includes chromatographs and spectrometers, light absorption analysis systems, and compositions and surface analysis systems to support the pharmaceuticals, petrochemicals, food and beverage, and environmental areas. Its Medical Systems unit offers digital X-ray, PET and CT scanners, and diagnostic ultrasound equipment. Shimadzu developed the direct conversion flat panel detector, which detects images not previously available. Its Aircraft Equipment business makes products for aircraft safety and passenger comfort, which are used in flight control systems, cockpit displays, and onboard air management systems.

The company is looking to expand its operations in emerging markets such as China and India, with a focus on products for the pharmaceutical, health care, and environmental industries. It established an R&D center in Shanghai to develop products tailored for the local market. Another part of its growth strategy is to expand its aftermarket sales, service, and support operations in those regions.

In established markets such as the US (through Shimadzu Scientific Instruments ) and Europe, Shimadzu is focused on developing value-added and energy-efficient versions of its instruments, in addition to introducing new products for applications including solar cell film deposition, molecular imaging, and automotive engine combustion measurement.

Shimadzu is actively involved in the development of new products for an aging society and next-generation medical treatment. Its research and development in the medical focuses on molecular imaging technology that allows symptoms to be detected before the onset of disease. It is also involved in the development of underground soil cleanup technology in the environmental field.
