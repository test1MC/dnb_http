Recro Pharma is in the business of providing non-opioid treatments for serious pain. The clinical stage specialty pharmaceutical firm is developing pain medications, for use initially in the post-operative setting. By focusing on non-opioid based drugs, Recro expects its products will avoid side effects associated with opioids, including addiction, constipation, and respiratory distress. Its lead product, an intranasal formulation of Dexmedetomidine, or Dex, has completed a Phase Ib placebo controlled trial demonstrating pain relief. Subsequent Dex products will be developed to treat chronic pain and cancer pain. Founded in 2007, the company went public in 2014 with a $30 million offering.

Recro Pharma went public in March 2014 with an offering of 3,750,000 shares priced at $8 each. Proceeds from the $30 million offering will be used to fund trials of DEX-IN for pain control following orthopedic and abdominal surgery, for preclinical animal toxicology studies, working capital, and other expenses.

A emerging growth company, Recro Pharma has no revenue. The company posted a loss of nearly $2 million in 2013, compared with a loss of about $1.5 million in 2012. Research and development expenses totaled $544,000 in 2013, an increase of 2% versus the prior year.

While focusing initially on treating post-operative pain, the company in looking to further develop non-opioid products for other indications including chronic pain and breakthrough cancer pain.
