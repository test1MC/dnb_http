Axalta Powder Coatings Systems (formerly DuPont Powder Coatings USA) wants to make sure your cold steel doesn't stay that way. A part of The Carlyle Group affiliate Flash Bermuda's Axalta Coatings business, it makes coatings for building materials, automotive OEMs, agriculture and construction equipment, and general metals. Its products are used in various applications, such as coatings for rebar and finishings for anti-corrosion pipe. Axalta Powder Coatings pioneered such coating technologies as UV curing. The company operates manufacturing facilities primarily in Asia, Europe, and the Americas. Brands include Alesta and Nap-Gard.

The company used to operate as a part of DuPont Performance Coatings . In 2013, DuPont divested the Performance Coatings business to Flash Bermuda Co. Ltd. (formed by affiliates of The Carlyle Group ) for $4.9 billion. Following this, DuPont Performance Coatings was renamed as Axalta Coating Systems; and DuPont Powder Coatings is known as Axalta Powder Coatings Systems.

Doing business in more than 130 countries, Axalta Coating Systems operates 35 manufacturing centers, seven R&D facilities and 42 customer training centers.

Axalta Powder Coatings manufactures coatings for pipelines and other applications.

Parent Axalta Coating serves more than 120,000 customers in 130 countries and offers a full range of coating systems. It supports customers in key industries such as transportation, aerospace, rail, marine, architecture, agriculture, and construction equipment.
