Who says nasal spray is just for sinus problems? Evoke Pharma is developing a nasal spray to treat gastrointestinal problems in women who also suffer from diabetes. It main drug candidate is designed to treat symptoms of gastroparesis, a common problem for diabetes patients, where the stomach takes too long to digest food. The nasal spray allows the drug to bypass the digestive system and directly enter the bloodstream to reduce symptoms such as bloating, nausea, and lack of appetite. Evoke Pharma bought the rights to the drug from Questcor in 2007; Phase III trials will begin by 2014. Evoke Pharma went public in 2013, raising $25 million in its IPO, which it will use to further fund drug development.
