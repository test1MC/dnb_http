Chr. Hansen lends a hand with the fixins. The company manufactures natural ingredients for companies the food, pharmaceutical, nutritional, and agricultural industries. It makes and markets cultures for dairy, meat, and wine production; enzymes; natural colors; flavoring ingredients; and products for health, nutrition, and food safety for both humans and animals. Chr. Hansen is also involved in making probiotics (products containing beneficial bacteria), such as cultures that are used in making probiotic yogurts, cheese, buttermilk, kefir, and sour cream. It has operations in more than 30 countries worldwide.

In 2007 the company created a Health and Nutrition business division in order to speed up product innovation in order to meet the growing global emphasis on health. It also combined its cultures and enzymes operations into a new division called Innovation. This move was made in conjunction with the 2008 retirement of  Peter Olesen as the company's EVP of corporate research in order to assure a smooth transfer of knowledge. Olesen continues to work for Chr. Hansen as chief science officer with a focus on functional foods and probiotics.

That year it sold its Spanish and Indian paprika businesses in order to focus on its core value-added products such as natural color and flavor pigments and compounds, for the food and beverage industries, as well as culture and enzyme products. The sale included paprika and spice oleoresin, rosemary extract, paprika powder, turmeric, and chlorophyll products.
