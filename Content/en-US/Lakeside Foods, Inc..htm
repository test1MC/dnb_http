Lakeside Foods likes that it's full of beans -- green, wax, lima, and more. The company specializes in the production of private-label canned and frozen vegetables and beans. Lakeside's customers are retail food industry companies, including Roundy's and IGA , located in the Midwest. The food manufacturer and marketer also offers other food items, such as canned meat and stews; jams, jellies, and preserves; frozen and shelf-stable meals; salsa and other sauces; and whipped toppings. The food maker operates more than a dozen plants and about 10 distribution centers in both Minnesota and Wisconsin. Lakeside exports its food products to nearly 15 countries overseas.

Based in Wisconsin, Lakeside Foods operates both manufacturing plants and distribution centers in Minnesota and Wisconsin. It serves customers across the US and internationally across nearly 15 countries.

Private brand leader Lakeside Foods serves customers in several sectors, such as retail, industrial, and foodservice. It also co-packs for other companies. The company is a key supplier to major national retailers.

To make its products even more convenient for its customers, Lakeside has introduced a stand-up steamable pouch, which was featured in industry publication Packing World for its improved merchandizing capabilities for retailers over typical lay-down pillow-style bags.

Lakeside Foods, Wisconsin's first commercial canning company, traces its roots back to 1883, when founder Albert Landreth began canning peas in the kitchen of a small hotel overlooking Lake Michigan. Landreth built his first canning plant in 1887, directly off of the hotel.
