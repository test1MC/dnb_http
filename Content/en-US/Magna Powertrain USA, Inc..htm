Magna Powertrain is not a charter to restrain the tyrannical power of a high-speed bullet train -- the name is purely automotive and refers to Magna Powertrain USA, Inc., which concerns itself with the manufacture of powertrain components. The company makes driveline & chassis control systems, including its Dynamax all-wheel-drive coupling system, transfer cases, axle drives, and steering systems. Fluid pressure & controls products include engine, oil, water and vacuum pumps. It also offers stampings, die castings, and engineering services. An operating unit of Canada-based auto parts maker giant Magna International , Magna Powertrain has about 25 global manufacturing sites.

Headquartered in Troy, Michigan, the company has operations in North America, Europe, and Asia. It has about 25 manufacturing facilities nationwide.

Leveraging global partnerships, Magna Powertrain continues to expand operations in emerging markets. Magna Steyr India, a joint venture between Magna Steyr and Magna Powertrain, produce engine transmission systems with Hero Motors (HML), a Hero Group subsidiary.

The company supports its global expansion with high-tech products that provide enhanced driving performance to its customers. Over the years Magna Powertrain has debuted its intelligent AWD coupling system -- Dynamax. The system continuously monitors driving conditions, anticipating AWD system requirements. The technology improves traction, thus reducing fuel consumption and CO 2 emissions.

The company operates under the Powertrain System segment of Magna International. Powertrain System accounted for about 12% of its parent's revenue in 2012.

The auto sector has showed that a large quantity of new sales will be from customers in India and China and the company is focused on growth in these markets. In mid-2013, Magna Powertrain launched a new facility in the Tianjin Airport Economic Area in China to produce rear axle drive modules and power take-off units for locally assembled Volkswagen and Audi models.

Also that year Magna Powertrain announced it will invest $12 million to expand its Muncie, Indiana, manufacturing center to be completed in early 2014. The initiative will add equipment for a new automotive component manufacturing and assembly line to accommodate new business from General Motors.

Magna Powertrain also grows by acquisitions fueled by its parent. In 2012 it purchased Germany-based ixetic Verwaltungs GmbH, a manufacturer of automotive vacuum, engine, and transmission pumps with two manufacturing facilities in Germany and one in each of Bulgaria and China. The deal improved its market position in the field of vacuum and transmission oil pumps, and it enhanced its footprint in Europe.
