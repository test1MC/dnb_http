58.com aims to be China's #1 website. The company operates an online marketplace for local businesses and consumers in China. Like a cross between Alibaba and Craigslist , 58.com features postings for housing, jobs, used goods, automotive, pets, and tickets. Its website and mobile app count more than 125 million visitors every month. 58.com lists postings in 380 cities, but most of its content comes from China's five major cities -- Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, and Chengdu. Businesses pay 58.com to become members and list their services; altogether, the website hosts more than 4 million active local merchants. Founded in 2005, 58.com went public in the US in 2013, raising $187 million.
