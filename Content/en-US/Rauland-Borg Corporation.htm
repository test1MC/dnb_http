Rauland-Borg helps open up communications in some very important places - namely hospitals and schools. The company makes integrated communications systems, incorporating handsets, intercoms, software, and other elements for use in the health care and education sectors. Its Responder product line, used in some 4,000 hospitals worldwide, offers various integration capabilities to traditional nurse call systems. The Telecenter system provides intercom functionality and integrated internal communications to educational facilities. Rauland-Borg also makes accessories for the two markets, including synchronized clock systems, call cords, and overhead paging systems. The company dates back to 1922.

Rauland-Borg's Biamp Systems subsidiary makes professional audio equipment such as mixers, amplifiers, and digital signal processors. Its products are used in settings such as corporate boardrooms, airports, courtrooms, civic centers, and sports arenas.

After selling the Rauland Corporation to Zenith Radio (now Zenith Electronics ) in 1948, Norm Rauland and George Borg formed Rauland-Borg.
