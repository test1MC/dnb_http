QUIKRETE is set in its ways as the largest maker of packaged concrete and cement mixes in the US and Canada. It makes more than 200 dry-packaged cement and concrete products, including concrete mixes, mortar mixes, and stuccos, as well as sand and aggregates. Products are sold to do-it-yourself consumers and professional contractors. QUIKRETE also makes coatings and concrete repair products, such as vinyl patching mixes and tubed crack sealers. QUIKRETE operates more than 150 manufacturing plants throughout the US, Canada, Puerto Rico, and South America. Founded in 1940 by Gene Winchester, QUIKRETE is still owned and operated by the Winchester family.

QUIKRETE makes and markets products under four categories: core products, commercial grade products, specialty products, and repair products. The company's subsidiaries include SPEC MIX, Pavestone, Custom Building Products, Target Technologies, Daubois, and QPR.

Residential products allow homeowners to design their own exterior landscapes by laying their own bricks and stepping stones, creating walls, fences, walkways, and trellises, or repairing chipped concrete surfaces.

In addition to home use, QUIKRETE's concrete products have been used in building and renovating a variety of large projects, including the National Museum of the American Indian and the San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge. Its structural repair products are used by government agencies, such as the Colorado Department of Transportation , to repair roads, bridges, runways, tunnels, and other infrastructure systems. QUIKRETE's special non-shrink grout products have been used in the construction of several wind farms.

Product testing, research and development, and quality assurance activities are located in Atlanta, where QUIKRETE calls home. QUIKRETE operates plants in the US, Canada, Puerto Rico, and Central and South America.

The company serves a variety of industries, such as concrete, masonry and stucco construction and repair. Particularly, QUIKRETE serves contractors, architects, specifiers, dealers, distributors, and other construction professionals.

Sports venues are a big part of the company's projects portfolio. QUIKRETE's products have been used in the development of Gillette Stadium in Foxboro, Massachusetts; Ford Field and Comerica Park in Detroit; Coors Field in Denver; and Fenway Park in Boston.

QUIKRETE works to keep its product mix fresh by finding ways to incorporate recycled materials into its concrete. The 2016 launch of QUIKRETE's Form and Pour product, for example, expanded its line of structural repair products. Its "green concrete" product, which contains recycled aggregates, fly ash, and slag cement, helped the company reach new markets along the West Coast.

QUIKRETE has also been focused on its professional products. In 2013 QUIKRETE rolled out an enhanced line of professional concrete coatings, as well as extensions to its suite of Zip & Mix and stucco items, to provide professionals and consumers with repeatable coating application and design.
