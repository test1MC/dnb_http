Lytx (formerly DriveCam) has its electronic eyes on your driving skills. The company designs, manufactures, and sells video surveillance equipment used by commercial fleets to improve driver and traffic safety. Video cameras installed in vehicles are used to record accidents and otherwise monitor drivers' actions, with an eye toward controlling insurance costs. In addition to video monitoring, the company's Driving Behavior Management System incorporates driving performance management software and driver counseling. Customers include utility, waste management, and transit companies, as well as operators of taxi, ambulance, and bus fleets. The company also has a product that monitors teen drivers.

Lytx has operations in North America, Europe, Australia, South Africa, and Asia, and its products are used in some 100,000 commercial and government vehicles. In order to extend its international scope, the company in 2013 opened a new office in London.

The company serves more than 900 commercial and government fleet clients. Lytx monitors 20 billion driving miles each year and protects more than 450,000 drivers around the globe. It owns 16 patents and has 26 patents pending.

The company ended 2013 with orders hovering around the $100 million mark for the second straight year. Lytx claims it has been profitable for four years in a row and added 55,000 new subscriptions during 2013.

It attributed the growth to global expansion, strategic partnerships, and sustained growth within its existing customer fleets. It has also been helped by higher demand for its products and services within the mid-market, government, waste, transportation, and logistics spheres.

The company was founded in 1998 by Gary Rayner after an incident in which he was the victim of another driver's road rage. It changed its name from DriveCam to Lytx in 2013.
