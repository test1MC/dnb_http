Plumbers take a shine to BrassCraft. The company is one of North America's largest makers of water and gas connectors for residential and commercial construction projects. A subsidiary of building products maker Masco , BrassCraft's product list includes flexible gas and water connectors, faucet and toilet repair parts, brass fittings, showerheads, and commercial water supply parts. The company has four manufacturing facilities in the US. BrassCraft sells its products through direct sales and via a network of wholesale distributors and retailers in the US, Canada, and other countries.

The company has facilities in California, Michigan, New Jersey, North Carolina, and Texas.

BrassCraft has retooled its marketing and product development divisions in order to focus on developing new product lines. The company is looking to grow its core water and gas products. Key initiatives include streamlining package design and developing environmentally friendly concepts. One such product is a line of tankless water heater installation kits.

Expanding its reach, in 2012 the company launched a new Spanish-language website, appealing to a growing number of Hispanic plumbing professionals among its customers. On the product side, that year BrassCraft launched a new line of coated stainless steel water heater connectors designed to provide superior corrosion resistance to deterioration from harsh chemicals.

BrassCraft's products are marketed in thousands of plumbing wholesale and retail outlets across Canada, the US, and other countries around the world. It uses print advertising to market its products and sells its products through its sales force and as well as sales representatives such as Big Rivers Marketing (throughout southern Illinois and eastern Missouri) and W.T. Leonard & Associates.

The company operates as a part of the Plumbing Products segment of its parent company. This segment contributed 39% to its net revenue in 2013.

Robert Zell founded the company, which lays claim to originating the idea of flexible plumbing components, in 1946.
