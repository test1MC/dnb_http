Putting all its media eggs in one basket, Cox Media Group is the organization that holds Cox Enterprises' broadcasting, publishing, and digital operations. The independently-operated divisions comprise about 15 broadcast television stations (Cox Television), more than 55 radio stations ( Cox Radio ), seven daily newspapers, more than a dozen non-daily newspapers ( Cox Newspapers ), and more than 100 digital services. Daily papers include The Austin American-Statesman and The Atlanta Journal-Constitution. Cox Media also includes direct marketing firm Cox Target Media , and sells television and online advertising through its leading national ad-sales company, Cox Reps.

Cox Media has broadcasting, publishing, and digital operations throughout the US.

The group accounts for about 12% of its parent company's revenues. Cox Enterprises established the relatively young Cox Media Group in 2009 in order to take an integrated approach to its media holdings.

Cox Enterprises formed the group to save costs and leverage sales, research, and digital functions across its newspaper, television, and radio businesses. An example of such efforts includes using reporters interchangeably and employing collaboration across all media platforms.

In 2013 Cox Media was accepted into the Google AdWords Premier SMB Partner (PSP) Program. The partnership furthers Cox Media's ability to provide digital marketing solutions for its local and regional advertising clients.

The company's strategy includes focusing on larger markets, cross-media collaboration, and heightened impact in fewer markets.
