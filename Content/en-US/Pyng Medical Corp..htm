Pyng Medical commercializes trauma and resuscitation products for emergency medical treatment. It makes an intraosseous infusion device called FAST1 (First Access for Shock and Treatment) that hydrates victims of rapid blood loss or vein collapse -- usually the result of cardiac arrest or severe trauma. The device is used by emergency medical personnel, including those in the military (specifically, the US Department of Defense ), which accounts for nearly half of Pyng Medical's total units shipped. Other products include tools for pelvic stabilization, hemorrhage control, and emergency airway management. The firm sells its devices in North America, Europe, and Asia. Pyng Medical is being acquired by Teleflex .
