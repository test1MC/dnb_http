International Fibres (formerly Chapelthorpe) has it covered. The company produces colored polypropylene fibers used in the automotive, floor coverings, technical textiles, geotextitiles, and home furnishings markets. It also has a joint venture in the specialist coatings industry and supplies vinyl-coated paper and plastisols to the wall coverings industry. International Fibres is one of the largest producers of colored polypropylene fiber in Europe and a top supplier to the US automotive market. The company has operations in the US and UK (Drake Extrusion), Austria (Asota), and Belgium (Exelto).

The company makes colored polypropylene staple fiber and filament yarn and is a leading independent global supplier.

International Fibres serves customers in industries such as automotive, concrete, textiles, and home furnishing.

As part of the company's long term growth strategy, in 2013 International Fibres acquired Exelto NV, a Belgium-based polypropylene staple fiber producer. It plans to use its position as the technical leader in polypropylene fiber production to build on Exelto's highly efficient fiber production lines. The deal also enables Exelto to enhance its product portfolio by accessing the R&D and strong market positioning of International Fibres.

In early 2011 Chapelthorpe delisted its stock from the London Stock Exchange after Swedish investment firm Bronsstadet AB acquired all the shares it didn't already own. It then formed International Fibres Group Limited as a holding company for its fiber businesses.
