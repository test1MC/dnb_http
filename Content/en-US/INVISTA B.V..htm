As one of the word's largest makers of spandex, INVISTA's business model surveys an expanding vista of spandex, nylon, and polyester product users. The Koch Industries company is a global leader in polymer and fiber manufacturing. INVISTA consists of four major business units -- Apparel, Intermediates, Performance Surfaces and Materials, and Polymers and Resins -- and its portfolio includes brand names CoolMax, Antron, Terate Dacron, Cordura, Lycra, Polyclear, Stainmaster, and Thermolite. The company's products are used in clothing, plastic packaging, automobile airbags, and pharmaceutical ingredients.

INVISTA operates in about 20 countries worldwide, with 24 sales offices and 16 manufacturing sites North America, South America, Europe, and the Asia-Pacific region. It has a presence in Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, Colombia, France, Germany, Hong Kong, India, Italy, Japan, Luxembourg, Mexico, Netherlands, Singapore, South Korea, Spain, Switzerland, Taiwan, Thailand, Turkey, the UK, and the US.

INVISTA is a producer of chemical intermediates, primarily for nylon, spandex and polyester applications. The company's specialty chemicals provide value-adding solutions to customers in a variety of applications: apparel, carpeting, polyurethanes, transportation, coatings, adhesives, building materials, pharmaceutical intermediates, cleaning solutions, fragrances, and printing inks.

The company sells its products via its sales offices around the world.

In response to growing demand, the company has been expanding in Latin America and Asia.

The company pursues growth via investment in expanding facilities and seeking innovative technologies. For example, in 2016 it invested in expanding capacity at its Terate and TERRIN polyols manufacturing facility in Vlissingen, the Netherlands. enabling the company to better serve the growing European demand for competitively priced products with sustainable content.

Originally a unit of DuPont called DuPont Textiles and Interior, INVISTA was sold to Koch in 2004. Koch then merged the company with its own fibers unit, KoSa , leaving only INVISTA remaining.

In 2011 it made plans to invest more than $100 million in a new spandex plant in Brazil. INVISTA has two other sites in Brazil, which produce its SUPPLEX brand fabric and its LYCRA fiber.

In 2012, the company announced the start-up of a new production line in Singapore designed specifically to produce a series of specialty Lycra fiber products to serve the growing innovation needs of the Asian region.

In 2013 it won approval from the Shanghai Environmental Protection Bureau to build 215,000-ton per year hexamethylene diamine plant at the Shanghai Chemical Industrial Park. In 2012 Hengli Petrochemical opened a purified terephthalic acid project using INVISTA technology Dalian, Liaonin. (Earlier, in 2009, INVISTA opened a textile R&D center in China, its third such facility worldwide).

INVISTA also teamed up with ARVIND in 2013 to develop new denim products in India, giving INVISTA an entry into the high-potential global denim market.
