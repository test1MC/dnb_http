Adiabatic Logic tries to take an even-keeled approach to microchip design. The company creates and licenses intellectual-property designs for buffer and output driver chips, which are used to reduce power usage in portable electronic devices including PCs and wireless phones. Adiabatic Logic touts its use of energy recycling techniques as allowing its designs to operate with much lower power consumption than traditional models. ("Adiabatic" means "without gain or loss of heat.") Its technology can be applied to settings ranging from part of a chip to full board systems.The company, which was founded in 2002, is a subsidiary of holding company Cambridge Technology Group.

Adiabatic's two main products are the Intelligent Output Driver (IOD) and the Adiabatic Super Buffer (ASB).

The IOD uses the speed of submicron CMOS to mimic the voltage-current drive characteristics of a classic driver with a source (or series) terminator resistor. The ASB is on-chip technology which enable loaded interconnects to be driven using just o50% of the power used by a conventional buffer.
