OMNOVA Solutions is not quite omnipresent but it does manufacture and sell performance chemicals and engineered decorative surfaces around the world. It is an innovator of emulsion polymers in dry powder and latex forms, specialty chemicals and also decorative and functional surfaces for a variety of commercial, industrial and residential end uses. The performance chemicals segment produces emulsion polymers and specialty chemicals to makers of paper, carpet, and textiles. The engineered surfaces segment makes vinyl- and paper-based wall coverings, coated fabrics, laminates, and films.

The company serves the global marketplace from 10 chemical production facilities in Europe, Asia, and North America. It has strategically located manufacturing plants in China, France, India, Thailand, and the US. In fiscal 2013 the US accounted for about 64% of OMNOVA's total sales.

OMNOVA operates two business segments: Performance Chemicals and Engineered Surfaces. Of the company's fiscal 2013 net sales, 76% were derived from the Performance Chemicals segment and 24% were derived from the Engineered Surfaces segment.

The Performance Chemicals segment sells its products directly to manufacturers through dedicated internal marketing, sales, and technical service teams. Major customers include Sherwin Williams, RPM , Rhodia , PPG, PGI, Freudenburg, Hyosung , Shurtape , Xerox , and Fiberweb.

Engineered Surfaces markets its products under numerous brand names to different industries. Its customers include Armstrong , CGT, BYD , Ashley Furniture , Patrick Industries , Herculite, and Masco .

In fiscal 2013 OMNOVA's revenue declined by 10% due to an 11% drop Performance Chemicals sales decreased by 11% (as the result of reduced customer pricing caused by lower raw material costs as well as competitive pricing pressure) and 6% decline in Engineered Surfaces sales due to due to lower volumes.

The company's net income decreased by 29% that year due to a decline in revenues and a loss from discontinued operations.

OMNOVA's operating cash inflow decreased to $45.8 million in fiscal 2013 (from $65.3 million in fiscal 2012) primarily due to a decline in net income and a change in working capital as a result of cash spent on liabilities.

In 2014 OMNNOVA and Muraspec announced an expanded distribution arrangement that will enable Muraspec to offer OMNOVA's complete range of coated fabrics to customers in Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.

That year OMNOVA introduced PreVaill XMT and XMT Plus vinyl upholsteries, designed to meet the rigors of the transit market's busy buses and trains, while creating a comfortable and easy-to-maintain passenger environment.

To focus on its higher value operations, OMNOVA has divested its commercial wallcovering businesses.

Following the sale and exit from the commercial wallcovering business (in 2012), the company changed the name of its Decorative Products segment to Engineered Surfaces to better reflect the role of innovation and the functional performance features that are valued in its served markets. (It sold some of its North American wallcovering operations to J. Josephon, and ts UK-based Muraspec wallcovering business to a2e Venture Catalysts. The divestments, which have netted the company about $10 million for the North American business and $6.2 million for the UK business, allowed OMNOVA to focus on its global performance chemicals unit and the coated fabrics, laminates, and performance films operations within its decorative products unit).

The company acquired specialty chemicals manufacturer ELIOKEM International from AXA Private Equity in 2011 for $300 million. The deal creates a larger, more diverse company by adding manufacturing capabilities in Europe and Asia to OMNOVA's production facilities in the US. France-based ELIOKEM is now part of OMNOVA's performance chemicals business. As part of the ELOKEM integration, in 2012 OMNOVA expanded manufacturing capability for its Pliotec water-based acrylic coating resins to include three locations in the US.

In 2010 OMNOVA acquired Dow Chemical 's hollow sphere plastic pigment, or HPP, product line. At the same time, the company terminated its RohmNova joint venture with partner Rohm and Haas , which was acquired by Dow. The JV, in place since 2002, made and marketed coated paper and paperboard. The acquired HPP business serve the same markets.

FMR LLC owns 15% of the OMNOVA.
