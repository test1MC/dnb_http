Sourcebooks is an independent publisher of a wide variety of fiction and non-fiction titles through imprints such as Sourcebooks Casablanca, Sourcebooks Hysteria, Sourcebooks Landmark, Sourcebooks MediaFusion, and Sphinx Publishing. Sourcebooks authors include college expert Edward B. Fiske ( The Fiske Guide to Colleges ), NPR commentator and Sports Illustrated writer Frank Deford ( An American Summer ), and CBS Early Show host Hannah Storm ( Go Girl!). The company was founded in 1987 by former advertising executive Dominique Raccah who left Leo Burnett , cashed in $17,000 from her 401K plan, and launched a publishing house from her upstairs bedroom.
