Gems Sensors is a global manufacturer of liquid level, flow, and pressure sensors, and solid-state relays and barriers. It designs, develops, and manufactures hundreds of products for the environmental/process controls and tool and component markets. Individual items include continuous level transmitters, pressure transducers, safe relays, solenoid valves, turbine sensors, and switches that monitor and control temperature, float type level, and flow levels. Its products are manufactured for use in various industries such as industrial, marine, medical, military, and nuclear, as well as wastewater treatment plants. Gems Sensors is a subsidiary of industrial and medical conglomerate Danaher Corporation .

The company primarily serves customers in the US, as well as European countries. Gems Sensors has offices in China, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, and the UK.

Gems is part of its parent company's Industrial Technologies' segment, which generates nearly 15% of Danaher's revenues ($3.3 billion in 2015).

This company sells its products through distributors, the Internet, a direct sales force. It targets the medical sciences, oil and gas, alternative energy, semiconductor manufacturing, off-highway vehicles, process tank gauging, marine and shipboard service, boiler control, and water and wastewater processing markets.

In 2014 Gems launched new 3500 series low-pressure transducers, which brought a versatile low pressure capability to the product line and opened up a host of new applications across medical and general industrial markets.

Gems Sensors has its origins in 1955 when Edward Moore developed a level indicator in his basement. He patented the invention in 1959, and with associate Gordon Seigle founded the Gems Company. The Gems name comes from a slightly unconventional combination of their initials.
