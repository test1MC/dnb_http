Shanghai Hua Hong NEC Electronics knows its way around the semiconductor circuit. As an integrated circuit foundry, Hua Hong NEC manufactures microchips for OEMs on a contract basis. Its products include flash memory (used in handheld electronic devices and smart cards), as well as microprocessors and mixed-signal, analog, and logic devices (wireless and PC products). The company also makes chips that power LCD screens and store data for electronics when they are turned off. Hua Hong NEC was founded in 1997 as a joint venture between NEC Electronics (now part of Renesas Electronics) and Huahong Group, a subsidiary of Chinese state-run company China Electronics Corp.
