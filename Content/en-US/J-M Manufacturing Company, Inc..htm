Extruding PVC and HDPE is as easy as ABC for J-M Manufacturing. The company and PW Eagle Inc. do business under the JM Eagle trademark, and produce polyvinyl chloride (PVC) and high-density polyethylene (HDPE) pipes, fittings, and tubing products. Pipe diameters range from half an inch to 63 inches; the lineup's applications include sewer as well as water main construction, and electric and communication line projects. JM Eagle operates about 20 manufacturing facilities, mainly in the US, catering to the demands of utility, plumbing, irrigation, and electrical industries.

J-M Manufacturing is based in California and has 20 manufacturing plants spanning 15 US states and Mexico.

The company aims to piggyback on the wave of replace and repair pipeline infrastructure projects. To this end, it has introduced a number of product innovations, including a dual wall polyethylene (PE) pipe and an internal joint restraint system The latter features an easier to install, less costly improvement over systems needing external joint restraints. The company annually produces 2.2 billion pounds of pipe.

J-M Manufacturing markets its products through TV advertising. It sells its products direct and via distributors. The company serves a variety of industrial sectors, including utility, plumbing, irrigation, electrical, natural gas, drainage, and sewage, and retail.

Like most companies in the manufacturing sector, J-M Manufacturing grows by expanding its manufacturing capacity and launching new products.

J-M Manufacturing was formed after eight PVC pipe plants were purchased from the Johns Manville Corporation in 1982. The company operated as a subsidiary of Formosa Plastics Corporation, U.S.A. , until late 2005, when J-M exited through a management buyout.
