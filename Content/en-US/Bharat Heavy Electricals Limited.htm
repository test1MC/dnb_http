When Mother India needs heavy electrical work to be done she turns to Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL). A major engineering and manufacturing company and one of the world's largest vendors of power generation equipment, BHEL manufactures more than 180 products, including boilers, generators, turbines, and transformers for power, transmission, industry, rail transportation, renewable energy, oil and gas, and defense industries. The company separates its operations across two segments: Power (60% of sales) and Industry (20%). India generates about 95% of its net sales, but BHEL also sells its products in some 75 other countries.

BHEL operates a footprint in 77 countries with an installed capacity of manufactured power plants nearing 10,000 MW residing in Malaysia, Oman, Libya, Iraq, the UAE, Bhutan, Egypt, and New Zealand.

The company serves a wide range of sectors which include power, transmission, industry, transportation (railway), renewable energy, oil and gas, and defense.

In 2014 BHEL's revenues decreased 20% due to a 26% drop in Industry segment sales and a 18% decline in Power segment sales.
