Xylem primes the pump for the future of water and other fluids. The company (whose name derives from a Greek term about water and roots) makes fluid-handling and related products for treating and recycling wastewater. It operates in two divisions: Water Infrastructure and Applied Water. Water Infrastructure makes pumps, treatment and testing equipment, and controls and analytical instruments, while Applied Water offers pumps, valves, heat exchangers, controls, and dispensing equipment. Xylem's products are sold under about 40 different brands, including Flygt and Goulds.

Xylem's two main segments are Water Infrastructure (61% of net sales) and Applied Water (39%). Xylem's water products are used by public utilities, residential and commercial building services, industrial customers and agricultural applications. It products address critical water issues such as growing water scarcity, aging infrastructure, and environmental regulations.

The company has operations in more than 150 countries around the world, with over 350 locations in more than 40 countries. Xylem generates almost 60% of its revenue from outside the US.

Xylem's principal customers are located in North America, Central and South America, as well as EMEA countries (Europe, the Middle East, and Africa). New plants have opened in China, India, the Czech Republic, and Saudi Arabia. Xylem has gained a position in China's urban water recirculation projects, which are supported by research and development centers in a number of China's major cities.

Xylem's revenues fell 7% from $3.92 billion in 2014 to $3.65 billion in 2015. Its profits remained static for 2014 and 2015, hovering around the $340 million mark for both years. In addition, its operating cash flow surged by 7% due to changes in inventory and improved collections of receivables.

The revenue decline was fueled by a 9% drop in Water Infrastructure sales and a 4% decline in Applied Water sales. The industrial market in 2015 experienced a decline due to decreases in dewatering applications in the oil and gas market, which more than offset increases in the balance of the industrial market. Irrigation applications organic revenue also declined due to severe flooding conditions in the southeast and southwest regions of the US and the lacking of a strong fourth quarter in 2014.

Xylem pursues acquisitions that broaden its core product portfolio, grow its geographic footprint, and improves its position in strategic markets. In mid-2016, it agreed to acquire Sensus for about $1.7 billion in cash. Sensus provides smart meters, network technology, and data analytics services for the water, electric, and gas industries. Sensus had $837 million in revenue during 2016.

Xylem was spun off from ITT Corp. as a standalone company in October 2011.
