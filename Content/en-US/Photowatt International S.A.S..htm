Photowatt International is wise in the ways of solar power. Founded in 1979 as a research organization to study photovoltaic (PV) technologies, the company engages in virtually every stage of solar energy production, from growing ingots and manufacturing wafers, cells, and modules to designing generators and developing infrastructures in remote areas. Also known as Photowatt Technologies, it makes some of the thinnest cells on the global solar market used to produce high-performance modules said to last for 25 years. Other products include inverters and solar kits for residential and industrial customers. Photowatt is a subsidiary of EDF Energies Nouvelles .

The company sells its products through a network of distributors in Africa, Asia, Europe, and North America.

Photowatt's products are used in both on-grid and off-grid applications. They can be used in solar energy systems connected to a utility grid to provide additional power to homes and businesses. Grid-connected applications continue to experience rapid growth due to government incentive programs around the world and favorable cost savings on monthly utility bills for consumers. Photowatt's solar products are also applied to standalone systems in places where utility-generated power is unavailable or too costly. Off-grid solar power is used in everything from charging boat batteries to supplying electricity to lights or water pumps in rural areas.
