When The Girl from Ipanema goes walking chances are she'll be wearing sandals made by Grendene, one of the world's leading makers of synthetic footwear. The company makes and sells plastic footwear for men, women, and children. It produces more than 145 million pairs of shoes each year, including flipflops and athletic footwear, at about a dozen factories in Brazil. Grendene brands include Melissa, Rider, Ipanema, and Bad Boy Fight. Its shoes are sold primarily in Brazil through distributors and sales partners; foreign subsidiaries Grendha Shoes (US), Saddle Calzados (Argentina), and Saddle Corp. (Uruguay) distribute its products abroad. Another subsidiary, Ilhabela, sells shoes specifically for young women.
