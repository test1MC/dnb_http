For ThyssenKrupp Elevator Americas, business is a series of ups and downs. The company is the North American business unit of one of the world's leading elevator makers, ThyssenKrupp Elevator . ThyssenKrupp Elevator America makes and maintains elevators throughout the US and Canada, as well Central and South America. Its products include escalators, moving sidewalks, freight elevators, Oildraulic hydraulic elevators (for low-rise buildings), traction elevators, and elevator fixtures. The company's more than 200 sales and service offices help serve large construction projects. ThyssenKrupp Elevator Americas' products are installed at the Time Warner Center in New York and the Wynn Las Vegas casino hotel.

The company has operations in the US and Canada, and in Central and South America.

ThyssenKrupp Elevator Americas offers a range of products and services, including elevators, escalators, moving walks and passenger boarding bridges; automotive components and systems; and material trading, logistical, and industrial services.

Selling through sales representatives, the company serves a range of markets including commercial, education, government, healthcare, hospitality, industrial, residential, and transportation.

In fiscal 2013 Elevator Technology contributed about 15% of parent company ThyssenKrupp Elevator 's total revenues. That year the parent company also generated about 21% of its revenues from North and Central America.

Ramping up its product offerings, in 2013 ThyssenKrupp Elevator Americas offered a new line of finish options for elevator cabs and in 2012 it launched a new gearless modernization package that facilitates the upgrading of existing geared elevators.

As part of its growth strategy in the US in 2013 ThyssenKrupp Elevator acquired Cleveland-based Edmonds Elevator, Inc., (elevator maintenance, repair and modernization services)and Indiana based AMCO Elevators (elevator technology).

Building upon its success, in 2009 ThyssenKrupp Elevator was awarded an $88 million contract from The Port Authority of New York and New Jersey to make and install several express elevators for the One World Trade Center (also known as the Freedom Tower and formerly the World Trade Center). With a top speed of 2,000 feet per minute, they take visitors to the restaurant and observatory. Only Taiwan's Taipei 101, the tallest completed building in the world, is believed to have faster elevators.
