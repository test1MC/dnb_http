Appleton Coated manufactures and markets coated free-sheet carbonless paper products under brands names including Altima, Curious, and Utopia. The company's products are used to make such goods as books, magazines, corporate annual reports, and consumer product packaging. Its Curious products include metallic, tactile, and translucent sheets. Appleton Coated primarily serves printers and paper merchants, and produces about 400,000 tons of paper per year. The company was purchased by Virtus Holdings in 2014.

Once owned by France-based paper manufacturer ArjoWiggins , Appleton Coated in 2014 was acquired by Virtus Holdings, a newly formed company established by members of Appleton Coated's management team.

There's no such thing as "just paper" at Appleton Coated. The Altima Gloss & Silk product line is acid-free with a finish that allows for sharp images. Utopia has five paper grades: Premium (metallics), One X: Green (made with recovered fabric), and Utopia One, Two, and Three. Utopia also makes paper for textbooks, trade publications, catalogs, inserts, and technical publications.

The Curious Collection offers more unique products -- some made of paper and fabric and others of printable plastic. Finishes include glittery, iridescent, or translucent, while other sheets are see-through, have a fabric feel, or are bendable and waterproof. C1S Litho is designed for the packaging, label, and book jacket markets. Utopia Digital is used for offset, laser, and color copier applications, while Direct Mail is used for print communications.
