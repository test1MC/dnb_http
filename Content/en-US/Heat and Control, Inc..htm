Couch potatoes are grateful to Heat and Control, a key link in the snack food chain. The company makes equipment systems to process and package baked and fried snacks, meat, poultry, and seafood. Its offerings include single-piece process-machinery as well as multiple components for a complete operating line -- high-capacity spiral ovens and fryers to conveyor systems. Heat and Control's French fry system can turn 50,000 pounds of potatoes into fries in an hour. The company also distributes Ishida's automatic weigh filler, for saving labor and ingredients, and green equipment systems, for reducing air pollution, fuel, and water use. The Caridis family controls the company.

The company has more than 30 locations worldwide, including manufacturing plants in Australia, China, Mexico, South Africa, and the US. It has regional sales offices in Argentina, Brazil, Canada, India, Malaysia, the Netherlands, New Zealand, Scotland, and Singapore.

Heat and Control designs, engineers, and manufactures food processing, seasoning, conveying, inspection, and packaging equipment to prepare and package prepared meats, poultry and seafood, French fries, and baked and fried snacks.

Its Food Processing and Packaging Systems include Process Prepared, Snacks, French Fries, Product Handling, Inspection, and Control and Information. The company also offers raw potato handling, storage, and preparation equipment (including truck and rail car unloading systems). It can also equip an entire packaging room.

Heat and Control serves a range of food markets, including Bakery Food, Candy and Confectioneries, Cereal, Cheese, Coffee and Tea, French Fries, Fruit and Vegetables, Pasta, Tortilla Products, and Snack Food systems. Its customers include Fishery Products, Leo's Foods, MeMeMe, Mister Snacks, and Townsend Culinary.

The manufacturer serves an ever-growing list of industries and geographic regions, and has gained 211 US patents for its products.

Heat and Control was founded in 1950.
