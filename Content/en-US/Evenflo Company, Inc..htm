Those having a baby say hello to Evenflo. Named for a nursing device patented in 1935, the longtime company offers hundreds of items for the well-equipped baby and tot, including car seats, strollers, high chairs, gates, and feeding and safety products under the names Evenflo, ExerSaucer, Gerry, and Snugli, among others. Its website offers parenting resources with safety recalls and child care information. Evenflo has international sales operations in Canada, Mexico, and the Philippines. The firm sells its products through specialty retailers and big-box stores, such as Toys "R" Us, Target and Wal-Mart . Founded in 1920, Evenflo has agreed to be acquired by Goodbaby International Holdings.

The Ohio-based company boasts marketing and sales operations in the US, Canada, Mexico, and the Philippines. The company rings up more than 90% of its sales in North America.

Evenflo, which enjoys several #1 and #2 market share positions across its portfolio, sells its products through retailers, specialty retailers, and food and drug retailers likes of Toys "R" Us , Babies "R" Us , Wal-Mart , Target , Sears Canada , and Target .

The infant and juvenile products marker in 2012 sold its worldwide feeding business to Kimberly-Clark de México . The divestiture did not include Evenflo's Ameda breast pump business, which it later sold later to Platinum Products Holding. Both deals have enabled Evenflo to focus exclusively on its portfolio of leading infant and travel gear brands: Evenflo, ExerSaucer, Snugli, and Gerry. Proceeds from the selloffs are funding its growth and innovation.

To that end, Evenflo entered the stationary jumper category with the launch of ExerSaucer Jump & Learn in 2012.

The company announced in 2012 that all its car and booster seats sold in Canada were in compliance with Transport Canada's booster seat and restraint system regulations. A spate of recalls in its industry had Evenflo concerned about and tightening up its safety stance with a Safety Made Easier section on its website. The move was in response to recalls, such as its Evenflo Maestro Combination Booster Seat in 2010 and in recent years its Evenflo Majestic High Chairs, Evenflo Happy Camper play yard, and the Evenflo Envision high chair.
