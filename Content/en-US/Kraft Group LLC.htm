Family-owned diversified firm Kraft Group operates in six different industries in more than 90 countries, with distribution facilities worldwide. It owns paper and wood trader Interfor , as well as several paper and packaging companies under the Rand-Whitney banner. Its sports holdings include NFL 's New England Patriots , the New England Revolution pro soccer team, and Gillette Stadium. The stadium-adjacent Patriot Place shopping centers and private equity investments coupled with various philanthropic initiatives round out the company's activities.

Kraft Group is based in Foxboro, Massachusetts. It has additional offices in Albany, New York; Gothenburg, Sweden; Hong Kong; Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia; London; Miami; Moscow; Portland, Oregon; Santiago, Chile; Shanghai; Tel Aviv; and Vancouver, Canada.

Kraft Group's business is concentrated within six different industry categories. Its paper and packaging group features the International Forest Products Corporation, in addition to the Rand-Whitney containerboard brands. Operations within the sports and entertainment group include the New England Patriots , New England Revolution, Gillette Stadium, and Patriot Place.

The $350 million Patriot Place opened with a Bass Pro Shop , and has quickly grown to a 1.3-million sq. ft. development with other retail and restaurants, as well as a museum dedicated to the Patriots, a movie theater, an outpatient health care center of Brigham and Women's Hospital , and a Renaissance Hotel by Marriott .

Other industry categories include private equity investments and philanthropic pursuits.

The Kraft Group began with the founding of International Forest Products in 1972. The company bought the Patriots for $172 million in 1994, and the New England Revolution was established a year later. Gillette Stadium, home to both teams, was finished in 2002 for $325 million.
