With Dewhurst in control, you're sure to be able to get a lift. Founded in 1919, Dewhurst makes electrical components and control equipment, mainly for the elevator and rail industries. Products include push buttons, display screens, and speech synthesizers used in elevators; vandal-resistant keypads used in automated banking, ticketing, and vending machines; and body-side door status indicators and driver-operated controls for the rail industry. Dewhurst offers ancillary equipment packages for specific needs. Customers have included ADTRAN , the City of London, and the UK Train Operating Companies.

The company operates in the UK (28% of net sales), Europe (17%), the Americas (28%), and Asia & Australia (26%).

Dewhurst operates across three main segments: Lift (75% of net sales), Keypad (20%), and Transport (5%).

Dewhurst's revenues dipped by 1% from 2014 to 2015 mainly fueled by a 16% decline in UK revenue across all domestic companies. The companies were affected by reduced infrastructure spending in the UK. However, the company strengthened its overseas market which increased by 10% in its export sales. The two primary areas of growth were Canada and the Middle East.

One primary area of growth for Dewhurst includes Canada, where it has been able to broaden the range of products the company sells to its sister company, Dupar Controls. The Middle East is its second area of growth. The company also has projects in the UK, Canada, and Dubai for both new builds and modernization.
