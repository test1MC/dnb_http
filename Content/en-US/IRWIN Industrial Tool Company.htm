If all you have is a hammer, IRWIN Industrial Tool has the right tool for jobs that need more than a nail. Once known as American Tool Companies, IRWIN is a manufacturer and global distributor of professional-grade hand tools and power-tool accessories. Offerings include Hanson taps and dies, Marathon saw blades, Marples fine woodworking tools, Quick-Grip clamping tools, Speedbor flat bits, Strait-Line marking tools, Unibit step drill parts, and Vise-Grip locking tools. IRWIN's offices in the US, Brazil, Europe, and New Zealand serve a range of trades, from automotive to drywaller and electrician, masonry, and woodworker. It is a subsidiary of housewares giant Newell Rubbermaid .

Headquartered in North Carolina, the company boasts sales offices in Australia, Brazil, New Zealand, Switzerland, and the US.

IRWIN makes and distributes professional grade hand tools and power tool accessories. Its brands include IRWIN, Vise-Grip, Marathon, Quick-Grip, Speedbor, Strait-Line, Marples, Unibit, and Hanson.

Thecompany operates under the tools segment of Newell Rubbermaid. The Tools segment designs, manufactures or sources and distributes hand tools and power tool accessories, industrial bandsaw blades, tools and industrial labeling solutions. Hand tools and power tool accessories are primarily sold under the Irwin, Vise-Grip and Marathon trademarks, while industrial bandsaw blades and cutting and drilling accessories are sold under the Lenox trademark. Heating, ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC) tools are sold under the hilmor trademark, and industrial label makers are sold under the Dymo brand.

Customers include the likes of D.P. Welding, DIYer, Hot Shot Handyman Service, GEI, Hubbell Incorporated, and Hodge Heating & Air Conditioning.

Wal-Mart and subsidiaries including Sam's Club accounted for 11% of its parent's revenues in 2014. Its top 10 customers also include include Amazon , Bed Bath & Beyond , Lowe's , Office Depot , Staples , Target , The Home Depot , Toys R Us , and Essendant .

The company operates as part of Newell Rubbermaid's tools segment, which contributed about 15% of its parent's total revenues in fiscal 2014. The Tools segment generated mid-single-digit core sales growth in North America, as strong volume growth from Lenox products was partially offset by the impact of a slower-than-expected transition of an Irwin distribution center in 2014.

IRWIN regularly rolls out new items to extend the reach of its portfolio of products. In 2013, for instance, it launched the Impact Performance Series line of screwdriving bits, nutsetters, socket adapters, and bit holders, as well as a series of folding utility knives using proprietary bladelock technology.

Started in the 1880s with an idea for an auger bit, IRWIN joined Newell Rubbermaid's name-brand toolkit in 2002.
