Stupendous may be too strong a term, but Stupp Bros. has set a high standard as a structural steel fabricator. Founded all the way back in 1856, holding company Stupp Bros. owns Stupp Bridge (fabricator of plate girder bridges), Hammerts Iron Works (maker of structural steel used in commercial, institutional, medical, and specialized buildings), Stupp Corporation (produces electric resistance welded pipe for oil and gas transmission), and Bayou Coating (coating for steel line pipe). The company also owns a majority of community bank Midwest BankCentre . The Stupp family controls Stupp Bros.

Stupp Bros. divides its operations across several divisions. It offers steel fabrication services for steel building structures and steel bridges; general construction services that include building information modeling, construction management, and design/build services; steel construction services; and steel erection services for manufacturing plants, power generation stations, office buildings, warehouse and distribution facilities, stadiums, arenas, shopping malls, and bridges.

Its Fabrication Group consists of Stupp Bridge Company and Hammert's Iron Works; each has an annual fabricating capacity of 50,000 tons. Its Steel Pipe Group consists of Stupp Corporation, a maker of pipe used for oil and gas transmissions, and Stupps Coating, an applicator of steel pipe coating. Stupp Bros.' APCI, LLC unit works with its clients to provide custom services to improve the quality, repeatability and reliability of welds. The company also owns a majority stake in Midwest BankCentre, which has seven branches and $1 billion in deposits.

The provides materials, engineering, and financial services to customers in both the private and public sectors.

Stupp Bros. stated it generated $630 million in net revenue for 2012, a 20% increase from the previous year. It was helped by higher demand for pipe products within the energy market. Both Stupp Bridge and Hammert's Iron Works experienced year-over-year revenue growth for 2012.

In 2013 the company reported continued growth, though not as rapid, with tight margins due to growing competition from Asian, European, and US rivals.

Stupp Bros. uses acquisitions to fortify its welding and fabricating operations.

In 2013 it purchased a majority share in APCI, a company owning a patented welding process based in Indiana. The year before, it bought enough of an interest of Ben Hur Construction Co. to merge it with its Hammert's Iron Works operations.

The company was formed in 1990 as a holding company for the various businesses owned by the Stupp family. The company traces its roots to the South St. Louis Iron Works, which was founded in 1856 by John Stupp to make steel and ornamental iron products.
