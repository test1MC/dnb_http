Without hardware, doors are just pieces of wood; Kwikset makes the devices that turn those pieces into doors. Kwikset manufactures handlesets, levers, knobs, as well as deadbolts and electronic keyless entry locks for light commercial and residential use. Its products are marketed in primarily in Europe and North America under brands Kwikset, Kwikset Signature Series, and SmartSeries (including SmartCode). SmartSeries comprises several trademark electronically controlled access mechanisms. Customers are retailers, wholesalers, distributors, and jobbers; Home Depot and Lowe's are Kwikset's largest. Kwikset is part of Spectrum Brand 's Hardware and Home Improvement unit.

Kwikset sells its products primarily in the US.

The company sells its products through distributors, wholesalers, and online retailers such as Home Depot , Amazon , Ace , and Lowes .

The Hardware & Home Improvement segment's net sales in 2013 was $870 million (or 21% of its parent's net revenues). Sales growth was driven by double-digit improvements in the segment's US residential security and plumbing categories due to the housing market recovery.

Partnerships and product innovation are key the company's growth. In 2013, Kwikset entered into a strategic partnership with UniKey Technologies to offer hands-free access control technology enabled by a smartpone. That year, to support dealers in satisfying growing homeowner demand for sophisticated and flexible wireless security and home automation solutions, Kwikset offers its complete line of SmartCode locks with Home Connect technology for both Z-Wave and ZigBee wireless protocols at ADI branches across North America through a partnership with ADI.

In 2013, in a continuing effort to offer consumers locks that are part of a complete security and home automation system, Kwikset announced a new integration with ipDatatel, an innovator in the telecommunications and security industry. As a result of the arrangement, the Zigbee version of Kwikset's SmartCode deadbolt locks with Home Connect technology integrates with ipDatatel's Gateway product.

In late 2012 Spectrum acquired the Hardware & Home Improvement from Stanley Black & Decker for $1.4 billion. Hardware & Home Improvement is a leader in the North American market for making residential locksets, hardware for homebuilders, and faucets. The deal widened Spectrum's product diversity and solidified its relationships with key retail partners.
