One entryway SARGENT products won't lock is the door to opportunity. SARGENT Manufacturing Company makes security hardware, including cylinders, cylindrical locks, deadbolts, door closers, electro-mechanical access control products, exit devices, hinges, holders and stops, mortise locks, and padlocks. SARGENT's SARGuide exit device includes an illuminated touchpad that aids in the evacuation of dark or smoke-filled passages. The company's customers have included Reliant Stadium in Houston and Cook County Hospital in Chicago. SARGENT is a subsidiary of Sweden-based lockmaker giant Assa Abloy .

The company operates under the ASSA ABLOY Door Security Solutions division of ASSA ABLOY.

The company primarily sells its products through its sales representatives and distributors. It serves health care, electromechanical, construction, and other industrial markets. Customers include Harvard and Castle Rock Adventist Hospital.

The company was founded in 1864 by Joseph Bradford Sargent and his two brothers as a general hardware manufacturing business. In the post-war era SARGENT began to narrow its product offerings to locks and door-related mechanisms. The company was acquired by Swedish lock manufacturer Assa Abloy in 1996.
