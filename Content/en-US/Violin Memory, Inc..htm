Violin Memory plays with more than a dash of flash in orchestrating computer memory. The company designs and sells flash memory arrays and memory cards used in enterprise-level servers that offer more storage than hard disk drives. It pitches its products for use in cloud, Big Data, analytics, and virtualized environments. More than 350 companies use Violin Memory products in markets that include financial services, health care, Internet, government, media and entertainment, and telecommunications. Its products are sold directly in 30 countries and through resellers such as Dell , ePlus , and IBM . Violin doesn't manufacture its storage devices; contract manufacturer Flextronics does the heavy lifting.

Violin Memory's flagship product is the Flash Storage Platform, an integrated design of software, firmware, and hardware. With the platform, active data (Tiers 0, 1, and 2) can be consolidated onto a single tier, which simplifies administration. The Symphony Management Suite Version 3.0 allows software to be orchestrated in just a few clicks of the mouse.

The company draws almost three quarters of its revenue from product sales with the rest coming from its service component.

Violin Memory is headquartered in California and has offices in New York, Washington, D.C., China, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, and the UK. In 2015 (ended January), its US operations accounted for about 60% of sales.

Violin Memory works through its direct sales force and a global channel network to move memory. The company has relationships with systems vendors including Dell, Fujitsu Limited , Microsoft , SAP , Symantec , Toshiba , and VMware . Enterprise Vision Technologies bought 10% of Violin Memory's products in 2015.

The company's channel partners include Arrow , Commtech Innovative Solutions, ePlus , OnX, and Mainline Information Systems and provide sales coverage in more than 30 countries.

After a 46% revenue increase in 2014, Violin Memory's 2015 (ended January) revenue struck a sour note, dropping 27%. The fall to $79 million in revenue reflected the company's switch from its 6000 All Flash Arrays line to the newer Flash Storage Platform. The 6000 Series All Flash Arrays still accounted for 83% of revenue in 2015 although the dollar amount was $24 million lower than in 2014.

In 2015, Violin Memory reordered its product line to focus on it Flash Storage Platform. In doing so, the company cut headcount in sales and marketing and research and development and wrote off a significant amount of the All Flash Arrays line of products. It also sold intellectual property to SK Hynix for about $17 million.

In 2013 the company went public, raising $162 million, which it will use toward sales and marketing efforts, research and development, and working capital.
