Japan's oldest paint company is also its largest. Founded in 1881, Nippon Paint produces paints and coatings for makers of cars, industrial products, and ships. It also makes paints for residential and commercial buildings and for the do-it-yourself market. Nippon Paint's fine chemicals unit produces adhesives, as well as surface treatment chemicals. The company's manufacturing operations are located principally in Asia, but also in North America and Europe. In the US, Nippon Paint makes automotive coatings and powder coatings through Nippon Paint (USA). Growing its US footprint, in 2016 the company agreed to buy California-based Dunn-Edwards Corp.
