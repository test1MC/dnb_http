For Microbot Medical (formerly StemCells), success stems from reinvention. Formerly focused on the discovery of cell-based therapies to treat diseases of the central nervous system (CNS), the company faced a major setback in mid-2016 when its unsuccessful Pathway Study in spinal cord injury was terminated. StemCells' initial response was to begin winding down operations. However, within months the company merged with private firm Microbot. The merged company is now focused on the development of robotic medical devices such as a titanium miniature robot that can clean artificial drainage plants implanted in the body. It is hoped that those devices will eventually be used to clean plaque from blood vessels to prevent heart attack or stroke.
