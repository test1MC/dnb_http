Having learned from the three pigs' example, builders now use many components other than straw and sticks when constructing a building. And MiTek USA makes a lot of those building components. The company manufactures metal connector plates and bracing, as well as machinery for making structural trusses, wall paneling, and related components for the construction industry. Subsidiary Heat Pipe Technology makes energy recovery and dehumidification systems, used primarily in research laboratories, hospitals, and manufacturing facilities. MiTek, a subsidiary of Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway , also provides design, engineering, and job summary software.

Other main subsidiaries include Aegis Metal Framing, a provider of pre-fabricated cold formed steel truss and panel systems, and ComputuTrus, which makes truss connector plates. Hardy Frames makes light gage steel panels and frames, and Hohmann and Barnard provides architects and engineers with masonry anchors, repair and restoration anchors, veneer anchors, and other related anchoring products.

The company owns almost 10 manufacturing, engineering, and stamping plants located in Arizona, California, Florida, Missouri, and North Carolina.

MiTek expands both its line of products and it geographic footprint through acquisitions. It has made at least two acquisitions, often more, since 2010. It occasionally launches new products on its own, usually to complement existing lines.

MiTek has been expanding its product offerings and geographic reach through acquisitions. The company acquired 60-year-old Ellis & Watts Global Industries in 2014.The same year it also bought structural building component maker Truss Industry Production Systems.

In 2013 the company acquired Wisconsin-based Cubic Designs to expand into new end markets, thanks to Cubic's highly customized, prefabricated structural steel products. Other purchases included RKL Building Specialties, Benson Industries, and Kova Solutions; all expanded the company's reach in the residential and commercial construction markets.
