Karyopharm Therapeutics is ready to combat cancer on a chromosomal level. The company is developing an orally administered drug for patients with certain blood and solid tumor cancers. Its lead drug candidate, Selinexor, is being studied to determine its effectiveness in suppressing a cancer-causing protein. In addition to cancer, Karyopharm hopes its compounds have the potential to also combat autoimmune and inflammatory diseases, HIV, and influenza, as well as promote wound healing. The company went public in 2013, raising $108 million, with which it plans to fund further clinical development of Selinexor.
