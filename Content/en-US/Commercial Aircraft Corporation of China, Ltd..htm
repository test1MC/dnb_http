Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China (COMAC) is ready for takeoff. The company, China's first and only civilian aircraft manufacturer, is developing two passenger plane models to meet the country's growing air travel needs. Its single-aisle C919 model will seat 156 first class and economy passengers, while an all-economy class seating model will offer 168 seats. The smaller ARJ21 model (short for Advanced Regional Jet for the 21st century) will offer 78 first class and economy seats or 90 all-economy seats. Both models will use CFM's LEAP-X engines. COMAC was founded in 2008; the first ARJ21 is expected to be ready in 2013 and the C919 is scheduled to launch in 2016.
