All you'll find in this bureau is legal and regulatory information. The Bureau of National Affairs (Bloomberg BNA) publishes advisory and research reports, books, and newsletters for business, government, and academic professionals. BNA has a staff of 2,500 reporters, editors, and legal experts who gather information on topics such as economic, health care, labor, public policy, and tax issues. The firm delivers content online and via print and electronic products, some available through subscription services such as LexisNexis and Thomson Reuter's Westlaw. Bloomberg BNA is owned by Bloomberg .

Bloomberg BNA operates through three business segments: publishing, printing, and software. Publishing, its largest segment, is composed of two units: Legal, Tax, & Regulatory (LTR) and Specialized Business Publishing (SB). LTR accounts for nearly 80% of revenues and includes the company's Tax Management Inc. subsidiary, which offers information for tax planning and compliance. Bloomberg BNA's printing segment includes McArdle Printing, which provides commercial printing services, while its software segment consists of BNA Software, a producer of financial planning and tax software. Among Bloomberg BNA's more than 300 news and information services are the Daily Labor Report, U.S. Law Week, and Daily Report for Executives.

Electronic products account for more than 75% of the company's LTR subscription offerings, and Bloomberg BNA is integrating its content with the latest technology, processes, and applications. The company constantly rolls out new and improved products and features to keep up with the vast amount of data and information its customers need to know about.
