YM BioSciences is a drug development company that focuses on treatments for blood and immune cell disorders. Its main candidate, CYT387, is in development for treating myelofibrosis, a rare blood disorder with limited treatment options. YM BioSciences was founded in 1994 with the backing of Canadian venture capital firm Covington Funds. In early 2013 it was acquired by Gilead Sciences for about $510 million; YM BioSciences was subsequently delisted and is now a wholly owned subsidiary of Gilead Sciences, itself a large HIV/AIDS drug maker. The acquisition adds a complementary clinical research program to treat blood cancers to Gilead Sciences' growing oncology portfolio.
