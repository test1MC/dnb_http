Keeping waterworks working, keeps Romac Industries in business. The company manufactures all manner of fluid system products. A short list includes repair clamps, basic couplings, service saddles, sewer products, dismantling joints, high density polyethylene (HDPE) pipe, and restraint systems. Parts for the waterworks lineup are cast from high-grade ductile iron at Romac's US foundry. A fabricated steel division produces a myriad of standard size parts and accessories, offered through its catalog, as well customized offerings for non-traditional applications. Spanning North America, the company caters to water and wastewater industries, as well as power generation, pulp and paper, mining, and industrial plants.

Romac is stationed in Bothell, Washington, and has additional locations in Charlotte, North Carolina; Fort Worth, Texas; and Sultan, Washington. It sells its products throughout Canada, Puerto Rico, Mexico, and the US.

Founded in 1969, Romac Industries set out to make and market cast iron and fabricated steel products for the waterworks industry. The company has grown with such successful introductions as the industry's first all-stainless steel repair clamp.
