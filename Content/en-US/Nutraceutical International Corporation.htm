Nutraceutical International manufactures, markets, and even retails a world of nutritional supplements, vitamins, minerals, body care products, and diet and energy products in the US and abroad. Its branded products include such names as KAL, Herbs for Kids, Nature's Life, Solaray, Sunny Green, and VegLife. Its company-owned US retail stores include Cornucopia Community Market, Granola's, and The Real Food Company, while its Au Naturel unit markets the company's brands in about 70 other countries. Nutraceutical International also publishes natural health books under the Woodland name. The acquisitive company is actively buying up and integrating smaller firms.

The vertically integrated company is engaged in the manufacturing, distribution, and sale (through its own stores and those operated by others) of its branded nutritional supplements. It manufactures about 80% of its branded products. Third-party manufacturers supply the balance, including supplements, herbs, and body care products.

The company also distributes some products for other manufacturers. Its Monarch Nutraceuticals subsidiary manufactures bulk ingredients used in its own products and sold to other manufacturers.

Retailing is only one corner of its sales; more comes from selling its products in other retail stores including independent health and natural food stores; health and natural food stores affiliated with local, regional, and national chains (such as Whole Foods Markets ); and vitamin store chains such as the Vitamin Shoppe and GNC stores.

Nutraceutical International rings up nearly 90% of its sales in the US. Its 70 foreign markets include the UK, the Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, and Japan.

The company has manufacturing facilities in Phoenix; Sebastopol, California; Tampa and Bowling Green, Florida; Brooklyn, New York; Tulsa, Oklahoma; and Ogden, Utah.

Domestically, Nutraceutical International sells primarily to health and natural food stores. Its international unit, Au Naturel, distributes its branded nutritional supplements and other products to health and natural food distributors and retailers in about 70 countries.

The company sells its products via telephone customer service to retailers and distributors. Subsidiary Monarch Nutraceuticals provides sales and marketing for bulk materials through a separate sales force in the US; it sells directly to manufacturers and distributors abroad.

Marketing efforts are focused on product development, in-store marketing support, and the education of retailers on its products.

Nutraceutical International has posted several years of revenue increases and fiscal 2016 (ended September) was no exception. The 8% increase, to $233 million, was driven by branded product sales growth, both abroad and in the US. Net income also grew, rising 22% to $18.7 million, due to the higher revenue.

Formed with the goal of consolidating a fragmented industry, Nutraceutical International has steadily bulked up its product lines and manufacturing and distribution capacity through more than 50 acquisitions since 1993. In fiscal 2016 (ended September), it acquired two businesses -- certain operating assets of Dynamic Health Laboratories and Aubrey Organics (for a total of $26.2 million in cash).

The company is also focused on introducing new products and packages to its growing consumer base. During fiscal 2016 it introduced some 200 new stock keeping units (SKUs), bringing its total to some 17,500 individual SKUs.
