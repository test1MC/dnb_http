General Dynamics Broadband (formerly IPWireless) makes broadband wireless infrastructure and network access equipment. The company's base stations, network controllers, and PC modems use UMTS, an international mobile broadband standard. It sells to wireless operators and broadband network service providers, as well as government agencies and public safety organizations. The company, with offices in the UK and the US, was founded in 1999 by CEO William Jones and CTO Roger Quayle. In 2012 General Dynamics acquired IPWireless and changed the company's name to General Dynamics Broadband. The company became a subsidiary of General Dynamics C4 Systems .
