Cybex International won't get you to cyberspace, but it can help you get a second glance at the grocery store. The company makes premium-priced strength-building gear and cardio fitness equipment that is sold to commercial markets (including health clubs, hotels, and schools) and to consumers through independent retailers. Cybex's portfolio of products includes stationary bikes, treadmills, free weights, and single- and multi-station strength-training equipment. The company markets its products through its own sales team, e-commerce site, and about 55 independent dealers worldwide. Cybex was acquired by boat maker Brunswick Corporation in 2016.

Massachusetts-based Cybex sells its products in about 90 countries.

Cybex counts commercial customers, such as health clubs, hotels, and schools, and consumers as its primary customers. It sells its products to individuals through independent retailers.

With roots reaching back to the 1960's, Cybex has built a name for itself supplying the commercial fitness industry with its products. Indeed, its Cybex Research Institute in 2014 partnered with Atlantic Health System, one of New Jersey's largest non-profit health care organizations, as an official research and education partner. The pair is collaborating on outcome-driven research and educational programs.

The company has been pumping out new products. In 2014 it launched CybexGO, a platform that provides users with personalized entertainment, such as on-demand options the likes of TV shows, movie trailers, music videos, sports, and video game trailers. It has also rolled out new treadmills that list from nearly $5,000 to $8,000. Cybex has also introduced a multigym station, which makes use of limited spaces, and it began selling Big Iron free weights, which are geared to professional and college-level athletes. Cybex's product launches are a result of its research and development efforts. The company dedicates a portion of its revenue to R&D to improve existing products and to look for innovative and competitive products to develop. To this end, Cybex in 2014 inked a deal to buy Columbus Holding GmbH, which specializes in making and marketing juvenile products under the Cybex and CBX brands, in a deal valued at about $89.7 million.

As North America works to make a full recovery, the company is boosting revenues from international customers in places like Brazil, China, India, and Germany. Sales outside North America have grown to represent a larger portion of Cybex's operations.
