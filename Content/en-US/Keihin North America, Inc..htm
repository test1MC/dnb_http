Keihin North America (KNA) is a manufacturer of automotive systems such as air and fuel management, HVAC, and electronic components. These parts are used for engine control and supplemental restraint systems control (for airbag deployment), and its ABS units are used to make braking systems. KNA also makes mechanical parts such as fuel injectors, throttle bodies, and intake manifolds. It is the parent company of several business units: Keihin IPT Manufacturing, Keihin Aircon North America, Keihin Carolina System Technology, Keihin Aircon Thermal Technology of America, and Keihin Michigan Manufacturing. Keihin began US manufacturing in 1989.

KNA operates four manufacturing facilities in the US located in Greenfield and Muncie, Indiana; Dublin, Ohio; Tarboro, North Carolina; and Capac, Michigan. It also has operations in Mexico.

Keihin IPT Manufacturing makes and sells fuel injection devices and is located at the KNA headquarters; Keihin Aircon North America (Muncie, Indiana) produces and sells automotive HVAC units, primarily for Honda; Keihin Carolina System Technology (Tarboro, North Carolina) constructs and markets engine analyzers (EA) and sensors (ECU); and Keihin Michigan Manufacturing (Capac, Michigan) manufactures carburetors for motorcycles and ATVs. KNA also makes industrial valves, electronically controlled fuel injection for watercraft and marine engines, and components for generators, cultivators, lawn mowers, and other agricultural and industrial engines.

KNA sells its products through its network of distributors. It supplies fuel systems for major customers like Honda , Kawasaki, Yamaha .
