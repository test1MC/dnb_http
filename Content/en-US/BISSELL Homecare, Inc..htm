BISSELL's in the business of seeing spots and taking care of dust bunnies. A pioneer in the carpet-cleaning industry, BISSELL Homecare makes a full line of vacuum cleaners, sweepers, steam cleaners, deep cleaners, and cleaning chemicals for home use. Its models include the Powersteamer, Big Green, and Spotlifter machines. The company sells its products worldwide under the BISSELL and Woolite brand names through mass merchandisers ( Best Buy , Target , Wal-Mart ), home centers ( Lowe's , Home Depot ), hardware stores ( Ace Hardware ), and through BISSELL.com. It entered the deep-cleaning rental business in 2010. Founded in 1876 by Melville and Anna Bissell, the company is still owned and operated by the Bissell family.

Based in Michigan, BISSELL boasts manufacturing facilities in its home state, as well as in Mexico and China. The company enlists the help of third party contractors in China and South Korea to supplement its manufacturing capabilities.

Globally, BISSELL maintains a presence in North America, Europe, Asia, the Middle East, and Africa.

The floor-care company relies on multiple channels to sell its products. It peddles its products through mass merchandisers, home centers, hardware stores, and BISSELL.com. European distributes include such companies as Mesa Products International BV and Rafianz/Orbico CZ s.r.o.

As part of its marketing efforts and to support its corporate culture, the company sponsors the BISSELL Pro Cycling Team for cycling's international appeal and because the company supports a health conscious lifestyle that includes exercise. The team typically trains in West Michigan.

Having earned a 20% market share, BISSELL Homecare has become one of the top floor-care companies in North America. The floor-care firm has achieved this by concentrating on innovation and by boosting its factory staff with engineers, market researchers, data analysts, and industrial designers. The company's practice of hiring new college graduates alongside advanced-degree professionals has allowed the appliance maker to arm itself with fresh ideas and lessons learned. As a result, it has pulled ahead of rival brands the likes of Hoover , Eureka , and Dirt Devil . BISSELL Homecare is chasing after market share established by longtime appliance provider Rug Doctor in the carpet-cleaning rental business with its Big Green Deep Cleaning Machine. To stay competitive, BISSELL in 2013 shed 5% of its salaried workforce through layoffs.

BISSELL runs a Bissell Innovation Center, which focuses on providing value-added components, which are the more glamorous parts of the appliances business.

BISSELL works to stay at the forefront of the home appliances market by rolling out new products and making add-on acquisitions. Launched in 2014, the BISSELL Symphony All-in-One Vacuum and Steam Mop is the company's latest invention to debut on Home Shopping Network. It also launched its Healthy Home Vacuum and ProHeat 2X Healthy Home deep cleaner. BISSELL Homecare touts the vacuum line as locking in 100% of captured allergens and the deep cleaner as removing two times more embedded allergens than ordinary vacuuming alone. BISSELL Homecare's acquisition of the Woolite rug and upholstery cleaning unit of Playtex Products has added another big brand name to its products portfolio. The deal, inked for $62 million, allowed the company to expand its cleaning solutions products, as well.

The company is one of the oldest family-run businesses in the US. Co-founder Anna Bissell became the first female corporate CEO in 1889 following the untimely death of her husband Melville Bissell.
