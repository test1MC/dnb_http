Austin Powder is very much in favor of a booming economy, both in the US and overseas. Austin Powder's commercial explosives are used primarily for construction, mining, quarrying, and seismic exploration. Products include detonating cord, detonator-sensitive emulsions, cast boosters, and high-explosive emulsions. Subsidiary Austin Star Detonator makes a full line of electric and non-electric (shock tube) detonators. It other unit, Austin International, through joint ventures and partnerships, provides explosives for projects outside the US, mostly in other parts of the Americas.

A global supplier, Austin Powder's main plants are in McArthur, Ohio; Camden, Arkansas; Brownsville, Texas; and Valle Hermosa, Mexico.

The company has main two subsidiaries, Austin International and Austin Star Detonator.

Austin International, founded in 1985, distributes Austin Powder products to a global clientele. It has been involved in projects such as widening the Panama Canal, building roads and tunnels in Mexico, copper mines in Chile, Poland and Bulgaria, and building tunnels in Austria. Austin Star Detonator, founded in 1988, manufactures electric and non-electric detonators primarily for the North and South American markets. The subsidiary acquired Indet, an explosives supplier in the Czech Republic in 1999, and renamed it Austin Detonator. That unit distributes Austin Star Detonator products and services across Europe and to other global locations.

Austin Powder's company includes the construction, quarrying, surface mining, and seismic survey industries. The company distributes products in the US and Canada primarily through 65 company-owned stores.

The company was founded in 1833 to manufacture black powder used to blast rocks, mine coal, and create canals.. Austin Powder is the second oldest manufacturing company in Ohio.
