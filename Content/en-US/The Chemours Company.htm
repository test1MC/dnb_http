The Chemours Company, a global chemical company with leading market positions in titanium technologies, fluoroproducts, and chemical products (sulfuric acid and sodium cyanide), was spun-off from former parent DuPont in 2015 as an independent, publicly traded corporation. Chemours' ingredients are found in plastics and coatings, refrigeration and air conditioning, mining and oil refining operations, and in general industrial manufacturing. The company's flagship products include prominent brands such as Teflon, Ti-Pure, Krytox Viton, Opteon, and Nafion.

Chemours became an independent publicly traded company in July 2015.

The company operates 37 manufacturing sites in 12 countries serving more than 5,000 customers in more than 130 countries in North America, Latin America, Asia-Pacific, and Europe.

Chemours is the world's largest global producer of titanium dioxide (a highly reflective mineral is used in many applications, from paint and coatings to sunscreen, pharmaceuticals, and food coloring) with an annual capacity of 1.2 million metric tons.

The company continues to capitalize on its global operations network, distribution infrastructure, and technology to pursue global growth. It focuses on geographic areas and end products it believes offer the most attractive growth and long-term profitability prospects.

In this regard, Chemours is expanding capacity at its Altamira, Mexico facility by 200,000 metric tons. Increased production at the expansion plant is scheduled to start up in mid-2016.
