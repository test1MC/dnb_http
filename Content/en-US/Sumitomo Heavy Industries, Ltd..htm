Like a Sumo wrestler, Sumitomo Heavy Industries (SHI) welds great weight in competitive circles. The company manufactures machinery for five business areas: mass-produced machinery; environmental control and energy; steel structure, bridge and shipbuilding; industrial and construction machinery. Its equipment includes power transmission, cryogenic tools, waste treatment facilities, pressure vessels, material handling, and mobile cranes. SHI operates through more than 190 companies in Asia, Europe, and the US, serving automotive to chemical, construction, electronics, medical, and utility markets. Japan is its largest market.

The company has operations in Japan, the US, Asia, China, and Europe. SHI's largest market is Japan, representing 48% of net sales. Other major markets include the US (15%) and China (10%).

SHI operates through several segments, including construction (31% of net sales), precision machinery (22%), machinery component (6%), environmental facilities and plants (14%), industrial machinery (13%), ships (2%), and other (2%).

SHI's net sales in 2014 climbed ¥29.4 billion, to ¥615.3 billion. With the exceptions of its industrial machinery as well as ships segments, the growth was fueled by all its business segments during the year. The company has a goal to generate ¥700 billion in net sales by 2016.

Going forward, SHI plans to grow globally by generating additional revenue from its product lines of power transmission and control equipment and plastic injection molding machines. It also plans to expand its lineup of energy-saving products by launching products and models that comply with premium efficiency motor regulations that come into effect in Japan during 2015. In addition, SHI plans to create local companies for its sales network in the fast-growing Southeast Asian region.
