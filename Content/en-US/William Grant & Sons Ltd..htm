You can take it for granted that William Grant & Sons is a top maker of top-shelf Scotch. The Scottish distiller produces single-malt Scotch whiskeys under brands such as Grant's, Glenfiddich, and Balvenie. William Grant & Sons was founded in 1887 by William Grant; today it is owned and overseen by the Gordon family, a fifth generation of Grant's heirs. Glenfiddich Scotch is one of the world's top-selling single-malt and most expensive whiskys. The company also owns several other liquor brands including Sailor Jerry Spiced Navy Rum, Milagro Tequila, and Hendrick's Gin.

William Grant & Sons-branded beverages are sold in more than 180 markets around the world. The company is focusing it expansion on Asia, where it has locations in Hong Kong, Shanghai, and Taipei.

One way William Grant & Sons grows is by acquiring other brands and companies. In 2014 it acquired Drambuie Liqueur Company Limited, the owner of the Drambuie whiskey liqueur brand. Drambuie is a blend of aged Scotch whiskey, spices and heather honey which boasts a history going back to 1745. The deal enhanced the company's portfolio of premium consumer led brands.
