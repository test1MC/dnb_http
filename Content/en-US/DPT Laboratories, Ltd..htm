DPT Laboratories helps to turn discovered drugs into developed and commercialized drugs. The contract manufacturer and developer makes prescription and over-the-counter (OTC) drugs, with an emphasis on semi-solid, liquid, and sterile formulations. Its products include aerosol foams, nasal sprays, eye drops, and creams, as well as metered inhalers, dosage vials, and prefilled syringes. The firm works with conventional and biological compounds and can manufacture small quantities for clinical trials or large batches for commercial production. It also provides development-stage analysis and testing services. DPT is owned by Renaissance Acquisition Holdings, which is being acquired by Mylan for $1 billion.

The company's sterile and specialty products business (based in New Jersey) provides development and aseptic production of products that meet sterile manufacturing standards, such as ophthalmic preparations, nasal sprays, paraenterals, and sterile ointments. Meanwhile the semi-solids and liquids division (based in Texas) makes products including lotions, gels, and foams. DPT employs more than 100 engineers and scientists and produces about 80 product lines in about 1,000 dosage formulations.

DPT has five facilities that handle research, manufacturing, and packaging operations in New Jersey (Lakewood) and Texas (Brooks City Base and San Antonio).

DPT's customers have included small to large drugmakers ranging from Pfizer to Imprimis Pharmaceuticals. The company makes small batches of products used in clinical trials, as well as large-scale commercialization batches.

The company has been using its recent private equity backing to accelerate its long-term strategic plans to maximize its semi-solid and liquid business in San Antonio, Texas, and grow its Lakewood, New Jersey-based sterile and specialty products business. In 2014 the company acquired Meda Pharmaceuticals ' Lakewood, New Jersey facilities, adding two more buildings in the area.

The company is also conducting expansion and enhancement projects to improve its manufacturing and R&D facilities. It has made the upgrades to keep up with continued demand and to upgrade its compounding and packaging facilities, as well as to create new infrastructure to handle larger deliveries and support increased production.

In 2013 DPT integrated operations with Quebec-based Confab Laboratories. Confab, which provides complementary contract manufacturing services, including development of complex solid dosage forms, rebranded as a DPT company.

Parent company Renaissance Acquisition agreed to be acquired by Mylan in 2016; the deal includes DPT Laboratories. Mylan expects to retain the firm's sterile-focused operations.

DPT was formed in 1938 as Texas Pharmacal Company and is known as the first US-based dermatology company. The company was renamed Dermatological Products of Texas in 1980. Then, in 1990, DFB Pharmaceuticals acquired the firm and renamed it as DPT Laboratories. The company's focus was then expanded into drug development and manufacturing services. Renaissance Acquisition Holdings, a portfolio company of RoundTable Healthcare partners, acquired DPT in 2012.
