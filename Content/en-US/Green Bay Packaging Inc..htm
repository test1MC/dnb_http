Green Bay Packaging is the other Green Bay packers ' enterprise. The diversified yet integrated paperboard packaging manufacturer operates through 30 locations. In addition to corrugated containers, the company makes pressure-sensitive label stock, folding cartons, recycled container board, white and kraft linerboards, and lumber products. Its Fiber Resources division in Arkansas manages more than 210,000 acres of company-owned timber land and produces lumber, woodchips, recycled paper, and wood fuel. Green Bay Packaging also offers fiber procurement, wastepaper brokerage, and paper-slitting services.

The company operates 30 locations in 15 US states. It has distribution centers in Green Bay; Kansas City; Los Angeles; Minneapolis; and Winchester, Virginia. It also has distribution channels throughout Canada and Mexico.

Green Bay serves automotive, beverage, direct mail, food, industrial, medical, textile, and health and beauty industries.

Green Bay in 2014 acquired Great Lakes Packaging Corporation and Midland Container Corporation from MidCon Holdings. The deal strengthened its point of purchase printing and display capabilities, broadened its reach into new markets, and provided the potential to expand its presence with current customers.

Founded during the Great Depression in 1933 as Green Bay Box, the company is also notable for its entrepreneurial spirit. The company continues to stand as one of the 10 largest closely held companies in Wisconsin, and as one Arkansas' top 10 forest products companies (by number of employees).

Green Bay Packaging initially took root as a packaging alternative to the boxes used by Wisconsin cheese makers and other producers. Starting with corrugated shipping containers, the company has evolved through acquisition and expansion to manufacture a breadth of paper packaging options for both large and small companies, from Xerox Corp. to Field's Pies in Pauls Valley, Oklahoma.
