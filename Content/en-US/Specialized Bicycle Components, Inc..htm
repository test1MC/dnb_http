Specialized Bicycle Components didn't set out to make a museum piece, but its original StumpJumper (the first mass-produced mountain bike) is now permanently parked inside the Smithsonian Institution . Its 1981 debut established Specialized as a top-seller in bike shops alongside products from Trek and Giant for high-end cycles. Founded in 1974, the firm also makes road bikes, hybrid bikes, BMX bikes, and children's bikes. Specialized offers branded apparel and shoes, bags, eyewear, helmets, and bike parts and accessories (such as crank sets, saddles, and tires). It peddles merchandise through its website. Cycling enthusiast and founder Mike Sinyard owns a majority stake with Merida holding a minority share.

California-based Specialized enjoys a global reach with offices located worldwide. It boasts an extensive global distribution network of 5,000 retailers across 35 countries in Asia, North America, South America, and Australia.

The company designs and manufactures bicycles, components, and cycling apparel for riders and cycling fans. It covers a variety of categories, such as mountain, road, transport/fitness, path/gravel, dirt/park/street, kids, and equipment.

Specialized gets its products in the hands of consumers through a vast network of retailers and dealers worldwide. It works to maintain its status as the technological leader in the bike and bike accessories markets.
