Warning! Warning! There's no danger for Lost in Space ' s Will Robinson as long as FANUC America makes intelligent robots that automate industrial manufacturing. A subsidiary of Japan-based robotics giant FANUC LTD , the company's robotic devices automate assembly, cleaning, dispensing, grinding, packaging, painting, polishing, and welding processes. FANUC's robots supply equipment to industries as diverse as aerospace, agriculture, food and beverage, medical devices, and textile industries, to name a few. It also provides engineering, service support, analysis, and system maintenance. Brand names include its ROBODRILL machining centers and CNC Systems factory automation products.

FANUC America operates about 20 facilities in Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Mexico, and the US. It has facilities in Atlanta; Boston; Charlotte; Chicago; Cincinnati; Cleveland; Dallas; Indianapolis; Los Angeles; Minneapolis; Montreal; Pine Brook, NJ; San Francisco; Toronto; Buenos Aires, Argentina; Sao Paulo, Brazil; and Aguascalientes, Mexico City and Monterrey, Mexico.

The company installed more than 3 million computer numerical control (CNC) systems. FANUC America is the exclusive provider of industry leading FANUC CNC systems in the Americas, providing industry-leading control systems, a complete range of drives and motors, and CO2 laser equipment.

Its robotic product line also includes simulation packages, application software, controls, and integrated iRVision products.

The company serves a wide range of industries including aerospace, automotive, consumer goods, education, food, metal fabrication, medical, pharmaceutical, solar panel, and wood.

FANUC America generated 22% of its parent's revenues in 2013.

For greater operating efficiency, in 2013 FANUC merged all of its operations in the Americas into a single company named FANUC America Corporation.

In 2013 Absolute Machine Tools, Inc., (Lorain, Ohio) a distributor of a wide selection of CNC machine tools and related products, achieved Authorized Integrator status with FANUC Robotics, enabling Absolute to enhance its systems with robots that handle payloads from 0.5kg to 1,350kg, as well as simulation packages, application software, force sensing, and infrared vision systems.

That year the company also partnered with RCBI to offer the FANUC Robotics Certified Education Robot Training program.

In 2013 FANUC America unveiled a CNC and robot-integrated automotive architecture demonstrating connectivity to Rockwell Automation programmable automation controller environments.

The company began as GMFanuc Robotics Corporation, a 50/50 joint venture established in 1982 with General Motors. It became a 100% subsidiary of FANUC LTD in 1992.
