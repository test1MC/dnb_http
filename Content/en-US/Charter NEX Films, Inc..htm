Charter NEX Films is a manufacturer of various performance films that are used in a broad range of applications, including packaging, printing, laminating, labels, banners, and surface protection. Among the raw materials used in its films are nylon, polyethylene, and polypropylene. The company has a handful of manufacturing facilities in Massachusetts and Wisconsin. Charter NEX Films formed in early 2013 through the combination of two similar Wisconsin-based film extruders, Charter Films and NEX Performance Films. Charter NEX Films is a portfolio company of private equity firm Mason Wells .

Headquartered in Milton, Wisconsin, the company operates three plants (Milton, Rhinelander, and Superior) in Wisconsin, and one in Massachusetts (Turners Falls Plant).

Charter NEX blown film products are used in industries such as agriculture, automotive, consumer goods, food and beverage, graphics, lawn and garden, medical, packaging, veterinary, and wood products.

NEX Performance Films acquired Charter Films in January 2013 for an undisclosed amount. Both serve converters, who use films to produce flexible packaging for a variety of markets. NEX Performance Films brings expertise in monolayer, coextruded, and barrier films for flexible packaging, while Charter Films specializes in blown film extrusion for the agriculture, automotive, consumer goods, and food and beverage markets. Combined, the company plans to invest in additional extrusion capacity to meet growing demand from converters.

Along these lines, in 2013 purchased two new Windmoeller & Hoelscher 3-layer blown film lines to install in its Milton, Wisconsin, facility. The addition boosted the company's total lines up to 30.
