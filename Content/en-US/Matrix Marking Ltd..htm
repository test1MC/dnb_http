Mark my words, Matrix Marking specializes in electronic systems designed for tagging and tracking surgical equipment and other small components. The company offers a number of techniques which are offered to hospitals to mark their surgical instruments. The company's Identimark reader uses a camera to identify a pattern of small dots that has been imprinted onto a metal surface using a method called dot-peening. The code can contain such information as the name of the hospital where an instrument was originally used or what procedure the instrument was last used to perform. Matrix Marking's system is also used in the automotive and aerospace industries to label components using a durable marking method.

Although the company is headquartered in, and does its the majority of its business there, the company does serve clients in the both the UK and the US.

The processes and equipment used by the company is well-suited to identifying and tracking surgical instruments - it can be done quickly, is long lasting, and does not damage the instruments. The company's processes and products include dot peen marking, laser marking, laser bonding, chemical etching, and marked labels.
