The nuts and bolts of MacLean-Fogg's business are nuts, bolts, and other engineered parts. The family-owned company provides engineered metal and plastic components to the industrial, automotive, military, and aerospace markets, and unique devices used in the transmission and distribution of electrical power. Its MacLean Component Solutions unit offers an array of fasteners and components used by auto, railroad, agricultural, and construction industries. The company's operations include extruding, forging, grinding, and machining. MacLean Power Systems sells silicone rubber insulators, automatic connectors, and other hardware to electric utility and communications customers worldwide.

MacLean-Fogg Component Solutions is a leading supplier of fastener components, engineered components, and linkage and suspension components serving a broad range of industries. The company's brands include Whiz-Lock, ESNA Lock Nuts, Securex and Axilok, Step-Lok, Foresight Civil Anchors, and MPS Civil Products.

MacLean Power Systems is a leading manufacturer of products used by utilities for building transmission and distribution lines and substations. The unit produces more than 10,000 items at seven US production facilities.

MacLean-Fogg's hundreds of patented inventions range from products to processes for making products, such as U-bold assembly, leakdown plungers, and suspension link assemblies for vehicle suspension systems. Others manufacture bolts, studs, and fuse cutouts for electrical power distribution systems.

The company also operates MacLean Investment Partners, which includes investments and private equity holdings outside of the two primary businesses. Primarily focused on transportation related products, the business includes firms like Tramec LLC, Reynolds Cycling, Janco Composites, and Boyt Harness.

The company has 35 manufacturing facilities around the world including operations in China, Malaysia, Thailand, Australia, France, Brazil, Canada, and Germany. About 75% of the company's operations are outside North America.

MacLean-Fogg continues to evolve through building and acquiring product lines that supply the vehicle, industrial, and utility markets around the world. The company invested in new machining equipment which expanded the manufacturing capabilities of its Fastener Components division. The investment allows the company to expand their product lines in valves, sleeves, fittings, pintles, shafts, small gears and fasteners.

In 2014, MacLean-Fogg acquired the automotive division of of Curtis Screw Company. As a part of Component Solutions division, the unit will operate as a subdivision of the company, named MacLean Curtis. The acquisition, which includes manufacturing operations in New York and North Carolina, expands the company's business and product range in precision-machined components. In 2013, the company acquired the assets of Utility Marketing Corporation and U.S. Utility Enclosures, collectively doing business as Highline Products.

In 1925 the company was launched to make just one product: a lock nut for the railroad industry.
