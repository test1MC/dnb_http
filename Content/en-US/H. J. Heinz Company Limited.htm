H. J. Heinz Manufacturing UK Limited is full of beans, canned beans. The UK subsidiary of food behemoth Heinz offers some of the same products sold in the US, plus several specifically aimed to please the sovereign state's tastes. After all, consumers in the UK and Ireland have been known to buy a whopping 1.5 million cans of beans a day! In addition to Heinz Beanz, the company's mainstay products (tomato ketchup, spaghetti in tomato sauce, cream of tomato soup, and salad cream) are sold under brand names Weight Watchers (used under license), 57 Sauces, Heinz Big, Daddies, and others. H. J. Heinz Manufacturing UK operates through almost 10 facilities in the UK.

In 2006, parent Heinz sold its frozen meat-free business, including the Linda McCartney brand of frozen vegetarian meals, to The Hain Celestial Group in order to focus on its three core European product areas: ketchup, condiments, and sauces; convenience meals; and infant nutrition. It also sold off its European seafood businesses, which included John West Foods (UK), Petit Navaire (France), and Mareblu (Italy).
