Where else would you expect to find a company doing precision engine component machining work. Michigan-based Engineered Machined Products (EMP) designs, manufactures, and assembles mechanical and electronically controlled heating and lubrication management products and systems. Its lineup includes oil management systems, engine heating and cooling systems, hydraulic motor housings, mechanical oil and water pumps, electric fans, and electronic thermostats. EMP's custom components are produced at its facilities in Michigan and Indiana. Its products are used in hybrid/specialty and transit buses, trucks, fire engines, as well as in industrial machinery.

EMP has manufacturing and assembly facilities in North America. Its corporate headquarters and product launch center is located in Escanaba, Michigan. EMP also has a facility located in Greenfield, Indiana, and has 17 distributors with over 100 locations across the US.

EMP manufactures products primarily for the diesel engine and automotive vehicle markets. Its two primary product lines include: Advanced Thermal & Oil Management Components & Systems, and Conventional Precision Machined & Assembled Products.

Advanced Thermal & Oil Management Components & Systems makes electric water pumps, electric oil and fuel pumps, electric fans, air pumps, thermal management modules, and other related products. Conventional products include water pumps, oil pumps, hydraulic pumps, oil rails, cylinder heads, pistons, rocker arm carriers, intake manifolds and a variety of other machined and assembled components.

EMP caters to the on-highway, off-highway, hybrid specialty, and transit bus markets. It sells its products through distributors like Australis Marine & Industrial, AVID Technology , Cummins Eastern Canada , and EUROPUMP SYSTEMS. Some of its customers include Pulaski Public School District, TRIMET, Royal City Public School District, Walter Welch Logging, Capital Area Transit Authority, and B.J. Forrest Trucking.

To grow, EMP has invested in a number of facility expansions, manufacturing advancements, including robotics automation, and business acquisitions, to support its evolving customer base. Its mini-Hybrid Thermal conversion kits are gaining a following in civil sectors. Among the first, three Michigan cities have used federal stimulus money to retro-fit some of their diesel buses with the conversion kits. The kits, considerably less expensive than replacing buses with electric hybrids, improve fuel efficiency, and reduce emissions and noise to levels comparable to green models.
