EMCORE has gone full fiber. The company makes optical computer chips and systems for fiber optic cable and broadband systems. Its products are components in cable TV, fiber-to-the-premise (FTTP) networks, telecommunications and data centers, satellite communications, aerospace and defense, wireless networks, and broadcast audio/video systems. EMCORE sold its photovoltaic business in 2014 to focus on fiber. The company makes about two-thirds of its revenue from companies based in the US.

EMCORE is based in Alhambra, California, where it also has a manufacturing plant. It has other manufacturing operations in Ivyland, Pennsylvania and Langfang, China. With the US accounting for 68% of the company's revenue, it generates 21% from customers in Asia and 10% from customers in Europe.

EMCORE offers its products through direct sales force, third party representatives, distributors, and application engineers. The company relies on its distributor partners to expand coverage in international and some domestic markets. It endeavors to establish long-term relationships with customers to develop products tailored to customers' needs.

It counts as customers Arris Technology Inc. , BAE Systems , BUPT-GuoAn Broadband, Cisco Systems Inc. , and Pace Plc .

EMCORE compared its 2015 (ended September) financials to the 2014 results that did not include the photovoltaics business, which was sold ni 2014.

By that reckoning, the company posted 2015 revenue of $81.6 million, up $26 million (47%) from 2014 increase. The company sold more of its TV products and chip-level devices to US customers. The company also expanded sales (up 95% on 2015 from 2014) to customers in Asia.

EMCORE posted a profit of $63 million in 2015, a marked rise from$4.8 million in 2014. Increased sales contriobuted to the rise as did and income from discontinued operations ($61 million from the photovoltaics business).

Using the full 2014 results (which included the photovoltaics operation), EMCORE's revenue fell to $81 million from $174 million in 2014. The profit picture, however, remained the same as the company reported.

The company had a negative cash flow of about $4 million in 2015, compared to a positive $1 million cash flow in 2014.

The company has generally tried to improve liquidity by reducing costs and selling assets, among other measures.

In 2014 it agreed to sell its Space Photovoltaics Business to Veritas Capital for $150 million as the first major action resulting from EMCORE's review of strategic alternatives for the financially stressed company. The company also sold its digital products unit for about $17 million.
