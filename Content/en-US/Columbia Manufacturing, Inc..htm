Cyclists have been sharing the road with Columbia Manufacturing for more than a century. The company makes a range of bicycles for men, women, and children. Models include the Comfort and Classic cruisers; the Eagle and Sienna mountain bikes; and the Intruder boys' freestyle bike. In addition to two-wheelers, Columbia Manufacturing produces metal and plastic school furniture in a variety of colors, including desks, tables, stools, and chairs. Products are made at its manufacturing center in Massachusetts and distributed throughout the US and in Puerto Rico. Originally named Pope Manufacturing, the company was founded in 1877 by Albert Pope and is known for producing some of the first bicycles in America.
