You could call it easy going, because Cerro goes with the flow. Cerro Flow Products supplies copper tubing and other flow products to the plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, and refrigeration industries. Its products include plumbing and refrigeration tubing; level wound coil for use in boilers, chillers, and space heaters; and grooved tube used in evaporators and condensers. The company has a half-dozen manufacturing and distribution centers located throughout the US and is a part of The Marmon Group , an international conglomerate of industrial manufacturing companies owned by Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway .

Headquartered in St. Louis, Cerro has a handful of distribution and manufacturing facilities in Missouri and Illinois.

Cerro markets its flow products under several brand names. Its copper tubes are marketed under the CerroSpec and CerroGroove lines, while line sets are sold under the CerroSet name. In addition, its nitrogen pressurized ACR tubes are sold under the CerroPure brand. Its HVAC products are primarily used in air conditioning and refrigeration field service applications.

Cerro operates under Berkshire's Marmon segment, which contributed 3% of Berkshire's total annual revenue in fiscal 2014.

Cerro Flow Products traces its historical roots back to 1908, when it was founded as L.J. Cohen Co., a scrap iron dealer. It became part of The Marmon Group in 1975.
