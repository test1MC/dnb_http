Sharp Electronics positions itself as a purveyor of cutting-edge products for both consumers and corporate clients. The company is the US sales and marketing arm of Japan-based appliance and electronic components maker Sharp Corporation . Sharp Electronics is known as a leading seller of LCDs, which are used in everything from airplane cockpits to pinball machines and computers to HDTV sets. The company also markets solar energy products (modules, inverters), home appliances (air conditioners, microwave ovens), entertainment products (TVs, Blu-ray Disc players), and business electronics (cash registers, PCs, calculators, copiers). Corporate parent Sharp was purchased by Foxconn in 2016.

In addition, the company has two manufacturing plants in North America. Sharp Manufacturing Company of America, located in Tennessee, makes microwave ovens, copier toner, and solar modules. Sharp Electronica Mexico, located in Baja California (less than an hour south of San Diego), makes LCD displays for the North American and Latin American markets and LCD TVs (with screen sizes between 42 and 52 inches) for the North American market.

The company also has an R&D facility, Sharp Laboratories, in Camas, Washington that develops multimedia communications, digital imaging, digital video, and mobile computing products. That location also serves as the sales and marketing arm for the Microelectronics division to sell liquid crystal display (LCD), optoelectronics, infrared and semiconductors across the Americas.

Sharp Electronics also oversees sales and marketing subsidiaries in Canada and Mexico, and a Miami-based office oversees its operations across Latin America (excluding Brazil and Mexico).

Sharp Electronics sells through retailers and resellers across the Americas.
