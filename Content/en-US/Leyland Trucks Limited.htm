Leyland Trucks, which has laid claim to the roads in the UK and western Europe for more than 100 years, has been barreling into wide territory. The company, a subsidiary of US-based truck maker PACCAR since 1998, makes light- and medium-duty trucks, primarily under the DAF nameplate. The company also manufactured Foden vehicles for PACCAR until 2006, when the line was retired. Working with DAF Trucks (another PACCAR company) and Eaton , Leyland has developed a hybrid medium-duty truck that uses a parallel diesel/electric drive system. Founded in 1896, the company makes about 50% of its sales in the European Union, with the rest spread out across the world.
