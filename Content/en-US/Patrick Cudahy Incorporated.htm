Patrick Cudahy, established in 1888, calls itself the "Home of Sweet Apple Wood Smoked Flavor," and that says it all. The company makes sliced ham, salami, and specialty Italian and deli meats for sandwich shops, delicatessens, and fast-food operators. Its retail products include sausage, bacon, boneless hams, lean ham and turkey products, and sliced luncheon meats. The company has expanded its products to include such items as chorizo, salami higueral, salchichon servecero, and more. Patrick Cudahy also makes foodservice products and oils for industrial and institutional use. The company is part of pork processor John Morrell & Co. , itself owned by US pork giant Smithfield Foods .

Patrick Cudahy is based in Cudahy, Wisconsin and has a microwave bacon processing plant in Sioux Center, Iowa, and a small specialty salami processing plant in Elizabeth, New Jersey.

Patrick Cudahy operates as a part of John Morrell & Co, which is a part of the Pork Segment of Smithfield Foods. This segment accounted for about 83% of its parent's 2013 revenue.

The company sells its products to restaurants, commissaries, convenience stores, business and industry customers, hospitals, health care facilities, and stadiums and sports complexes, as well as to schools, colleges, and universities. It has significant market presence, both domestically and internationally, where it sells its products to more than 3,200 customers.

It utilizes a variety of consumer advertising and trade promotion programs designed to build awareness and increase sales distribution and penetration. It also provides sales incentives for its customers through rebates based on achievement of specified volume and/or growth in volume levels.
