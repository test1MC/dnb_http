Nanospectra is lighting a new path to treating cancer. The company has developed nanoshells, small particles of silica coated with gold. They can be injected into a tumor, and when exposed to a near-infrared laser, they convert the light to heat, which destroys the tumor. The procedure, called Aurolase, is non-invasive and will not destroy any tissues surrounding the tumor. Besides targeting cancerous tumors, the nanoshells can be used to create images or "weld" wounds together to accelerate healing. Jennifer West, one of Nanospectra's founders, helped develop the medical applications of nanoshells in conjunction with Rice University and MD Anderson Cancer Center .
