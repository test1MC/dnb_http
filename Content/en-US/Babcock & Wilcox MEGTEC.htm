MEGTEC Systems (trade name Babcock & Wilcox MEGTEC or B&W MEGTEC) provides engineered products, innovative technologies, and optimization services for multiple industries helping to meet rising global demand for environmental protection and advanced manufacturing equipment that promotes more ecologically sustainable business operations. With a long track record in also supplying air flotation drying, emission control systems, process thermal management, and web handling, B&W MEGTEC's businesses serve a diversity of customers in the web coating, flexible packaging, and multiple process industries. The company is a subsidiary of BWX Technologies .

In mid-2014 the company was acquired by power generation systems and equipment maker BWX Technologies for $143 million. It currently operates as a subsidiary of Babcock & Wilcox Power Generation Group, under the Power Generation segment of its parent company.

Besides its headquarters in Wisconsin (which includes a 365,000 sq. ft. manufacturing plant and engineering, service, and sales units, B&W MEGTEC has other US sites in Columbus, Ohio; Vero Beach, Florida; and Raleigh, North Carolina. It also has operations in Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Italy, Sweden, and the UK.

The company's environmental product offerings include oxidizers, solvent recovery, distillation systems and precipitators to incinerate, filter, recover and/or purify air, liquid and vapor-phase effluents from a variety of manufacturing processes. B&W MEGTEC also supplies ovens and dryers, specialized coating lines and material handling systems for energy storage, membranes, digital printing, and other advanced manufacturing processes that promote sustainability. The company offers stand-alone systems, integrated solutions, and/or process optimization services.

B&W MEGTEC serves companies in a range of industries, including automotive and industrial painting, cement, chemical and pharmaceutical, coating, composites, electronics, energy storage, ethanol and biofuels, iron and steel, membranes, metallurgical, odor control, and packaging.

It also has more than 75 technical support representatives around the world to assist clients with its products.

In early 2013 B&W MEGTEC added to its environmental product offerings through the purchase of TurboSonic Technologies , a maker of wet electrostatic precipitator systems, semi-dry and wet scrubbers, gas treatment systems, evaporative gas cooling systems, and related parts and services. TurboSonic operates as a separate business unit within its Environment, Climate and Energy (ECE) Group.

B&W MEGTEC traces its historical roots back to 1961. It was acquired by BWX Technologies in 2014.
