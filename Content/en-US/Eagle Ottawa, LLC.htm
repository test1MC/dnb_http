Eagle Ottawa is the world's largest designer and manufacturer of leather upholstery for automotive seats, instrument panels, and door panels. Touting an eco-friendly process, Eagle Ottawa supplies natural shrunken grain leather, chrome tanned leather, non-chrome leather, and auto-grade semi-aniline leather (custom colors, grains, and aromas). OEM customers BMW , Ford , General Motors , and Toyota use its leather in more than 100 models. Eagle Ottawa has about 20 plants in the Americas, Europe, and Asia. Lear Corporation acquired the company in 2015.

The company operates 20 plants around the world, many of which are located near its OEM customers. Its plants and other facilities are found in the US, Mexico, Argentina, Brazil, Japan, Tokyo, China, Korea, Thailand, and a half-dozen European countries.

The company is not only one of the world's oldest suppliers of interior leather to the automotive industry but its largest -- nearly one-third of all leather used in vehicles comes from Eagle Ottawa. Its products, such as "tipped" seat inserts, upholster a wide range of vehicle models, including luxury brands (Lexus IS-F), top-line trucks (Toyota Tundra), and mid-priced sedans ( Honda Accord).

Eagle Ottawa attributes its longevity, in part, to its specialization in natural interiors coupled with fashion- and furniture-oriented materials. Moreover, its leathers can be solar treated, textured, and even embossed with a natural grain or logo, or infused with a fragrance. Services also include seat testing, sewing and perforation, and door trim wrapping and embroidery.

Looking ahead, Eagle Ottawa is joining the wave of manufacturers going green. Its finishing processes are water-based (as opposed to solvent-based) and its leather tanning is recyclable and biodegradable (organic). The company was the first tanner to implement an on-site waste-water treatment plant.

Eagle Ottawa's origins date all the way back to 1865.
