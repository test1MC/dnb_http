Unison Industries' component parts do not just work together in harmony, but in unison. The company, a subsidiary of GE Aviation , designs, manufactures, and integrates electrical and mechanical components such as ignition systems, sensors, and other products for aerospace, marine, military, and industrial applications. Unison makes exciters, ignition leads, and igniter plugs for turbine and piston engines. Other offerings include alternators, bellows, controls, switches, metal tubing and ducting, brackets, and wiring harnesses. Unison operates four manufacturing centers in North America.

Unison has 15 manufacturing and logistics/service facilities in Canada, China, Mexico, Poland, Romania, the UK, and the US.

For one of its biggest customers -- the US Department of Defense -- the company makes sensors and other engine components for the F35 Joint Strike Fighter "Competitive" Engine at Unison's Norwich, New York, plant. The F35 Strike Fighter will eventually replace about 95% of the entire US fighter jet fleet for the Army , Navy , and Marine Corps .

Unison is part the aviation segment of GE Aviation . The aviation segment contributed 15% to the parent company's net revenues in 2013.

The company focuses on new product introductions, strategic acquisitions, technological innovations, and is committed to providing higher quality products and excellent customer service.

In 2014 Unison launched the Acclimate Ignition System, a family of high energy, high temperature ignition systems for commercial turbine engines. In 2013 it introduced new air cooled oil cooler applications for helicopters that can eliminate fans and "brick" style engine and gearbox oil coolers.

Growing its portfolio, in 2013 the company signed an agreement with Turbine Technics Inc. of Boca Raton, Florida, to act as a worldwide distributor of its marine and stationary land-based gas turbine engine components and services.

Another prestigious customer is Boeing . Unison entered into a data license agreement with Boeing in late 2010, which allows the company to produce licensed PMA parts (Parts Manufacturing Authority) for Boeing's military and commercial airplane models.

Founded in 1980, Unison Industries was acquired by GE in 2002.
