Key Plastics has a grip on car parts; it is one of the leading North American manufacturers of exterior door handles for the auto industry. Other exterior and engineered plastic components include cladding (covering one material with another) and mirror shells. Pressurized coolant and power steering bottles are also made by the company. Interior and trim products round out its portfolio. Major customers have included Chrysler , Mitsubishi , Nissan , and Volkswagen , as well as suppliers to the world's top car makers. Key Plastics operates more than 10 locations in seven countries throughout Asia, Europe, and North America. Minnesota-based Wayzata Investment is its controlling stakeholder.

Key Plastics owns a dozen locations and three technical centers across China, the Czech Republic, Germany, Japan, Mexico, Portugal, and the US.

In Europe the company makes center console, headrest assemblies, assist handles, air louvers, shift knobs, interior trim assemblies and radio bezels in servicing its European customers through localized operations in Germany, the Czech Republic and Portugal. In Asia Key Plastics is a supplier of air vents, mechanisms, radio climate and navigation bezels and interior handles. It provides customers with localized design and engineering support from operations in China and Japan.

Around 75% of the company's customers are Tier 1 suppliers, and 25% are OEMs. Key Plastics also serves customers across the aerospace, automotive, and medical sectors.

Key Plastics in 2012 sold a portion of its European operations in a management buy-out. The newly-formed Innovative Components Technologies GmbH (ICT) of Germany became the owner and operator of three facilities in Germany and others in Spain and the Czech Republic. The move allowed Key Plastics to focus on its global core products, where it is recording growth across all regions. (Innovative Components Technologies GmbH is an entity formed by the company management team for the purpose of acquiring former Key Plastics facilities.)
