Audio & Video Labs, doing business as Disc Makers, provides CD and DVD manufacturing and related services, including CD and DVD replication and duplication, custom design, printing and packaging, and distribution and financing. The company is outfitted with graphic, printing, and duplication equipment, as well as in-house studios for mastering and post-production services. It operates its own production plant in New Jersey. Disc Makers targets independent artists and record labels, as well as corporate, government, educational, and religious organizations. Corinthian Capital Group owns the company.

In addition to the company's plant and main office located in New Jersey, Disc Makers operates about 25 regional sales offices located throughout the US.

Disc Makers has found innovative ways to deal with the music industry downturn. In addition to discs, the company produces promotional materials, such as T-shirts, hats, postcards, and stickers. It also offers Web hosting services.

While CD sales in general have been plummeting in recent years thanks to digital music's rise in popularity, independent artists still find merit in distributing physical albums, and Disc Makers has managed to expand.

The firm was founded in 1946 as The Ballen Record Company by Ivin Ballen, father of company chairman Morris Ballen. It ceased to be family owned in 2006, when Morris sold the company to Corinthian Capital Group.
