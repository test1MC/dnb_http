This meat processor works to make it right and in minutes. Maid-Rite Steak Company produces more than 200 different fresh and quick-frozen beef, lamb, pork, and veal products under such brands names as namesake Maid-Rite, Minit Chef, Chef Italia, and Landis. Maid-Rite Steak is a leading supplier of portion-controlled beef and other meat products to food retailers (about 30% of sales), food service operators -- including giants Sysco and US Foods -- and institutional customers, such as schools, nursing homes, and the military. Maid-Rite's own fleet of refrigerated trucks delivers meat to local distributors. Company president Don Bernstein founded Maid-Rite Steak in 1960.

While Maid-Rite Steak has customers throughout North America, the Pennsylvania-based company's sales are concentrated in the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic states. Maid-Rite Steak has facilities in the Pennsylvania cities of Scranton and Dunmore.

Its fresh and quick-frozen beef, lamb, pork, and veal items also include sandwich steaks, hamburger patties, breaded products, and meatballs. For the foodservice industry, Maid-Rite offers a complete line of healthcare and child nutrition products, as well as fully cooked items under The Chosen Frozen brand name.

The meat purveyor is responding to increased demand for pre-cooked product, including its barbeque pork rib, sausage patties and links, char-flavored steakhouse burger (complete with grill marks), as well as its Chef Italia line of frozen meatballs and the Minit chef line of quick preparation foods.

Maid-Rite Steak maintains a two-week inventory of all its products to meet immediate demand for its products.
