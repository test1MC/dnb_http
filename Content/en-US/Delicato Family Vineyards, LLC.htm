Delicato Family Vineyards owns and operates 10,000 acres of vineyards in Monterey, Lodi, and Manteca, California. The company, also known as DFV Wines, produces a variety of red and white wines, including chardonnay, cabernet sauvignon, pinot grigio, shiraz, white zinfandel, and merlot, all under the Delicato label. Its boxed Delicato wine, called the Bota Box, is said to stay fresh for up to 45 days after opening. The company's wines are available in restaurants and at wine retailers throughout the US. In 2010 it acquired the Black Stallion Winery located in Napa Valley, adding to its holdings in California. The family-owned winery was founded in 1924 by Gasparé Indelicato.

Delicato Family Vineyards harvests grapes from the California regions of Napa, Lodi, Monterey, and Sonoma.

The company boasts offices in the US, Canada, and London.

The winery continues to grow its portfolio of more than two dozen brands. In 2013 Delicato Family Vineyards nationally launched Sequin wines, a line of delicately bubbled, Moscato, Pinot Grigio, and Rosé of Syrah varieties.

Delicato Family Vineyards has seen its Bota Box wine label log successes in recent years. The three-liter boxed wine label serves eco-conscious Millennial consumers and has added more than 1.6 million cases to its total in three years. Delicato Family Vineyards added a Pinot Noir in 2014. Other popular and profitable brands within the Delicato Family Vineyards portfolio include Twisted, Noble Vines, and Gnarly Head.
