Instrumentation Laboratory develops and makes diagnostic medical equipment primarily for hospitals and clinical laboratories worldwide. It provides critical care systems, hemostasis products, and information management services. Product lines include GEM analyzers for critical care diagnostics and ACL and HemosIL analyzers and reagents for automated blood and coagulation testing. Established in 1959, Instrumentation Laboratory is a division of private Spanish health care company Werfen Group, which specializes in medical and scientific equipment and in-vitro diagnostics.

Instrumentation Laboratory holds more than 100 patents for its medical devices. The company has developed and marketed proprietary products and technologies throughout its history, including centrifugal analyzers, co-oximetry systems, and enhanced solid-state fluidics systems. It also has expanded its line of information management offerings over the years to include a number of process software and quality control systems.

Instrumentation Laboratory enjoys a global reach. While it is based in Massachusetts, the company operates its business in about 90 countries throughout Europe, the Middle East, Africa, the Asia/Pacific region, and the Americas.

The company's products portfolio is marketed through global affiliates and third-party distributors. It sells its hemostasis products directly to customers around the world. Clients include networks of laboratories, Group Purchasing Organizations, and hospital consortiums.

In 2014, the company had sales of $764.4 million.

Instrumentation Laboratory works to improve its testing supplies and equipment to better serve the needs of medical professionals. It employs a research and development team to bring innovative, efficient, and accurate technology systems to market. The company furthers its internal development programs by forming alliances with other laboratory equipment firms, as well as by making occasional acquisitions.

In early 2017, Instrumentation Laboratory acquired Accriva Diagnostics, which provides in vitro diagnostic blood testing products. That deal expanded its position in the point-of-care critical care testing market, as well as boosting its portfolio of hemostasis products.
