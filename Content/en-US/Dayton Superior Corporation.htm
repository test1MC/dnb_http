Dayton Superior Corporation is built on a strong foundation. The company makes metal accessories and forms for keeping nonresidential concrete and masonry structures in place during construction. Dayton Superior's sales come largely from lines comprising concrete accessories, such as anchoring and bracing for walls and bridge support framework. It also makes masonry products, welded dowel assemblies, paving products, and corrosion-preventing epoxy coatings and other chemicals. The company's rental equipment includes concrete forming systems and shoring systems.

Dayton Superior's portfolio includes more than 17,000 items, and the company has a network of 15 distribution centers and 13 manufacturing facilities.

The company's products -- under such brand names as Aztec, BarLock, Conspec, Edoco, and Unitex, among others -- continue as a dominant feature in the construction landscape, particularly in infrastructure projects such as highways, airports, and bridges, as well as in commercial buildings, schools, hospitals, sports arenas, wind turbine foundations, and more.

Customers include rebar fabricators, precast and pre-stressed concrete manufacturers, engineers, and general contractors. It sells its products through a nationwide distribution network of more than 2,700 dealer locations and 15 company owned distribution centers.

Dayton Superior relies on new product launches and expanding its sales force as a means for growth.

In 2015, the company added a new high-strength brace, the B33F, to its Accubrace brand product portfolio.

That year Dayton Superior expanded its operations in Bogota, Colombia. It also integrated its paving manufacturing at Valparaiso, Indiana into the regional paving hub in Kankakee, Illinois, to service customers throughout the Midwest, South Central, Northeast and Southeast. In 2015, the company also announced plans to invest in a 79,000 sq. ft. service and distribution facility to house its Toronto operations.

In 2014 Dayton Superior opened its Innovation Center at the company's corporate headquarters in Miamisburg, Ohio.

On the product innovation front, that year the company introduced the Max-A-Form Handling Hook and the Super She Bolt, two accessory products which are used with the company's existing concrete forming systems, along with two supplemental chemical products.

In 2013 it introduced new brokerage services to bring together buyers and sellers of new and used equipment. Also that year, it increased its sales mix in the Western and Southern parts of the US as a result of increased revenue and higher demand for its products. It also beefed up its Latin American sales force serving the concrete construction market in Columbia, Mexico, and Panama in 2013.

Expanding the company's capacity to create a competitive edge for its tilt-up construction customers, in 2014 the company acquired TiltWerks, a fully-integrated, web-based technology developed by Tilt-Up Design Systems LLC.
