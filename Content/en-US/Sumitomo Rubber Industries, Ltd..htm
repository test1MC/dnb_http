Sumitomo Rubber is on a roll and is yet to tire of tires. A key player in the Sumitomo Rubber Group, the company is Japan's #2-maker of passenger, truck, and racecar tires. Its line offers aluminum car wheels, too. Sumitomo Rubber allies with Goodyear Tire & Rubber to make and sell Goodyear-brands in Japan, and Goodyear makes and markets Sumitomo's Dunlop brand tires in North America and Europe. Procurement and technological development are also linked. Its smaller sister operations, SRI Sports, makes golf and tennis gear and SRI Hybrid, industrial wares such as precision rubber components for office automation equipment and marine products, dock fenders to skirts.

The company has operations China, India, Indonesia, Malaysia, Indonesia, japan, Singapore, Taiwan, Thailand, Vietnam, South Africa, Turkey, the UAE, Belgium, Germany, Switzerland, Netherlands, Russia, the US, Brazil, Australia, and Chile.

Sumitomo Rubber operates through three business segments: Tire, Sports, and Industrial and Other Products.

Tire manufactures and sells tires, mainly the Dunlop, Falken and Goodyear brands.

Sports offers several products, including rackets, balls, and golf product lineups.

Industrial and Other Products offers a wide variety of products encompassing precision rubber parts for printers and photocopiers.

In 2014 the company's net revenues increased by 7% in local currency due to higher tire segment sales.

Sumitomo Rubber's sales of tire segments increased mainly due to higher sales of high-value-added products, such as fuel-efficient tires.

The company's net income increased by 19% in local currency due to higher net revenues and changes in equity in earnings and losses from unconsolidated subsidiaries and affiliates.

In 2014 Sumitomo Rubber's cash inflows increased by 41%.

The company's Vision 2020 includes entering emerging markets, technology evolution, and category innovation.

It plans to expand sales in emerging nations while stepping up marketing in Australia through a newly established local sales subsidiary. It is also looking will penetrate markets around the globe by introducing tires compliant with environmental regulations.

In 2015 Sumitomo Rubber launched its latest line of flagship tires: "SP SPORT MAXX 050+" and "SP SPORT MAXX 050+ for SUV," combining high handling stability with superior wet grip performance to meet the needs of the next generation of high-performance vehicles. It also plans to release these products in China, Russia, the Middle East, Central and South America and other regions in the near future.

As part of the global expansion of its tire business, in 2014 it entered into a joint venture with Sumitomo to establish Sumitomo Rubber Australia Pty. Ltd., a new local tire sales business in Australia.

To expand its rubber parts for pharmaceutical packages business in Europe, in 2015, the company and BV Holding AG, a private equity fund, acquired Switzerland-based Lonstroff Holding AG, for CH F44 million.

Sumitomo Electric Industries owns about 27% of the company. The tire maker is part of Sumitomo Electric's automotive business segment, the main contributor to the parent's operating profit.
