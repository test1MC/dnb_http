Feel like you're being watched? More likely you're being recorded. March Networks makes video surveillance systems that include cameras, digital video recorders, encoders (converts an analog signal into digital format), and related software applications. Banks, airlines, retail stores, and mass transit systems use the company's products to track cargo, monitor ATMs, combat vandalism, and prevent theft. March Networks sells directly to end-users and through channel partners and OEMs. Customers have included such big names as Wal-Mart , Alaska Air , Fifth Third Bancorp , and Sandia National Laboratories . The company is an independent subsidiary of New Jersey-based Infinova.

Headquartered in Canada, March Networks conducts research and development services in three countries around the world: Canada, Italy, and India. It has sales offices located in Mexico, Italy, the United Arab Emirates, Kuwait, India, Hong Kong, Australia, and the US. March Networks' systems are delivered through an extensive distribution and partner network in more than 50 countries.

The company has sold more than 120,000 systems worldwide. March Networks supports and manages a single customer enterprise installation of over 30,000 networked video recorders.

The company offers its services to financial institutions, retailers, commercial and industrial enterprises, and government organizations.

March Networks and Bombardier Transportation provide comprehensive video surveillance for passenger rail environments worldwide, including the London Underground and South Africa's Gautrain. Wabtec Railway Electronics and March Networks have been delivering VideoTrax DVRs to large freight rail operators since 2005.

The company's other customers include Marine Federal Credit Union, Fifth Third Bank , Danske Bank , Total Wine & More, New York Fries Franchisee, Woolworths , and Auckland Airport .

Product innovation is key to March Networks' growth. In 2014 the company introduced its new 6700 Series Hybrid Network Video Recorders (NVRs) and the Edge 4e Encoder.

In 2013 it introduced 8000 Series Hybrid NVRs platform which provides all the power banks need for video surveillance; it also introduced 8708 S and 8508 S NVRs for the retail market. It expanded its product portfolio with the launch of the new March Networks MegaPX 360 Indoor Dome IP camera and a local ATM/teller integration capability for smaller credit unions and community banks seeking more powerful fraud detection.

The company was acquired by Infinova in 2012 for an estimated C$ 90 million. Infinova is owned by Shenzhen Infinova Ltd., a surveillance system manufacturer based in China. The all cash acquisition gave Infinova access to a 50 country distribution network and added the Command IP video management system to its portfolio. March Networks continues to operate independently.
