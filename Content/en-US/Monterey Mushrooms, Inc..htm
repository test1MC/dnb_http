Monterey Mushrooms has operations popping up all over. A leading cultivator and marketer of fresh mushrooms, the company started with a single farm in 1971 and has since grown into an international giant. Monterey Mushrooms maintains about a dozen North American farms. The company's bulk and packaged fresh mushrooms are available at food retailers and foodservice operators, and as ingredients for food manufacturers. In addition to fresh mushrooms, the company offers mushroom products packed in cans, jars, and in frozen sauces. The company's biotech business serves other mushroom growers through its spawn supplier, Amycel, and nutritional supplement provider Spawn Mate.

Based in California, Monterey Mushrooms operates production, sales, and administrative offices worldwide. It boasts regional production sites, including nearly a dozen fresh mushroom growing facilities located in California, Texas, Tennessee, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Florida, and Mexico.

Monterey Mushrooms operates through three divisions: Fresh Division, Process Products Division, and International Division.

Its Fresh Division consists of fresh mushrooms, which represent the majority of the company's marketing and sales. Sold under the Clean N Ready name, among others, the division houses white domestic bulk and prepackaged; specialty mushrooms such as Portabella, Shiitake, Oyster, Baby Bella, Enoki, Portabellini, and value-added mushroom packages.

The company's Process Products Division serves a variety of markets, such as national chain restaurant, foodservice, private label, ingredient, and club store.

Monterey Mushrooms' International Division includes Champinones San Miguel, its affiliate in Mexico.

Monterey Mushrooms has grown to become one of the country's largest national marketers of fresh mushrooms. The company's Mexican operation is the largest single mushroom farm in Mexico with 20 million pounds grown and processed annually.

The company produces its private-label processed products for major retailers and international sale.

Once part of the mushroom business of bankrupt conglomerate Amfac, Monterey Mushrooms was acquired in 1988 by a group led by president and CEO Shah Kazemi.
