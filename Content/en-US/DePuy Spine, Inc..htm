When faced with an orthopedic dilemma, DePuy Spine shows a lot of backbone. The company, a subsidiary Johnson & Johnson 's DePuy , develops and manufactures devices and equipment used in spinal surgeries in about 55 countries worldwide. Its portfolio of products covers every spinal surgery option and includes everything from spinal fusion and disc replacement products to external fixation devices, plates and rods for fixing spinal fractures, and devices used to correct spinal deformities. The company is designing an increasing number of its products for use in minimally invasive surgeries. Additionally, DePuy Spine makes and sells biologic products, such as bone grafting materials.

Massachusetts-based DePuy Spine sells its products to surgeons worldwide in some 55 countries.

DePuy Spine has manufacturing plants in California and Massachusetts in the US, as well as in Germany, Ireland, and Switzerland.

The division aims to benefit from J&J's acquisition of Swiss orthopedic device maker Synthes. The parent purchased Synthes for $21 billion in 2011, adding the maker of surgical implants, tools, and fixation devices to the DePuy organization.

During the past five years, DePuy Spine has launched more than 40 new products, such as the VIPER MIS System, COUGAR LS Interbody Fusion Cage, and the EXPEDIUM Spine System.

It expands its portfolio further through partnerships. For instance, the medical developer offers specialized allograft tissues -- such as VERTIGRAFT Structural Allograft and the SYMPHONY I/C Graft Chambers -- through an exclusive alliance with biologics firm LifeNet, which provides organ donation and tissue banking services. DePuy Spine struck an agreement with Osseon Therapeutics in 2010 to distribute that company's Osseoflex steerable spine augmentation needles for the treatment of compression fractures.
