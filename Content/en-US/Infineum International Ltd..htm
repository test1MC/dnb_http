Add the additives product line of ExxonMobil 's chemical business to that of Shell Chemicals and you'll get, if not infinity, at least Infineum. The joint venture makes petroleum additives for fuel and lubricants. Infineum has ten product lines: Passenger car motor oil additives (for cars and light-duty trucks); Heavy-duty diesel engine oil additives (for general engine applications); Small engine oil additives (for two-stroke and four-stroke engines); Gas engine oil additives; Fuel additives (to improve engine performance); and Marine fuel and oil additives.

Infineum products are used to formulate lubricants across the full range of gasoline and light-duty diesel engine performance requirements, including the latest American Petroleum Institute , European Automobile Manufacturers' Association, European Centre for Ecotoxicology and Toxicology of Chemicals, ElectroMotive Diesel, and Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) specifications.

Infineum maintains a sales presence in 70 countries and operates business centers in China, Singapore, the UK, and the US. Its customers are oil and fuel marketers located throughout the world.

The company serves a range of industry groups, including automotive, heavy-duty diesel and marine engine oils; diesel fuels; and specialty applications such as transmission fluids and industrial oils.

Broadening its geographic reach, in 2017 Infineum set up its first wholly owned business in India, Infineum India Additives Private Limited, based in Mumbai.

In 2014 Infineum opened a second manufacturing plant in Singapore. The new plant makes calcium salicylate, a critical component in its passenger car and heavy-duty oil formulations, as well as its marine oil additive products. Previously, calcium salicylate was manufactured only by Infineum's UK operations.

Continuing its expansion in the Asia/Pacific region, that year the company opened a new Business and Technology Center in Jinqiao, Shanghai. In 2014 it also broke ground on a new blending plant at Zhangjiagang, China.

The joint venture was formed in 1999 by ExxonMobil and Shell Chemicals . The parent companies attempted to divest Infineum in 2008 but pulled back from the move when offers for the joint venture fell short of their expectations. The bidders were mostly big global private equity firms.
