NanoBio aims to put its NanoStat antimicrobial nanoemulsion technology to work to make drugs work better. The firm's nanoemulsions easily penetrate skin and mucosal tissues, and can carry along drugs to destroy bacteria, fungi, spores, and viral invaders. NanoBio's two lead candidates are in development to treat cold sores and nail fungus. Other candidates target acne, drug-resistant bacterial infections, and viral respiratory infections. NanoBio is also using its technology to develop vaccines for hepatitis B and influenza. The company received a boost in 2010 when it was awarded $6 million from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation to develop a nasal spray vaccine to prevent respiratory syncytial virus (RSV).
