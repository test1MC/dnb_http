The HON Company invites customers to take a seat, and a desk, and a filing cabinet. A subsidiary and the largest operating company of office furniture giant HNI , HON designs and makes value-priced workspace furnishings, including desks, tables, panel systems, workstations, seating, and filing and storage units. Its products are sold through distributors and retailers under the trademarks Park Avenue, Valido, Perpetual, and Simplicity. Founded in 1944 as Home-O-Nize, the company made index card file boxes for kitchen use. It later shortened its name to HON and moved into the office furniture industry.

Iowa-based HON Company's operations consist of office furniture manufacturing facilities in the US. The company has showrooms in Chicago, Atlanta, New York City, Washington, DC, and Muscatine, Iowa.

The company mainly markets its products through distributors and retailers located in the US. Its customer base consists of small and mid-sized businesses, government agencies, and schools.

Financial Performance

The company is a part of Office Furniture segment of HNI . This segment accounted for 78% of HNI's total revenues in 2014.

HNI's strategy has included tight management of costs. In May 2014, HON announced it will close its furniture plant in Florence, Iowa by the end of the year. The operation in Florence will be consolidated with operations in other states. The office furniture maker's latest collection, Endorse, features task seating, lounge seating, and occasional tables. It debuted in 2014.

In response to customer requests and market needs for collaborative workplace furnishings, the company expanded its line of Flock Collaborative Solutions in 2013.
