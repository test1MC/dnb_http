Falcon Pharmaceuticals has a keen eye for alternatives. The company specializes in making generic equivalents of ophthalmic drugs to treat a range of eye conditions, as well as otic (ear) ailments. Its products include, Timolol GFS, a generic equivalent of glaucoma treatment Timoptic-XE gel, and trifluridine solution, a generic version of ophthalmic antiviral drug Viroptic. The company also offers anti-infective, steroid, allergy, and anesthetic pharmaceutical products without the markup typically associated with brand names. Falcon Pharmaceuticals is a subsidiary of Novartis .

The company manufactures about 35 generic formulations of name-brand drugs. In addition to Tomolol GFS and trifluridine, Falcon Pharmaceutical's offerings include glaucoma treatments brimonidine and dorzolamide; eye inflammation treatment prednisolone; eye allergy medication ketorolac; antibiotics such as ciprofloxacin; and otic and ophthalmic suspensions of hydrocortisone.

Sales, marketing, and support services for Falcon Pharmaceuticals' products are handled by Novartis' main generic subsidiary, Sandoz .

Falcon Pharmaceuticals regularly looks for opportunities to develop additional products as patented drugs reach their expiration dates. The company utilizes its R&D department towards its first-to-market goal of introducing new generic product before the competition.

Falcon Pharmaceuticals was formed by eye care products company Alcon in 1994 to develop and market generic versions of Alcon and other drugmakers' products. Alcon became a wholly owned subsidiary of Novartis in 2011. Following the transaction, Novartis began to transition the Falcon operations under the Sandoz organization, leaving Alcon to focus on branded product offerings.
