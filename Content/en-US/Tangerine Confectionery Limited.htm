When it comes to its products, Tangerine Confectionery hopes its customers will be like kids in a candy store. One of the UK's largest privately-owned candy companies, Tangerine makes a variety of sweets, including toffees, licorices, fruit chews, bon-bons, hard candy, gum, and chocolate. Popular products include its Taveners Proper Sweets line, Princess Marshmallows, Barratt Dip Dabs, and Butterkist popcorn. Tangerine operates six British factories and sells primarily through large UK retailers like Tesco , Asda , Morrisons , Waitrose , and Marks and Spencer , as well as through small independent candy shops. Since mid-2011 UK investment firm The Blackstone Group has owned a 40% stake in the company.

From its headquarters in England Tangerine sells its sweets throughout Europe and the Middle East as well as in Australia and Canada.

The company sells branded and private label products through retailers (grocery, discount, and convenience stores).

Tangerine was formed in 2006 when Growth Capital Partners (not to be confused with Houston-based Growth Capital Partners L.P. ) invested in what was then a subsidiary of Danish candy-maker Toms Gruppen. The subsidiary's assets were combined with the confectionery division of biscuit-maker Burtons Foods to form Tangerine.
