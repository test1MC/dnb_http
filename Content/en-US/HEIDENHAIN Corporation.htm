HEIDENHAIN is in plain sight when it comes to devices for measuring and inspection. The company develops and makes angular, linear, and rotary encoders, digital readouts, length gauges, and numerical controls for semiconductor and electronics manufacturers for use in measuring equipment and automated production machines. Its encoders measure rotation accuracy and thermal stability, while its controls are used in milling, drilling, and boring machines. HEIDENHAIN's devices have also been applied in telescope projects and satellite-receiving antennas. HEIDENHAIN is the North American wholly owned subsidiary of Dr. Johannes Heidenhain GmbH, a German manufacturer of precision measurement and control equipment.

HEIDENHAIN sells a comprehensive line of absolute and incremental rotary encoders, linear and angular and rotary encoders including more than 15 million rotary and angle encoders, about six million linear encoders, about 500,000 position displays, and nearly 260,000 TNC controls.

HEIDENHAIN is based in Schaumburg, Illinois and has representatives stationed at sites around the world. Customers have included CNC Heads, which makes automotive cylinder heads, and Winbro Group Technologies, which makes machining systems for turbine manufacturers.
