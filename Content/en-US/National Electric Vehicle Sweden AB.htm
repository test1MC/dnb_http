Saab Automobile's bumpy ride to bankruptcy is over now that National Electric Vehicle Sweden (NEVS) is taking the wheel. Saab Automobile AB , which went bankrupt in 2012, had been plagued by dwindling sales and differing owners. (Former owner GM put Saab into liquidation in early 2010, and Spyker later bought it for $400 million before Saab finally filed for bankruptcy a year later.) NEVS bought the assets of Saab Automobile AB and its subsidiaries for an undisclosed amount of cash in 2012, but it doesn't plan on making the same old Swedish car. NEVS retooled the Saab 9-3 model as an electric vehicle (EV) for the Chinese market.
