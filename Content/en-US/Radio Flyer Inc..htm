Radio Flyer makes those wittle wed wagons adults adore and kids covet. An icon of childhood play, the #18 Classic Red Wagon has rolled out of the plant for more than 70 years -- an American toy industry record and the reason Radio Flyer owns a sizable stake in the US toy market. The company makes plastic wagons and models with oversized, terrain-friendly tires and wooden rails, as well as bikes and trikes for little tikes and scooters and pedal-powered cars. Radio Flyer sells its items in retail outlets in the US and Canada and online through the likes of Amazon.com . Italian immigrant Antonio Pasin chose the name "Radio Flyer" in 1933 because it sounded futuristic. His grandchildren own and operate the firm.
