Overhill Farms delivers America's favorite staples to the frozen-food aisle. It custom-makes frozen entrées, including beef, chicken, pasta, and seafood meals, as well as soups, sauces, and side items for customers in the foodservice, retail food, and airline industries. It specializes in private labels and co-packing for food retailers and other manufacturers and restaurants, such as Target , Pinnacle Foods , and Panda Express , and airlines American . Overhill Farms also holds a license to make and sell frozen foods under the Boston Market label. In addition to Panda Express, Overhill's largest customers are weight-loss business Jenny Craig and grocer Safeway . Bellisio Foods owns Overhill Farms.

Overhill Farms bought Bellisio Foods in late 2013 in a deal valued at $80.9 million. Bellisio Foods was attracted to the business for its bulging brands portfolio and West Coast manufacturing presence.

Overhill Farms, based in California, operates a headquarters, entrée manufacturing and warehousing facility, product development unit, sales office, quality control facilities, and a protein cooking plant in the city of Vernon, outside Los Angeles.

The company serves several customer types, including foodservice companies and retailers. Panda Restaurant Group , Jenny Craig , Safeway , Pinnacle Foods Group , American Airlines , and Target are some of its customers. It produces foods for others through licensing agreements.

As Overhill Farms weathered the national economic downturn, its sales to major customers declined in almost all markets. (Airline revenues were the exception due to new products developed for an existing customer.) Overhill Farms cited poor consumer confidence and lower demand as driving customer decisions to limit their orders and inventory levels. One of the company's key accounts, the H. J. Heinz Company , has opted to self-manufacture products previously produced by Overhill Farms. The company's expectations for a licensing agreement with Better Living Brands Alliance to exclusively produce and sell frozen entrees under the Eating Right and O Organics brands, failed to materialize.

Other factors denting Overhill Farms' performance include higher fuel and commodity costs. In addition, the company made sizable one-time start-up investments in 16 new products under the Boston Market brand and 16 new products for Target. As a result, net revenues plummeted for several years.

The frozen food maker has managed to reduce its debt and refinance its remaining loans on more favorable terms. To extend the Boston Market line, late 2011 it inked an agreement with Bellisio Foods to share in the manufacture, sale, and distribution of the brand. Overhill Farms has targeted opportunities, as well, in the foodservice market. To this end, it maintains an agreement with the J.R. Simplot Company , naming Simplot as the distributor of certain Overhill products to some of the largest foodservice accounts in the US. Overhill Farms is also standing by American Airlines, which filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy October 2011. The airline has assured suppliers that it will meet its bills.
