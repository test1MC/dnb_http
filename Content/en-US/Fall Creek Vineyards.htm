The folks at Fall Creek Vineyards have branched out in a new direction. The Texas vineyard has helped upgrade people's image of the Lone Star State. Long known for its oil and cattle, Texas now has a growing reputation for growing grapes and making great wines. The red and white wines that come from the 65 acres at Fall Creek Vineyard have won acclaim in state, national, and international competitions. Texas businessman and rancher Ed Auler and his wife Susan started Fall Creek in 1975. The first wines were bottled in 1979. Today, the vineyard offers varieties that include cabernet sauvignon, chardonnay, merlot, and zinfandel. Its labels include MERITUS, Cascade, Mission, Granite Blush, and Fall Creek.
