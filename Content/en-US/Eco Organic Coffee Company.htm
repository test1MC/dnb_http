San Diego-based Eco Organic Coffee imports organic coffee from small village co-ops located in Ethiopia and Papua New Guinea, as well as Central and South America. The company was started in 1989 by Karen Cebreros, who, after a trip to Peru, became interested in passing along organic, shade-grown methods to small coffee bean farmers who were struggling to stay in business due to the overuse of pesticides. Eco Organic Coffee also has implemented fair trade practices, which guarantee a living wage for coffee farmers. It is owned by InterAmerican Coffee.
