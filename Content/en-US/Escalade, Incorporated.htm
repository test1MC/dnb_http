Escalade isn't in the business of making high-end SUVs, but rather helping customers get their game on as the world's largest producer of tables for table tennis under the Ping-Pong, STIGA, Prince, and Head brands. Its other sporting goods include pool tables, hockey and soccer tables, play systems, archery, darts, and fitness equipment. Products are sold under the Goalrilla, Silverback, Minnesota Fats, USWeight, and Woodplay names, as well as private labels. Escalade operates through half a dozen manufacturing and distribution facilities across North America.

Based in Indiana, Escalade operates half a dozen manufacturing and distribution facilities across North America in Arizona, Indiana, Illinois, Florida, North Carolina, and Mexico and abroad in Germany. It has a presence in China, Germany, France, the UK, Spain, South Africa, and Sweden.

The company manufactures its products in the US, Mexico, and Germany and imports products from Asian contract manufacturers. North America accounts for 98% of net revenue.

Escalade sells its products through retailers and sporting goods stores. Dick's Sporting Goods contributed about 18% of Escalade's 2015 total revenues.

Escalade has seen its revenues fluctuate during the past five years. After declining in 2014, revenues climbed by 13% during 2015. The growth was attributed to the introduction of new products, expansion of product distribution, acquisitions, and increased investment in consumer marketing.

Its profits, however dipped by 2% mainly due to a higher ratio of cost of products sold over revenue during 2015. Cash flow from operations has also fluctuated over the years; after rising in 2014, cash flow fell by 20% in 2015 due to changes in accounts receivable.

The company has been working to increase revenue by developing and rolling out new products, expanding its product distribution, divesting non-core operations, and acquiring new companies. In 2014 it sold its former Information Security & Print Finishing segment, which made and marketed data shredders, paper trimmers, and folding machines sold worldwide under several names (Intimus, Paper Monster, Martin Yale) and private labels.

Escalade in 2015 acquired Onix Sports to expand its offerings of pickleball paddles, balls, sportswear, and accessories. It also obtained Goalsetter Systems in order to add another premium brand of residential in-ground basketball goals to its portfolio. In 2016, the company acquired Triumph Sports USA, a line of indoor and outdoor games brand, which expanded its company's product profile and provided opportunities in tailgating and lawn games.
