BeneFit Cosmetics will make you look lovely, and for a good cause. The company, which donates a portion of proceeds to charity, boasts products that have clever names and pin-up lasses. BeneFit sells its lines and offers facial services through more than 3,000 counters (called "Beauty Bars") in department stores and beauty specialty shops in nearly 40 countries worldwide. It also boasts more than a dozen dedicated boutiques located mostly in Eastern US. BeneFit Cosmetics, whose celebrity-endorsed Benetint product brought the fledgling company into the mainstream, was founded in 1976 in San Francisco by sisters Jean Ann and Jane Ann Ford as The Face Place. Glamour leviathan LVMH holds a 70% stake.

BeneFit Cosmetics sells its products worldwide throughout the Americas, Asia Pacific, and Europe, the Middle East, and Africa (EMEA).

BeneFit sells its products online at benefitcosmetics.com, as well as through shops-in-shops located in department stores, boutiques, licensed retail stores, and beauty specialty shops located in the US and internationally.

The company's known for its playful and offbeat style, operating Glam Up & Away kiosks (effective 2013) in airports in the US and abroad, and beauty product vending machines in the shape of vintage pink buses.

Thanks to the continued growth of flagship products They're Real! and POREfessional, BeneFit helped its parent's Perfumes and Cosmetics segment log 3% revenue growth in fiscal 2013 and organic revenue growth of 7%. Pumping up profits further was BeneFit's latest strong rollout: Fake Up moisturizing concealer.

The company relies on new product launches and international retail expansion. In 2013, the company opened stores in London, Dublin, and Madrid. The company rolls out new products as it continues to extend its reach into perfumes, as well. BeneFit also extended its retail presence to Russia, opening about 20 stores there. The moves are showing signs of paying off. Its products have helped BeneFit to log sales increases for LVMH, which considers BeneFit to be a rapidly growing American brand and a profitable business.

As part of its 2014 strategy, BeneFit is adding Paris to its US, Asia, and UK expansion plans. The beauty firm sells its products in France through retailer Sephora and online through that brand's e-commerce site.

It also partners with others, such as LeSportsac , to create accessories perfect for the BeneFit customer. Through an alliance with the consumer products company, BeneFit in 2014 will begin offering a line of makeup bags, travel bags, and handbags.

BeneFit operates under LVMH 's Perfumes and Cosmetics segment, which in fiscal 2013 contributed 13% of revenue. Cosmetics, specifically, accounted for about 87% of the segment's revenue during the reporting period.

The cosmetics company operates a small chain of boutiques in Illinois, New York, Connecticut, Massachusetts, and California.

As its name implies, BeneFit is focused on fundraising, as well as lip gloss. It has worked with the Twin Towers Fund and the American Heart Association , donating a percentage of its sales. BeneFit's largest partnership to date is with Big Brothers Big Sisters .
