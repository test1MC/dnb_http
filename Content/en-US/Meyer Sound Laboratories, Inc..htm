Berkeley, California's Meyer Sound Laboratories isn't for the faint of hearing -- or maybe it is. The company has built a business manufacturing high-quality digital loudspeaker systems for venues of all sizes. Its products have been used in cinemas, sports venues, hotels, museums, recording studios, cruise ships, corporations, theaters, restaurants, retail outlets, and theme parks, among other settings. Proportionate to company size, Meyer Sound Laboratories employs one of the largest engineering staffs in the audio industry. It has sales forces across the US and abroad. President and CEO John Meyer and executive VP Helen Meyer established the company in 1979.

Based in Berkeley, California, Meyer Sound Laboratories enjoys an international presence across nearly a dozen countries, including the US, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, Russia, the United Arab Emirates, Mexico, Spain, and Portugal.

The audio expert operates its business in North America, Central and South America, Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and Asia Pacific.

Its products are used in a variety of applications, such as corporate audio/visual, live performance venues, live sound, restaurants/bars/clubs, sports venues, theater, worship, cinema, retail, cruise ships, creative art exhibits, and other installations.

The audio company sells or rents its products through dealers, distributors, and rental companies. Its dealers and distributors include the likes of Audio Dynamic Engineering & Consultancy, Audio Visions, ACS Enterprises, Visioneering Design, and Shanghai People's Sound AV Science & Technology.

To ensure that researching and buying its products is easier than ever, Meyer Sound Laboratories in 2014 rolled out a newly redesigned and revamped website. Features include streamlined navigation of essential product information, enhanced user experience, and more intuitive product selection parameters. Customers also have more direct access to tools like the Amperage/BTU calculator and rigging guides.
