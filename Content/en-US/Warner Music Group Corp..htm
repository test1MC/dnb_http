Warner Music Group (WMG) is one of the world's largest recording companies, ranking #3 in terms of US market share (behind Universal Music Group and Sony Music Entertainment ). It operates through two businesses: Recorded Music and Music Publishing. Its Recorded Music catalog includes best-selling albums The Eagles: Their Greatest Hits 1971-1975 and Led Zeppelin IV . Its Music Publishing business holds more than one million copyrights from more than 70,000 songwriters. In mid-2011, WMG was acquired by diversified business group Access Industries.

The company operates through two business segments: Recorded Music and Music Publishing.

WMG's Recorded Music business produces, markets, and distributes recordings primarily through units Atlantic Records Group and Warner Bros. Records along with Parlophone. Its Music Publishing business operates through Warner/Chappell Music .

The company also has a stake in music firms and record labels such as Sub Pop , Roadrunner Records , and Rykodisc, and distributes its music through subsidiaries such as Rhino Entertainment (compilations and reissues) and Alternative Distribution Alliance . Its Independent Label Group (ILG) is composed of WMG's Asylum Records (urban), East West Records (rock), and Cordless Recordings (digital-only).

Recorded Music accounted about 85% of WMG's revenue in fiscal 2016. It produces revenue primarily through the marketing, sale and licensing of recorded music in various physical (such as CDs, Vinyl and DVDs) and digital (such as streaming and downloads) formats. It also benefits from additional revenue streams associated with artists, including merchandising, fan clubs, sponsorships, concert promotions and artist management, among other areas.

The company's Music Publishing business owns and acquires rights to musical compositions, exploits and markets the compositions, and receives royalties. It publishes music across a broad range of musical styles and hold rights in more than one million copyrights.

WMG generates more than half of its revenues from outside of the US.

The company sells and markets its products through subsidiaries, affiliates and non-affiliated licensees; digital distribution channels(direct marketing through access to consumers via websites and pre-release activity); retail and wholesale outlets including music specialty stores, general entertainment specialty stores, supermarkets, mass merchants and discounters, independent retailers and other traditional retailers; and partners with online service providers and mobile service providers, such as Amazon, Apple, Deezer, KKBox, Napster, Spotify, Telefonica, TIM, YouTube and Google.

The digital sales channel includes both online and mobile. Online sales include sales of traditional physical formats through both the online distribution arms of traditional retailers and traditional online physical retailers such as amazon.com, bestbuy.com, and barnesandnoble.com. ,WMG promotes it albums through television and radio coverage or live appearances of key artists.

WMG reported $3.2 billion in revenue for fiscal 2016. That was an increase of about $300 million compared to the $2.9 billion the company reported for revenue the prior fiscal year. However, the extremely high cost of doing business means that WMG has either suffered a net loss or claimed a very modest net income in recent fiscal years.

Like all companies in the music business, WMG struggles in the face of plummeting CD sales and slow growth in paid digital downloading. The company has responded to the difficulties in the market by embracing new technologies such as ringtones, videos, and downloaded songs and albums.

In 2016, WMG acquired Stockholm, Sweden based X5 Music Group. X5 specializes in licensing deep catalog and specific genres of music to create playlists, streaming and download products.

This acquisition will provide new opportunities for WMG's deep catalog, will help WMG to pioneer the digital music business, and also provides ability to curate specific genres of music and create engaging and accessible digital products and playlists.
