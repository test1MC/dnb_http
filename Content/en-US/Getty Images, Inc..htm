Getty Images doesn't have a photographic memory, but with its accumulation of images it's something of a memory for photography. The visual content provider is a major supplier of current and archival creative and editorial imagery, photography, stock footage, and music. Its offerings news, sports, and entertainment content, plus rare and contemporary archival imagery under brands iStock and Thinkstock. It also offers photo services for corporate clients. The company targets markets such as advertising and graphic design firms; editorial organizations (newspapers, magazines, and online publishers); corporate communications departments; and film and broadcast producers. Getty Images was founded 1995 by Mark Getty (chairman) and Jonathan Klein (CEO).

In 2014, Getty Images' photographers covered more than 16,000 news events, 28,000 sports events, and 50,000 entertainment events, delivering imagery to publishers around the world. It gets revenue from customers by licensing its images and other content in a variety of licensing formats.

Getty Images has customers in more than 100 countries around the world; most of its sales come from outside the US.

Getty has moved from selling a small number of images to a small number of customers to selling many images to more customers - at prices. At the same time, the company has gone from individual sales of rights to use images for a certain time to develop more subscriptions that allow unlimited use. Getty also allows free use of embeddable images through its partnership with Embed.ly. That provides users - from developers, online publishers, and individuals - access to millions of embeddable images. The company also has benefited from the number of publishers who have outsourced photography to a degree to Getty and its cadre of active photographers and deep archives.

In 2014 Getty Images partnered with Pantone and Tumblr to launch Stories and Trends. The company also partnered with Embed.ly which provides tens of thousands of developers, online publishers and individual users with access to millions of embeddable images directly within the Embed.ly ecosystem. Getty also partnered with Storify by LiveFyre, a social network service that enables the creation of stories or timelines using content sourced online and via social media.

In 2013 the company broadened its geographic presence as well as expanded its access to services such as photo assignment and digital asset management through the acquisition of third party distributor G&S Imagens do Brasil Ltda.
