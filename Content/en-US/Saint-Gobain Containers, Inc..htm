Saint-Gobain Containers (doing business as Verallia North America) is a glass act. The company is the #2 producer of glass containers in the US, behind Owens-Illinois . Its glassmaking process includes melting, glass conditioning, forming, and annealing and coating. Its products mainly serve the food and beverage industry. Everything from apple juice and beer to pickles and barbecue sauce are sold in Saint-Gobain Containers' jars and bottles. The company is also a major consumer of recycled glass. To focus on its core businesses, French building materials giant Compagnie de Saint-Gobain, which owns Verallia North America, announced plans in 2013 to sell it to Ireland's Ardagh Group SA for $1.7 billion.

Verallia North America is one of several subsidiaries within Compagnie de Saint-Gobain 's packaging sector business, but accounts for less than 10% of total sales. The glassmaker's operations in North America, which focus solely on producing glass containers, include 16 manufacturing facilities across the US. It annually produces more than 9 billion bottles and jars, with ranging from 8oz to 32oz in capacity.

It sells through such distributors as Berlin Packaging and Continental Packaging Solutions . Among the company's key customers is Anheuser-Busch , for which it regularly manufactures beer bottles.

The packaging sector accounted for 9% of Saint-Gobain's total revenues in 2012; the US, about 14%.

Extending the company's existing family of dairy glass packaging products, in 2012 Verallia North America introduced a new line of four and six ounce capacity single serve yogurt glass jars for national distribution.

In 2011 Verallia North America moved forward in installing an estimated $112 million in pollution-control equipment at its manufacturing plants. Under the Clean Air Act, US Department of Justice and the Environmental Protection Agency directed Verallia North America to reduce its emissions (sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides) and particulate matter, spewed by its plants into the environment.
