Drugmaker Concordia International (formerly Amdipharm Mercury Company, or AMCo) manufactures nearly 200 over-the-counter vitamin supplements and medications for a variety of ailments, including joint and heart health, skin care, menopause, and weight loss. It distributes its pharmaceutical products in more than 100 countries, primarily in Europe, the Middle East, and Africa. The firm was founded as Goldshield Group in 1991 by the Patel family. It merged with Amdipharm in 2013 to become AMCo; in late 2015, AMCo was acquired by Canadian firm Concordia Healthcare for $3.5 billion.
