Krizia's business is more than just sew-sew. The company has been creating clothes and accessories for the fashion-conscious since 1954. It offers some 50 lines per year, which it sells in franchised stores and a handful of company-owned ones (London, Milan, New York, Paris, Rome, and Venice). Named after a character in a dialogue by Plato about vanity, the company experiments with new materials, including eel skin, rubber, and cellophaned velvet. It also licenses its name for other products, including fragrances, watches, and champagne. Sales in Japan account for a growing portion of its revenue. Founder and designer Mariuccia Mandelli and her husband, Aldo Pinto, own the firm.
