If you XYZ (examine your zipper), it might well be a YKK. However, YKK Corporation of America, a subsidiary of Tokyo-based YKK , makes more than just zippers for jeans, suitcases, and tents. Its line of fastening cover-ups, often sold under the YKK label, includes hook and loop self-closing tape systems, fabric webbings, metal snaps, buttons, and buckles. The company's architectural products subsidiary supplies aluminum sliding parts such as green entrance and window systems for storefronts, window walls, and curtain wall systems. On the home front, YKK produces residential windows and doors. A vertical manufacturer, the company makes not only YKK products, but the machines and materials used to make them.

Although YKK Corporation of America today operates more than a dozen companies across North, South, and Central America, its Georgia facility is touted as the flagship production center for zippers.

YKK Corporation of America consists of 14 operating companies, but primarily operates in two divisions: Architectural Products, which produces entrances and window systems for commercial buildings, and vinyl windows and doors for homes; and Fastening Products, which produces the famous YKK brand zippers and metal buttons and snaps, hook and loop, and plastic buckles and notions, along with the Tapecraft brand webbing.

The group operates through the companies of YKK (U.S.A.), Tape Craft Corporation, YKK Snap Fasterners America, and YKK Canada.

North and Central America contributed 82% and South America contributed 18% YKK Corporation of America's revenues in 2014. American operations of account for 8% to the parent YKK 's net revenues of 2014.

In North and Central America, the parent company plans to strengthen development of highly-functional products, and focus on the R&D of automotive seat fastening products and processing lease equipment.

In 2015, YKK added 35HL/50HL impact resistant entrances to the Protek product line, for use where hurricane impact performance and cost are priorities. In 2014 it introduced the YPI 1500 Interior Access Panel Windows for new and retrofit installations. This product is an interior secondary access window system that improves thermal performance, reduces sound infiltration and adds privacy and security with optional between-glass Venetian blinds.

That year YKK Corporation of America planned to expand its residential window plant in Macon, Georgia, in 2015 to double the plant's production capacity. YKK will relocate its production lines into a larger building on its new campus and add two new production lines dedicated to vinyl window and multi-layered glass production.

YKK also opened a new manufacturing plant in Texas during 2014.

The company was established in 1960 when its parent opened its first US office in New York City.
