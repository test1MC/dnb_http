For Van Drunen Farms, thyme is on its side. As the nation's largest supplier of organically grown culinary herbs, the company grows and processes all-natural and functional food ingredients. Its expertise lies in providing certified organic and conventional fruits, vegetables, herbs, and specialty items to the food industry. Van Drunen grows herbs on 1,200 acres of farmland in Illinois and California and procures produce from US and European growers. The company also processes grains, legumes, dairy, and specialty food ingredients, such as freeze-dried cheeses, miso, surimi, and tofu. Owned and operated by the Van Drunen family, the company wholesales its products to other food processors and packers.

Van Drunen Farms operates its business from a headquarters office in Momence, Illinois. It cultivates a portion of its crops on farmland in its home state of Illinois and in California. Van Drunen Farms sources other produce from suppliers in the US and Europe.

From its modest beginnings as a small herb farm, Van Drunen Farms has grown to become one of the nation's largest suppliers of freeze-dried, drum-dried, low-moisture, and individually quick frozen (IQF) specialty products.

As part of its business, Van Drunen Farms cultivates 1,200 acres of farmland. Some 500 acres are dedicated to organic crops and about 700 acres are designated for growing conventional crops.

Looking to provide consumers with popular superfoods, Van Drunen Farms in 2012 introduced an IQF and freeze-dried kale. The leafy vegetable, which is grown on the company's Illinois farm, is offered in both conventional and organic varieties.
