When osteoarthritis brings patients to their knees, MAKO Surgical has a medical device that might help. The company developed and markets the MAKOplasty partial knee resurfacing system, a minimally invasive alternative to traditional knee replacement surgery for patients with early- to mid-stage osteoarthritis. Proprietary components of the system consist of patient-specific visualization technology and a robotic arm (RIO) that helps orthopedic surgeons prepare the knee joint for insertion of MAKO's resurfacing implants (RESTORIS) through a small incision. It also has developed a total hip arthroplasty application. MAKO was acquired by medical device maker Stryker for some $1.65 billion in 2013.

Stryker reached a deal to purchase MAKO in September 2013 to expand its robot-assisted surgery offerings; the transaction was completed in December 2013. MAKO will benefit from Stryker's strong presence in the orthopedic device industry.

The RIO is an interactive tactile surgical platform incorporating a robotic arm and patient-specific visualization technology for accurate placement of RESTORIS knee and hip implants.

In 2013 the company reported an intellectual property portfolio of more than 300 owned and licensed patents and patent applications in the areas of computer assisted surgery, robotics, haptics, and implants.

MAKO sells its products in the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and the Asia/Pacific Region.

MAKO markets to hospitals and surgeons in the US and Europe via a direct sales force and independent orthopedic product agents and distributors. It also has a network of distributors in Canada, China, Hong Kong, Israel, Italy, Japan, Korea, Singapore, and Turkey (which support activities in Africa), the Asia/Pacific region, Canada, the Middle East, Central America, South America, Europe, and Russia.

The company is part of Stryker's Orthopaedics division, which provides 43% of its total revenue.

MAKO has gained FDA clearance for both its RIO and RESTORIS implants. Other R&D efforts include testing the viability of MAKOplasty on such areas of the body as the shoulder and spine. MAKO's research is based on its portfolio of owned or licensed US and foreign patents and patent applications.

To enhance its robotic platform in 2013 MAKO has agreed to settle all patent infringement actions and acquire certain assets of Stanmore Implants Worldwide related to its Sculptor Robotic Guidance Arm business.

MAKO was formed in 2004.
