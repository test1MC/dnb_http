For Hampden Papers, wrapping a gift is more than covering it in order to surprise a recipient. The company manufactures converted coated papers and paperboard, and laminated film and foil. Its products are sold in 80-plus markets, used in gift wrap to greeting cards, liquor bags, as well as book covers and cosmetic packaging. Much of the line is fine lithographic paper, and glazed and embossed papers. Hampden's sole plant is equipped with some 30 machines for embossing, foil and film laminating, four-color gravure printing and air knife coating, sheeting, slitting, and trimming.

The company's products are used to create albums, art papers, entertainment and video packaging, gameboards, gift bags, greeting cards, jewelry boxes, and liquor cartons.

The company was established in 1880, as Hampden Glazed Paper and Card Company in Holyoke, Massachusetts, by Aaron Bagg, George Fowler, and Joseph Parsons. It was renamed Hampden Papers in 1973.
