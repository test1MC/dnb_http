MicroVention is into interventions. The company makes the MicroPlex Coil System and HydroCoil Embolic System, two endovascular products for treating cerebral aneurysms. The coils, which come in various sizes, are inserted into the problem arteries and either work to expand the artery or clear and support the aneurysm. Some 50,000 people in the US suffer from aneurysms, which frequently do not cause any obvious symptoms but can cause a stroke if ruptured. The company's other neurological offerings include occlusion balloons, flow diverters, and intraluminal stents. MicroVention was founded in 1997 and is owned by Japanese medical device firm Terumo Corporation .

MicroVention has facilities in Aliso Viejo, Tustin, and Santa Ana, California, as well as in San José, Costa Rica. The company's products are sold in more than 60 countries around the globe, including areas in the Americas, Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and the Asia/Pacific region.

MicroVention's systems are sold through direct sales representatives in countries including the US, Canada, France, Germany, and the UK, as well as through global distributors. It serves a network of about 500 global hospitals that perform neuroendovascular surgeries.

The company works to expand its product offerings through development and commercialization efforts. Its research and development programs are focused on the stroke device, acute stroke therapy, and neuro-interventional device markets. Current clinical trials include development efforts on a dual-layer stent for flow diversion in cerebral aneurysm treatment.

To expand its manufacturing capacity, MicroVention opened a new manufacturing facility in Costa Rica in 2013.
