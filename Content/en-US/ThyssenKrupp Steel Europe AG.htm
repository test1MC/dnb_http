For more than a century, the names Thyssen and Krupp have practically been synonyms for "steel" in Germany. ThyssenKrupp Steel Europe is the holding company for the European steel operations of global industrial titan ThyssenKrupp AG . It operates a steel mill in Germany, steel service centers throughout Europe, and an automotive unit. The processing unit produces tinplate materials and electrical steel products. ThyssenKrupp's steel operations produce more than 15 million tons annually worldwide, placing it among the largest in the world along with the likes of Arcelor Mittal , Nippon Steel , and Posco . ThyssenKrupp Steel Europe operates more than 90 facilities around the world.

The company supplies high-quality steel products for applications in a wide range of industries and offers customer-specific material products and services. ThyssenKrupp Steel Europe fulfills the increasing demands for ever-more-efficient lightweight construction and safety standards, researches and develops new high-tech steels, and sets standards for surface and processing technologies.

More than 90% of the material produced by the ThyssenKrupp Rasselstein plant (Germany's only tinplate manufacturer) is used in the packaging field (food, drinks) or chemical-technical products such as aerosol or paint cans.

Industry groups served include Automotive/Trucks, Special-Purpose Vehicles, Construction, Machine and Plant Engineering, Energy, Packaging and Household.

In order to safeguard its technology base and secure its production sites, ThyssenKrupp Steel Europe invests continuously in the modernization of its facilities. It has invested €1.35 billion since 2012 to keep its equipment up-to-date and maintain its position as a leading producer of flat-rolled steel.

Steel is ThyssenKrupp's largest business, with the deepest roots in its history: Friedrich Krupp opened his first steel factory in 1811, and August Thyssen founded his first steel mill in the 1870s.

In 2011 ThyssenKrupp acquired German logistics service provider Eisenbahn und Häfen GmbH. Under the terms of merger, the operating activities -- port, rail, and technology -- will be integrated into the Logistics Services unit of ThyssenKrupp Steel Europe. Prior to the merger, Eisenbahn und Häfen had been a provider of logistics services to ThyssenKrupp.

It has expanded its business in Asia in part through joint ventures and alliances with other steelmakers, including JFE Holdings and China's TAGAL. In 2006 the company also acquired Arconic 's aerospace service business. The main steelmaking unit is in the process of expanding internationally and began production in 2009 at a mill in Brazil.
