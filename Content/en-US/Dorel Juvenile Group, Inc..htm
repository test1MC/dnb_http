Getting little ones around town safely is Dorel Juvenile Group's (DJG) primary push. The group makes children's car seats, strollers, high chairs, changing tables, monitors, and toddler beds. DJG's brands include Cosco Juvenile, Maxi-Cosi, and Safety 1st and in Europe, Bébé Confort, Maxi-Cosi, Quinny, Baby Relax, Safety 1st, HOPPOP, Tiny Love, and BABY ART. Under license, it also makes the name brand infant safety products of Eddie Bauer , and Disney Baby. Its products are sold in almost 100 countries. DJG is part of Canadian Dorel Industries . Formed in 2000, the group, which combined the child safety lineup of Safety 1st with parent Dorel's juvenile business, Cosco, has expanded internationally.

Dorel Juvenile Group (DJG) has a car seat development and Center for Child Safety in Columbus, Indiana. Car seat manufacturing takes place in Foxboro, Massachusetts, and Ontario, California. Most other products are designed and made in Foxboro. DJG has divisions in North America, Europe, Latin America, Australia, and New Zealand.

The majority of the juvenile products maker's sales are made through large retailers, such as mass merchants and department stores. It also sells products through independent boutiques, juvenile specialty stores (such as Babies "R" Us), and online.

Sales by Dorel Juvenile Group (DJG) fell 5% in 2013 versus 2012, to $992.9 million, on economic weakness and a slower retail climate in certain markets, particularly Europe and North America. (DJG accounted for 41% of its parent company's total sales.) Latin America was a bright spot with Chile and Peru performing well, and improvement in Brazil. DJG's operating profit declined 22% year over year, to $57.2 million, or 6% of sales.

Dorel's focus in 2014 is on Asia, where it has agreed to acquire the juvenile business of Hong Kong-based Lerado Group , one of the largest juvenile product makers in China. Dorel Industries has agreed to pay HK$930 million ($120 million) for the business, which manufactures a wide range of juvenile products. the pending purchase, which is expected to close by the end of the year, will give Dorel more control over its supply chain.

During 2012 a comprehensive plan was implemented at Dorel Juvenile Group (DJG) USA to improve performance and Dorel Europe put in a solid performance despite economic woes there. In 2013, Latin America was the company's growth engine. Indeed, during the past five years, Dorel has broadened its Juvenile segment from its traditional base in North America and Europe by acquiring majority interests in businesses in Australia and Latin America.

In January 2014, the company acquired Tiny Love Ltd., a baby products and developmental toy company based in Tel Aviv, Israel. Developmental toys are a new yet complementary area for Dorel. Tiny Love has about $45 million in annual sales and its products are sold in more than 50 countries.
