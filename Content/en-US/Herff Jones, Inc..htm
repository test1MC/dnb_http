Herff Jones plays a small part in many memorable milestones, ceremonies, and traditions. It produces commemorative items for educational and athletic institutions, including caps, gowns, diplomas, awards, rings, medals, and yearbooks, and makes and markets robes used by religious and judicial entities, including the Supreme Court . Its education unit, Nystrom, makes and publishes materials for history and social studies classes, including globes, maps, atlases, and other items. Part of the same educational unit, It's About Time publishes programs for math and science study that are supported by the National Science Foundation . In 2014 the company was acquired by an investment group led by Charlesbank Capital.

Based in Indianapolis, Indiana, Herff Jones operates nationwide and boasts US-based manufacturing facilities.

Herff Jones' brands include Framing Success, Masters of Design and Murphy Robes.

Framing Success makes custom diploma and certificate frames and serves more than 2,000 universities, colleges, corporations, government agencies, and professional organizations.

Founded in 1971, Masters of Design has become the Nation's premier provider of award and championship rings, fine emblematic jewelry and professional awards. Masters of Design produces custom-crafted products for sports champions, corporate clients, medical, safety, group organizations, and to honor and commemorate military and public service.

Murphy Cap & Gown Company makes choral and pulpit attire. Its popular Qwick-Ship program includes a wide variety of pulpit, choral, and judicial apparel with next business day shipping from factory receipt of order and guaranteed satisfaction. It also offers the US' most extensive selection of custom tailored pulpit attire, choral attire, church supplies, judicial attire, and academic attire.

Herff Jones is a dominant force in school products. Along with American Achievement and Jostens, the trio dominates the class ring and yearbook segments of the school products market. Herff Jones also makes the Heisman Trophy and made a Super Bowl Champion Ring for the Indianapolis Colts . To grow its mature yearbook publishing business, Herff Jones has teamed up with Denver-based EffectiveUI to develop an online publishing tool called eDesign, a multi-user Web-to-print systems for collaboratively publishing school yearbooks.

The company makes and sells recognition products for customers across the US. It serves colleges, high schools, grade schools, middle schools, churches, commercial firms, sports teams, movie studios, and courtrooms.

Herff Jones specializes in offering graduation announcements, diplomas, caps and gowns, diploma frames, educational learning materials, class rings and jewelry, motivation and recognition products, sports jewelry and awards, fraternity and sorority jewelry, and yearbooks.

To raise cash and cut costs, in 2014 Herff Jones closed its Gettysburg plant.

In 2013 The National Football Foundation announced its continued partnership with Herff Jones.

In 2013 Herff Jones acquired Dallas-based BSN Sports , a sporting goods firm, and operates the business as a subsidiary.

As sales stall for hardbound yearbooks, Herff Jones has been investing in digital platforms for its yearbooks business. As part of the transition, the company shuttered a yearbook manufacturing plant in North Carolina in 2012.

Buying Varsity Brands in 2011 for about $250 million joined two leaders in providing complementary products to the educational market, as both cater to US high schools and colleges. Varsity Brands, the world's largest maker and distributor of cheerleading uniforms and accessories, is the cornerstone of the school spirit industry, operates instruction camps in the US for cheerleading and dance teams, and produces cheerleading competitions. As part of the transaction, smaller supplier Varsity Brands operates independently under Herff Jones's umbrella and maintains its Memphis, Dallas, and Los Angeles offices. The strategic acquisition follows a failed attempt by Herff Jones years earlier to acquire rival American Achievement Corp. (the Austin, Texas-based maker of class rings and yearbooks) from buyout firm Fenway Partners.

Other changes included selling off its photography division to rival Lifetouch in 2011.

The company was founded in 1920 by Harry Herff and Randall Jones. It became employee-owned in 1995.
