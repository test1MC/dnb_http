Korbel produces champagnes and other wines from the fruit of its vineyards and the products of its winery and distillery that are situated along California's Russian River in western Sonoma County. Long noted for its premium champagne and less expensive versions of the sparkling wine, Korbel also bottles a Cabernet Sauvignon from grapes grown in the nearby Alexander Valley, and a Chardonnay. Korbel diversified by buying three wineries in Sonoma County. Korbel was founded in 1882 by activist and cigar-maker Francis Korbel and his two brothers. The family name means "goblet" in Czech. Korbel heirs sold the company in 1954 to Adolf Heck. Heck's son Gary still runs the privately-owned business.

Sonoma County's Korbel makes and markets about a dozen champagnes and still wines across the US.

The California-based company sells its products nationwide. It ships its wines and champagnes to about 35 US states.

Hundreds of miles from the champagne cellars in Guerneville, California, Korbel Media of Beverly Hills coordinates Korbel's advertising, product design, and promotion, such as product placement in movies and television shows. Korbel's products are distributed by Brown-Forman .

In addition to tours of the winery and a tasting room, the sprawling complex on River Road offers tours of its Antique Rose Gardens, a shop for selling Korbel products, and a delicatessen/market for picnic lunches and gourmet groceries, along with souvenirs of the California wine country.
