UNICOM Engineering (formerly Network Engines) revs up hardware and software integration for many a technology provider. The company designs and builds server appliances and provides software integration services. Its integration services are used by software vendors and equipment manufacturers who resell and support the finished appliances under their own brands. UNICOM Engineering also offers platform management software and support services. The company targets the communications, storage, and security sectors. Customers include EMC (60% of sales), Tektronix (10%), ArcSight , Juniper Networks , and Sophos .

In 2012 Network Engines was bought by UNICOM Global in a deal valued at about $63 million. Network Engines was renamed UNICOM Engineering.

UNICOM Global works with major players in the industry, including Dell , HP , and NetApp to offer both products and turnkey installations.

The company has its headquarters in Massachusetts with engineering facilities in Texas and Ireland. UNICOM conducts manufacturing at all three sites.

UNICOM Engineering's strategy for continued growth hinges on the combination of products with support services encompassing functions such as integration control, platform management, global logistics, and support and maintenance after the sale. The company sees this focus on a comprehensive offering as its competitive advantage, providing a lower total cost of ownership to customers, thereby helping the company strengthen existing client relationships and win new ones. Consequently, NEI works not only on developing technologies that advance its products' functionality and deployment, but also on creating services that help it provide an improved time to market and total cost of ownership.
