Toshiba America, Inc. (TAI) is the spitting image of its old man. The US-based holding company, a subsidiary of Japan's high-tech titan Toshiba , handles North American business for its formidable parent. TAI's operating companies market laptops, netbooks, and PCs; MRIs and other medical imaging systems; semiconductors and hard disk drives; copiers and other office equipment; and a range of consumer electronics products (television sets, HD camcorders, DVD players). The company also provides grants for science and mathematics education through its not-for-profit organization, the Toshiba America Foundation. North American operations account for about 18% of Toshiba's total revenue.

TAI targets customers in a variety of markets, such as education, government, medical, manufacturing, and retail. It is a holding company for seven companies: Toshiba America Information Systems , a provider of laptops and notebooks, LCD and LED TVs, DVDs and Blu-Ray players, camcorders, and imaging systems; Toshiba American Medical Systems , which markets, sells, distributes and services diagnostic imaging systems, including CT scanners, Ultrasound, and MRI machines; Toshiba America Business Solutions , a maker of copiers, fax machines, and printers; Toshiba America Research, a research group focused on wireless and Internet communication; Houston-based Toshiba International Corp., provides motors, instrumentation, power generation and other systems to industrial customers; Toshiba America Electronic Components , a maker of semiconductors, memory and storage products, displays, and other electronic components; and Toshiba America Nuclear Energy Corp., which promotes Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) operations and maintenance support of existing US BWR nuclear power plants.

New York-based TAI has operation in the US, Canada, Mexico, and the Caribbean.

Toshiba reported that its North American sales rose 10% to $11.3 billion in 2014 (ended March). North America is Toshiba's third largest market after Japan and Asia.

The TAI group of companies specialize in customer markets comprised of retailers, consumers, hospitals and medical facilities, municipalities, universities, businesses, and high-technology manufacturers.

The Toshiba America Research Institute has trained its focus on big data technologies for applications in healthcare and building systems. The Toshiba researchers are developing technologies to analyze of large amounts of information to uncover patterns and predict outcomes for prevention and treatment of illnesses. They also are applying similar methods to make buildings more efficient to build and operate.
