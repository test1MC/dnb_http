Its name may sound like baby talk, but that's just so the little ones can ask for Smoby Toys' (formerly named SMOBY) innovative toys by name. The company's toy subsidiaries manufacture creative playthings for children ages 0-12, including play strollers (Picó) and other role-playing and creativity toys (Smoby), stacking and building toys (Écoiffier), balloons and balls (Unice), and football tables (Monneret). Another unit makes plastic packaging products, bottles, flasks, and containers for industrial customers. Sales outside France account for more than half of the total. Germany-based toy rival Simba Toys acquired the company in 2008 and made Smoby Toys a subsidiary.

Smoby Toys also provides role play toys in the areas of kitchen, cleaning, DIY, racing, music, beauty, market, medical, and heroes. In addition, it offers art and craft toys, such as desks, easels, and accessories; rides and tricycles, including tractors, karts quads, powered vehicles, and patinettes; and outdoor toys, such as houses, slides, multi games, swingsets, tables, and sand pits rockers.

The company offers its products online.

Looking to fill up its portfolio, Smoby Toys in 2010 purchased its former subsidiary, Majorette, a toy car maker. The company had been acquired by investment fund MI-29 in 2007, when Smoby Toys shed the car maker while it was under receivership. The deal, which brought the brands Solido and Bao, cost Smoby Toys close to $1 million.

Smoby Toys had a number of suitors in 2007 as it attempted to recover from a bankruptcy filing. The US's MGA Entertainment took a 56% ownership stake and 70% of the voting rights in May 2007. However MGA did not invest in the company or have a viable recovery plan, as the courts viewed it, and Smoby Toys was put under court-appointed receivership. Germany-based Simba Toys acquired Smoby Toys in 2008.

Founded in 1924, the company acquired fellow European toy maker Berchet in mid-2005.
