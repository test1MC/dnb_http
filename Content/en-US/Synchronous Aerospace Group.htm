Synchronous Aerospace Group has a lot of things going on at the same time. The Precision Castparts ' company, which specializes in the high-precision machining of structural aerospace components makes thousands of different parts for all sections of an aircraft, such as the airframe and engine. It also provides overhaul/repair and kitting services of hard metal and composite parts. Services include CNC (computer numerical control), milling, turning, assembly, program management, and grinding. Synchronous Aerospace operates five manufacturing facilities in the US.

The company operates manufacturing plants in Santa Ana, California; Kent, Washington; Prescott, Arizona; Wichita, Kansas; and Tulsa, Oklahoma.

Synchronous Aerospace's manufacturing operations are conducted through five subsidiaries, Brittain Machine, Compass Aerospace Northwest, Fortner Aerospace Manufacturing, Helicomb International, and StratoFlight, and through affiliate Weaver Manufacturing. Brittain Machine assists commercial and defense aircraft manufacturers and general aviation companies, including Lockheed Martin , Bombardier , and General Dynamics , with services that include kitting and assembly and long bed and complex machining at a 200,000 sq. ft. facility in a strategic location of the aerospace industry, Wichita, Kansas. Compass Aerospace Northwest provides machined flight safety parts and complex and overhaul flight safety critical assemblies for such major industry players as Boeing and Airbus .

Fortner Aerospace Manufacturing makes 15,000 precision small to medium parts at an 80,000 sq. ft. machining facility in Arizona for craft that include commercial airliners, fighter planes, and missiles. Clients include BE Aerospace , Honeywell , and Raytheon . Helicomb International manufactures and repairs composite and metal-bonded parts, sub-assemblies, and assemblies for the Department of Defense , aerospace OEMs, and fleet operators, among others. StratoFlight serves the West Coast commercial and defense aerospace industry with the machining of specialty type metals, sheet metal forming, and the assembly of complex components. Founded in 1942, affiliate Weaver Manufacturing, also in the aerospace center of Wichita, provides high-speed, close-tolerance machined parts and assemblies, machined parts in titanium and aluminum alloys, engine attachment rings, and kitting and assembly.

Synchronous Aerospace was founded in 2002.
