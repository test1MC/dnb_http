Think you have a high pressure job? Husky Injection Molding Systems makes high pressure injection molding equipment used by manufacturers to produce such plastic products as bottles, containers, computer housings, cell phones, auto parts, and medical devices (syringes and vials). The world's #1 equipment molding brand supplier builds molding and hot runner systems, which distribute plastic resin to mold cavities, as well as part-handling robots that remove the plastic products from the mold, to sort and stack them. The PET (polyethylene terephthalate) packaging market accounts for more than half of Husky's sales.

Husky operates manufacturing plants in Canada and the US, as well as in Luxembourg and China. Its sales and service network, some 40 offices, dot more than 100 countries; about 75% of all sales are made beyond North America.

Husky's equipment is used to manufacture a wide range of plastic products such as bottles and caps for beverages, containers for food, medical components, and consumer electronic parts.

The company designs and manufactures a comprehensive range of complete, integrated injection molding systems for a variety of key markets. Its systems include Hylectric, HyPET, H-PET AE, H-MED AE, HyPAC and HyCAP.

Husky's goal is to continuously develop innovative technologies to help its customers streamline their operations and improve their bottom lines. The company's investment in research and development has resulted in a slew of product patents and improvements.

In 2012, to complement Husky's existing suite of beverage closures products, the company launched its next generation HyCAP ™ system for high-output beverage closure manufacturing.

Its all-electric injection press for medial molding, launched in 2011, was the plastic machinery industry's first simulated clean environment. Drives and motors are supplied by Toyo Machinery & Metal Co. The addition enables Husky to tap demand for medical clean room molding. In the market for molding PET preforms, Husky has introduced a new generation of its hot runner system, which decreases the weight of beverage containers and closures, thus mitigating resin costs, and increases productivity and energy efficiency by up to 15%. It is also developing a line for molding recycled PET containers into preforms.

Also in 2011, the company acquired Austria-based Kunststofftechnik Waidhofen a.d. Thaya Ges.m.b.H. (KTW), a closure mold maker. The deal, which builds upon a 14-year collaboration, improves the marriage of KTW-brand molds with Husky's systems. As a result, Husky stands as the only provider of fully integrated injection molding systems.

Like Apple and Hewlett-Packard , Husky grew, literally, out of a garage. Robert Schad started the company as a small machine shop in 1953. Schad served as president and CEO of Husky until 2005. He held more than a 40% controlling interest in the company until its takeover 2007.
