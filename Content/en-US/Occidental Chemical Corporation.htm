The chemicals business of Occidental Petroleum , Occidental Chemical (OxyChem) manufactures basic chemicals, vinyls, and performance chemicals. The chemical production company is the top US maker of chlorine and caustic soda (used in bleach, plastics, and pulp and paper) and the top producer of potassium hydroxide, or caustic potash (used in fertilizers). Through subsidiary OxyVinyls it's the leading North American maker of vinyl chloride monomer (VCM), which is used to make polyvinyl chloride (PVC) resins. The company operates some 24 manufacturing plants.

OxyChem owns and operates manufacturing plants at 22 sites in the US (in Alabama, Georgia, Illinois, Kansas, Louisiana, Michigan, New Jersey, New York, Ohio, Pennsylvania and Texas) and at two international sites in Canada and Chile. The company also has a joint venture Ingleside Ethylene LLC in Mexico.

OxyChem holds leading market positions for almost every product it makes. Subsidiary OxyVinyls is the world's largest VCM producer and third-largest PVC producer, and the second-largest PVC supplier in North America.

OxyChem has a joint venture with Church & Dwight Co. and has formed Armand Products Company, which sells potassium carbonate and potassium bicarbonate. The venture annually produces 108,000 tons of potassium carbonate and 5,000 tons of potassium bicarbonate.

It has a joint venture with Dow Chemical to produce a new chlorocarbon for next-generation, climate-friendly refrigerants. The chlorocarbon, known as HCC-120xa, is used to produce the refrigerant HFO-1234yf, which has low global-warming potential (GWP) and zero ozone depletion potential (ODP). It is used in auto air-conditioning systems.

OxyChem operates salt mines in Louisiana, Kansas, and Wisconsin, from which it manufactures chlor-alkali chemicals like chlorine, caustic soda, and hydrochloric acid.

The company has an annual capacity of producing 6.2 billion pounds of VCM, 2.1 billion pounds of ethylene dichloride, and 50 million pounds of Resorcinol (a wood adhesives and flame retardant chemical).

OxyChem's products are used in processing of raw material for ethylene dichloride, water treatment and pharmaceuticals; for pulp, paper and aluminum production; in refrigerants, fertilizers, batteries, soaps, detergents, and specialty glass; as a precursor for PVC; and for piping, building materials, automotive, and medical products.

Oxy Chem does not report separate financials. Occidental Petroleum reported that its Chemicals segment's revenues grew by 2% in 2013 In The basic chemical business saw lackluster domestic demand and pricing for basic chemical products. However, exports of downstream chlorine derivatives into the vinyls chain remained competitive as a result of the North American feedstock cost advantages (driven by natural gas prices). In the US, anticipation of additional capacity coming online 2014 from the commissioning of three additional chlor-alkali plants, (including OxyChem's plant in Tennessee), put downward pressure on liquid caustic soda prices. Calcium chloride and potassium hydroxide segments improved in 2013 as US demand grew and margins remained stable.

The company's Vinyls business improved in 2013 thanks to a 4% growth in US demand due to strengthening housing and commercial construction markets. This was offset by lower exports, resulting in no change in industry operating rates in 2013.

The company, which grows organically and through joint ventures and acquisitions, focuses on being a low-cost producer in order to maximize cash flow. It concentrates on the chlorovinyls chain beginning with chlorine, which is co-produced with caustic soda, and markets both to third parties. Chlorine, together with ethylene, is converted through a series of intermediate products into PVC.

During 2013 OxyChem completed a 182,500-ton-per-year membrane chlor-alkali plant in Tennessee.

In a further expansion, in 2013, OyChem and Mexichem formed a 50/50 joint venture, Ingleside Ethylene LLC, to build a 1.2-billion-pound per year capacity ethylene cracker at the OxyChem plant in Ingleside. The VCM produced by the cracker will be delivered to Mexichem to produce PVC and PVC piping systems. The project should be completed in 2017.

OxyChem owned 50% of Carbocloro, a Brazilian chlor-alkali and derivatives producer. In 2013 joint venture partner Unipar acquired OxyChem's 50% stake for $273 million and formed Carbocloro Chemical Industries Ltd.
