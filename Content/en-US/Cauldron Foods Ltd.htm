Cauldron Foods cooks up an appetite-sating range of meatless food offerings. The Exponent Private Equity company makes a chilled and frozen meat-free lineup of food products under the Cauldron brand name. Offerings include vegetable-based burgers and sausages, as well as pâtés, snacks, pantry ready meals, and plain and seasoned tofu. The company has grown from supplying small health food shops to major supermarket chains, such as Sainsbury's and Tesco , throughout the UK. Its suppliers have long term agreements with growers in Canada, China, and Europe, sourced from strict sustainable areas where beans must be grown in ways that are organically, ecologically, and ethically wholesome.

Cauldron products are produced in totally meat free factories, its main ingredients are tofu (made from organic soya beans), chickpeas, soya and vegetables. The leading chilled tofu brand in the UK, Cauldron products are made with natural ingredients, inspired by authentic recipes from across the globe.

Cauldron products are available at leading supermarkets and health food stores.

Bolstered by funding for marketing activities and a broader distribution, Cauldron looks to benefit under a revamped business strategy that educates consumers about the line's contribution to a healthy lifestyle. To this end, the brand is targeted for even wider distribution coupled with an investment in new product launches and promotional efforts. Competition from other meat-free offerings, however, is high; Cauldron shares the category with the Linda McCartney Foods, made under license by Hain , and Amy's Kitchen , among several.

Cauldron was founded in 1981.

Food manufacturing giant Premier Foods paired Cauldron with meat-free Quorn Foods maker Marlow , but sold off the two vegetarian branded businesses to Exponent Private Equity in 2011 for £205 million ($320 million).
