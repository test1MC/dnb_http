Xerox Canada is a smaller copy of its parent company. The subsidiary of US-based Xerox Corporation , provides document imaging products, including printers, copiers, facsimile machines, all-in-one multifunction printers, and related document management software. The company's extensive line of printers ranges from small office models to large-scale digital production presses. Serving Canada's consumer, enterprise, and professional printing markets, Xerox Canada also provides consulting, outsourced document management services, technical support, and training. The Xerox Research Centre of Canada in Mississauga, Ontario, is a prominent center for technology innovation for companies in Canada.

Xerox Canada's document outsourcing services include enterprise print services, document transaction processing services, enterprise marketing services, and product lifecycle communication services.

Xerox Corp. subsidiaries affiliated with Xerox Canada include FairCopy Services Inc., Xerox Canada Capital Ltd., Xerox Canada Facilities Management Ltd., Xerox Canada Finance Inc., and Xerox Canada Leasing Company. Delphax Systems, a Massachusetts firm acquired by Xerox, was renamed Ionographic Operations Partnership and now is owned by Xerox Canada Inc. and Xerox Canada Ltd.

The Toronto-based company has satellite offices in Vancouver; Calgary; Winnepeg, Manitoba; Ottawa, Onatario; Montreal; Dartmouth, Nova Scotia; and Saint John, New Brunswick.

Xerox Canada, which had a staff of 3,300 employees in 2015, serves small, medium, and large enterprises in the financial services, retail, telecommunications, healthcare, education, and transportation industries, among others in Canada. Its customers have included the University of British Columbia and Western Forest Products , as well as the government of Canada.

Xerox Canada also had alliances with McAffee and Cisco in 2015, which provided security services for the company's products.

In 2012, parent Xerox bought Ontario-based managed print services company LaserNetworks, making it a subsidiary of Xerox Canada. The deal expanded Xerox's presence in the country and added to its capabilities.

Originally known as The Haloid Company of Canada, Xerox Canada was established in 1953. Its name was changed to Xerox of Canada Limited in 1961 and to Xerox Canada Inc. in 1980.
