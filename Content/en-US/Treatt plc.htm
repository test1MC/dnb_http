Treatt gives your nose and taste buds a real treat. Although the company is not a flavor and fragrance maker, Treatt does make raw materials used in the flavor and fragrance, and food and beverages industries. It blends and distills essential oils. Products include flavor-enhancing citrus oils and Treattarome, a line of flavors named after the food from which they are distilled. The company's ingredients are used in air fresheners, cosmetics, soaps, drinks, confectionery, and pharmaceuticals. Treatt operates as RC Treatt and Earthoil in the UK, and outside the UK as Treatt USA and Treatt China.

Treatt makes and supplies ingredients for the flavor, fragrance, and consumer goods markets. Earthoil, the personal care division of Treatt, specializes in pure, organic, fair trade, and natural, essential, and vegetable seed oils.

The company has manufacturing plants in the UK, the US, and Kenya, and sales offices in China, France, the UK, and the US. It serves customers in more than 90 countries.

The US accounts for 40% of its Treatt's global revenue.

Treatt sells products through its own sales team and contracted distributors. It primarily serves the beverage sector.

The company's top five customers account for about 30% of total revenue.

In fiscal 2016 (September year end) Treatt's revenue grew by 2.5% to £88 million due to the success of the company's focus on added-value products (sugar reduction, tea, natural distillates, and citrus), which was partially offset by the drag of low-margin products.

Net operating margin rose by 11% as the result of higher revenue, the strategic shift to higher value products, and a tighter control on costs.

In fiscal 2016 the company's operating cash flow grew to £8.8 million (up from £7.2 million a year earlier) due to a change in working capital.

In 2016 Treatt and Northumbria University at Newcastle secured funding from Innovate UK (an executive non-departmental public body, sponsored by the Department for Business, Innovation, and Skills) for a project involving biotransformations of natural and inexpensive feedstocks to high value compounds.

In response to increased consumer demand, in 2015 Treatt opened a new beverage applications center at its UK headquarters in Bury St Edmunds.

Treatt was founded in 1886.
