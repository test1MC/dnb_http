Interlake Mecalux is not lax about making racks. The company builds industrial storage pallet racks used in distribution centers, manufacturing plants, warehouses, and retail outlets, and many other facilities. These racks are primarily used to make storage systems. Interlake Mecalux's products are delivered directly and through distributors to a diverse mix of businesses, including Amazon.com , The Home Depot , Nestl√© , UPS , and Wal-Mart . The company is a subsidiary of Spanish shelving manufacturer Mecalux and is a part of its US division under the Interlake Mecalux name. It operates through its locations in Illinois and South Carolina.

Based in Melrose Park, Illinois, Interlake Mecalux has manufacturing units in Chicago and Pontiac, Illinois; and Sumter, South Carolina.

The company markets its products through its own sales force and via distributors. It serves companies in a range of industries, including Distribution and Warehouse, Manufacturing, Archive and Record Storage, Food, Grocery, Energy, and Health Care. Interlake Mecalux's major customers include General Electric , Carnival Cruise Lines , B/E Aerospace , DHL , Red Bull , Baxter , PepsiCo , Coca Cola , and NASA .

Interlake Mecalux's predecessor company traces its historical roots back to 1882. Following development of a barbed steel staple (used in making bed frames) the business gained momentum by expanding with a line of implements and assuming the name, Quincy Hardware Manufacturing Company in Quincy, Illinois.
