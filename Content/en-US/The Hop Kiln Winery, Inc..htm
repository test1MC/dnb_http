The Hop Kiln Winery produces a number of custom blends that go by names like "A Thousand Flowers" or "Big Red." The company also produces its own Chardonnay. Wines are available from the company's Web site to states that allow such shipments. The company traces its roots back to 1960, when physician and environmentalist Martin Griffin purchased a 240-acre sheep ranch along the Russian River. The property was dominated by a huge building, dating back to 1905, where hops were dried for making beer. The dilapidated building was carefully repaired and restored; it's now the picturesque centerpiece of Hop Kiln Winery.
