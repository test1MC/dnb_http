No more hospital hide and seek. Versus Technology makes it easier to track down staff and patients in bustling health care environments with its real-time location systems (RTLS) -- made up of hardware, software, and infrared and radio frequency identification (RFID) technologies. The systems can be integrated within an existing IT infrastructure to pinpoint the moving locations of people, equipment, and charts. They are also used to control access, record and report events, and permit communication. Its badges and tags are worn by emergency room, nursing, and security staff and can be attached to mobile medical equipment. Founded in 1988, Versus was acquired by medical equipment maker Midmark in 2016.

The acquisition of Versus Technology boosts Midmark's position in clinical workflows. Midmark's existing Midmark Clinical Solutions (MCS) tool helps health systems improve workflows and patient experiences; the addition of Versus' RTLS technology will improve the MCS offering.

With health care as its core market, customers include Christiana Care Health System , Memorial Health System , PeaceHealth , and Womack Army Medical Center at Fort Bragg, one of the largest US Army installations in the world.

The company markets its products primarily in North America.
