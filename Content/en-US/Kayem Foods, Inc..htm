Kayem Foods makes one of the top-selling hot dogs in New England and is a fixture at Fenway Park. Rolling out some 1 million dogs daily, the company also makes traditional Italian sausages, bratwurst, deli meats, spiral hams, and fresh gourmet chicken sausage. Products are sold under brand names Deutschmacher, Essem, Kayem, al fresco, Genoa Sausage, Meisterchef, Schonland's, and Triple M. Outside the New England states, Kayem sells its meats nationwide. US food customers include retailers Hannaford , Price Chopper , Kroger , and Town & Country . It also serves food service accounts the likes of U.S. Foodservice and SYSCO . Founded more than a century ago, Kayem is still owned and operated by the Monkiewicz family.

The food company operates from a headquarters office in Chelsea, Massachusetts, and has additional operations in nearby Woburn. Fenway Franks are not distributed outside of New England and in Connecticut east of the Connecticut River.

As the largest meat processor in New England, Kayem produces some 400 products. It makes more than 100 varieties of hot dogs and 300 varieties of deli meats, sausage, and dinner hams. Its al fresco brand is the best-selling chicken sausage nationwide. Kayem Franks are New England's #1 brand of franks.

To remain the top dog among customers at sporting events, Kayem regularly inks multi-year licensing deals with professional teams. Its Fenway Frank is the official hot dog of the Boston Red Sox . The Tampa Bay Rays also secured Kayem products to became the team's official hot dog and sausage. In 2012 Kayem became the official hot dog and sausage of the Miami Marlins Baseball team and Marlins Park. It followed up the deal in 2013 by becoming the official frank and sausage brand of the Boston Bruins and TD Garden.

Kayem serves both retailers and food service operators nationwide. It counts retailers Kroger , Town & Country , Hannaford , Price Chopper, SYSCO , and U.S. Foods among its customers.
