Reserve Alloys has no reservations about scrap aluminum. A leading supplier of aluminum in the Midwest and southeastern US, the company recycles more than 100 million pounds of aluminum scrap from 30 US states and Canada annually. At its facilities in Ohio and Georgia, Reserve Alloys sorts, shears, and shreds various grades and types of aluminum and other non-ferrous metals. The company processes the recycled aluminum to manufacture aluminum ingot, sow, and deoxidant cone, which the company sells to manufacturers of aluminum products. Reserve Alloys' parent, Reserve Management Group, bought the assets of the bankrupt Commercial Alloys Corporation in 2009 and renamed the operation Reserve Alloys.
