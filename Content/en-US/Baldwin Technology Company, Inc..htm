Baldwin wins when your printing machinery runs smoothly. Baldwin Technology Company makes and supplies an array of process automation technologies for commercial printing and newspaper publishing. It manufactures printing press equipment and control systems, used for press cleaning, ink control, drying, water-regulation, spray dampening, UV and LED curing, and paper flow. Baldwin products are marketed as a premium technology via subsidiaries and distributors in about a dozen countries. Customers include printing press OEMs, who bolt the products onto their own systems for sale to printers. The company also sells directly to printers wanting to upgrade their presses.

Baldwin is headquartered in St. Louis, Missouri, and has operations located in nine countries (Hong Kong, China-Shanghai, Australia, New Zealand, India, Central Europe-Mediterranean, the UK, Ireland and Japan), on 19 sites, around the globe.

Baldwin markets its products to printing press manufacturers, newspaper publishers, and commercial printers.

Baldwin's product segments include commercial-web printing, digital printing, flexo printing, sheetfed printing, newspaper and semi-commercial printing, and press consumables. It also provides press services and spare parts.

Baldwin grows by introducing new products from its portfolio. In 2016 it launched the new FlexoDry2 and Flexo Cleaner Brush brands. It also developed a new and advanced series of PREPAC cleaning products for blanket and impression cylinder cleaning.

William Gegenheimer founded the company in 1918 in his garage in the town of Baldwin on Long Island in New York. A former printer and press service technician, Gegenheimer invented a system for cleaning offset printing presses in minutes (a process that previously had taken hours). The company grew as it expanded its line of products.
