Helpful Terminology

Support Definitions

“Acknowledged by D&B” the duration between the time the issue is submitted to D&B by and D&B’s acknowledgment of the issue to .

"Customer core business impacted" is defined on a case-by-case basis by .

“Issue Definition” means the description of issues impacting user(s) per Severity Level Table as decided by representative.

“Notification Channel” is the means through which a message is sent by to D&B through creation, 800 telephone support number, email alias.

“Resolved SLA by D&B” the maximum duration by which the issue is considered resolved by .

“Resolution” a written acceptance through Notification channel that Issue has been resolved to ’s satisfaction.

“Security breach / Lost Data” shall mean data is compromised or lost.

“Severity Levels” level at which issues will be categorized and handled according to assigned severity. The severity level will be decided by the representative at time of creation at its sole discretion and in line with the definitions in this document.

“Update by D&B” the duration by which the issue is updated by D&B to .

"Data Quality" covers to the state of completeness, validity, consistency, timeliness and accuracy that makes data appropriate for a specific use.

Severity Level Terms

“Outage” Critical production issue affecting all users of , including system unavailability or bugs having a significant impact. There is no workaround available and resolution is required immediately or within the boundaries of the Service Level Agreement (SLA) defined below.

“System-Wide or Multi-customer Regression” Fundamental, system-related issue affecting multiple customers that occurs after a release or patch. Functionality, which was previously working before a patch/major, release and is no longer functioning as it was previously. This is NOT a performance issue affecting only a single customer.

Customers will most likely experience systemic regression issues due to poor performing pl/SQL and/or app server “out of memory” conditions.

“Data Corruption” Data customers rely upon have been overwritten with incorrect or otherwise out of date data.

“Data Breach” Applications, databases, or files have been accessed by those other than explicitly defined users.

“Data Theft” Loss of data asset to unauthorized parties or for unintended use

Availability

The D&B APIs that requires access to in order to deliver the Combined Offerings will each have an availability rate of 99.00% (excluding scheduled maintenance and downtime, which will take place during a weekly window from 11:00 PM Saturday night until 9:00 AM Sunday morning (unless otherwise notified to at least 48 hours in advance), not to exceed ten (10) hours per week,

The average response time should be of less than or equal to three (3) seconds, (except for Family Tree, which may have an average response time of less than four (4) seconds) as calculated for a given calendar month; provided, that in no event shall an API suffer outage(s) exceeding forty eight (48) hours in the aggregate in a given calendar month.
